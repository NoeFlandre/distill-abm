 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model allows users to manipulate parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration .  Trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test shows a consistent increasing trend in the early phases, which gradually diminishes and eventually shows a slight decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation period . For decision-makers, these trends imply that initial hunting pressures are intense but eventually reach a steady state .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and significant decreasing trend over time . For decision-makers, this trend indicates that under the current model parameters, the male animal population is not sustainable and will eventually go extinct . Understanding these dynamics can inform conservation strategies and policies aimed at maintaining sustainable wildlife populations . By the end of the simulation, the total energy of female animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy reserves among the prey animals . The consistent decrease in energy levels highlights the pressure on the female animal population .
