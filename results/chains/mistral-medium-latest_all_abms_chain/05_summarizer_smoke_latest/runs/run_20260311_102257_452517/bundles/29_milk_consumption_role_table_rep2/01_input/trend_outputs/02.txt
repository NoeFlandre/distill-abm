The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several key trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent over the observed time steps. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29. This is followed by a rapid and significant increase in consumption, reaching up to 1265.47 by the 11th time step. This initial phase of rapid growth is statistically significant, as indicated by the Mann-Kendall trend tests, which show strong increasing trends with high tau values and significant p-values in the early windows. 

Following this rapid increase, the consumption values enter a phase of relative stability with minor fluctuations. During this period, which spans from approximately the 10th to the 24th time step, the Mann-Kendall tests indicate no significant trends, suggesting a plateau in consumption levels. The values oscillate around a mean of approximately 1220.21, with minor peaks and valleys, such as peaks at 1240.35 at the 13th time step and 1226.86 at the 21st time step, and valleys at 1233.62 at the 12th time step and 1209.70 at the 23rd time step. 

Towards the end of the simulation, there is a resurgence in the increasing trend of milk consumption. This is evidenced by the significant increasing trend in the Mann-Kendall window from the 25th to the 29th time step, where the tau value is 1.000 and the p-value is significant. The consumption reaches its maximum value of 1456.60 at the final time step, indicating a renewed upward trajectory in the latter part of the simulation. 

The dominant period of oscillation is identified as 15 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the consumption data. The inflection points and oscillation indices further highlight the periods of change and stability within the simulation. Overall, the simulation demonstrates an initial rapid adoption phase, a middle phase of stabilization with minor fluctuations, and a final phase of renewed increase in the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk.
