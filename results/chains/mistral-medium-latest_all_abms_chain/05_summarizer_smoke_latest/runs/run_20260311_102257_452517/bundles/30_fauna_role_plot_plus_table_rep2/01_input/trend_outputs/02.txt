The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and steady decline throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy of foragers starts at a value of 2000 units. This value rapidly decreases in the early stages, crossing zero around the 358th time step. The decline continues steadily, reaching approximately -5000 units by the 650th time step. This trend persists with the energy levels dropping further to around -10000 units by the 1075th time step and continuing to decrease linearly. By the 1500th time step, the energy levels are around -15000 units, and this downward trend continues, reaching approximately -20000 units by the 1925th time step. Finally, at the end of the simulation period, the total energy of foragers is around -38229 units. The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decreasing trend across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values indicating a strong and steady decline in the total energy of foragers throughout the simulation.
