The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero and rapidly increases, reaching around 100 by approximately 500 steps. This rapid increase is followed by a gradual rise, eventually plateauing around 120 females killed. The plateau is maintained with minor fluctuations until the end of the simulation at 73000 steps. Key inflection points occur at various steps, notably around 245, 262, and 285 steps, indicating significant changes in the rate of increase. The data shows a consistent upward trend in the early stages, which stabilizes as it approaches the maximum value. The statistical analysis reveals multiple change points and inflection indices, highlighting periods of significant shifts in the trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates increasing trends in the initial windows, with a shift to a decreasing trend in the later stages, suggesting a stabilization in the number of females killed. The oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation suggest minor periodic variations around the plateau. Overall, the dynamics reflect an initial rapid hunting phase followed by a stabilization phase where the number of females killed remains relatively constant.
