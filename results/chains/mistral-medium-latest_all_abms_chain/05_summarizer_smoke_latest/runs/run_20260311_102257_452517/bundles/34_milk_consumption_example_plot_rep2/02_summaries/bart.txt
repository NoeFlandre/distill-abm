 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The total number of choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 30 time steps . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,600 units . The overall trend demonstrates a significant shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . The low variance across simulation runs indicates a high degree of predictability in the adoption behavior .  The mean value health deviation starts at a low value close to zero, indicating a high level of agreement or similarity in the agents' initial choices . The habit factor continues to rise steadily, reaching a value of approximately 1.6 by the 30th step . This consistent upward trend indicates a growing preference and habit formation for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the agents over the simulated time period .
