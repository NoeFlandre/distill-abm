agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero . this initial slow increase could be attributed to the initial low adoption rate as agents begin to consider and switch to healthier milk options . by step 15 to step 30, the increase continues at a steady linear rate . the variance among different simulation runs remains relatively low throughout the entire period . the mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 . it remains relatively constant for the first few steps, indicating a stable initial habit factor among the agents . following this, the habit factor continues to rise gradually until approximately the 10th step onwards .
