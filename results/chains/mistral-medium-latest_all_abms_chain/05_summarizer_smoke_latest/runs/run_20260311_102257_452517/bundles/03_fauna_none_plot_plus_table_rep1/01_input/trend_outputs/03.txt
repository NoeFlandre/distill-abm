The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of males killed starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the rate of increase begins to slow down. By approximately 1000 steps, the cumulative number of males killed reaches a plateau, stabilizing around 120 males. This plateau is maintained with minor fluctuations for the remainder of the simulation period up to 2000 steps. The statistical analysis indicates that the mean number of males killed is approximately 100.4, with a median of 116.5, suggesting that the data tends to cluster around the higher end of the range. The standard deviation of 32.5 indicates some variability in the number of males killed across different simulations. The maximum value recorded is 120.2 males killed, while the minimum remains at zero, reflecting the initial condition. The analysis of trends using the rolling Mann-Kendall test shows significant increasing trends in the early phases of the simulation, transitioning to periods with no significant trend, and eventually to a significant decreasing trend towards the end. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points further highlights the dynamic nature of the hunting pressure over time. Overall, the model demonstrates an initial rapid increase in the number of males killed, followed by a stabilization phase with minor oscillations.
