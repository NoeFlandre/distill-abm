The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the energy level has dropped significantly to approximately 2,000 units. The decline continues more gradually after this point, with several inflection points indicating changes in the rate of decrease. Notable inflection points occur around time steps 299, 472, 506, and several others up to 2194, suggesting periods where the rate of energy decline changes. The energy levels exhibit minor oscillations with a dominant period of 63 time steps, indicating some periodic fluctuations in the energy values. However, the overall trend remains a steady decline. By the end of the simulation at 2,311 time steps, the total energy of female animals reaches zero. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows, reinforcing the consistent decline in energy levels over time.
