the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . the number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period of 73000 time steps . around the 500 time step mark, the total energy starts to decline gradually . the cumulative number of postpartum females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 500 time steps, reaching approximately 100 . this stabilization suggests that a balance is reached where the remaining posts are either less accessible or better able to avoid predation . these trends can inform conservation efforts aimed at protecting vulnerable populations during their most susceptible life stages . the number of male animals in the simulation starts at approximately 120 individuals . this initial population size reflects the set value of the initial number of prey, which is 250, with a roughly balanced sex ratio . initial sharp decline underscores the vulnerability of male animals to these pressures . subsequent slower decline suggests a potential threshold beyond which the population cannot sustain itself . this information could be crucial for developing conservation strategies or understanding the dynamics of extinction in similar contexts.
