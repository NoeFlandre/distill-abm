The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at a baseline value of 0.0000, indicating an initially equal distribution of livestock health across households. Over the course of 100 time steps, the Gini coefficient exhibits a consistent upward trend, reaching a final value of 0.0726. This indicates an increasing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households over time. The mean value of the Gini coefficient across all time steps is 0.0302, with a median of 0.0229, suggesting that while the inequality increases, the central tendency remains relatively low. The standard deviation of 0.0239 and variance of 0.0006 indicate some variability in the inequality levels over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results show a consistent increasing trend in inequality across all windows, with significant p-values, reinforcing the observation of a steady rise in inequality. Inflection points are noted at various time steps, suggesting periods where the rate of increase in inequality changes. Change points at time steps 51, 71, and 100 highlight significant shifts in the trend. The oscillation indices suggest some periodic fluctuations in the latter part of the time series, although the overall trend remains upward. This analysis reflects the dynamics of livestock health distribution becoming increasingly unequal among households as the simulation progresses.
