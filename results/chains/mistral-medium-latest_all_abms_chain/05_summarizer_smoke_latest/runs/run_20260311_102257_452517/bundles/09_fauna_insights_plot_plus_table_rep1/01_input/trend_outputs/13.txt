The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred steps, indicating a sharp drop in the overall energy of the prey population. By around 500 steps, the total energy has decreased significantly to roughly 4,000 units. This rapid decline continues, and by approximately 1,000 steps, the total energy stabilizes at much lower values, fluctuating around a few hundred units. The trend continues to decrease gradually, eventually approaching zero by the end of the simulation at 73,000 steps. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with multiple significant windows identified by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, all showing a significant decrease in total energy over time. The presence of several inflection points and change points further supports the observation of a rapidly declining energy level with periods of slight stabilization before continuing to decrease. The dominant oscillation period of 63 steps suggests some periodic fluctuations in the energy levels, but these are minor compared to the overall downward trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, the prey population's energy reserves are consistently diminishing, which could lead to potential extinction events or significant reductions in population size over time.
