 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment . This rapid increase continues more gradually until around 1000 steps, where the cumulative count stabilizes around 120 .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . Initially, the number of deaths is zero, reaching approximately 200 deaths by around 500 steps . This rapid increase is followed by a more gradual rise, eventually plateauing at around 235 deaths by approximately 1000 steps . The plateauing of deaths indicates a potential equilibrium state in the model . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing strategies to manage or mitigate extinction risks in similar ecological scenarios .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time . For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, the animal population is not sustainable and heads towards extinction . This insight can guide further investigations into adjusting parameters such as hunting pressure, environmental conditions, or animal life history traits to potentially achieve more stable or equilibrium conditions .
