the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . the cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population exhibits several distinct trends over the simulated time steps . Initially, there are no births until around 500 steps, where a gradual increase begins . by approximately 750 steps, the number of births stabilizes at around 2 births . total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and rapid decline over time . within the first 500 steps, there is a steep decrease in total energy, dropping to around 2,000 units . this rapid decline continues, and by 1,000 steps, the total energy remains very low, close to zero .
