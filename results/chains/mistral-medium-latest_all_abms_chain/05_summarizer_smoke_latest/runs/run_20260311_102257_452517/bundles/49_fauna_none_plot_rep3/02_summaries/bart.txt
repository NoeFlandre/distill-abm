 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  Model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and rapid decline over time . The number of deaths among the prey animal population exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . By around 2000 steps, the number of animals in the model is very close to zero, indicating a near-complete extinction of male animals .  Total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of approximately 25,000 units . In the early stages, up to around 500 steps, there is a steep decline in the total energy . This trend suggests a significant reduction in the energy available within the female animal population over the course of the simulation . The stabilization near zero suggests that the remaining animals are either very few or have minimal energy .
