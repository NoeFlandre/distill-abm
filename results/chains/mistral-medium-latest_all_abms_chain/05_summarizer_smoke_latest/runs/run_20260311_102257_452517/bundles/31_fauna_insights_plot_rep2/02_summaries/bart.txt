 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment .  The number of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent declining trend across all simulations . Initially, the number of deaths among the prey animal population shows a rapid increase initially, followed by a gradual leveling off . For decision makers, these trends highlight periods of stability and growth in the prey population .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . The total energy of all animals in the model exhibits a sharp decline over time . For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters, the prey population is not sustainable over the long term, leading to a rapid collapse in energy .  The trends observed in the percentage of green patches provide insights into the dynamics of grass regrowth and grazing pressure within the model . The initial stability, followed by rapid increases and decreases, suggests periods of balance and disruption in the ecosystem . The fluctuations in green patch percentages highlight the importance of monitoring and potentially intervening in grazing practices to maintain ecological balance .
