the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . cumulative number of postpartum females killed starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase initially . this rapid increase continues until about 1000 steps, where the cumulative number stabilizes around 120 postspartum women killed . trend lines plateau, indicating that after an initial period of high hunting activity, the system reaches a state where the number of posts killed slows down significantly and eventually stops . the total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model starts at 250 . this number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline . by around 1000 steps, the number of animal drops to about 20 . the decline becomes more gradual as time progresses . the percentage of green patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . initially, the percentage is around 32% . this value remains relatively stable for approximately the first 300 steps. following this period, there is a sharp increase, reaching a peak of around 34% at approximately 500 steps.
