The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent downward trend over time. Initially, the total energy of foragers starts around 0 and remains relatively stable for a short period. However, after approximately 500 steps, the total energy begins to decline steadily. By around 1000 steps, the total energy drops to about -10,000 units. This decline continues linearly, reaching approximately -20,000 units by 1500 steps and further decreasing to around -35,000 units by 2000 steps. The trend indicates a continuous and steady reduction in the total energy available to foragers throughout the simulation period. For decision makers, this trend suggests that the energy balance for foragers is not sustainable over the long term under the current model parameters. This could imply that the foraging strategy, energy gain from prey, or movement costs may need to be re-evaluated to ensure the survival and energy sustainability of the forager population. The steady decline in energy highlights the need for potential interventions or adjustments in the model to prevent energy depletion and potential extinction of the forager population.
