 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model allows users to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges .  Initial phase is characterized by a steep upward trend, indicating a high rate of hunting success or pressure on female prey . After this peak, the trend shows a slight decrease, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall window from 1856 to 2087, which shows a significant decreasing trend .  Understanding these dynamics can help in formulating strategies to manage and conserve megaherbivore populations effectively . For decision-makers, these trends imply that the prey population experiences periods of growth and decline, influenced by various factors within the model .
