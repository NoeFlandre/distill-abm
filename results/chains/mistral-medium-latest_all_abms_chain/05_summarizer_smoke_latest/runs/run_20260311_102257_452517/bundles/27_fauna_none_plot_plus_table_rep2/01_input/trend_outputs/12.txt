The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent downward trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12500 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has decreased significantly, indicating a sharp drop in the energy levels of female prey animals. Throughout the subsequent time steps, the energy continues to decline, albeit at a slower and more gradual rate. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this decreasing pattern, with significant negative trends observed in multiple windows, particularly in the early stages of the simulation. There are minor fluctuations and small peaks observed around the 376 to 410 time steps, but these do not represent significant deviations from the overall downward trajectory. The energy levels eventually approach zero, indicating the near-complete depletion of energy among female prey animals by the end of the simulation period at 2311 time steps. The inflection points and change points identified in the data further support the observation of a continuous decline, with notable changes in the rate of decrease at specific intervals. The oscillation indices suggest some periodic variations, but these are minor compared to the overall trend of decreasing energy.
