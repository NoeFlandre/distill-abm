The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid increase initially, followed by a gradual stabilization. Initially, the number of females killed rises sharply from 0 to approximately 120 within the first 500 steps. This rapid increase is followed by a more gradual rise, eventually stabilizing around 123 females killed. The trend shows significant inflection points at steps 245, 262, 285, 1204, 1336, 1487, 1631, 1725, 1800, 1940, and 1943, indicating periods where the rate of increase changes. The data also reveals several change points at steps 125, 375, 475, 625, 850, 1600, and 2310, suggesting shifts in the dynamics of the hunting process. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent increasing trend in the number of females killed across various windows, with a notable shift to a decreasing trend in the final window. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 steps, with oscillations observed towards the end of the simulation period. The mean number of females killed is approximately 104.56, with a median of 119.06, indicating that the data is skewed towards the higher end of the range. The standard deviation of 31.10 reflects some variability in the number of females killed across different simulations.
