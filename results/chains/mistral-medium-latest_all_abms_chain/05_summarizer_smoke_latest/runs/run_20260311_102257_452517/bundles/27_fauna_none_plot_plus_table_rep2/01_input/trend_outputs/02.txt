The total energy of foragers in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the total energy starts at 2000 units. This value gradually declines over time, with notable inflection points occurring at various intervals, indicating changes in the rate of energy decrease. The energy levels experience a series of peaks and valleys, with prominent peaks at ticks 2284, 1826, 1594, 394, 2, 370, 55, and 295, and valleys at ticks 2310, 2283, 1825, 1593, 393, 1, 367, and 54. The energy levels reach a minimum value of -38233 units at tick 2310. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend across multiple windows, with significant negative slopes ranging from -11.070 to -20.000. Change points are identified at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310, indicating shifts in the rate of energy decline. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 ticks, with oscillations occurring towards the end of the simulation. Overall, the total energy of foragers exhibits a steady decline, reflecting the cumulative effects of hunting success, movement costs, and other environmental interactions over time.
