 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The total number of times agents have chosen whole milk starts at zero and increases steadily throughout the simulation . The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . This initial value suggests that at the start of the simulation, the adoption of alternative milk types is minimal, likely due to strong initial habits and preferences for whole milk . The consumption then exhibits a rapid and significant decline over the initial time steps,  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at zero and increases steadily over the entire simulation period, reaching a final value of 32,000 . For decision makers, the steady increase in milk choices implies a gradual and consistent adoption process, which could be leveraged to introduce new milk types or promote healthier choices .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at a very low value of 5.29 units . The consumption then increases rapidly, reaching around 1265.47 units by the 11th time step . The trend continues with some fluctuations, showing peaks and valleys, but generally maintaining high consumption levels around 1200 units . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of understanding the dynamic nature of consumer behavior and the factors driving shifts in milk consumption .  The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 . The trend continues to increase steadily, albeit at a slower rate, reaching a final value of 0.7508 . The consistent upward trend, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results, indicates a continuous diversification of choice functions among agents over time .
