The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several key trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent over the observed time steps. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29. This is followed by a rapid and significant increase in consumption, reaching a peak of approximately 1265.47 around the 11th time step. This initial phase of rapid growth is statistically significant, as indicated by the increasing trends in the rolling Mann-Kendall test during the early windows. Following this peak, the consumption values exhibit minor fluctuations but generally stabilize around a high mean value of approximately 1220.21. These fluctuations are characterized by several smaller peaks and valleys, indicating periods of slight increases and decreases in consumption. Notable peaks occur at time steps 13, 21, and 19 with values around 1240.35, 1226.86, and 1221.57 respectively. Corresponding valleys are observed at time steps 12, 20, and 23 with values around 1233.62, 1214.46, and 1209.70 respectively. The middle phase of the simulation shows no significant trend, suggesting a period of stability in consumption patterns. Towards the end of the simulation, there is a slight but significant increasing trend, culminating in a maximum consumption value of 1456.60 at the final time step. This late-phase increase is also statistically significant, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test. The overall mean consumption across all time steps is approximately 1051.59, with a standard deviation of 405.15, highlighting the variability in consumption patterns throughout the simulation. The dominant period of oscillation is 15 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in the consumption data. The inflection points and oscillation indices suggest multiple points where the trend in consumption changes direction, contributing to the observed fluctuations. In summary, the simulation demonstrates an initial rapid increase in the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, followed by a period of stabilization with minor fluctuations, and ending with a slight increase towards the final time steps.
