The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of approximately 0, indicating a relatively equal distribution of healthy livestock among households. As the simulation progresses, the Gini coefficient gradually increases, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 by the end of the simulation at time step 100. This trend suggests that the disparity in the distribution of healthy livestock among households grows over time. The increasing trend is statistically significant across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, indicating a robust and consistent pattern of growing inequality. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points suggests that there are periods where the rate of increase in inequality varies, but the overall direction remains consistently upward. For decision makers, this trend implies that over time, certain households may be accumulating more healthy livestock while others are falling behind, which could have implications for the overall resilience and sustainability of the pastoral system. This growing inequality may necessitate interventions or policies aimed at promoting more equitable distribution of resources among households.
