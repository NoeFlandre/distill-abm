The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the grazing system model remains constant throughout the simulation period. The mean household initial risk attitude starts at approximately 0.0074 and remains at this value consistently across all time steps up to 100. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, showing no significant trends or changes in the data. The standard deviation and variance are both zero, indicating no variability in the risk attitude values. The peaks and valleys identified in the data all share the same value of 0.0074, further reinforcing the lack of fluctuation. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results across various windows indicate no significant trends, with all p-values being non-significant. This suggests that the initial risk attitude of households does not change over time, maintaining a steady state throughout the simulation.
