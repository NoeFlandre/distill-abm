agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model adds learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . the mean household risk attitude starts with a value of approximately 0.5 . by 20 time steps, the risk attitudes have diverged significantly . some households reaching a risk attitude close to 0.8 and others dropping to around 0.2 . the mean destock of households begins with a high value of approximately 7 livestock per household . this high initial value indicates a significant immediate reduction in livestock, likely due to initial resource constraints or environmental conditions that necessitate a rapid adjustment in herd sizes . the rapid decline suggests that households are quickly adapting to the initial conditions, possibly through improved resource management or adjustments in grazing strategies. the count of non-learning households starts with a wide range of values . some simulations start with as many as 40 households and others with fewer than 10 . this initial variability suggests diverse starting conditions or behaviors among the households in different simulation runs .
