 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The overall trend demonstrates a clear and sustained shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the 32-year period . Health and environmental perceptions, along with social influences, have solidified the consumer base for these milk types .  The overall mean consumption across the simulation is 1053.2062 units, with a median of 1220.2054 units . The standard deviation of 401.0659 and variance of 160853.8264 indicate considerable variability in the consumption data, suggesting fluctuations in consumer behavior over time . These peaks suggest periods where the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk is particularly high .  At the beginning of the simulation, there is no discrepancy between the agents' perceived health values and the health implications of their milk choices . The mean deviation across the entire simulation period is 0.4965, with a median of 0.5584 . The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend in the deviation across multiple windows .
