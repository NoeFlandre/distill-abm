 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  The rolling Mann-Kendall test results show significant increasing trends in early phases (time steps 0 to 32) and significant decreasing trends in the middle phases . The presence of multiple inflection points and oscillations, particularly between time steps 20 to 35, indicates periods of adjustment and fine-tuning in household decision-making processes .  The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at 0.0, indicating perfect equality initially . The trend of decreasing destocking rates continues, with minor oscillations, until it stabilizes around 0.31 livestock by the final time step . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial adaptive strategies in managing livestock under environmental pressures .  Trend analysis indicates a significant increasing trend in the first 10 steps, followed by a significant decreasing trend from step 11 to step 65 . After step 66, the trend stabilizes with no significant changes, indicating that the number of non-learning households reaches a relatively stable state .
