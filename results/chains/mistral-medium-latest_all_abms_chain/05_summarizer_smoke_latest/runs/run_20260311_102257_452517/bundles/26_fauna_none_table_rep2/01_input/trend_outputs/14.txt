The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a relatively stable trend over the simulation period, with minor fluctuations. Initially, the proportion of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33.0002%. This value slightly decreases over time, reaching a minimum of about 24.9108% around the 365th tick. Following this dip, the percentage of vegetated patches gradually recovers and stabilizes around a mean value of 32.6558%. The data shows several inflection points and change points, indicating periods where the trend experiences subtle shifts. Notably, there are multiple windows where the trend is either increasing or decreasing significantly, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test. For instance, from the start to around the 1855th tick, there are periods of increasing trends, followed by decreasing trends. The dominant oscillation period is approximately 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the vegetation coverage. Overall, the vegetation coverage remains relatively stable with minor oscillations, reflecting the dynamic interactions between grazing, regrowth, and environmental conditions within the model.
