agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model adds learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . Initially, there is a sharp increase in the mean household risk attitude for most simulations, reaching a peak around the 20th step . During this period, the values rise from approximately 0 to a range between 0.2 and 0.8 . following this initial rise, the trends diverge into different patterns . some simulations show a stabilization of risk attitude around the peak values, maintaining a steady state for the remainder of the simulation . the initial sharp decline highlights the importance of early intervention strategies to mitigate rapid losses in livestock . the subsequent stabilization and gradual recovery phases suggest that adaptive management and learning strategies can be effective in maintaining and eventually increasing livestock numbers . the rate of increase in destocking slows down, but the total continues to rise steadily . by around 40 steps, the mean destock total ranges between 70 and 100 . the highest values observed towards the end suggest that some households continue to make substantial adjustments even after the initial rapid changes .
