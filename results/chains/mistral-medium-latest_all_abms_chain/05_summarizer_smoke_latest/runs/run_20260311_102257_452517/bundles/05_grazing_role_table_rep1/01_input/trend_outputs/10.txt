The number of households following the E-RO strategy, which represents non-learning agents, exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the number of E-RO households starts at 20.0. There is a slight increase in the early stages, reaching a peak of 21.1 at time step 8. Following this peak, a general declining trend is observed. The decline is marked by several significant decreasing trends identified through the rolling Mann-Kendall test, particularly notable between time steps 11 to 65. During this period, the number of E-RO households decreases steadily, with occasional minor fluctuations. Key inflection points are noted at time steps 5, 12, 17, 18, 25, 30, 35, 41, 53, 60, 71, 76, 82, 90, and 95, indicating periods where the rate of change in the number of E-RO households shifts. The most significant change points occur at time steps 20 and 45, marking shifts in the overall trend. The latter part of the simulation, from time step 66 onwards, shows a stabilization with no significant trend, as the number of E-RO households fluctuates around a lower value, ultimately reaching approximately 14.7 by the end of the simulation. The dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps suggests a cyclical pattern in the fluctuations of E-RO households, although the overall trend remains a decline from the initial count.
