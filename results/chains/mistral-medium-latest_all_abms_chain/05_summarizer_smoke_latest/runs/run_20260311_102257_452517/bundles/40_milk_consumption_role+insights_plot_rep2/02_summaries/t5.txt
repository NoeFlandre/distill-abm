agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . boolean parameter indicates degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . higher conformity means agents are more likely to align their choices . average weekly consumption of whole milk starts at 2600 units at the initial time step . starting from 0 choices at step 0, the total number of choices rises steadily, reaching approximately 32,000 choices by step 30 . this linear increase indicates a consistent and uniform rate of decision-making among the agents throughout the simulation period . the linear increase suggests that the agents are continuously active and making choices at a constant rate . a steady increase in the habit factor suggests that over time, the influence of habit on the consumption of whole milk strengthens . this trend suggests that interventions aimed at reducing whole milk consumption may need to be more robust and sustained to overcome the growing habit factor . environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero and rapidly increases within the first five steps, reaching a value of approximately 13 . after this initial rapid increase, the rate of change slows down, and the deviation stabilizes around 13.5, maintaining this level for the remainder of the simulation steps up to 30 . this suggests that interventions aimed at influencing milk consumption choices might see rapid initial changes in consumer behavior .
