the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . the number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the simulation, starting and ending at a value of 20 . low variance among simulation runs suggests consistent hunting patterns and outcomes across different scenarios . for decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial conditions and early interventions in managing hunting pressures to prevent rapid decline in prey populations . over the first 500 time steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of female animals, dropping to around 40 individuals . this sharp decrease could be attributed to the combined effects of hunting pressure, internal and external mortality and possibly limited food resources as the grass regrows slowly . over the first 500 time steps, there is a rapid decline in total energy, dropping to around 5,000 units . this sharp decrease suggests a significant reduction in the prey population, potentially due to high predation pressure, environmental stress, or a combination of both factors .
