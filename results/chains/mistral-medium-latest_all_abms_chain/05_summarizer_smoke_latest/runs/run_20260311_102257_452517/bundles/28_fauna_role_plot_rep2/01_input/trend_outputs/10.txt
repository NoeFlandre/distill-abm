The simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend across multiple runs. Initially, the total number of prey animals starts at approximately 250, which aligns with the set value for the initial number of prey. In the early stages, up to around 500 steps, there is a gradual decline in the number of animals. This decline accelerates significantly between 500 and 1500 steps, where the number of animals drops sharply from around 200 to near zero. By approximately 2000 steps, the total number of animals in most simulations reaches zero, indicating extinction. This trend suggests a rapid decline in the prey population after an initial period of stability, leading to complete extinction within the simulated timeframe. The consistency across multiple simulations indicates a robust pattern of decline and extinction under the given parameters and conditions.
