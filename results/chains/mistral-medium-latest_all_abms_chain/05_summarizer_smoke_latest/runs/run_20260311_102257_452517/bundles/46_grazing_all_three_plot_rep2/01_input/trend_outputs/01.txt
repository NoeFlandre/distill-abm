The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation, indicating that the initial disposition to deviate from the recommended number of livestock does not change over time for social learning agents. The values of the mean household initial risk attitude are distributed across a range from approximately -0.1 to 0.17. This distribution suggests a variety of initial risk attitudes among households, with some households being more risk-averse and others more risk-tolerant. For decision makers, this implies that there is a diverse set of risk attitudes present within the household population from the beginning, which could influence the overall dynamics of livestock management and pasture utilization in the grazing system. Understanding this diversity can help in designing interventions that cater to different risk preferences and potentially improve the overall resilience and sustainability of the grazing system.
