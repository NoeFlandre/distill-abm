 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The total number of times agents chose whole milk starts at zero and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation . The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at a very low value of 5.29 . The consumption then increases rapidly, indicating a significant shift in consumer behavior . By the 11th time step, the consumption reaches a peak of 1265.47 . The simulation ends with the highest consumption value of 1456.5991 .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at zero and increases steadily over the entire simulation period . Means and median values of 16,000 indicate a symmetric distribution of choices around the midpoint of the simulation . The absence of detected peaks or valleys suggests a smooth transition without significant disruptions or reversals in the adoption trend .  The mean factor applied to the skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choice function due to habit starts at a value of 1.0000 . The trend analysis shows a consistent increase in the habit factor, with significant trends identified in multiple rolling windows, suggesting a steady growth in the influence of habit on milk choice .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 . It increases rapidly in the initial time steps, reaching a value of approximately 13.4756 by the 13th time step . After this rapid increase, the deviation stabilizes and fluctuates slightly around a mean value of 13.4302 .
