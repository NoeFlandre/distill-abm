the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . this trend suggests that initial hunting activities have a significant impact on the female prey population, but over time, the system reaches a balance . this could inform strategies related to sustainable hunting practices and conservation efforts, highlighting the importance of monitoring and managing hunting pressures . by around 500 steps, the number of female animals drops to about 20 . this steep decline continues more gradually until approximately 1500 steps . the total energy of male animals starts at a high value of approximately 12000 units . rapid initial decline in total energy highlights the importance of managing hunting pressure and resource availability to prevent swift depletion of prey energy reserves . around step 200, there is a sharp decline in the percentage of green patches, reaching a minimum of about 24% by step 500 .
