the agent-based model in question is an extension of the RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making . we have set it to 20. - **timesteps**: This parameter determines the number of random households . this parameter sets the standard deviation of rainfall . the model allows for the evaluation of various scenarios, such as the influence of demographic settings and herd size . from the 40th to the 60th time step, there is a noticeable increase in the mean green biomass, rising to approximately 55,000 units . this increase could be due to favorable growing conditions, such as adequate rainfall and reduced grazing pressure, allowing the vegetation to recover and grow . over the first 20 steps, there is a rapid increase in the mean destock total, reaching approximately 60 . this sharp rise indicates an initial phase where households are actively reducing their livestock numbers, possibly in response to immediate environmental or economic pressures .
