 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the simulation, starting and ending at a value of 20 . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains constant until around 700 time steps . After this period of no births, there is a sharp increase in the number of deaths . This rapid rise suggests a period of favorable conditions for reproduction, possibly due to abundant resources or low predation pressure . For decision makers, understanding these trends can help in assessing the resilience and sustainability of the prey population under different environmental and predation conditions .  Understanding the causes behind these energy dynamics could be crucial for developing strategies to maintain ecological balance and prevent potential extinction events . Understanding these dynamics can inform conservation strategies and interventions aimed at supporting vulnerable populations during critical periods . Understanding the rapid energy decline could inform strategies to mitigate predation pressure or enhance food availability during these crucial early stages .  Percentage of green patches gradually increases again, reaching around 33% by time step 1000 . This recovery phase indicates a period of regrowth and stabilization of vegetation . Overall trend indicates periods of stability interspersed with sudden changes in vegetation coverage . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of understanding the factors influencing vegetation dynamics .
