 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  Starting from an initial value of 0, the metric rises progressively, reaching a final value of approximately 2194.57 . The trend is uniformly increasing throughout the simulation, as evidenced by the multiple rolling Mann-Kendall windows, all showing a significant increasing trend . For decision-makers, this trend implies a growing preference or habit formation towards whole milk among the agents over time, which could be influenced by the model parameters such as habit threshold, social susceptibility, and network interactions . Understanding  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents in the simulation shows distinct trends over time . Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29 units, followed by a rapid and significant increase in the early time steps, reaching approximately 1265.47 units by the 11th time step .  The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . This trend implies that factors influencing milk consumption choices, such as perception, habits, and social influence, are leading to increasingly varied decision-making processes among agents . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing targeted interventions or policies to influence milk consumption behaviors effectively .
