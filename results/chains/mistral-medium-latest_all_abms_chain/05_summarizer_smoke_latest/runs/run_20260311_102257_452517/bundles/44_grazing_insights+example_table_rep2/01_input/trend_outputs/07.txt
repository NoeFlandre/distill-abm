The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at a value of approximately 0. This indicates an initially equal distribution of healthy livestock among households. Over the course of the simulation, the Gini coefficient exhibits a consistent increasing trend, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 at the final time step. This suggests a gradual increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock over time. The trend is consistently upward, with no detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady and continuous rise in inequality throughout the simulation period. Several inflection points are observed, suggesting subtle changes in the rate of increase in inequality at specific time steps. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across all windows, with significant results indicating a robust and steady rise in the Gini coefficient. Change points detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100 suggest notable shifts in the rate of change in inequality at these points. The presence of oscillations towards the latter part of the simulation indicates periodic fluctuations in the rate of increase in inequality. For decision-makers, this trend implies that over time, the distribution of healthy livestock becomes increasingly unequal among households. This insight can inform policies aimed at mitigating inequality and promoting more equitable distribution of resources in pastoral systems.
