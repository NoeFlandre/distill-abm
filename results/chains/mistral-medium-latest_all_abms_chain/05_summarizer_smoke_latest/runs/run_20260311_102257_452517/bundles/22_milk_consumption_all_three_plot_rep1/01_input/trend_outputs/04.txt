The total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero and increases gradually over the first 5 time steps. This initial slow increase could be attributed to the initial low perception of health and environmental benefits of alternative milk types, as well as strong initial habits for whole milk. As agents start to consider and adopt skimmed/semi-skimmed milk, the rate of adoption begins to accelerate.

Between time steps 5 and 15, there is a rapid increase in the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices. This period of rapid growth could be due to the cumulative effects of social influence, habit formation, and positive evaluations of past choices. As more agents adopt skimmed/semi-skimmed milk, the social norms and interactions further encourage others to make similar choices.

From time step 15 onwards, the growth rate starts to slow down and the curve begins to flatten, indicating a saturation point where the majority of agents have adopted skimmed/semi-skimmed milk. This saturation could imply that the market has reached a stable state where most consumers have integrated skimmed/semi-skimmed milk into their habits and social norms.

The total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 25,000 by the end of the simulation at time step 30. This final value suggests a high level of adoption among the agents, reflecting a significant shift in consumer behavior towards healthier and more environmentally friendly milk options.

For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial perceptions and social influence in driving the adoption of new products. The slow initial growth underscores the need for targeted marketing and education efforts to overcome established habits and perceptions. The rapid growth phase indicates the potential for social influence and network effects to accelerate adoption, suggesting that fostering social interactions and positive evaluations can be crucial. Finally, the saturation phase implies that once a critical mass of adopters is reached, the market can stabilize with widespread acceptance of the new product.
