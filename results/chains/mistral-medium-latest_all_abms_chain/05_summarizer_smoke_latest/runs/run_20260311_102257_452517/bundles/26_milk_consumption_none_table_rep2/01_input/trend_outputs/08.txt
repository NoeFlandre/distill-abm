The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the factor starts at a value of 1.0000 and gradually increases to reach a final value of 1.0502. Throughout the simulation, the mean factor exhibits a steady rise, with minor oscillations detected at various points. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across multiple windows, each with a consistent slope of 0.001. The inflection points identified at indices 7 and 12 suggest subtle changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trajectory remains upward. The dominant oscillation period of 15.00 indicates some periodic fluctuations, but these do not detract from the primary trend of gradual increase. The maximum value of 1.0502 is observed at the end of the simulation period, highlighting a slight but steady reinforcement of the habit factor influencing whole milk consumption over time.
