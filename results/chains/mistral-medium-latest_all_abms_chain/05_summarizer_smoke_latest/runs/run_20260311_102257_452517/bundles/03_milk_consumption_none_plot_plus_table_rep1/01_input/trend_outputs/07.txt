The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents in the simulation shows distinct trends over the time steps. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units. This is followed by a rapid increase in consumption, reaching around 1265.47 units by the 11th time step. This period of rapid growth indicates a significant shift in milk consumption preferences among the agents. After this peak, the consumption slightly fluctuates but generally stabilizes around the range of 1200 to 1265 units. Notable peaks occur at time steps 11, 13, and 21 with values of approximately 1265.47, 1240.35, and 1226.86 units respectively. The valleys, or lower points in consumption, are observed at time steps 12, 20, and 23 with values around 1233.62, 1214.46, and 1209.70 units respectively. The inflection points, indicating changes in the trend, are identified at time steps 4, 12, 13, 16, 19, 22, 27, and 30. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test shows an initial increasing trend in the early time steps, followed by periods of no significant trend, and a final increasing trend towards the end of the simulation. The dominant period of oscillation is 15 time steps, suggesting regular intervals of fluctuation in the consumption pattern. The simulation ends with a mean consumption value of approximately 1456.60 units, indicating a slight upward trend towards the end. Overall, the simulation demonstrates a rapid initial adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, followed by a stabilization period with minor fluctuations, and a slight increase towards the end of the observed time steps.
