 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  Total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero and increases gradually over the first 5 time steps . The variance among the simulation runs is minimal, implying a consistent pattern of whole milk adoption across different simulations . This trend suggests that the factors influencing the agents' choices, such as perception, habits, and social influence, are consistently applied .  The mean habit factor for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 for the first few steps, indicating a stable initial habit factor among the agents . Around the 5th step, there is a slight increase, suggesting a minor shift in habits . Following this, the habit factor continues to rise gradually until about the 10th step where it begins to increase more rapidly .
