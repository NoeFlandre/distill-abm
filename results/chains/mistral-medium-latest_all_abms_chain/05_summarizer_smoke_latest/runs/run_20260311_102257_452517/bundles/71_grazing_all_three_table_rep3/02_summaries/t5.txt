the agent-based model in question is an extension of the RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making . we have set it to 20. - **timesteps**: This parameter determines the number of random households . this parameter sets the standard deviation of rainfall . the model allows for the evaluation of various scenarios, such as the influence of demographic settings and herd size . from time step 50 onwards, there is a consistent increasing trend in the biomass . this steady increase suggests a long-term improvement in biomass availability . the average number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, starts at 90.0 . trend continues to rise steeply, reaching around 30 households by time step 21 . from time step 22 to 32, growth rate slows down, indicating a saturation point where the majority of households that are likely to adopt social learning have already done so .
