The simulation results indicate a significant and consistent decline in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents over the observed time period. Initially, the average consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units. This value experiences a sharp and continuous decrease, reaching around 253.10 units by the end of the simulation. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this decreasing pattern, with significant downward trends observed in multiple windows, particularly in the early and middle phases of the simulation. The inflection points identified at various time steps suggest periods where the rate of decline changes, indicating phases of accelerated or decelerated reduction in consumption. The presence of peaks and valleys, although minor, suggests some fluctuations in the rate of decline, but the overall trend remains consistently downward. The dominant oscillation period and multiple oscillation indices indicate some periodic variations, but these do not alter the primary trend of decreasing consumption. For decision makers, this trend suggests a strong shift away from whole milk consumption over time, which could be influenced by the model parameters such as changing health perceptions, social influences, and habit thresholds. Understanding these dynamics can help in strategizing interventions or marketing efforts to either support or counteract these trends based on broader public health or economic goals.
