agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . this parameter indicates the degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . a higher conformity means agents are more likely to align their choices . by the midpoint of the simulation, the consumption has decreased substantially . the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows consistently indicates a significant increasing trend with high tau values and significant p-values . the absence of detected peaks and valleys implies a smooth and continuous increase without significant fluctuations . for decision-makers, this trend highlights the growing preference for whole milk over time . the simulation of the agent-based model reveals several key trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time . this trend is evident from the start value of 5.29 units to a significant rise, with notable inflection points around time steps 4 and 12 . the habit factor reaches its maximum value of 1.5879 at the final time step, indicating a substantial influence of habit on the choice of alternative milk types by the end of the simulation period . for decision-makers, this trend implies that habits play an increasingly significant role in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time . the initial rapid increase in the mean deviation suggests a significant shift in the alignment between agents' environmental values and their milk choices early in the simulation . the subsequent stabilization indicates that, over time, the agents' choices and their environmental values reach a relatively steady state .
