The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent trend of decreasing prey population over time. Initially, the prey population starts at approximately 166.67 animals. This number steadily declines throughout the simulation, reaching zero by the final time step. The mean number of prey animals across all time steps is 25.01, with a median of 1.48, indicating that for the majority of the simulation, the prey population is quite low. The standard deviation of 44.90 reflects significant variability in the prey population, particularly in the earlier stages of the simulation. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend in prey population across multiple time windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values. The presence of several inflection points and change points suggests periods where the rate of decline changes, potentially due to varying interactions between predators and prey, or environmental factors. The oscillation indices and dominant period indicate some periodic fluctuations in the prey population, likely corresponding to seasonal or cyclical environmental changes. For decision makers, these trends highlight the critical impact of hunting pressure and environmental conditions on megaherbivore populations, emphasizing the need for sustainable management practices to prevent extinction.
