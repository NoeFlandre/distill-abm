 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  Simulations of the agent-based model show a clear and consistent trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices over time . Initially, at step 0, the total number of choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over the time steps . By the end of the simulation period, which corresponds to the year 2005, a substantial majority of agents have shifted their preference towards skimmed milk .  The mean choice function deviation starts at a value of 0.0 at the initial time step . The trend is consistently upward, indicating a continuous increase in the habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time . By step 25, the mean habit factor reaches approximately 1.5, and it continues to rise linearly .
