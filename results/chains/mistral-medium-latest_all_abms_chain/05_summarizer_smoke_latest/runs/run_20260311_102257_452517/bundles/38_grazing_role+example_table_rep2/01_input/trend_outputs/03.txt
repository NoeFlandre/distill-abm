Initially, there is a slight increase, reaching a peak of approximately 90,523 units by the third time step. Following this peak, the reserve biomass experiences a notable decline, reaching its lowest point of around 84,114 units at the 26th time step. This decline could be attributed to the initial grazing pressure exerted by the livestock as they begin to utilize the available biomass. After this low point, the reserve biomass begins to recover gradually. From the 33rd time step onwards, there is a consistent increasing trend in the reserve biomass. This trend continues steadily, with significant growth observed particularly between the 44th and 54th time steps, where the slope of increase is quite steep. The reserve biomass continues to rise, albeit at a slower rate, through to the end of the simulation, culminating in a value of approximately 121,073 units at the 100th time step. The overall trend indicates an initial adjustment period where the biomass is heavily utilized, followed by a recovery phase where the biomass levels stabilize and eventually increase, suggesting an adaptive balance between grazing pressure and biomass regrowth.
