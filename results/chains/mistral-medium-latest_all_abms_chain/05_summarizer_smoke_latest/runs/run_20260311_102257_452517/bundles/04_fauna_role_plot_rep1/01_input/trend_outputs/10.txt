The simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibit a consistent trend across multiple runs. Initially, the total number of prey animals starts at approximately 250, reflecting the set value for the initial number of prey. In the early stages, up to around 500 steps, there is a gradual decline in the number of animals. This decline accelerates significantly between 500 and 1500 steps, where the number of prey animals drops sharply from around 200 to near zero. By approximately 2000 steps, the number of prey animals in most simulations reaches zero, indicating a complete extinction event. This trend suggests a rapid collapse in the prey population under the given conditions of the model. The key parameters influencing this trend include the initial number of hunters, the energy dynamics of hunting and processing prey, and the life history traits of the animals. The consistent pattern across simulations indicates a robust dynamic response within the model's framework.
