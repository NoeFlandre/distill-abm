The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent downward trend over the simulated time steps. Initially, the total energy of foragers starts around 0 and remains relatively stable for a short period. Around the 500-step mark, the total energy begins to decline steadily. By approximately 1000 steps, the total energy of foragers has decreased to around -10,000 units. This downward trend continues linearly, reaching approximately -20,000 units by 1500 steps and -30,000 units by 2000 steps. The decline in total energy reflects the cumulative energy costs and gains experienced by the foragers over time, influenced by factors such as hunting success, movement costs, and the energy values associated with processing different types of prey. The consistent negative slope indicates that, on average, the energy expenditure of the foragers exceeds their energy gains from hunting and other activities throughout the simulation period.
