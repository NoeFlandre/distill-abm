the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . the low variance among simulation runs suggests consistent hunting patterns and prey availability across different simulations . for decision-makers, these trends imply that initial hunting pressure is high but stabilizes over time . this surge indicates a favorable period for reproduction . over the first 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of female animals, dropping to around 20 . this sharp decrease suggests a high rate of mortality or predation pressure early in the simulation . between 500 and 1000 steps, the decline continues but at a slower rate, with the population reducing to nearly zero . between 500 and 1,000 time steps, the decline continues but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 1,000 units of total energy . this phase indicates a period where the remaining prey animals are either less accessible to predators or have adapted to survive better under the given conditions . trends indicate that initial conditions are critical in determining survival trajectory of the prey population .
