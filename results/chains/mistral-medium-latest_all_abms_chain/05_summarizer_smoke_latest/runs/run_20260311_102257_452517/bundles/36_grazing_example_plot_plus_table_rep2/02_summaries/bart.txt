 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  The mean deviation across the entire simulation period is 0.2333, with a median of 0.2013, suggesting that while there are periods of higher deviation, the central tendency remains moderate . The mean reserve biomass in the grazing system starts at approximately 90,000 units . The overall trend shows an initial slight increase, followed by a decline, and then a steady recovery .  The Gini coefficient for the total healthy livestock starts at a value of approximately 0.0, indicating an initially equal distribution of healthy livestock among households . After the twentieth time step, the destocking rate stabilizes and remains relatively low, fluctuating slightly around an average of 0.31 . This indicates that households have likely reached a more sustainable balance in their livestock management, with fewer instances of forced destocking .  The number of non-learning households stabilizes towards the end of the simulation, with minor fluctuations around the mean value of 14.7 . This stabilization indicates that the system may have reached a new equilibrium .
