the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . by around 1000 steps, the cumulative number of postpartum females killed stabilizes, reaching a value close to 120 . this trend suggests an initial phase of high mortality, potentially due to a combination of natural causes and predation pressure, followed by a stabilization phase where the population reaches a new equilibrium. the energy level drops steeply, reaching around 2,000 units by the 500th tick . by the 1,000th tick, the total energy of male animals is close to zero . this trend indicates a significant reduction in the energy available in the male animal population over time .
