The average number of livestock destocked across all households in the simulation exhibits distinct trends over the 100 timesteps. Initially, the destocking rate starts at 0 and rapidly increases, reaching a peak of approximately 6.61 livestock at timestep 3. This sharp increase is followed by a series of fluctuations with decreasing magnitudes, indicating a period of high variability and adjustment in the early stages of the simulation. Subsequent peaks occur at timesteps 9, 12, 17, and 19, with values of 3.37, 2.74, 1.47, and 1.38 livestock respectively. These peaks are interspersed with valleys at timesteps 8, 11, 16, and 18, where the destocking rates drop to around 3.33, 2.47, 1.45, and 0.90 livestock respectively. After timestep 30, the destocking rate stabilizes and exhibits a general decreasing trend with minor fluctuations. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates significant decreasing trends in the early windows (0-10, 11-21, 22-32, and 44-54), a period of no significant trend (33-43 and 55-65), and a slight increasing trend in the later window (66-76). The simulation reaches a relatively stable state towards the end, with the destocking rate settling around 0.31 livestock at timestep 100. The dominant oscillation period of 49 timesteps suggests a cyclical pattern in the destocking behavior, reflecting periodic adjustments in household strategies in response to ecological and climatic conditions.
