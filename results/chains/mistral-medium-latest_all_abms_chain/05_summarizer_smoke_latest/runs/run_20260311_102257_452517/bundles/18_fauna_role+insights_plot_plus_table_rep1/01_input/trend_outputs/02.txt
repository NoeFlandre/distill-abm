The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy starts at 2000 units. This value rapidly declines, reaching approximately -2000 units by around 400 steps. The decline continues steadily, with notable values of around -23807 units at 1594 steps and -28403 units at 1826 steps. The energy levels further decrease to approximately -37713 units by 2284 steps and ultimately reach around -38229 units by the end of the simulation at 2310 steps. The statistical analysis confirms this downward trend, with all rolling Mann-Kendall windows indicating a significant decreasing trend in energy levels. The change points identified at 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 steps mark significant shifts in the rate of decline. The consistent decrease in total energy suggests that the foragers are experiencing a continuous net energy loss over time, which could be due to a combination of factors such as high movement costs, low hunting success, or high processing costs of prey. For decision makers, this trend indicates that the current parameters and environmental conditions may not be sustainable for the foragers in the long term, potentially leading to a scenario where foragers are unable to maintain their energy levels, which could have broader implications for the ecosystem and the survival of both foragers and prey.
