Following this decline, the percentage of green patches begins to recover, gradually increasing and stabilizing around 33% again by approximately time step 725. This recovery phase suggests a period of regrowth and stabilization of grass patches, potentially due to reduced grazing pressure or favorable environmental conditions for grass regrowth.

After time step 725, the percentage of green patches remains relatively stable with minor fluctuations around the 33% mark until the end of the simulation. The minor fluctuations indicate periodic variations in grass availability, possibly due to seasonal changes or dynamic interactions between grazing and regrowth.

The statistical analysis reveals several inflection points and change points, indicating periods of significant change in the trend of green patches. The rolling Mann-Kendall test shows both increasing and decreasing trends in different windows, highlighting the dynamic nature of grass patch availability over time.
