The mean destock total of households in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the 100 steps of the simulation. Initially, the mean destock total starts at 0 and rises sharply in the early steps, indicating a rapid increase in the number of livestock being destocked. This sharp increase begins to taper off around step 25, which is identified as the first change point. Beyond this point, the rate of increase slows down, but the trend remains positive throughout the simulation. The second change point occurs at step 60, after which the rate of increase becomes even more gradual. By the final step, the mean destock total reaches approximately 96.78 livestock. The statistical analysis reveals multiple inflection points and oscillations, suggesting periods where the rate of destocking experiences minor fluctuations. However, the overall trend remains consistently upward, as confirmed by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, which indicates a significant increasing trend across all windows. The model's parameters and the context of adaptive management strategies suggest that the observed trends are influenced by the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household decision-making processes under varying ecological and climatic conditions.
