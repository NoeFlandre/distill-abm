 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  Initially, the mean household risk attitude starts at a low value of approximately 0.0074 . During the early stages, from time step 0 to around time step 21, there is a significant increasing trend . This period is characterized by a rapid adjustment in household risk attitudes as agents adapt to initial conditions and begin to incorporate learning behaviors .  The average number of livestock destocked across households in the agent-based model shows a sharp initial increase, peaking at approximately 6.61 units by the third time step . The trend continues to decrease, with notable peaks and valleys observed at various points . The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at 0 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period .  The number of non-learning households then fluctuates, showing several peaks and valleys . Notable peaks occur at time steps 6, 4, 14, 25, 27, 34, and 37 . The oscillation analysis reveals a cyclical pattern in the data . The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant trends in the initial phases .
