The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits several notable trends and characteristics over the simulation period. Initially, the proportion of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33.0002% and ends very close to this value at 32.9979%, indicating a general stability in the overall vegetation coverage over time. The mean vegetation coverage across the simulation is about 32.6558%, with a median value of 32.9696%, suggesting that the data distribution is relatively symmetric and centered around the mean. The standard deviation of 0.9503 indicates low variability in vegetation coverage throughout the simulation. The minimum vegetation coverage observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, marking the lowest point in the availability of fodder for prey animals. The maximum value remains at the initial 33.0002%, indicating that the highest vegetation coverage is observed at the start of the simulation. The analysis of peaks and valleys reveals minor fluctuations, with peaks and valleys showing minimal prominence, suggesting very slight periodic variations in vegetation coverage. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates several windows of increasing trends in vegetation coverage initially, followed by periods of decreasing trends towards the latter part of the simulation. This suggests that while there are periods of slight increase and decrease, the overall trend remains relatively stable. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 ticks, indicating a cyclical pattern in vegetation coverage with this periodicity. The change points identified at ticks 150, 300, 475, 725, and 2310 suggest notable shifts in the dynamics of vegetation coverage at these specific time steps. Overall, the vegetation coverage in the MHPM simulation demonstrates a high degree of stability with minor periodic fluctuations and slight trends of increase and decrease at different intervals.
