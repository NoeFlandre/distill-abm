the agent-based model in question is an extension of the RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making . we have set it to 20. - **timesteps**: This parameter determines the number of random households . this parameter sets the standard deviation of rainfall . the model allows for the evaluation of various scenarios, such as the influence of demographic settings and herd size . over the first 20 time steps, there is a rapid decline in the mean livestock plus destock, dropping to around 45 units . this sharp decrease suggests an initial adjustment period where households are likely destocking livestock to align with their carrying capacity or in response to initial environmental conditions . step 40 to step 100, the mean destock total continues to increase but at a much slower and more linear rate, eventually reaching values between 100 and 140 . the variance between different simulation runs becomes more pronounced as the steps progress .
