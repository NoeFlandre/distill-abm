agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . agent-based model shows steady increase in total number of times agents chose whole milk from 1974 to 2005 . trend is uniformly increasing, with no detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady adoption behavior without significant fluctuations . the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates several windows of significant decreasing trends . the dominant pattern remains a steady decrease in consumption over time . by the time step corresponding to the early 1980s, the mean consumption reaches around 1265.47 units, marking the first significant peak . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across multiple windows . inflection points are noted at time steps 7, 12, and 28, suggesting notable changes in the rate of increase at these points .
