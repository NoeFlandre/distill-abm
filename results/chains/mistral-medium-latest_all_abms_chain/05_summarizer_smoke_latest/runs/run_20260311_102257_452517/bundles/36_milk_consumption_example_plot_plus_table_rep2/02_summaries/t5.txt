agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the total number of whole milk choices starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . this initial increase can be attributed to a late-stage adoption by the remaining agents or reinforcement of social norms favoring skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2654.81 units . this value declines rapidly within the first few time steps, indicating a sharp reduction in whole milk consumption . the trend continues to decrease but at a slower rate, reaching around 773.96 units by the 19th time step . the deviation continues to rise but at a slower rate, reaching around 0.6 by step 12 . this period of slower increase suggests a phase where agents are adjusting their choices based on initial perceptions and social influences .
