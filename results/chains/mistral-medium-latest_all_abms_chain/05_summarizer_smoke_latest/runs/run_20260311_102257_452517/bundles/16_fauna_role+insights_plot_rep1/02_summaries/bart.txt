 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . Ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values and processing costs for males and females . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback .  Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions that balance the ecological impact with the needs of human hunters . Understanding the impact of hunting on postpartum females is most intense during the initial phases of the simulation, which could have significant implications for the population dynamics and survival of the prey species .  Simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend across all runs . In the early stages, up to around 500 steps, there is a gradual decline in the number of animals . This decline accelerates significantly between 500 and 1500 steps, where the number drops sharply from approximately 200 to near zero . By around 2000 steps, most simulations reach zero, indicating a complete extinction event .  The percentage of green patches in the landscape starts at approximately 32% and remains stable for the first 200 steps . By the 500th step, the total energy has dropped to around 5,000 units, marking an 80% reduction from the initial value . This trend suggests that the initial conditions of the simulation lead to a rapid depletion of energy reserves among the animal population .
