The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands exhibits a rapid increase in the initial phase, followed by a gradual plateau. Initially, the number of males killed rises steeply from 0 to around 100 within the first 500 steps. This rapid increase continues, reaching approximately 120 males killed by around 1000 steps. Beyond this point, the rate of increase slows down significantly, and the cumulative number of males killed stabilizes, fluctuating slightly around the 120 mark for the remainder of the simulation. This trend suggests that the initial hunting pressure is high, leading to a quick reduction in the male prey population, followed by a period where the hunting rate balances out with the remaining male population, leading to a steady state. For decision makers, this implies that initial hunting efforts are highly effective in reducing the male prey population, but sustained hunting pressure may not significantly increase the cumulative number of males killed beyond a certain threshold.
