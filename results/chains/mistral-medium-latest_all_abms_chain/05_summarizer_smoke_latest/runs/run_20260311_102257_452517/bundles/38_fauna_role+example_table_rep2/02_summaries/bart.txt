 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . Ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values and processing costs for males and females .  The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the initial time steps . The cumulative count continues to rise gradually, reaching a peak value of approximately 123.67 around 1826 ticks . After this peak, the cumulative count exhibits minor fluctuations but generally stabilizes around this value .  The number of female prey animals in the simulation begins at 125 and exhibits a rapid decline in the initial phase . The population continues to decrease gradually, with occasional small rises and falls, until it eventually reaches zero by the end of the simulation at 2312 time steps . This trend reflects the cumulative impact of sustained hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and possibly other factors modeled in the model . After the initial sharp decline and subsequent oscillations, the energy levels continue to decrease more gradually .
