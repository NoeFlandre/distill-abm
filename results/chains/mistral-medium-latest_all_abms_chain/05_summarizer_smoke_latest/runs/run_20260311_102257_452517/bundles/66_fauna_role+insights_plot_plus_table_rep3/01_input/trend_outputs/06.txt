The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct phases over the simulation period. Initially, there are no births, as indicated by the starting value of 0.0. The first notable increase in births begins around time step 725, marking the first change point. This period shows a gradual rise in the number of births, reaching a peak of approximately 2.875 at time step 1073. The trend continues to increase significantly, with another peak of around 3.45 at time step 1214. The number of births then fluctuates, showing a series of increases and decreases. A significant peak is observed at time step 1673, where the number of births reaches its maximum value of 5.6. Following this peak, there is a noticeable decline in the number of births starting around time step 1850, which is identified as another change point. The decline continues, with minor fluctuations, until the end of the simulation period. The statistical analysis reveals several inflection points and change points, indicating shifts in the trend of births over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms significant increasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation, followed by significant decreasing trends in the later phases. The oscillation analysis suggests periodic fluctuations in the number of births, with a dominant period of 50 time steps. For decision makers, these trends highlight the dynamic nature of prey population births under varying environmental and hunting pressures. The initial increase in births suggests a period of population growth, potentially due to favorable conditions or lower predation pressure. The subsequent decline indicates a possible shift in environmental conditions, increased predation, or other factors leading to reduced birth rates. Understanding these trends can inform strategies for conservation and management of megaherbivore populations, as well as insights into the impacts of human hunting behaviors and environmental changes.
