the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges . ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values . animals can reproduce, and users can control sex ratios and reproduction probability . a dominant period of approximately 63 ticks is observed towards the very end of the simulation period . the cumulative count stabilizes, but does not deviate from the stabilized mean value . early inflection points around time steps 245, 262, and 285 indicate periods where the rate of increase begins to change . births starts at zero and remains relatively low . by the 1673rd tick, the cumulative number of births reaches its peak value of 5.6 . following this peak, there is a noticeable decline in the birth rate starting around the 1850th tick, which is identified as another change point . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend across multiple windows, with occasional brief periods of increase . the dominant trend is a steady decline to extinction, highlighting the pressure exerted by the model's parameters on the prey population . key inflection points occur at time steps such as 309, 460, and 513 . despite these oscillations, the overall trend remains a significant decrease in energy, culminating in the complete depletion of energy in prey animals by the final time step .
