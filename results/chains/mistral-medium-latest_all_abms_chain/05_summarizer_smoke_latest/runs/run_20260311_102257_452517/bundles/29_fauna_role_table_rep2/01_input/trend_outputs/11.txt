The combined total energy available in the population of male prey animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear overall decreasing trend over time. Initially, the energy level starts at 12500 units. This high value is followed by a rapid decline in the early stages of the simulation, with significant drops observed within the first few hundred ticks. By around tick 377, the energy level decreases to approximately 4002.51 units, marking a substantial reduction from the initial value. This period of rapid decline continues, with several peaks and valleys indicating fluctuations but an overall downward trajectory. Notable peaks during this phase occur at ticks 377, 380, and 388, with values around 4000 units, suggesting temporary stabilizations or minor recoveries in energy levels. However, these peaks are followed by continued declines. The inflection points identified around ticks 316, 453, and 516 further highlight the dynamic changes and shifts in the rate of energy decline. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms a consistent decreasing trend across multiple windows, with significant negative slopes indicating steady reductions in energy levels. After the initial rapid decline, the rate of decrease slows down, as evidenced by the less steep slopes in later windows and the identification of change points around ticks 625 and 875. Despite some periods of minor increases or stabilizations, as indicated by the increasing trends in windows 7 and 9, the overall trajectory remains downward. The energy levels eventually approach zero, with the minimum value of zero units reached by tick 2284, indicating the complete depletion of energy in the male prey population. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations, but these do not alter the overall decreasing trend. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 ticks, reflecting regular intervals of minor recoveries and declines within the broader downward trend.
