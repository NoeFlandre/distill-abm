the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . the prey population has reached a dynamic equilibrium where the number of postpartum females being killed is balanced by the remaining population's reproductive and survival rates . this initial period of no births could be attributed to the initial conditions of the simulation . between 1000 and 1500 time steps, number of births experiences a more significant rise, reaching up to 8 births by 1000 time steps . between 500 and 1000 steps, the decline continues but at a slower rate, with the population reducing to around 10 animals . this phase indicates a period where the remaining animals are either more successful at evading predators or the predator population has stabilized .
