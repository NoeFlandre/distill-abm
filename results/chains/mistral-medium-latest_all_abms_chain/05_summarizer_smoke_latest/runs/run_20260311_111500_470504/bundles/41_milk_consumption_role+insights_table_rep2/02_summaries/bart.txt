 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  Simulation of the agent-based model reveals key trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent over the observed period . For decision makers, this trend implies a substantial shift away from whole milk consumption . Understanding these dynamics can help in strategizing interventions or marketing efforts aimed at influencing milk consumption patterns .  Simulation results indicate a consistent and steady increase in the total number of milk choices made by agents combined over the observed time steps . Initially, the number of times agents chose alternative milk types starts at zero, but this value steadily increases, reaching a maximum of approximately 25,643.83 by the end of the simulation . These trends imply that interventions or policies aimed at promoting healthier milk choices could be effective over time .  Simulation of the agent-based model reveals key trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time . These trends suggest a substantial shift away from whole milk consumption over time, which could be influenced by the model parameters such as changing health perceptions, social influences, and habit thresholds . Understanding these dynamics can help in strategizing interventions or marketing efforts aimed at influencing milk consumption patterns .  The researchers found that habits play an increasingly significant role in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time . This insight can inform strategies aimed at reinforcing positive habits related to healthier milk choices . For decision-makers, this trend implies that over time, agents in the model are diverging in their choice function scores .
