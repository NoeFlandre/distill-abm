The trend of decreasing consumption continues throughout the simulation, albeit at varying rates. The most significant drops in consumption are observed in the early and middle phases of the simulation, with the rate of decline slowing down as the simulation progresses. By the end of the simulation, the mean quantity of whole milk consumed falls to approximately 253.10 units. The data also exhibits some minor oscillations, particularly around the middle time steps, but these do not significantly alter the overall downward trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms significant decreasing trends in multiple windows, reinforcing the overall decline in whole milk consumption. For decision makers, these trends suggest a substantial shift away from whole milk consumption over time, which could be influenced by the model parameters such as changing health perceptions, social influences, and habit thresholds.
