agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . boolean parameter indicates degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . higher conformity means agents are more likely to align their choices . average weekly consumption of whole milk starts at approximately 2600 units . by the fifth step, the mean quantity has decreased to about 2000 units . from the tenth to the fifteenth steps, the decline becomes more gradual . after the twentieth step, a slight downward trend resumes, eventually reaching around 500 units by the twenty-fiveth step . the deviation is approximately 0.45, showing a continued but slower increase . from step 10 to step 20, the deviation begins to rise again . by step 25, the deviation continues to increase gradually, reaching a value slightly above 0.7 . this final phase indicates a renewed divergence between perceived and implied health values .
