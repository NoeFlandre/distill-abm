 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at approximately 2600 units at the initial time step . There is a sharp decline in consumption within the first 5 steps, dropping to around 1000 units . After step 25, there is a slight decline followed by a small increase around step 30, after which the consumption stabilizes again, ending just below 700 units .  The mean whole milk quantity consumed by agents starts at a high value of approximately 2600 units . During the first five steps, there is a rapid increase in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed . The mean habit factor for whole milk exhibits a consistent upward trend, indicating an increasing influence of habit on the choice of whole milk .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early steps of the simulation . By around 5 steps, the deviation reaches approximately 12.5 by step 5 . By step 10, it stabilizes around a value of approximately 13.5 for the remainder of the steps up to 30 .
