 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases linearly over the 30 time steps, reaching a value of approximately 32,000 . For decision makers, this trend highlights the importance of understanding the steady and predictable nature of whole milk adoption, which can inform strategies related to milk consumption patterns .  The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at a value of 1.00 and rapidly increases within the first few steps, reaching approximately 1.02 by the 10th step . By the 15th step, the habit factor is around 1.2, reflecting a more pronounced habit formation among consumers . The trend suggests that over time, consumers are increasingly favoring skimmed and semi-skimmed milk due to habit .
