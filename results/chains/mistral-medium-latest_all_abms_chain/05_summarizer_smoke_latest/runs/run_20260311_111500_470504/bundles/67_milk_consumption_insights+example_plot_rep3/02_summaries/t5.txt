agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero and increases steadily over the 30 steps . this consistent upward trend indicates a growing cumulative adoption of whole milk choices among the agents over time . the rate of increase is relatively linear, suggesting a steady and predictable pattern in the adoption behavior . between steps 10 and 20, the mean quantity stabilizes around 1,200 units . this stabilization suggests a continued, albeit slower, adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . trend suggests ongoing social influence, habit formation, and positive reinforcement of health benefits continue to drive the consumption upward . over the first 10 steps, there is a rapid increase in the mean value health deviation, reaching approximately 0.4 . this sharp rise suggests that as agents begin to interact and influence each other, their health perceptions and actual choices start to diverge significantly .
