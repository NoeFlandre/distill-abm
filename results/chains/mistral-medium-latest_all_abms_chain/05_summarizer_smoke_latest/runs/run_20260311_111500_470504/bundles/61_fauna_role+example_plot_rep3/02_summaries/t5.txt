the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . the number of active hunter-gatherer bands does not change over time, suggesting a stable presence of human hunting pressure within the modeled environment . by 1000 time steps, the cumulative number of postpartum females killed stabilizes, fluctuating slightly around 120 . this stabilization suggests that a balance has been reached where the remaining posts are less accessible to hunters or have adapted behaviors to reduce their vulnerability . the total number of female animals starts at approximately 125 and declines rapidly over the first 1000 time steps . this initial decline could be attributed to the combined effects of human hunting pressure, environmental factors, and internal mortality rates . the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33%, reflecting the initial proportion of the landscape covered in grass . this value remains stable for the first 200 time steps, indicating a period of equilibrium where the consumption of grass by ungulates is balanced by its regrowth .
