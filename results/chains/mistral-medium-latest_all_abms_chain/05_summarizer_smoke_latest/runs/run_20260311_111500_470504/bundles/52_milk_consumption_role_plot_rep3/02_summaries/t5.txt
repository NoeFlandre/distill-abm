agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . boolean parameter indicates degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . higher conformity means agents are more likely to align their choices . average weekly consumption of whole milk starts at approximately 2600 units . at step 30, the total number of choices made by all agents combined reaches approximately 30,000 . this indicates a consistent and linear accumulation of milk choices over time, reflecting the ongoing decision-making processes of the agents throughout the simulation period . gradual increase suggests that agents are still diverging in their choice function scores, but the rate of divergence is slowing down . from step 20 to step 30, the deviation continues to increase very slowly, reaching a value slightly above 0.7 . this indicates that agents initially explore different choices widely but eventually settle into more consistent behavior patterns .
