The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a relatively stable trend over the simulation period, with minor fluctuations. Initially, the percentage of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33% and remains close to this value throughout the simulation, ending at around 32.998%. The mean percentage of vegetated patches is 32.6558%, with a median of 32.9696%, indicating a slight skew towards lower values. The standard deviation of 0.9503 suggests minimal variability around the mean. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, while the maximum value is 33.0002% at the start of the simulation. The data shows several inflection points and change points, indicating periods of slight shifts in the trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals both increasing and decreasing trends in different windows, with significant changes detected. The dominant oscillation period is 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the vegetation coverage. For decision makers, the stability in vegetation coverage implies a consistent availability of fodder for prey animals, which could influence the dynamics of the ecosystem and the interactions between hunters and prey. The minor fluctuations and cyclical patterns may indicate seasonal or periodic changes in vegetation regrowth and grazing pressure.
