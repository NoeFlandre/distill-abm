 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a general increasing trend over time, with specific dynamics and periods of stability . For decision makers, these trends highlight the dynamic interaction between hunting pressure and prey population, emphasizing the need to consider temporal variations and potential stabilization points in hunting activity when evaluating extinction risks .
