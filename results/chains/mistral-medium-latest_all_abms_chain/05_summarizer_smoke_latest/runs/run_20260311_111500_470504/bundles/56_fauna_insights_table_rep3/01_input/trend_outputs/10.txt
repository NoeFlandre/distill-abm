The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at approximately 166.67 animals. This number steadily decreases throughout the simulation, eventually reaching zero by the final tick. The trend is characterized by a series of small peaks and valleys, indicating minor fluctuations in the population, but the overall trajectory remains downward. Key inflection points and change points highlight periods where the rate of decline varies, but the general trend of decreasing prey numbers persists. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this decreasing trend across multiple windows, with significant negative slopes indicating a continuous decline in the prey population. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations, but these do not alter the overall downward trend. For decision makers, this simulation suggests that under the given parameters, the prey population is unable to sustain itself and eventually faces extinction. This insight could be crucial for understanding the dynamics of megaherbivore extinction and the impact of hunting pressure and environmental conditions.
