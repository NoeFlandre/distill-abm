agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . this parameter indicates the degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . higher conformity means agents are more likely to align their choices . the rolling Mann-Kendall test results shows significant positive slopes across all windows . this suggests that agents are increasingly inclined to choose whole milk over time, possibly due to habit formation or social influence . for decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial conditions and the role of habit and social influence in shaping long-term consumption patterns . by the early 1980s, the consumption reaches around 1265.47 units . this trend suggests that agents in the model are increasingly adopting skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, possibly due to growing health awareness or social influence . the deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a general increasing trend throughout the simulation . the deviation continues to rise steadily, with several inflection points indicating periods of accelerated change .
