 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  Model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment .  The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands begins at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early time steps . By around 245 ticks, the rate of increase starts to slow down, marking the first inflection point . The trend continues to rise but at a progressively slower rate, with additional inflection points observed around ticks 262, 285, and subsequent intervals .  The overall trend is a consistent decrease in the number of male prey animals, with the most significant changes occurring in the early stages of the simulation . Notable peaks occur around the 730th, 709th, and 803rd ticks, although these peaks are relatively small compared to the initial population size . The population continues to decline steadily, with minor oscillations, until it reaches zero by the 2284th tick .  The combined total energy available in all prey animals within the simulation begins at approximately 16,667 units . This initial value represents the starting energy level of the prey population . The energy level exhibits a rapid decline in the early stages of the simulation, dropping significantly within the first few hundred time steps . The dominant oscillation period of 63 time steps indicates a cyclical pattern in the energy dynamics .
