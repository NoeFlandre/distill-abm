The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits several notable trends over the simulation period. Initially, the proportion of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33.0002% and remains relatively stable with minor fluctuations. The mean percentage of vegetated patches across the simulation is around 32.6558%, with a median value of 32.9696%, indicating a slight skew towards lower values. The standard deviation of 0.9503 suggests minor variability around the mean. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, indicating a significant drop in vegetation cover at this point. The maximum value remains close to the initial value at 33.0002%. The data shows several peaks and valleys, with peaks reaching up to 32.9957% and valleys slightly lower at 32.9942%, indicating minor oscillations in vegetation cover. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals significant trends in different windows. Initially, there is a consistent increasing trend in vegetation cover, as seen in the first seven windows, with tau values indicating strong positive trends and significant p-values. However, this trend shifts to a decreasing pattern in the eighth and ninth windows, with negative tau values and significant p-values, suggesting a decline in vegetation cover towards the latter part of the simulation. The dominant oscillation period is 63 ticks, indicating a cyclical pattern in vegetation cover. The change points identified at ticks 150, 300, 475, 725, and 2310 mark significant shifts in the trend of vegetation cover. Overall, the vegetation cover in the MHPM shows a general stability with minor fluctuations and a slight decline towards the end of the simulation period.
