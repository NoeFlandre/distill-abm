The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 female prey animals. This number steadily decreases throughout the simulation, with the population eventually reaching zero by the final time step. The trend is characterized by a rapid initial decline, followed by a more gradual decrease as the population approaches extinction. Key inflection points and change points indicate shifts in the rate of decline, but the overall trend remains consistently downward. There are minor fluctuations and small peaks in the population at certain time steps, such as around ticks 730, 710, and 803, but these are relatively insignificant compared to the overall declining trend. The statistical analysis confirms this downward trend, with significant decreasing trends identified in multiple rolling Mann-Kendall windows. The dominant period of oscillation is relatively short, suggesting minor periodic variations but no substantial recovery in the population. The simulation ultimately results in the complete extinction of female prey animals by the end of the observed time steps.
