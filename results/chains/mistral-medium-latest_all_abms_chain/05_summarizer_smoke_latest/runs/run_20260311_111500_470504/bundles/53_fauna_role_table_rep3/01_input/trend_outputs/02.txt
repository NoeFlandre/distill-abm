The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. From the onset, there is a rapid decline in energy levels, as indicated by the significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall windows, which consistently show a decreasing trend with high significance (p=0.000). The energy levels experience several inflection points, suggesting changes in the rate of decline, notably around time steps 358, 1238, and 1597 among others. Key peaks and valleys in the energy levels are observed, with notable peaks at time steps such as 2284, 1826, and 1594, and valleys at time steps like 2310, 2283, and 1825. The energy levels reach their minimum value of -38233 units at the final time step (2310), indicating a substantial overall decline. The presence of multiple change points further underscores the dynamic shifts in the rate of energy depletion. Additionally, there are oscillations detected towards the end of the simulation period, with a dominant period of 63 time steps, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the energy levels. Overall, the energy status of the forager bands deteriorates steadily, reflecting the cumulative impact of hunting activities and movement costs over time.
