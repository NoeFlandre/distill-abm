 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  The mean reserve biomass in the grazing system initially starts at 90,000 units . In the early stages, there is a rapid increase in the deviation, reaching a peak value of around 0.3814 at time step 33 . The decline is marked by significant decreasing trends in the subsequent rolling Mann-Kendall windows, with negative tau values and significant p-values .  Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions that enhance the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . For decision makers, these trends imply that initial conditions or strategies may lead to a rapid decline in livestock numbers, but adaptive management practices could facilitate recovery and stabilization over time .  The number of non-learning households in the simulation starts at 20 and exhibits an initial increase, peaking at approximately 21.1 households . Following this peak, there is a notable decreasing trend, with the count dropping to around 14.7 households by the 92nd time step . For decision makers, these trends imply that over time, households are increasingly destocking livestock .
