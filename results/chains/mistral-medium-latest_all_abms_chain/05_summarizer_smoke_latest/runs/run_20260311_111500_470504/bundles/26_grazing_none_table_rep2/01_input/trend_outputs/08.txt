The number of households engaging in social learning, as indicated by the variable household-SL being true, exhibits a clear increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, at the start of the simulation, no households are engaged in social learning. This value gradually increases, with significant growth observed in the early stages of the simulation. By the midpoint of the simulation, around time step 50, the number of households participating in social learning stabilizes, fluctuating around a mean value of approximately 27. The maximum number of households engaged in social learning, around 32, is reached by time step 63 and remains relatively stable thereafter. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across various windows, with significant statistical evidence supporting this observation. The inflection points and oscillation indices suggest periods of slight fluctuations, but the overall trajectory remains upward. This indicates a steady adoption of social learning behaviors among households over time, reflecting the diffusion of learning strategies within the population.
