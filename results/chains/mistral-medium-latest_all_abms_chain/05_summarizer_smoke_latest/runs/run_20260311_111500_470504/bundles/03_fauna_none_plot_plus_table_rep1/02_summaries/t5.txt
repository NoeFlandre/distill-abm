the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time . Initially, the number of postpartum females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly . this rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the rate of increase begins to slow down . Initially, the decline is steep, with the number of male animals dropping rapidly . this steep decline continues until approximately 1000 steps, where the rate of decline starts to slow down . by around 500 steps, this number rapidly declines within the first hundred ticks, showing a steep drop . inflection points occur around time steps 299, 472, 506, and several others up to 2194, suggesting periods where the rate of energy decline changes . however, the overall trend remains a steady decline . by the end of the simulation at 2,311 time steps, the total energy of female animals reaches zero .
