The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent trend across the simulations. Initially, the total energy of all animals starts at a high value, approximately 25,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period, indicating a stable and abundant energy state among the prey population at the beginning of the simulation. 

As the simulation progresses, there is a sharp decline in the total energy of all animals. This decline begins almost immediately after the initial stable period and continues steeply until around 500 steps. During this phase, the total energy drops from approximately 25,000 units to around 5,000 units. This significant reduction suggests a rapid depletion of energy resources among the prey, potentially due to high hunting pressure, environmental factors, or a combination of both. 

Following this steep decline, the rate of energy loss slows down, and the total energy of all animals continues to decrease more gradually. By around 1,000 steps, the total energy stabilizes at a much lower level, close to zero. This stabilization indicates that the prey population has reached a state where the remaining animals have minimal energy, suggesting a critical phase where most animals are either dead or on the verge of starvation. 

Throughout the remainder of the simulation, the total energy of all animals remains very low, with minimal fluctuations. This prolonged period of low energy levels indicates that the prey population has been significantly reduced and is struggling to recover, maintaining a state of near-extinction or complete extinction.
