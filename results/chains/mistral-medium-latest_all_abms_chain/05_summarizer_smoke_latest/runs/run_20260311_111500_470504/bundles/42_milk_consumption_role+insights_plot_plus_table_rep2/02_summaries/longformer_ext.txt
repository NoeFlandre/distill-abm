An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents, with the goal of assessing their effects on the system as a whole. In this case, the ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The simulation results indicate a clear and consistent downward trend in the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent over the observed time steps. Initially, the mean whole milk consumption starts at approximately 2654.81 units. This high initial value suggests a strong preference or habit for whole milk consumption among the agents at the beginning of the simulation.                                                                                                           with the mean quantity of whole milk consumed stabilizing around 253.10 units, indicating a significant reduction from the initial consumption levels. This final stabilization suggests that the agents have largely adopted new milk consumption habits, influenced by their perceptions, social interactions, and the parameters governing their decision-making processes.                                                                                                                                                                                                     .
