The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk shows a clear increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, the habit factor starts at a value of 1.0000. For the first few steps, there is little to no change, indicating a stable period where habits related to milk choice remain relatively constant. Around step 5, the habit factor begins to increase gradually. This marks the start of a significant upward trend that continues consistently throughout the remaining steps. By step 32, the habit factor reaches its peak value of approximately 1.5879. The statistical analysis indicates that this increasing trend is significant, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results across various windows, all showing significant increasing trends with p-values of 0.027. The inflection points at steps 3 and 12 suggest notable changes in the rate of increase, with the trend becoming more pronounced after these points. The oscillation indices indicate some variability in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. For decision makers, this trend implies that over time, the habit factor for choosing skimmed and semi-skimmed milk becomes stronger. This could suggest that interventions or policies aimed at promoting healthier milk choices may become more effective as time progresses, as the habit of choosing these milk types becomes more ingrained in the population.
