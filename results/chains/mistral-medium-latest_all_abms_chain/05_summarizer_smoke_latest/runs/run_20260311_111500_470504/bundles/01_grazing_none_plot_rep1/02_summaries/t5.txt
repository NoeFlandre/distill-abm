agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model adds learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . by around step 20, the values begin to stabilize into more defined patterns . a significant portion of the households exhibit an increase in risk attitude . another group shows a decrease, with values ranging from 0.2 to -0.2 . the metric representing the Gini coefficient of total healthy livestock starts at a value of 0 for all simulations, indicating an initial state of perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households . by around 20 steps, the gini coefficient values range approximately between 0.01 and 0.06, showing a more pronounced disparity in livestock distribution among households. Throughout the simulation, the trend shows a consistent and steady increase, reflecting a continuous rise in inequality over time.
