The total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12500 units. This high energy level is maintained briefly before beginning a sharp decline. By around 500 steps, the energy level has dropped significantly to approximately 4000 units. This rapid decrease continues, and by around 1000 steps, the energy level falls below 2000 units. The decline becomes more gradual after this point, but the overall trend remains consistently downward. By around 2000 steps, the energy level approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy in the male animal population. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend, with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows, reinforcing the consistent decline in energy levels. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points suggests periods where the rate of decline varies, but the overall trajectory remains downward. The implications for decision-makers are significant, as the consistent decline in energy levels of male animals suggests a potential risk of extinction or severe population decline under the current model parameters and conditions.
