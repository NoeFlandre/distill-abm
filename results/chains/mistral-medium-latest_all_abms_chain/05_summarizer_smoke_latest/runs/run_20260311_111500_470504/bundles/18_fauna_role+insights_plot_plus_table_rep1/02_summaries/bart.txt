 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback .
