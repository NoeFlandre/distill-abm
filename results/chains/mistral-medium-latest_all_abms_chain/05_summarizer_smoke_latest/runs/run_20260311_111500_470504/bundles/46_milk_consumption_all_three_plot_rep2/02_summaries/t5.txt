agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . boolean parameter indicates degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . there is a rapid decline in consumption within the first 10 steps, dropping to around 1,000 units . by the thirtieth step, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 25,000 . this high initial value indicates a strong preference or habit for whole milk consumption . over the first 10 steps, there is a rapid decline in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed, dropping to around 1,000 units . the mean habit factor for whole milk starts at a value of 1.00 and experiences a rapid increase within the first few time steps, reaching approximately 1.02 . this initial surge indicates a quick establishment of habit influence on the consumption of whole milk among the agents . by step 15, it reaches around 1.15, indicating a period where influence of habits on milk choice is not changing significantly . the deviation starts at a low value near zero, indicating that initially, the agents' health perceptions closely align with their milk choices . this rapid rise suggests that agents are adjusting their perceptions and choices, potentially influenced by social norms and habit thresholds . between steps 20 and 25, the deviation remains relatively stable .
