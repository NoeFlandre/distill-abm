 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, economic outcomes . The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration of the model run .  The agent-based model focuses on the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . It shows distinct trends in the average total number of livestock across all households over 100 time steps . The average reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over time .  The average number of livestock destocked across all households in the simulation exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the entire time span of 100 time steps . The number of households engaging in social learning continues to increase, reaching a peak of approximately 32 households around the 63rd time step . The Gini coefficient is steady and significant across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows .
