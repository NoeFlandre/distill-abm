The simulation data on the total number of times agents chose whole milk shows a consistent increasing trend over the observed time steps. Initially, the value starts at zero, indicating no consumption of whole milk. As the simulation progresses, the consumption of whole milk steadily rises. Key inflection points are observed at early, middle, and near the end of the simulation timeline, suggesting phases of accelerated adoption. The rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows consistently indicates a significant increasing trend with high tau values and significant p-values, reinforcing the steady rise in whole milk choices. The absence of detected peaks and valleys implies a smooth and continuous increase without significant fluctuations. The dominant oscillation period and multiple oscillation indices towards the end of the series suggest some periodic variations in the later stages, although the overall trend remains upward. For decision-makers, this trend highlights the growing preference for whole milk over time, which could be influenced by the initial strong habit parameters set for whole milk and the social influence dynamics within the agent-based model. Understanding these trends can help in strategizing marketing and health campaigns aimed at influencing milk consumption behaviors.
