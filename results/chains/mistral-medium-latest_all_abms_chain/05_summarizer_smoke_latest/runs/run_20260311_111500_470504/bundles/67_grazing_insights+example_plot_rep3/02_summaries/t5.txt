agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model adds learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . in the initial 20 time steps, there is a noticeable divergence in the trends . some households exhibit a sharp increase in risk attitude, reaching values as high as 0.8, while others show a significant decrease, dropping to values as low as -0.2 . the stabilization of risk attitudes implies that households have established their new risk attitudes . over the first 20 time steps, the mean destock continues to decline but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 1 livestock per household . this period indicates a phase of adjustment where households are fine-tuning their strategies to better align with the available resources . the variability in the destocking totals becomes more pronounced as the simulation progresses . this suggests different households may be responding to changing conditions in varied ways, influenced by their learning strategies and behavioral types .
