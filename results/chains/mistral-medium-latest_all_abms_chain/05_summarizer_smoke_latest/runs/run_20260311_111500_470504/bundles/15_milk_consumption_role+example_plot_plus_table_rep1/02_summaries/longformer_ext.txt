An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents, with the goal of assessing their effects on the system as a whole. In this case, the ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice. The main outcome of the ABM is the average weekly consumption of whole milk, reaching around 1500 units. This sharp decline suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior, likely influenced by changing perceptions, social interactions, or other model parameters.
