 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  Simulations show a consistent upward trend in the total number of whole milk choices made by agents over the steps . The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing targeted interventions or marketing strategies to shift consumer preferences towards healthier or more environmentally friendly milk options .  The total number of choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over the 30 steps of the simulation . The linear increase implies that the agents are continuously active and making choices at a constant rate . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents shows a clear and consistent downward trend over the simulation period .  Initially, the mean habit factor starts slightly above 1.0. For the first five steps, the factor remains relatively stable with minimal fluctuations . By the twentieth step onwards, the increase becomes more pronounced . This trend indicates that over time, the influence of habit on the choice of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk becomes stronger . For decision makers, this suggests that interventions aimed at promoting healthier milk choices should consider the growing influence of habits over time .
