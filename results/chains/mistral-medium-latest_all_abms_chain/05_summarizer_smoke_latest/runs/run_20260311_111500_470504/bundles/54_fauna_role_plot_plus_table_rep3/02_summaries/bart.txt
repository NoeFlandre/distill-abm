 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges .  Trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent increasing trend in the early phases of the simulation, which transitions to a decreasing trend towards the end . The mean number of females killed across the simulation is about 104.56, with a median of approximately 119.06 . The maximum value reached is approximately 123.67 females killed, with this peak occurring around the 1826-step mark . The model's parameters, such as high initial prey numbers, specific energy costs and gains for hunters, and environmental factors, contribute to the observed extinction dynamics . The simulation effectively captures the impact of hunting pressure and environmental conditions on the male animal population .
