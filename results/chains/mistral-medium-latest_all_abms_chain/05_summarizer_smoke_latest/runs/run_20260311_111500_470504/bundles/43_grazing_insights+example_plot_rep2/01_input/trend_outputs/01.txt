The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation period. The values are distributed across a range from approximately -0.1 to 0.17. This indicates that the initial risk attitudes of households do not change over time and are evenly spread across different levels of risk aversion and risk-seeking behavior. For decision-makers, this suggests a stable and diverse set of initial risk attitudes among households, which could be important for understanding the baseline behavior and decision-making processes of households in the context of the model.
