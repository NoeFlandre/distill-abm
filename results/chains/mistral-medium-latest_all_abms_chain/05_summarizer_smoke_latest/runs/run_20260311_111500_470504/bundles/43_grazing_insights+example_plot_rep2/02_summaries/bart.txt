 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  The mean reserve biomass in the grazing system starts at approximately 90,000 units . After 40 steps, the risk attitudes generally stabilize, with minor fluctuations observed until the end of the simulation at 100 steps . The trends in mean green biomass provide insights into the dynamics of vegetation growth and grazing pressure over time .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 time steps . The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at 0 across all simulations, indicating an initially equal distribution of livestock among households . Some simulations result in relatively low inequality, while others show high inequality in livestock distribution .  The count of non-learning households starts with a wide range of values, with some simulations beginning with as many as 40 households and others starting with fewer than 10 . By around 20 steps, there is a noticeable decline in the number of E-RO households across all simulations . This decline continues steeply until approximately 40 steps, where the number stabilizes across different simulations .
