The data shows the cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals killed over time in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM). Initially, the number of postpartum females killed is zero. As the simulation progresses, the number of postpartum females killed increases rapidly. By around 500 steps, the cumulative number of postpartum females killed reaches approximately 100. This rapid increase continues until about 1000 steps, where the cumulative number stabilizes around 120. Beyond 1000 steps, the rate of increase slows down significantly, indicating a plateau in the number of postpartum females killed. This trend suggests that the majority of the hunting and killing events occur within the first 1000 steps of the simulation, after which the rate of such events diminishes, leading to a steady state.
