The mean green biomass across all patches exhibits a consistent trend across the 30 simulations. Initially, the mean green biomass starts at approximately 45,000 units. Over the first 20 steps, there is a slight decline in biomass, reaching a low point of around 44,000 units. Following this initial decline, the biomass begins to increase steadily. By step 40, the mean green biomass has recovered to its initial level of approximately 45,000 units. From this point onward, the biomass continues to rise more rapidly, reaching around 55,000 units by step 60. The upward trend persists, and by step 100, the mean green biomass stabilizes at approximately 62,000 units. This pattern suggests an initial period of adjustment followed by a significant and sustained increase in biomass over time. For decision makers, this trend indicates that while there may be an initial decline in green biomass due to grazing, the system has the potential to recover and even exceed initial levels, suggesting resilience and potential for sustainable management practices.
