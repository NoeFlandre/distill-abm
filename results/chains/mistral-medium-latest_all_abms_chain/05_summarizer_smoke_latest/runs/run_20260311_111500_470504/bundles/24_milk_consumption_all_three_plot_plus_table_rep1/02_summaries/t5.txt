agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the average weekly consumption of whole milk starts at approximately 2655 units . this high initial value reflects the strong initial habit strength for whole milk consumption among the agents, as well as the lack of immediate alternatives . the trend continues to rise consistently, reaching a final value of approximately 2194.57 choices . the mean number of whole milk choices across all time steps is 1130.88, with a median of 1158.97 . the quantity declines rapidly within the first few steps, dropping significantly by around 10 steps . this sharp decline suggests a swift change in consumer behavior, potentially due to increasing awareness or influence of alternative milk types . by around 15 steps, the mean quantity stabilizes somewhat, fluctuating around 800 units . habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at 1.0000 and remains relatively stable for the first few steps . around the fifth step, there is a minor dip to 1.0167, indicating a brief period where the habit factor slightly decreases, possibly due to initial resistance or skepticism towards alternative milk types . the deviation starts to increase again, reaching approximately 0.6 by step 28 . this renewed increase could be attributed to further social interactions and adjustments in perceptions, indicating that agents are continually refining their understanding of health implications . however, once a steady state is reached, further interventions may have diminishing returns .
