 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The average weekly consumption of whole milk per person starts at approximately 2655 units . Over the first five steps, there is a rapid decline in consumption, dropping to around 1500 units . This sharp decrease can be attributed to the introduction and initial adoption of alternative milk types . The overall trend highlights the impact of health and environmental perceptions, social influence, and habit adjustment on consumer behavior .  The total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across all windows . This trend suggests that efforts to shift consumption patterns towards alternative milk types may need to be more aggressive or innovative to counteract the entrenched habits and preferences for whole milk .  Simulation shows an initial rapid adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, followed by a period of stabilization and a final increase, indicating a long-term shift in consumption habits . For decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial consumer habits and the rapid impact of awareness campaigns or social influences in shifting consumption patterns .  The mean choice function deviation among agents starts at 0.0000 and increases rapidly within the first few steps, reaching approximately 0.6 by step 7 . By step 12, the deviation reaches around 0.65, and it continues to rise steadily, albeit at a slower pace, until it reaches about 0.75 by step 32 . This steady increase indicates a strong and sustained shift in consumer habits towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at 0 and increases rapidly in the first 10 steps, reaching a value of approximately 13.4 . This rapid increase indicates that initially, there is a significant discrepancy between the agents' environmental values and the environmental implications of their milk choices . As agents begin to interact and influence each other, their choices start to align more closely with their environmental values .
