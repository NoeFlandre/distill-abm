 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . Ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values and processing costs for males and females .  The number of females killed starts at 0 and increases rapidly within the first 250 time steps, reaching approximately 100 . This rapid initial increase suggests a high initial hunting pressure on postpartum females, likely due to their vulnerability and the high food value they provide to hunters . The rate of increase in deaths slows down significantly after this initial phase, indicating a potential stabilization in the population dynamics .  The total number of prey animals in the simulation starts at approximately 166.67 and exhibits a rapid decline within the first 500 time steps . The overall trend is a consistent decline with minor oscillations, reflecting the challenging survival conditions for female animals within the parameters of this model . By the end of the simulation, the number of female animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete extinction .  The energy levels eventually approach zero by the end of the simulation at 2,312 time steps . Sharp decline suggests a significant reduction in the prey population, potentially due to high predation pressure, environmental factors, or a combination of both . The dominant oscillation period of 63 time steps suggests a cyclical pattern in the vegetation dynamics .
