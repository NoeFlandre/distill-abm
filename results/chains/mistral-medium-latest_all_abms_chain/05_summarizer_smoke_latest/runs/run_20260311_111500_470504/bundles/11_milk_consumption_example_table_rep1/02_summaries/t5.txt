agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . consumption peaks at 1456.60, indicating a sustained and significant increase in the preference for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over whole milk . this peak suggests that the health and environmental perceptions, along with social influences, have solidified the consumer base for these milk types . the total number of times agents chose whole milk starts at zero and increases steadily throughout the simulation period . the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at a very low value of 5.29 units . this initial value suggests that at the beginning of the simulation, the consumption of these types of milk is minimal . the consumption then increases rapidly, indicating a significant shift in consumer preferences or behaviors . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across multiple windows . absence of detected peaks and valleys suggests a smooth and continuous increase without significant fluctuations . the oscillation indices indicate periodic variations within the overall increasing trend .
