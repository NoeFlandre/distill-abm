The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at a value of 0. This indicates an initial state of perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock. Over the course of the simulation, the Gini coefficient exhibits a consistent increasing trend, reaching a final value of approximately 0.0726. This suggests a gradual shift towards a slightly more unequal distribution of healthy livestock among households. The mean value of the Gini coefficient over the entire simulation period is 0.0302, with a median value of 0.0229, indicating that the central tendency of the data remains relatively low but positive throughout the simulation. The standard deviation of 0.0239 and variance of 0.0006 suggest low variability in the Gini coefficient values over time. Multiple inflection points are detected at various time steps, indicating changes in the rate of increase in inequality. Notably, these inflection points occur around time steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend in the Gini coefficient across all tested windows, with significant p-values indicating strong statistical evidence for this trend. Change points are identified at time steps 51, 71, and 100, suggesting notable shifts in the dynamics of inequality at these points. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 49 time steps, with several oscillation indices detected towards the latter part of the simulation, indicating periodic fluctuations in the rate of change of the Gini coefficient. Overall, the simulation suggests a gradual and consistent increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock, with periodic fluctuations and notable change points indicating shifts in the dynamics of this inequality.
