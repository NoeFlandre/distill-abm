The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of approximately 0, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock. Over time, the inequality gradually increases, reaching a value of 0.0726 by the end of the simulation. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across all windows, with significant positive slopes and tau values of 1.000, indicating a strong and consistent upward trend. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points suggests that the rate of increase in inequality varies at different stages of the simulation. The dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps indicates some periodic fluctuations in the rate of change, but the overall trend remains upward. For decision makers, this trend implies that over time, the distribution of healthy livestock becomes increasingly unequal among households. This could have significant implications for the sustainability and resilience of the pastoral system, as greater inequality may lead to disparities in household livelihoods and potentially affect the overall stability of the grazing system.
