The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend in all windows, indicating a steady rise in inequality over time. Several inflection points are noted at steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, suggesting periods where the rate of increase in inequality changes. Change points at steps 51, 71, and 100 further highlight significant shifts in the trend. The oscillation indices suggest some periodic fluctuations in the latter part of the simulation. For decision-makers, these trends imply that over time, the distribution of healthy livestock becomes increasingly unequal among households. This growing disparity could have significant implications for the sustainability and resilience of pastoral systems, potentially necessitating interventions to ensure more equitable resource distribution and support for households that may be at a disadvantage.
