 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The overall trend demonstrates a significant shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the simulated period . For decision-makers, these trends underscore the importance of initial exposure and continuous reinforcement of information to sustain and increase the adoption of healthier milk alternatives .  The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,655 units . This high initial value indicates a strong preference for whole milk at the beginning of the simulation period . By the end of the simulated period, the mean quantity consumed drops to approximately 253 units . The overall trend is a significant and steady decline in whole milk consumption .  Trend analysis shows significant increasing trends in multiple windows, particularly from the fifth step onward . For decision makers, this trend implies a growing preference and habit formation towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time, which could inform marketing strategies and public health campaigns aimed at promoting healthier milk choices .
