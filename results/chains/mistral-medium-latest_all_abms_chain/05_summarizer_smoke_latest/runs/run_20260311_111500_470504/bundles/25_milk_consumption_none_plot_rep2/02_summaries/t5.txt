agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the data from the repeated simulations of the agent-based model show a consistent rate of adoption or choice behavior among the agents over the given time steps . Initially, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices is very low, starting close to zero . this indicates that at the beginning of the simulation period, which corresponds to the year 1974, the majority of agents are not choosing skimmmed or semi-shammed milk . by around the 5th step, the number of choices habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of approximately 1.0 . around step 5, there is a slight increase, indicating a gradual rise . by step 15, the habit factor is approximately 1.2, and it continues to rise more steeply .
