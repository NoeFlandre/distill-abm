The model's context is rooted in understanding the long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types and their decision-making processes under various ecological and climatic conditions. The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems. The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes.
