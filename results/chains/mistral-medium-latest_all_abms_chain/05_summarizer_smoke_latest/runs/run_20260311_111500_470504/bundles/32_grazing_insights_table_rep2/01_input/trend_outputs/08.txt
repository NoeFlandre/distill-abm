The number of households engaging in social learning, as indicated by the variable household-SL being true, exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, no households are engaged in social learning. However, this number steadily rises, reaching a peak of approximately 32 households around the mid-point of the simulation timeline. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across all windows, with varying rates of increase. Early in the simulation, the rate of increase is relatively high, as seen in the first two windows where the slope is 1.430 and 1.139 respectively. This rate of increase slows down in subsequent windows, indicating a gradual adoption of social learning behaviors among households. The inflection points and change points suggest periods where the rate of adoption experiences subtle shifts, but the overall trend remains upward. The oscillation indices indicate some periodic fluctuations, but these do not disrupt the overall increasing trend. For decision makers, these trends imply that social learning behaviors diffuse gradually through the population of households, with a notable acceleration in the early stages followed by a more gradual adoption phase. This suggests that interventions aimed at promoting social learning could see significant initial uptake, but may require sustained efforts to achieve widespread adoption.
