agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model adds learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . the simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents over the course of 100 time steps . Initially, the deviation starts at a low value of 0.0074 . there is a rapid increase in deviation during the early time steps, reaching a peak value of approximately 0.3814 around time step 33 . the inflection points at time steps 3 and 46 indicate notable changes in trend direction . the oscillation analysis suggests periodic fluctuations in the livestock numbers, with a dominant period of 49 time steps . for decision makers, these trends imply that initial management strategies may lead to a significant reduction in livestock numbers . trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across all windows . inflection points are observed at various time steps, suggesting periods where the rate of increase slightly changes, but the overall trend remains upward . for decision makers, this trend suggests a continuous and increasing pressure on households to destock livestock over time .
