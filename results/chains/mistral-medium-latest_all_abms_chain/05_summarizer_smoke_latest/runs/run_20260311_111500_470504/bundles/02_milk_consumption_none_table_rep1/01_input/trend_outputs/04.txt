The simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent and increasing trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the observed time period. Initially, the number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk started at zero. Over time, this number steadily increased, reaching a peak of approximately 25,643.83 at the final time step. The mean number of choices for skimmed or semi-skimmed milk across all time steps is around 11,157.35, with a median value of 10,679.43, indicating a steady rise throughout the simulation. The standard deviation of 8,241.32 suggests a significant spread in the data, reflecting the increasing trend over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows consistently indicates a significant increasing trend, with all windows showing a tau value of 1.000 and significant p-values, reinforcing the steady adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The change point detected at the final time step suggests a continuous trend without any significant shifts in the pattern of adoption. The oscillation analysis indicates some periodic fluctuations, but the dominant trend remains one of consistent increase. Overall, the model demonstrates a clear and steady shift towards the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the agents.
