The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent over the observed time period. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29 units. There is a rapid increase in consumption during the early stages, with significant growth observed up to around the 9th time step, reaching approximately 1265.47 units. This period of increase is statistically significant, indicating a strong upward trend in milk consumption. Following this rapid rise, the consumption levels enter a phase of stabilization where fluctuations occur but without a clear upward or downward trend. This phase spans from around the 10th to the 24th time step, with consumption values oscillating around a mean of approximately 1220 units. Key peaks during this phase are observed at the 11th, 13th, 19th, and 21st time steps with values around 1265.47, 1240.35, 1221.57, and 1226.86 units respectively. Corresponding valleys are noted at the 12th, 18th, 20th, and 23rd time steps with values around 1233.62, 1183.39, 1214.46, and 1209.70 units respectively. Towards the latter part of the simulation, there is a resurgence in the increasing trend of milk consumption. This is evident from the 25th time step onwards, culminating in a peak consumption value of 1456.60 units at the final time step. The overall trend indicates an initial rapid adoption phase, followed by a stabilization period with minor fluctuations, and ending with a renewed increase in consumption levels.
