The simulation of the agent-based model shows a clear and consistent downward trend in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents over the time period from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the average consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units. This value experiences a steep decline in the early years, dropping significantly within the first few time steps. By the mid-point of the simulation, the mean consumption has decreased to around 816.85 units, indicating a substantial shift in consumption patterns. The trend continues to decline, albeit with some fluctuations and minor peaks and valleys, but the overall trajectory remains downward. Notable peaks occur at time steps 19, 27, and 30 with values of approximately 773.96, 620.98, and 335.18 units respectively. Conversely, valleys are observed at time steps 18, 26, and 29 with values around 760.00, 594.35, and 323.46 units respectively. The simulation ends with the mean consumption at approximately 253.10 units, marking a significant reduction from the initial value. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates several windows of significant decreasing trends, reinforcing the overall decline in whole milk consumption. The inflection points and oscillation indices suggest periods of varying rates of decline and minor fluctuations, but the dominant pattern remains a steady decrease in consumption over time.
