Starting from an initial value of 0, the total number of choices rises uniformly, reaching a final value of 32,000. The mean and median values of the series are both 16,000, reflecting a symmetric distribution of data points around the center. The standard deviation of approximately 9,522 suggests a moderate spread of values around the mean. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across all analyzed windows, with a consistent slope of 1,000, indicating a steady and predictable rise in milk choices over time. No peaks, valleys, or inflection points were detected, reinforcing the uniformity of the trend. The oscillation analysis suggests some periodic behavior, but the dominant trend remains the consistent increase in choices. The steady rise suggests a stable and predictable adoption pattern, which can be useful for forecasting and strategic planning.
