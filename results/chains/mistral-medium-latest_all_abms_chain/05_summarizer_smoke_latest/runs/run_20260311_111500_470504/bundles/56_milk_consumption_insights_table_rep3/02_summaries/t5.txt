agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the consumption reaches its peak at approximately 1456.60 units by the end of the simulation in 2005 . for decision-makers, these trends highlight the initial rapid adoption phase followed by a stabilization period . the final increase suggests a potential renewed interest or additional factors influencing the adoption of healthier milk options . Initially, the average consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units . this value experiences a significant and steady decline, reaching a low of about 253.10 units by the end of the simulation period . from the start to around the first quarter of the time steps, there is a sharp decline in consumption followed by periods of continued decrease . the mean factor exhibits a consistent upward trend, reaching a final value of 1.5879 at the last recorded time step . the trend analysis indicates significant increasing trends across multiple time windows, suggesting a steady growth in the habit factor influencing the choice of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . a deviation between personal and implied environmental values quickly stabilizes . this suggests interventions aimed at aligning personal values with consumption choices may be most effective if implemented early in the adoption process .
