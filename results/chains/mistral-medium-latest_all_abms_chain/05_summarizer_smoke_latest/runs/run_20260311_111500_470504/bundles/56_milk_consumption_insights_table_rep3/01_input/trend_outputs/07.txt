The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29 units. There is a rapid increase in consumption during the early time steps, with significant growth observed up to around the 9th time step, where the mean consumption reaches approximately 1220.21 units. This period of rapid increase is followed by a phase of stabilization where the consumption values oscillate around a high mean value, indicating a plateau in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. Key peaks in consumption are observed at time steps 11, 13, 19, and 21, with values around 1265.47, 1240.35, 1221.57, and 1226.86 units respectively. Similarly, valleys are noted at time steps 12, 18, 20, and 23, with values around 1233.62, 1183.39, 1214.46, and 1209.70 units respectively. The model exhibits several inflection points, indicating changes in the trend dynamics, particularly around time steps 4, 12, 13, 16, 19, 22, 27, and 30. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early and late phases of the simulation, with periods of no significant trend in the middle phases. The dominant oscillation period is around 15 time steps, suggesting cyclic patterns in the consumption behavior. The final consumption value reaches a peak of 1456.60 units at the end of the simulation. For decision makers, these trends imply that initial adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk is rapid, followed by a stabilization phase with minor fluctuations. This suggests that interventions or marketing efforts might be most effective during the early stages to accelerate adoption, while ongoing efforts may be needed to maintain high consumption levels during the stabilization phase.
