The simulation results show a consistent and increasing trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk by agents over the observed time period. Initially, the number of times agents chose these milk types started at zero. The standard deviation of approximately 8,241 indicates some variability in the adoption rates across different time steps. The maximum value reached by the end of the simulation is 25,644, highlighting significant growth from the starting point. The rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows consistently shows increasing trends with significant p-values, reinforcing the steady rise in adoption. The change point detected at the final time step suggests that the increasing trend is maintained throughout the simulation period. The oscillation analysis indicates some periodic fluctuations, but the dominant trend remains one of consistent growth in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk.
