The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, the mean habit factor starts at a baseline value of 1.0000. This value remains relatively stable for the first few steps before beginning a gradual and steady increase. By the end of the simulation period, the mean habit factor reaches a value of approximately 1.0502. The overall mean of the habit factor across all time steps is 1.0359, with a median value of 1.0376, indicating a slight right skew in the distribution of values. The standard deviation is 0.0110, suggesting low variability around the mean. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across all windows, with significant positive slopes indicating a steady rise in the habit factor over time. Inflection points are noted around steps 7 and 12, suggesting slight changes in the rate of increase. The oscillation analysis indicates periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of 15 steps, but the overall trend remains upward. This suggests that over time, the habit factor influencing whole milk consumption gradually increases, reflecting a growing influence of habit on the agents' choices in the model.
