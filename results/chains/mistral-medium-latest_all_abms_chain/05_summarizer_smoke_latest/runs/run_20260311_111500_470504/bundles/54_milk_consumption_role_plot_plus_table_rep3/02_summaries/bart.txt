 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  Simulation of the agent-based model shows a clear and consistent downward trend in the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent over the observed time steps . Initially, the mean whole milk consumption starts at approximately 2654.81 units . This high level of consumption rapidly declines in the early steps, showing a sharp decrease . By the fifth time step, the consumption has already dropped significantly .  Simulation of the agent-based model shows a clear and consistent downward trend in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents over the time steps . Initially, the mean whole milk quantity starts at approximately 2654.81 units . This high value indicates a strong initial preference or habit for whole milk consumption among the agents . By the final time step, the total number of skimmed or semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 25,643.83 .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows the trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over a series of steps . The presence of peaks and valleys, along with inflection points, underscores the dynamic interactions and adjustments in agents' behaviors throughout the simulation period .  The mean choice function deviation consistently trends upwards, reflecting an increasing divergence in the choice function scores among agents over time . This pattern indicates that as the simulation progresses, agents' choice functions increasingly diverge, potentially due to the cumulative effects of perception, habits, and social influence on their decision-making processes .
