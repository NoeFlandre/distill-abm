The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several key trends in the adoption behavior of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption among the British public from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the mean weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per person is very low, starting at approximately 5.29 units. This low consumption rate persists for a brief period before a rapid increase begins. 

By around the 4th time step, there is a noticeable inflection point where the consumption starts to rise sharply. This upward trend continues steeply until about the 12th time step, where the consumption reaches a peak of approximately 1265.47 units. Following this peak, the consumption rate exhibits minor fluctuations but generally stabilizes around a mean value of 1220.21 units. These fluctuations are relatively small, indicating a period of stability in consumption patterns. 

The data shows several peaks and valleys during this stable period, with notable peaks at time steps 11, 13, 21, and 19, and valleys at time steps 12, 20, 23, and 18. Despite these minor oscillations, the overall trend remains relatively flat, suggesting a steady state in the consumption behavior. 

Towards the end of the simulation, around the 25th time step, there is another inflection point where the consumption rate begins to increase again. This second rise continues until the end of the simulation period, reaching a final value of approximately 1456.60 units. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these trends, indicating significant increasing trends in the initial and final phases of the simulation, with periods of no significant trend in between. 

The dominant oscillation period of 15 time steps suggests a cyclical pattern in the consumption behavior, although the overall trend is characterized by initial rapid growth, a period of stability, and a final increase. The statistical analysis supports these observations, with a mean consumption value of 1051.59 units and a median of 1220.21 units, highlighting the central tendency of the data around the stable period.
