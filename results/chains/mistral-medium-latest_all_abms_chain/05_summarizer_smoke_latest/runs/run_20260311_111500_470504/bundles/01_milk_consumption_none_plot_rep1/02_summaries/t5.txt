agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the data from the repeated simulations of the agent-based model show a clear and consistent trend in the adoption of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices over time . at step 0, the total number of choices made is very low, close to zero . this indicates that at the beginning of the simulation period, which corresponds to the year 1974, the majority of agents are not choosing . around step 10, the increase becomes more pronounced, indicating a growing trend towards the adoption . mmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of approximately 1.0 . by step 15, the mean habit factor is about 1.35, and it continues to rise linearly . the deviation shows a consistent upward trend, indicating an increasing mean difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores .
