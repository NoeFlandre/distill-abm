The standard deviation of 654.69 suggests some variability in the number of choices across different time steps, but the overall trend remains upward. The absence of detected peaks or valleys further supports a smooth and continuous increase in whole milk choices. The inflection points at indices 1, 7, and 31 suggest slight changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains positive. For decision-makers, this trend implies that despite the introduction and potential promotion of alternative milk types, the initial strong habit for whole milk plays a significant role in its continued consumption.
