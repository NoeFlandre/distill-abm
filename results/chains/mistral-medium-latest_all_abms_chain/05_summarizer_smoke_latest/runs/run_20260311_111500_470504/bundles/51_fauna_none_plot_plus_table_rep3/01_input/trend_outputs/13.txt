The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred ticks, indicating a sharp reduction in the energy available among the prey animals. By around 500 ticks, the total energy has decreased significantly, reaching values closer to 4,000 units. This period marks a substantial drop, reflecting a critical phase where the energy reserves of the animals are being depleted rapidly. 

Following this initial steep decline, the rate of energy reduction slows down, but the overall trend remains downward. Between 500 and 1,000 ticks, the total energy continues to decrease, albeit at a slower pace, stabilizing somewhat around values closer to 1,000 units. This phase suggests a period where the remaining animals have lower energy reserves, and the rate of energy loss is less pronounced. 

As the simulation progresses beyond 1,000 ticks, the total energy of the animals continues to decline gradually. There are minor fluctuations and small peaks observed around certain ticks, such as slight increases around 700 and 800 ticks, but these are not sustained, and the overall trend remains a decrease. By the time the simulation reaches around 2,000 ticks, the total energy of the animals approaches near-zero values, indicating that the energy reserves of the prey animals have been almost entirely depleted. 

The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with multiple significant windows identified by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, all showing a significant downward trend in total energy. The presence of several inflection points and change points further supports the observation of a consistent decline in energy levels over time. The oscillation indices suggest some periodic fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the overall decreasing trend. The dominant period of oscillation is relatively short, indicating that any periodic variations in energy levels are not sustained and do not significantly alter the primary downward trajectory.
