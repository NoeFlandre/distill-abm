From step 40 to step 60, the Gini coefficient continues to rise more steeply, reaching values between 0.030 and 0.075. This trend implies that disparities in livestock distribution are becoming more pronounced, potentially due to the compounding effects of successful adaptive strategies in some households and less successful strategies in others. This suggests that while inequality in livestock distribution continues to grow, the rate of increase slows down as the system approaches a new equilibrium. The overall trend indicates that over time, certain households are becoming more successful in maintaining and growing their livestock, leading to greater inequality. For decision makers, this trend highlights the importance of understanding the factors contributing to successful adaptive strategies and the potential need for interventions to support households that are falling behind in livestock accumulation.
