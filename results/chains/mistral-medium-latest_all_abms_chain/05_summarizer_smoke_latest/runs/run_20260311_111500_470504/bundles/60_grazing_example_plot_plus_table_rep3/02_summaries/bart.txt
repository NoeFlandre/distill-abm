 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  Trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates an increasing trend in the early stages (time steps 0 to 32), followed by a decreasing trend from time steps 33 to 65 . After time step 66, there is no significant trend, indicating that the system has reached a relatively stable state .  The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at a value of 0, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 time steps . By around 40 time steps, the number with social learning stabilizes around 32 households .
