The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the number of male prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 male prey animals. The trend is characterized by a rapid decrease in the early stages, with significant drops observed within the first few hundred ticks. By the 730th tick, the population is reduced to approximately 10.54 males, marking a substantial decline. This downward trend continues, albeit at a slower rate, with notable inflection points around the 390th, 755th, and 817th ticks, indicating periods where the rate of decline changes. The population experiences minor fluctuations and temporary stabilizations, as evidenced by the peaks and valleys identified around ticks 709, 803, and 820, but the overall trajectory remains downward. By the 2284th tick, the population of male prey animals reaches zero, indicating complete extinction within the simulation timeframe. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend across multiple time windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values, reinforcing the consistent decline in the male prey population throughout the simulation.
