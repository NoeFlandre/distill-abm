The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. From the onset, there is a rapid decline in energy levels, with notable early values such as approximately 1989.925 units at tick 2 and 1263.3 units at tick 55. The energy levels continue to drop sharply, reaching negative values by around tick 394, where the energy level is approximately -2005.95 units. This downward trend persists with several inflection points indicating changes in the rate of decline, notably around ticks 358, 1238, and 1329. Key change points at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 mark significant shifts in the trend, each followed by a steeper decline in energy levels. By the end of the simulation at tick 2310, the energy level plummets to -38233 units. The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decrease, with all rolling Mann-Kendall windows showing a significant decreasing trend and negative slopes. The presence of oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks suggests periodic fluctuations in the energy levels, although the overall trajectory remains a steady decline. This trend indicates that the forager bands are consistently losing energy over time, likely due to a combination of hunting costs, movement costs, and insufficient energy gain from prey.
