 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The mean weekly whole milk consumption per agent begins at approximately 2600 units . There is a rapid decline in consumption within the first 5 steps, dropping to around 1000 units . This sharp decrease suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior away from whole milk . By the final step, the total number of whole milk choices approaches 2,000, indicating a high level of adoption .  The total number of choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases linearly over the 30 steps . At step 10, approximately 10,000 choices have been made, and at step 20, this number doubles to around 20,000 . The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at a low value close to zero . It then increases rapidly within the first 10 steps, reaching approximately 1200 units .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero . It then rises rapidly within the first 5 steps, reaching a value of approximately 12 . The overall trend shows that while initial differences in choice function scores emerge quickly, the system gradually reaches a more stable state with slower changes in deviation .
