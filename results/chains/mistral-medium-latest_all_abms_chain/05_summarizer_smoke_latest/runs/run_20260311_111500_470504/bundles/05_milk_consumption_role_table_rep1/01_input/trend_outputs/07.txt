The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several key trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time. Initially, the mean quantity starts at a low value of approximately 5.29. This value rapidly increases in the early time steps, indicating a swift initial adoption of alternative milk types. By the time step corresponding to index 4, there is a notable inflection point, suggesting a change in the rate of adoption. The mean quantity continues to rise, reaching a peak of around 1265.47 at index 11. Following this peak, the trend exhibits minor fluctuations with several smaller peaks and valleys, indicating periods of slight increases and decreases in consumption. For instance, there are peaks at indices 13, 21, and 19 with values around 1240.35, 1226.86, and 1221.57 respectively, and valleys at indices 12, 20, and 23 with values around 1233.62, 1214.46, and 1209.70 respectively. These oscillations suggest periodic variations in the adoption rates, potentially due to the interplay of social influence, habit formation, and cognitive dissonance among the agents. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early and later phases of the simulation, with notable increasing trends in windows 1, 2, and 6. The final time step shows a substantial increase, with the mean quantity reaching approximately 1456.60, marking the highest consumption level in the simulation. This overall upward trend, punctuated by periods of stabilization and minor fluctuations, reflects the complex dynamics of milk consumption behavior influenced by various social and psychological factors.
