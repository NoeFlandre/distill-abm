The average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent in the simulation shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the average consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units. This value rapidly declines in the early stages of the simulation, indicating a significant reduction in whole milk consumption. By the midpoint of the simulation, the average consumption has decreased to around 773.96 units, marking a substantial drop from the initial value. This downward trend continues, albeit with some fluctuations, as evidenced by the peaks and valleys identified in the data. For instance, there are notable peaks at around 773.96, 620.98, and 335.18 units, followed by subsequent declines. The simulation ends with the lowest average consumption value of approximately 253.10 units, reinforcing the overall decreasing trend. The statistical analysis confirms this trend, with multiple windows showing significant decreasing trends in the rolling Mann-Kendall test. The presence of several inflection points suggests periods where the rate of decline changes, indicating varying rates of reduction in whole milk consumption at different stages of the simulation. The oscillation indices and the dominant period of oscillation further suggest that while there are periodic fluctuations, the overall trajectory remains downward. This trend reflects a significant shift in consumption patterns, likely influenced by the model parameters governing agent behavior and interactions.
