the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . the number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration . by 2000 steps, the total energy of foragers drops to approximately -35,000 units . after the initial surge, the number of deaths rises steeply from 0 to approximately 200 within the first 500 steps . this sharp increase indicates a high mortality rate among the prey population during the early phase of the simulation . after 1000 steps, the rate of deaths begins to slow down significantly, and the population is experiencing fewer deaths per time step. the total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model starts at a high value of approximately 12,000 units . this initial high energy level is maintained for a brief period before a sharp decline begins around the 100th step . the energy levels drop steeply, reaching around 2,000 units by the 500th step, indicating a significant reduction in the energy available within the male animal population . this decline indicates a significant reduction in the availability of grass . this recovery suggests a period of regrowth or reduced grazing pressure . the percentage of green patches then stabilizes once more, maintaining this level until around step 1100 .
