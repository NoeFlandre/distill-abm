The simulation of the agent-based model shows a clear and consistent downward trend in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents over the time period from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the average consumption of whole milk starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units. This value experiences a sharp decline in the early years, dropping significantly within the first few time steps. By the mid-1980s, the consumption rate stabilizes somewhat but continues to exhibit a general decreasing trend with some fluctuations. Notable inflection points occur around the early 1980s, mid-1980s, and early 1990s, indicating periods where the rate of decline changes. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms significant decreasing trends in multiple time windows, particularly in the early and late stages of the simulation period. The mean consumption value reaches its lowest point of approximately 253.10 units by the end of the simulation in 2005. Throughout the simulation, there are minor peaks and valleys, suggesting temporary stabilizations or slight reversals in the trend, but the overall trajectory remains a steady decline. The dominant oscillation period of 15 time steps indicates some cyclical variations within the broader downward trend.
