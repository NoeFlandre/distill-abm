The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29. There is a rapid increase in consumption during the early years, with significant growth observed in the first few time steps. By the mid-1980s, the consumption rate reaches a peak around 1265.47, indicating a substantial shift in milk preferences among the agents. Following this peak, the consumption values exhibit minor fluctuations with several smaller peaks and valleys, suggesting periods of stabilization and slight adjustments in consumption patterns. Notable peaks occur around values of 1240.35, 1226.86, and 1221.57, while valleys are observed at approximately 1233.62, 1214.46, and 1209.70. The latter part of the simulation shows a general trend of increasing consumption, culminating in a maximum value of 1456.60 by the end of the period. The data suggests that after an initial rapid adoption phase, the consumption patterns stabilize with minor oscillations before resuming an upward trend towards the end of the simulation period.
