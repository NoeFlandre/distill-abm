agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent and increasing trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . over time, the consumption steadily increases, reaching a peak of approximately 2194.57 at the end of the simulation period . Initially, the mean consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . this value rapidly increases in the early time steps, indicating a swift initial adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the agents . by the early 1980s, the consumption reaches around 1265.47 units, marking the first significant peak . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across multiple windows, with consistent positive slopes ranging from 0.003 to 0.063 . a dominant oscillation period of 15.00 is detected, with oscillation indices suggesting periodic fluctuations around the overall increasing trend .
