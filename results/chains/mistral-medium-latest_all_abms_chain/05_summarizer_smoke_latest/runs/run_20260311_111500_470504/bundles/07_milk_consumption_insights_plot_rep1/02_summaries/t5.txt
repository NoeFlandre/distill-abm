agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . by the 30th step, the total number of whole milk choices approaches 3000 . this trend indicates a constant and steady preference for whole milk among the agents . for decision makers, this suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior towards healthier or lower-fat milk options . habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the time steps . from step 10 to step 20, the habit factor continues to increase steadily, reaching a value of approximately 1.3 . this trend suggests that interventions or policies aimed at promoting healthier milk choices may become more effective .
