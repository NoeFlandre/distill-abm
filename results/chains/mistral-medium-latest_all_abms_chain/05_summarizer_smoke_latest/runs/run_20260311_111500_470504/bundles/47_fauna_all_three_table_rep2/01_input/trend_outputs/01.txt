The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration. Starting at an initial value of 20 bands, the number does not fluctuate, maintaining this value consistently from the beginning to the end of the simulation. This stability indicates that the parameters governing the hunter-gatherer dynamics, such as immortality of hunters and their movement costs, are balanced in a way that neither promotes growth nor decline in the number of bands. For decision makers, this suggests that under the given conditions and parameters, the model predicts a stable human hunting pressure on the megaherbivore population. This stability could be crucial for understanding the long-term impacts of consistent hunting pressure on prey populations and the broader ecosystem.
