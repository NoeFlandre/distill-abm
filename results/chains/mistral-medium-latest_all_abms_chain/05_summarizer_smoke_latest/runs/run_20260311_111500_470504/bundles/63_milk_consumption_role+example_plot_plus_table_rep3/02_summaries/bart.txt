 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  Average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per person starts at approximately 5.29 units . Total number of whole milk choices starts at zero, indicating that initially, no agents have chosen whole milk . By step 7, the total number of total milk choices reaches around 530, indicating a significant uptake in the choice of whole .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . The trend reflects a strong and sustained adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, driven by cumulative social influence, habit reinforcement, and positive perceptions of health and environmental benefits .  The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at a baseline value of 1.0000 . Initially, there is a rapid increase in the habit factor within the first few steps, indicating a quick adjustment period where agents begin to form habits related to whole milk consumption .
