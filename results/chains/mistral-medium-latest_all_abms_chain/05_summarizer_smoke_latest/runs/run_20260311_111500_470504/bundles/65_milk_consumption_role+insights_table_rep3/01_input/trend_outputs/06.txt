The simulation data reveals a clear and consistent downward trend in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents over the observed time period. Initially, the consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units. This value experiences a sharp decline in the early stages, followed by a more gradual decrease over time, eventually reaching a low of about 253.10 units. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant decreasing trends in multiple windows, particularly in the early and middle stages of the simulation. There are periods where the trend stabilizes temporarily, but the overall trajectory remains downward. The presence of several inflection points suggests that the rate of decline varies at different stages, with notable changes around time steps 5, 13, 16, 19, 24, 25, 27, and 30. The oscillation analysis indicates periodic fluctuations, but these do not alter the overall declining trend. For decision makers, this trend suggests a significant shift away from whole milk consumption over time, which could be influenced by the model parameters such as health perceptions, social influences, and habit thresholds. Understanding these dynamics can help in strategizing interventions or marketing strategies aimed at influencing milk consumption behaviors.
