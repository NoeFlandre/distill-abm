 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . The mean number of whole milk choices across the time steps is around 1130.88, with a median of 1158.97, indicating a relatively symmetric distribution of values around the mean . The overall trend demonstrates a significant transition from low to high consumption rates, reflecting changes in consumer preferences and behaviors .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 5.29 units at the beginning of the simulation period . The mean habit factor for whole milk shows a clear and steady increase, reflecting a growing influence of habit on the consumption of whole milk over time, reflecting the impact of perception, habits, and social influence on agents' decision-making processes .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at 0.0000 and increases rapidly in the initial steps . By around the 5th step, the deviation reaches approximately 12.0, showing a steep initial adjustment period . The deviation continues to increase but at a slower rate, stabilizing around the value of 13.4 by the 13th step . This stabilization suggests that the agents' environmental values and their milk choices have reached a form of equilibrium or steady state .
