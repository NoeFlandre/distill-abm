 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  Average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at approximately 2,600 units . This high initial value indicates a strong initial habit and preference for whole milk among the agents . Over the first 10 steps, there is a rapid decline in consumption, dropping to around 1,000 units . Sharp decrease suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior, likely influenced by changing perceptions of health and environmental benefits of alternative milk types .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases linearly over the 30 steps . At each step, the number of choices made increases by approximately 1,000, reaching a total of about 30,000 choices by the end of the simulation . The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents begins at approximately 2,600 units .  The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at a value of 1.00 and rapidly increases to approximately 1.02 within the first few steps . From the 10th and 20th steps, the figure shows a consistent upward trend, reaching about 1.04 . This period reflects a phase where the habit becomes more entrenched among the agents . Understanding the timeline and strength of habit formation can help in designing more effective strategies to encourage the consumption of healthier milk .  Trend of increasing mean value health deviation implies that over time, agents' choices deviate more from initial health perceptions . For decision-makers, this trend highlights the importance of understanding the factors that drive this deviation, such as social influence and habit formation, to better predict and potentially guide consumer behavior towards healthier choices .
