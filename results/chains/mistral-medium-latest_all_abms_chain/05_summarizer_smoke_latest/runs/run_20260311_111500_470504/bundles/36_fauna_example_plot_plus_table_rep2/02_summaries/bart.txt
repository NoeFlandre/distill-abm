 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains at this level until around 600 time steps . The number of deaths continues to rise steeply until around 1000 time steps, reaching approximately 200 deaths . After this steep increase, the rate of deaths starts to plateau, indicating a potential stabilization in the population dynamics .  The total energy of all animals in the simulation starts at approximately 16,667 units and declines rapidly over the first 500 time steps . The energy levels drop sharply as the animals consume energy for their daily activities and face predation pressure . By around 2,311 steps, the total . energy of female animals reaches zero, indicating the complete depletion of the female animal population .  The simulation shows a consistent downward trend in the total energy of prey animals . The percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable throughout the simulation . The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant trends in different windows, with initial increasing trends followed by decreasing trends in later windows .
