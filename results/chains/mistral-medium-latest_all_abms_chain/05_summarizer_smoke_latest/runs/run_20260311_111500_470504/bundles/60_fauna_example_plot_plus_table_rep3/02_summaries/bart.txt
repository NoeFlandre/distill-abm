 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains at this level for an initial period . The data exhibits several oscillation periods, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the birth rate . The mean number of deaths across the simulation runs is approximately 201, with a median of 233 . The rolling Mann-Kendall test results show significant increasing trends in the early phases of the simulation .  The total energy of male animals begins at 12,500 units and exhibits a rapid decline within the first 500 time steps . After approximately 1000 steps, the number of female animals stabilizes at a very low level, with minor oscillations continuing intermittently . After 1,500 time steps the energy levels gradually approach zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy resources among the female prey population . The final stages of the simulation show the population hovering around zero .  The percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable for the initial time steps . The statistical analysis shows a consistent decreasing trend in energy levels across different time windows . The overall trend shows that after an initial decline and subsequent recovery, the system reaches a relatively stable state with minor oscillations .
