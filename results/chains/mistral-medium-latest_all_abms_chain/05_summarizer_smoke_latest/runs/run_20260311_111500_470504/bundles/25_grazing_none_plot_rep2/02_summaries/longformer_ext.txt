The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system. The extension adds learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, to study adaptive management strategies. The model's context is rooted in understanding the long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types and their decision-making processes under various ecological and climatic conditions. The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems.
