agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . despite the introduction and potential influence of alternative milk types, whole milk continues to be chosen consistently . this could imply that factors such as habit, social influence, and initial perceptions significantly contribute to the sustained preference for whole milk among the agents . habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk shows a gradual increase over the time steps in the simulation . by step 10, the habit factor reaches around 1.1, indicating a slow but steady increase in the habit formation . this trend suggests that over time, the agents in the model are increasingly influenced by their habits when choosing . a shift in the environmental values implied by the agents' milk choices quickly aligns and stabilizes . this suggests that interventions or changes in the early stages can have a substantial impact on aligning consumer choices with environmental values .
