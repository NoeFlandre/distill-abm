 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at zero and increases steadily over the entire simulation period, reaching a final value of 32,000 . Several peaks and valleys are observed throughout the simulation, indicating fluctuations in consumption trends . The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across multiple time windows, reinforcing steady and sustained approach to milk consumption .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents begins at a very low value of 5.29 units in 1974 . The consumption then increases rapidly, indicating a significant shift in consumer preferences and behaviors . For decision makers, these trends imply that interventions aimed at promoting healthier milk choices can have a significant impact over time .
