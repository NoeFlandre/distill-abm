The average number of livestock destocked across all households in the simulation exhibits a notable decreasing trend over time. Initially, the destocking rate starts at 0 and rapidly increases, reaching a peak of approximately 6.61 livestock at time step 3. Following this peak, there is a sharp decline to around 3.33 livestock by time step 8. The trend continues to decrease, albeit with smaller fluctuations, reaching a value of approximately 0.31 livestock by the final time step of 100. The data shows several inflection points, indicating changes in the rate of destocking, particularly around time steps 6, 21, 23, and 27. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms significant decreasing trends in the initial phases of the simulation, with periods of no significant trend and a slight increasing trend observed in later phases. The dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps suggests cyclical patterns in the destocking rates. For decision makers, these trends imply that initial conditions and early time steps are critical in understanding and managing livestock destocking rates. The rapid initial increase followed by a general decline suggests that adaptive management strategies may be effective in reducing destocking over time, but the cyclical patterns indicate the need for continuous monitoring and adjustment of strategies.
