The data from the repeated simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) reveal several key trends in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the number of female animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of female animals has decreased to roughly 80. This downward trend continues, with the number of female animals dropping to about 40 by 1000 steps. The decline becomes more gradual as time progresses, with the number of female animals reaching near zero by around 2000 steps. Beyond this point, the number of female animals remains very low, approaching extinction. 

For decision makers, these trends suggest that under the given parameters, the population of female prey animals is under significant pressure and tends toward extinction within a relatively short time frame. This insight could be crucial for understanding the dynamics of megaherbivore populations under similar environmental and hunting pressures, and for developing strategies to mitigate the risk of extinction.
