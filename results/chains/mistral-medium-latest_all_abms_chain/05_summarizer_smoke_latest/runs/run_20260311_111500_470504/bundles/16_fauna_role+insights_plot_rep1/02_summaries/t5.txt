the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . by 1000 steps, the total energy of foragers has decreased to approximately -10,000 units by 1500 steps . over time, the system reaches a new equilibrium where the rate of hunting stabilizes . this could inform strategies for sustainable hunting practices and conservation efforts to ensure that prey populations are not depleted beyond recovery . the cumulative number of postpartum females killed increases from 0 to approximately 100 steps . male prey animals face high mortality rates, which could be due to a combination of hunting pressure, environmental factors, and internal dynamics of the animal population . this trend highlights the potential vulnerability of male animals under the simulated conditions and the need to consider conservation strategies . rapid depletion of energy resources suggests conservation efforts may need to focus on reducing hunting pressure, enhancing food availability, or both to prevent population collapse . the total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent trend across simulations .
