The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant throughout the simulation period. Starting at a value of approximately 0.0074, it shows no variation or trend across all time steps, maintaining this value consistently until the end of the simulation at time step 100. The lack of variance or significant peaks and valleys indicates a stable and unchanging initial disposition among social learning agents regarding their willingness to deviate from the recommended number of livestock. For decision makers, this implies a predictable and uniform risk attitude among agents, which could simplify the modeling of household behaviors and their impacts on the grazing system. The stability suggests that external factors or internal learning mechanisms within the model do not influence the initial risk attitude, providing a consistent baseline for evaluating other dynamic aspects of the system.
