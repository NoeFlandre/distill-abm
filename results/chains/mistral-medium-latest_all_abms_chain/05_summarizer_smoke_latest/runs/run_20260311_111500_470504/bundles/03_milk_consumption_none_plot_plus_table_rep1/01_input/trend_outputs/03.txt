The agent-based model simulates the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The model tracks the total number of times agents choose whole milk over the simulation period. Initially, the total number of whole milk choices starts at zero. As the simulation progresses, the total number of whole milk choices increases steadily. By the end of the simulation period, the total number of whole milk choices reaches approximately 2194.57. The mean total number of whole milk choices across the simulation is around 1130.88, with a median of 1158.97. The standard deviation is 654.69, indicating variability in the number of whole milk choices over time. The minimum value remains zero at the start, while the maximum value is observed at the end of the simulation. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test shows a consistent increasing trend in the total number of whole milk choices across different time windows, with significant positive slopes ranging from approximately 64 to 76. This indicates a steady increase in the adoption of whole milk choices over time. The model identifies inflection points at specific time steps, suggesting changes in the rate of increase. The oscillation analysis indicates periodic fluctuations in the latter part of the simulation, with dominant periods and oscillation indices identified towards the end of the time steps.
