The simulation results indicate a consistent and gradual increase in the inequality of healthy livestock distribution among households over the 100 timesteps. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of approximately 0, suggesting an equal distribution of healthy livestock across households. Throughout the simulation, the Gini coefficient exhibits a steady upward trend, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 by the final timestep. This trend is statistically significant across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, indicating a persistent and consistent increase in inequality over time. The absence of detected peaks and valleys suggests a smooth and continuous rise in inequality without any notable fluctuations or abrupt changes. Inflection points are observed at various timesteps, notably around timesteps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, which may indicate subtle shifts in the rate of increase. Change points detected at timesteps 51, 71, and 100 further support the notion of a gradual but steady increase in inequality. The oscillation indices towards the latter part of the simulation suggest some periodic behavior, although the dominant period of oscillation is relatively long at 49 timesteps. Overall, the simulation demonstrates a clear and steady trend towards increasing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households.
