agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . Initially, at time step 0, the total number of whole milk choices starts close to zero . this upward trend continues, and by time step 20, the total total number reaches 2500 . trend indicates a growing preference for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk over time . habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk reaches around 1.04 by the tenth step . by the fifteenth step, the habit factor begins to rise more noticeably . this trend indicates a strengthening habit towards the consumption of milk . for decision makers, this implies that habits related to whole milk become more entrenched as time progresses .
