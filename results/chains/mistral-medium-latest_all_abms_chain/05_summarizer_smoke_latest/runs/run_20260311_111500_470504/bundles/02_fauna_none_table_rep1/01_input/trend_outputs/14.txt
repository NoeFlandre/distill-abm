The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a relatively stable trend over the simulation period, with minor fluctuations. Initially, the percentage of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33.0002% and ends very close to this value at 32.9979%. Throughout the simulation, the mean percentage of vegetated patches is around 32.6558%, with a median of 32.9696%, indicating that the data is fairly consistent and centered around this mean value. The standard deviation of 0.9503 suggests minor variability in the data. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, indicating a notable dip in vegetation coverage at this point. The maximum value remains at the initial value of 33.0002%. The analysis of peaks and valleys shows very minor fluctuations, with peaks and valleys having almost identical values, suggesting a highly stable system with minimal oscillations. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates periods of both increasing and decreasing trends, but these changes are minimal and significant. The dominant oscillation period is 63 ticks, indicating a regular, albeit minor, cyclical pattern in the vegetation coverage. Overall, the vegetation coverage in the model remains remarkably stable with slight periodic variations.
