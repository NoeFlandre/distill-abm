agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the agent-based model simulates the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . the model tracks the total number of times agents choose whole milk over the simulation period . as the simulation progresses, there is a consistent and steady increase in the number of whole milk choices made by the agents . the trend continues to show minor fluctuations with local peaks and valleys . by the 30th time step, the mean quantity of whole milk consumed drops to about 335.18 units, and it continues to decrease, reaching its minimum value of approximately 253.10 units . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various windows, with significant positive slopes . the oscillation indices suggest periodic minor fluctuations, but these do not disrupt the overall increasing pattern .
