The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time. Initially, the mean consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29. This value rapidly increases in the early time steps, indicating a swift initial adoption of alternative milk types. By the 11th time step, the mean consumption reaches a peak of approximately 1265.47, suggesting a significant shift in consumer behavior towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. Following this peak, the consumption levels exhibit minor fluctuations but generally stabilize around a high mean value of approximately 1220.21, which is also the median value. These fluctuations are evident in the identified peaks and valleys, such as the peak at time step 13 with a value of 1240.35 and the valley at time step 12 with a value of 1233.62. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early and later stages of the simulation, with notable increasing trends in windows 1, 2, and 6. The periods of no significant trend, particularly in the middle time steps, suggest a stabilization phase where the consumption levels plateau. The dominant oscillation period of 15 time steps further supports the presence of periodic fluctuations around the stabilized mean. Overall, the model demonstrates an initial rapid increase in the adoption of alternative milk types, followed by a stabilization phase with minor periodic fluctuations.
