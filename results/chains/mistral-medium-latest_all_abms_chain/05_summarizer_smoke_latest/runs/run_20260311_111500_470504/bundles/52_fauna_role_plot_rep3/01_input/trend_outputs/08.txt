The data from the repeated simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of male prey animals over time. Initially, the number of male animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of male animals has decreased to roughly 80. The decline continues gradually, reaching about 40 male animals by 1000 steps. Beyond this point, the rate of decline slows, and the number of male animals approaches zero around 1500 steps. After 1500 steps, the number of male animals remains at zero for the remainder of the simulation. This trend indicates a gradual extinction process influenced by the various parameters set in the model, including hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and animal life history traits.
