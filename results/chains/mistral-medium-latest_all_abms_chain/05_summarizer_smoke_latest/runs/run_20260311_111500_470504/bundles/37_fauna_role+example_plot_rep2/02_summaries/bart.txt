 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory . Ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values and processing costs for males and females . Animals can reproduce and users can control sex ratios and reproduction probability .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains constant until around 750 time steps . The number of male animals in the simulation begins at approximately 120 individuals . After around 500 time steps, there is a rapid increase in the number of deaths, reaching approximately 200 deaths by around 1000 time steps. After this rapid increase, the rate of deaths begins to plateau, indicating a stabilization in the population dynamics .  The total number of animals in the simulation starts at 250, reflecting the initial population set in the model . Over the first 500 time steps, there is a rapid decline in total energy, dropping to around 2,000 units . This sharp decrease suggests a significant reduction in the male population, potentially due to hunting pressure, environmental stress, or a combination of both factors .
