 The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . The simulation consists of 1,000 agents representing consumers situated within a social network . Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms .  The average quantity of whole milk consumed per agent begins at a baseline of 26.81 units at the onset of the simulation . The aggregate count of alternative milk trials initiates at zero and climbs consistently to approximately 25,644 over the 33 recorded time steps . This represents a continuous accumulation of agent choices for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk .  The overall pattern demonstrates a transition from high habitual consumption to a stabilized low level driven by the interplay of individual disposition and network-based social conformity . Agents with higher social blindness or stronger habit retention resist switching, while those more susceptible to social influence continue to abandon whole milk, creating a mixed equilibrium state .  By the final time step 32, the habit factor reaches a maximum of 1.5879. This sustained increase across the simulation horizon demonstrates a successful evolution in consumer behavior dynamics . This evolution reflects the underlying consumer behavior where initial homogeneity gives way to stratification due to habit formation and social influence . The population has segmented into distinct preference clusters by the conclusion .
