RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average household risk attitude is recorded at a fixed value of 0.0074 from the beginning to the end of the simulation period. There are no observed fluctuations, trends, or changes in this metric over the entire duration. The average reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits a distinct non-linear trajectory. The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and exhibits a consistent upward trend across the simulation duration. The average cumulative number of livestock destocked by households starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 100 time steps. The overall trend suggests a gradual decline in the prevalence of non-learning agents over the simulation period.
