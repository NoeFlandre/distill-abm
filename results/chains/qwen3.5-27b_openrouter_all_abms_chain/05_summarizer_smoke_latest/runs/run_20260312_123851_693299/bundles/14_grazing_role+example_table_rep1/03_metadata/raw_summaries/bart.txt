 Simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors . The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time . The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock .  The average available green biomass initiates at 45,000 units . During the initial phase, the metric experiences a decline, reaching a minimum of 42,057 units at time step 27 . By time step 33, the trajectory shifts significantly upward, with trend analysis confirming a positive slope that persists through the remainder of the simulation .  The population of households adhering to the E-RO strategy begins the simulation at a mean count of 20.0.0 . The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across households initiates at zero and demonstrates a sustained upward trend over the entire simulation duration . The low variance in the latter stages of the simulation reflects a mature state where social influence on livestock decisions is fully established .
