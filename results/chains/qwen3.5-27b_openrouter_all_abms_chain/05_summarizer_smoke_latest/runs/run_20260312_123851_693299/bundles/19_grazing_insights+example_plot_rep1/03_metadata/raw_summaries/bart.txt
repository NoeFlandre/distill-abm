 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The mean household risk attitude for extension agents begins near 0.0 at the start of the simulation . The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at approximately 45,000 units . In the initial phase, from time step 0 to about time step 20, the biomass levels generally decline slightly or remain stable . This early decrease suggests an initial period of overgrazing or resource depletion .  The average number of livestock destocked by households begins near zero and rises sharply in the first 5 time steps, peaking at approximately 7.5 livestock per household . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero across all simulation runs and begins to rise sharply between time steps 5 and 10 . After time step 60, the increase in the Gini coefficient accelerates significantly, reaching values as high as 0.09 by time step 100 .  The number of households following the E-RO strategy begins with an average of 20 households at the start of the simulation . Within the first 5 time steps, there is a rapid divergence among the simulations, with some reaching values as high as 38 and others falling as low as 10 .
