The mean household initial risk attitude for all social learning agents remains constant across all 100 time steps. Multiple distinct levels of risk attitudes are observed, ranging approximately from -0.11 to 0.18, indicating a heterogeneous distribution of risk dispositions among the agents from the start. Since these initial risk attitudes do not change over time, they serve as a static determinant of how agents perceive uncertainty and deviate from recommended livestock numbers throughout the simulation. Decision makers should recognize that variability in risk attitudes exists inherently among agents and could influence how uniformly strategies or recommendations are adopted, even if individual dispositions do not evolve.
