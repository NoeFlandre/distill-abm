The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed shows a steep initial rise across all simulation runs, with values increasing rapidly from zero to approximately 60 by step 200. By step 500, the majority of simulations reach a cumulative count between 100 and 120. The rate of increase slows significantly after step 500, and most simulations stabilize near a plateau between 110 and 125 by step 1500. The variation among runs suggests that stochasticity in forager movement and prey distribution influences the exact timing and total number of postpartum females hunted.
