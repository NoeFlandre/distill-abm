The simulation consists of 1,000 agents representing consumers situated within a social network. Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms. Simulated average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a profound upward trajectory over the 33 recorded time steps. Simulation results demonstrate a robust and monotonic increase in the cumulative selection of whole milk across the 33 recorded time steps. The rate of increase, measured by slope, remains relatively stable, ranging from 76.199 in the early phase to 64.067 in the later phase. No local peaks or valleys were detected, confirming the consistency of the growth trajectory throughout the period. The average quantity of whole milk consumed per agent exhibits a marked downward trajectory across the 33 time steps analyzed. For decision makers, this trajectory underscores that while entrenched habits initially protect market share, prolonged exposure to alternative health narratives and social influence eventually overcomes inertia. The British milk market shift from whole to lower-fat varieties is a gradual process characterized by an initial plateau followed by accelerated habit formation. Oscillatory behavior is present with a dominant period of 15.00, evidenced by activity indices between 17 and 30.
