Context and goals : the overall objective of the agent - based model is to reproduce adoption behaviours of milk consumption by the british public (1974 - 2005), by replicating individual preferences and decision factors. Decision - making follows a basic structure of : agent perception of choice characteristics, the triggering, or not, of disposition to consider alternatives, a set of scored choice functions made up of the perceived characteristics and modulated by habit and social influence, and finally, choice evaluation where agents consider the impact of their choices and may adjust their future decisions. Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice. Alt - health - mean - initial : range 1. Alt - env - mean - initial : range 0. Cognitive - dissonance - threshold : range 0. Mean whole milk consumption starts at approximately 2, 650 units per agent at the beginning of the simulation, maintaining this level for two initial time steps. A slight increase occurs around step 19, reaching approximately 780 units, followed by another gradual decrease to about 650 units by step 25. The overall trend indicates a substantial reduction in whole milk consumption over the simulation period, with periods of rapid decline interspersed with brief periods of stabilization. From step 29 to the end of the simulation at step 31, the consumption rate slows and plateaus, fluctuating slightly above 1450 units. The data exhibits a steady upward trend in the total number of skimmed or semi - skimmed milk choices over the course of 32 time steps. By step 30, the cumulative choices are close to 25, 000, and by the final recorded step of 32, the total number of skimmed or semi - skimmed milk choices is approximately 26, 000. This upward trend continues consistently, with the total choices reaching approximately 10, 000 by step 10, 20, 000 by step 20, and exceeding 30, 000 by step 30. The mean whole milk quantity starts at approximately 2, 650 at step 0 and shows a sharp decline, reaching around 1, 250 by step 5. A minor increase is observed around step 18, where the value rises to approximately 750, before decreasing again. A rapid increase is observed between steps 1 and 11, where consumption levels rise from approximately 100 to a peak of roughly 1250 units. Between steps 19 and 27, the consumption levels stabilize and oscillate within a narrow range around 1220 units. Starting at step 28, a second upward trend emerges, continuing through step 32, where consumption reaches a final value of approximately 1450 units. Following this initial rapid increase, the deviation continues to grow but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 0. The trend indicates that the diversity in choice function scores among agents increases rapidly at the beginning of the simulation and then plateaus, suggesting that initial heterogeneity in decision - making factors quickly establishes and then remains relatively stable over the course of the simulation. This trend suggests that over time, there is an increasing discrepancy between the health values agents hold and the health values implied by their milk consumption choices. The mean value environmental deviation exhibits a sharp increase from step 0, where the value is approximately 0, to around step 4, where it reaches approximately 11. After step 4, the rate of increase slows down, and the value gradually stabilizes, reaching approximately 13. 5 by step 10. From step 10 onwards, the mean value environmental deviation remains relatively constant, hovering around 13. 5 through step 31. This indicates a rapid adjustment period followed by a stable state in the deviation between agents'held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices.
