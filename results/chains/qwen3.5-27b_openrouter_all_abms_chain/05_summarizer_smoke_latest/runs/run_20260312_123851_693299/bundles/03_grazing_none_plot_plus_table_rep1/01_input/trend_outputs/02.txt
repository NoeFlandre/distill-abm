The average household risk attitude exhibits a distinct three-phase trajectory over the simulation period. Initially, from the start through approximately timestep 25, the metric demonstrates a significant upward trend, rising from a mean starting value of 0.0074 to a peak near 0.3814. This phase is characterized by high volatility and a sharp divergence among individual agents, indicating that households are rapidly increasing their risk attitudes following the simulation initialization. A structural change occurs around timestep 33, where the aggregate trend shifts from increasing to decreasing. Between timesteps 33 and 70, the average risk attitude declines steadily, moving from the peak levels down to approximately 0.19. During this decline, the spread between the most and least risk-tolerant agents remains pronounced but stabilizes relative to the earlier phase. From timestep 70 to the conclusion at timestep 100, the data indicates a stabilization phase where the aggregate average fluctuates without a significant directional trend, maintaining a value around 0.1911. Throughout the simulation, the distribution of risk attitudes evolves from a clustered initialization around 0.5 to a heterogeneous spread, with some agents converging toward highly risk-seeking behaviors (near 0.8) while others shift toward risk-averse or negative risk attitudes (below -0.3).
