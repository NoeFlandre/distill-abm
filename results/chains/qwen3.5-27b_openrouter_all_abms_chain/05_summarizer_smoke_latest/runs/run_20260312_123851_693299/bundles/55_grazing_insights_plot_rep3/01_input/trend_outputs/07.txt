The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0 across all simulations at the initial time step, indicating an equal distribution of healthy livestock among households. A gradual increase in inequality begins shortly after, with the coefficient rising to approximately 0.02 to 0.03 by time step 40. The rate of increase accelerates significantly from time step 40 to 80, with the Gini coefficient reaching between 0.05 and 0.08 across different simulation runs. By time step 100, the inequality stabilizes within a range of approximately 0.03 to 0.095. This trend suggests that while the distribution of healthy livestock remains relatively balanced in the early stages of the simulation, it becomes increasingly uneven over time. This widening gap may result from differential learning capabilities and risk attitudes among households, leading to varying herd management outcomes. Decision-makers should consider these dynamics to support equitable resource distribution and to prevent potential livelihood disparities.
