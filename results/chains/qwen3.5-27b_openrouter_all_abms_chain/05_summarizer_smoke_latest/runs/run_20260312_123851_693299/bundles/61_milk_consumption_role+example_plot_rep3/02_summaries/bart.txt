The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005. The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties. Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms. The total number of milk choices made by all agents begins at zero at time step 0 and increases linearly as the simulation progresses. By step 5, the count reaches approximately 1,000, indicating a gradual adoption of lower-fat milk options. This steady increase suggests a sustained adoption of healthier milk options driven by evolving consumer perceptions and social influences. The trajectory reflects a consistent and accelerating influence of habit formation on the preference for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the simulated period. The steady and nearly uniform growth across repeated simulations indicates a robust pattern of habit development in the population of consumers represented in the model.
