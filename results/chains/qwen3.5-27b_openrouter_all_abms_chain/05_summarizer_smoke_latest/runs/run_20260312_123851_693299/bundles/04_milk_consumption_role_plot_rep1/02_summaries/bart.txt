The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005. The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties. Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms. The total number of milk choices made by all agents exhibits a consistent linear increase over time. At the initial step, the total choices start near zero at the initial time step. The total choices gradually rise, surpassing 5,000 by step 10. The data suggests that as the simulation progresses, habits increasingly reinforce the choice of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, aligning with the broader societal shift observed in milk consumption patterns. The mean value environmental deviation exhibits a rapid increase in the initial phase of the simulation, starting from 0 at step 0.0. The trend then slows, with the deviation hitting roughly 12 by step 4 and 13 by step 6. After step 6, the growth tapers off significantly, and the metric stabilizes.
