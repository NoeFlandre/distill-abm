 The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005 . The simulation consists of 1,000 agents representing consumers situated within a social network . Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms . The data demonstrates a robust long-term transition from high whole milk consumption to very low levels .  The average value across the entire series was 1130.8818, with the median recorded at 1158.9667 . The statistical analysis confirmed an increasing trend with high significance (p=0.027) across all rolling windows, with a slope that gradually decreased from 70.808 in the early steps to 64.067 in the final steps . No local peaks or valleys were identified, indicating a monotonic progression without reversals .  The mean percentage difference in choice function scores across agents exhibits a consistent and statistically significant upward trend over the simulation period . The data indicates a change point at time step 15, suggesting a shift in the underlying dynamics of habit formation or reinforcement . The overall trajectory suggests that as the simulation progresses, the influence of habit strength on the preference for lower-fat milk varieties increases .
