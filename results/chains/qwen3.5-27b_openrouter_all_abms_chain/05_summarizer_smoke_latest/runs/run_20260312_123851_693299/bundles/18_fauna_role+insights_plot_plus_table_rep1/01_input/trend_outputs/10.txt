The total number of prey animals declined consistently across all 120 simulation runs. The aggregate mean started at 166.67 at the first recorded time step and reached zero by the final time step of 2311. The data exhibits a rapid, steep decrease during the initial 500 time steps, reducing the population from approximately 166.67 to below 10. Following this initial collapse, the rate of decline slowed significantly, characterized by minor fluctuations where the mean population hovered between roughly 2.0 and 11.5 between time steps 700 and 1030. Statistical analysis confirms a statistically significant decreasing trend across all initial time windows. While later windows (specifically time steps 1392–1623 and 1856–2087) detected statistically significant but numerically negligible increases in the slope, these occurred when the population was effectively near extinction, and the overall trajectory remained downward until the population was zero. The data indicates that the model configuration results in the guaranteed and relatively rapid extinction of the megafauna.
