The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. Decision-making follows a basic structure of: agent perception of choice characteristics, the triggering, or not, of disposition to consider alternatives, a set of scored choice functions made up of the perceived characteristics and modulated by habit and social influence. The data shows a consistent overall downward trend, with a median consumption value of 816.85 units across all time steps. This pattern mirrors the historical substitution of whole milk with lower-fat alternatives driven by changes in consumer preferences, health awareness, and social dynamics captured by the model. The mean habit factor for whole milk is at its minimum value of 1.0 at the start of the simulation. A sharp increase occurs immediately, reaching approximately 1.02 by the second step. By time step 32, consumption of whole milk reaches a peak value of 1456.60 units.
