The temporal evolution of the simulation reveals a distinct upward trajectory in the number of households adopting social learning behaviors within the grazing system. Beginning at zero at the onset, the count demonstrates a statistically significant increasing trend, confirmed by rolling Mann-Kendall tests across all analyzed windows with p-values of 0.000. A primary inflection point occurs at timestep 7, marking the initial acceleration of adoption, followed by a structural break detected around timestep 40. The mean count rises to a peak of approximately 32.07 households by timestep 63. Subsequent observations indicate the system stabilizes near this level for the remainder of the 100-timestep horizon. Given a total agent population of 60, this saturation point implies that approximately 53% of the farming community eventually engages in social learning to inform their grazing management strategies. While there is moderate inter-run variability with a standard deviation of approximately 8.9, the aggregate behavior consistently converges to this stable equilibrium state, indicating a robust diffusion of the social learning dynamic within the agent population.
