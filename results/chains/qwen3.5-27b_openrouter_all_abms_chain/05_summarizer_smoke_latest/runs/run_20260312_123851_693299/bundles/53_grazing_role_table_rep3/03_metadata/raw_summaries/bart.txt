 Simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors . The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time . The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock .  Simulation monitors the average available green biomass across all patches over 100 time steps . At the onset of the simulation, the biomass level is recorded at 45,000 units . During the initial phase, the pasture undergoes a period of depletion, reaching a minimum value of 42,057 at timestep 27 . During this period, grazing pressure initially outpaced grass regeneration .  Simulation tracks the average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households over a 100-timestep period . The Gini coefficient representing inequality in livestock distribution begins near zero and exhibits a sustained increase over the 100 time steps . The simulation concludes at time step 100 with an average destocking value of 0.31 .
