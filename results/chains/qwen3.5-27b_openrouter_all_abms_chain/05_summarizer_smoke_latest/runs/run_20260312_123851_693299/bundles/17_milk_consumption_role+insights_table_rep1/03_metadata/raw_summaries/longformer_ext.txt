Context and Goals: This agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005. The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties. We aim to explore how individual perception, established habits, and social influence drive decision-making processes. The model measures the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person to validate against observed macro-level data. The simulation output indicates the average weekly consumption of each milk type per agent across a thirty-three step period representing the years 1974 to 2005.
