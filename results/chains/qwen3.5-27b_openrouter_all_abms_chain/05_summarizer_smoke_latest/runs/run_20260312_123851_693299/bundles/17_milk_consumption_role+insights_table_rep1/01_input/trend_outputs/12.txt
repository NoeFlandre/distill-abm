The simulation output for the mean deviation between held environmental values and consumption choices demonstrates a rapid divergence from baseline conditions. Starting at a value of 0.0 at the initial step, the metric rises sharply to reach a maximum of 13.4756 by time step 13. Following this peak, the system stabilizes, with the deviation oscillating narrowly between 13.41 and 13.45 for the remainder of the 33 observed steps. Statistical analysis confirms a significant increasing trend in the initial phase (p=0.027), which transitions into a non-trend stationary state for the majority of the simulation period. Within the context of UK milk consumption, this trajectory suggests that while agents may initially align their values with their choices, behavioral factors such as strong habit formation (incumbent-initial-habit=10) and social network effects (Watts-Strogatz network) quickly decouple purchasing decisions from underlying environmental values. For decision-makers, this implies that sustained misalignment is a structural outcome of consumer behavior under high habit strength, indicating that interventions focusing solely on value alignment are likely ineffective unless they simultaneously address the entrenched habits and social conformity norms driving the deviation.
