The RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant across the 100 timesteps, with no changes observed. The metric exhibits multiple distinct, flat lines, each representing a different agent group or simulation run. This suggests that agents' disposition to deviate from the recommended number of livestock remains fixed throughout the simulation. The Gini Coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at 0.000 at time step 0, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock among the 60 households. From time step 0 to approximately time step 10, the values remain near 0.000, showing that inequality in livestock distribution is minimal and stable. A gradual increase in inequality begins, with the values rising to approximately 0.030 by time step 40.
