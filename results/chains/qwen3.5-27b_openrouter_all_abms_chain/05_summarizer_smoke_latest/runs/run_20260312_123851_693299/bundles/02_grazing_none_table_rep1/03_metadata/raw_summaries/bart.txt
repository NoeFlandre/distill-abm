 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock begins at a low level of 0.0074 . During the initial phase, this value increases steadily, reflecting a growing divergence in household decision-making from the extension recommendations . This period of high deviation suggests that the heterogeneous population of learning agents, influenced by risk attitudes and environmental feedbacks, is adjusting their livestock strategies away from the baseline recommendations .  The average number of livestock destocked across all households exhibits a sharp initial decline followed by a period of relative stability and a slight recovery towards the end of the simulation period . At the onset, destocking activity is zero, but it spikes immediately, reaching a maximum value of 6.6144 at time step 3 .  The simulation initializes with 20 households adhering to the E-RO strategy . The rate of accumulation is not uniform, exhibiting distinct phases of deceleration . Minor fluctuations in the growth rate are observed around timesteps 45 to 75 . From timestep 66 onwards, the metric stabilizes with no significant trend .
