The total number of prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model starts at 250 and exhibits a rapid decline over time. By approximately 400 steps, the population decreases to around 50. The decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, with the population dropping to less than 10 by about 800 steps. By 1200 steps, the number of prey animals approaches zero, indicating near-extinction. A few simulations show a slightly delayed decline, with some populations persisting until around 1600 steps, but ultimately, all simulations result in the complete or near-complete extinction of prey animals by 2200 steps. This trend suggests that under the current model parameters, the combined effects of hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and herd dynamics lead to the inevitable extinction of the prey population. Decision makers should consider the impact of initial prey numbers, hunting strategies, and environmental factors to prevent such outcomes in real-world scenarios.
