The average reserve biomass in the grazing system demonstrates a distinct three-phase dynamic over the 100-step simulation period. Initially, the biomass starts at approximately 90,000 units and undergoes a decline, reaching a nadir of roughly 84,114 units at step 26. Following this initial drop, the system enters a recovery phase starting around step 48. This period is characterized by a significant and rapid increase in biomass, with the most pronounced growth rates occurring between steps 44 and 54. By step 100, the average reserve biomass stabilizes at a peak level of approximately 121,073 units, representing a net increase of over 30% from the starting value. The final trajectory shows the biomass leveling off, indicating the system has approached a new carrying capacity. These trends suggest that the combination of behavioral types and model parameters led to an initial phase of resource pressure followed by a substantial period of resource regeneration and stabilization.
