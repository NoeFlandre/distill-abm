Over the 100-timestep simulation, the average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock follows a distinct inverted V-shaped trajectory. The metric begins at a value of 0.0074, indicating strong initial alignment between farmer livestock numbers and extension recommendations. A minimum value of 0.0074 is recorded at timestep 3. Subsequently, the deviation increases significantly, supported by statistically significant upward trends observed in the first three decades of the simulation. The metric reaches its global maximum of 0.3814 at timestep 33. High values persist between timesteps 21 and 37, with peaks recorded at indices 25, 27, 29, 33, and 37. In the context of the grazing model, this divergence suggests that as households apply different learning strategies, their stocking decisions deviate substantially from the baseline recommendations. Following the peak at timestep 33, a significant decreasing trend emerges, continuing until approximately timestep 65. During this phase, the deviation falls to a local valley of 0.1913 at timestep 64. After timestep 66, the trend becomes statistically insignificant, indicating a stabilization of the metric. The simulation ends at timestep 100 with a final value of 0.1911. A change point is identified at timestep 50, coinciding with the transition from the decreasing phase to the stable phase. Ultimately, while farmers return closer to the recommendations after the mid-simulation peak, the final deviation remains higher than the initial state, reflecting a lasting adjustment in livestock management behavior.
