The average number of livestock destocked across households initiates at zero and escalates rapidly to a maximum of 6.6144 at timestep 3, reflecting an immediate need to reduce herd size following the simulation start. A statistically significant decreasing trend dominates the early phase, confirmed by rolling Mann-Kendall tests across intervals from timestep 0 to 32, driving the metric down to a local minimum of 0.5083 at timestep 27. The overall mean for the simulation duration is 0.9582, with a standard deviation of 1.4331 indicating substantial variability in destocking intensity. Between timesteps 33 and 65, the trajectory exhibits periods of stability with no significant trend, followed by a statistically significant increasing trend from timestep 66 to 76. Despite this late-stage increase, the metric concludes at 0.3111 at timestep 100, remaining significantly lower than the initial peak. This pattern suggests that while initial livestock levels likely exceeded carrying capacity, leading to heavy destocking, the mixed behavioral strategies among farmers facilitated a recovery of pasture health or adjustment of herd sizes, resulting in sustained low levels of livestock removal for the majority of the simulation period.
