 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . The simulation uses both a theoretical grounding and empirical data to inform the ABM, with calibration performed against macro level data .  Simulated trajectory of average weekly consumption for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk reveals non-linear adoption pattern over 33 time steps . Consumption initiates at a baseline of 5.29 and undergoes a rapid expansion phase, reaching a local maximum of 1265.47 by time step 11 . Consumption metric stabilizes between time steps 12 and 24, oscillating within a range bounded by 1183 and 1240.35 .  At time step 0, the metric begins at 2654.81, consistent with the historical dominance of whole milk in 1974 . By time step 32, the value reaches a minimum of 253.10, illustrating the substantial shift towards skimmed and semi-skimmed alternatives . The decline is statistically significant across most temporal windows .  Researchers tracked the mean deviation between agents' held health values and the health values implied by their milk choices . Over the course of 33 time steps, the deviation demonstrates a persistent upward trend, culminating in a maximum value of 0.7251 at time step 32 . This trajectory suggests that as the simulation progresses, agents increasingly select milk varieties that do not align with their internal health assessments .
