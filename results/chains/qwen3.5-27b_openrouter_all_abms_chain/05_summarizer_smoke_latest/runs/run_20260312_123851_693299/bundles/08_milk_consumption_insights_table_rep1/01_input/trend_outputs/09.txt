The simulation records the mean habit factor applied to the choice function for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk alternatives across 33 time steps. At time step 0, the value begins at 1.0000. A minor local peak occurs at step 1 with a value of 1.0205, followed by a dip to a local minimum of 1.0167 at step 5. Inflection points are identified at steps 3 and 12. During the first five steps, there is no statistically significant trend. Starting from step 5, a significant increasing trend is observed that persists for the duration of the simulation. The growth rate accelerates notably between steps 10 and 14. A structural change point occurs at step 15. The factor continues to rise steadily, reaching its maximum value of 1.5879 at the final step 32. Within the context of milk adoption behaviors modeled from 1974 onwards, this trajectory indicates that the habitual influence supporting alternative milk choices strengthens as agents progress through the decision-making cycles. The initial stability suggests agents are anchored by their initial whole milk preferences. The subsequent sustained increase reflects the gradual modification of habits through evaluation and social influence, making the alternative options more habitual over time. Decision makers should note the initial inertia lasting approximately five steps, where habit does not favor the alternatives. Strategies to shift consumption patterns must persist beyond this early plateau to capitalize on the period where habit formation begins to actively reinforce the transition to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk.
