The mean habit factor for whole milk exhibits a consistent upward trajectory across all simulation runs. At the initial step, the value stands at 1.0, indicating no initial habit influence. From steps 2 to 32, the habit factor continues to rise steadily, reaching values between 1.05 and 1.06 by the final step. The variation among simulation runs remains relatively narrow, indicating robustness in the trend. This increasing habit factor implies that, over time, agents develop stronger habitual preferences for whole milk, which could make it more challenging to shift consumption patterns toward other milk varieties without targeted interventions.
