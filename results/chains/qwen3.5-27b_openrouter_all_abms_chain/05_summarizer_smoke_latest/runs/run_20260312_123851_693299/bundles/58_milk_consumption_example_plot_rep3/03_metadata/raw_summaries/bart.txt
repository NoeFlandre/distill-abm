 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice . Each choice function is modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The data demonstrates a clear trend of decreasing whole milk consumption over time, consistent with the model's goal of simulating the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . The total number of milk choices made by all agents begins at zero at time step 0 and increases linearly as the simulation progresses . The mean whole milk quantity starts at approximately 2,650 at the initial time step .  The trends demonstrate a two-stage adoption process, reflecting the interplay of habit, social influence, and evolving perceptions in shaping milk consumption behavior . The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents begins at zero at the start of the simulation .
