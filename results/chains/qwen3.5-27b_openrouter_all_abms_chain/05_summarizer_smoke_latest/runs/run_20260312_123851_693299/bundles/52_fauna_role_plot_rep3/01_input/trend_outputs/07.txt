The cumulative number of deaths among the prey population exhibits a consistent pattern across all simulations. Over the initial 500 steps, the death count rises steeply from 0 to approximately 180, indicating high early mortality from both natural causes and predation. Between steps 500 and 1500, the rate of increase slows, with deaths climbing to a range between 210 and 250. By the 1500th step, the majority of simulations stabilize, with cumulative deaths plateauing between 220 and 250. Beyond step 2000, the death counts remain largely static, with only minor fluctuations observed in some cases. These trends suggest that the megaherbivore population experiences intense mortality early in the simulation, followed by stabilization as the system reaches a quasi-equilibrium state driven by the interplay of hunting pressure and environmental constraints.
