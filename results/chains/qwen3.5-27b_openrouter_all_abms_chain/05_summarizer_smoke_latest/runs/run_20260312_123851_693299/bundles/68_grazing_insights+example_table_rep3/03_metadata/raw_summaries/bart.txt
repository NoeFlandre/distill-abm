 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at 45,000 units . During the initial phase of the simulation, specifically between time step 0 and time step 27, the biomass experiences a decline, reaching its lowest point of 42,057 units . By time step 100, the average reserve biomass reaches its peak value of 121,073, surpassing the initial starting conditions .  The average number of livestock destocked across households starts at zero and rises sharply, reaching a peak of 6.61 animals at time step 3 . By time step 9, the average drops to 3.37, and continues to decrease significantly through time step 32 . As agents adapt differently to environmental uncertainty, their herd sizes diverge, leading to increased disparity in asset ownership .  The average cumulative livestock destocked by households starts at zero and exhibits a persistent upward trajectory throughout the simulation . The number of households adhering to the E-RO strategy starts the simulation at 20 and rises slightly to a peak of 21.1 around the eighth time step . By the 100th time step, the count stabilizes at 14.7.7 .
