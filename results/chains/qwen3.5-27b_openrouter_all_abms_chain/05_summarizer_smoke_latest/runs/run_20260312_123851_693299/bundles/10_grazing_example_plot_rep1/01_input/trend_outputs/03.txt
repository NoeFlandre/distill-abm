The average amount of reserve biomass starts at approximately 90,000 units at time step 0. A general decline is observed in the initial phase, with values reaching a minimum of around 80,000 units between time steps 10 and 40. This decrease suggests a period of increased grazing pressure or limited vegetation growth, potentially linked to the initial adjustment period of households in managing their herds on private pasture patches. From time step 40 onwards, the reserve biomass shows a significant upward trend, increasing steadily to reach values between 110,000 and 128,000 units by time step 100. This recovery and growth phase may reflect the effectiveness of learning-by-doing and social learning processes adopted by the households, leading to better management practices and improved pasture conditions. By the end of the simulation, the reserve biomass stabilizes at these higher levels, indicating a more sustainable balance between livestock numbers and pasture capacity.
