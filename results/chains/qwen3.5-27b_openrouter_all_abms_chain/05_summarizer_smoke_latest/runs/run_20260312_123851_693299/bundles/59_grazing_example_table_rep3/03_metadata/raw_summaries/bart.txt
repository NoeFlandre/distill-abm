 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock begins at 0.0074, indicating strong initial adherence to extension guidelines . The average available green biomass across all patches initiates at 45,000 units . By the final time step 100, the average reserve biomass reaches its maximum recorded value of 121,072.59, surpassing initial conditions .  The number of households engaged in social learning starts at zero and increases steadily over the course of the simulation . The average cumulative number of livestock that have been destocked across all households begins at zero . The Gini coefficient demonstrates a consistent upward trajectory, concluding at a value of 0.0726 .  The number of households adhering to the E-RO strategy begins at 20.0 at the start of the simulation . This count exhibits a minor initial increase, reaching a peak of 21.1 at time step 8 . The subsequent steady decline implies that the pressure from ecological uncertainty drives a portion of the population away from static behavioral types .
