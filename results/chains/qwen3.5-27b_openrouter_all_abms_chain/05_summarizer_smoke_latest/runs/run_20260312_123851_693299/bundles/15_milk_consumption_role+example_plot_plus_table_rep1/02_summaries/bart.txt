The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005. The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties. Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms. The total number of milk choices made by all agents starts at 0.0000 and increases steadily to 32000 by the final time step. The trend is uniformly upward, with a constant slope of 1000.0000 per step, as identified across multiple time intervals using the Mann-Kendall test. The trend demonstrates a non-linear progression characterized by an initial rapid adoption, a period of stabilization, and a final surge in consumption. Despite the initial rapid formation of habits, the continued growth indicates that the agents' attachment to whole milk becomes progressively more entrenched. The curve exhibits a concave shape throughout the simulation, characterized by inflection points around steps 7, 12, and 28.
