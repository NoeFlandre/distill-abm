The total number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation duration. Starting at 20 at the initial time step, the count maintains this exact value across all 2312 time steps with zero variance. This static population trajectory is a direct result of the model configuration where foragers are immortal, eliminating mortality or recruitment mechanisms. For decision makers, this indicates that any changes in megafauna populations are not influenced by shifting human demographic pressures. Instead, extinction dynamics are driven entirely by the interaction between a fixed hunting force and environmental constraints, allowing for isolated analysis of predation intensity without the confounding variable of human population growth.
