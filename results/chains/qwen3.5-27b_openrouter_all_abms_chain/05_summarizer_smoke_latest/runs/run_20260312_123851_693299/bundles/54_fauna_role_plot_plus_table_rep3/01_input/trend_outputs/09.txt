The female megaherbivore population begins at an initial mean of 125 individuals and exhibits a rapid decline during the early simulation steps. A significant decrease occurs by the first 231 steps, where the population drops to approximately 72, representing a loss of about 42% of the initial cohort. This sharp reduction aligns with the model parameters of twenty immortal foragers and high seasonality (0.8), suggesting intense pressure from predation combined with seasonal resource fluctuations. By the 500th step, the mean population falls to 16, indicating that the majority of the female population has been removed from the system. The population continues to dwindle, reaching a median of 1 individual by the middle of the simulation timeline. Minor fluctuations are observed in the low population phase between steps 700 and 1000, with local peaks reaching approximately 6.5, but these do not sustain population recovery. Statistical analysis confirms a consistently significant decreasing trend across the majority of the simulation duration, with a rolling Mann-Kendall slope remaining negative and highly significant (p=0.000) for the first 1000 steps. The population reaches extinction, defined as a mean of 0, at the final step of the simulation. The dominant oscillation period of 63 steps suggests that environmental or foraging cycles drove short-term variability, yet the overarching dynamic was a monotonic collapse toward extinction.
