The simulation consists of 1,000 agents representing consumers situated within a social network. Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms. The data series exhibits an initial rapid increase in mean skimmed/semi-skimmed milk consumption. The mean weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at 2654.81 units and follows a consistent downward trajectory across the entire simulation period. The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents began at 5.29 units and exhibited a steep increase during the initial phase of the simulation. By the final time step 32, the aggregate count reaches 25,643.8333. The overall pattern indicates a growing influence of habit on the preference for lower-fat milk varieties over time. Decision-makers should recognize the increasing role of habit in shaping consumer choices, suggesting strategies to reinforce or leverage these habits to drive further adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk.
