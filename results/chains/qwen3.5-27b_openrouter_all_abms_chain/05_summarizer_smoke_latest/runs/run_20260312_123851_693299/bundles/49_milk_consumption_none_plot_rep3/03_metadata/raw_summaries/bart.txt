 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice . An agent's milk choice function is modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  At step 0, the mean weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent is 0 . A rapid increase begins immediately and continues through step 10, where consumption reaches approximately 1200 units . After step 27, there is a brief period of stability before a final sharp decline between time steps 27 and 30, where the average consumption falls to approximately 300 units . These trends reflect a significant and sustained reduction in whole milk consumption over time .  The data indicates a robust positive trajectory in the habit factor influencing the choice functions for these milk types over the simulated duration . The trend indicates that the diversity in choice function scores among agents increases significantly during the early steps and then levels off, suggesting a stabilization of decision-making dynamics over time .
