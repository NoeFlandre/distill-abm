The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0.0 at timestep 1 and rises steadily to 0.0726 by timestep 100, with an overall mean of 0.0302 and a median of 0.0229. The increase is significant, as confirmed by rolling Mann-Kendall tests across all 10-year windows (tau values between 0.978 and 1.000, p-values of 0.000). Key inflection points occur at timesteps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, where the rate of increase shifts slightly. Change points are detected at timesteps 51, 71, and 100, indicating transitions in the dynamics of inequality. While the general trend is monotonic, oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 49 years is observed, particularly evident in the final 15 timesteps (78–92). These patterns suggest that inequality in healthy livestock distribution grows systematically over time, with periodic fluctuations potentially linked to underlying environmental or behavioral cycles. For decision makers, this implies that without intervention, disparities in livestock health outcomes among households will likely widen, requiring policies to mitigate unequal access to resources or learning opportunities.
