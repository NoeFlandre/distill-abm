The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at approximately 45,000 units. Over the first 40 time steps, there is a noticeable decline in biomass, reaching a minimum value of around 40,000 units. This reduction likely reflects the initial grazing pressure exerted by the livestock as the farmers, operating under the E-RO (baseline) behavioral type, adjust their herds without significant learning effects. From step 40 onwards, a robust recovery in biomass is observed, with levels increasing rapidly and stabilizing between 60,000 and 64,000 units by step 80. The divergence in trajectories across simulation runs suggests that variations in farmer decision-making, particularly in adjusting livestock numbers in response to grass availability, contribute to differing levels of pasture recovery. For decision-makers, this highlights the importance of considering how initial grazing pressure impacts pasture health and the potential variability in recovery outcomes under baseline management strategies.
