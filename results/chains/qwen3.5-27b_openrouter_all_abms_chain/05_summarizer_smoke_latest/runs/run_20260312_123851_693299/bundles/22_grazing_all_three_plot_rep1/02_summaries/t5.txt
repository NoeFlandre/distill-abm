The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. Satisficing-threshold: Range from 0.0 to 100.0. We set it to 0. Social-susceptibility: range from 0 to 1.0. By time step 50, the rate of increase slows, and most simulations stabilize between 110,000 and 125,000 units by the end of the simulation period. This plateau suggests that the system has reached a new equilibrium where the grazing pressure is balanced with the regenerative capacity of the grass. The average amount of actual available green biomass begins at approximately 45,000 units. By timestep 80, the Gini coefficient ranges between approximately 0.045 and 0.075. Some simulations show higher inequality. This trend suggests that certain learning and decision-making strategies lead to more unequal outcomes in livestock health distribution.
