The average reserve biomass across all patches begins near 90,000 units at the start of the simulation. Within the first 30 time steps, a slight decline occurs, with the lowest point reaching approximately 80,000 units around time step 30. From this point, a sustained and significant increase in biomass is observed, accelerating sharply between time steps 40 and 60, during which levels rise from roughly 95,000 to over 120,000 units. By time step 80, the growth rate slows as the biomass levels off, reaching a plateau between 110,000 and 128,000 units by the final time step of 100. These trends suggest that initial resource depletion transitions into a strong recovery phase, eventually stabilizing at a high equilibrium level, indicating that the system supports effective biomass regeneration under the modeled behavioral dynamics.
