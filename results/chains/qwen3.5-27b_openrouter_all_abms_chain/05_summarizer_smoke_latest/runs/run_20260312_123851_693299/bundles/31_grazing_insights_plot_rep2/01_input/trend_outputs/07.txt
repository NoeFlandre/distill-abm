The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0 across all simulations at time step 0, indicating an initially equal distribution. A gradual increase begins between steps 0 and 40, with most simulations remaining below 0.015. After step 40, the Gini coefficient accelerates rapidly, diverging into two distinct groups of trajectories. By step 60, values range approximately between 0.020 and 0.045. In the final 40 steps, the divergence widens significantly, resulting in Gini coefficients at step 100 ranging from approximately 0.030 to 0.095. The lower-bound simulations show a steady, modest increase in inequality, while the upper-bound simulations exhibit an exponential rise in disparity. This implies that while some households manage to maintain a relatively equitable distribution of livestock wealth, others experience substantial stratification, suggesting that learning behaviors (specifically E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1 under these parameters) combined with risk attitudes and ecological noise can lead to varied social outcomes.
