 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average total number of livestock, including healthy livestock and destocked animals, begins at 90 for all households . The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at approximately 45,000 units . By the end of the simulation at time step 100, the average reserve biomass is maintained at levels close to 120,000 .  The number of households following the E-RO strategy begins with an initial value of 20 for all simulation runs . The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock begins near zero at time step 0, indicating an initially equitable distribution of healthy livestock among the households . By step 60 to 100, the average number of livestock destocked stabilizes at or near zero for most simulation runs, though a few outliers show slight activity .
