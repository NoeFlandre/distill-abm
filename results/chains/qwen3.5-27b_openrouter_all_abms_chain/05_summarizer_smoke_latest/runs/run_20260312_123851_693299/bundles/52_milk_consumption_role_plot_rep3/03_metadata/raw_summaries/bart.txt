 The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005 . The simulation consists of 1,000 agents representing consumers situated within a social network . Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms .  At the initial step, the total choices start near zero . By step 10, the cumulative total reaches approximately 10,000 choices . The linear growth suggests a steady rate of decision-making activity among the agents . The mean consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at zero in the initial time step .  In the initial phase of the simulation, the deviation has already risen sharply, reaching approximately 7.5 by step 1 . The trend then slows, with the deviation hitting roughly 12 by step 4 and 13 by step 6 . After step 6, the growth tapers off significantly, and the metric stabilizes . This pattern indicates that the discrepancy between the agents' internal environmental values and the environmental implications of their milk choices .
