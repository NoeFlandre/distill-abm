 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average household risk attitude exhibits a distinct phased evolution over the simulation timeline . During the initial phase, specifically from the start through approximately time step 22, the risk attitude demonstrates a sharp and consistent increasing trend, rising from a starting value of 0.0074 to a peak of approximately 0.3573 . For decision-makers, this indicates a stable baseline in the predisposition of social learning agents towards risk-taking, which does not evolve based on the simulation dynamics .  The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0.0 at time step 0 and rises to 0.0726 by time step 100 . The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households increases consistently over the simulation period . The number of households practicing social learning started at zero and showed a consistent upward trend across the simulation duration .
