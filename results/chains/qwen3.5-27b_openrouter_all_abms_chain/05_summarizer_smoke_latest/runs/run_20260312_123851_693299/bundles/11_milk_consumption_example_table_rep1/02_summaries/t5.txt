The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. The rate of decline moderates around index 13, and the trajectory becomes more variable between indices 15 and 30. Temporary peaks are observed at index 19 (773.96 units), index 27 (620.98 units), and index 30 (335.18 units) these fluctuations indicate periods where the interplay between habit persistence and social influence creates transient resistance to the substitution trend. This progression signifies a steady accumulation of 1,000 choices per simulation interval, aligning with the total agent count of 1,000. The trajectory remains uniform throughout the simulation run. Overarching dynamic demonstrates a population-level consolidation of habits around the lighter milk alternatives. The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores starts at 0.0000 at time step 0. This initial value indicates that all agents possess identical evaluations of the milk alternatives at the beginning of the simulation, consistent with the uniform initialization where whole milk holds a dominant position.
