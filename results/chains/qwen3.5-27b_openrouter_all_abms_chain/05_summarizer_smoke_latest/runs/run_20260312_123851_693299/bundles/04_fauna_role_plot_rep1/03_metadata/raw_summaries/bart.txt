 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape .  The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands shows a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation, with most of the hunting activity occurring within the first 500 steps . The rate of increase slows considerably after step 500, with the cumulative count leveling off to a range between 110 and 125 by step 1000 . Beyond step 1500, the number of male prey killed stabilizes .  Total energy of male animals begins at approximately 12,000 units at the start of the simulation . By step 1500, the number of animals is nearly zero, indicating that the megaherbivore population is effectively extinct . The percentage of green patches in the simulation initially stabilizes around 32% but shows significant drops at specific intervals . This trend suggests that the combined effects of human hunting pressure and environmental factors lead to the eventual extinction of the population within the simulation timeframe .
