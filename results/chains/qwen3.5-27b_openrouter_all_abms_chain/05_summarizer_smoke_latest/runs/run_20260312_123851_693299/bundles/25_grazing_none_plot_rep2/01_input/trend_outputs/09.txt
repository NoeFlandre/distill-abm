The average cumulative number of livestock destocked by households increases over time for all simulation runs. In the initial phase, from timestep 0 to 20, the destocking metric rises rapidly for all scenarios, reaching values between approximately 60 and 80. Following this initial surge, the rate of increase slows, and the trajectories diverge into two distinct patterns. Approximately half of the simulation runs exhibit a stabilization effect, where the cumulative destocking flattens significantly between timesteps 40 and 100, maintaining relatively constant levels ranging from 60 to 85. The remaining simulation runs continue to accumulate destocked livestock at a steady, near-linear rate throughout the simulation, surpassing the stabilized runs. By the final timestep of 100, the trajectories in this group span a wide range, with cumulative destocking totals reaching from approximately 90 to 145.
