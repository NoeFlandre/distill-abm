the RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . it implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences . the average initial risk attitude for social learning agents remains fixed at 0.0074 across the entire simulation duration, from time step 0 to 100 . there is no observable variation or trend, with a standard deviation of zero . this indicates that the baseline disposition of these agents regarding their willingness to deviate from recommended livestock numbers was established at initialization . trajectories settling near 41 while others reach above 60 . initial collapse highlights vulnerability in herd management during early stages . subsequent recovery and divergence suggest learning capabilities allow for resilience but may result in unequal outcomes across different households . households are quickly reducing their livestock holdings in response to resource constraints or risk aversion in the early stages of the simulation . the accumulation continues at a slower, more constant pace, indicating a sustained effort to manage herd sizes against the backdrop of the specified ecological parameters .
