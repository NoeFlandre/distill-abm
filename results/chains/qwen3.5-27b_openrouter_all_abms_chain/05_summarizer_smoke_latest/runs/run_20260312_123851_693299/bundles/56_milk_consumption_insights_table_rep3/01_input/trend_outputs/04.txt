The metric representing the total number of agent choices for skimmed or semi-skimmed milk demonstrates a consistent upward trajectory throughout the simulation. Starting at a value of 0.0 at time step 0, the metric rises steadily to reach 25,643.83 by time step 32. Statistical analysis confirms a significant increasing trend across all measured rolling windows, with the Mann-Kendall test returning a tau of 1.000 and a p-value of 0.027 in each interval. The rate of increase accelerates from an initial slope of 201.46 during the first five time steps to a stabilized rate of approximately 935 choices per step between time steps 10 and 29. Minor oscillations are detected in the final time steps from 27 to 31, yet the overall growth remains uninterrupted. In the context of the model, this trend reflects the gradual substitution of whole milk as agents overcome initial habit thresholds and respond to social influence and health perceptions. For decision makers, the stabilization of the growth rate suggests that policies encouraging alternative milk adoption can expect a predictable, long-term cumulative increase in consumption once the initial inertia is resolved.
