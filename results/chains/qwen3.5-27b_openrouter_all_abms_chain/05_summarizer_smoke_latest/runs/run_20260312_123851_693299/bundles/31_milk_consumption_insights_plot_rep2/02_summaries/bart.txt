The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice. An agent's milk choice function is modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices. The data indicate a steady upward trend in the cumulative number of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices over the simulation period. This trend indicates a significant shift in consumer preferences towards healthier milk options over time. Decision makers should consider these trends to understand how evolving consumer behavior can be influenced by targeted interventions that address health concerns and social norms. The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the simulation steps. The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents starts at 0.0 at step 0.
