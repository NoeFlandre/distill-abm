 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history .  The cumulative number of male prey killed exhibits a rapid upward trend from the start of the simulation, increasing from 0 to approximately 110 by time step 500 . Following this initial surge, the rate of increase decelerates significantly, transitioning into a phase of stabilization where the total remains between 110 and 120 . Decision makers should consider these trends when planning conservation strategies .  Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model begins at 125 individuals and undergoes a rapid decline, averaging 16.48 individuals across the simulation . The median population stabilizes at a low of 1.0, indicating that most simulations reach near-extinction levels quickly . Under the specified conditions of hunting pressure and environmental constraints, the female reproductive segment is unable to sustain a viable population .  The total energy available in all prey animals begins at 16666.67 and declines monotonically to 0.0 by the final time step . The eventual total energy reaching zero implies the complete depletion of the female prey population's energy reserves . Decision makers should consider that the initial conditions or parameter settings may have led to an unsustainable population dynamic in this scenario .
