The mean weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at 2654.81 units and follows a consistent downward trajectory across the entire simulation period, ending at 253.10 units. The aggregate trend is statistically significant, as indicated by multiple rolling Mann-Kendall tests showing a 'decreasing' trend (tau = -1.000, p = 0.027) for the initial and middle phases (indices 0–14 and 20–24). The decline is steepest during the early phase: between indices 5 and 9, the mean consumption drops at a rate of approximately -221.11 units per step, indicating a rapid abandonment of the incumbent product. An inflection occurs around index 13, where the rate of decline slows. A subsequent period of relative stability or weak trend is observed between indices 15 and 19, followed by renewed significant declines starting at index 20. The data exhibits a change point at index 32, marking the absolute minimum value. Implications for decision-makers include the observation that deep-seated habits and social norms are eventually overcome by shifting consumer perceptions over time, leading to a near-total market displacement of the incumbent product.
