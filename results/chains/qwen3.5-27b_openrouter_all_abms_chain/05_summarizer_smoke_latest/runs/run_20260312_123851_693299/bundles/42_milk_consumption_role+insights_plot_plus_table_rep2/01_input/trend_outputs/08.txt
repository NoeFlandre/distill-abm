The mean habit factor for whole milk exhibits a consistent and statistically significant upward trend throughout the simulation. The metric begins at a baseline value of 1.000 at the first time step. Following an initial sharp rise to approximately 1.020 by time step 1, the factor continues to increase gradually, reaching a terminal value of 1.050 by time step 32. The trajectory is monotonic with no detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady accumulation of habitual influence on the decision to purchase whole milk. Rolling Mann-Kendall analyses confirm this increasing trend with high significance (p < 0.05) across all sequential windows. The mean value over the observation period is 1.036 with a very low standard deviation of 0.011, suggesting a high degree of convergence among the agents. This implies that the habit mechanism effectively reinforces incumbent preferences, creating a compounding effect that strengthens the stickiness of whole milk consumption habits over time.
