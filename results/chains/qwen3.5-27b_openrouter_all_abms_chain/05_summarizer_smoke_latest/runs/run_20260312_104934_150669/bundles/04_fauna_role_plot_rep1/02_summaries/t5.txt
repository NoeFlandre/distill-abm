The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape. It simulates a socio-ecological system containing human foragers and sexually dimorphic megaherbivore herds. From step 500 to around step 1500, the rate of increase slows, with the cumulative kills plateauing between 110 and 120 in most simulations. This stabilization suggests a potential depletion of available male prey or a shift in forager behavior or prey demographics over time. From step 800 to 2000, the population hovered between 0 and 5 individuals. After step 500, the rate of decline slows, and the population continues to decrease. By step 1500, the number of animals is nearly zero, indicating that the megaherbivore population is effectively extinct.
