The cumulative number of female prey animals killed by forager bands increases rapidly during the initial phase of the simulation, reaching approximately 120 kills by around step 1000. After this point, the rate of increase slows significantly, and the cumulative number stabilizes, with most simulation runs converging between 115 and 125 kills by step 2000. This pattern suggests that the hunting pressure exerted by the forager bands is initially intense but diminishes as the available female prey population decreases, likely due to depletion or other limiting factors. The stabilization indicates a potential equilibrium between hunting activity and prey availability, or the exhaustion of accessible female prey. These findings highlight the importance of understanding prey dynamics and hunting efficiency in modeling megafaunal extinction scenarios, as they suggest that hunting pressure alone may not lead to complete eradication without additional compounding factors.
