The total number of prey animals declined consistently across all 120 simulation runs. The aggregate mean started at 166.67 at time zero and fell to zero by the final recorded step at index 2311. The median population count of 1.48 indicates that in the majority of the simulation runs, the population collapsed to near-extinction levels well before the end of the observation period. The mean value of 25.01 is significantly higher than the median, suggesting that a small number of runs maintained higher populations for a brief duration, skewing the average. A statistically significant decreasing trend was observed in the initial three time windows (indices 0 to 695), with the decline rate slowing considerably in subsequent windows. The population showed minor local fluctuations between indices 700 and 1150, characterized by small peaks and valleys, before dropping to zero. The extinction of the megaherbivore population was complete by step 2311 in all simulated scenarios. For decision makers, this indicates that under the specified hunting pressure and environmental parameters, the megafauna population is not viable and faces certain extinction.
