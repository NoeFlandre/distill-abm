The combined total energy available in all prey animals begins at approximately 25,000 units at the start of the simulation. Within the first 500 time steps, a sharp decline occurs, with the total energy dropping to below 10,000 units. By 1,000 time steps, the total energy has decreased further to less than 1,000 units. After this point, the decline slows, and the total energy remains near zero for the remainder of the simulation up to 2,300 time steps. This rapid depletion of energy suggests that the interaction between hunting pressure and animal demography leads to a swift reduction in prey biomass. Decision makers should note that such a trajectory could indicate high vulnerability of the prey population to extinction under similar socio-natural dynamics.
