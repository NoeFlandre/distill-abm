The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed increases sharply during the initial phase of the simulation, reaching approximately 120 by step 600 across most simulations. The rate of increase slows significantly after step 600, with minimal additional hunting observed between steps 600 and 2000. By step 2000, the cumulative number stabilizes, indicating that forager bands have either exhausted the available postpartum females or shifted their hunting focus to other prey groups. The stabilization suggests that high hunting pressure early in the simulation significantly impacted the reproductive capacity of the prey population. Decision makers should consider the potential for rapid depletion of reproductive individuals in prey populations due to targeted hunting strategies, which could lead to long-term population declines.
