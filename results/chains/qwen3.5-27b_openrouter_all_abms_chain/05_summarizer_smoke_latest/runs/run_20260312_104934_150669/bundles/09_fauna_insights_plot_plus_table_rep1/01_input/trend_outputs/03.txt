The cumulative number of male prey killed exhibits a rapid upward trend from the start of the simulation, increasing from 0 to approximately 110 by time step 500. Following this initial surge, the rate of increase decelerates significantly, transitioning into a phase of slower growth between time steps 500 and 1200. By time step 1250, the cumulative count stabilizes, hovering around a plateau of 118 to 120 for the remainder of the simulation period, reaching a maximum mean value of roughly 120 at time step 1876. Statistical analysis indicates a shift in the trend around time step 1241, where the previously increasing trajectory effectively ceases, resulting in a stable plateau with minor fluctuations. This pattern suggests that the forager bands reached a point of equilibrium where the harvest of male prey stabilized relative to the available population density and environmental carrying capacity.
