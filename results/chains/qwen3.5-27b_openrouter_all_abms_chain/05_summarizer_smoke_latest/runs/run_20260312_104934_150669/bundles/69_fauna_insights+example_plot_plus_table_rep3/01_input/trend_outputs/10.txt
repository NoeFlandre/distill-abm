The steady decrease in prey numbers indicates a system unable to buffer hunting pressure or maintain equilibrium under the given socio-natural conditions. For decision makers, this suggests that without adjustments to human foraging behavior or prey resilience factors—such as reproduction rates or herd size—the population collapses toward extinction, underscoring the sensitivity of megaherbivore systems to combined pressures from hunting and environmental factors.
