The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. A seasonal oscillation with a dominant period of 63 ticks is observable throughout the series, likely reflecting environmental cycles. The persistent and deepening negative energy balance indicates that foraging returns were insufficient to cover movement and processing costs over the long term. A change point at tick 725, after which the accumulation accelerates, reaching a maximum value of 5.6000 at tick 1673. This peak indicates the period of highest reproductive output, likely driven by favorable initial herd demography and resource availability before hunting pressures intensify. A dominant oscillation period of 50.00 ticks is observed throughout, correlating with the environmental seasonality parameter. The extinction of the female population signifies the cessation of reproduction, leading to the complete collapse of the species within the modeled landscape. The total number of prey animals begins at approximately 167 individuals and follows a consistent downward trajectory throughout the simulation. The total energy available in all prey animals begins at approximately 16,667 units. Over the first 385 time steps, the system undergoes a precipitous decline. This initial phase is marked by the steepest negative slope in the early simulation windows. A dominant cycle of approximately 63 time steps is evident.
