 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape .  The cumulative tally of female megaherbivores removed from the population by foraging groups begins at zero and demonstrates a persistent upward trend across the majority of the simulation timeline . The accumulation rate is most pronounced in the initial phases, driven by the interaction between forager movement costs and the high food value assigned to female prey . Towards the end of the observation window, specifically after index 2170, a dominant oscillation period of approximately 63 time steps becomes evident .  The early trajectory is characterized by a highly significant positive trend, the latter half of the simulation shows a degradation in growth momentum . By the final windows of analysis, the trend test indicates a lack of significant upward movement, suggesting the active predation on this specific class of prey has reached a point of exhaustion or saturation . This trajectory aligns with a scenario of targeted harvest pressure .  The total number of prey animals initiates at approximately 166.7 individuals and exhibits a consistent decline over the course of the simulation, ultimately reaching zero at time step 2311 . By tick 2311, the population reaches zero, confirming total extinction of the species under these specific parameters of hunting pressure and environmental seasonality .
