The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape. It simulates a socio-ecological system containing human foragers and sexually dimorphic megaherbivore herds. The cumulative number of male prey animals killed by forager bands rises steeply. This period reflects high hunting pressure, likely driven by the presence of a substantial male population and effective foraging strategies. Between steps 500 and 1000, the rate of increase slows, with the cumulative numbers of males killed reaching values between approximately 100 and 125. The number of female megaherbivores begins at approximately 125 at the initial step. A rapid decline is observed across all simulation runs between steps 0 and 500. Female populations remain non-zero until approximately step 2300, where the count reaches 1 before extinction.
