 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history .  The cumulative number of female prey animals killed starts at zero and rises sharply, reaching approximately 80 by step 200 . The rate of accumulation slows significantly after step 800, as the number of males killed stabilizes between 100 and 125 for the majority of runs . Variability among simulation runs increases after step 600, with some runs reaching higher cumulative totals, while others plateau earlier .  The number of prey animals in the simulation starts at 250 and undergoes a rapid decline over the first 400 time steps . The decline is consistent across all simulation runs, with minimal divergence observed in the early stages . By around 1000 steps, the number of female animals drops to near zero, suggesting the population has reached a critical threshold leading to potential extinction .  The percentage of green patches in the simulation begins near 33% at the initial time step . By approximately 1,500 steps, the total energy stabilizes at minimal values, indicating that the prey population has either been reduced to very low numbers or that the surviving individuals are in a state of chronic energy deficit . The pattern suggests that the interaction between environmental conditions, hunting pressure, and animal life history has led to a significant depletion of prey population's energy reserves .
