 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape .  By the final phase of the simulation, the energy levels suggest complete resource exhaustion and system failure . Decision makers should interpret these results as evidence that under the current parameters settings, the environment cannot sustain the metabolic costs of the forager population . The relentless energy drain implies that hunting yields are insufficient to offset movement costs and that external resource inputs are absent or inadequate .  At time step zero, the cumulative death count is zero . The aggregate population, starting at 125 individuals, exhibits an immediate and steep downward trajectory . By tick 500, the average population has collapsed to critically low levels, falling below 20 individuals . Extinction occurs with all simulation runs reaching zero by the final time step .  The combined energy of male prey animals begins at approximately 12,500 units and exhibits a steep, monotonic decline, reaching total extinction (0 energy) by tick 2284 . The rate of decline was most rapid during the initial phase, dropping significantly before the 400th time step, after which the rate of reduction slowed, transitioning into a phase of gradual attrition .  Peaks and valleys in vegetation coverage occur periodically but remain confined to a narrow range between 32.994% and 32.996% . These dynamics imply that under the modeled parameters, vegetation availability remains sufficiently stable to support the megaherbivore population without inducing long-term resource shortages .
