The cumulative number of female prey animals killed by forager bands increases rapidly from the start, reaching approximately 100 individuals by step 500 across all simulations. Between steps 500 and 1000, the rate of increase slows, with the cumulative count stabilizing around 110 to 120 females. Beyond step 1000, there is minimal to no additional increase in the cumulative number of females killed, with most simulations plateauing between 120 and 125 females. A few simulations show slightly lower plateaus around 110 to 115 females, while others extend marginally higher, up to approximately 126 females. This trend suggests an early phase of intense hunting activity followed by a leveling off as hunting pressure diminishes or prey availability becomes limiting.
