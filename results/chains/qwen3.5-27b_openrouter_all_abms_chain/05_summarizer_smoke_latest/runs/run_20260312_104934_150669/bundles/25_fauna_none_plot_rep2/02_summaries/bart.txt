Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs. The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is based on the model's parameters such as herd demography, environmental conditions, and hunting pressure. The number of deaths among the prey animal population exhibits a rapid increase in the initial phase of the simulation. By step 1000, the majority of simulations stabilize, with the cumulative count ranging between 110 and 125 females killed. There is a sharp decline in the population, with the numbers dropping to approximately 100 animals. By step 500, the population further diminishes, reaching close to 50 animals in most simulations. After step 1000, the number of animals remains near zero across all simulations, indicating a complete or near-complete extinction.
