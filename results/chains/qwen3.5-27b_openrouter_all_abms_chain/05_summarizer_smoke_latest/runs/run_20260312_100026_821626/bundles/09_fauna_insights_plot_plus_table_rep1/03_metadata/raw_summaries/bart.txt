 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs .  The cumulative number of female prey killed exhibits a steady upward trend, starting at zero and reaching a maximum average of approximately 123 individuals . In the early phase, the rate of increase is rapid, averaging roughly 0.21 individuals per step between steps 0 and 231 . By time step 1250, the cumulative count stabilizes around a plateau of 118 to 120 for the remainder of the simulation period .  The prey population begins at approximately 125 individuals and undergoes a rapid decline . The population drops precipitously, reaching values below 5 by approximately step 730 . Brief periods of stabilization or minor increases occur between steps 1160 and 1391, as well as between 1856 and 2087, though the population fails to recover to viable levels .  The final extinction of the prey population occurs at step 2311.0, indicated by a total energy value of 0.0 . The percentage of vegetated patches remains remarkably stable throughout the simulation, fluctuating around a mean of 32.66% with an initial start value of 33.00%.
