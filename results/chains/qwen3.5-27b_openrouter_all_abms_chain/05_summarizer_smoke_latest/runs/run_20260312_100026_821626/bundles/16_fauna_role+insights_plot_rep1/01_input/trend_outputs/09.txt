The number of female prey animals exhibits a sharp decline from an initial average of approximately 125 individuals at step 0, dropping to fewer than 25 animals by step 500. The rate of decline accelerates between steps 200 and 400. By step 700, the population stabilizes at critically low levels, averaging below 5 animals across most simulation runs. A significant portion of the runs result in complete extinction of female prey animals by step 1000. For the runs that do not immediately collapse, the population remains stagnant at near-zero levels between steps 1000 and 2300, indicating a failure of the remaining population to recover or sustain reproduction. The high seasonality setting (0.8) combined with the mortality parameters suggests that environmental stress and predation pressure exceed the reproductive capacity of the female cohort.
