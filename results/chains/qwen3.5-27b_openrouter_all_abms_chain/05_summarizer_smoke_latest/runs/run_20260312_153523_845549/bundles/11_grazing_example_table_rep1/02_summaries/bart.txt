RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock begins at a minimal value of 0.0074. During the initial phase, the metric declines until step 26, where it reaches a minimum of 84,114.07. The average available green biomass across all patches begins at 45,000 units at the start of the simulation. The simulation concludes at step 100 with the reserve biomass peaking at 121,072.59 units. The number of households exhibiting social learning behavior begins at zero at the start of the simulation. As these agents manage their private pastures, differences in learning efficacy and risk tolerance result in divergent livestock management decisions. The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households rises steadily to 96.7783 by the final time step. The E-RO population is less resilient under the configured ecological conditions, particularly given the high rainfall variability and active risk modes. The attrition of these households suggests a competitive disadvantage compared to the learning-capable agents present in the heterogeneous population, as the non-adaptive strategy proves less sustainable.
