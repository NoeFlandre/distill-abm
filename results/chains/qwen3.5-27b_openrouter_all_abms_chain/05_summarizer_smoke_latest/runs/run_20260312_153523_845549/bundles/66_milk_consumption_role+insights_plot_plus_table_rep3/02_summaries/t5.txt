Model measures the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. Each agent evaluates milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics. Decisions are influenced by habit strength, likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms. Data shows a significant positive trend with a slope averaging approximately 68 units per time step. No significant peaks or valleys were detected, and the variance remains relatively stable. The growth appears to be driven by a cumulative accumulation of choices. Consumption grows at a rate of 48.54 units per step, surpassing previous plateau levels. By time step 32, the mean consumption reaches its maximum observed value of 1,456.60 units. The trajectory implies a two-stage market evolution. The deviation between agents' environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0 at the initial time step. From time step 5 onwards, the deviation stabilizes, hovering around a mean value of 12.6 to 13.4 for the remainder of the simulation period.
