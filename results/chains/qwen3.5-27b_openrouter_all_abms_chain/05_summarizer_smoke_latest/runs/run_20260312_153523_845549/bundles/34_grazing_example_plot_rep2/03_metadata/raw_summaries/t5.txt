the RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . it implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences . the average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant throughout the 100 time steps . the values range from approximately -0.11 to 0.18 . each simulation run maintains its initial value without change over the entire duration of the simulation . the average number of destocked livestock declines steadily, reaching values close to 1 by time step 30 . the rate of decline slows down as time progresses, stabilizing around 1 between time steps 40 and 100 . this stabilization reflects a balance between livestock management practices and resource availability over time. s for each simulation run, indicating the system has reached a long-term equilibrium for this type of behavioral type . s is a symposia of a behavioral type that has reached an equilibrium .
