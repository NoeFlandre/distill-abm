The average number of livestock destocked by households begins near zero and rises sharply to a peak of approximately 7.5 during the first 5 time steps. This initial spike indicates that, in the early stages of the simulation, resource scarcity forces households to remove a significant portion of their livestock. Following this peak, the average number of destocked livestock declines steadily, reaching values close to 1 by time step 30. The rate of decline slows down as time progresses, stabilizing around 1 between time steps 40 and 100. This stabilization reflects a balance between livestock management practices and resource availability over time. Throughout the simulation, there is considerable variability in the trajectories, with some households consistently destocking more livestock than others. This variability suggests differences in household strategies and environmental conditions across the population.
