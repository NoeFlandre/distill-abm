The simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and farmers' success over time. The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. The average number of livestock destocked by households begins near zero and rises sharply in the first 5 time steps, peaking at approximately 7.5. After this peak, there is a steady decline in destocking, with values dropping to around 3 by time step 20. By time step 60, the rate of increase slows, and most simulations stabilize between 110,000 and 125,000 units by the end of the simulation period. The average number of livestock destocked across all households starts at zero and rises sharply in the initial phase, reaching approximately 80 by time step 20. By the end of the simulation at 100 time steps, the number of households engaged in social learning ranges between 16 and 54.
