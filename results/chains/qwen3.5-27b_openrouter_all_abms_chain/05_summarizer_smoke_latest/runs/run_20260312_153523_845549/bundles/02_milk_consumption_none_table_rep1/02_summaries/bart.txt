The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice. An agent's milk choice function is modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices. The simulation tracks the cumulative total of instances where agents selected whole milk. The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at 5.2900 at time step 0. The trajectory reflects the modeled shift in consumer behavior where initial low adoption of alternative milk types increases. The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a pronounced upward trajectory over the course of the simulation. The mean habit factor applied to the incumbent whole milk choice function begins at 1.0000 at time step 0 and increases steadily to 1.0502 by time step 32. By the final time step, index 32, the mean quantity reaches a maximum of 1456.60. The overall mean deviation for the series is 0.4965 with a median of 0.5584 and 0.1702. Within the context of the milk consumption model, this increasing deviation indicates a growing divergence between the agents' internal health valuations and the health implications of their selected milk types over the simulated historical period.
