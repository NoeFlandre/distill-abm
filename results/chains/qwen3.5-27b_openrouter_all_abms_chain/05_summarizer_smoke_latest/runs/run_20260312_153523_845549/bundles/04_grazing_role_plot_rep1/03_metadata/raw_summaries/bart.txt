 This simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors . In this version, each farmer stays on their own plot of land . They decide how many animals to keep based on how the grass is doing and what others are doing . The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock .  The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, starts at an initial value of 90 . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero for all simulation runs . The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across all households begins at a value of zero at the start of the simulation . By step 60, the average green biomass has risen significantly, reaching levels around 60,000 units .  Simulations start at 20 at step 0, reflecting the initial setup . By step 10, the counts diverge, with some increasing to peaks near 40 while others drop sharply to levels below 10 . After this initial divergence, there are periods of stabilization and fluctuation . Some household counts stabilize near 25, while others decline further or increase slightly .
