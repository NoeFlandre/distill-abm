The habit factor applied to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices initiates at a baseline value of 1.0000 at the start of the simulation. During the initial five time steps, the metric remains stable with no significant trend detected. From time step 5 onwards, a statistically significant upward trajectory is observed, continuing until the final time step at index 32, where the value reaches 1.5879. The average value across the full period is 1.2615. A structural change point occurs at index 15, indicating a shift in the rate of habit accumulation. These patterns suggest that consumer inertia regarding alternative milk varieties was present initially but was subsequently overcome, resulting in strengthened habitual preferences for lower-fat options. For decision makers, this implies that strategies to shift consumption patterns must account for an initial period of resistance before habit mechanisms can effectively sustain the new behavior.
