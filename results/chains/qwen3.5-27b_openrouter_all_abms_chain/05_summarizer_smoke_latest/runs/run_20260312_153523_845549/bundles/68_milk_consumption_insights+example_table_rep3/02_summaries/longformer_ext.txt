The overall objective of the agent-based model is to reproduce adoption behaviours of milk consumption by the British public (1974-2005), by replicating individual preferences and decision factors. Specifically, the goal of the model is to explore the influence of perception, habits and social influence in an individual's decision-making process of milk choice. The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. Strategies aiming While some consumers may hold environmental concerns, these are consistently overridden by other factors such as habit strength or health perceptions regarding milk types. The model also suggests that consumers may be influenced by social factors that influence their choices.For decision makers, this persistent misalignment highlights a challenge in influencing behavior through environmental messaging alone. Since the deviation stabilizes at a high level, interventions aiming to reduce the gap between values and actions must address the habit threshold or social susceptibility mechanisms that appear to lock in choices inconsistent with environmental preferences. The model suggests that without altering the strength of existing habits or the weight of social influence, consumer choices will continue to reflect a disconnect from environmental values even after market trends shift.
