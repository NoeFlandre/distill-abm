The simulation output characterizes a decisive structural shift in aggregate whole milk consumption. The series initiates at a peak of approximately 2655 units at the initial time step. Following a statistically significant downward trend confirmed by rolling window analyses, the aggregate metric declines to a minimum of approximately 253 units by the final time step. This trajectory represents a reduction of over 90% from the baseline. The progression is marked by specific inflection points at indices 5, 13, 16, 19, 24, 25, 27, and 30, indicating phases of accelerated change within the broader decline. An underlying oscillatory pattern with a dominant period of 15 steps is also detectable. From a consumer behavior perspective, this trend confirms the effectiveness of social influence and habit formation mechanisms in driving a mass transition away from the incumbent product toward healthier alternatives, while a residual base of consumption suggests a segment resistant to the social shift.
