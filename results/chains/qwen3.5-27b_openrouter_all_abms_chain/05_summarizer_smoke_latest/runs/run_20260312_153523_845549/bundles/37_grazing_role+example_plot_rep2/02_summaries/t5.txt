The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. Satisficing-threshold: Range from 0.0 to 100.0. We set it to 0. Social-susceptibility: range from 0 to 1.0. A distinct and rapid growth phase occurs between step 40 and step 70. The reserve biomass increases sharply, surging from approximately 82,000 units to over 120,000 units. This significant rebound implies a shift in system dynamics. After timestep 40, the Gini coefficient rises more sharply, reaching values between 0.03 and 0.09 by timestep 100. This acceleration in inequality likely corresponds to divergent outcomes among households, as some accumulate more healthy livestock while others struggle due to differences in learning strategies.
