 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents begins at 0.0074 at the start of the simulation . For decision makers, this consistency ensures that any observed changes in herd management or pasture conditions are driven by behavioral dynamics and learning mechanisms rather than fluctuations in the underlying risk attitude .  The average number of livestock destocked across households begins at zero and experiences a sharp surge within the first few time steps . For decision makers, this pattern indicates a critical transition period in the first 40 years where household livelihoods face significant pressure . The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock, measured by the Gini coefficient, follows a distinct and consistent increasing pattern over the simulation period .  The number of households following the E-RO strategy starts at 20 and increases slightly to a peak of 21.1 at step 8 . It subsequently enters a prolonged period of decline, with a significant downward trajectory continuing from step 11 through step 65 . The rate of decrease stabilizes after step 65, indicating that the remaining agent population has reached a new equilibrium .
