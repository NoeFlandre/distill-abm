the RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . it implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences . the average initial risk attitude for social learning agents remains completely constant at 0.0074 across the entire simulation duration from step 0 to step 100 . there is no variation, trend, or deviation observed in the data over time; the metric is static . for decision-makers, this result confirms that the risk attitude parameter acts as a static input for the social learning strategy . the average number of livestock destocked by households begins at zero and spikes sharply in the initial steps, reaching a maximum average of 6.61 at step 3 . the destocking metric continues to oscillate with diminishing amplitude, eventually settling into a low, stable range near zero for the remainder of the simulation . the average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households increases consistently throughout the simulation period, rising from 0 at the initial time step to approximately 96.8 at the final time step of 100 . following this initial surge, the rate of destocking decelerates significantly, shifting to a slower, steady incline for the remainder of the simulation .
