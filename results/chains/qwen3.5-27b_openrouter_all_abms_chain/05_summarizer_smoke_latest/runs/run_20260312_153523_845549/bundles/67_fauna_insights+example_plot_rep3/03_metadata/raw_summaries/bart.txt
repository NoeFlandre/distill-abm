 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs . This sharp decrease is likely due to the combined effects of high hunting pressure from foragers, limited food resources, and internal mortality rates .
