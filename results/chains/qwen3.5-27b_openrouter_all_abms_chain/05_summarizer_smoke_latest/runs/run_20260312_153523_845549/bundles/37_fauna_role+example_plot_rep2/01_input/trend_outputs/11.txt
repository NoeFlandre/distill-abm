The total energy of male prey animals starts at approximately 12,500 at the initial time step. This value experiences a rapid and nearly linear decline, reaching roughly 3,000 by time step 350. Following this initial phase, the rate of energy loss accelerates, and the trajectories diverge significantly, indicating a high degree of variance among the simulations. By time step 600, the median energy level drops to below 1,000, with a majority of runs approaching zero energy. The decline continues with diminishing returns in energy values, and by time step 1000, most simulations show total male energy levels effectively at zero. While a small subset of simulations exhibits a delayed collapse, maintaining low energy levels until approximately time step 1500, the overarching trend is a definitive crash in the energetic state of the male population within the first 1,000 steps, suggesting the total depletion of available energy reserves or the removal of the males from the system.
