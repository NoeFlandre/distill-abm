 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . The simulation uses both a theoretical grounding and empirical data to inform the ABM, with calibration performed against macro level data .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent initiates at 5.29 units and demonstrates a pronounced upward trajectory across the simulation timeline . In the early phase, covering the first ten time steps, the metric rises sharply, reflecting a period of rapid adoption likely driven by evolving health perceptions and initial social network interactions .  Given population of 1,000 agents, this indicates that every agent consistently executes a choice decision at each step . This uniformity reflects a stable decision-making process regarding milk consumption throughout the simulated era from 1974 to 2005 . For decision makers, the consistent activity level suggests that strategies to influence milk substitution can be deployed with the expectation of regular consumer engagement across all periods .
