The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at 45,000 units at the start of the simulation. During the initial phase, the biomass experiences a decline, reaching a minimum value of approximately 42,057 units at time step 27. This initial reduction corresponds to a period of significant decreasing trend observed between time steps 11 and 21, suggesting that initial grazing pressure from the 60 households exceeded the immediate regeneration capacity of the vegetation. This pressure likely reflects the initial herd sizes and the adjustment period before learning mechanisms fully influence decision-making among the E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1 agent types.

Following the trough at time step 27, the system transitions into a phase of recovery and growth. By time step 33, a significant increasing trend emerges, which persists until the end of the simulation. The rate of biomass accumulation is most pronounced between time steps 44 and 54, where the slope reaches its maximum, indicating a period of rapid vegetation regrowth likely driven by adjusted stocking rates. The biomass value climbs steadily to reach its maximum of 60,535 units at time step 100. As the simulation progresses beyond time step 77, the rate of increase slows considerably, with the slope diminishing from 11.6 to approximately 3.1 by time step 98. This deceleration suggests that the social-ecological system is approaching a new equilibrium where vegetation growth balances the sustained grazing activities managed by the learning agents. The overall trajectory demonstrates a recovery from initial degradation to a state of higher resource availability by the end of the 100-step period.
