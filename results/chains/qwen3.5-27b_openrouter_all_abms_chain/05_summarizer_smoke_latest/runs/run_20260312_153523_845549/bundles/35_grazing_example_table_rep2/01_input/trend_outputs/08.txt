The total count of households engaging in social learning begins at zero and follows a consistent upward trajectory for the first phase of the simulation. An initial acceleration in the adoption rate becomes apparent at time step 7. The growth continues with shifting intensities marked by inflection points at time steps 28, 30, 33, and 38. A notable structural change point occurs around time step 40, after which the accumulation rate moderates. The mean number of learning agents reaches a peak of approximately 32.07 by time step 63. Following this peak, the count stabilizes and remains constant through the final time step of 100. Statistical tests confirm a significant positive trend across all evaluated intervals. This pattern indicates that social learning behaviors diffuse through a substantial portion of the agent population, saturating at a level representing just over half of the total 60-household system.
