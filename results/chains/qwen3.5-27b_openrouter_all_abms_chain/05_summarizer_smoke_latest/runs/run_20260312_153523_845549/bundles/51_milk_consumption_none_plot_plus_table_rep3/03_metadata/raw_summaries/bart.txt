 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice . An agent's milk choice function is modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at 5.29 units at the initial time step . After time step 26, the consumption values resume a steady upward trajectory, climbing from approximately 1250 to a final value of 1456.60 units by time step 32 . The total number of milk choices made by agents exhibits a consistent linear increase over time .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at 1.0 at the initial time step . By time step 32, the factor increases steadily to a final value of approximately 1.59. The trend shows a relatively flat trajectory in the early phase . Statistical analysis confirms a significant increasing trend across multiple windows .
