The average total number of livestock, including destocked animals, starts at 90 and declines rapidly over the first 37 time steps, reaching a minimum value of approximately 41.01. This initial sharp decline is driven by the interaction of households managing their herds against the fixed carrying capacity of their land, leading to initial destocking. The rate of this decline slows significantly between steps 20 and 37, as the herd size approaches the minimum threshold where destocking stabilizes. Following the minimum at step 37, the trend reverses, and the average livestock count begins to increase. This upward trajectory is statistically significant and accelerates through step 55. After step 55, the growth rate diminishes, and the values plateau, indicating that the system has reached a new dynamic equilibrium. By the end of the 100 time steps, the average total livestock has recovered to approximately 47.55. The variability among simulation runs increases during the recovery phase, particularly between steps 40 and 70, before the trajectories diverge into stable but distinct levels by the end of the simulation.
