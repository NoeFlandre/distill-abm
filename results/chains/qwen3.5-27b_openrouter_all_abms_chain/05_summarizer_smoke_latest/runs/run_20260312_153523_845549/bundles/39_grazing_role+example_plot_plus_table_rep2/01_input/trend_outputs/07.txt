The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock, measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at 0.0000 at the beginning of the simulation and exhibits a consistent upward trend over the entire duration of 100 time steps, concluding at a value of 0.0726. The mean Gini coefficient for the series is 0.0302, indicating that on average, the distribution remains relatively equitable, though the variance of 0.0006 suggests growing divergence among simulation runs. The trend is characterized by an initial slow increase followed by an acceleration in inequality. Specifically, inflection points are detected at time steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, corresponding to moments where the rate of inequality growth changes. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms a statistically significant increasing trend across all consecutive 10-step windows, with the slope becoming slightly steeper in the later stages of the simulation, moving from 0.000 in the first 40 steps to 0.001 from step 41 onwards. Change points are identified at steps 51, 71, and 100, signaling distinct shifts in the trajectory of inequality accumulation. By the end of the simulation, the Gini coefficient reaches its maximum, reflecting that the variance in herd health outcomes among households has widened as a result of the divergent learning strategies employed by the agents.
