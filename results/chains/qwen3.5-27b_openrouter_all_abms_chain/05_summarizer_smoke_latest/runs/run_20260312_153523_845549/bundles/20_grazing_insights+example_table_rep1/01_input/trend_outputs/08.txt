The number of households engaging in social learning begins at zero and follows a sustained upward trajectory throughout the simulation. In the initial phase, the count increases rapidly, indicating a strong early response to the extension model's influence. This growth persists until approximately time step 40, after which the rate of increase diminishes, marking a shift in the dynamics of adoption. The metric reaches a peak value of approximately 32 households around time step 63, representing roughly half of the total population of 60 households. By the conclusion of the simulation at time step 100, the value stabilizes near this peak, showing no further significant accumulation. These trends indicate that the extension module successfully activates social learning behaviors in a significant portion of the agent population, particularly during the first half of the simulation period. The subsequent plateau suggests that inherent factors, such as household risk attitudes or the limits of the knowledge radius, constrain further diffusion. For decision makers, this highlights that while extension interventions are effective at initiating adaptive management strategies, reaching saturation across the entire community may require targeted adjustments to address the specific risk dispositions or information constraints of the remaining households.
