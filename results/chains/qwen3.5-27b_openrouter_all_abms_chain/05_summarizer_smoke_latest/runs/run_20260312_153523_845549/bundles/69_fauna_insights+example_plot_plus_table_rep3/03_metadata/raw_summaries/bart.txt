 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs .  The cumulative number of male prey killed by foragers increases sharply from 0 to approximately 100 over the first 500 time steps, averaging a rise of about 0.2 animals per step . The rate of increase slows significantly after 400 steps, with the cumulative count gradually approaching a plateau between 119 and 124 from roughly step 1200 to 2312 . The number of postpartum female prey animals hunted rises sharply during the first 475 time steps .  Nearly the entire initial prey cohort (250 individuals) is removed within approximately 2000 time steps, suggesting a system trending toward local extinction rather than equilibrium . Foraging pressure and mortality parameters create an environment where the species cannot sustain itself, leading to total depletion of the female demographic, and consequently the total herd .  The total energy available in prey animals within the simulation begins at approximately 16,667 units at the start of the simulation . This aggregate value exhibits a consistent and rapid decline across the first 460 time steps, reaching a level of roughly 4,100 units by index 385 . The decline continues, though at a variable rate, past index 500, with the average energy falling to approximately 1,000 units .
