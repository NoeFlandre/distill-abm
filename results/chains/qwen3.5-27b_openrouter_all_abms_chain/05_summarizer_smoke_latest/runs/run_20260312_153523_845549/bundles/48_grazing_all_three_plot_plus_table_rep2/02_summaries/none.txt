Context and Goals

This simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. In this version, each farmer stays on their own plot of land. They decide how many animals to keep based on how the grass is doing and what others are doing. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time. There are three main ways farmers behave: baseline (E-RO), learning from their own results (E-LBD), and learning from neighbors (E-RO-SL1). The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock.

The average household risk attitude begins near 0.01 at the start of the simulation and climbs steadily over the first 33 time steps to reach a maximum value of approximately 0.38. This initial upward trend, which is statistically significant, suggests that households rapidly adapt their behavior, becoming more risk-averse as they accumulate experience and observe the outcomes of their grazing decisions. Following this peak, the metric reverses course, entering a significant decline between time steps 33 and 65, dropping to approximately 0.19. This downturn indicates that as the simulation progresses past the initial adaptation phase, households begin to stabilize their strategies or perceive a reduction in the volatility of their environment, leading to a moderation in their risk attitudes. From time step 70 through the end of the simulation, the metric stabilizes around 0.19 with no significant trend, fluctuating within a narrow range. This long-term equilibrium suggests that the system has reached a state where the collective risk tolerance of the farmers has settled, likely due to the convergence of learning dynamics and environmental constraints. For decision-makers, the data implies that early intervention strategies must account for a transient period of high risk-aversion, while long-term planning can rely on a stable, moderately conservative risk profile among the farming community.
