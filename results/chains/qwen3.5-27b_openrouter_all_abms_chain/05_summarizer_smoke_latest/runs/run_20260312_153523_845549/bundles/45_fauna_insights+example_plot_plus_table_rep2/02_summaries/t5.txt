The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The cumulative number of male prey killed by foragers increases sharply from 0 to approximately 100 within the first 500 time steps. This initial surge likely reflects the high density of available prey early in the simulation, enabling hunters to successfully acquire energy-rich male targets.
