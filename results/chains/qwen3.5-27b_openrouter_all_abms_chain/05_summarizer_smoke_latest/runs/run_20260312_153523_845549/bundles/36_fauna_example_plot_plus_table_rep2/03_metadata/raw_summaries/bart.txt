 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . Model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history .  The rate of accumulation is rapid in the first 500 steps, indicating high prey density and efficient hunting strategies . After 1,500 steps, the cumulative count stabilizes near 120, showing negligible growth until the end of the simulation at 2,312 steps, where the final value reaches 117.5 .  The population of male prey animals begins at approximately 125 and declines sharply over the initial 400 time steps . The decline continues steadily past 700 time steps, with the population reaching critically low levels . By step 2310, the male population reaches zero across all simulation runs, indicating complete local extinction .  The total energy of prey animals within the simulation begins at approximately 25,000 units and exhibits a rapid, monotonic decline, reaching near-zero levels by approximately step 1,200 . The percentage of landscape patches that are vegetated, representing fodder availability for prey animals, begin at approximately 33.0% and maintains a relatively stable level .
