The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant at 0.0074 across all 100 time steps. This indicates that the initial disposition to deviate from the recommended number of livestock does not change over the simulation period. For decision makers, this suggests that the initial risk attitudes of the agents are stable and do not influence the dynamics of grazing land management or the health of the pasture over time. The lack of change implies that the initial settings for risk attitude are robust and do not require adjustment in the context of this model.
