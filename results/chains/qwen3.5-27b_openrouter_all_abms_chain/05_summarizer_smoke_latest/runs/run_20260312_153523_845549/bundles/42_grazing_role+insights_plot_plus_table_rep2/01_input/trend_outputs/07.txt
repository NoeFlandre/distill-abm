The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0.0 at timestep 1 and rises steadily to 0.0726 by timestep 100, with an overall mean of 0.0302 and a median of 0.0229. The increase is significant, as confirmed by rolling Mann-Kendall tests across all time windows (p < 0.001), showing a monotonic upward trend. Inflection points occur at timesteps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, where the rate of inequality increase shifts, with the most notable acceleration beginning around timestep 40. Change points are identified at timesteps 51, 71, and 100, indicating distinct phases in the growth of inequality. Early growth (timesteps 1–40) is gradual, followed by a steeper rise from timesteps 40 to 70, and a slightly moderated increase thereafter. By timestep 100, inequality levels have diverged significantly across simulation runs, with some trajectories exceeding 0.09, while others remain below 0.04. This progression implies that over time, unequal access to or accumulation of healthy livestock becomes increasingly pronounced among households. Decision-makers should consider implementing mechanisms to address widening disparities in livestock health, such as targeted support for underperforming households or promoting practices that enhance equitable resource distribution.
