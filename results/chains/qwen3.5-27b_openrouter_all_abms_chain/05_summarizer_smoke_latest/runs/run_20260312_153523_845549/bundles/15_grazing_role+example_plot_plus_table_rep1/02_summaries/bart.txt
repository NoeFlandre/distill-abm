Simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time. The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at 45000 and experiences a slight initial increase before declining to reach its minimum value of 42057.0353 at timestep 27. The biomass values rise steadily through the remainder of the simulation, culminating in a maximum value of 121,072.59 at the final time step, step 100. The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households starts at zero and rises sharply during the first 25 time steps, reaching approximately 80. The variability among individual simulation runs increases over time, indicating that farmers' herd management decisions become increasingly divergent as the simulation progresses.
