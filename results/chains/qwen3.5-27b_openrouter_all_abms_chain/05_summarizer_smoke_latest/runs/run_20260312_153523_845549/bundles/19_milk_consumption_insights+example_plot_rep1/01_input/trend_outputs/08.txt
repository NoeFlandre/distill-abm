The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.00 and increases sharply to approximately 1.02 within the first step. This rapid initial increase indicates an immediate and consistent reinforcement of existing whole milk consumption habits among the agents. Following this initial jump, the mean habit factor rises gradually and steadily from 1.02 to approximately 1.05 over the subsequent 30 steps. This continuous upward trend suggests that as long as agents continue to select whole milk, their habit strength accumulates, thereby increasing the resistance to considering alternative milk types. The low variance observed across the simulation runs implies that this reinforcement mechanism is robust and consistent across different network realizations. For decision-makers, this highlights that entrenched habits for whole milk are difficult to disrupt without a significant initial intervention to break the choice cycle, as the model shows a self-reinforcing dynamic where continued consumption strengthens the habit further.
