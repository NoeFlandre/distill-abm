The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005. The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties. Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms. The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined exhibits a consistent linear increase over time. The growth pattern indicates a gradual shift in consumer preferences towards skimmed or semi-skimmed milk over time, reflecting the influence of individual perceptions, habits, and social dynamics within the model. The mean deviation between agents' held health values and the implied health values from their milk choices starts at zero at the initial time step. The mean value environmental deviation exhibits a rapid increase in the initial phase, starting from 0 at step 0 and reaching approximately 12.2 by step 5. Following step 10, the deviation stabilizes, maintaining a value close to 13.2 through step 30.
