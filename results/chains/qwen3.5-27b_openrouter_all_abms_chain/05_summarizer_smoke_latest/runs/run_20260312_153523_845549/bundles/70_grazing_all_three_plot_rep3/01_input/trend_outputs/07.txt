The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0 across all simulations and remains near zero for the first 10 time steps. After this initial period, the coefficient begins a consistent upward trend, reaching values between 0.03 and 0.09 by the final time step at 100. The rate of increase accelerates notably after time step 50. There is a divergence in outcomes across the simulations, with some trajectories showing a much steeper rise than others. This growing inequality suggests that over time, households accumulate varying levels of healthy livestock, likely driven by the different learning strategies (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) interacting with environmental variability. The increasing disparity implies that early advantages in livestock management compound over time, leading to a concentration of resources among a subset of households while others lag behind. Decision makers should consider interventions that support underperforming farmers to mitigate these widening gaps and ensure equitable pasture management outcomes.
