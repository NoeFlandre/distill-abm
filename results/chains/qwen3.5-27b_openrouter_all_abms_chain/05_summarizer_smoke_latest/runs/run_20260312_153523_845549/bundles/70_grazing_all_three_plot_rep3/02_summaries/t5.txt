The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. Satisficing-threshold: Range from 0.0 to 100.0. We set it to 0. Social-susceptibility: range from 0 to 1.0. Around time step 10, the biomass levels begin to stabilize, maintaining values between 85,000 and 90,000 units until approximately time step 40. This stabilization phase may indicate a balance between livestock consumption and grass regrowth, as farmers adjust their livestock numbers or grazing strategies based on initial observations. The rate of increase accelerates notably after time step 50. There is a divergence in outcomes across the simulations, with some trajectories showing a much steeper rise than others. Early advantages in livestock management compound over time.
