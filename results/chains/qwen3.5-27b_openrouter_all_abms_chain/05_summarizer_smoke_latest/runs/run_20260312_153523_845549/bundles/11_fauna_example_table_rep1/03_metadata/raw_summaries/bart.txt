 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history .  The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted begins at zero and increases rapidly during the initial phase of the simulation . The rate of accumulation decelerates significantly after time step 1204, with inflection points appearing at 1336 and 1487 . The system appears to approach an equilibrium where hunting activity is balanced by prey demography and environmental constraints .  The population of male animals begins the simulation at a count of 125 individuals . The data reveals an immediate and precipitous decline, with the numbers plummeting from this initial maximum during the early simulation steps . By step 730, the population has already collapsed significantly, fluctuating around a low single or double-digit average .  The population stabilizes at very low numbers, fluctuating between approximately 2 and 11 individuals between time steps 700 and 1030 . The median population value across the entire duration remains below 1.5 individuals, indicating the herd spent most of the simulation near extinction thresholds . Under the specified parameters, the combination of hunting pressure, movement costs, and internal mortality rates exceeded the herd's ability to sustain reproduction and growth .  The system experiences multiple inflection points between steps 1,284 and 2,167, yet the overall direction is fixed on total exhaustion . By the final time step at index 2,311, the total energy in the system reaches exactly zero . This endpoint signifies the complete depletion of the measured resource .
