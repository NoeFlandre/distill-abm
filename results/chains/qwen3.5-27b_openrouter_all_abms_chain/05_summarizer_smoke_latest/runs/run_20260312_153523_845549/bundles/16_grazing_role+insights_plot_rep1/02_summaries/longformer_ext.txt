Context and Goals of the RAGE simulation are as follows:This simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. In this version, each farmer stays on their own plot of land. They decide how many animals to keep based on how the grass is doing and what others are doing. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time. There are three main ways farmers behave: baseline (E-RO), learning from their own results (E-LBD), and learning from neighbors (E-RO-SL1). The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock.Parameters of the RAGE simulation are as follows:The average total number of livestock per household begins at 90 at the start of the simulation. This period represents a significant reduction in herd sizes. Following step 40, the trajectory diverges among the different simulation runs. The majority of runs stabilize within a range of 41 to 51 by the end of the simulation at step 100.
