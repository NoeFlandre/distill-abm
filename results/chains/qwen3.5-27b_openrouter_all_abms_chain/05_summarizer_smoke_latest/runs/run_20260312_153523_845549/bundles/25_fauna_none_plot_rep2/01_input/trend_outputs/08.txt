The number of male prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model starts at an initial value of approximately 125 across all simulation runs. A rapid and sustained decline is observed, with the population dropping significantly to values ranging between 0 and 40 by approximately 500 time steps. The decline continues at a slower rate, with the majority of the male population reaching or falling to zero between 700 and 1500 time steps. By approximately 2000 time steps, the population of male prey animals remains at zero across nearly all simulation runs, indicating the extinction of male prey animals in most scenarios.
