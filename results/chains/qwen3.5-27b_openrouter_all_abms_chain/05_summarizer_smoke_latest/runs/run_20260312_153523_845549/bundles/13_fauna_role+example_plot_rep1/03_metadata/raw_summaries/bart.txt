 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape . It incorporates principles of optimal foraging theory, where hunters make decisions based on energy costs and food value .  The cumulative number of male prey animals killed starts at zero and rises sharply, reaching approximately 100 to 120 individuals by step 600 across different simulation runs . The rate of increase slows down significantly between steps 600 and 1200, with most simulations leveling off between 110 and 125 cumulative kills . By step 2000, the number of cumulative male kills remains stable, with no further significant increases observed .  The number of male prey animals begins at approximately 125 in all simulation runs . There is a consistent and rapid decline in the male population across all runs from the start until roughly step 450, where the population drops to approximately 20 individuals . By step 400, the population has fallen to roughly 20% of its initial size (around 50 animals) by step 400 . The population stabilizes near zero in most simulations, though a few simulations retain small numbers of females, indicating limited variability in outcomes .  The total energy of all animals within the simulation begins at approximately 25,000 units at the initial time step . This value reflects the aggregate bioenergetic potential derived from the starting population of 250 prey individuals . A rapid, monotonic decline is observed from the first step, with the energy levels dropping precipitously to approximately 8,000 by step 300 .
