The simulation initializes with 20 households adhering to the E-RO strategy. Between timestep 0 and 10, the number of these households increases slightly, peaking at 21.1 at timestep 8. Subsequently, from timestep 11 to approximately timestep 65, a significant decreasing trend is observed, driven by a consistent reduction in the population count during this interval. The value declines from the early peak to approximately 15.5 around timestep 54. From timestep 66 onwards, the metric stabilizes with no significant trend, fluctuating minimally around the minimum value of 14.67 recorded at timestep 92 and ending at 14.7 at timestep 100. Given that strategy switching is disabled in this configuration, the observed decline in E-RO households indicates attrition rather than behavioral adaptation. This suggests that non-learning agents are less resilient than their learning counterparts over the long term, leading to a cumulative loss of approximately 26 percent of the initial E-RO population. The stabilization in the later period implies that the remaining non-learning households have reached a threshold of resilience sufficient to persist under the given rainfall and grazing conditions.
