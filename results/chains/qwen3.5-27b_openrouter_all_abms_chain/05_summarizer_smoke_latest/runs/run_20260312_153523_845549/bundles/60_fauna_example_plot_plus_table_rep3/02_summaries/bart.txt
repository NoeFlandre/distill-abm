The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs. Ungulates are sexually dimorphic, reproduce, age, and move as herds. The total energy of all prey animals within the simulation begins at approximately 25,000 units and exhibits a rapid, monotonic decline over the first 500 time steps. By step 2312, the total energy reaches zero, indicating the complete extinction of the prey population.
