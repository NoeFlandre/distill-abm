 The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms .  There are no observed peaks, valleys, or periods of decline; the data indicates a persistent accumulation of whole milk selection events over time under the specified model parameters . The data reflects a growing adoption trend, with no observable peaks or inflection points disrupting the progression .  The overall progression reflects a gradual strengthening of the habit factor associated with skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption, culminating in a 58.79% increase from the initial value by the final time step . The mean deviation between agents' held health values and the implied values of their milk choices begins at zero at the start of the simulation .
