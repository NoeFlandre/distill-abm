The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. From time step 28 onwards, consumption drops sharply, falling to approximately 300 units by the end of the simulation at time step 32. This final decline indicates a sustained and widespread transition away from whole milk, driven by the combined effects of habit formation, social influence, and perceived characteristics. After step 26, a secondary rise occurs, with the mean quantity increasing to approximately 1450 units by step 32. This second phase of growth could be attributed to cumulative social influence and evolving perceptions as agents evaluate their choices over time. This rapid early increase followed by a stable plateau suggests agents quickly adjust their choices or perceptions to create a consistent, steady-state deviation between their internal environmental values and their observed milk consumption choices.
