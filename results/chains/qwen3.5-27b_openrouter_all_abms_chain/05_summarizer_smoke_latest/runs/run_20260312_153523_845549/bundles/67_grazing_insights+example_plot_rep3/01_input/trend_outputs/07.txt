The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock begins near zero, indicating a uniform distribution of assets across households at the start of the simulation. This equality persists until approximately time step 20. From this point, the coefficient exhibits a gradual increase, which accelerates significantly after time step 40, transitioning into a steeper upward trajectory. By time step 60, the Gini coefficient for most simulation runs falls within the 0.040 to 0.060 range. The metric continues to rise until the simulation concludes at time step 100, where the coefficient values diverge into two distinct clusters: a lower group ranging from 0.030 to 0.045 and a higher group ranging from 0.060 to 0.095. This trend signifies a progressive and substantial accumulation of inequality in livestock health over the long term. For decision-makers, this indicates that under the current model configuration, the system naturally tends toward wealth disparity, with a significant risk of a few households accumulating the majority of healthy assets while others fall behind. The wide variance in the final inequality levels suggests that household-specific factors or stochastic events play a critical role in determining whether a household experiences asset growth or stagnation.
