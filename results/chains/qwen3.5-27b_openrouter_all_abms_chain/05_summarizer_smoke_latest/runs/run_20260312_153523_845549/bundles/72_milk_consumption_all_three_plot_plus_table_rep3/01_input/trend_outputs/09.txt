The mean habit factor applied to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices begins at an initial value of 1.0. For the first five time steps, this metric remains nearly flat, with a minor fluctuation around 1.02 and a slight dip to 1.017 at step five. From step six onwards, the habit factor demonstrates a consistent and significant upward trend. Statistical analysis confirms this shift begins with a positive slope around step five. The metric continues to rise steadily, reaching a maximum value of approximately 1.59 by the final recorded step. There is minimal variance between simulation runs throughout the duration, indicating a highly uniform evolution of habit strength across agents. This increasing factor suggests that as consumers engage with lower-fat milk alternatives, their established habits reinforce these choices over time, reducing the likelihood of reverting to incumbent products. For decision-makers, this implies that strategies aimed at overcoming initial adoption barriers are critical, as the strengthening habit loop significantly supports long-term retention of new product categories.
