The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero across all simulation runs and rises sharply between time steps 7 and 30, reaching a peak average value of approximately 32 households by time step 63. This rapid increase suggests that, under the specified model conditions, social learning behavior spreads quickly among the population during the initial phase. The trend then slows, with the aggregate count stabilizing for the remainder of the simulation, indicating that social learning adoption reaches a saturation point. The inflection points at time steps 28 and 33 coincide with the transition from rapid growth to gradual stabilization, implying a shift in the dynamics of information exchange or behavioral adjustment among households. This pattern of initial rapid uptake followed by stability is consistent with scenarios where social influence drives early adoption of new practices, which then levels off as the majority of households adapt.
