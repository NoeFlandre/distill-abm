the RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . it implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences . the average initial risk attitude of social learning agents maintains a constant value of 0.0074 from time step 0 through time step 100 . the metric shows no variation, with a standard deviation of zero . for decision makers, this stability implies that system outcomes are driven by the interaction of this fixed risk profile with adaptive behaviors and ecological conditions . between timesteps 33 and 43, a significant increasing trend begins, marking a recovery phase . the biomass continues to rise steadily through the remainder of the simulation . by the final timestep 100, the available green biomass reaches its peak value of 60535, substantially exceeding the initial conditions . social learning can become a dominant behavioral mode, but the system naturally saturates at a specific threshold without additional intervention . the average cumulative number of livestock destocked across households begins at 0.0 and rises monotonically to 96.78 .
