The mean health deviation, representing the discrepancy between agents' internal health perceptions and the implied health values of their chosen milk types, exhibits a consistent upward trend throughout the simulation. The first phase occurs from step 0 to approximately step 10, during which the deviation rises sharply at a rate of 0.063 per step, reaching a value near 0.52. Following this initial surge, the growth rate decelerates significantly between steps 10 and 27, where the deviation increases more gradually, reaching approximately 0.60. From step 28 onwards, the growth rate accelerates again, pushing the metric toward its maximum observed value. While the overall trend is positive, the data shows oscillation behaviors with a dominant period of 15, indicating fluctuations in the rate of change, particularly evident in the later stages of the simulation around steps 18 through 31. The Mann-Kendall test confirms that the trend is statistically significant and consistently increasing across all observed time windows.
