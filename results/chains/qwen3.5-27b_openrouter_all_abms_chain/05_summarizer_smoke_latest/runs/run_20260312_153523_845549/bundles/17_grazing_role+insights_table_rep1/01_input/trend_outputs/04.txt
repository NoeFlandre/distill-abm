The simulated mean green biomass follows a distinct U-shaped trajectory over the 100-timestep simulation. The system initializes at 45,000 units, representing the starting forage availability. An initial phase of depletion is observed, where grazing pressure likely exceeds regrowth rates, driving the biomass to a local minimum of 42,057 units at time step 27. Statistical analysis of the trend confirms significant downward pressure in the initial phase, specifically between steps 11 and 21. Following this nadir, the system undergoes a structural shift. From time step 33 onwards, a consistent and statistically significant increasing trend emerges and persists for the remainder of the run. By the final time step (100), the biomass not only recovers but surpasses initial levels, peaking at 60,535 units. This dynamic suggests an initial adjustment period where land management practices were perhaps misaligned with carrying capacity, but over time, the learning behaviors modeled allowed the herd management to adapt, leading to a net accumulation of forage and improved pasture health by the end of the simulation horizon.
