 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape .  The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands shows a consistent upward trend across all simulation runs . The linear and pronounced decrease suggests a lack of compensatory mechanisms, such as improved hunting efficiency or increased food availability, to offset the energy costs . For decision makers, this indicates that the current parameters result in unsustainable foraging conditions .  The total number of prey animals starts at approximately 250 and exhibits a rapid decline over the initial 500 steps . By around 1,000 steps, the population stabilizes near zero in most simulations, indicating near-extinction conditions . By step 1,500, nearly all simulations show the total energy of male animals at or effectively close to zero .
