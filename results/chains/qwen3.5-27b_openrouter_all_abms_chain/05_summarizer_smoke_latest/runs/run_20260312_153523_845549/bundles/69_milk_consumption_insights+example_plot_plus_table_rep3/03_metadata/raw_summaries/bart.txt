 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . The simulation uses both a theoretical grounding and empirical data to inform the ABM, with calibration performed against macro level data .  Average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at approximately 5.29 units per person at the start of the simulation . Trend stabilizes between steps 11 and 26, fluctuating within a narrow range between 1,183 and 1,265 units . Trend suggests that social influence and habit formation mechanisms effectively drive the population toward a new equilibrium preference .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at approximately 5.3 units at time step 0.3 . The mean habit factor applied to the whole milk choice function begins at 1.0000 and increases steadily to a final value of 1.0502 over the simulation duration . After step 25, the rate of decline slows, with consumption stabilizing in a range between 700 and 850 units .  The mean deviation between agents’ held health values and the implied health values of their milk choices begins at 0.0000 at step 0 and increases steadily to a maximum of 0.7251 by step 32 . For decision-makers, this widening deviation implies that over time, targeted interventions may need to be more segmented, as a unified population approach becomes less effective .
