The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. Consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at 5.29 units per person. A rapid increase occurs over the first 10 time steps, rising to over 1,200 units by step 11. This sharp initial growth suggests a swift shift in consumer behavior. From step 10 to step 25, the rate of decline slows, with consumption stabilizing in a range between 700 and 850 units. Multiple minor fluctuations occur during this period, including small peaks around steps 19 and 27, but no sustained reversal of the downward trend is observed. The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents starts at 0.0 at step 0 and shows a rapid increase, reaching approximately 0.55 by time step 5. This initial surge suggests a quick divergence in agent preferences, likely driven by the early activation of disposition mechanisms and the immediate influence of social networks.
