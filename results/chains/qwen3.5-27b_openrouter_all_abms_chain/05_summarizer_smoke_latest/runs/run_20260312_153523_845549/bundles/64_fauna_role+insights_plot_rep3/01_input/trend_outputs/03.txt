The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands shows a consistent upward trend across all simulation runs. Initially, the number of males killed increases rapidly, reaching approximately 120 by around 500 steps. After this point, the rate of increase slows down, and the cumulative number stabilizes between 100 and 130 from 1000 steps onwards. This trend suggests that forager bands achieve a significant level of hunting efficiency early on, which plateaus as the system reaches a dynamic equilibrium. The stabilization indicates that the population of male prey animals is being effectively managed, or that environmental constraints limit further hunting success. These findings highlight the importance of understanding early hunting dynamics and environmental carrying capacities in predicting long-term outcomes for megaherbivore populations.
