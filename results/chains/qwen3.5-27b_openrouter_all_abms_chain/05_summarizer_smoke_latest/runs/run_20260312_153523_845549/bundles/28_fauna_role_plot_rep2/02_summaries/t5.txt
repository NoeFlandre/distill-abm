The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape. It simulates a socio-ecological system containing human foragers and sexually dimorphic megaherbivore herds. The cumulative number of male prey animals killed by forager bands shows a steady pattern of rapid increase. This period reflects high hunting pressure, likely driven by the presence of a robust male population and effective foraging strategies. Between steps 500 and 1000, the rate of increase slows, with most simulations converging to 125 males killed. By step 1000, the majority of simulation runs reach a population count between 0 and 10. By step 2000, the number of male animals is consistently zero across all simulation runs, indicating the extinction of the male population within this timeframe.
