The decline is characterized by a strong negative trend, evidenced by statistical analysis showing consistently decreasing slopes across the majority of the timeline. While minor oscillations with a dominant period of approximately 63 steps are observed, particularly in the early phases around steps 385 and 730 where local energy peaks reached roughly 4,100 and 1,083 units respectively, these do not arrest the overall downward trajectory. The population crashes significantly between steps 0 and 500, followed by a slower, asymptotic approach to zero energy as the simulation progresses towards step 2311. This data indicates that under the specified parameters, specifically the interaction between human forager density and prey mortality, the megafaunal population is unable to sustain its energy reserves against predation and environmental constraints, leading to rapid extinction.
