A structural change point occurs at timestep 50, reinforcing the shift into a period of sustained recovery. The biomass levels rise steadily through the remainder of the simulation, culminating in a maximum value of 60,535 at timestep 100. This final value represents a substantial increase over the starting conditions. The statistical analysis confirms a strong upward trend across multiple rolling windows from the midpoint to the end of the simulation. In the context of the grazing management model, this pattern suggests that the initial stocking levels exert pressure on the pasture, but the integration of learning behaviors allows farmers to adjust their herds over time. The recovery phase indicates that as farmers gain experience or observe neighbors, they likely reduce grazing pressure to levels that support regrowth. For decision makers, this highlights that while short-term degradation may occur during the adoption phase of new management strategies, the long-term outcome favors pasture regeneration and increased biomass availability. Interventions during the first 30 timesteps could help minimize the initial dip in pasture health.
