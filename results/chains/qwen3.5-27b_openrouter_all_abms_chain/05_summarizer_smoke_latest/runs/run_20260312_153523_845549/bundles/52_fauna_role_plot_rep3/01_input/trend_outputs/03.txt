The cumulative number of male prey animals killed by forager bands exhibits a consistent pattern of rapid increase followed by a plateau across all simulation runs. Between steps 500 and 1000, the rate of increase slows, with the cumulative number of males killed reaching values between approximately 100 and 125. After step 1000, the growth in the cumulative number of males killed levels off, with most simulation runs stabilizing between 100 and 125 males killed by step 2000. This plateau suggests that the hunting pressure has reached a limit, potentially due to a decline in the male population, resource saturation, or other ecological constraints within the model.
