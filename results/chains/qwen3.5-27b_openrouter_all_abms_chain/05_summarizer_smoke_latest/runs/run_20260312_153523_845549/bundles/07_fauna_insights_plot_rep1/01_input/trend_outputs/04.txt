The cumulative number of female prey animals killed by forager bands increases rapidly from the start, reaching approximately 100 individuals by step 500 across all simulation runs. By step 1000, the majority of simulations stabilize, with the cumulative number of female kills plateauing between 120 and 125. A few simulations show a slower initial rise, reaching this plateau closer to step 1500. After step 1500, the number of cumulative kills remains constant in all runs, indicating that hunting of female prey animals ceases entirely. This trend suggests that early hunting pressure quickly depletes the available population of female prey or leads to a shift in hunting strategies, potentially impacting long-term herd demography and stability. Decision makers should consider the implications of high initial hunting pressure on herd sustainability and equilibrium conditions within the model.
