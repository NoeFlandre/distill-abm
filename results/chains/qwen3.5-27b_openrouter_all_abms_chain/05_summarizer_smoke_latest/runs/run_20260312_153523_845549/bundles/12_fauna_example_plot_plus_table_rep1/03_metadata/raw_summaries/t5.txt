the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . the model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the rate of increase is highest in the early phase, with a mean slope of 0.173 per step, indicating sustained and efficient predation . as the simulation progresses between 1,000 and 1,500 steps, the growth rate slows, reaching values near 118 by step 1,208 . the trend plateaus thereafter, fluctuating narrowly around 119 to 120 male deaths . by step 1200, the number of deaths stabilizes near 235, reaching a peak value of approximately 240.3 around step 1876 . this leveling off suggests that the prey population has stabilized at a lower density due to equilibrium conditions or has become too sparse to sustain high mortality rates . the variance among simulation runs is most pronounced during the rapid growth phase up to step 600 . the total energy of male prey animals starts at 12500 and declines rapidly over the first 400 time steps . this steep initial decrease suggests high predation pressure or intense resource consumption . the energy levels drop to approximately 4000 by step 377 . the vegetation cover demonstrates resilience to the pressures exerted by the prey population . maintaining a stable environment conducive to the continued existence of the herbivore herds .
