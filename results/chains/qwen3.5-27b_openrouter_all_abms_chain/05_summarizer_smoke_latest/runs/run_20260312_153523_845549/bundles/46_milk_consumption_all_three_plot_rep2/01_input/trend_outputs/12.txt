The mean value environmental deviation begins at zero at the initial time step, indicating a perfect alignment between agents' environmental values and their milk choices. This alignment rapidly deteriorates, with the deviation increasing sharply to approximately 10 by time step 3 and reaching roughly 13 by time step 5. From time step 10 onwards, the mean deviation stabilizes and plateaus near 13.5, maintaining this level with minimal variance for the remainder of the simulation up to time step 32. This trajectory suggests that while agents initially choose milk types that match their environmental beliefs, the dynamics of the model quickly introduce a significant and persistent misalignment between their values and their purchasing behavior. For decision makers, this implies that even in a system where social influence and individual disposition are active, a large and stable gap can form between consumer values and actions. This suggests that merely relying on social norms or individual habits may be insufficient to bridge the gap between environmental concern and sustainable consumption without more targeted interventions.
