The total energy of male prey animals starts at approximately 12,500 at the initial time step. This value experiences a rapid and nearly linear decline, reaching roughly 3,000 by step 350. A brief period of fluctuation occurs between steps 350 and 500, where the energy levels hover between 3,000 and 5,000 before resuming a downward trajectory. The decline continues until approximately step 700, where the total energy stabilizes near zero and remains at this level until the simulation concludes. Across all simulation runs, there is high variance in the exact timing of the energy depletion, though the general trend of rapid collapse followed by stabilization remains consistent. This suggests that under the given parameters, the combined effects of hunting pressure and environmental constraints lead to a swift reduction in the male prey population's energy reserves.
