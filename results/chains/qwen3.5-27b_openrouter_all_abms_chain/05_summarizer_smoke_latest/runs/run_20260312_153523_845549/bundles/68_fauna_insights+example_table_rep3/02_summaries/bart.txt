The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs. Ungulates are sexually dimorphic, reproduce, age, and move as herds. Conservation strategies should focus on mitigating impacts during this critical initial window to prevent long-term demographic erosion, as the majority of cumulative losses occur before the system stabilizes.
