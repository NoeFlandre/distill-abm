 The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others and susceptibility to social norms .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents consistently increased over the simulation period, starting from zero at the initial step and reaching 32,000 by the final step . This pattern suggests a growing shift towards lower-fat milk choices among the simulated consumers . The mean weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at 2654.81 units and follows a consistent downward trajectory .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at 1.0 at time step 0 and gradually increases, reaching 1.5879 by time step 32 . The Mann-Kendall test confirms a statistically significant increasing trend throughout the entire duration .
