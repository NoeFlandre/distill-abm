 This simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors . The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time . The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock .  The average amount of actual available green biomass starts at 45000 and experiences a slight initial increase before declining to reach its minimum value of 42057.0353 at step 27 . This early reduction in biomass likely reflects the initial impact of grazing pressure from the livestock, which begins to deplete the pasture resources . By step 100, the average biomass reaches its maximum value of 60535.2808 .  The number of households practicing social learning starts at zero across all simulation runs . The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households begins at zero and shows a rapid increase during the initial phase of the simulation, reaching approximately 80 by step 25 . The data indicates a statistically significant increasing trend throughout the initial and middle phases, with the strongest momentum occurring within the first 20 time steps .
