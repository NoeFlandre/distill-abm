The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at approximately 45,000 units. In the initial phase, specifically between time steps 0 and 15, there is a general decline in biomass, dropping to a low range between 40,000 and 43,000 units, with some variation in the timing of the nadir among different simulation runs. Following this decline, a distinct inflection point occurs around time step 35 to 40, where the trend reverses into a rapid, steep increase. During this growth phase, which extends to approximately time step 70, the biomass accumulates significantly, with most runs surpassing 60,000 units. By time step 80, the rate of growth decelerates, and the trajectories begin to plateau. At the conclusion of the simulation at time step 100, the mean green biomass stabilizes at high levels, ranging from approximately 61,000 to 64,000 units, indicating a recovery and saturation of the pasture resource.
