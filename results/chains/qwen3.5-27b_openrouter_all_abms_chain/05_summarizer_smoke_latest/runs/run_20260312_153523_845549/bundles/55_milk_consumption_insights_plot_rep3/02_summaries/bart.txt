The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice. An agent's milk choice function is modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices. Initially, skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption is near zero at time step 0. By time step 5, consumption rises sharply to approximately 900. By step 10, consumption reaches around 1250, followed by a plateau that persists until time step 20, where values hover between 1200 and 1250. Overall trend shows a significant reduction in whole milk consumption over the simulation period. The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents starts at 0.0 at step 0. This trend reflects the progressive strengthening of habitual behaviors in favor of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices over time. For decision makers, this trend implies that initial conditions and early social interactions play a critical role in shaping long-term consumption behaviors.
