The simulation tracks household risk attitude (r-parameter) across 100 steps for 60 households comprising three behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1). 

**Initial Conditions (Steps 0-10):**
Households begin with heterogeneous risk attitudes distributed between approximately -0.1 and +0.15. Most values cluster near zero or slightly below, with a few households starting at modest positive values around 0.1 to 0.15. The population remains relatively stable during this phase with minimal adjustment.

**Rapid Transition Phase (Steps 10-30):**
Beginning around step 10-15, the majority of households undergo sharp increases in risk attitude. By step 20, values span from approximately 0.0 to 0.65. The upward trajectory continues through step 30, where the population reaches maximum dispersion with some households attaining values of 0.75-0.8 (the upper bound observed in the simulation) while others stabilize at intermediate levels between 0.2 and 0.6. This period marks the most dynamic change, with slopes reaching steep positive rates.

**Differentiation and Crisis Phase (Steps 30-60):**
Between steps 30 and 45, most trajectories stabilize or exhibit minor fluctuations around established levels (0.6-0.8, 0.4-0.5, and 0.2-0.3). However, a notable divergence occurs between steps 40 and 55, where approximately 8-10 households experience catastrophic declines. These trajectories drop precipitously from moderate positive values (0.2-0.4) to strongly negative territory, bottoming out at approximately -0.3 to -0.35 by step 55-60. Concurrently, several households maintaining values around 0.3-0.4 exhibit volatility before settling or declining to near-zero levels.

**Long-term Stabilization (Steps 60-100):**
From step 60 onward, the population segregates into approximately seven distinct stable bands:
1. High risk-takers: 0.75-0.78 (2-3 households)
2. Upper-moderate: 0.68-0.72 (4-5 households)  
3. Mid-high: 0.60-0.65 (3-4 households)
4. Central cluster: 0.45-0.55 (8-10 households)
5. Conservative: 0.20-0.40 (10-12 households)
6. Near-neutral: -0.05 to +0.15 (15-18 households)
7. Highly conservative/negative: -0.30 to -0.20 (5-6 households)

The negative-valued group maintains stable positions around -0.35 to -0.20 throughout the final 40 steps, indicating persistent risk-averse behavior or substantial deviation below recommended livestock levels. The highest group sustains values near 0.8 with minimal variation (±0.02), suggesting robust adoption of risk-tolerant strategies. Intermediate groups exhibit minor oscillations of ±0.02 to ±0.05 but maintain clear separation, indicating that initial learning trajectories and social-ecological feedbacks lock households into distinct risk-management regimes.
