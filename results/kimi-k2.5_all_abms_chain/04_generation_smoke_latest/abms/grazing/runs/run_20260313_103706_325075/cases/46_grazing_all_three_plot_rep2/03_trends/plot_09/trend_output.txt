The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across households begins at zero and undergoes a rapid increase during the first approximately 20 time steps, reaching values predominantly between 50 and 80 animals. This initial surge reflects an adjustment period where farmers reduce herd sizes substantially, likely responding to feedback about pasture conditions or initial misalignment between stocking rates and carrying capacity.

After time step 20, the rates of destocking diverge markedly across simulation runs. Some households continue destocking at steady or accelerating rates throughout the 100-year period, with cumulative totals reaching 130 to 173 animals by the final time step. Conversely, another subset experiences a sharp reduction in destocking rates between time steps 30 and 40, with cumulative levels stabilizing at approximately 58 to 70 animals for the remainder of the simulation. Intermediate outcomes exhibit moderate, sustained increases, ending with cumulative totals between 80 and 120 animals.

The divergence patterns suggest that learning strategies fundamentally alter long-term stocking dynamics. Trajectories stabilizing at lower levels indicate households that successfully calibrated herd sizes to sustainable levels early in the simulation, minimizing the need for further animal sales. Conversely, trajectories showing continuous accumulation of destocked animals suggest ongoing struggles to maintain equilibrium, possibly due to delayed learning, conservative risk responses to rainfall variability, or reactive rather than proactive adjustment patterns.

For decision makers, the wide range in cumulative destocking outcomes—with some households requiring nearly three times more animal sales than others over the century—highlights the critical importance of learning approach selection. Strategies associated with the lower destocking trajectories offer substantial economic advantages by reducing forced asset liquidation, thereby preserving household wealth and productive capacity over multi-decadal timeframes under variable climatic conditions.
