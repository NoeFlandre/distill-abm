This reduction reflects rapid adaptive adjustments as households recalibrate stocking levels based on ecological feedback, with inflection points at steps 6 and 21 marking transitions in the rate of decline as different learning strategies begin to differentiate in their effectiveness.

After approximately step 30, the destocking trajectory stabilizes into a low-level equilibrium, fluctuating around a mean of 0.3 animals per household through the remainder of the 100-step simulation. While the aggregate trend remains essentially flat from step 30 to 100, with only negligible variations including a minor uptick between steps 66 and 76, individual simulation runs exhibit persistent divergence. Some trajectories stabilize near zero destocking, indicating successful long-term adaptation, while others maintain elevated baselines between 0.5 and 1.0 animals, suggesting that certain learning strategies or rainfall sequences result in chronic low-level resource shortages.
