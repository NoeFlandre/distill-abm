**Implications for Decision Makers**

Fixed rule-of-thumb strategies result in approximately 26% household attrition over a century under variable climatic conditions, indicating that non-adaptive management approaches carry substantial long-term risks in uncertain environments. The stabilization of remaining E-RO households after year 66 suggests that while some farmers may persist through fortuitous location or minimal stocking levels, reliance on experiential rules without learning mechanisms leads to significant population decline. For policy interventions, promoting adaptive learning strategies—whether through individual experience-sharing or social observation—appears critical for maintaining household persistence in stochastic grazing systems.
