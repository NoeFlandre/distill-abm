At initialization (Step 0), the Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock equals approximately 0.000, reflecting the homogeneous initial condition where all households possess identical livestock endowments (90 units each). Throughout the first 40 steps, inequality accumulation remains subdued, with coefficient values generally remaining below 0.020 despite minor fluctuations. This initial phase represents a period of relative equity where heterogeneous behavioral types—E-RO (baseline), E-LBD (learning-by-doing), and E-RO-SL1 (social learning)—have not yet generated substantial divergence in livestock health outcomes.

Between approximately steps 40 and 60, the system undergoes a marked transition characterized by accelerated inequality growth. During this window, Gini coefficients rise rapidly from roughly 0.020 to values ranging between 0.030 and 0.050 across simulations. This inflection suggests the onset of cumulative differentiation processes, where initial variations in risk management strategies and learning effectiveness begin amplifying under private property constraints and climatic variability (rain-mean: 200, rain-std: 100).

From step 60 to step 100, simulation trajectories diverge significantly, producing a broad distribution of inequality outcomes. Final Gini coefficients at step 100 range widely from approximately 0.030 in the most equitable realizations to over 0.095 (and approaching 0.100) in the most unequal scenarios. The majority of trajectories cluster between 0.060 and 0.090, indicating that substantial concentration of healthy livestock among fewer households becomes the dominant pattern, though high sensitivity to stochastic factors persists. This divergence occurs despite the presence of social learning mechanisms intended to facilitate adaptation through knowledge sharing.

**Implications for Decision Makers:**
Inequality in livestock health distribution appears inevitable in mixed-learning smallholder systems under variable climate conditions, but its magnitude is highly sensitive to early dynamics. The critical window for intervention occurs between steps 40 and 60, before divergence becomes entrenched. Policymakers should anticipate that learning mechanisms alone (social learning and learning-by-doing) may be insufficient to prevent high inequality (Gini >0.090), suggesting the need for complementary equity-focused policies such as safety nets or targeted support during the transition period. Monitoring systems should prioritize early detection of diverging trajectories before step 60 to prevent extreme concentration of livestock assets.
