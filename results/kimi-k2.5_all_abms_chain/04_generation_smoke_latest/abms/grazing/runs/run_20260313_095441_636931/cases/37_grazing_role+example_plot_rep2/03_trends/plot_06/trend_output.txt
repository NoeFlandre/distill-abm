The average number of livestock destocked per household begins at approximately zero, then rises sharply to reach peak values between 7.0 and 7.5 animals around time steps 5 to 8. This initial surge reflects the immediate resource pressure created by placing 90 animals on pastures with productivity limited by mean rainfall of 200 units and high inter-annual variability, forcing households to remove substantial portions of their herds due to insufficient forage.

Following this peak, destocking rates decline rapidly through approximately time step 25, dropping to values ranging between 0.5 and 2.0 animals. This steep reduction indicates that farmers across all three learning strategies—baseline rule-of-thumb, learning-by-doing, and social learning—are adjusting their stocking decisions in response to observed pasture conditions and neighbor outcomes, thereby reducing the mismatch between herd size and carrying capacity.

From time step 25 to approximately time step 45, the decline continues at a moderated pace, with most trajectories settling below 1.0 animal. After time step 45, the system stabilizes into a sustained low-destocking regime that persists through time step 100, with values fluctuating between 0 and approximately 0.8 animals for the majority of simulation runs.

One notable trajectory maintains persistently higher destocking rates throughout the simulation, stabilizing at approximately 1.0 animal from time step 45 onward. This outlier suggests that particular sequences of rainfall variability or specific interaction dynamics between the learning strategies can result in less optimal long-term outcomes in certain stochastic realizations.

The substantial divergence across simulation runs during the first 30 time steps, followed by convergence toward minimal destocking levels by time step 50, indicates that while stochastic environmental factors and initial risk dispositions initially create highly variable outcomes, the adaptive mechanisms generally enable households to achieve sustainable stocking levels where forced animal removal becomes a rare event.
