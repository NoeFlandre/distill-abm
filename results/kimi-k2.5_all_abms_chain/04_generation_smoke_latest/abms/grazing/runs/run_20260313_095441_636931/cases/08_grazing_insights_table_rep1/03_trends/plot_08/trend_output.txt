**Implications for Decision Makers:**

The diffusion of social learning requires substantial time to reach saturation—over 60 timesteps—suggesting that interventions promoting social learning should be implemented with long-term horizons and patience for gradual adoption. The persistence of high engagement levels once the plateau is reached (post-timestep 63) indicates that social learning becomes an embedded, stable behavior for approximately half the population rather than a temporary experimental strategy. However, the failure to reach universal adoption (plateauing at 53% rather than 100%) suggests the presence of structural barriers or differential suitability of social learning for specific household circumstances, warranting targeted investigation into why the remaining 47% of households do not engage. The early rapid uptake phase (first 20-30 timesteps) represents a critical window where initial conditions and early-adopter dynamics strongly influence the ultimate penetration rate, emphasizing the importance of securing early engagement to establish social learning norms.
