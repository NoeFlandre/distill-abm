**Implications for decision makers**: The rapid crystallization of risk attitudes within the first 20 years suggests that early intervention strategies, such as initial training or information provision, may have lasting effects on how farmers perceive and respond to uncertainty. The wide dispersion of final attitudes implies that uniform policy approaches may be ineffective, and targeted interventions accounting for divergent risk profiles developed through individual learning histories may be necessary for sustainable pasture management.
