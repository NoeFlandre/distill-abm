The average cumulative number of destocked livestock per household initiates at zero and increases monotonically throughout the 100-step simulation, ultimately reaching approximately 97 animals. The trajectory exhibits a pronounced deceleration pattern characterized by three distinct temporal regimes. During the initial phase spanning roughly the first 25 steps, destocking occurs at the highest rate, with the accumulation exceeding 4.9 animals per step in the first 10 steps alone. This rapid early reduction reflects the system's response to initial stocking levels of 90 animals per household under stochastic rainfall conditions (mean 200, standard deviation 100), as agents with heterogeneous risk attitudes and diverse learning mechanisms (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1 types) adjust to private pasture constraints and vegetation feedbacks.

A first regime transition occurs around step 25, marked by a substantial decline in the destocking rate to approximately 0.36 animals per step. This deceleration continues through step 60, where a second transition establishes a stabilized accumulation rate of roughly 0.31 animals per step for the remainder of the simulation. This persistent linear increase in the later period indicates ongoing environmental pressures requiring continued adaptive destocking despite learning processes and behavioral adjustments.

Superimposed on this long-term trend are oscillatory fluctuations concentrated around steps 22-26, 49-61, and 79-80, exhibiting a dominant periodicity of 49 steps. These cyclical variations align with the yearly vegetation dynamics cycle, wherein destocking decisions respond to seasonal biomass updates, livestock feeding periods, and household observations of reserve biomass states.

Substantial variability exists across the 30 simulation runs, with terminal cumulative destocking values diverging considerably between trajectories, ranging from approximately 58 to 173 animals per household. This divergence reflects the high sensitivity of household outcomes to stochastic rainfall variability and the interaction between different learning behaviors in the mixed population. The aggregate mean stabilizes around 75 animals across the simulation timeframe, indicating consistent upward pressure on destocking requirements throughout the 100-year sequence despite adaptive management capabilities.
