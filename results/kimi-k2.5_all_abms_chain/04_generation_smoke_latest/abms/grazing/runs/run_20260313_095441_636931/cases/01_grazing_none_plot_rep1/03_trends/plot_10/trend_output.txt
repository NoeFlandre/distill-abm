The ensemble of simulations tracking E-RO (non-learning) household counts over 100 steps reveals substantial trajectory divergence from a common initial condition of 20 households.

**Early Phase (Steps 0–20):**  
All simulation runs initiate with exactly 20 non-learning households. Within the first 20 steps, the population counts separate rapidly into three distinct dynamic regimes. One subset of trajectories exhibits sharp increases, reaching values between 30 and 40 households by step 15–20. A second subset declines precipitously to levels between 0 and 15 households. Notably, at least one trajectory maintains a constant value of exactly 20 households throughout the entire simulation duration without deviation.

**Middle Phase (Steps 20–40):**  
This period represents the point of maximum divergence among simulation runs. The high-growth trajectories plateau at peak values of approximately 40 households, sustaining levels between 35 and 40 until approximately step 35–40, after which they undergo a steep decline. By step 40, these trajectories fall to values between 23 and 34. Meanwhile, the declining trajectories continue their descent, with several reaching near-extinction levels of 0–5 households. Intermediate trajectories stabilize at distinct levels: approximately 26, 25, 23, 18, and 13 households. Some trajectories that had declined to the 10–15 range stabilize temporarily before further adjustment.

**Late Phase (Steps 40–100):**  
The system settles into persistent quasi-equilibrium states with minimal fluctuation. By step 100, the final distribution of non-learning households spans the full range from 0 to approximately 26 households. Distinct stable outcomes emerge at specific abundance levels: near-extinction (0–3 households), low persistence (8–10 households), moderate persistence (13 and 18 households), and high persistence (23–26 households). The trajectories that peaked at 40 households stabilize in the 23–26 range. Several trajectories stabilize at exactly 10 households, 13 households, or fall to and maintain 0 households from approximately step 65 onward. The trajectory fixed at 20 households remains unchanged throughout.

**Interpretation:**  
Under the parameter configuration with 60 total households initially divided equally among three behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1), the competition and potential strategy switching among behavioral types generates highly variable outcomes. Non-learning households may temporarily dominate the population (increasing to twice the initial count), maintain baseline levels, or decline to near-extinction. The stabilization of distinct abundance levels—particularly the convergence of multiple independent runs to specific values such as 10, 13, 23, and 26 households—suggests the existence of multiple stable attractors in the social-ecological system. The sensitivity of outcomes to stochastic processes occurring primarily within the first 40 steps determines the long-term persistence of non-learning behavioral strategies in the household population.
