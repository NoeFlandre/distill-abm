The subsequent recovery demonstrates that learning-based adjustments can restore and ultimately enhance biomass beyond baseline levels. Strategies that achieve the upper range of biomass outcomes (125,000–128,000 units) demonstrate significantly greater resilience and productive capacity than those plateauing at lower levels (111,000–115,000 units), suggesting that policy interventions supporting effective learning mechanisms could yield substantial ecological dividends over multi-decadal timeframes.
