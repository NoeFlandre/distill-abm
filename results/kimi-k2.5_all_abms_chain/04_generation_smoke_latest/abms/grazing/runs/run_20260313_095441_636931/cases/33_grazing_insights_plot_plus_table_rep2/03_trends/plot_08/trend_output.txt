The high variability in final adoption levels across simulation runs (ranging from 20% to 90% of the population) suggests that outcomes of social learning diffusion are inherently unpredictable for individual communities, even when underlying parameters are identical. This implies that policy interventions promoting adaptive management should not expect uniform adoption rates and should prepare for wide outcome variation.

Decision makers should recognize that universal adoption of social learning practices is unlikely and plan for heterogeneous behavioral landscapes.

The rapid initial uptake within the first seven steps suggests that when social learning diffusion occurs, it happens quickly in early stages, providing a critical window for intervention. Policies aimed at facilitating social learning should prioritize early-stage support, as the trajectory of adoption appears to become largely fixed within the first 40 steps.
