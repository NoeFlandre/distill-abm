Across the 30 simulation realizations, the number of households employing the non-learning E-RO strategy exhibits a marked divergence from initial conditions, coupled with a clear aggregate decline over the 100-year period. At initialization (timestep 0), all realizations converge at exactly 20 households, consistent with the experimental design assigning 20 agents to this behavioral type. This uniformity rapidly dissipates, however, as stochastic rainfall variability (mean 200 mm, standard deviation 100 mm) generates divergent trajectories. While individual realizations spread to encompass ranges between approximately 0 and 40 households by mid-century—indicating high sensitivity to initial environmental conditions and local feedbacks—the ensemble mean follows a distinct downward trajectory.

The aggregate series peaks slightly above initialization at timestep 8, reaching 21.1 households, suggesting a transient population increase before ecological feedbacks intensify. Following this peak, a statistically significant decline commences around timestep 10-12, with pronounced negative trends persisting through timestep 65 (Mann-Kendall tau ranging from -0.96 to -0.71, p<0.01 across consecutive windows). This decline exhibits structural breaks at timesteps 20 and 45, where the rate of attrition shifts, ultimately reducing the mean population from approximately 20 to 15 households.

After timestep 65, the decreasing trend dissipates, and the series stabilizes with no significant directional trend through the remainder of the simulation (windows 66-76, 77-87, and 88-98 show tau values near zero with p>0.05). The count settles around a median of 15.3 households, reaching a minimum value of 14.7 at timestep 92 and terminating at 14.7 households at year 100. This terminal value represents a 26.5% reduction from initial conditions, indicating that while the non-learning strategy experiences substantial attrition under variable rainfall and grazing pressure, a persistent residual population of approximately 15 households maintains this behavioral mode for the duration of the century.
