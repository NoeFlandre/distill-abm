At initialization, the population consists of 20 households employing the E-RO (non-learning) behavioral strategy. During the first 10 time steps, this count rises to a peak of approximately 21 households around time step 8, representing a brief initial increase in the adoption of baseline behaviors. Following this peak, a sustained decline commences between time steps 10 and 65, with the number of E-RO households decreasing significantly from roughly 20 to around 16. This downward trajectory continues through the middle phase of the simulation. From approximately time step 66 onward, the decline stabilizes, with the count oscillating without significant trend around 14.7 to 15 households through the end of the simulation at time step 100. Overall, the system exhibits a net reduction of approximately 5.3 E-RO households (a 26.5% decrease from initial conditions) over the 100 time-step period. This trend indicates that in the mixed population context containing E-RO-SL1 (social learning) and E-LBD (learning-by-doing) agents, non-learning E-RO households are gradually outcompeted or displaced over time, with the population composition shifting toward learning-capable behavioral types despite the initial transient increase in baseline strategy adoption.
