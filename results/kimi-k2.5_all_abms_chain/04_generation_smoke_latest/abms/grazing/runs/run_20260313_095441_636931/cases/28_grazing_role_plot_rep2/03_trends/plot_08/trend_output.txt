At timestep 0, the number of households engaged in social learning is zero across all simulation runs. Between approximately timesteps 5 and 25, the count increases rapidly and monotonically in a stepwise fashion, with most trajectories rising sharply from near-zero values to between 30 and 50 households. During this phase, discrete jumps occur at irregular intervals, indicating that households adopt social learning at specific time points rather than gradually.

Following the initial rapid adoption phase, the rate of increase slows considerably between timesteps 25 and 40, with occasional small incremental steps. By approximately timestep 40 to 60, the vast majority of trajectories stabilize at constant values, maintaining these levels through timestep 100 with no further changes.

Terminal values at timestep 100 exhibit substantial variation across runs, ranging from approximately 18 households at the minimum to 54 households at the maximum. This represents a spread from 30% to 90% of the total population of 60 households. Intermediate stable values cluster at approximately 18, 30, 36, 42, 48, and 54 households, suggesting the existence of discrete attractor states or adoption thresholds in the system dynamics.

The stepwise, monotonic increase observed in all trajectories indicates that social learning engagement is an absorbing state—once a household engages in social learning, it remains in that state for the duration of the simulation. The considerable variation in final counts, combined with the fact that the maximum exceeds the 20 households initially designated as the social learning type (E-RO-SL1), demonstrates that social learning spreads to or is adopted by households of other behavioral types (E-RO and E-LBD) during the simulation period, with the extent of this adoption varying stochastically across replicate runs.
