The average initial risk attitude among social learning agents remains constant at approximately 0.0074 throughout the entire 100-timestep simulation period. This stability reflects the static nature of the initial risk parameter, which is fixed at the outset of each simulation run to establish the baseline heterogeneity in uncertainty management among households.

For decision makers, this constancy indicates that the initial risk attitudes serve as persistent baseline characteristics that do not evolve through learning or experience during the simulation timeframe. Consequently, any variation in system outcomes across different simulation scenarios would primarily reflect the initial distribution of risk attitudes rather than endogenous adaptation of these dispositions.
