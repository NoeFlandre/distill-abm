The mean household risk attitude across the population begins at a very low value of approximately 0.007, indicating that agents initially operate with minimal risk aversion or uncertainty perception. During the first 32 time steps, this metric undergoes a rapid and significant increase, with statistically significant upward trends detected across consecutive windows from steps 0 through 32. By time step 33, the metric reaches its maximum value of approximately 0.38, suggesting a temporary convergence toward high risk aversion or elevated uncertainty management among households.

Following this peak, the metric enters a pronounced decline phase lasting from approximately time step 33 to step 65. During this period, statistically significant decreasing trends are observed across multiple consecutive windows, with values falling from the peak of 0.38 to approximately 0.27 by step 48, and continuing downward. This decline indicates a calibration process where households adjust their risk attitudes downward from the initial overestimation.

After time step 66, the metric stabilizes and exhibits no statistically significant trend through the remainder of the simulation, settling at approximately 0.19 by the final time step. This stabilization suggests that the learning and adaptation processes have converged, with the mixed population of E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1 behavioral types reaching a steady-state equilibrium in their aggregate uncertainty management parameters.

The observed dynamics reflect the model's learning mechanisms, particularly the social learning processes of E-RO-SL1 agents who update their r-parameters based on neighbor performance. The initial rapid increase suggests an early phase of information gathering and heightened caution, while the subsequent decline and stabilization indicate that agents learn to moderate their risk attitudes as they accumulate experience with local pasture conditions and neighbor strategies. For decision makers, these results suggest that interventions aimed at influencing risk perceptions should be targeted during the early simulation phases (first 30 time steps) before attitudes solidify, and that mixed learning populations naturally converge to moderate risk attitudes without external intervention, suggesting inherent resilience in the adaptive co-management system.
