**Implications for Decision Makers**

The rapid initial uptake of social learning within the first 30 timesteps indicates that early interventions promoting social learning mechanisms can achieve quick penetration in smallholder grazing communities. However, the pronounced saturation at approximately 53% adoption suggests inherent structural barriers—potentially stemming from fixed behavioral heterogeneity, limited knowledge radii, or risk attitude distributions—that prevent universal adoption even under stable climatic conditions. Policymakers should anticipate that social learning will likely stabilize among a subset of households rather than diffusing throughout the entire population, and should design resilience strategies that leverage the socially learning cohort while addressing the information needs of non-adopting households through alternative channels.
