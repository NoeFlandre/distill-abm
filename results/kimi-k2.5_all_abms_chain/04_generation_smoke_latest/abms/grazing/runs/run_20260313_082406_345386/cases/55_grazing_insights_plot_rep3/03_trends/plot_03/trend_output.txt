The consistent recovery to levels substantially above initial conditions indicates that promoting learning-by-doing and social learning behaviors can enhance long-term ecological resilience. However, the broad variation in final outcomes implies that uniform policy approaches may yield inconsistent results; instead, supporting diverse learning strategies and accounting for household heterogeneity in risk management may be necessary to ensure reliable pasture rehabilitation across different contextual settings.
