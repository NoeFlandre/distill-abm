The mean number of livestock destocked per household initiates at zero across all simulation runs. Within the first two to five steps, values surge sharply to maxima ranging between approximately 6.0 and 7.5, reflecting immediate and substantial herd reductions as the initial livestock population of 90 animals exceeds the sustainable carrying capacity of private pasture patches. This initial destocking event represents the system's abrupt adjustment to founding conditions where available green biomass cannot support the initial grazing pressure.

Following this peak, destocking rates decline rapidly over the subsequent fifteen to twenty steps, decreasing from their maxima to ranges predominantly between 0.5 and 2.5 by step 20. This rapid decrease indicates that households quickly adjust their livestock management strategies, reducing herds to levels more commensurate with local resource availability.

Between steps 20 and 40, destocking continues to diminish but at a reduced pace and with pronounced volatility, exhibiting oscillations between roughly 0.2 and 2.0. These fluctuations likely stem from the interaction between stochastic rainfall variations (with mean 200 and standard deviation 100) and the heterogeneous decision-making processes of the three behavioral types—E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1—as they adapt their risk parameters and grazing allocations in response to observed vegetation states.

After approximately step 40, the metric stabilizes, converging to distinct steady-state levels ranging from near zero to approximately 1.0 by step 100, where it remains for the duration of the simulation. The persistence of variation across runs at equilibrium, with some outcomes approaching zero destocking while others stabilize near 1.0, indicates that initial stochastic variations in rainfall and vegetation dynamics, combined with specific trajectories of learning-by-doing and social learning processes, generate divergent long-term resilience outcomes among households despite identical starting conditions and behavioral compositions.
