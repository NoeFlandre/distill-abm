The metric tracks the number of households employing the E-RO (experiential rule-of-thumb) non-learning strategy across 100 simulation steps, beginning with an initial count of 20 households in all trajectories.

**Early Phase Dynamics (Steps 0-25):**
The simulations exhibit pronounced divergence within the first 20 steps. In several trajectories, the count rises sharply, peaking between 30 and 42 households around steps 10-25. This surge suggests that under initial environmental variability (rain-std=100), households with learning capabilities—particularly the 20 E-RO-SL1 social learners—temporarily revert to rule-of-thumb behaviors when social information becomes unreliable or environmental feedback is ambiguous. Conversely, other trajectories show immediate declines, with counts falling to between 5 and 15 households by step 20, indicating that the baseline E-RO households are vulnerable to exit or failure under certain stochastic rainfall realizations.

**Transition Phase (Steps 25-50):**
Trajectories that spiked early generally experience a decline between steps 30 and 50, stabilizing from peaks of 35-42 down to levels between 23 and 26. This pattern suggests that as the system adjusts or as learning households regain capacity to process environmental feedback, some transition out of pure rule-of-thumb behavior. Meanwhile, trajectories with declining counts continue to decrease, with several reaching near-zero levels (0-2 households) by step 60, implying complete exit of non-learning households from the system in those simulation runs.

**Long-term Stabilization (Steps 50-100):**
By the final phase, the trajectories stabilize across a wide spectrum of outcomes, ranging from approximately 0 to 26 households. Distinct clusters emerge: a high cluster stabilizing between 24-26 (suggesting scenarios where the original E-RO households persist and some social learning households remain in or revert to non-learning modes), a middle cluster around 18, lower clusters at 13 and 8-10, and minimal clusters near 2-5 or zero. This broad distribution indicates high sensitivity to stochastic environmental conditions—under identical initial conditions, the robustness of non-learning strategies varies dramatically, with some realizations supporting persistence of 25+ non-learning households while others result in their effective elimination from the system.

**Key Observation:**
The horizontal reference at 20 households represents the initial condition, with final outcomes distributed both above and below this line. Values exceeding 20 suggest that social learning households (E-RO-SL1) adopt non-learning behaviors under stress, while values below 20 indicate mortality or exit of the dedicated E-RO households, which occurs in approximately half of the simulation runs.
