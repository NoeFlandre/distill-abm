The rate of decline moderates significantly after step 20, transitioning from a steep decrease to a slower reduction until reaching the nadir at step 37.

Between steps 33 and 43, the aggregate series exhibits stabilization with no significant directional trend, suggesting a temporary equilibrium where destocking rates balance with remaining livestock holdings. This valley period marks a critical transition point in the social-ecological system. Following step 40, the aggregate mean enters a sustained recovery phase characterized by monotonic increase through step 100, though the rate of increase diminishes progressively over time. From steps 44 to 54, the increase occurs at approximately 0.31 units per step, slowing to 0.15 units per step during steps 55 to 65, and further decelerating to 0.005 units per step by steps 77 to 87. By the conclusion of the simulation at step 100, the aggregate mean stabilizes at approximately 47.5 units, representing roughly 53 percent of the initial livestock endowment.

While the aggregate mean converges toward 47 to 48 units, individual trajectories diverge significantly after step 40, with outcomes ranging from approximately 41 to 45 units in poorly performing cases to 58 to 62 units in top-performing scenarios by step 100. This divergence becomes pronounced after step 55, indicating that learning capabilities—specifically learning-by-doing and social learning mechanisms—generate markedly different resilience outcomes despite identical initial conditions and climatic parameters. The baseline behavioral type likely drives the lower bound of this distribution, while agents employing social learning or learning-by-doing achieve superior recovery rates.

The oscillation dynamics exhibit a dominant period of approximately 49 steps, corresponding to the complete cycle from the initial collapse through the recovery phase toward the new equilibrium. Change points detected at steps 20, 55, and 100 demarcate transitions from crisis to stabilization, then to differential recovery, and finally to long-term equilibration.

For decision makers, these dynamics indicate that initial high stocking densities inevitably trigger significant herd reductions within the first 40 steps regardless of behavioral type, implying that preventive destocking or conservative initial allocations are critical for avoiding severe livelihood losses. The subsequent divergence demonstrates that investing in social learning infrastructure and experiential adaptation capacities yields substantially better long-term outcomes, with effective strategies achieving approximately 30 percent higher terminal herd sizes than baseline approaches. Recovery trajectories require approximately 60 steps to approach stability, suggesting that resilience interventions and policy evaluations in private grazing systems must adopt multi-decadal horizons rather than short-term crisis response frameworks.
