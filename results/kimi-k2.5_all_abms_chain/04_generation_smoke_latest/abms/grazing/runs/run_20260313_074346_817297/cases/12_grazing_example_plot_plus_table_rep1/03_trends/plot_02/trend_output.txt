Between steps 33 and 50, the system transitions into a decline phase characterized by statistically significant decreasing trends in risk attitudes. The aggregate mean decreases from its peak through this interval, reaching an inflection point at step 50. Substantial bifurcation emerges during this period: while some households maintain elevated risk attitudes stabilizing between 0.6 and 0.8, others experience sharp reductions, with trajectories falling to negative values as low as -0.4 by step 60. This divergence likely corresponds to the differential performance of the three behavioral types—E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1—as households employing learning-by-doing and social learning processes update their uncertainty parameters in response to observed ecological feedbacks and neighbor performance.

From step 50 through step 100, risk attitudes stabilize with no significant directional trend. The aggregate mean settles at 0.1911 by the conclusion of the simulation, representing a net increase from initial conditions but remaining below the mid-simulation peak. Individual simulation runs display persistent heterogeneity, with final values clustering into distinct bands: high-risk-attitude households maintaining values near 0.7-0.8, moderate clusters around 0.4-0.6, and low or negative attitudes near -0.3. The system exhibits an oscillation cycle of approximately 49 steps throughout the simulation, indicating periodic adjustment patterns in household decision-making.

This path-dependent outcome suggests that early simulation experiences (within the first 20 steps) critically determine long-term household uncertainty management approaches in the grazing system.
