This represents a period of active adjustment where households rapidly reduce livestock numbers in response to initial pasture conditions and learning experiences.

Following the change point at approximately step 25, the system enters a transitional phase lasting until around step 60, where the rate of destocking continues to increase but at a progressively declining rate, with slopes decreasing from approximately 0.7 to 0.3 units per step. During this middle period, households continue to accumulate destocking totals but at a moderated pace as they refine management strategies based on accumulated experience.

From approximately step 60 onward, the system stabilizes into a late phase characterized by a relatively constant linear increase in destock totals, maintaining a consistent slope of approximately 0.31 units per step through the remainder of the simulation. This steady accumulation suggests that households have settled into stable management routines where destocking occurs at a predictable background rate.

Across the 30 simulation runs, substantial variation emerges in outcomes, with individual trajectories diverging significantly over time. While the aggregate mean ends at approximately 96.8 units, individual runs exhibit final values ranging from approximately 60 to over 170 units, with a standard deviation of approximately 20.3 units by step 100. This growing variance indicates that different households or simulation trajectories experience markedly different cumulative pressure to reduce livestock numbers, likely reflecting the different learning strategies and stochastic environmental conditions encountered.

In the context of the grazing system model, the increasing cumulative destock totals represent the ongoing adaptive response of smallholder farmers to variable rainfall, declining pasture conditions, and learning processes. The three-phase pattern suggests an initial period of crisis adjustment followed by strategic refinement and eventual stabilization of stocking strategies. The persistent positive trend indicates that under the modeled conditions of private pasture management with the specified learning strategies, households continuously accumulate destocking events over the century-long simulation, reflecting ongoing pressure to balance livestock numbers with available forage resources in a variable climate.
