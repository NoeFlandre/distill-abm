This initial collapse reflects intense hunting pressure from the forager population combined with the demographic vulnerability of the prey during the early establishment phase.

Between time steps 375 and 410, the decline temporarily stabilizes into an oscillating pattern, with values fluctuating between approximately 2,094 and 2,233 units. These oscillations, occurring with a dominant period of roughly 63 time steps, likely correspond to intermittent reproductive events within the female population—specifically, the addition of nursing offspring increasing maternal energy values—followed by renewed hunting pressure depleting these gains. Despite these temporary fluctuations, the overall trajectory remains downward.

Following this oscillatory phase, the energy resumes its decline, though at variable rates as indicated by multiple inflection points occurring at time steps 472, 506, 744, and 836. The rate of decline slows considerably after step 875, with the rolling trend analysis revealing periods of slight increase between steps 1,160 and 1,391, and again between 1,856 and 2,087. These brief upward trends suggest temporary demographic recoveries where birth rates momentarily outpaced hunting mortality, likely due to reduced forager efficiency or spatial refugia in the wrapped universe.

However, these recoveries prove insufficient to reverse the long-term trajectory. After step 1,400, the energy enters a final phase of terminal decline, passing below 1,000 units and continuing to fall through subsequent change points at 1,594, 1,679, 1,763, and beyond. The population experiences increasingly severe bottlenecks as energy levels drop below 500 units around step 1,800, and finally below 100 units by step 2,000. The simulation concludes at step 2,311 with the total energy reaching exactly zero, indicating complete energetic depletion and functional extinction of the female prey population.
