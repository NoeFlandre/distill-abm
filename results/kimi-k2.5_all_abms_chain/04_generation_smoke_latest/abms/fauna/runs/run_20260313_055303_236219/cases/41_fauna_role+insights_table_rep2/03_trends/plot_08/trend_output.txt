**Implications for Decision Makers:**

These dynamics indicate that under conditions of continuous hunting pressure combined with strong environmental seasonality, megaherbivore populations may collapse within a single decade despite initial abundances exceeding 100 individuals. The transient recovery observed in year four demonstrates that population inertia can generate false signals of resilience, potentially delaying necessary conservation interventions while the population remains in a cryptic extinction vortex. The sex-specific vulnerability evidenced by the complete loss of males before the simulation limit implies that harvest strategies must avoid male-biased exploitation to prevent reproductive collapse. Conservation protocols should implement immediate, sustained hunting moratoria once populations fall below approximately 20 individuals, as the subsequent low-density phase permits no viable recovery windows despite temporary stabilization.
