The cumulative number of prey deaths increases monotonically throughout the simulation period across all repeated runs, beginning at zero and rising to plateau values between approximately 220 and 250 deaths by the conclusion of the observation window. The temporal progression exhibits three distinct phases: an initial rapid accumulation phase spanning roughly the first 500 time steps, during which the cumulative death count rises steeply to values between 150 and 230; a transitional slowing phase between approximately 500 and 1000 time steps, where the rate of increase diminishes substantially as values approach 200 to 240; and a final plateau phase beyond 1000 steps, where cumulative deaths stabilize with minimal additional accumulation, indicating cessation of mortality events.

Considerable stochastic variation exists across simulation runs, particularly in the timing of mortality accumulation during the initial phase. While most runs follow similar trajectories reaching plateau values near 245 by approximately 1500 to 2000 steps, individual runs exhibit divergent patterns, with one run showing markedly slower mortality accumulation that reaches only approximately 225 by step 1500. The final plateau values approaching the initial prey population size of 250 indicate near-complete turnover of the founding population through combined natural mortality and predation pressure over the simulated temporal span equivalent to approximately 200 years given the parameterization of 365 ticks per year.
