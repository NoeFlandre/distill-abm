The simulation results indicate a consistent pattern of population collapse across all 120 replicates, with the megaherbivore population initiating at approximately 250 individuals and undergoing a sustained decline to extinction.

The population experienced a precipitous drop during the initial phase, with significant change points in the decline rate occurring at approximately 125, 475, 625, and 875 time steps. From the initial condition, the population fell rapidly to below 50 individuals within the first 500 time steps, and subsequently declined to fewer than 10 individuals by approximately 750 to 1000 time steps. Statistical testing confirms strongly significant monotonic decreasing trends throughout the initial 1400 time steps, with negative slopes ranging from -0.32 to -0.001, indicating an initially steep descent that gradually attenuated as population density decreased.

Superimposed on this declining trajectory, the dynamics exhibited periodic oscillations with a dominant cycle of approximately 63 time steps, consistent with the model's strong seasonal environmental forcing. These oscillations created temporary local increases in population numbers, with statistical windows between time steps 1392-1623 and 1856-2087 showing brief intervals of increasing trend (tau=0.203 and tau=0.445 respectively), punctuating the overall negative trajectory.

By approximately 1500 to 2300 time steps, the population reached complete extinction across all simulation replicates, with the final recorded value being zero. The aggregate statistics reveal a median population value of 1.48 across the entire simulation duration, contrasting with the initial population of 250 and indicating that populations remained at very low levels for the majority of the observation period. The high variance (2015.71) relative to the mean (25.01) reflects substantial heterogeneity in extinction timing across different model runs, with some populations persisting longer than others while ultimately converging on the same outcome.

This pattern demonstrates that under the configured parameter regime—incorporating 20 human hunters operating under dynamic optimal foraging theory with immortal status and food storage capabilities, high environmental seasonality (0.8), and the specified life history parameters including sexual dimorphism and interbirth intervals of 730 ticks—the megaherbivore population collapses from 250 individuals to extinction within a timeframe well short of the maximum simulation duration of 73,000 ticks.
