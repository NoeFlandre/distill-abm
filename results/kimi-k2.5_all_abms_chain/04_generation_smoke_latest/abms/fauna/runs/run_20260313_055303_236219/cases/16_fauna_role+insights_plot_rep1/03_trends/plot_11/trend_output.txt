**Implications for decision makers:** The consistent collapse of male energetic resources within 1,500 steps (approximately 4.1 years at the calibrated temporal scale) across replicate simulations indicates that current hunting pressure and environmental parameters generate unsustainable extraction rates for the male megaherbivore subpopulation. The parameter combination of immortal hunters with food storage capabilities targeting high-value males in seasonal environments with restricted vegetation coverage creates a demographic sink that rapidly eliminates the breeding male cohort. Management interventions should focus on reducing hunter encounter rates with males or modifying the sexual value differential to prevent population collapse, as the current configuration leads to near-certain male extinction regardless of stochastic environmental variation. Conservation strategies must specifically address hunter selectivity for large-bodied males, as this trait combination drives the rapid energetic trajectories observed.
