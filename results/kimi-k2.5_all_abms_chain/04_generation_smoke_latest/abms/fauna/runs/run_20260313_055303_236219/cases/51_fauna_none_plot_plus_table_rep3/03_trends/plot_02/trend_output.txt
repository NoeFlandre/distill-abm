The aggregate energy status of forager bands exhibits a continuous and pronounced decline throughout the simulation period. At initialization, the total energy across all foragers begins at 2000 units. During the initial phase (approximately steps 0–650), the energy declines at a moderate rate, though with some short-term fluctuations including a temporary elevation to near 2000 units around step 2 and subsequent dips.

Between steps 650 and 1160, the rate of energy depletion accelerates substantially. The decline steepens, with the slope of decrease reaching approximately -15.7 to -20.0 energy units per step during windows 464–695 through 1160–1391. This acceleration phase transitions around step 1075–1160, after which the rate of decline stabilizes.

From approximately step 1160 through the end of the simulation at step 2312, the energy continues to decrease at a consistent rate of roughly 20 units per step. Throughout this period, the total energy remains in deeply negative territory, indicating sustained energy debt across the forager population. By step 2312, the aggregate energy reaches approximately -38,229 units, representing the minimum value observed.

Superimposed on this long-term declining trend are oscillatory patterns with a dominant period of approximately 63 steps, particularly evident in the later stages of the simulation (steps 2170–2265). These cyclical variations suggest periodic fluctuations in forager energy acquisition or expenditure, though they do not reverse the overall negative trajectory. The consistent decline across all 40 simulation runs indicates that forager bands collectively experience insufficient energy returns from hunting to offset movement and processing costs, resulting in progressive energy depletion throughout the experimental period.
