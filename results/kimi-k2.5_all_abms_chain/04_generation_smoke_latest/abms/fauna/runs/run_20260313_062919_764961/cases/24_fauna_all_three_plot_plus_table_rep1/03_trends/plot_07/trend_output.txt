After approximately step 1000, the cumulative death count stabilizes, fluctuating narrowly around a mean of approximately 235 to 240 deaths through the remainder of the simulation extending to 2000+ steps. This plateau indicates that the system has reached a quasi-equilibrium where the mortality rate has declined to match the recruitment rate from births, or alternatively, that the population has been reduced to a level where further deaths occur at a very low rate.

The maximum cumulative mortality observed across simulation runs reaches approximately 240 individuals, representing nearly the entire initial population of 250 animals. Conservation or management interventions would need to be implemented rapidly to be effective, as the mortality rate slows significantly after this initial period, potentially locking the population into a depleted state. The eventual stabilization suggests that while local extinction may not occur immediately, the population persists at a substantially reduced level, which could have long-term viability implications for the species.
