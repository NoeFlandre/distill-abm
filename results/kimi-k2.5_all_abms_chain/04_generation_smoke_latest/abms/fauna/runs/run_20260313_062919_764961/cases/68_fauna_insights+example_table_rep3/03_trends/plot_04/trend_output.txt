**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The rapid accumulation of female mortality within the first 375 ticks (approximately one year) indicates that harvesting pressures quickly exceed sustainable thresholds, compromising population viability early in the interaction cycle. The brief mid-simulation plateau may offer a narrow window for intervention where reduced hunting intensity could theoretically stabilize herds, but the subsequent resurgence and terminal decline demonstrate that without dramatic regulatory measures, the system inevitably progresses toward functional extinction of females within roughly five years. The persistent 63-tick oscillation suggests that seasonal management strategies would have predictable, recurring windows of effectiveness, but cannot alone counteract the overarching depletion trend.
