The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the simulation duration from step 0 through step 2312. This value exhibits zero variance with no detectable peaks, valleys, or directional trends, maintaining identical start and end values across all 40 aggregated simulation runs.

This absolute stability reflects the model's configuration establishing an initial hunter population of 20 agents with the immortality parameter enabled and no reproductive capability for human agents. Consequently, the hunter population is insulated from demographic fluctuations, remaining unaffected by energy depletion, mortality risks, or recruitment dynamics regardless of environmental conditions, prey availability, or hunting expenditure.

For decision makers, this fixed hunter density functions as a critical experimental control that isolates hunting pressure from population dynamics. Any observed variations in megaherbivore extinction probabilities, herd demographic shifts, or spatial movement patterns can be attributed directly to environmental parameters—such as seasonality, grass regrowth rates, or prey life history traits—rather than changes in predator density. This configuration enables precise attribution of causality, ensuring that outcomes reflect the interaction between constant hunting pressure and dynamic ecological feedback loops.
