The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation duration of 2312 time steps. The population starts at 20 bands, maintains this exact value without deviation at every recorded interval, and concludes at 20 bands when the simulation terminates. There is no variance in the time series, with standard deviation and variance both equal to zero, indicating no births, deaths, or migration events affecting hunter numbers. This static dynamics reflects the parameter configuration where foragers are immortal and do not experience mortality from energy depletion, combined with the absence of demographic turnover or recruitment mechanisms for human agents in the model design. Consequently, the hunting pressure exerted on megaherbivore populations remains demographically stable over time, serving as a controlled baseline against which to measure the effects of environmental variability and herbivore life history traits on population viability.
