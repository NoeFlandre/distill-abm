The intermediate phase spanning steps 390 to 875 transitions to a decelerated decline characterized by high variance and temporary recruitment pulses. The terminal phase commences around step 875 and extends to step 2284, characterized by a protracted low-density decline fluctuating between 0 and 5 individuals. Change points at steps 875 and 2310 bracket this extinction vortex, where small cohorts persist for extended durations (evidenced by oscillation periods averaging 63 steps) but ultimately succumb to stochastic mortality processes. The median population value of 1.3462 across the full simulation underscores the asymmetry of the decline, with the majority of temporal samples concentrated near extinction thresholds after step 1000.

**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The trajectories indicate that current hunting pressures (20 foragers) on a population of 250 ungulates generate unsustainable male-specific mortality leading to guaranteed extinction within 6-7 years. Management interventions must prioritize reducing hunter numbers or foraging radii before step 390 (the first inflection point) to prevent the initial demographic collapse. The temporary recruitment peaks at biennial intervals (step 730) suggest that hunting mortariums synchronized with calving seasons could briefly extend population persistence, though the terminal decline phase demonstrates that once male numbers fall below 5 individuals, recovery becomes demographically impossible regardless of environmental conditions.
