**Phase 2: Deceleration and Asymptotic Approach (Ticks 475–1600)**
Following tick 475, the accumulation rate undergoes a marked deceleration, with the slope of cumulative kills decreasing progressively through ticks 625, 850, and 1200. The temporary equilibrium around 120 cumulative kills indicates a hunting pressure that has effectively suppressed the female population to levels where further encounters become stochastic rare events.

**Phase 3: Brief Resurgence (Ticks 1600–1850)**

The brief nature of this increase suggests rapid depletion of these final accessible individuals.

**Phase 4: Terminal Stabilization and Decline (Ticks 1850–2312)**
Following the peak at tick 1826, the cumulative count exhibits a slight decline, stabilizing at approximately 123 individuals by the simulation termination at tick 2312. The 63-tick cyclicality likely corresponds to seasonal hunting patterns or demographic cycles within the prey population, superimposed upon a system where cumulative hunting pressure has effectively removed the majority of reproductive females from the population.
