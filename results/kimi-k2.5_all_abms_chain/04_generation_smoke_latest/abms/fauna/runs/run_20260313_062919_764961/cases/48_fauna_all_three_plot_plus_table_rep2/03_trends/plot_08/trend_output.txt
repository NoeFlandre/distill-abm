For decision makers: The simulation results demonstrate that hunting pressure from 20 human foragers represents a catastrophic threat to male megaherbivore populations, driving complete extinction within a short timeframe. The brief period of low-level persistence suggests that while the population exhibits some demographic resilience through breeding, this is insufficient to counteract hunting mortality. Management strategies would need to either substantially reduce hunter numbers, restrict hunting access during critical breeding periods, or enhance habitat productivity to potentially alter this trajectory toward extinction.
