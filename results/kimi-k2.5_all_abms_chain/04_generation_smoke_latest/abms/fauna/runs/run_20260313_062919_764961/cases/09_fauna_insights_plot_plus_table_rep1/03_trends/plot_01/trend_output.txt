The number of foragers remained constant at 20 agents throughout the entire simulation duration, persisting from initialization through 2,312 time steps without deviation. The metric exhibited zero variance and zero standard deviation, with minimum, maximum, mean, and median values all fixed at 20. This absolute stability reflects the model configuration wherein foragers are immortal and do not reproduce, thereby maintaining the initial population established at setup without susceptibility to energy depletion mortality or other demographic changes.

For decision makers, this constant forager population establishes a controlled baseline of steady hunting pressure. Without fluctuations in predator numbers, any observed extinction dynamics or changes in prey demography can be attributed to environmental factors, prey life history traits, or the fixed intensity of hunting pressure itself, rather than to feedback between prey availability and hunter population density. This configuration isolates the impact of forager behavior and prey vulnerability from complicating density-dependent predator dynamics, allowing clearer attribution of extinction causality to the specific experimental variables under investigation.
