Across all simulation repetitions, the population of female prey animals initiates at approximately 125 individuals and exhibits a consistent downward trajectory toward extinction, though with substantial stochastic variation in the rate of decline.

The most rapid population collapses reach fewer than 10 individuals by step 300-400 and effective extinction (0-2 individuals) by step 400-500. Conversely, the most gradual declines maintain populations above 100 individuals through step 200, above 50 individuals through approximately step 400-500, and above 20 individuals through step 600. By step 750, most populations across runs have declined below 20 individuals.

The majority of simulation runs reach effective extinction between steps 800 and 1200. However, a minority of runs demonstrate extended persistence, with small populations of 1-5 individuals surviving until steps 1400-2200.

Under the parameter configuration with 20 immortal hunters, differential processing costs favoring female predation (3 versus 6 for males), food values of 27-30, high environmental seasonality (0.8), and megaherbivore life history constraints (730-tick birth spacing, senescence at age 7300), the prey population experiences unsustainable mortality pressure. The consistency of extinction outcomes indicates that hunting pressure systematically exceeds the population's reproductive replacement capacity, overwhelming the initial population of 125 females.

**Implications for Decision Makers:** The inevitability of extinction across all runs suggests the current hunter density and hunting efficiency parameters create a non-viable scenario for megaherbivore persistence. Interventions would need to substantially reduce hunter numbers (below the current 20 foragers), increase processing costs or movement costs for hunters, or enhance prey fecundity through reduced birth spacing intervals to achieve population sustainability. While stochastic demographic and environmental factors can delay extinction by several hundred steps, they cannot prevent it under the current parameter regime.
