Statistical trends indicate increasing vegetation coverage during the recovery phase (approximately ticks 0–1600) followed by slight fluctuations in later periods. By the conclusion of the series at tick 2312, vegetation coverage returns to approximately 33.0%, effectively matching initial conditions. This stability indicates that grass regrowth mechanisms successfully balance herbivore consumption, maintaining constant baseline fodder availability despite continuous grazing pressure from the megaherbivore population under the configured seasonal and regrowth parameters.
