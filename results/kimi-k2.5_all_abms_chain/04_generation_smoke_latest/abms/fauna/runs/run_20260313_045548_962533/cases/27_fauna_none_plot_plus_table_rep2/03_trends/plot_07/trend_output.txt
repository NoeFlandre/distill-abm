The cumulative mortality of megaherbivore prey follows a sigmoidal trajectory over the simulation duration. Beginning from zero deaths at initialization, mortality accumulates rapidly during the initial approximately 500 steps (roughly 1.4 years), rising sharply to reach between 200 and 230 cumulative deaths. This phase contains distinct inflection points at steps 125, 250, 375, and 500, marking discrete transitions in mortality rates as hunting pressure establishes and the initial population of 250 prey individuals experiences heavy turnover. The acceleration of deaths is maximal during the first 362 steps, after which the rate of increase moderates significantly.

Between steps 500 and 1200, mortality continues to accumulate but at a substantially decelerated pace, approaching an asymptotic range of 235–240 total deaths. Statistical trend analyses indicate a shift from strongly increasing patterns in early time windows to negligible trends between steps 1160 and 1391, signaling that the death rate is approaching equilibrium with population recruitment. By step 1876, the cumulative mortality reaches its maximum observed value of approximately 240 individuals, representing near-complete turnover of the initial prey cohort.

From roughly step 1200 onward, the cumulative death count stabilizes, exhibiting minor fluctuations around 235–240 deaths while maintaining a plateau through the remainder of the observation period. Notably, between steps 2170 and 2265, the stable cumulative total displays superimposed oscillations with a dominant period of approximately 63 steps (roughly two months), suggesting the emergence of regular demographic cycles or seasonal fluctuations in mortality rates. These late-stage dynamics indicate that the prey population has settled into an equilibrium state where deaths from predation and natural causes (including senescence after step 7300 for individuals reaching maximum lifespan) are balanced by births occurring at the specified interval of 730 steps (two years), resulting in a stable population with sustained but fluctuating cumulative mortality.
