Occasional deviations reduce coverage to 29–32% at irregular intervals, notably around steps 700–800, 1000, and 1400–1500, before the system rebounds.

Statistical trend analysis reveals predominantly increasing tendencies through the first 1,623 time steps, indicating progressive stabilization following the initial seasonal shock, while subsequent windows exhibit weak decreasing trends. The modest standard deviation of 0.95 percentage points, combined with the proximity of mean and maximum values, indicates that vegetation coverage spends the majority of the simulation near full capacity, with brief seasonal depressions creating the primary dynamic variation in fodder availability.
