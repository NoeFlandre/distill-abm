**Phase 3: Terminal Decline (Ticks 1850–2312)**
After peaking at tick 1826, the cumulative mean undergoes a slight but statistically significant decrease to 117 by tick 2312, with rolling trend analysis confirming a declining trajectory between ticks 1856 and 2087. As these runs tip into extinction, they cease contributing positive increments to the cross-run average, causing the aggregate mean to recede from its maximum even as individual runs may maintain high cumulative counts.

**Cross-Simulation Variance**
The series exhibits substantial variability, with a standard deviation of 30.0 relative to a mean of 102.4. This dispersion, combined with the bimodal pattern of early rapid rise versus late decline, suggests divergent outcomes: some populations sustain continuous hunting pressure on reproducing females throughout the 200-year simulation span, while others suffer rapid demographic collapse, resulting in fewer total postpartum mortalities accumulated by termination.
