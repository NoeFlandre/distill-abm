Following this initial collapse, the decline resumes with continued oscillatory behavior but at substantially reduced magnitudes. The trajectory maintains a dominant decreasing trend through approximately tick 1,400, though the rate of decline moderates considerably compared to the initial phase.

An notable interruption occurs between roughly ticks 1,400 and 1,600, where the trend temporarily reverses to a weak positive slope, suggesting a demographic rebound or temporary relaxation of energetic constraints. This recovery proves ephemeral, as the system returns to decline between ticks 1,600 and 1,900. A second, more robust recovery phase emerges between approximately ticks 1,850 and 2,100, characterized by stronger positive momentum and suggesting another temporary population resurgence despite sustained hunting pressure.

After the second recovery fails, the final decline accelerates toward extinction. By tick 2,311, aggregate prey energy reaches zero, indicating complete population collapse. Throughout the simulation, the oscillatory pattern with 63-tick periodicity persists, overlaid upon the general extinction trajectory, with amplitudes diminishing as total energy approaches zero.
