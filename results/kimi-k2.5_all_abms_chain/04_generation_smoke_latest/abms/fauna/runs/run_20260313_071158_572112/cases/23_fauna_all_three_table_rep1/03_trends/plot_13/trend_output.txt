**Implications for Decision Makers**
The trajectory reveals that under sustained hunting pressure combined with high environmental seasonality, megaherbivore populations exhibit rapid initial depletion followed by a prolonged period of low-density volatility rather than immediate collapse. Decision makers should recognize that temporary stabilization or minor recoveries during years 3–5 (ticks 875–1623) may create misleading signals of population resilience, whereas the system is actually approaching an irreversible tipping point. The consistent ~63-tick seasonal pulses throughout the decline suggest that resource availability alone cannot counteract hunting pressure when hunter populations are maintained at initial levels. Conservation strategies must prioritize reducing hunting pressure during the critical early phase (first year) when energetic decline is most severe, as interventions during later low-density phases may be insufficient to prevent eventual extinction.
