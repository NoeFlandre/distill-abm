The simulation data reveal a consistent pattern of population decline toward extinction across all replicates. Initial populations began at approximately 250 individuals. During the first 500 steps, populations underwent rapid reduction, with the fastest declining replicates reaching near-extinction levels by step 400, while slower declining replicates retained approximately 100 individuals at step 500. By step 1000, nearly all replicates had declined to fewer than 10 individuals. A small subset of simulations exhibited prolonged persistence, maintaining minimal populations of fewer than 5 individuals until approximately steps 1500 to 2200, after which all populations reached extinction. The data demonstrate substantial variation in the rate of decline among replicates, particularly during the initial 500 steps, followed by a universal convergence toward zero population size.
