The cumulative number of prey deaths initiates at zero and exhibits a rapid saturating growth pattern over the course of the simulation. During the initial 500 time steps, mortality accumulates steeply, with the rate of increase peaking between steps 232 and 463 where the slope reaches 0.438 deaths per step, before decelerating to 0.170 by step 695. This early phase reflects the initial encounter between the 20 forager agents and the naive prey population of 250 animals, where hunting pressure is maximized due to high prey density and the absence of learned avoidance behaviors.

Between approximately 700 and 1200 steps, the accumulation rate diminishes substantially, with slopes dropping to 0.059 and then 0.017, indicating that the easily accessible portion of the prey population has been depleted. Inflection points occurring around step 362 and again near step 1241 mark transitions from accelerating to decelerating mortality accumulation, corresponding to the shift from initial population exploitation to a regime where prey scarcity limits hunting success.

After roughly 1200 steps, the cumulative death count stabilizes into a plateau phase with no significant trend detected between steps 1160 and 1391, and again between 1624 and 1855. The mean cumulative mortality reaches a maximum value of approximately 240 deaths around step 1876, with the series terminating at 235 deaths by step 2312. This saturation indicates that active mortality has effectively ceased, likely reflecting prey population extinction or reduction to densities so low that encounter rates with foragers approach zero, despite the continued presence of hunters and vegetation regrowth mechanisms.

Across the 40 replicate simulations, substantial variability exists in both the timing of saturation and the total mortality achieved. Final cumulative death counts range from approximately 225 to 250, with some trajectories reaching their asymptote as early as step 1000 while others continue accumulating deaths until beyond step 2000. This heterogeneity reflects stochastic variation in agent movement patterns, the spatial distribution of vegetation, and the dynamic optimal foraging decisions of hunters who must evaluate pursuit costs against diminishing returns as prey populations decline.

A weak oscillatory pattern with a period of 63 steps emerges in the final phase of the simulation (after step 2170), superimposed on the overall plateau. These minor fluctuations likely reflect asynchronous extinction events across replicate runs or the interaction of seasonal resource pulses (governed by the high seasonality parameter of 0.8) with the reproductive biology of the prey, creating brief windows where limited recruitment allows for additional mortality events in populations that have not yet reached complete extinction.
