The percentage of vegetated landscape patches exhibits characteristic transient dynamics over the 2,200 time steps observed. At initialization (Step 0), the landscape maintains approximately 33% green coverage, consistent with the initial grass proportion parameter setting. During the first approximately 300 steps, vegetation cover undergoes a modest decline to around 32%, followed by a precipitous crash between Steps 300-350, where coverage plummets to minimum values ranging from 23% to 29% across simulation replicates.

This initial collapse is followed by rapid recovery, with vegetation cover returning to approximately 32.5-33% by Step 600, facilitated by the short regrowth time parameter (3 ticks). The system subsequently enters a phase of dampened oscillations: a secondary decline occurs around Step 700 to approximately 29-30%, followed by recovery to 32-33% by Step 800. A tertiary disturbance manifests between Steps 1100-1200, with coverage dropping to 30-31% before recovering.

Beyond Step 1500, the simulations exhibit divergence across replicates, with vegetation stabilizing between 32% and 33%, though some trajectories display minor fluctuations and step-like discontinuities (particularly around Steps 1400-1500 and 1800-2200) while others maintain more constant values. The overall trend indicates that despite strong seasonal forcing (seasonality = 0.8) and intense initial grazing pressure, the rapid vegetation regrowth enables the system to stabilize near the initial 33% cover, with oscillation amplitudes decreasing over time as the predator-prey dynamics approach a quasi-equilibrium.
