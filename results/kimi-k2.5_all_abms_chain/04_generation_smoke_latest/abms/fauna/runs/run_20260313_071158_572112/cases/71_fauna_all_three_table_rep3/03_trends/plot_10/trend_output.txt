**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The trajectory demonstrates that constant hunter populations of modest size (20 foragers) generate sufficient predation pressure to drive megaherbivore extinction within approximately 6.3 simulation years, even when hunters face no mortality risk and can store resources indefinitely. The brief recovery periods during favorable seasons prove insufficient to overcome the demographic constraints of long birth-spacing (730 ticks) and high hunter nutritional extraction rates, indicating that sustainable coexistence requires either reducing hunter numbers below critical thresholds or implementing seasonal hunting moratoria that align with calving periods and vegetation peaks to prevent recruitment bottlenecks from coinciding with hunting pressure.
