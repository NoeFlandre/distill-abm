**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The saturation at approximately 94% male mortality indicates that under current parameter settings (20 hunters, foraging radius of 6, high seasonality of 0.8), male megaherbivore populations face deterministic collapse within 6–7 years. The rapid initial harvest phase (first 500 steps) represents a critical window for intervention; management strategies must address hunting pressure before step 500 to prevent irreversible demographic collapse, as the deceleration phase reflects Allee effects and mate limitation rather than sustainable coexistence. The high variance in extinction timing suggests that small changes in initial hunter density or landscape configuration could significantly alter collapse trajectories.
