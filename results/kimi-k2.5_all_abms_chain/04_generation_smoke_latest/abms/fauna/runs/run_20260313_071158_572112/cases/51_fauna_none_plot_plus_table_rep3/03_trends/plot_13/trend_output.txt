The aggregated total energy of prey populations initiated at approximately 16,667 units and underwent a comprehensive decline to zero over the course of 2,312 time steps, indicating the eventual energetic extinction of prey across all 120 simulation replicates.

The depletion trajectory was characterized by distinct phases of decay. The most rapid decline occurred during the initial periods, particularly between steps 232 and 463, where the rate of decrease reached its maximum intensity with a slope of -36.027 units per step. This followed an already steep descent in the opening interval (steps 0–231) with a slope of -24.950. By step 350, the first major change point, the system had transitioned from its initial high-energy state.

Between steps 464 and 695, the decline continued at a moderated yet substantial rate (-10.956 slope), followed by a slowing deceleration through steps 696–927 (-3.139) and 928–1159 (-0.963). During this transitional phase, local maxima appeared around steps 385 through 440, where total energy temporarily stabilized between approximately 3,700 and 4,100 units, and again near steps 709 and 730 at reduced levels between 800 and 1,080 units. These fluctuations corresponded with inflection points detected around steps 309, 460, and 513, marking shifts in the concavity of the decay curve.

The rate of decline diminished considerably after step 875, the fourth change point. Between steps 1160 and 1391, the system approached a near-stationary state with a minimal negative slope of -0.069. Notably, this extended late phase exhibited brief intervals of weak positive trend between steps 1392–1623 (+0.034 slope) and 1856–2087, suggesting temporary stabilization or minor recoveries amidst the overall downward trajectory. However, the decreasing trend resumed between steps 1624–1855 (-0.111 slope).

Throughout the simulation, early oscillatory behavior exhibited a dominant periodicity of approximately 63 steps, concentrated between steps 55 and 150. As the system progressed toward termination, total energy remained at minimal levels for extended durations; the median value of 146.375 across all time steps was substantially lower than the mean of 2,368.77, reflecting a highly right-skewed distribution where populations persisted at low energy densities before final exhaustion. The terminal change point at step 2310 preceded the complete depletion of reserves at step 2311.
