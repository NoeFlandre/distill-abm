The rate of accumulation varies considerably across simulation runs during the middle phase (approximately steps 300-800), with some trajectories showing steeper slopes indicating more rapid success in encountering and killing males, while others exhibit more gradual step-wise increases. Despite this variability in timing, the final cumulative totals converge within a narrow band, suggesting a consistent maximum harvestable pool of male prey under these parameter conditions.

This pattern reflects the dynamics of optimal foraging behavior where hunters prioritize males due to their higher food value relative to females. The initial rapid increase corresponds to a phase of abundant male prey and efficient hunting returns. The subsequent saturation indicates the progressive depletion of the male subpopulation to near-extinction levels, effectively exhausting the supply of preferred prey targets. The step-like nature of the accumulation indicates discrete hunting events occurring as encounters permit, with intervals between kills lengthening as male density declines.

**Implications for decision makers:** The model demonstrates that male-targeted hunting pressure rapidly extirpates the male subpopulation within approximately 3 to 5 years (1,000 to 2,000 ticks), eliminating most or all breeding males from the system. This sex-biased mortality creates severe demographic consequences for herd viability, potentially leading to reproductive collapse even before total prey numbers reach critically low levels. Management interventions should focus on protecting male cohorts from selective hunting pressure, as the data indicates that male populations are highly vulnerable to depletion under optimal foraging strategies that prioritize energy returns. The convergence of final kill counts near the maximum plausible male population size suggests that hunters will persistently pursue males until they are effectively eliminated, indicating that sustainable hunting of male megaherbivores is unlikely under these energetic and demographic parameters without explicit conservation measures.
