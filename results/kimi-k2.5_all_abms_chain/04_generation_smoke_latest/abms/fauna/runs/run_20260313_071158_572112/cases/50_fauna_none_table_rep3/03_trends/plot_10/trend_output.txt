The prey population initiated at approximately 167 individuals and underwent deterministic extinction by tick 2311. The trajectory exhibited a pronounced, sustained decline characterized by an initial rapid collapse during the first 475 time steps, followed by a prolonged phase of decelerating descent interspersed with periodic oscillations.

Throughout the decline, dampened oscillations occurred with a regular periodicity of approximately 63 ticks, superimposed upon the overall decreasing trend. Local maxima emerged at tick 730 (~11 individuals), tick 803 (~7 individuals), and tick 982 (~3 individuals), demonstrating successively lower recovery peaks as the population diminished. These oscillations likely reflect seasonal environmental fluctuations interacting with the herds' demographic cycles, given the strong seasonality parameter and two-year birth spacing interval.

Transient demographic recoveries appeared between ticks 1392–1623 and 1856–2087, where the rate of decline temporarily reversed to slight increases. However, these intervals proved insufficient to overcome the constant predation pressure exerted by the immortal forager population of 20 hunters. The prey population failed to maintain viable densities despite differential food values between sexes (males: 30 energy units, females: 27 energy units) and nursing calf values, suggesting that the encounter rates with persistent hunters overwhelmed the megaherbivores' K-selected life history strategy characterized by extended interbirth intervals and delayed senescence at 20 years.

The terminal phase exhibited an accelerating decline to zero, with the final detected local maxima below 3 individuals at tick 1030, indicating that Allee effects or stochastic demographic fluctuations prevented recovery below critical population thresholds. The mean population size across the simulation duration was approximately 25 individuals, though the median of roughly 1.5 individuals reveals that the population spent the majority of the simulation duration at critically low densities prior to ultimate extinction.
