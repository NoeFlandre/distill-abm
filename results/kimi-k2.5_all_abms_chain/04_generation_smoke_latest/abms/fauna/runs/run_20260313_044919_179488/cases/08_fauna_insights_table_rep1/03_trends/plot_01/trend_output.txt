The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remained constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period. This metric exhibited no variation from the initial time step through the conclusion of the experiments, maintaining the starting value without deviation across all recorded intervals.

This stability reflects the model configuration wherein hunter agents are immortal and do not reproduce, resulting in a fixed population size regardless of environmental conditions, energy dynamics, or prey availability. The constant density of 20 hunting bands establishes a steady baseline of predation pressure against which the demographic responses and extinction risk of megaherbivore herds can be evaluated.

Implications for decision makers: The fixed hunter population isolates the effect of constant hunting pressure on megaherbivore extinction dynamics, eliminating confounding feedback loops where hunter populations might decline due to prey scarcity. This design allows clear attribution of extinction events or equilibrium states to the interaction between fixed human predation pressure, environmental conditions, and ungulate life history traits, rather than to density-dependent hunter population regulation.
