The cumulative mortality of male prey initiates at zero and undergoes a rapid increase during the initial phase, reaching approximately 80 individuals by time step 500 and continuing to accumulate steeply through the first 850 time steps. This early period is characterized by monotonic growth with trend magnitudes that vary across sub-intervals, showing particularly steep accumulation between time steps 232 and 463.

Between approximately time step 850 and 1876, the rate of increase diminishes substantially as the cumulative count approaches its maximum value. The series reaches a peak mean value of 120.2 males killed at time step 1876. Following this peak, the cumulative count exhibits a statistically significant declining trend, falling to 117.5 by the final observation at time step 2312. This late-phase decline from 1876 to 2312 coincides with the emergence of oscillatory behavior characterized by a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps, particularly evident between time steps 2170 and 2265.

The trajectory transitions through distinct temporal regimes: strongly significant increasing trends dominate the first 1159 time steps with progressively decelerating slopes (ranging from 0.219 to 0.008), followed by periods of no significant trend around time steps 1160-1391 and 1624-1855. A brief resumption of significant increase occurs between time steps 1392 and 1623 before the final declining phase takes hold after time step 1856.

These dynamics indicate that male prey mortality accumulates most rapidly during the initial encounters when prey populations are abundant, then decelerates as the male cohort becomes depleted through sustained hunting pressure. The observed decline in the mean cumulative count after time step 1876 reflects selective attrition across simulation replicates, wherein runs with higher male hunting success terminated earlier, leaving runs with lower cumulative mortality to comprise the sample during the final time steps. Individual simulation runs exhibit variation in their terminal cumulative values, with most runs plateauing between 110 and 125 males killed, though the timing of these plateaus varies considerably across replicates.
