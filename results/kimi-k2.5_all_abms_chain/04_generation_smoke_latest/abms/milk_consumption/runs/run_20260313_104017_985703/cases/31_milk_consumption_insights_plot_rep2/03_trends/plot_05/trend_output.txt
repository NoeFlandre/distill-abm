The cumulative count of milk choices increased linearly at a constant rate of 1,000 choices per simulation step. Beginning from zero at step 0, the total reached approximately 10,000 choices by step 10, 20,000 choices by step 20, and 32,000 choices by step 32.

This linear accumulation indicates that all 1,000 agents made exactly one choice at each time step throughout the simulation, reflecting continuous and uniform decision-making participation without attrition, dropout, or variation in decision frequency. The model therefore maintains a constant level of engagement across the entire simulated population throughout the 1974-2005 study period.

For decision makers, this uniform accumulation pattern establishes that observed transitions in milk type preferences result from active choice behaviors rather than demographic changes or intermittent consumption patterns, providing a stable baseline for analyzing how social influence and habit formation drive the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties.
