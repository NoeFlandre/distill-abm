**Implications for Decision Makers:**

The persistence of whole milk consumption at a stable rate suggests that habitual behavior creates substantial inertia in food choice transitions. The inflection points at steps 1 and 7 identify critical early windows where consumption dynamics are most malleable, indicating that interventions should prioritize early-stage engagement before patterns solidify. The gradual slope deceleration (from 76 to 64) implies that sustained, long-term campaigns are necessary to achieve incremental reductions in incumbent consumption, as abrupt market shifts are unlikely when initial habit strength is high. Finally, the oscillatory behavior in the final periods (indices 27–30) indicates potential instability or fluctuation in mature markets, implying that maintenance strategies may be required to prevent regression even after prolonged transition efforts.
