The subsequent plateau reflects a temporary equilibrium where a resistant subpopulation maintains habitual whole milk consumption, creating a period of market stabilization where network effects and habit formation temporarily balance the diffusion process. The secondary growth phase suggests the eventual accumulation of sufficient social pressure or the erosion of habit strength among remaining holdouts, driving skimmed and semi-skimmed milk to dominance by the conclusion of the 31-year simulation period.
