**Implications for Decision Makers:** The accelerating adoption curve demonstrates that shifts in food consumption patterns can exhibit delayed but explosive growth once critical thresholds of social influence and perception change are reached. Interventions should therefore be designed with long time horizons in mind, anticipating that initial slow adoption phases are not indicative of failure but rather necessary precursors to subsequent rapid diffusion.
