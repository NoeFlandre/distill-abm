**Insights for Decision Makers:**
Market transitions in habitual consumer goods exhibit predictable non-linear patterns with distinct acceleration, plateau, and renewal phases. Early rapid growth should not be interpreted as guaranteed continuous expansion; anticipate mid-cycle plateaus where consumption may oscillate for extended periods due to social resistance and habit maintenance. Sustained intervention strategies prove necessary for the final conversion of late adopters, as initial enthusiasm typically plateaus before reaching full market saturation. The oscillatory nature of transition periods suggests that temporary market volatility is inherent to socially-influenced adoption processes rather than indicative of strategy failure.
