Average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk began at approximately 5.3 units per person at the outset of the simulation period representing 1974, reflecting the historical market dominance of whole milk during that era. This initial baseline gave way to a pronounced acceleration phase spanning roughly the first ten time steps, during which consumption increased steeply as disposition mechanisms—whether threshold-based or probability-based—triggered agents to consider alternatives to their incumbent habits. The trajectory exhibited significant upward momentum through time step 9, driven by the interaction of health perception shifts, environmental awareness, and early social influence propagation through the Watts-Strogatz network topology.

Following this initial expansion, the dynamics entered an extended plateau phase approximately between time steps 10 and 24, where consumption stabilized in the vicinity of 1200 to 1265 units. This middle period was characterized by oscillatory behavior rather than smooth growth, featuring cyclical peaks and valleys that suggest temporary reversals and adjustments in consumption patterns. These fluctuations likely reflect the tension between strong incumbent habits (initially set at maximum strength) and evolving social norms, mediated by cognitive dissonance thresholds and justification mechanisms that caused agents to alternate between alternative milk choices and previous behaviors. The oscillations followed a dominant periodicity of approximately fifteen time steps, indicating systematic cyclical adjustments within the population's adoption behavior.

Subsequently, from time step 25 through the conclusion of the simulation at step 32, the series resumed a significant upward trend, albeit with a gentler slope than the initial phase, ultimately reaching approximately 1456.6 units by 2005. This late-stage growth suggests that accumulated memory effects, combined with sustained social susceptibility and conformity pressures operating at the global network level, eventually overcame residual habit persistence among the remaining holdouts. Throughout the entire temporal sequence, the distribution of consumption values remained left-skewed, with the median value of approximately 1220 units exceeding the mean of 1053 units, indicating that the system spent a greater proportion of the simulation period at lower consumption levels before the transition accelerated toward the dominant high-adoption state observed by the end of the study period.
