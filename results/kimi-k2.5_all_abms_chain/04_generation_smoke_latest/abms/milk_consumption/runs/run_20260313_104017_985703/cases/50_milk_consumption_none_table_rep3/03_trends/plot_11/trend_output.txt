The simulation tracks the temporal evolution of cognitive dissonance in health-related milk choices across the British population from 1974 to 2005, measured as the mean deviation between agents' privately held health valuations and the health implications of their actual consumption behaviors.

**Initial State and Overall Trajectory**
At the outset of the simulation (1974), the deviation registers at 0.0000, indicating perfect alignment between agents' health beliefs and their milk choices. Over the subsequent 32 time steps, this metric exhibits a monotonically increasing trend, culminating in a value of 0.7251 by 2005. The series maintains a mean deviation of 0.4965 across the entire period, with a median of 0.5584, indicating that the distribution of values skews toward higher deviation in later years.

**Phased Dynamics of Divergence**
The trajectory is characterized by distinct phases of acceleration and deceleration, marked by inflection points at time steps 4, 6, 16, 19, 22, 28, and 31.

*Phase 1: Rapid Initial Divergence (1974–1980)*
Between time steps 0 and 4 (approximately 1974–1978), the deviation increases steeply with a slope of 0.063. This rapid escalation continues through time step 6 (1980), suggesting an initial period where agents' choices increasingly diverged from their health valuations as market alternatives became available and awareness of health differences grew.

*Phase 2: Deceleration and Plateau (1980–1996)*
From time step 6 to 16 (1980–1990), the rate of increase diminishes substantially to a slope of 0.012. This slowing continues through time steps 16–22 (1990–1996), where the trajectory nearly flattens with slopes approaching 0.003–0.004. During this middle period, the gap between health beliefs and behaviors stabilized, suggesting that agents reached a temporary equilibrium where habit strength and social influence balanced against health considerations.

*Phase 3: Renewed Acceleration (1996–2005)*
Beginning at time step 22 (1996), the deviation resumes a steeper ascent with a slope of 0.019, continuing through the terminal time step 32. This late-stage acceleration indicates a renewed divergence between health valuations and consumption behaviors, potentially driven by cumulative social influence effects or shifting normative pressures that overwhelmed individual habit persistence.

**Oscillatory Patterns**
Superimposed on the long-term trend is an oscillatory component with a dominant periodicity of approximately 15 time steps. This cyclical variation manifests through indices 18–20 and 27–31, suggesting that agents periodically reassess their choices, creating temporary fluctuations in the alignment between beliefs and behaviors around the underlying growth trajectory.

**Statistical Significance**
Rolling Mann-Kendall trend analysis confirms statistically significant monotonic increases (p = 0.027) across all examined windows, with Kendall's tau consistently equal to 1.000, indicating that the observed divergence between health beliefs and milk choices represents a robust, directional process rather than random fluctuation throughout the 1974–2005 simulation period.
