The metric tracking the mean deviation between consumers' health valuations and their actual milk selections exhibits a monotonic upward trajectory across the 33 time steps, beginning at a baseline of perfect alignment (0.0000) and culminating at 0.7251. This progression indicates a systematic decoupling of dietary choices from health-related perceptions throughout the simulation period spanning 1974 to 2005.

The trajectory unfolds through distinct kinematic phases characterized by varying rates of divergence. During the initial interval (time steps 0–4), the deviation accelerates rapidly with a slope of 0.063, reflecting an immediate emergence of cognitive dissonance as health perceptions regarding alternative milk varieties begin evolving while habitual attachment to whole milk consumption remains near-maximum strength. This steep initial ascent moderates substantially during time steps 5–9, where the rate of increase decelerates to 0.041, suggesting the onset of initial behavioral adjustments among segments of the population.

A prolonged plateau phase dominates the middle period, specifically between time steps 10 and 24, where the slope diminishes to approximately 0.003–0.012. This deceleration corresponds with inflection points detected at time steps 16, 19, and 22, indicating a temporary stabilization in the aggregate misalignment as social influence mechanisms and shifting norms gradually facilitate transitions toward alternative milk varieties, partially offsetting the continued divergence among persistent habitual consumers.

In the latter phase (time steps 25–29), the trajectory exhibits renewed acceleration with a slope of 0.019, generating inflection points at time steps 28 and 31 before reaching the terminal value. This late-stage re-intensification suggests that remaining holdouts—characterized by high initial habit strength (10.0) and elevated social blindness parameters (0.51)—increasingly maintain whole milk consumption despite substantially evolved health valuations, creating a widening gap between attitudes and behaviors.

Statistically, the series maintains a mean deviation of 0.4965 across all time steps, with the median value of 0.5584 exceeding the mean, confirming the concentration of higher deviation values in the latter portion of the simulation. The absence of detected peaks or valleys, combined with consistently significant increasing trends (p=0.027) across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, confirms the persistent unidirectional divergence between health perceptions and consumption behaviors throughout the historical transition period.
