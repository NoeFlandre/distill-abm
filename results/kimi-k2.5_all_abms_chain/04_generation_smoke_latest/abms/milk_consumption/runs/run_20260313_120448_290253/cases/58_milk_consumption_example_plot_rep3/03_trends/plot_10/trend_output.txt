The trajectories exhibit minimal variance across simulation runs, remaining tightly clustered throughout the entire time series with deviations between runs rarely exceeding 0.02 units at any given step. This consistency indicates that the emergence of preference heterogeneity is robust to stochastic variations in network topology and agent interactions within the tested parameter ranges.

By the final time steps, the system approaches an asymptotic value near 0.75, with the rate of increase diminishing slightly. This suggests that while agents continue to diverge in their choice functions over time, the population is converging toward a stable level of heterogeneity characterized by persistent but bounded diversity in milk consumption preferences, reflecting the historical transition from market uniformity to differentiated consumption patterns.
