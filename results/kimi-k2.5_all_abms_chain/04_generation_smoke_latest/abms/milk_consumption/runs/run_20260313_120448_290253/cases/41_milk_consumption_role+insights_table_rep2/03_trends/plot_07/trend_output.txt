**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The pronounced plateau phase (steps 10-24) demonstrates that achieving majority adoption does not guarantee linear progression to total market dominance; sustained intervention is required to overcome the "chasm" of resistant habitual consumers. Additionally, the cyclical patterns indicate that timing policy interventions or marketing campaigns to coincide with natural reconsideration cycles (every ~15 steps) could improve efficiency, while the late-stage growth validates long-term persistence in health education campaigns even when initial rapid adoption slows.
