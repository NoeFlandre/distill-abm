The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk follows a sigmoidal growth trajectory across all simulation runs, beginning at a baseline value of 1.0 and reaching approximately 1.58–1.60 by step 32. The series exhibits three distinct phases of development.

During the initial latency phase (steps 0–7), the metric remains relatively stable. Following a minor increase from 1.0 to approximately 1.02 within the first two steps, values plateau between 1.02 and 1.03 through step 7. This stagnation reflects the persistence of established consumption patterns, where the high initial habit strength associated with whole milk (parameterized at 10.0) suppresses the formation of alternative milk habits despite exposure to health and environmental information.

The transition phase (steps 7–15) initiates around step 7–8 with the onset of accelerating growth. By step 10, the habit factor reaches approximately 1.07, and by step 15, values climb to roughly 1.22. This period of increasing slope indicates that agents have begun consistent adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties, with habitual tendencies strengthening as repeated consumption reinforces the behavior. The inflection point around step 10–12 marks the critical threshold where accumulated social influence and perception updates overcome the initial resistance to habit change.

The consolidation phase (steps 15–32) maintains a strong positive trajectory though with slightly decelerating acceleration. The metric increases from approximately 1.22 at step 15 to 1.35–1.37 at step 20, 1.46–1.48 at step 25, and 1.55–1.57 at step 30, terminating near 1.59 at step 32. The continued upward slope throughout this extended period suggests that habit formation for alternative milk varieties remains an ongoing process that has not reached saturation within the 31-year simulation window. The minimal variance across simulation repetitions (all trajectories remain tightly clustered) indicates that the habit formation dynamics are robust and consistent given the specified parameter configuration, particularly the memory lifetime of 5.0 steps and active habit mechanisms.
