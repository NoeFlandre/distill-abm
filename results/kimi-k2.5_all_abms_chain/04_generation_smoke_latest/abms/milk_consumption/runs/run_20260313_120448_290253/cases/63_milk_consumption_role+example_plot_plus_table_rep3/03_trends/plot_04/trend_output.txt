The cumulative adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at zero and increases monotonically throughout the entire simulation period, reaching approximately 25,644 total choices by the final time step. The trajectory exhibits three distinct phases characteristic of innovation diffusion processes.

During the initial phase spanning approximately the first five time steps, growth occurs at a modest rate with an average slope of roughly 201 choices per time step. This period represents the early adoption stage where consumers begin to experiment with alternative milk varieties despite strong incumbent habits and initially neutral perceptions regarding health and environmental attributes.

The second phase, encompassing time steps five through fourteen, demonstrates rapid acceleration in adoption rates. The growth slope increases progressively from approximately 737 to 927 choices per time step, indicating a period of mass adoption. This acceleration likely reflects the amplification of social influence mechanisms as early adopters' choices become visible to network connections, combined with evolving perceptions of alternative milk attributes as consumers accumulate positive experiences within their five-step memory windows.

The third phase, from time step fifteen onward, exhibits stabilization in the growth rate, maintaining a nearly constant linear increase of approximately 933 to 936 choices per time step through the end of the simulation. This sustained high rate of adoption suggests that alternative milk choices have become normalized within the consumer population, with social norms and reduced habitual resistance facilitating continued selection of skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties. The final value of approximately 25,644 represents roughly eighty percent of the theoretical maximum cumulative choices possible given the population size and time horizon, indicating substantial but not yet complete market transformation.

Statistical validation confirms significant monotonic increases throughout all analyzed windows, with Mann-Kendall tests yielding tau values of 1.000 and p-values of 0.027 across all six rolling windows, indicating consistent directional growth with minimal variance across the thirty simulation runs.
