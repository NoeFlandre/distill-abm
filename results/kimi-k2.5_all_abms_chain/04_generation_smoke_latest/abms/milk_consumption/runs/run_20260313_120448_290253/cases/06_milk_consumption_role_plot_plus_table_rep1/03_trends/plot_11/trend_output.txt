The mean value health deviation exhibits a monotonic increasing trend throughout the 32 time steps, beginning at 0.0 and terminating at approximately 0.725. This trajectory indicates a progressive divergence between consumers' held health valuations and the health implications of their actual milk consumption choices.

The dynamics unfold through three distinct phases characterized by varying rates of divergence. During the initial phase (steps 0–10), the deviation increases rapidly from 0.0 to approximately 0.5, representing the steepest growth period with a slope of approximately 0.063 in the earliest window decreasing to 0.041 by step 9. This suggests an immediate and substantial emergence of tension between health perceptions and consumption behaviors as agents begin processing health information about alternative milk varieties while potentially maintaining habitual whole milk consumption.

The middle phase (steps 10–25) exhibits a marked deceleration in the rate of increase, with the deviation growing from approximately 0.5 to only 0.6 and slopes dropping to 0.012 and then 0.003–0.004 between steps 15–24. This plateau-like behavior suggests a temporary stabilization of the value-action gap, potentially indicating that social influence mechanisms and habit persistence reached a temporary equilibrium with emerging health motivations, slowing the divergence between internal valuations and observable choices.

The final phase (steps 25–32) demonstrates renewed acceleration, with the deviation increasing from 0.6 to 0.725 and the slope rising again to 0.019. This late-stage intensification of the value-action gap suggests that as the market transition toward alternative milk varieties nears completion, the remaining consumer choices become increasingly misaligned with health valuations, or alternatively, that health perceptions have evolved to create larger gaps with legacy consumption patterns.

Statistical analysis confirms significant monotonic increasing trends across all observation windows, with inflection points detected at steps 4, 6, 16, 19, 22, 28, and 31 indicating subtle shifts in the underlying behavioral dynamics. The oscillation indices in the later stages suggest increasing variability in how individual agents resolve the tension between health cognition and consumption behavior as the simulated market approaches the historical crossover point where skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties surpassed whole milk consumption.
