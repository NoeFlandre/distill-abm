**Implications for Decision Makers**

The monotonic increase in habit strength indicates that delayed interventions face progressively higher barriers to success; each year of inertia allows habitual preferences to compound, making behavior change exponentially more difficult. The immediate jump to 1.021 at simulation start suggests that public health messaging must account for pre-existing, well-entrenched habits rather than assuming neutral initial conditions. Furthermore, the substantial divergence across simulation outcomes (spanning 0.026 units by 2005) implies that uniform national campaigns may prove inefficient compared to targeted strategies recognizing that social network structures create pockets of high habit resistance requiring intensive, localized intervention.
