The initial rapid adoption phase (1979–1983) indicates high susceptibility to change when social networks reach critical thresholds, while the final plateau (post-1999) reveals the existence of a persistent minority resistant to behavioral change. Decision makers should recognize that once consumption drops below approximately 600 units per capita, further reductions require fundamentally different strategies than those effective during the initial transition, as remaining consumers exhibit high habit strength and social blindness to alternative behaviors.
