The close convergence of outcomes across simulation runs, particularly during the initial growth and final ascent phases, indicates robust aggregate behavior with low stochastic variance, despite individual agent heterogeneity. For decision makers and public health officials, these dynamics reveal that skimmed milk adoption follows a punctuated equilibrium pattern with distinct growth, plateau, and renewal phases rather than a simple logistic curve. The middle plateau phase (steps 12–24) represents a critical intervention window where policies targeting habit disruption and cognitive dissonance reduction could accelerate adoption. The significant growth in later steps demonstrates that persistent norm-building strategies yield delayed but substantial returns, suggesting that evaluation horizons for dietary transition policies should extend well beyond initial adoption curves to capture long-term behavioral shifts.
