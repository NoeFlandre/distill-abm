**Implications for Decision Makers**

The persistence of a 13-unit deviation gap after three decades suggests that environmental education and awareness campaigns alone are insufficient to align consumption with environmental values. High initial habit strength relative to deliberation thresholds creates substantial friction that moderate social influence mechanisms cannot fully overcome. Decision makers should recognize that significant latent demand for environmentally-aligned milk options exists—represented by the gap between values and behaviors—but that unlocking this demand requires interventions specifically targeting habit disruption rather than merely enhancing environmental valuation. The stabilization of deviation rather than its reduction suggests that without external shocks to the habitual system, the values-action gap will persist indefinitely, representing a market inefficiency where consumer preferences are not being translated into consumption patterns.
