The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skinned milk consumed across the virtual population demonstrates a distinct multi-phase pattern over the 31-step simulation period representing 1974 to 2005. At initialization (step 0), consumption registers near zero, approximately 0–50 units, consistent with the 1974 market conditions where whole milk maintained dominant incumbent status through strong habitual attachments (initial habit strength of 10.0) and alternative milk perceptions remained modest (health perception mean of 1.69, environmental perception mean of 1.12).
