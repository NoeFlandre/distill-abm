This steep increase reflects the swift diversification of agent preference structures as consumers begin processing health and environmental attribute information, engaging in social network interactions, and developing individual habits that diverge from the initial uniform state.

Following the first inflection point at step 7, the rate of increase decelerates markedly through step 12 (second inflection point), with the slope dropping to 0.007. During this transitional period, the metric continues to rise but at approximately one-eighteenth the initial velocity, suggesting the primary phase of preference differentiation has concluded as agents establish more stable, individualized evaluation patterns for milk varieties.

From step 12 through step 28 (third inflection point), the metric maintains a gradual, steady upward trajectory with slopes progressively declining from 0.009 to 0.004 across successive five-step windows. Despite the reduced rate of change, statistically significant increasing trends persist throughout all observation windows (p=0.027), indicating continued slow differentiation in consumer choice functions as the population approaches the final simulation period.

By step 32, the metric converges to 0.7508, representing substantial heterogeneity in consumer evaluation functions across the population. The overall mean value of 0.6323 across the 33 time steps indicates that agents maintained moderately to highly divergent preference structures throughout the majority of the simulation. The absence of detected peaks or valleys, combined with consistently positive Mann-Kendall trends across all rolling windows, confirms a monotonic increase in choice function deviation throughout the 31-year period. This trajectory suggests that while agents began with identical perceptions and habits, the combined effects of social influence, habit formation, information processing about alternative milk attributes, and stochastic psychological mechanisms generated increasing diversity in consumer decision-making structures over time, ultimately resulting in a population with significantly heterogeneous evaluation criteria by 2005.
