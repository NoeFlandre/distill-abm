The mean deviation between consumers' held environmental values and the environmental implications of their milk choices begins at zero, indicating perfect alignment between environmental values and consumption behaviors at the simulation outset. This alignment deteriorates rapidly during the initial thirteen time steps, with the deviation increasing sharply to reach a maximum value of approximately 13.48. This early divergence suggests that despite agents holding specific environmental values, the combined pressures of strong incumbent habits, social conformity mechanisms, and health attribute perceptions initially drive consumption choices that increasingly conflict with those environmental values.

Following this initial rapid escalation, the deviation stabilizes significantly after time step thirteen, exhibiting only minor oscillations around a persistent plateau of roughly 13.4 throughout the remaining simulation period. Statistical trend analysis confirms this transition, showing significant monotonic increases in early observation windows followed by non-significant stable trends in subsequent periods, indicating the system reaches a steady-state equilibrium where the value-action gap remains constant.

This persistent high deviation implies that structural barriers within the simulated marketplace—particularly the parameterization of habit persistence and social influence mechanisms—create a lasting disconnect between consumers' environmental aspirations and their realized purchasing behaviors. Once established during the initial simulation phase, this value-action gap becomes entrenched and resistant to spontaneous correction, suggesting that the natural market dynamics simulated here tend to lock consumers into behavioral patterns that systematically diverge from their stated environmental values.

For decision makers seeking to promote environmentally aligned consumption patterns, these dynamics indicate that intervention strategies must specifically target the early simulation period when value-behavior alignment is most malleable. Once the deviation stabilizes around the 13.4 plateau, the system exhibits strong path dependency, suggesting that preventive measures are substantially more effective than corrective policies aimed at disrupting established habits and social norms after the value-action gap has become entrenched.
