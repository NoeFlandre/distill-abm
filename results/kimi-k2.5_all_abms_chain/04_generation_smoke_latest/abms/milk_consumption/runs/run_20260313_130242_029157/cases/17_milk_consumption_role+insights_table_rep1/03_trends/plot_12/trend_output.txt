**Phase 2: Stabilized High-Deviation Equilibrium (Indices 10–32)**

This plateau exhibits quasi-cyclical behavior with a dominant oscillation period of 15 time steps, evidenced by inflection points at indices 16, 19, 24, 26, 28, and 31, indicating persistent but bounded instability in the value-action gap rather than resolution.

**Behavioral Interpretation**
The trajectory indicates that the historical transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties—driven primarily by health perceptions (initial health perception parameter at 1.69 versus environmental perception at 1.12)—generated a substantial and persistent misalignment between consumers' actual environmental values and the environmental implications of their behavioral choices. The rapid initial increase suggests that as consumers adopted alternative milk varieties for health reasons during the late 1970s and early 1980s, they simultaneously adopted consumption patterns with environmental implications that exceeded their personal environmental priorities. The subsequent plateau, maintained despite high justification capabilities (0.61) and low cognitive dissonance tolerance (0.15), indicates that consumers successfully rationalized this discrepancy without adjusting their underlying environmental values or reversing their behavior, creating a stable regime of "accidental environmentalism" where behavioral choices outperform stated values.

**Implications for Decision Makers**
This pattern suggests that health-focused interventions can drive pro-environmental behavioral transitions even when target populations hold weak environmental priorities, but such gains may create lasting value-behavior gaps that do not naturally resolve into value change. Decision makers should recognize that the first decade of market transition represents a critical window where behavioral patterns become decoupled from values and subsequently persist through justification mechanisms; policy interventions seeking to align values with behavior must therefore act during the initial growth phase, as the stabilized plateau phase demonstrates high resistance to value adjustment despite ongoing behavioral inconsistency. Additionally, the sustained high deviation implies that environmental benefits captured during health-driven transitions may remain vulnerable to future disruption if justification contexts shift, requiring continued reinforcement independent of value consolidation.
