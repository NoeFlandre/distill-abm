Beginning around step 6 and continuing through step 10, the habit factor enters a transition phase characterized by accelerating growth, rising from approximately 1.02 to 1.08. From step 10 through the conclusion at step 32, the habit factor exhibits sustained, roughly linear growth, ascending from 1.08 to approximately 1.58-1.60. This extended growth phase indicates continuous habit reinforcement across the consumer population as adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk becomes increasingly prevalent. The trajectory remains consistent across simulation runs with minimal variance, suggesting robust habit formation dynamics. The final value of 1.6 signifies that by the end of the 31-year period, skimmed and semi-skimmed milk have developed substantial habitual strength among consumers, though the factor remains considerably below the initial incumbent habit level of 10.0, reflecting the historical dominance of whole milk and the gradual, incomplete nature of the behavioral transition.

**Implications for Decision Makers:** The six-step lag before habit acceleration suggests a critical intervention window during which marketing efforts or policy measures could most effectively influence long-term consumption patterns. The final habit factor of 1.6, while substantial, suggests remaining market vulnerability to disruption, as alternative milk habits have not reached the entrenched strength historically associated with whole milk consumption.
