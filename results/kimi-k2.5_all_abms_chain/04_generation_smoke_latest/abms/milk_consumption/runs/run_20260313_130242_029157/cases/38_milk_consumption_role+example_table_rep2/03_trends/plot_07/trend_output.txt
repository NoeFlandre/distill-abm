The oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of approximately 15 time steps suggests cyclical dynamics in social influence propagation, where clusters of agents periodically align and realign their consumption patterns as local network effects ripple through the population.

In the final phase from time step 25 onward, the metric resumes a significant upward trend, though at a more moderate rate compared to the initial adoption phase, reaching approximately 1,457 units by the conclusion of the simulation at time step 32. This late-stage growth indicates the eventual triumph of sustained social influence and shifting normative perceptions over habitual inertia, capturing the historical crossover where alternative milk varieties surpassed whole milk consumption.
