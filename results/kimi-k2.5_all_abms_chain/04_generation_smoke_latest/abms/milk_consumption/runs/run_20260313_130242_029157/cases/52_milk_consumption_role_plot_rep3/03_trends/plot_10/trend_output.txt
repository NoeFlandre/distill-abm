This sharp escalation suggests a swift diversification in how agents evaluate milk options as they begin integrating health and environmental information, habitual considerations, and social influences into their decision-making frameworks.

Between approximately steps 3 and 5, the rate of increase moderates somewhat, with the deviation metric rising from roughly 0.52 to 0.58. This deceleration indicates that while agents continue to diverge in their choice functions, the initial burst of differentiation gives way to a more measured process of preference formation.

From step 5 through the conclusion of the simulation at step 32, the metric follows a gradual, approximately linear upward trend, ultimately reaching values around 0.75. Throughout this extended period, the deviation increases steadily at a rate of approximately 0.006 to 0.007 per step, suggesting a slow but persistent accumulation of heterogeneity in consumer decision-making processes.

Across all simulation runs, the trajectories remain remarkably consistent, with minimal variance between repetitions. This reproducibility indicates that the model's parameter configuration—including the habit threshold of 2.0, initial whole milk habit strength of 10.0, social susceptibility of 0.2, and social blindness of 0.51—reliably produces a systematic divergence in consumer choice evaluations over time.

Interpreted within the context of British milk consumption transition, these dynamics suggest that as the market evolved away from whole milk dominance, consumers developed increasingly individualized decision-making frameworks. The expanding deviation reflects growing heterogeneity in how agents weighted health perceptions, environmental concerns, habitual tendencies, and social pressures. By the end of the simulation period, the substantial deviation value indicates that the consumer population had fragmented into diverse psychological profiles regarding milk consumption, with agents exhibiting substantially different internal evaluations of choice options, even as aggregate behavior shifted toward skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties.
