This inflection likely represents the critical market crossover period where the consumption of skimmed and semi-skinned varieties approaches parity with whole milk, creating temporary market friction as social influence mechanisms and habitual maintenance forces compete for dominance in agent decision-making.

From step 12 through step 25, the quantity stabilizes within a relatively narrow band of approximately 1200 to 1250 units, exhibiting minor fluctuations characteristic of market equilibrium dynamics. During this middle period, the balance between social conformity pressures and habitual behavior produces a stable state where adoption continues but at a decelerated rate, reflecting the sustained influence of memory-limited learning and moderate social interaction probabilities within the network structure.

Following step 25, the metric enters a second growth phase, climbing steadily to reach approximately 1450 units by step 32. This late-stage increase suggests the consolidation of market dominance by skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties, as late adopter agents eventually overcome habitual barriers and normative social pressures reinforce the new consumption pattern. Throughout the entire 31-year simulation period, the multiple model runs demonstrate low variance, particularly in steps 15 through 32, indicating consistent macro-level outcomes despite stochastic elements in individual agent decision-making processes.
