The aggregate volume of milk choices accumulates linearly throughout the simulation period, commencing at zero choices and increasing to approximately 32,000 decisions by the final time step (step 32). This trajectory reflects the mechanical structure of the simulation, wherein 1,000 agents each execute exactly one consumption choice per time step across all 33 time steps representing the 1974–2005 period. The perfectly uniform growth rate of 1,000 choices per step indicates consistent agent participation without attrition, variation in decision frequency, or interruption in the simulation sequence.

For decision makers: This metric confirms stable model execution and sustained consumer engagement throughout the simulation horizon, but provides no diagnostic information regarding the distribution of choices between whole milk and alternative varieties, nor does it indicate the dynamics of market transition or adoption rates of specific product types.
