Despite the model's objective of capturing the historical shift toward skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties, the data indicate that agents maintaining whole milk selections develop progressively stronger habitual attachments, with the mean habit factor increasing by approximately 5% over the simulation period. For decision makers in public health and market strategy, these findings underscore that entrenched consumption habits represent dynamic rather than static barriers to change. The compounding nature of habitual behavior implies that intervention windows narrow over time, as each additional period of incumbent choice reinforcement increases the psychological inertia that alternative products must overcome.
