**Implications for Decision Makers**

These dynamics reveal that dietary transitions follow a non-linear, staged trajectory requiring sustained intervention beyond initial awareness campaigns. The pronounced middle-period plateau demonstrates that early adoption success does not guarantee continuous linear growth; rather, policymakers should prepare for potential stagnation periods where entrenched habits resist change despite prior gains. Maintaining consistent health messaging and social reinforcement mechanisms during these middle phases is essential to prevent reversion. Furthermore, the final acceleration toward the end of the simulation period suggests that patience in public health policy implementation yields compounding returns, as social network effects and habit reformation eventually generate self-sustaining adoption momentum once critical mass is achieved.
