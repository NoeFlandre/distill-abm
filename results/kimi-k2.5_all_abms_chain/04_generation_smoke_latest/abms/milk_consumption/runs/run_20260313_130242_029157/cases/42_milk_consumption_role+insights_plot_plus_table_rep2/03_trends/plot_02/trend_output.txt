**Implications for Decision Makers**

The three-phase trajectory—rapid early adoption, extended plateau with volatility, and late-stage growth—suggests that market interventions targeting habitual behavior change exhibit delayed but cumulative effects. Additionally, the critical importance of the initial rapid growth phase (steps 0-11) implies that early investment in health perception formation and social network activation yields disproportionate long-term returns, as these mechanisms generate self-reinforcing momentum that persists even during subsequent stagnant periods.
