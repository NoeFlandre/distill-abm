The simulated trajectory of alternative milk consumption follows a pronounced non-linear growth pattern spanning 33 time steps, beginning at approximately 5.3 units per agent at time step 0 and culminating at roughly 1456.6 units by time step 32. This represents a massive behavioral shift from negligible initial adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties toward near-dominant market penetration by the conclusion of the simulation period.

The trajectory unfolds through three analytically distinct phases marked by specific inflection points and local extrema. The initial establishment phase (time steps 0 through 4) demonstrates rapid acceleration consistent with early adopter behavior, where initial health perceptions (mean 1.69) and environmental valuations (mean 1.12) begin overcoming the strong incumbent habit strength (10.0) associated with whole milk consumption. The first inflection point at time step 4 marks a transition in the curvature of growth, after which the system continues expanding but encounters increasing resistance from habitual mechanisms.

The middle transition phase (time steps 11 through 24) exhibits complex oscillatory dynamics rather than monotonic growth, reflecting the contested nature of market evolution during the critical tipping period. Consumption reaches an initial local maximum of approximately 1265 units at time step 11, followed immediately by a local valley of roughly 1234 units at time step 12. This volatility continues with alternating peaks and troughs: a secondary peak near 1240 units at time step 13 (coinciding with an inflection point), a decline to the global minimum of this middle phase at approximately 1183 units by time step 18, a recovery to roughly 1222 units at time step 19 (another inflection point), a subsequent drop to about 1214 units at time step 20, a rise to approximately 1227 units at time step 21, and a final valley of roughly 1210 units at time step 23. These oscillations, occurring within the approximate band of 1180-1265 units, model the psychological friction generated by the threshold-based habit maintenance (threshold 2.0) competing against social influence mechanisms operating through the Watts-Strogatz network structure.

The final consolidation phase initiates around time step 25 (with significant inflection points at steps 27 and 30), where the system breaks through the previous plateau ceiling. This period exhibits statistically significant increasing trends through time step 29, driven by cumulative social learning effects (interaction probability 0.3, social susceptibility 0.2) and the five-period memory window allowing sufficient historical information to accumulate against entrenched preferences. The trajectory terminates at time step 32 with a change point marking the new equilibrium, where alternative milk consumption has achieved overwhelming dominance consistent with the historical crossover point where skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties surpassed whole milk consumption in the British market.
