A pronounced transitional phase emerges between roughly time steps 10 and 24, characterized by market turbulence and temporary stabilization. **Implications for Decision Makers:**

The non-linear adoption curve—with its extended plateau period—suggests that interventions targeting early adopters (time steps 0-9) require different strategies than those addressing the late majority (time steps 25+). The pronounced oscillation during the middle period indicates that market transitions face substantial resistance even after initial uptake, implying that decision makers should anticipate temporary consumption declines and plan sustained engagement rather than assuming smooth linear growth.
