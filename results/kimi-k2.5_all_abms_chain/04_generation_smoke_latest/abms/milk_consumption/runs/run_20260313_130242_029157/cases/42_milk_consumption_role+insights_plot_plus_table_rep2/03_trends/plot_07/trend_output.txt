**Implications for Decision Makers**

The bimodal growth pattern suggests that dietary transitions in established markets exhibit distinct launch and consolidation phases separated by significant time lags. Public health interventions targeting habitual food choices should anticipate initial resistance periods of approximately 10-15 years before network effects generate self-sustaining adoption. The prolonged plateau between steps 11-25 indicates that achieving majority market share requires sustained effort beyond initial early adopter conversion, as incumbent habits maintain residual strength through memory windows (5 steps) and justification mechanisms. Policymakers should prioritise interventions during the identified acceleration windows (steps 0-9 and 25-32) when populations demonstrate heightened receptivity to change, while maintaining consistent messaging during plateau phases to prevent regression to former consumption valleys noted at steps 18 and 23. The oscillation dynamics suggest that social influence campaigns must account for temporary setbacks in adoption rates, designing resilience strategies rather than expecting linear progress toward dietary goals.
