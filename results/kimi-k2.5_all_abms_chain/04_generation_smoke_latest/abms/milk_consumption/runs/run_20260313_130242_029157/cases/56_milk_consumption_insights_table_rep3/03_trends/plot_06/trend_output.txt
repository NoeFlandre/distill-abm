**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The phased nature of this transition suggests that dietary change campaigns may achieve initial rapid success through targeting susceptible segments but should anticipate periods of stagnation as habituated behaviors create entrenched resistance. Decision makers should prepare for non-linear adoption curves where early victories are followed by difficult middle phases requiring sustained intervention to overcome habit persistence. Additionally, the prolonged tail of decline indicates that achieving complete market transformation may require differentiated strategies targeting resistant late-stage consumers, as uniform approaches become less effective once only highly habituated or socially isolated individuals remain.
