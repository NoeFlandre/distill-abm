The cumulative count of whole milk selections initiates at 0 and increases monotonically throughout the 32-step simulation period, ultimately reaching approximately 2,195 instances. This accumulation reflects ongoing consumption of whole milk among the agent population as they navigate the transition from the 1974 market baseline toward alternative varieties.

The rate of accumulation follows a distinct temporal pattern: velocity accelerates modestly during the initial phase, peaking around steps 5 through 9 with a slope of approximately 76.2 choices per step, before entering a gradual deceleration phase that persists through the remainder of the simulation. By steps 25-29, the rate has declined to roughly 64.1 choices per step. This trajectory suggests that high initial habit strength for the incumbent whole milk variety drives rapid early consumption, while the subsequent slowing reflects the gradual activation of disposition mechanisms—whether threshold-based or probability-based—that prompt agents to evaluate skimmed and semi-skimmed alternatives, thereby diluting the growth rate of whole milk selections.

Statistical tests confirm significantly increasing trends across all examined intervals (p=0.027), with notable behavioral transitions occurring at steps 1, 7, and 31. The system exhibits increasing variability across simulation runs over time, with divergence becoming pronounced after step 7 and continuing through step 31. This heterogeneity indicates that stochastic elements within the Watts-Strogatz network topology and individual agent interaction probabilities generate pathway-dependent outcomes, where initial random variations in social influence transmission compound into measurably different cumulative consumption patterns by the simulation's conclusion. Despite these divergences between runs, the aggregate trajectory maintains a consistent upward slope, indicating that whole milk retains substantial market presence throughout the 1974-2005 transition period even as the marginal rate of selection gradually diminishes.
