The mean habit factor for alternative milk begins at a neutral baseline of 1.0 at the initial time step. During the first four time steps, the factor remains effectively constant, exhibiting no significant trend as habit formation for alternative milk remains dormant. Beginning at time step 5, the metric enters a sustained period of monotonic increase that continues through the remainder of the simulation. By the final time step 32, the habit factor reaches approximately 1.59, representing nearly a 59% increase from the baseline. This trajectory indicates that while consumers initially maintain strong habitual reliance on incumbent whole milk choices, a tipping point occurs around time step 5 after which habitual tendencies toward skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties progressively strengthen. The accumulation of habit creates self-reinforcing behavioral momentum that becomes increasingly difficult to reverse as the simulation progresses.

For decision makers, these dynamics suggest that interventions targeting milk choice habits face a narrow window of opportunity in the earliest time steps before behavioral patterns crystallize. Once the growth phase begins after time step 5, habit formation follows a predictable accumulation curve, implying that market strategies should account for the substantial inertia that develops by later periods, where the 59% habit strength increase indicates deeply entrenched consumer behaviors.
