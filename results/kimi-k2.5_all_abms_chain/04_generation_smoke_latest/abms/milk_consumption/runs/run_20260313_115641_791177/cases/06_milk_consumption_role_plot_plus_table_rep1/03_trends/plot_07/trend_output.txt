The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents follows a three-phase diffusion trajectory across the 33 time steps representing the 1974–2005 period. The series begins at a near-negligible baseline of approximately 5.3 units at step 0, reflecting the historical dominance of whole milk at the start of the simulation. During the initial adoption phase spanning steps 0 through 11, the metric exhibits a steep, statistically significant monotonic increase (Mann-Kendall tau = 1.000, p = 0.027), reaching a first major peak of 1,265.5 units at step 11. This rapid ascent corresponds to the early adoption period where consumer agents, starting with low initial health and environmental perceptions of alternative milk varieties (1.69 and 1.12 respectively) and facing strong incumbent habits for whole milk (habit strength 10.0), begin overcoming habitual barriers through social influence mechanisms and accumulated positive perceptions.

Following this initial surge, the series enters an extended plateau phase between steps 12 and 26, where values oscillate between approximately 1,183 and 1,267 units without significant directional trend (Mann-Kendall windows 3–5 show tau values near zero and p-values above 0.05). This stabilization period features notable fluctuations, including local valleys at steps 12 (1,233.6), 18 (1,183.4), 20 (1,214.5), and 23 (1,209.7), interspersed with peaks at steps 13 (1,240.3), 19 (1,221.6), and 21 (1,226.9). The oscillation dominant period of 15.0 steps suggests cyclical consumption patterns within this equilibrium phase, potentially reflecting the dynamic interplay between habit maintenance (habit-threshold 2.0), social conformity pressures (social-conformity 0.28), and cognitive dissonance resolution (threshold 0.15) as the market approaches saturation.

In the final phase from steps 27 through 32, the series resumes a significant upward trajectory (Mann-Kendall tau = 1.000, p = 0.027) with a slope of 48.5 units per step, culminating in a terminal value of 1,456.6 units. This late-stage growth indicates continued conversion of remaining whole milk consumers and consolidation of skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties as the dominant market choice by 2005. The overall curve exhibits an S-shaped adoption pattern with an asymmetric structure: rapid initial growth lasting approximately 11 steps, a prolonged fluctuating plateau spanning 15 steps, and a final acceleration phase of 6 steps. The median consumption level of 1,220.2 units substantially exceeds the mean of 1,053.2 units, indicating that the early low-consumption values and the extended plateau at moderate levels skew the distribution downward relative to the terminal high-consumption state.
