The mean value health deviation exhibits a monotonic upward trajectory across the 33 time steps, starting at 0.0 and terminating at 0.7251, with an aggregate mean of 0.4965 and median of 0.5584. The dynamics unfold in three distinct phases characterized by varying rates of increase.

During the initial phase spanning approximately the first 10 time steps, the metric rises sharply from 0.0 to approximately 0.52. This period features the steepest growth rates, with statistically significant monotonic increasing trends showing slopes of 0.063 and 0.041 in successive rolling windows (time steps 0–4 and 5–9).

The middle phase, extending from time step 10 to approximately time step 25, demonstrates markedly slower growth. The deviation increases gradually from roughly 0.52 to approximately 0.60, with rolling window slopes dropping to 0.012, 0.003, and 0.004 in windows covering time steps 10–14, 15–19, and 20–24 respectively. Inflection points at time steps 16, 19, and 22 mark transitions within this plateau-like behavior, indicating temporary stabilization in the divergence between agents' held health values and the health values implied by their milk choices.

In the final phase from time step 25 to 32, the growth rate accelerates again with a slope of 0.019, pushing the deviation from approximately 0.60 to the terminal value of 0.7251. Inflection points at time steps 28 and 31 occur during this renewed expansion. This late-stage amplification occurs despite relatively modest growth rates compared to the initial phase, suggesting a persistent widening of the misalignment between health perceptions and consumption behaviors.

Throughout the simulation, the variance remains moderate (0.0290) with a standard deviation of 0.1702, and all rolling Mann-Kendall windows confirm statistically significant increasing trends (p=0.027). The oscillation indicators and change point detection at time step 32 further confirm the sustained directional movement without reversal.

**Implications for Decision Makers:** The persistent growth in health value deviation suggests that as the British public transitioned from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties between 1974 and 2005, consumers increasingly experienced misalignment between their personal health valuations and their actual consumption choices. The middle-phase plateau (time steps 10–25) represents a critical window where public health interventions might effectively address emerging cognitive dissonance before it becomes entrenched. The late-stage acceleration indicates that once habits and social norms drive adoption patterns, the disconnect between health values and behaviors continues widening even as market transition matures. Campaigns promoting dietary change should account for the fact that health messaging becomes progressively less influential as social influence and habit formation mechanisms dominate decision-making, particularly during periods where deviation growth rates increase.
