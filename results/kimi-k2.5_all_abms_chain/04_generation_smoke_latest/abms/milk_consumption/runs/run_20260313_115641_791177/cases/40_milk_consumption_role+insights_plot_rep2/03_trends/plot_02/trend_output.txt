**Interpretation Within Model Context**

The distinct S-curve dynamics—with initial stagnation, explosive growth, mid-cycle plateau, and terminal rise—reflect the interplay between the threshold-based psychological mechanisms governing habit disruption and the stochastic social influence processes. The initial lag (Steps 0–2) verifies the model's successful initialization of strong incumbent habits. The steep ascent (Steps 2–10) demonstrates the critical threshold moment when accumulated social information (filtered through a social blindness parameter of 0.51) and cognitive dissonance tolerance (threshold 0.15) collectively enable mass behavioral transition. The plateau (Steps 10–25) captures the intermediate historical period where market share stabilized before final consolidation, while the late-stage growth (Steps 25–32) reflects the long-term equilibrium establishment as alternative milk types achieve normative status.

**Implications for Decision Makers**

The pronounced early growth phase (Steps 2–10) suggests that interventions targeting health perception and social network connectivity yield maximal impact when deployed prior to the adoption inflection point, as delayed engagement would encounter substantially higher resistance from entrenched habits. The extended plateau phase (Steps 10–25) indicates that maintaining momentum requires sustained intervention rather than one-time campaigns, as markets can stabilize at suboptimal equilibria without continuous norm reinforcement. Finally, the secondary growth phase demonstrates that long-term market transformation remains achievable even after initial adoption slows, provided that social influence mechanisms and justification capacities remain active throughout the extended transition period.
