The first phase spans the initial decade (1974–1983), marked by a steep monotonic decline with the most rapid reduction occurring between the fifth and ninth time steps, where consumption decreases at an accelerated rate compared to the initial five-year period. This early drop corresponds to the period when agents with lower habit thresholds and higher susceptibility to health and environmental perceptions begin adopting alternatives, disrupting the initial incumbent habit strength of 10.

Between the tenth and fourteenth time steps (1984–1988), the rate of decline moderates considerably but remains statistically significant, indicating continued but slower abandonment of whole milk as social influence mechanisms and global norms progressively penetrate the Watts-Strogatz network topology.

A stabilization phase emerges from the fifteenth to the nineteenth time step (1989–1993), where consumption temporarily plateaus with a local peak of approximately 774ml at time step 19 following a valley of 760ml at time step 18.
