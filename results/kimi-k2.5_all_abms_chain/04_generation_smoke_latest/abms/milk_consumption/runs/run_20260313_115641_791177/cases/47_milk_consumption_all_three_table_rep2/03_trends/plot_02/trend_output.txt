**Implications for Decision Makers:**

The pronounced early growth phase suggests that interventions targeting initial perception formation—particularly regarding health attributes of alternative milk varieties—yield disproportionate returns when deployed during the first ten time periods of market transition. The subsequent plateau indicates that habit formation creates substantial resistance to change even after initial adoption begins; therefore, sustained rather than one-time interventions are necessary to prevent market stagnation. Finally, the late-stage resurgence implies that social proof mechanisms become self-sustaining once critical network thresholds are crossed, suggesting that resources should be allocated to accelerate early adoption rather than forcing conversion among the most habitually entrenched consumers in later stages.
