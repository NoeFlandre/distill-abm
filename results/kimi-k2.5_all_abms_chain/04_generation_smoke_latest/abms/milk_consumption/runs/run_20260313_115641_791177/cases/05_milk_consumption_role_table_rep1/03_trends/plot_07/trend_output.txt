The simulation depicts a clear S-curve adoption trajectory for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption over the 31-year period, characterized by three distinct phases: rapid initial growth, an extended plateau with oscillatory dynamics, and a final acceleration toward market dominance.

**Initial Adoption Phase (Time Steps 0–9)**
Consumption begins at a minimal baseline of approximately 5.29 units, reflecting the 1974 market conditions where whole milk maintained overwhelming dominance through strong incumbent habits. During this opening phase, the system exhibits steep, statistically significant growth with slopes exceeding 112 units per time step in the earliest intervals and accelerating to approximately 139 units per time step by time step 9. This rapid ascent indicates that despite high initial habit strength for whole milk, the combined influence of health and environmental attribute perceptions, coupled with social network effects, successfully motivated a substantial segment of the population to overcome habitual barriers and adopt alternative milk varieties.

**Plateau and Stabilization Phase (Time Steps 10–24)**
Following the initial surge, the system enters a prolonged plateau spanning approximately fifteen time steps, corresponding roughly to the late 1980s through the mid-1990s. During this interval, consumption stabilizes in the 1,200–1,265 unit range, exhibiting non-significant trends with slopes near zero and minor oscillatory fluctuations. The data reveals localized peaks around time steps 11 (1,265.47), 13 (1,240.35), and 21 (1,226.86), interspersed with valleys at time steps 12 (1,233.62) and 20 (1,214.46). This oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of approximately 15 time steps suggests a dynamic equilibrium where adoption rates among susceptible agents were balanced by resistance or retrenchment among holdouts maintaining strong habitual preferences for whole milk. The plateau indicates that after the initial wave of early adopters, the remaining population required sustained social pressure or accumulated justification to overcome higher habit thresholds.

**Final Growth and Dominance Phase (Time Steps 25–32)**
Beginning around time step 25, the system exhibits renewed statistically significant growth with a slope of approximately 48.5 units per time step, continuing through time step 29. This final acceleration drives consumption from the plateau level of approximately 1,210 units to a terminal value of 1,456.60 units by time step 32. This late-stage surge suggests that persistent social influence mechanisms, combined with the psychological justification processes and evolving normative perceptions, eventually overcame the resistance of the remaining incumbent whole milk consumers. The final value represents the establishment of skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties as the dominant consumption choice, successfully capturing the historical crossover point where alternatives surpassed whole milk in market share by the conclusion of the 2005 simulation horizon.

Overall, the trajectory demonstrates a classic innovation diffusion pattern modified by strong habit persistence, resulting in an extended plateau rather than a smooth logistic curve, ultimately achieving the targeted historical transition from whole milk dominance to alternative milk prevalence.
