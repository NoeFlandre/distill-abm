The mean deviation between agents' held environmental values and the implied environmental values of their milk choices initiates at approximately zero and escalates rapidly during the first five time steps, ascending steeply to reach values between 12 and 13. This initial surge indicates an immediate decoupling of consumer values from actual purchasing behaviors as the population begins transitioning away from whole milk, driven by the tension between moderate initial environmental perceptions of alternatives and the powerful incumbent habits favoring traditional consumption patterns.

From time steps 5 through 10, the growth rate decelerates markedly as the deviation approaches and settles at approximately 13.5, representing a slowing adjustment period where social influence and perception updating continue to reshape the market but encounter diminishing returns in terms of value-behavior alignment. Beyond step 10, the metric exhibits remarkable stability, maintaining a consistent plateau near 13.5 through step 32 with only negligible fluctuation, suggesting that the environmental value-action gap crystallizes into a persistent equilibrium characteristic of the mature market state.

The consistently tight clustering across simulation runs indicates that this value-behavior divergence is a structurally deterministic outcome of the model's parameterization, particularly reflecting the interaction of high initial habit strength for whole milk, moderate levels of social conformity, and the probability-based mechanisms governing habit disruption.

Implications for decision makers: The swift emergence and subsequent stabilization of the value-action gap suggest that environmental attitude change does not automatically translate to behavioral alignment during market transitions. Stakeholders should anticipate that even after widespread adoption of alternative products, substantial misalignment between consumer environmental values and actual choices may persist indefinitely, necessitating targeted interventions that specifically address habitual persistence rather than relying solely on value-shift strategies.
