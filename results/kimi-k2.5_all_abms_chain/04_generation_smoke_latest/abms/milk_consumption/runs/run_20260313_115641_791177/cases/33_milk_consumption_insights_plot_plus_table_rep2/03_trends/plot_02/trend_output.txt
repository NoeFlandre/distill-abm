Between time steps 10 and 24, the system enters a stabilization phase where consumption fluctuates moderately around 1,220 units, exhibiting local peaks near steps 11 and 13 (reaching approximately 1,265 and 1,240 units) and temporary declines to valleys around 1,180-1,210 units. This plateau suggests that once early adopters have transitioned, remaining agents with strong incumbent habits (initially set at maximum strength) resist conversion, creating a temporary equilibrium in market share.

From step 25 through the terminal step 32, consumption resumes significant growth, climbing from approximately 1,220 units to a final value of roughly 1,457 units. This late-stage increase indicates that accumulated social norm effects and cognitive dissonance mechanisms eventually overcome persistent habit resistance, capturing the remaining resistant consumer segments.

The late-stage growth demonstrates that persistent social influence strategies can eventually achieve near-universal adoption, but effective planning horizons must extend well beyond initial uptake phases to capture resistant demographic segments.
