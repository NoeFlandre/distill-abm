**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The trajectory reveals that market transitions generate substantial consumer fragmentation rather than homogeneous shifts. As the deviation continues expanding throughout the simulation, reaching approximately 0.75 by the final period, decision makers must recognize that mature transition markets contain highly heterogeneous consumer profiles. This persistent heterogeneity necessitates increasingly segmented strategies capable of addressing the full spectrum from habit-bound traditionalists to perception-driven early adopters, as uniform messaging becomes progressively less effective throughout the 31-year transition period.
