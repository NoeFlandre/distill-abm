This early decline accelerates further between time steps five and nine, suggesting the emergence of network effects wherein social influence mechanisms—operating through the Watts-Strogatz topology with moderate interaction probability—begin to cascade through the population, amplifying the individual decision-making shifts driven by health and environmental perception updating.

Between time steps ten and fourteen, the rate of decline moderates considerably as the pool of socially susceptible agents begins to deplete, leaving consumers with stronger habitual attachments or higher resistance to social conformity.
