**Consumer Behavior Implications:**

These dynamics reveal a three-stage habit formation process: an initial resistance phase lasting approximately five time steps, a rapid acceleration phase where repeated consumption and social reinforcement crystallize alternative milk preferences, and a maturation phase characterized by continued but slowing habit accumulation.

For decision makers, the prolonged initial plateau suggests that converting consumers from whole milk requires overcoming substantial habitual inertia, as commitment to alternative products does not manifest immediately upon first exposure. However, once consumers cross the threshold around step 5, habit formation becomes self-reinforcing and highly resilient. The deceleration after step 15 indicates that while additional reinforcement continues to strengthen habits, marginal returns diminish over time.

This pattern suggests that marketing and policy interventions should prioritize intensive promotion during the initial five-step window to accelerate transition past the latency period, as subsequent behavior becomes increasingly locked in through self-sustaining habitual mechanisms that require minimal external reinforcement.
