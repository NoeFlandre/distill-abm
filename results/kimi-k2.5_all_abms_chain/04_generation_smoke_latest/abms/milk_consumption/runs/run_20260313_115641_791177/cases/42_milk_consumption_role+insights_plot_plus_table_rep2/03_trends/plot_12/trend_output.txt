**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The rapid establishment of a persistent high-deviation plateau suggests that interventions aimed at aligning environmental values with consumption choices face a critical temporal window during the initial market transition period (approximately the first 10 steps). Once the plateau phase begins, the value-choice relationship becomes resistant to fundamental change, stabilizing at a suboptimal alignment.
