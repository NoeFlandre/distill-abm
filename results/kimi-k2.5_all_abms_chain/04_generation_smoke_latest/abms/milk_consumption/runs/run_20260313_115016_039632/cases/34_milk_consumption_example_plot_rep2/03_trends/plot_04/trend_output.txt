The cumulative count of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices begins at approximately zero at the start of the simulation. Over the first ten time steps, the total increases in a curvilinear fashion, accelerating from near zero to approximately 5,000 cumulative choices. This initial acceleration reflects the gradual overcoming of strong incumbent habits favoring whole milk, as health and environmental perceptions begin to propagate through the social network and agents trigger disposition mechanisms to consider alternatives.

From approximately step 10 through step 32, the cumulative count increases in a roughly linear manner at a rate of approximately 900 to 1,000 choices per time step, rising from 5,000 to approximately 25,500-26,000. This linear trajectory indicates that after the initial transition period, the system achieves a steady-state equilibrium where agents consistently select skimmed or semi-skimmed varieties at a high, stable rate—representing roughly 90 to 95 percent of the 1,000-agent population making these choices during each subsequent time period.

Across all repeated simulation runs, the trajectories exhibit minimal variance, remaining tightly clustered throughout the entire time series. This consistency suggests that the interaction between the threshold-based disposition mechanism, social influence parameters, and habit formation dynamics produces robust adoption patterns that converge rapidly to a stable high-adoption equilibrium, with stochastic elements in agent interactions having limited impact on the aggregate outcome.
