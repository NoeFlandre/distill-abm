**Implications for Decision Makers**

The multi-phase trajectory indicates that market transitions in habitual food categories follow predictable patterns requiring differentiated intervention strategies. The initial rapid decline (steps 0-10) suggests that once social influence mechanisms (operating at 30% interaction probability with 0.2 susceptibility) and health perception shifts (initial alternative health mean 1.69) reach critical mass, habitual dominance erodes quickly without requiring individual-level intervention.
