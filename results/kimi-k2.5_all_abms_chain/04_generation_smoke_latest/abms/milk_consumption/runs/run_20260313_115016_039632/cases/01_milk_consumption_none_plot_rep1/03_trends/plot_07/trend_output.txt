From step 10 through step 25, the system enters a plateau phase characterized by relative stability. This stabilization suggests that habit formation mechanisms and social influence processes have reached a temporary equilibrium within the agent population.

Beginning at approximately step 25, a secondary growth phase commences, with consumption rising from roughly 1,220 units to reach values between 1,400 and 1,480 by step 32. This late-stage increase indicates continued diffusion of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk adoption throughout the social network.
