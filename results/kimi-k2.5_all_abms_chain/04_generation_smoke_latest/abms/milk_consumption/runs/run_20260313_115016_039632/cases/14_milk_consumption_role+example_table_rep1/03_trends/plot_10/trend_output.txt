The mean divergence in consumer choice evaluation begins at zero in 1974, indicating perfect homogeneity in how agents initially assess milk options when incumbent habits strongly favor whole milk. This metric increases monotonically throughout the simulation period, reaching approximately 0.75 by 2005, reflecting the progressive differentiation of consumer preferences as the market transitions toward alternative milk varieties.

The trajectory exhibits distinct phases of acceleration. During the first seven time steps (approximately 1974–1981), the divergence increases rapidly with a pronounced slope, suggesting an initial period where early adopters begin departing from the homogeneous whole-milk preference while the majority maintains entrenched habitual behaviors. An inflection occurs around 1981, after which the rate of divergence moderates but continues climbing through the mid-1980s.

Another shift manifests around 1986, where the expansion of preference heterogeneity decelerates further, indicating sustained but slowing growth in consumer evaluation differences throughout the late 1980s and 1990s. This deceleration suggests that while additional agents continue adopting alternative milk varieties, the marginal rate at which consumer evaluations diverge slows as the market approaches a stable configuration of mixed preferences.

By the final phase beginning around 2002, the increase becomes very gradual, approaching the terminal value of 0.7508. This late-period stabilization implies that the market reaches a state of persistent diversity rather than complete convergence on new alternatives, with substantial variation remaining between consumers maintaining traditional whole milk preferences and those having adopted skimmed or semi-skimmed varieties.

Superimposed on this upward trend, the system exhibits oscillatory patterns with a dominant period of approximately fifteen years, particularly visible around the early 1980s, early 1990s, and 2000s. These fluctuations likely reflect cyclical social influence dynamics and periodic reassessments of health and environmental perceptions, creating temporary micro-convergences within the broader trend toward market heterogeneity. The consistent positive momentum across all observation windows indicates robust progressive diversification of consumer choice functions throughout the entire 31-year transition period.
