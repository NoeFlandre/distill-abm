The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent commences at approximately 5 milliliters during the initial two time steps, consistent with the 1974 market conditions where whole milk dominated consumer preferences. Between steps 2 and 11, consumption undergoes a rapid, approximately linear increase, rising to values ranging between 1,200 and 1,280 milliliters. This steep ascent reflects the initial adoption phase where health perception mechanisms and social influence begin to overcome the strong incumbent habits established at the simulation's outset.

During the interval from step 11 to step 25, the growth rate attenuates considerably, with consumption values oscillating between approximately 1,180 and 1,250 milliliters. This plateau phase suggests a period of behavioral stabilization where habit formation mechanisms and cognitive dissonance thresholds temper further rapid shifts in preference. A localized minimum occurs around step 18, where consumption temporarily declines to approximately 1,180 milliliters before recovering, potentially indicating transient resistance to alternative milk varieties as agents evaluate choice outcomes against established preferences.

Following step 25, consumption enters a second growth phase, increasing from approximately 1,220 milliliters to reach values between 1,450 and 1,470 milliliters by step 32. This late-stage acceleration likely stems from the cumulative effects of social norm diffusion through the Watts-Strogatz network topology and the progressive weakening of whole milk habits via post-choice justification mechanisms.

Variability across simulation runs remains negligible during the initial steps but expands significantly during the rapid growth phase between steps 5 and 10, with individual runs diverging by up to 200 milliliters. This dispersion suggests that stochastic elements in agent interaction probabilities and disposition triggering generate heterogeneous adoption pathways during the critical transition period. Variance subsequently contracts during the plateau phase, indicating convergence in behavioral patterns, then remains moderate throughout the final growth phase as the system approaches a new equilibrium state.
