The mean value health deviation begins at zero and increases monotonically throughout the 32-step simulation period, reaching final values between approximately 0.70 and 0.75. This steep initial increase suggests an early period of significant misalignment as agents begin with choices aligned to their values but quickly develop deviations, likely due to the high initial habit strength of incumbent whole milk consumption conflicting with evolving health perceptions.

From step 10 to step 25, the rate of increase moderates substantially, with values progressing gradually from approximately 0.55 to 0.60. This plateau-like phase indicates a temporary stabilization of the value-action gap, where the forces of habit persistence, social influence, and incremental adjustments to health perceptions reach a dynamic equilibrium, slowing the growth of deviation.

In the final phase from step 25 to step 32, the metric accelerates again, climbing rapidly to reach 0.70 or higher by the conclusion of the simulation. This late-stage divergence, accompanied by increasing variance across simulation runs, suggests that as the market transition progresses toward completion and alternative milk types gain dominance, the remaining misalignments between deeply ingrained habits and new normative health values become more pronounced, creating greater heterogeneity in consumer behavior.

For decision makers, these trends indicate that interventions aimed at reducing the health value deviation should be timed strategically. The initial rapid growth phase (steps 0-10) represents a critical window for early education and awareness campaigns before habits become entrenched. The stabilization period (steps 10-25) suggests that incremental nudges and social norming interventions may be most effective when the system is in equilibrium. Finally, the late-stage acceleration (steps 25-32) indicates that addressing persistent value-action gaps among resistant consumer segments requires targeted, intensive interventions to overcome deep-seated habit inertia as the market completes its transition.
