**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The non-linear adoption pattern suggests that public health campaigns targeting dietary transitions should prepare for distinct phases rather than expecting steady growth. The prolonged plateau period indicates that intermediate interventions may be necessary to overcome habit persistence and social network inertia during the "messy middle" of behavioral transitions. Decision makers should anticipate oscillatory resistance periods and design sustained engagement strategies rather than relying solely on initial awareness campaigns, as the final 20% of population adoption required nearly a decade to achieve despite strong underlying health messaging.
