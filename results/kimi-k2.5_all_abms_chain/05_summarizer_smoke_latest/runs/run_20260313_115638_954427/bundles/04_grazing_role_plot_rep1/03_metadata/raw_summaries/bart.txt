 The agent-based model represents smallholder farmers managing private pastures in a grazing system . Each year, farmers observe their pasture condition and neighbors' situations to decide how many animals to keep . The model explores how different learning strategies affect long-term outcomes for both the land and the farmers' livelihoods .  This metric represents the initial disposition of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1 type) to deviate from recommended livestock numbers . Negative values indicate a conservative tendency to under-stock relative to recommendations, and positive values indicate an aggressive tendency to over-stock . Some simulation runs evolve toward risk-seeking behavior characterized by sustained overstocking .  In 30 replicate simulations, mean green biomass exhibits consistent temporal patterns while showing substantial variation in magnitude and timing of key transitions . The inflection point around time step 40 to 50 likely corresponds to the period when farmers' adaptive learning strategies, operating within their assigned behavioral types and initial risk dispositions, successfully adjust stocking rates in response to observed pasture degradation .  The time series reveals three distinct dynamic phases in the mean number of livestock destocked per household . Social learning and learning-by-doing strategies enable certain groups to rebuild herds to moderate levels approaching 60 animals, whereas baseline rule-of-thumb approaches result in persistently low stocking rates near 40 .  The ensemble of simulation runs reveals a wide dispersion in equilibrium outcomes . At the lower bound, several trajectories stabilize at approximately 18-20 households, which corresponds closely to the 20 households pre-designated as E-RO-SL1 (social learning) type . Up to 80-90% of the 60-household population ultimately adopted social learning .  The horizontal reference at 20 households represents the initial condition, with final outcomes distributed both above and below this line . Values exceeding 20 suggest that social learning households adopt non-learning behaviors under stress . Values below 20 indicate mortality or exit of dedicated E-RO households, which occurs in approximately half of the simulation runs .
