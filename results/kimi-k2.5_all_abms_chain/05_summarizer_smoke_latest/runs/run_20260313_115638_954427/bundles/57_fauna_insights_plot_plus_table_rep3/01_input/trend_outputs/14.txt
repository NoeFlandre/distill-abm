The vegetated landscape proportion maintains a dynamic equilibrium approximately at 32.7%, initializing at 33.0% and terminating near 33.0% after 2,312 steps, consistent with the target grass proportion parameterization. A pronounced transient occurs at step 365, where coverage plummets to 24.91%—the absolute minimum—before recovering to stable oscillations around 32-33% by step 500. 

The series exhibits strong oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 63 steps, indicating regular seasonal fluctuations in grass availability superimposed on the annual cycle. Trend dynamics shift markedly over the simulation: monotonic increases characterize steps 0 through 1,391 (Kendall's tau 0.211-0.935, p<0.001), while decreasing trajectories emerge from steps 1,624 through 2,087 (Kendall's tau -0.446 to -0.160, p<0.001). 

Fifteen significant inflection points pepper the series at steps 176, 273, 402, 640, 769, 1005, 1134, 1370, 1500, 1576, 1637, 1735, 1864, 2100, and 2217, signaling frequent reversals in directional momentum. Peaks and troughs cluster tightly around 32.995% and 32.994% respectively, with paired extrema occurring at consecutive steps (e.g., 1,826/1,827), reflecting high-frequency variability within the stable equilibrium.

For decision makers, the rapid recovery from the initial one-year shock to maintain long-term stability near configured levels suggests substantial ecosystem resilience to simultaneous grazing and hunting pressures. However, the statistically significant transition from positive to negative trends after step 1,600 indicates that prolonged simulation durations may reveal emerging pressures not apparent in short-term dynamics, warranting attention to potential gradual degradation mechanisms despite apparent equilibrium stability.
