 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change . Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances . Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores .  Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius . The number of active human forager bands remained constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period spanning 2,312 steps .  The final recorded mean value at tick 2312 stands at 117.5, suggesting that nearly all males present in the initial population of 250 ungulates succumb to hunting pressure over the course of the simulation . Wildlife managers should recognize that sex-biased harvesting at these parameters results in near-complete demographic collapse of males, potentially impairing herd reproductive viability long before the maximum simulation timeframe .  The series exhibits periodic oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks, concentrated in the late phase between ticks 2170 and 2265 . Substantial variability exists across the 40 simulation runs regarding both the timing of saturation and final cumulative totals . The majority of cumulative mortality occurs within the first 500–1000 ticks (approximately 1.4–2.7 years)  The simulation series demonstrates a trajectory of complete demographic collapse for the male megaherbivore subpopulation . The high terminal mortality rate (~94%) indicates that current parameter configurations (including 20 human foragers with the specified hunting efficiency and environmental seasonality of 0.8) lead to population collapse rather than sustainable coexistence .  The inevitability of extinction within 2-6 years under current hunting pressure (20 foragers) and environmental conditions suggests that immediate intervention is required to prevent population collapse . The protracted tail of the extinction process implies that detection of demographic collapse may lag significantly behind the onset of irreversible threats .  The 20-human forager configuration generates sufficient hunting pressure to eliminate male energy reserves within 1,500 daily time steps (roughly 4.1 years) The higher food value assigned to males drives preferential predation on this sex, accelerating depletion despite higher processing costs . The pronounced early-phase collapse (ticks 0–500) indicates that interventions to reduce hunting pressure must occur within the first year of simulation time to prevent irreversible male population decline .  By tick 2311, the mean total prey energy reaches exactly zero, indicating complete energetic depletion of the megaherbivore population . This pattern indicates that the configuration of 20 human foragers with immortal status and food storage capabilities, operating under high seasonality (0.8) with the specified energetic and reproductive parameters, generates sufficient hunting pressure to drive prey populations to deterministic extinction .
