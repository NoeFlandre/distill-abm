This initial crash indicates a period of high mortality where deaths from human hunting, resource depletion, and natural causes substantially exceed births.

Between approximately steps 400 and 1000, the rate of energy decline slows considerably, and the trajectories display pronounced oscillations or sawtooth patterns. During this phase, total energy fluctuates primarily between 0 and 5,000 units. These fluctuations reflect demographic dynamics where intermittent recruitment events, such as birth pulses, temporarily increase the population's total energy, which is subsequently depleted by continuous hunting pressure and ongoing mortality.

After step 1000, the majority of simulation runs converge toward zero total energy, with most populations reaching effective extinction or near-extinction levels by step 1200. A small number of trajectories persist with minimal energy, ranging from a few hundred to approximately one thousand units, until steps 1500 to 2000, but eventually all populations decline to zero by the termination of the simulations around step 2200.
