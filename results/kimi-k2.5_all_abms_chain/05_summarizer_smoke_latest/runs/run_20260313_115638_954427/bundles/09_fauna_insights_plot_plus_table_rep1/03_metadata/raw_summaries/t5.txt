the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of foragers remained constant at 20 agents throughout the entire simulation duration . the metric exhibited zero variance and zero standard deviation, with minimum, maximum, mean, and median values all fixed at 20 . forager population establishes a controlled baseline of steady hunting pressure . extinction dynamics can be attributed to environmental factors, prey life history traits, or the fixed intensity of hunting pressure itself . cumulative hunting mortality follows predictable saturation curves rather than linear escalation .
