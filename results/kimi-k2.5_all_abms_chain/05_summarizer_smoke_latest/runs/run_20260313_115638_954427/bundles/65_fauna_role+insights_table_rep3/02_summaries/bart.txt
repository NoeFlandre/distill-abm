Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances. Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores. Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius. Two critical behavioral switches control hunter mortality and storage: imortal-foragers is set to True, meaning hunters do not die from energy depletion. The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 individuals throughout the entire simulation period from initialization through tick 2312. Decision makers should recognize that an initial hunter-to-prey ratio of 1:12.5, combined with the specified sexual dimorphism in prey value (males 30, females 27) and processing costs, exceeds the landscape's sustainable yield under 80% seasonal resource fluctuation. The absence of any stabilization or recovery phase within the 6.3-year horizon indicates that this parameter configuration leads inevitably to megaherbivore population collapse.
