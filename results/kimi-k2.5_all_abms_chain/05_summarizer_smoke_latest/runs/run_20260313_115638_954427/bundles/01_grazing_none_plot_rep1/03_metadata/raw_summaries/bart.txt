 This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model, designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems . The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes central to adaptive co-management .  The simulation tracks household risk attitude (r-parameter) across 100 steps for 60 households . It comprises three behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) The mean reserve biomass trajectory begins at approximately 90,000 units and exhibits three distinct phases over the 100-step simulation .  The mean livestock holdings plus destocked animals per household begins at 90 units at the initiation of the simulation . During the first 20 steps, biomass declines sharply under grazing pressure, reaching minimum values between 40,000 and 44,000 units by step 20 . A steep sigmoid recovery phase dominates steps 25 through 60 .  At the initial phase (approximately steps-20), all simulation runs exhibit a rapid, stepwise increase in the number of households engaged in social learning . During this initial phase, the trajectories diverge substantially . At step 50 through step 100, the system reaches a steady-state regime where mean destock values stabilize at distinct constant levels with minimal temporal variation .  In the context of the grazing system model, these patterns indicate that household destocking behavior follows markedly different long-term trajectories depending on stochastic rainfall realizations and the interaction of heterogeneous risk attitudes with the three behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1)
