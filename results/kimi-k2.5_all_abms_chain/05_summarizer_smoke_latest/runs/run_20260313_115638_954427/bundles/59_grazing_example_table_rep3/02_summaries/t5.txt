Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. The value represents the baseline disposition toward uncertainty management and information noise that was assigned to agents employing social learning strategies at the onset of the simulation. The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock begins at a minimal value of approximately 0.007. From years 33 to approximately 65, the values decrease significantly, dropping from 0.38 to roughly 0.27. The transition around year 50 delineates the shift from rapid recovery to gradual stabilization. At time step 37, livestock holdings reach their minimum value of approximately 41 animals per household, representing the lowest point of the destocking process. Inequality in healthy livestock distribution, measured by the Gini coefficient, initiates at effectively zero, reflecting the uniform initial allocation of livestock units to each household. Over the 100-timestep simulation horizon, this metric demonstrates a consistent monotonic increase, concluding at approximately 0.073. The progression of inequality exhibits distinct temporal phases characterized by varying rates of accumulation. From time step 60 onward, the accumulation continues at a slower steady rate of approximately 0.31 to 0.69 per step, reflecting a stabilized system where ongoing destocking responds to persistent climatic variability rather than initial mismanagement. Superimposed on these trends are oscillatory patterns with a dominant period of approximately 49 time steps.
