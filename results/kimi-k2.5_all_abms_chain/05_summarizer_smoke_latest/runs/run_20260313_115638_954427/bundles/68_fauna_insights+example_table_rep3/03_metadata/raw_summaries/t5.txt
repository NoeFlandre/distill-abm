the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period of 2,312 time steps . since the hunter population neither grows nor declines, hunting pressure remains uniform over time, eliminating demographic stochasticity in the predator population as a variable affecting prey extinction dynamics . the rapid initial accumulation of male mortality suggests that male megaherbivores face acute vulnerability to hunting pressure early in the interaction period . the subsequent plateau and decline indicate that hunting pressure either self-limits through prey depletion or enter a dynamic equilibrium state . from approximately step 850 through 1850, the cumulative harvest stabilizes within a narrow range peaking at 119 around step 1826, while exhibiting persistent oscillations with a dominant period of 63 time steps . this plateau indicates a quasi-equilibrium state where hunting mortality balances against the prey population's reproductive output . the rate of mortality accumulation decelerates markedly through time step 1,160, with the slope diminishing progressively from 0.170 to 0.017 deaths per tick . this suggests the emergence of population equilibrium dynamics, where biennial birth intervals (730 ticks) begin offsetting losses . the resurgence at tick 730 coincides precisely with the first potential birth pulse permitted by the two-year (730-tick) interbirth interval . however, these additions fail to reverse the overall decline, as the oscillation amplitude diminishes progressively . each recruitment cohort is depleted faster than the next can be produced . between steps 125 and 475, the decline continues though potentially at a moderated rate, followed by additional transitional periods at steps 625 and 875 that demarcate shifts in the extinction trajectory . this resurgence proves ephemeral, as the population resumes declining between steps 1624–1855, only to exhibit a second temporary increase between steps 1856–2087 . trend reverses to a slight increase, suggesting a temporary recovery possibly driven by a maturing cohort of males reaching adulthood or sustained predator switching . despite earlier transient recovery, stochastic demographic processes combined with continued incidental mortality drive the male population to extirpation . from step 875 through 2,310, the population enters a prolonged terminal phase marked by high volatility within a general downward trajectory . complete extinction occurs at step 2,311, with available prey energy diminishing to zero . deterministic extinction within approximately 6.3 years demonstrates that the current configuration creates unsustainable predation pressure .
