 The agent-based model represents smallholder farmers managing private pastures in a grazing system . Each year, farmers observe their pasture condition and neighbors' situations to decide how many animals to keep . The model explores how different learning strategies affect long-term outcomes for both the land and the farmers' livelihoods .  The simulation trajectories reveal a pattern of rapid initial divergence followed by segmented stabilization across the 100-year period . The mean reserve biomass exhibits a consistent four-phase trajectory across all simulation runs, with increasing divergence in final outcomes . By step 100, households segregate into distinct clusters: a high-deviation group maintaining values around 0.75-0.78 .  Grazing systems with fixed initial stocking levels will inevitably experience a degradation-recovery cycle lasting approximately 60–70 time steps before stabilizing . Decision makers should anticipate an initial "crash" period around years 30–35 and plan for temporary livelihood adjustments during this transition .  The mean number of destocked livestock per household exhibits three distinct phases . During the initial five steps, values rise sharply from zero to peak levels between 7.0 and 7.5 animals, indicating an immediate system-wide adjustment shock . After step 40, the system stabilizes into a steady-state regime where values flatten and converge to differentiated equilibrium levels ranging from near zero to approximately 1.1 animals per household .  The study looked at 100-year simulation of farm animals . It found that social learning adoption among farmers will be context-dependent and highly variable . In the first 20 years, all simulation replicates show steep increases from 0 to mean values ranging between 50 and 80 animals destocked per household . By year 20, outcomes diverge substantially into distinct trajectory clusters .  The high-count trajectories undergo correction, declining from peaks of 35-42 down to stabilized ranges of 23 to 28 households by time step 60 . Low-count trajectory either stabilize at intermediate levels of approximately 10 to 18 households or continue descending toward extinction . The persistent volatility observed in most trajectories indicates ongoing demographic turnover .
