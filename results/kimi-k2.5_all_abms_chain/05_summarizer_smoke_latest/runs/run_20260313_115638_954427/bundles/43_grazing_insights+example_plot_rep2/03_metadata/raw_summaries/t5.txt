substantial heterogeneity emerges across runs . final equilibrium values varying by as much as 17,000 units between the lowest and highest outcomes . the metric declines steadily from step 5 through approximately step 40 . by step 60 and continuing through step 100, the values plateau at levels ranging from near zero to approximately 1.0 . this divergence in long-term outcomes indicates strong path dependency in the system . large variance across runs suggests that stochastic elements in rainfall patterns, initial household configurations, and neighborhood interaction dynamics create divergent pathways for social learning diffusion . in some runs, less than one-third of households engage in social learning while in others, nearly all households adopt these practices . the persistence of low-adoption equilibria in some simulations indicates that certain households may remain isolated from social learning networks . between time steps 40 and 60, most trajectories transition from rapid change to quasi-stable oscillations around distinct equilibrium levels . by time step 100, the final counts cluster into several distinct groups . one particular run reaches complete extinction of the E-RO strategy by step 65, maintaining zero non-learning households through the remainder of the simulation .
