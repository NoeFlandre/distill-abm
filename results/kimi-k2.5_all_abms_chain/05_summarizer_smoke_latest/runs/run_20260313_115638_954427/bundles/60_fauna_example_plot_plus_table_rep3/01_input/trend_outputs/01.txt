The population of human hunter-gatherer bands remains fixed at 20 throughout the entire simulation duration, from the initial time step through approximately 2,300 time steps. This constant value indicates that no births or deaths occur within the hunter population during the experimental period. The stability arises from the model configuration where foragers are immortal and do not reproduce, maintaining the initial population size of 20 bands regardless of energy levels, hunting success, or environmental conditions. Consequently, hunting pressure on megaherbivore herds remains constant over time, with the same number of active predators present throughout the simulation.
