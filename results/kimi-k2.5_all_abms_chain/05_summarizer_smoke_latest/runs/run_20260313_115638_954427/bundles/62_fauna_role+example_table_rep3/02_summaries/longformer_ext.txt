The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to the combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. Unlike traditional approaches that look at populations as uniform groups, this model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances. Grass-proportion ranges from 0.0 to 1.0, set at 0.33, determining what fraction of the landscape initially carries vegetation. The speed of vegetation recovery is set by regrowth-time, ranging from 0 to 365, set at 3 ticks. Environmental fluctuation is controlled by seasonality, ranging from 0.0 to 1.0, set at 0.8, representing the intensity of seasonal variation in resource availability. Three boolean switches control vegetation behavior: grass-proportion ranges from an initial exploitation phase characterized by intensive predation on reproductive females, followed by density-dependent constraints that limit further accumulation, and ultimate convergence toward a stable but severe mortality burden concentrated on the reproductive core of the ungulate population.
