The model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape. Each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for the herbivores. The model specifically incorporates sexual dimorphism. Animals-gain-from-food ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.5, representing energy obtained from grazing. Threshold for starvation mortality, animals-starvation-threshold, ranges between 0 and 100, set at 1. The distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius, ranging from 1 to 100. The cumulative number of male ungulates harvested by human foragers initiates at zero and follows a strongly saturating trajectory, ultimately stabilizing near 117.5 individuals. The series enters a protracted plateau phase characterized by minimal or statistically insignificant trends, with slopes approaching zero. The cumulative number of postpartum female ungulates hunted begins at zero and undergoes a rapid initial accumulation phase during the first 475 time steps. Inflection points occurring between steps 245 and 285 indicate a deceleration in the rate of increase as the system transitions from exponential growth in cumulative mortality to a more linear accumulation phase. Mortality accumulates steeply, with the rate of increase peaking between steps 232 and 463 where the slope reaches 0.438 deaths per step, before decelerating to 0.170 by step 695. Between 700 and 1200 steps, the accumulation rate diminishes substantially, with slopes dropping to 0.059 and then 0.017, indicating the easily accessible portion of the prey population has been depleted. Female megaherbivores begins at 125 individuals and undergoes a deterministic decline to extinction over the course of 2,311 time steps, terminating at a value of 0. During the initial phase, the population experiences rapid reduction from 125 to values below 20, marked by inflection points at 305 that indicate shifts in the rate of decline. The total energy stored in the male ungulate population begins at 12,500 units. The energy decreases precipitously from the starting value to roughly 4,000 units, representing a loss of over two-thirds of the initial energy reserves. This rapid decline occurs with high consistency across simulation runs, though minor fluctuations appear at tick 65. Population reaches complete energy depletion (zero biomass) at tick 2,311, representing total megaherbivore extinction. Percentage of vegetated landscape patches initiates at approximately 33.0%, reflecting the initial grass coverage parameter, and maintains a long-term mean of 32.7% throughout the simulation.
