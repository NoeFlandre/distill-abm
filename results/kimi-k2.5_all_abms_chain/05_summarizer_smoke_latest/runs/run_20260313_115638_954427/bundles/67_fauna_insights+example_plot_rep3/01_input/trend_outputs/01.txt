The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the simulation, maintaining this exact value from the initial time step through approximately 2,200 steps with no observable fluctuation. This absolute stability persists identically across all repeated simulation runs with zero variance, indicating completely deterministic population dynamics. The constant value directly reflects the immortal-foragers parameter setting, which prevents hunter mortality from energy depletion regardless of foraging success, movement costs, or prey availability, thereby maintaining the initial population established by the init-foragers parameter without attrition.

For decision makers examining megaherbivore extinction dynamics, this stable hunter population baseline indicates that any observed prey population declines or extinction events can be attributed solely to sustained predation pressure from a fixed cohort of 20 active foragers operating without demographic feedback. This experimental configuration effectively isolates the impact of constant hunting pressure on megaherbivore populations, removing the confounding variable of hunter population collapse, density-dependent forager mortality, or hunting pressure reduction due to energy stress, allowing clearer attribution of extinction outcomes to the interaction between fixed human predation pressure and animal life history traits.
