 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges . The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics .  The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the simulation, maintaining this exact value from the initial time step through approximately 2,200 steps . This absolute stability persists identically across all repeated simulation runs with zero variance, indicating completely deterministic population dynamics .  The model demonstrates that male-targeted hunting pressure rapidly extirpates the male subpopulation within approximately 3 to 5 years (1,000 to 2,000 ticks) This sex-biased mortality creates severe demographic consequences for herd viability, potentially leading to reproductive collapse even before total prey numbers reach critically low levels .  The cumulative mortality of prey animals initiates at zero and rises sharply throughout the first 400 to 600 time steps, with most simulation runs accumulating between 200 and 240 deaths . The presence of zero birth outcomes alongside successful reproduction sequences demonstrates that even in the absence of hunting pressure, demographic stochasticity and environmental constraints create significant extinction risk for individual lineages during the establishment phase .  Male animals begin at 125 individuals and undergoes a steady, monotonic decline toward extinction across all simulation replicates . During the initial phase spanning roughly the first 500 time steps, the population experiences its most rapid decrease, typically falling below 30 individuals and often reaching low single digits or zero . Beyond step 1000, only isolated individuals persist in scattered runs, with complete extinction achieved in most replicates by approximately step 1500 .  The initial collapse reflects the immediate impact of hunting pressure from 20 immortal foragers on the initial herd population . Some populations stabilize temporarily at low levels (between 10 and 30 individuals) while others continue decreasing toward single digits . The variation in extinction timing reflects stochastic elements in hunter pursuit decisions, herd movement patterns, and demographic events .  The current configuration of hunter density (20 foragers), female food value (27 units), and environmental seasonality (0.8) generates unsustainable predation pressure resulting in inevitable female population extinction . The percentage of green patches initiates at approximately 32% to 33%, remaining relatively stable with minor fluctuations over the first 200 time steps .
