Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances. Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores. Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius. Two critical behavioral switches control hunter mortality and storage: imortal-foragers is set to True, meaning hunters do not die from energy depletion. The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands maintains a constant value of 20 throughout the entire simulation duration.
