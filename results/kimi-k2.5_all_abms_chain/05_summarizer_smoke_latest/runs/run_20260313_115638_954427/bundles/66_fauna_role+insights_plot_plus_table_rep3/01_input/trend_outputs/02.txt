**Implications for Decision Makers:**

The results indicate that the current parameter configuration produces unsustainable forager energetics, with hunting returns insufficient to offset movement and processing costs. Decision makers should consider that prey populations initialized at 250 individuals with 20 foragers appear inadequate to support positive energy balance under the specified hunting and movement costs. The presence of immortal foragers masks what would otherwise be catastrophic population collapse, suggesting that realistic mortality constraints would likely result in forager extinction or abandonment of the area. The seasonal patterns indicate that interventions targeting resource availability during critical seasonal bottlenecks could potentially moderate the decline, though the secular downward trend suggests systemic overexploitation that seasonal management alone cannot resolve. Parameter adjustments reducing forager movement costs, increasing prey nutritional value, or reducing initial forager numbers may be necessary to achieve energetic sustainability.
