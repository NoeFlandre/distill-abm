**Implications for Decision Makers:**

Under the current configuration of 20 human hunters, high environmental seasonality (0.8), and 33% initial vegetation cover, extinction of the female megaherbivore population appears to be a deterministic outcome, yet the timing remains highly unpredictable with a variance span of over 1,500 steps. This suggests that management interventions must be implemented within the critical first 475 steps to prevent populations from falling below viable thresholds, as this initial window represents the steepest decline phase before demographic resilience is lost. Furthermore, the 63-tick oscillation period indicates that the timing of conservation actions relative to seasonal resource cycles may influence short-term population stability, offering potential tactical opportunities for intervention scheduling even during the overall decline phase. Decision makers should account for high outcome uncertainty when planning monitoring or mitigation strategies, as identical initial conditions can produce extinction timelines varying by several years.
