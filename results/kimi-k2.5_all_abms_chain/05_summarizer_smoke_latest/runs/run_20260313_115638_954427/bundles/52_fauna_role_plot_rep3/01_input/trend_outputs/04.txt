Across all simulation runs, the cumulative count of female prey killed exhibits a consistent three-phase dynamic. In the initial phase, spanning approximately the first 500 time steps, the metric increases rapidly from zero, with most simulations accumulating between 90 and 110 female deaths by step 500. This represents the period of most intensive hunting activity.

During the middle phase, from roughly step 500 to step 1000, the rate of increase decelerates markedly. The cumulative count rises more gradually, typically reaching values between 110 and 120 by step 1000. This deceleration suggests a depletion of accessible female prey as the population is reduced.

In the final phase, beyond step 1000 and extending to the maximum runtime of approximately 2200 steps, the metric stabilizes into a plateau. Final cumulative values consistently converge within a narrow range of approximately 115 to 125 females killed, with minimal additional accumulation after step 1200. This asymptotic behavior indicates effective cessation of female hunting mortality, consistent with the near-complete exhaustion of the initial female prey population, which would be approximately 125 individuals given the starting parameters of 250 total prey and expected equal sex ratios.
