 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges . The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics .  The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation duration . The total energy available to forager bands exhibits a pronounced monotonic decrease throughout the simulation, beginning at approximately 2000 units and declining to roughly -38230 units by the conclusion of the observed period at tick 2310 .  The cumulative count of hunted postpartum female prey initiated at zero and underwent a multiphasic accumulation trajectory over the simulation duration . The trajectory demonstrates how targeted hunting pressure on high-value reproductive classes can rapidly accumulate to a ceiling defined by population collapse, after which the prey base cannot sustain further hunting mortality of this specific demographic .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model suggests that prey populations initially establish and expand under parameters settings of moderate hunting pressure (20 foragers with differential processing costs by sex), grassland dynamics (seasonality=0.8, regrowth-time=3), and life history constraints (birth-spacing=730 ticks) The male megaherbivore population initiated at 125 individuals and underwent a progressive decline to approximately 6.284 ticks .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model began with a population of 125 female prey animals . Over the course of approximately 2,312 time steps, this population underwent a complete extinction, declining to zero individuals by the conclusion of the run . During the latter portion of the simulation, the population persisted at very low levels for an extended duration, with the median population across all time steps being approximately 1 .  The combined energy reserves of male prey animals begin at 12,500 units, representing the starting biomass of the sexually dimorphic ungulate population . Within the first 125 ticks, this energy undergoes a rapid contraction, briefly stabilizing near tick 65 at approximately 11,608 units before resuming decline . From this initial state, the system underwent a prolonged trajectory toward complete extinction, reaching zero available prey energy at approximately tick 2311 .  The last significant change point occurred at tick 2310, immediately preceding total collapse . The percentage of vegetated landscape patches maintains a stable equilibrium at approximately 33% throughout the 2312-tick simulation period . The median aggregate energy of 146.375 reflects the strongly right-skewed distribution dominated by high initial values followed by prolonged residence at low population levels .
