 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges . The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics .  The aggregate trajectory of total forager energy across 40 simulation runs exhibits a monotonic, statistically significant decline over 2312 steps . The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remained constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period, exhibiting no variation from the initial value at any point .  The cumulative mortality count of postpartum female ungulates initiates at zero and undergoes a rapid accumulation phase during the first approximately 500 steps, rising steeply to values between 100 and 120 . Within the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model framework, this saturating accumulation pattern reflects the finite availability of post partum females within the initial prey population of 250 agents .  The cumulative mortality trajectories exhibit a characteristic saturation pattern, beginning at zero deaths and rising steeply during initial steps before asymptotically approaching maximum values consistent with prey depletion . Across the 40 simulation replicates, the aggregate mean starts at 0 and terminates at 235 deaths, with individual runs converging on values between 225 and 250 deaths .  The total energy of male animals exhibits a downward trajectory from an initial value of 12,500 energy units, ultimately reaching complete depletion at approximately step 2,284 across all 40 simulation replicates . The decline follows a nonlinear pattern characterized by three distinct phases: an initial rapid decrease, a prolonged intermediate phase with decelerating reduction rates, and a terminal convergence to zero .  Some populations persisting until step 2,300 while others reach depletion by step 600-800 . The decline follows a distinct multi-phase pattern characterized by varying rates of depletion and temporary fluctuations . Some populations maintain residual energy levels beyond step 2000, whereas others deplete by step 500 .  Percentage of vegetated landscape patches initiates at approximately 33.0%, consistent with the parameterized grass proportion . Within the first 365 time steps, the system undergoes a severe decline in vegetation cover, reaching a minimum value of approximately 24.9% at time step 365 . This represents a reduction of roughly 8 percentage points from the initial state .
