Model focuses on stationary households each owning a specific patch of land. Each year, farmers observe their pasture condition and neighbors' situations to decide how many animals to keep. The model specifically investigates three distinct approaches to decision-making. The average initial risk attitude among social learning agents remains fixed at approximately 0.0074 throughout the entire 100-year simulation period. This constancy indicates that initial risk disposition is treated as a static attribute within the model. The metric preserves its initial value without fluctuation because it captures starting conditions rather than dynamically evolving risk perceptions. A 26-year period of biomass decline underscores the vulnerability of newly established private pasture systems to initial overstocking. The eventual recovery and overshoot of initial biomass levels demonstrates that adaptive learning mechanisms can effectively reconcile livestock production with pasture health. However, the decision makers must incorporate long-term horizons to accurately assess sustainability outcomes in smallholder grazing systems. Initial stocking levels exceeded the sustainable carrying capacity of the private pastures, triggering ecological degradation that necessitated severe destocking. The subsequent recovery demonstrates that learning-by-doing and social learning strategies enable farmers to adapt their management practices. A significant transition occurs around year 51 (change point), marking a shift to a new dynamic regime where inequality continues to increase but through different underlying processes. A second change point at year 71 indicates another structural adjustment, after which the metric progresses toward its final value of 0.0726 with superimposed oscillatory patterns. Inflection points clustered around years 45–47 and 53–57 indicate shifts in the acceleration of destocking rates. A change point at year 60 marks further stabilization in the accumulation pattern. By the final decades, the metric proceeds at a reduced but persistent pace with minimal additional deceleration.
