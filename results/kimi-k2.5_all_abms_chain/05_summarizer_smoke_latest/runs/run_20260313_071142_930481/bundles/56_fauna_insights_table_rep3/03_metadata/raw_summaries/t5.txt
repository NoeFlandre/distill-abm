the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period . this static trajectory reflects the model configuration in which hunters neither reproduce nor die from energy depletion . for decision makers, this constant predator density provides a stable baseline for experiments . between steps 1,160 and 1,391, the trend stabilizes into a plateau characterized by negligible net growth . males, despite offering higher nutritional returns, impose greater processing costs (6 versus 3), potentially causing forager bands employing dynamic optimal foraging strategies to encounter diminishing returns as prey availability contracts within the 211211 wrapped landscape . the trajectory reveals a dominant oscillatory period of 63 ticks superimposed on the long-term trend . this suggests regular pulsatile variation in hunting success potentially aligned with seasonal resource availability or herd movement patterns . the front-loaded nature of female mortality indicates that conservation interventions must be implemented immediately upon human entry into the system . a critical transition occurs around tick 1673–1702, after which reproductive output enters a significant decline phase . this degenerative phase contains additional inflection points at ticks 1777, 1847, and 1930, indicating changing rates of reproductive decline . pronounced oscillations persisting through the decline suggest continued seasonal breeding attempts despite deteriorating conditions . by approximately tick 700, the population stabilizes at a dramatically reduced density of roughly 8-10 individuals . the population exhibits marginal recovery attempts, peaking at 10.5 individuals around tick 730, then fluctuating between 2 and 9 individuals through tick 1000 . this recovery proves insufficient to achieve viability . a second minor resurgence appeared between years 5.1 and 5.7 (ticks
