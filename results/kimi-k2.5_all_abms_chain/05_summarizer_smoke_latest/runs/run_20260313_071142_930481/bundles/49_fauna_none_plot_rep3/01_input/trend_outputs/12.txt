Between approximately steps 400 and 800, several simulation runs exhibit temporary deceleration in the rate of decline, with values fluctuating between approximately 500 and 4,000 units. From approximately step 800 to 1,500, the metric continues to decrease across remaining active runs, with values predominantly falling below 2,000 units and approaching zero. By step 1,500, most trajectories have reached or nearly reached zero energy.

A subset of trajectories persists at low energy levels (generally below 500 units) for extended durations, with some runs maintaining small but non-zero values until approximately steps 1,800 to 2,200 before ultimately declining to zero.

The variation in extinction timing reflects stochastic processes in hunter movement, prey herd dynamics, and encounter rates.
