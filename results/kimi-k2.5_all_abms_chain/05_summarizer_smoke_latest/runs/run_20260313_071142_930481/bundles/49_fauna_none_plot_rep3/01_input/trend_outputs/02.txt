The total energy available to all forager bands exhibits a consistent downward trajectory throughout the simulation runs. At initialization (step 0), the collective energy reserve registers at approximately 2000 units. During the initial phase spanning roughly the first 500 steps, energy decreases rapidly to approximately -5000 units, representing a depletion of about 7000 energy units. Following this initial transient, the trajectory stabilizes into a steady, approximately linear decline through the remainder of the simulation.

From step 500 to step 2000, the rate of energy loss remains relatively constant, averaging approximately 15 to 20 units per step. By step 1000, total energy reaches approximately -12,000 units. At step 1500, values range between -20,000 and -25,000 units. By step 2000, most runs register between -28,000 and -32,000 units, with one extended run continuing to approximately -38,000 units by step 2200.

Across all repetitions, the trajectories demonstrate high congruence after the initial 500 steps, with minimal divergence among individual runs. The uniformity in the linear decline phase suggests a reproducible system behavior where the energetic costs of forager movement consistently exceed the energy gains obtained from hunting ungulate herds. Given that the model configuration specifies immortal foragers with food storage capabilities, the sustained negative trend indicates that the foraging radius, prey availability, and hunting success rates under the current parameter settings (including high seasonality of 0.8 and specific prey processing costs) result in a persistent energy deficit that accumulates linearly over time rather than stabilizing or recovering.
