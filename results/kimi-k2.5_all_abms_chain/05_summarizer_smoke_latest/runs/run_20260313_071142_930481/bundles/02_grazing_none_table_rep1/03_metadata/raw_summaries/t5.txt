model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . it introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making . the model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions . over the 100-timestep simulation period, the average deviation from recommended livestock levels among extension agents exhibits a distinct three-phase trajectory . the series begins at near-zero deviation (0.0074 at timestep 0), indicating initial compliance with recommended stocking rates . this peak represents a nearly 50-fold increase from initial conditions . despite initial resource depletion under combined grazing pressure of 20 households per behavioral type, the integration of learning capabilities enables a recovery that ultimately enhances pasture conditions beyond baseline levels . the average actual available green biomass initiates at approximately 45,000 units and exhibits relative stability throughout the initial nine timesteps . after timestep 30, destocking rates stabilize at low levels, fluctuating without significant directional trend through the middle period . the long-term trajectory indicates a transition from an initial crisis phase characterized by high resource pressure and forced animal removal, toward a stabilized system . despite the consistent upward trend, the system maintains a relatively equitable distribution of healthy livestock across households . by year 25, the system exhibits a significant behavioral shift as the rate of destocking drops sharply to less than 0.7 animals annually . this long-term deceleration is punctuated by oscillatory dynamics concentrated around years 22–26, 49–61, and 79–80 . these fluctuations generate multiple inflection points between years 45 and 75 . the metric continues its slow ascent toward the terminal value of 96.8 animals per household .
