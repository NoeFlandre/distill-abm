This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It is designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems. The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes. The mean reserve biomass initiates at approximately 90,000 units and immediately undergoes a brief marginal increase to roughly 91,000 within the first five steps. Subsequently, the system enters a decline phase lasting from approximately step 5 to step 35, during which biomass decreases monotonically to minimum values ranging between 80,000 and 88,000. The system exhibits persistent heterogeneity, with final values spanning a wide range from approximately -0.35 to 0.78. During the first 20 to 25 steps, biomass declines consistently across all simulation runs, reaching minimum values between 40,000 and 42,000. During this period, green biomass increases gradually from the minimum values to approximately 50,000 to 55,000 units. A recovery phase begins around step 25 and continues through step 50. All simulations commence with Gini coefficient values approaching zero at step 0, indicating initial equality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households. After step 40-60, simulations diverge into distinct trajectories: some maintain steady linear increases reaching intermediate values of 0.06-0.08 by step 100. Others exhibit accelerated growth rates in the final 40 steps, achieving steep slopes that terminate above 0.09. The values diverge rapidly across simulation runs, with trajectories spreading to range between approximately 10 and 40 households by step 20. By step 100, final values range from 0 to approximately 26 households. Interpreting these trends within the model context, the E-RO strategy represents non-learning baseline behavior.
