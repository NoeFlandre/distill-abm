However, this pulse proves insufficient for population stabilization, as hunting pressure from immortal, energy-storing foragers immediately resumes extraction. A second weaker positive trend appears between ticks 1,856 and 2,087, though with negligible magnitude, suggesting failed demographic rebounds as the population approaches critical thresholds.

**Terminal Collapse (Ticks 2,156–2,311)**

The final phase exhibits accelerating decline with multiple inflection points indicating increasingly unstable population dynamics. Energy levels fluctuate erratically before the final exhaustion at tick 2,311. The transition from oscillatory decline to final collapse suggests that the prey population falls below a critical density where Allee effects or mate limitation, compounded by continued predation, prevent any further energetic accumulation in the remaining individuals.

**Implications**

The configuration produces deterministic extinction within approximately six years, indicating that sustainable coexistence requires regulatory mechanisms absent in the current setup—specifically the introduction of hunter mortality to create density-dependent feedback, reduction of initial hunter numbers below the threshold of 20, or removal of unlimited food storage capabilities that enable continuous predation regardless of immediate energetic needs. The brief recovery windows around years 4 and 5 demonstrate that biological reproduction can temporarily outpace mortality under specific demographic structures, but without constraints on hunting pressure, these demographic pulses merely postpone inevitable collapse rather than preventing it.
