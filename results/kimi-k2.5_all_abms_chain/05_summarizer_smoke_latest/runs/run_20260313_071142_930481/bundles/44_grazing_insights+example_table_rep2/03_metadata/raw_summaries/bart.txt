 This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model, designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems . The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes central to adaptive co-management .  The mean household risk attitude across the population begins at a very low value of approximately 0.007 . During the first 32 time steps, this metric undergoes a rapid and significant increase, with statistically significant upward trends detected across consecutive windows from steps 0 through 32 . The initial rapid increase suggests an early phase of information gathering and heightened caution, while the subsequent decline and stabilization indicate that agents learn to moderate their risk attitudes .  The system exhibits strong resilience characterized by an initial vulnerability window (approximately the first 30 time steps) followed by sustainable recovery . The average livestock holdings per household begin at 90 animals. Over the first approximately 10 time steps, livestock numbers decrease sharply with a steep negative trajectory . This suggests an immediate correction phase where households adjust their herds downward in response to initial pasture conditions or uncertainty about resource availability .  The number of households engaging in social learning begins at zero and follows a logistic diffusion pattern throughout the simulation period . The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households begins at effectively zero, indicating perfect equality among all households at the outset . Over the 100-year period, this inequality rises steadily and monotonically, reaching approximately 0.073 .  The number of households employing the E-RO (non-learning) strategy begins at 20.0 and initially exhibits a modest increase, reaching a peak value of 21.1 at time step 8 . The decline continues through time step 45 but at a decelerating rate, with values dropping from roughly 17.5 to 15.6 . After step 45, the rate of decrease slows considerably, and from approximately step 66 onward the series stabilizes with no statistically significant trend .
