Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. The aggregate mean household risk attitude initiates at approximately 0.007. It undergoes a steep ascent during the initial 33 steps. This early phase culminates in a peak value of 0.381 around step 33. The average quantity of livestock destocked per household initiates at zero and undergoes an immediate, sharp escalation, attaining a maximum value of approximately 6.6 by the third yearly time step. This early peak reflects acute resource pressure as initial livestock populations encounter pasture biomass constraints. The initial expansion phase spans approximately steps 0 through 20. The count rises rapidly from zero as social learning behaviors activate and spread through the stationary household network. A significant change point occurs at step 40, signaling a system transition where active diffusion gives way to saturation dynamics. The population of households employing the non-learning E-RO strategy initiates at 20 and undergoes a modest initial expansion, reaching a maximum of approximately 21 households around step 8. Over the subsequent 55 steps, the count decreases significantly from roughly 20 to approximately 15 households. The rate of decline moderates considerably after step 45, and from step 65 through the conclusion at step 100, the population stabilizes with minor fluctuations around 15 households, ultimately reaching approximately 14.7.
