The metric measuring deviation from recommended livestock levels exhibits substantial heterogeneity across simulation runs, with trajectories diverging rapidly from initial conditions and stabilizing into distinct long-term patterns.

At initialization (Step 0), the average deviation clusters tightly around values between approximately -0.1 and 0.2, with most runs starting near 0.0, indicating that households begin with stocking levels close to recommendations.

During the early period (Steps 0-20), the system undergoes rapid divergence. Approximately one-third of simulation runs exhibit steep positive trajectories, with deviation values rising from near 0.0 to between 0.6 and 0.8 by Step 20-30, indicating persistent overstocking relative to recommendations. Conversely, another subset of runs declines to values between -0.2 and -0.1 during this same interval, representing conservative stocking strategies.

The middle period (Steps 20-60) reveals path-dependent stabilization and secondary transitions. The high-deviation group (0.6-0.8) maintains relatively stable overstocking behavior with minor fluctuations around a mean of approximately 0.75. A middle tier stabilizes between 0.4 and 0.6. The most risk-averse trajectories continue descending, reaching minimum values of approximately -0.3 to -0.4 by Step 60.

The divergence pattern suggests that stochastic variation in early-period rainfall or initial conditions interacts with the three learning strategies (experiential rule-of-thumb, learning-by-doing, and social learning) to produce divergent risk dispositions. Some simulation runs evolve toward risk-seeking behavior characterized by sustained overstocking (positive deviation of 0.6-0.8), while others evolve toward risk-averse behavior with sustained understocking (negative deviation of -0.3 to -0.4). The lack of convergence to a central value by Step 100 indicates that the learning mechanisms and environmental variability generate persistent heterogeneity in stocking strategies rather than driving the population toward uniform optimal behavior.
