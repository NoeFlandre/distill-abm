Model focuses on stationary households each owning a specific patch of land. Each year, farmers observe their pasture condition and neighbors' situations to decide how many animals to keep. The model specifically investigates three distinct approaches to decision-making. Values range from approximately -0.11 to +0.18, with multiple distinct horizontal trajectories maintained throughout the simulation period. Negative values indicate a conservative tendency to under-stock relative to recommendations, and positive values indicate an aggressive tendency to over-stock. At initialization (Step 0-20), the system undergoes rapid divergence. Some configurations maintain levels near the minimum of approximately 80,000 to 85,000 units through time step 40. Others exhibit early recovery, reaching values between 90,000 and 95,000 units. Biomass values at time step 100 vary considerably across runs. Between years 35 and 50, values stabilize at this minimum level, representing a trough where stocking rates hover near 40–42 as pastures remain degraded and households maintain minimal viable herds. By year 100, final outcomes stratify into distinct performance levels: approximately one set of households remains at low equilibrium levels around 40–45 animals, another cluster stabilizes in the 45–50 range, and a smaller subset achieves higher levels of 55–62 animals. The absence of livestock reproduction (b=0) indicates that these inequalities arise solely from differential success in preserving existing animal stocks under varying pasture conditions. At initialization (step 0-20), no households have activated social learning. By step 10, most trajectories show between 12-24 households with social learning and by step 20 counts range from approximately 20-50 households depending on the simulation run. Households with learning capabilities revert to rule-of-thumb behaviors when social information becomes unreliable or environmental feedback is ambiguous. Trajectories that spiked early generally experience a decline between steps 30 and 50, stabilizing from peaks of 35-42 down to levels between 23 and 26.
