 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges . The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics .  The number of active human foragers begins at 20 and remains fixed at this value throughout the entire simulation . The total energy available to all forager bands begins at approximately 0 at step 0, then declines continuously throughout the simulation . By step 1000, the total energy reaches approximately -15,000, and by step 2000, it falls to roughly -30,000 to -32,000 .  The timing of the timing of reproduction is most pronounced in the early stages of the cycle . The rate of mortality increases rapidly during the initial period of reproduction . By approximately 1000 to 1500 time steps, the cumulative number of postpartum deaths begins at zero .  The number of male animals initializes at approximately 125 individuals across all simulation runs, consistent with the starting prey abundance and sexual dimorphism parameters . The decline manifests in two distinct phases: an initial rapid depletion occurring within the first 300 to 500 steps, during which populations typically fall from 125 to between 20 and 40 individuals, followed by a more gradual attrition phase extending to step 1000 .  The total energy available in all prey animals begins at approximately 25,000 energy units across simulation runs . Within the first 400 to 500 time steps, total energy undergoes a steep reduction, with most simulations falling below 5,000 by step 500 . This rapid decline reflects intensive hunting pressure on the male subpopulation, as hunters target these individuals based on their higher food value .  The percentage of green patches initiates at approximately 33%, consistent with the initial grass-proportion parameter of 0.33% . Within the first 50 steps, it declines slightly to roughly 32.1% before recovering gradually to approximately 32.5% by step 300 as initial grazing pressure stabilizes against regrowth .
