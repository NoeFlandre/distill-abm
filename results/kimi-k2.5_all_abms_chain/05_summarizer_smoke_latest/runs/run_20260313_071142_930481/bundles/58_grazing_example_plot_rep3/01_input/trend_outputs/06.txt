The mean number of livestock destocked per household initiates at zero, then rises sharply within the first 5 time steps to reach peak values between 6 and 7.5. This initial surge reflects immediate resource pressure following system initialization, where households begin with 90 livestock units under variable rainfall conditions (mean 200, standard deviation 100), creating an initial mismatch between animal density and pasture carrying capacity in the stationary private property system.

Following this peak, the metric declines rapidly through approximately time step 20, dropping to values ranging between 0.5 and 2.0 across different simulation runs. This steep reduction indicates active destocking responses as the three behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) adapt to biomass constraints, with households reducing herd sizes to align available forage with animal needs.

Between time steps 20 and 40, the decline continues but moderates, exhibiting pronounced fluctuations while converging toward values below 1.5. By time step 40, distinct trajectories emerge, with most runs stabilizing at low destocking levels while maintaining heterogeneous outcomes. Through the remainder of the simulation period to step 100, the majority of runs converge toward zero destocking, indicating successful adaptation to sustainable stocking rates. However, several trajectories persist with low-level destocking between 0.5 and 1.0 animals per household, with one particular run maintaining values near 1.0–1.2 throughout the final 40 steps, suggesting differential effectiveness of learning-by-doing and social learning mechanisms in managing persistent uncertainty.

The variance across simulation runs is highest during the initial 20 steps, then gradually diminishes, though distinct clusters of behavioral outcomes remain evident through the conclusion of the simulation. This pattern reflects the divergent adaptive pathways of the heterogeneous agent population as they navigate the dual-loop learning processes, climatic variability, and fixed spatial constraints characteristic of private property grazing systems without mobility options.
