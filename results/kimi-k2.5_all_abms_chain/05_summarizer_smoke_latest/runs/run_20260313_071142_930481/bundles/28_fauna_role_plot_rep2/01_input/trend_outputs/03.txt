The cumulative count of male prey killed begins at zero across all simulation runs and increases monotonically throughout the temporal progression of the model. During the initial approximately 500 time steps, the rate of accumulation is steep and relatively consistent across runs, with cumulative kills reaching values between roughly 60 and 110 individuals. Between time steps 500 and 1000, the rate of increase decelerates substantially, exhibiting considerable variation across simulation replicates. Some trajectories plateau as early as approximately 800 time steps with cumulative totals near 105 kills, while others continue accumulating kills at reduced rates through approximately step 1500. By step 2000, all trajectories have asymptotically approached maximum values, with final cumulative counts ranging from approximately 105 to 125 males killed. The cessation of new kills after these plateaus are reached suggests the effective depletion of the male prey population available to hunters, with the variation in timing reflecting stochastic differences in encounter rates and hunter success across replicates. Given the initial ungulate population of 250 animals and the higher energetic return (food-value-males = 30) associated with males relative to females (food-value-females = 27), the plateau values approaching 110-125 cumulative kills indicate near-complete harvesting of the initial male cohort, with minor variation potentially attributable to male births occurring during the early simulation phases before population collapse.
