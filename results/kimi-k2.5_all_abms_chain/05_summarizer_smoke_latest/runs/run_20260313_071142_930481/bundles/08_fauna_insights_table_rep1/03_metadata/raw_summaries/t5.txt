the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remained constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period . this metric exhibited no variation from the initial time step through the conclusion of the experiments, maintaining the starting value without deviation across all recorded intervals . the fixed hunter population isolates the effect of constant hunting pressure on megaherbivore extinction dynamics . behavioral shifts occurring at regular 125-tick intervals (125, 250, 375, 500, 625) and a final inflection near tick 850 . pattern suggests initial intensive harvest of males followed by rapid depletion of accessible targets . plateau indicates temporary cessation of net hunting mortality accumulation . the cumulative count increases by fewer than 3 individuals over approximately 750 ticks, reaching roughly 119 by tick 1336 and stabilizing near 122–123 by tick 1600 . this plateau suggests effective cessation of female hunting, potentially indicating functional extinction of female herds, extreme prey scarcity forcing complete prey switching . trend analysis confirms transition from monotonic increase in early windows to negligible or slightly negative trends in the final observation periods . rapid saturation of cumulative mortality within approximately five years suggests that postpartum females face extreme vulnerability to hunting pressure due to their enhanced nutritional value . a secondary accumulation phase occurs from approximately tick 1,400 to tick 1,876, during which cumulative deaths rise again to reach a maximum value of approximately 240.3 deaths . the trajectory enters a stabilization phase where cumulative deaths hover between 235 and 240 . this represents the upper bound of mortality observed across the simulation series . temporary recovery during phase three may indicate a window of reduced hunting efficiency, stochastic demographic escape, or the maturation of subsequent cohorts temporarily offsetting mortality . however, the sustained low median population level of approximately 1.3 individuals across the full temporal sequence indicates that for the majority of the simulation, the male population existed in a critically endangered state . local population maxima occur with progressively diminishing amplitude . the population enters its terminal decline after tick 2087, representing complete megaherbivore extinction . decision makers should interpret the intermittent population increases occurring during years 4–6 as stochastic demographic noise . rapid initial depletion within the first 350 ticks indicates intervention thresholds must be established and triggered almost immediately upon detection of hunting activity . however, the ultimate extinction outcome despite this resilience implies that sustained hunting pressure creates an extinction debt that manifests after approximately 6.25 years (2,284 ticks), rendering short-term recovery indicators unreliable . the total available energy oscillated with a dominant periodicity of approximately 63 ticks (roughly bi-monthly cycles given the temporal scaling of 365 ticks per year) these oscillations generated temporary stabilization points where energy levels briefly plateaued—evidenced by recurring local peaks and valleys . by approximately tick 1,600, the system entered
