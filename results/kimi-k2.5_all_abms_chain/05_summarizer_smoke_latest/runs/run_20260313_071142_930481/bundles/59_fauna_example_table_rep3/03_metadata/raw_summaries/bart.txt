 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges . The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics .  The population of human hunter-gatherer bands maintains a fixed value of 20 across all 2312 recorded time steps . This absolute stability reflects the model's parameterization where hunters are immortal and do not reproduce, effectively creating a constant predator pressure scenario .
