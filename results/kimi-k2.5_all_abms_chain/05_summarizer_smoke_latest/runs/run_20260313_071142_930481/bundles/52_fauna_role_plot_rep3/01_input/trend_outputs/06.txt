: {The cumulative birth trajectories exhibit an initial reproductive quiescence lasting approximately 750 steps, during which no births occur across any replicates. This interval corresponds to the temporal delay required for the initial cohort of 250 ungulates to reach reproductive maturity and complete first gestation cycles given the specified interbirth interval of 730 ticks.

Around step 750 to 800, nearly all simulations exhibit a synchronized pulse of reproduction, with cumulative counts instantaneously increasing to values ranging between 1 and 8. This initial birth event represents the first reproductive wave of the founding population, with inter-replicate variation in magnitude reflecting stochastic differences in initial sex ratios, age structures, or differential survival during the establishment phase.

Following this initial reproductive episode, the trajectories diverge markedly into distinct dynamic regimes. Several replicates demonstrate continued but sporadic accumulation of births at discrete intervals—specifically around steps 900, 1000, 1100, 1400, and 1600—with cumulative births reaching terminal values between 5 and 10 by steps 1500 to 2000. These stepwise increases occurring at intervals approximating the 730-tick birth-spacing parameter indicate that surviving females in these populations completed multiple reproductive cycles despite pressures from human hunting and seasonal resource variability.

Conversely, other trajectories plateau immediately after the initial birth pulse, maintaining constant cumulative birth counts of 2, 3, or 4 for durations extending to steps 1400 through 1500. These stagnations indicate that while some females successfully reproduced once, subsequent reproductive cessation occurred due to either maternal mortality, offspring mortality, or energetic constraints preventing completion of additional interbirth intervals.

Across the ensemble, final cumulative birth counts range from approximately 1 to 10, with individual runs terminating between steps 1400 and 2100. This limited reproductive output—representing fewer than 10 total additions to the population from an initial cohort of 250 animals over nearly 2000 timesteps—indicates that the combined pressures of 20 human foragers and strong environmental seasonality (0.8) create demographic conditions where mortality rates substantially exceed reproductive recruitment, resulting in population trajectories characterized by early reproductive pulses followed by either gradual accumulation of rare additional births or rapid reproductive cessation.
