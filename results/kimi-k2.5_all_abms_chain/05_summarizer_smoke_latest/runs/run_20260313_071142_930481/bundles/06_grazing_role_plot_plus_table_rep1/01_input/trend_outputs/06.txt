The average number of livestock destocked per household exhibits high initial volatility followed by progressive stabilization. The series begins at zero, indicating no forced removals at initialization, but rises sharply to reach a maximum exceeding 6.6 animals per household by year 3. This represents the peak resource constraint across the simulation.

From year 3 through approximately year 30, destocking rates decline significantly despite intermittent fluctuations. Secondary peaks occur at years 9 and 12 with values of approximately 3.4 and 2.7 animals respectively, followed by diminishing oscillations through year 26. Statistical trend analysis confirms significant decreasing patterns across three consecutive windows spanning years 0–10, 11–21, and 22–32, with slope magnitudes moderating from -0.381 to -0.060, indicating slowing but consistent improvement.

A structural change occurs around year 30, after which the system enters a low-level equilibrium phase. Destocking rates stabilize near the median value of 0.31 animals, with minimal variation through year 100. Between years 33 and 65, no significant trends are detected, suggesting the system has reached a steady state. A minor increasing trend appears between years 66 and 76, though destocking remains below one animal per household, before returning to stable low levels.

By year 100, mean destocking settles at approximately 0.31 animals per household. The long-term mean of 0.96 reflects the dominance of early high-destocking years, while the convergence of terminal values to the median indicates the system has achieved consistent low-stress conditions.

In the context of private pasture management with 90 initial animals per household and significant rainfall variability, these patterns indicate an initial mismatch between herd size and carrying capacity under variable precipitation. The three learning strategies—experiential rule-of-thumb, learning-by-doing, and social learning—facilitate rapid adaptation, with households progressively aligning their stocking decisions with resource availability. By year 30, the learning mechanisms have enabled farmers to establish sustainable management practices, resulting in minimal forced destocking for the remaining 70 years despite ongoing environmental uncertainty.
