The mean household risk attitude across the farming population exhibits a distinct trajectory over the 100-year simulation period, characterized by four sequential phases. Initially, during the first approximately 10 years, risk attitude increases rapidly and significantly from a starting value near zero (0.0074) to approximately 0.22. This early escalation continues through year 33, though at a decelerating rate, reaching a maximum value of 0.3814 around year 33. This peak represents the highest average risk disposition observed, indicating that farmers initially develop greater risk tolerance or confidence in their decision-making as they accumulate experience with their private pastures.

Following this peak, a sustained decline phase spans roughly years 33 to 65, during which the average risk attitude decreases significantly through consecutive periods, dropping from 0.3814 to approximately 0.1913 by year 64. This downward adjustment suggests a collective correction toward more conservative risk positions after the initial period of experimentation. Notably, this decline exhibits an oscillatory pattern with a dominant period of approximately 49 years, indicating cyclical adjustments in how farmers perceive and respond to uncertainty.

The final phase, from approximately year 65 through year 100, shows stabilization with no significant trend, maintaining values around 0.19 and ending at 0.1911. This equilibrium suggests that after six decades of adaptation, the population's aggregate risk disposition stabilizes at a moderate level above the initial starting point but well below the mid-simulation peak.

Throughout the simulation, substantial heterogeneity exists among individual households, with risk attitudes diverging considerably across the population. While the aggregate mean follows the described rise-and-fall trajectory, individual households exhibit risk attitudes ranging from highly risk-averse (negative values) to highly risk-seeking (approaching 0.8), reflecting the different adaptation trajectories of the three learning strategies operating under variable rainfall conditions. The standard deviation of 0.0936 around a mean of 0.2333 indicates persistent diversity in how different farmers adjust their uncertainty management over time.
