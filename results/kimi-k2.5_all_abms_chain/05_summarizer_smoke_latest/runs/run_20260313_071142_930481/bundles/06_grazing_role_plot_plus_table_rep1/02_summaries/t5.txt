Model focuses on stationary households each owning a specific patch of land. Each year, farmers observe their pasture condition and neighbors' situations to decide how many animals to keep. The model specifically investigates three distinct approaches to decision-making. The aggregate mean initial risk attitude among social learning households remains constant at 0.0074 across all 100 timesteps of the simulation period. This metric exhibits complete temporal stability, with identical start and end values, zero variance, and no statistically significant trends detected in any examined time window. The progressive slowing of growth rates suggests convergence toward a higher equilibrium carrying capacity under adapted management regimes.
