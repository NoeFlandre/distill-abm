 This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . It is designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems . The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes .  The study found that E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RBD types increased their risk-setters' risk levels over the course of the study . The study also found that the population has converged toward a steady-state level of uncertainty management, where the learning-by-doing and social learning processes have increased .  The average number of livestock destocked begins at zero, then surges to a maximum of approximately 6.6 animals per household by the third time step . This pattern—characterized by rapid initial destocking, a protracted trough near step 37, and gradual recovery mediated by adaptive learning behaviors—reflects the capacity of heterogeneous learning agents .  The metric reaches its maximum value of approximately 32 households by step 63, after which it stabilizes for the remainder of the simulation, concluding at roughly 32 households at step 100 . This plateau suggests that under the current configuration, with strategy switching disabled, the population achieves an equilibrium where approximately 53% of the 60 total households maintain active social learning engagement .
