The rapid recovery to 32.8% by step 350 demonstrates the effectiveness of the three-tick regrowth parameter, allowing vegetation to rebound quickly once grazing pressure is alleviated.

Subsequent dynamics exhibit regular cyclical fluctuations superimposed on a gradually stabilizing baseline. At approximately step 700, a secondary vegetation crash occurs, though with reduced severity (dropping to 29-31%), suggesting the herbivore population has adjusted to available resources or spatial heterogeneity has developed. Similar oscillations appear at steps 1100, 1450, 1800, and 2200, with each successive decline showing diminished amplitude and faster recovery times.

Between these periodic crashes, vegetation coverage maintains relatively stable plateaus at approximately 33%, representing the dynamic equilibrium where consumption rates by the ungulate population balance with the regrowth potential of the grassland system. The variance across simulation runs is highest during the first major crash at step 300, where trajectories diverge significantly, but convergence increases over time as the system settles into predictable seasonal patterns governed by the strong seasonality parameter (0.8).

By the conclusion of the simulation period around step 2200, the vegetation coverage stabilizes near 33% with only minor perturbations, indicating that the coupled herbivore-vegetation system has achieved a resilient oscillatory state where seasonal depressions in grass availability are regularly compensated by rapid regrowth within the spatial constraints of the initial vegetation pattern.
