**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The simulation demonstrates that current parameter configurations generate unsustainable boom-bust dynamics rather than stable coexistence, with population viability limited to approximately five to six years before reproductive collapse. This indicates that management strategies must either reduce hunter density, expand grassland regeneration capacity, or buffer seasonal resource fluctuations to prevent megaherbivore extinction. The two-year lag between initialization and reproductive onset (evident at tick 725) provides a critical intervention window before irreversible recruitment failure begins at year five.
