**Implications for Decision Makers**

Decision makers should recognize that male megaherbivore populations face disproportionate extinction risk under moderate hunting pressure when males offer even modestly higher energetic returns than females, with collapse occurring rapidly within the first year and ultimate extinction by year six. Conservation interventions must prioritize sex-specific protection protocols or spatial refugia during the critical initial 350-tick window when energy depletion rates are highest, as the population spends the majority of its existence below 25% of initial energy capacity. While temporary recoveries observed in years 4 and 5 suggest that seasonal protection measures could briefly bolster populations, such strategies only delay rather than prevent ultimate collapse without concurrent reduction in hunting pressure bias toward males.
