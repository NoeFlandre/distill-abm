**Implications for Decision Makers**

The multi-decade diffusion timeline indicates that promoting sustainable grazing practices through social learning requires long-term institutional commitment, as behavioral change spreads gradually through observation and experience rather than immediate adoption. The eventual plateau at roughly half the population suggests persistent heterogeneity in farmer decision-making—approximately 47% of households maintain non-social learning strategies despite decades of exposure to alternative approaches. Policy interventions should account for this resistant subset and recognize that social learning mechanisms, while effective at spreading knowledge, may not achieve universal adoption even after extended periods. The early rapid uptake (years 0–20) represents a critical window for reinforcing learning networks, while the subsequent slowing (years 20–63) suggests diminishing returns to passive diffusion, potentially requiring targeted engagement to reach remaining holdout households.
