The number of human foragers remains constant throughout the entire simulation period. The population of hunter agents is established at 20 individuals at initialization and persists without deviation through all 2,312 recorded time steps. No increases, decreases, or fluctuations in hunter population occur at any point during the simulation. This absolute stability results from the immortality condition applied to forager agents, which prevents mortality from energetic depletion, combined with the absence of hunter recruitment, reproduction, or immigration mechanisms in this model configuration. Consequently, the human hunting pressure operates as a fixed constant rather than a dynamic variable that would respond to resource availability, hunting success, or demographic processes.
