The cumulative destocking of livestock exhibits a monotonic increase throughout the 100-year simulation period, beginning at 0 animals and rising to approximately 97 animals by the final time step, with a series mean of roughly 75 animals and median of 81 animals. This trajectory reflects a strongly concave pattern characterized by diminishing rates of accumulation over time.

The initial phase (approximately time steps 0-25) demonstrates the most rapid accumulation, with an initial slope averaging nearly 5 animals per time step in the first decade, suggesting intensive destocking activity during the early establishment period. By approximately time step 10, cumulative destocking reaches roughly 50 animals, indicating significant initial adjustment to the stationary, private-property grazing context. This early phase coincides with the first detected change point around time step 25, marking a transition from rapid to more moderated destocking rates.

During the middle phase (approximately time steps 25-60), the rate of destocking accumulation slows considerably to roughly 0.3-0.6 animals per time step. This period contains multiple inflection points between time steps 45-57, indicating subtle shifts in the pace of destocking as the three behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) interact and adapt. By time step 60, the second change point occurs, with cumulative destocking reaching approximately 85-90 animals.

The late phase (time steps 60-100) maintains a consistently low but positive accumulation rate of approximately 0.3 animals per time step, approaching an asymptotic pattern as the system stabilizes. All rolling trend windows show statistically significant increasing trends (p<0.001), confirming the persistent though decelerating growth in cumulative destocking throughout the entire simulation.

Superimposed on this long-term trend is an oscillatory pattern with a dominant periodicity of approximately 49 years, visible across multiple time intervals including oscillation indices clustered around time steps 22-26, 49-61, and 79-80. This cyclical component likely reflects destocking responses to rainfall variability (with mean precipitation of 200 and standard deviation of 100) and corresponding vegetation dynamics.

In the context of the smallholder private-property system with heterogeneous risk attitudes (r-parameter initially set to 0.5), the declining rate of destocking accumulation suggests that learning processes—particularly learning-by-doing and social learning among the mixed population of 60 households—contribute to improved anticipatory management over time. The substantial cumulative destocking (exceeding the initial herd size of 90 animals per household when accounting for the entire simulation) indicates repeated destocking events driven by ecological pressures, yet the flattening curve implies progressive adaptive capacity in managing climatic uncertainty without addressing whether this represents optimal or suboptimal system performance.
