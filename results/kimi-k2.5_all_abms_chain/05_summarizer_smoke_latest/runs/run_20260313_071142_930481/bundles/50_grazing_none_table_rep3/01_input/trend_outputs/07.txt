The distribution of healthy livestock across households begins at perfect equality, with the inequality metric effectively at zero, reflecting the homogeneous initial condition where all 60 households commence with identical livestock endowments of 90 units each. Over the century-long simulation horizon, inequality emerges through a persistent monotonic upward trajectory, culminating in a terminal value of approximately 0.073. This modest final level indicates that while disparities in livestock health develop over time, the system maintains relatively equitable distributional outcomes despite heterogeneous agent behaviors and environmental variability.

The accumulation of inequality proceeds through distinct temporal phases characterized by shifts in growth rates. During the initial establishment period spanning approximately the first 25 timesteps, the system undergoes rapid structural differentiation, with inflection points occurring at timesteps 8, 13, 15, 20, and 25 marking successive transitions in the velocity of divergence. These early adjustments reflect the initial differentiation between the three fixed behavioral cohorts—baseline expectation-based rotation (E-RO), learning-by-doing (E-LBD), and social learning (E-RO-SL1)—as their distinct approaches to uncertainty management and decision-making under private property constraints begin generating differential livestock health outcomes.

Between timesteps 25 and 57, inequality continues mounting through a prolonged phase of steady accumulation, punctuated by an additional inflection at timestep 37. This middle period corresponds to the operational maturation of learning processes, where households with E-RO-SL1 capabilities update their risk parameters through social observation within a knowledge radius of one patch, while E-LBD agents refine decisions through individual experience and E-RO agents maintain static strategies. A significant structural break occurs at timestep 51, where the slope of inequality growth steepens perceptibly, transitioning from negligible incremental gains to a sustained increase of approximately 0.001 per timestep.

The final phase from timestep 57 onward exhibits continued divergence interrupted by cyclical dynamics. Following a second major transition at timestep 71, the trajectory enters an oscillatory regime with a dominant periodicity of approximately 49 timesteps, manifesting prominently between timesteps 78 and 93. These fluctuations likely emerge from feedback interactions between climatic variability—with rainfall exhibiting substantial standard deviation around the mean—and the adaptive grazing decisions of heterogeneous agents managing stationary private pastures. Throughout this late period, inequality approaches its maximum value of 0.0726 at timestep 100, having maintained consistent upward momentum across all analytical windows despite the superimposed oscillations.

The modest magnitude of terminal inequality, combined with the absence of extreme divergence, suggests that the fixed behavioral heterogeneity and adaptive learning mechanisms operate within constraints imposed by private property institutions and environmental uncertainty, preventing the emergence of severe stratification in household livestock wealth while permitting the gradual differentiation of outcomes across the mixed population of learning agents.
