 This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . It is designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems . The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes .  The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock begins at approximately 0.007 . Each household maintains its specific initial value without temporal variation throughout the simulation period . This constancy reflects the intrinsic nature of initial risk disposition as a fixed baseline attribute . The average available green biomass across all patches initiates at approximately 45,000 units .  The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock begins at effectively zero, indicating perfect equality in livestock health distribution across all 60 households . The recovery trajectory demonstrates that learning capabilities enable some households to rebuild herds beyond the minimum crisis level . The engagement of households in social learning initiates at zero and increases monotonically throughout the simulation period .  The average cumulative number of destocked livestock per household initiates at zero and increases monotonically throughout the 100-step simulation . Social learning diffuses to about half the households on average, though stochastic variation leads to divergent outcomes across runs ranging from 30% to 90% adoption rates .
