Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. Individual household initial risk attitudes are heterogeneous and distributed across a range spanning approximately -0.11 to 0.18. Each household maintains its specific initial value without temporal variation throughout the simulation period. This constancy reflects the intrinsic nature of initial risk disposition as a fixed baseline attribute established at the start of the simulation. Over the first 37 time steps, this value declines rapidly and significantly, reaching a minimum of approximately 41 animals. The average livestock holdings per household, including destocked animals, begins at approximately 90 animals,. This decrease reflects the adjustment period as households adapt to private property constraints and ecological limits. The maximum average value of 32.07. Across the population of 60 households in the private property grazing system, final engagement levels vary considerably among simulation runs, ranging from approximately 18 to 54 households. Step-wise nature of individual adoption trajectories suggests discrete behavioral switching events. A subset of E-RO households persists, likely representing those with favorable initial conditions or risk parameter combinations that buffer them against the selective pressure favoring adaptive learning behaviors.
