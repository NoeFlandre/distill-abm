The trajectory reveals a non-linear adoption process characterized by distinct phases: explosive early growth driven by individual perception shifts, a prolonged middle period of social negotiation and habit competition, and renewed late-stage diffusion as network effects consolidate the transition. For decision makers, this pattern suggests that public health interventions may face initial rapid uptake followed by unexpected resistance periods as social norms stabilize, requiring sustained engagement strategies rather than single-phase campaigns to achieve complete market transformation. The oscillatory nature of the middle phase indicates that consumption behaviors may temporarily regress before advancing, implying that policymakers should anticipate cyclical feedback rather than linear progress when designing long-term dietary transition programs.
