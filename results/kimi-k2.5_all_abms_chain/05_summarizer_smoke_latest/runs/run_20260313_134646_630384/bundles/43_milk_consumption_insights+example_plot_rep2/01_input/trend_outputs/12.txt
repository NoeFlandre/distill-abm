The mean value environmental deviation initiates at approximately zero, indicating that agents begin with milk choices fully aligned with their held environmental values. Over the initial two to three steps, the deviation undergoes a rapid escalation, surging to roughly 10 to 12 units. This swift increase suggests that as agents engage in social interactions and activate disposition mechanisms within the Watts-Strogatz network, habitual tendencies and social influences quickly decouple consumption decisions from pure environmental valuations.

Between steps five and ten, the rate of increase decelerates substantially, with the metric approaching and settling at approximately 13 to 13.5 units. Beyond step ten, the deviation stabilizes into a consistent plateau, maintaining values around 13.3 units with minimal fluctuation through the remainder of the simulation period. This stabilization indicates that the system reaches a dynamic equilibrium where the reinforcing effects of incumbent habits (initially set to maximum strength), social conformity pressures, and cognitive dissonance thresholds balance against agents' intrinsic environmental preferences.

The trajectory exhibits remarkably low variance across the repeated simulation runs, with all instances following nearly identical paths. This high consistency suggests that the emergence of substantial environmental value deviation is an intrinsic and robust property of the model dynamics under the specified parameterization, particularly given the high initial incumbent habit strength and moderate social susceptibility settings.

Implications for decision makers indicate that habitual consumption patterns and network effects rapidly override environmental value considerations in driving milk choice behavior, with the system locking into a stable misalignment within the first ten temporal steps. Policy interventions aiming to shift consumption toward environmentally preferable options should prioritize disrupting established habits and leveraging social influence mechanisms rather than relying solely on environmental value appeals, as the window for effective intervention appears to be concentrated in the initial phases of behavioral transition before the deviation stabilizes.
