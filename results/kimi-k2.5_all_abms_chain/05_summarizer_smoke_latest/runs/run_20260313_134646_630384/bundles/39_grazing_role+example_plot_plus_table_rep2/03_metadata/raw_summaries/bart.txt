 The agent-based model represents smallholder farmers managing private pastures in a grazing system . Each year, farmers observe their pasture condition and neighbors' situations to decide how many animals to keep . The model explores how different learning strategies affect long-term outcomes for both the land and the farmers' livelihoods .  The population-level baseline risk attitude does not drift or fluctuate over time . Initial risk dispositions are static reference points for how farmers perceive uncertainty when evaluating pasture conditions . The mean reserve biomass begins at approximately 90,000 units and undergoes a statistically significant decline over the first 26 steps .  The mean biomass across all observations averages 51,530 units, with a median of 51,197 units . The trajectory exhibits an oscillatory pattern with a dominant period of 49 steps, suggesting cyclical adjustments in the human-vegetation system . The mean number of livestock plus destocked animals per household per household begins at 90 animals at step 0 .  The number of households adopting social learning undergoes a rapid increase over the first approximately 40 time steps, though the rate of adoption decelerates progressively . By year 25, the cumulative destocking reaches approximately 40–50 animals on average, representing the initial correction from the starting herd size of 90 animals to sustainable stocking levels .  The count of non-learning households (E-RO behavioral type) begins at 20 at step 0 . During the initial 8 steps, the metric rises slightly to a maximum of approximately 21.1 households . A sustained decline commences around step 8 and continues with significant downward trends through approximately step 65 . The most rapid decrease occurs between steps 11 and 21, after which the rate of attrition moderates but remains significant .
