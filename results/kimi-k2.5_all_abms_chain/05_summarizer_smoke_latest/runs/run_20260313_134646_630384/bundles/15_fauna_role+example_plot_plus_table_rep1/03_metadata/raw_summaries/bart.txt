 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change . Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances . Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores .  The number of active human foragers remains constant at 20 individuals throughout the entire duration of the simulation . The total energy of foragers begins at 2000 units and undergoes a continuous, steep decline throughout the simulation, reaching approximately -38229 units by step 2312 . The population maintains its initial state of 20 throughout all 2,312 time steps .  The trajectory exhibits a pronounced saturation curve characterized by three distinct phases of accumulation . By step 1000, cumulative mortality reaches roughly 100 to 110 individuals . The terminal plateau phase (beyond step 1200) demonstrates stabilization with no significant increasing trend . This deceleration corresponds to density-dependent prey depletion, where dwindling male populations increase search costs for hunters .  The cumulative number of prey deaths initiates at zero and follows a sigmoidal trajectory, ultimately converging to approximately 235 deaths by the conclusion of the simulation period . The trajectory exhibits three distinct phases: an initial exponential-like increase, a subsequent deceleration in mortality accumulation, and a final asymptotic plateau .  The number of male animals initiates at 125 individuals and undergoes a precipitous decline throughout the simulation, ultimately reaching extinction at step 2284 . The trajectory exhibits high variability across the 40 simulation runs, with a mean population size of 21.03 males and a standard deviation of 36.04 .  The terminal phase from step 1,000 to 2,310 witnesses the final extinction of remaining lineages . The terminal value of zero across all 40 replicates indicates the megaherbivore population lacks a viable refugium from which to recover . The population undergoes a rapid collapse during the first 475 steps .  The distribution of energy values exhibits high positive skew, with a mean of 1,984 and a median of 135 . For the majority of the simulation duration, male animal energy reserves persist at minimal levels, with only occasional temporary recoveries or slower decline periods in certain runs maintaining higher values for extended durations .  This represents the nadir of the first seasonal cycle under high seasonality conditions (0.8), where the combined pressure of the initial herd population (250 animals) drives fodder availability to its lowest point . Individual simulation runs exhibit even more extreme reductions, with some trajectories plunging to approximately 23% vegetation cover during this critical bottleneck .
