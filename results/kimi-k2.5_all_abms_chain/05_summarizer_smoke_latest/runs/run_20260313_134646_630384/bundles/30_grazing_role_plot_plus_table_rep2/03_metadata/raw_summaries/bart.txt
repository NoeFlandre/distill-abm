 The agent-based model represents smallholder farmers managing private pastures in a grazing system . Each year, farmers observe their pasture condition and neighbors' situations to decide how many animals to keep . The model explores how different learning strategies affect long-term outcomes for both the land and the farmers' livelihoods .  The aggregate mean household risk attitude, representing the average deviation from recommended livestock numbers across all simulation runs, initiates at nearly neutral levels (0.0074) at timesteps 0–33 . Over the first third of the simulation, the metric undergoes a sharp, statistically significant escalation (Mann-Kendall slope=0.016–0.018, p<0.01), culminating in a maximum deviation of 0.3814 .
