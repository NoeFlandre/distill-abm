Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances. Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores. The population of active human hunter-gatherer bands remained invariant at exactly 20 agents throughout the entire 200-year simulation horizon. The aggregate energy dynamics of the forager population exhibit a pronounced monotonic decline over the simulation duration. The decline occurs in distinct phases characterized by varying rates of energetic loss. The cumulative mortality of female megaherbivores exhibits a characteristic saturation curve with distinct temporal phases reflecting the interaction between forager efficiency and prey population dynamics. The trajectory achieves a maximum value of approximately 120.17 male fatalities near tick 1876, after which the cumulative count exhibits a significant declining trend through tick 2087. The cumulative mortality of postpartum female ungulates (females accompanied by offspring) initiates at zero and undergoes distinct phased dynamics over the 2,312-tick simulation horizon. The median cumulative kill (119.06) exceeds the mean (104.56), indicating a left-skewed distribution across runs where most simulations accumulated kills rapidly toward the upper asymptote. The cumulative mortality trajectory exhibits a complex four-phase pattern characterized by initial rapid accumulation, intermittent stabilization, terminal peaking, and ultimate decline. During the initial collapse (ticks 0–305), the population declined precipitously from 125 females, passing an inflection point at tick 305. The population exhibited a strongly right-skewed distribution with a mean of approximately 16.5 females but a median of 1. The simulated megaherbivore population initiates at a mean abundance of approximately 167 individuals. The trajectory exhibits monotonic decline punctuated by seasonal oscillations, ultimately terminating in extinction at approximately 6.3 years (tick 2311) The population reached zero females at tick 2311, immediately following a change point. The median energy value across the entire series (134.5 units) falls substantially below the mean (1,984 units), indicating a profoundly right-skewed distribution dominated by high initial biomass with prolonged low-energy conditions preceding extinction. By tick 2310, the final transition to zero occurs, with energy diminishing from residual levels to complete absence.
