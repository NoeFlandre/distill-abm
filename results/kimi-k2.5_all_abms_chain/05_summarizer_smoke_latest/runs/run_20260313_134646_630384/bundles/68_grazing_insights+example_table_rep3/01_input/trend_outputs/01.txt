The average initial risk attitude of social learning agents maintains a constant value of 0.0074 across all 100 time steps of the simulation, from initial setup through the final period. This reflects the static nature of initial risk parameter assignment to E-RO-SL1 behavioral types, which remains unchanged throughout the simulation as households engage in decision-making and learning processes.

For researchers interpreting these results, this stability indicates that the baseline disposition toward deviating from recommended livestock levels is fixed for social learning agents, meaning observed variations in pasture conditions, herd sizes, and household resilience must be attributed to the dynamic learning processes and behavioral adaptations rather than changes in initial risk attitudes.
