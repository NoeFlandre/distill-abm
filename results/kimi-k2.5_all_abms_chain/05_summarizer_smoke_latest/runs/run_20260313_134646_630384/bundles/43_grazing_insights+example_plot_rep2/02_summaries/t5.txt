Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. Positive values indicate initial tendencies to exceed recommended livestock numbers. Negative values reflect conservative initial dispositions favoring livestock levels below recommendations. Trajectories maintain high risk attitudes around 0.8 or plateau at moderate levels of 0.4 to 0.7. Trajectories remain largely synchronized during initial decline and early recovery phases. However, during the later recovery and stabilization phases, substantial heterogeneity emerges across runs. Final equilibrium values varying by as much as 17,000 units between the lowest and highest outcomes. The metric declines steadily from step 5 through approximately step 40. By step 60 and continuing through step 100, the values plateau at levels ranging from near zero to approximately 1.0. This divergence in long-term outcomes indicates strong path dependency in the system. Large variance across runs suggests that stochastic elements in rainfall patterns, initial household configurations, and neighborhood interaction dynamics create divergent pathways for social learning diffusion. In some runs, less than one-third of households engage in social learning while in others, nearly all households adopt these practices. The persistence of low-adoption equilibria in some simulations indicates that certain households may remain isolated from social learning networks. Between time steps 40 and 60, most trajectories transition from rapid change to quasi-stable oscillations around distinct equilibrium levels. By time step 100, the final counts cluster into several distinct groups. One particular run reaches complete extinction of the E-RO strategy by step 65, maintaining zero non-learning households through the remainder of the simulation.
