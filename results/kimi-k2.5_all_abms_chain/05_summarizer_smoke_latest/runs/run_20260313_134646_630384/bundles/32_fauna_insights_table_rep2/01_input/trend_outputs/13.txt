**Insights for Decision Makers**

The trajectory indicates that moderate hunter densities (20 agents) with immortal foraging populations and high environmental seasonality (0.8) generate relentless predation pressure that drives megaherbivore populations through a predictable sequence of rapid initial collapse, followed by prolonged decline punctuated by temporary, non-sustainable demographic recoveries. The six-year timeline to extinction provides multiple potential intervention points, particularly during the stagnation and weak recovery phases observed between years 3.2 and 4.5 (ticks 1,160–1,620), where population declines temporarily decelerate.
