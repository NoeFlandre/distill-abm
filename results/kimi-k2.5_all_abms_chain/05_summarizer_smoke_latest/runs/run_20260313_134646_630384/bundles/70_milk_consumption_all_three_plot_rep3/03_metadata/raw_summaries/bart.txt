 The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . It captures how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts . Success is measured by the model's ability to reproduce the historical crossover point where skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption surpassed whole milk consumption .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed milk per agent begins at approximately 5 units at step 0, consistent with initial conditions reflecting 1974 market dominance of whole milk . During the first 11 time steps, consumption increases rapidly following an approximately sigmoidal pattern, accelerating most sharply between steps 5 and 10 . By step 11, values reach between 1250 and 1280 units across simulation runs, representing the culmination of initial adoption driven by evolving health perceptions and social network influences .  The cumulative count of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk selections across the consumer population begins at approximately zero and follows a trajectory characterized by initial acceleration followed by sustained linear growth throughout the 32 time-step simulation period . By the conclusion of the simulation period corresponding to 2005, alternative milk varieties account for roughly 80% of total consumption decisions .  The mean quantity of whole milk consumed initiates at approximately 2,650 units at time step zero . Means of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed begins at approximately zero units . Consumption increases rapidly in a convex pattern, accelerating through the middle phase and reaching values between 1,200 and 1,280 units .  The mean habit factor applied to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choice functions begins at 1.0 and follows a distinct three-phase trajectory over the 32 time steps . This consistency suggests that the macro-level market transition between whole milk and skimmed varieties is highly deterministic given the specified parameters .  The mean deviation between agents' environmental values and their milk choices begins at approximately zero at the outset of the simulation period . The growth rate decelerates considerably between steps 5 and 10, with the metric climbing more gradually to approximately 13.5 . The curve does not plateau entirely, suggesting that the social influence mechanisms and information processing continue to drive marginal increases in population .
