The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005. It captures how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts. Each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers in their network. The multi-phase nature of the decline suggests that intervention strategies must be temporally adaptive. The non-linear trajectory suggests that market projections assuming constant rates of change will significantly underestimate consumption in early periods and overestimate it in middle periods. Policy makers should focus on infrastructure support and availability of alternatives while managing the social isolation effects on remaining traditional consumers. The cumulative count of whole milk selections exhibits a linear growth pattern across the 32 time steps of the simulation. With a population of 1000 agents, the observed rate suggests that approximately 6 to 7.5 percent of consumers select whole milk at each decision point. The stability of this rate persists despite the presence of social influence mechanisms, habit formation dynamics, and alternative milk options. The simulation reveals a pronounced non-linear decline in mean whole milk consumption over the 32 time steps. Consumption falls from approximately 2,650–2,700 units at initialization to roughly 200–250 units by the conclusion. Decision makers should note the absence of engagement decay or intermittent participation patterns suggests an idealized model environment where habitual consumption triggers continuous evaluation cycles. The mean habit factor for whole milk exhibits a monotonic upward trajectory across the 32 time steps, beginning at a baseline value of 1.00 and terminating at values ranging between approximately 1.043 and 1.057 by the final time step. The trajectory suggests that market transitions driven by habit-dependent consumer behavior exhibit punctuated equilibrium dynamics rather than smooth logistic growth.
