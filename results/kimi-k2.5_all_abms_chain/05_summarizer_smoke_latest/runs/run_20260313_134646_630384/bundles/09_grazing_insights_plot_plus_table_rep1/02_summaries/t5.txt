Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. The variance and standard deviation are zero, indicating complete temporal stability with no oscillation or drift. Initial risk attitudes function as static baseline parameters rather than dynamic variables. The near-zero mean value suggests that, on average, households begin simulations with a neutral disposition toward risk. The subsequent correction phase (steps 30-60) represents a critical window where households adjust their strategies based on observed outcomes. The long-term divergence into distinct risk-attitude clusters implies that uniform policy interventions become ineffective after step 60; instead, differentiated approaches are required to address heterogeneous risk profiles that crystallize. Outcomes converge toward values ranging between approximately 55,000 and 64,000 units by the terminal step. Initial decline reflects impact of high initial livestock densities (90 animals per household across 60 stationary households) on available vegetation. Subsequent recovery demonstrates resilience of the vegetation sub-model. A spike immediately transitions into a steep decline characterized by significant oscillations. The metric drops to roughly 3.3 units by step 8 and continues to decrease through step 30. Step 30 constitutes a critical change point where dynamics shift from active decline to relative stabilization. Inequality trajectories appear to become increasingly entrenched and difficult to reverse. The count of households engaged in social learning exhibits an initial phase of rapid diffusion followed by deceleration and eventual stabilization. Between steps 40 and 63, the system approaches saturation. The count of households following the E-RO (non-learning baseline) strategy exhibits a three-phase temporal pattern over the 100-step simulation period. The population begins at 20 households and increases slightly during the first 8 steps, reaching a maximum value of 21.1 households. This early growth phase is followed by a prolonged decline spanning approximately steps 9 through 76.
