**Implications for Decision Makers:**

The gradual abandonment of non-learning baseline strategies in favor of learning-based approaches (E-LBD and E-RO-SL1) suggests that adaptive capacity emerges endogenously in private property grazing systems when learning mechanisms are available. Instead, targeted support recognizing the coexistence of multiple stable strategies—where approximately one-quarter of non-learning households eventually adopt learning behaviors while three-quarters persist with traditional approaches—may better serve diverse farming communities. The stabilization after approximately 75 time steps suggests that strategy adoption reaches equilibrium within the simulation timeframe, indicating that longitudinal studies or interventions should plan for multi-decadal horizons to observe full adaptive dynamics.
