the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period of 2,312 time steps . the total energy available to all forager bands initiates at 2000 and declines continuously throughout the simulation . the trend slope drops to approximately 0.023 per step in the 696-927 window and diminishes to 0.006 per step by steps 928-1159 . this deceleration suggests diminishing returns as female herd densities decline . the system enters a saturation phase where cumulative mortality asymptotically approaches its maximum . cumulative births ranging from zero to approximately ten individuals by the end of the observation period . this spread suggests divergent demographic outcomes stemming from stochastic variation in individual herd survival, mating opportunities, and environmental conditions . the average trend across forty simulation runs exhibits a general increase over time . the population of female animals initiates at 125 individuals and follows a deterministic trajectory toward extinction across all simulation iterations . the population subsequently resumes its decline toward zero individuals, with final extinction occurring universally by step 2310 . the population reaches complete extinction at step 2,311, with the aggregate mean converging to zero . this protracted persistence at low densities followed by final collapse indicates that terminal extinction involves stochastic processes operating when populations fall below critical viability thresholds . the combined total energy available in all prey animals begins at approximately 16,667 units . it undergoes a precipitous decline over the first 500 time steps . the most rapid decrease occurs between steps 232 and 463, where the slope reaches -36.0 units per step .
