the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human foragers begins at 20 and remains fixed at this value throughout the entire simulation, extending from step 0 to approximately step 2,300 without any observable decline or increase . the total energy available to all forager bands begins at approximately 0 at step 0, then declines continuously throughout the simulation . a subset of runs exhibits markedly delayed accumulation patterns . the final values demonstrate relatively tight convergence toward the 115-125 range . this pattern indicates an initial phase of intense hunting pressure exploiting the initial female herd population . the cumulative death count stabilizes at plateau values ranging from approximately 225 to 250 across different simulation runs . this plateau indicates that mortality events have effectively ceased or slowed to negligible rates, consistent with the prey population having been driven to extinction or reduced to a minimal residual population where births balance deaths . between steps 500 and 1000, the remaining populations continue to decline erratically, with trajectories diverging considerably across runs . some populations stabilize temporarily at low levels (between 10 and 40 animals) for several hundred steps, likely reflecting periods where reduced herd density lowers hunting encounter rates or demographic stochasticity in reproduction temporarily offset mortality . the total energy available in all prey animals begins at approximately 25,000 energy units across simulation runs . this initial value
