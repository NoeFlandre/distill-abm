The proportion of the landscape carrying vegetation initiates at approximately 33 percent, constrained by the initialization parameters that restrict vegetation to one-third of the grid patches. Throughout the simulation duration, this metric demonstrates exceptional stability, concluding at essentially the identical value of roughly 33 percent, with a long-term mean of approximately 32.7 percent and minimal variance (standard deviation under 1 percent).

A pronounced annual cycle manifests, evidenced by an absolute minimum of approximately 24.9 percent occurring precisely at the 365th time step, coinciding with the completion of the first simulated year. This seasonal nadir reflects the strong environmental seasonality (0.8 intensity) creating periodic resource bottlenecks, where grass availability contracts significantly before recovering. The timing suggests this represents the annual period of minimal productivity within the environmental cycle.

Superimposed upon these annual fluctuations is a regular oscillatory pattern with a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps, indicating sub-seasonal pulses in vegetation availability. These high-frequency cycles likely emerge from the interaction between rapid vegetation regrowth (every 3 ticks) and continuous grazing pressure from the herbivore population, creating localized waves of depletion and recovery across the landscape.

Trend analysis reveals distinct phases in vegetation dynamics. Early periods (approximately the first 1,600 ticks) exhibit slight positive trends, potentially representing an initial equilibration phase where herbivore grazing patterns distribute across the landscape and localized depletion patches recover. Later periods (beyond tick 1,624) show weak negative trends, suggesting a gradual long-term adjustment, though the magnitude of change remains negligible relative to the overall mean. Fifteen inflection points distributed throughout the record indicate frequent reversals between local expansion and contraction phases, consistent with a dynamically stable system where consumption and regrowth remain tightly coupled.

The maintenance of vegetation coverage within a narrow band (rarely deviating more than 7 percentage points from the mean despite strong seasonality) indicates that the rapid regrowth capacity and restricted spatial domain (confined to initially vegetated patches) successfully buffer the vegetation base against the foraging pressure exerted by the initial cohort of 250 ungulates and 20 human hunters.
