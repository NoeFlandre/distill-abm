 This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . It is designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems . The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes .  The mean reserve biomass initially registers approximately 90,000 units at step 0, representing the starting ecological condition across private pasture patches . During the first 10-15 steps, the metric undergoes a pronounced decline, with values dropping to a minimum range between 80,000 and 88,000 across different simulation runs . The model produces a wide distribution of stocking strategies, with some simulation runs converging to severe overstocking, others to significant understocking .  The U-shaped trajectory reflects the interplay between initial grazing pressure and gradual effectiveness of learning mechanisms in the private property system . During the first 5 steps, the system experiences a sharp transient spike where mean destocking rapidly increases from 0 to peak values between approximately 6.0 and 7.5 livestock per household . During this period, mean green biomass rises from minimum values to between 55,000 and 60,000 .  The long-term convergence to low destocking levels indicates successful adaptation to private property grazing constraints, though the non-zero floor in some runs reflects ongoing climatic risk that cannot be fully eliminated through behavioral adaptation alone . The data exhibit high variability in cumulative destocking outcomes across the 100-step simulation horizon .  The non-learning E-RO strategy experiences path-dependent outcomes . The strategy can achieve temporary dominance with counts exceeding 35-40 households . The decline from transient peaks of 40+ to stable levels of 25-27 in high-performing runs indicates competitive pressure from adaptive strategies or differential household mortality favoring non-E-RO types .
