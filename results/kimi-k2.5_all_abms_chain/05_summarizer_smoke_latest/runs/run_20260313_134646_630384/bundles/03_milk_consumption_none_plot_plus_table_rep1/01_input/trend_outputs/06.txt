The agent-based model simulating British milk consumption adoption from 1974 to 2005 generates time-series data showing mean whole milk quantities per agent declining from approximately 2654.8 units at initialization to roughly 253.1 units by the terminal period. This trajectory aligns with the historical transition from whole milk toward skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties, with the aggregate metric averaging 1078.2 across all observations (median: 816.9).

The temporal dynamics reveal distinct phases of behavioral change. During early steps (0–9), the system exhibits steep, statistically significant decreases (p = 0.027), with slopes reaching -221.11 between steps 5–9 and -115.85 between steps 0–4, reflecting rapid initial shifts in consumer preferences and habit disruption. 

The middle period (steps 10–24) shows moderated decline rates (slope = -38.13 for steps 10–14) punctuated by temporary stabilization. Specifically, steps 15–19 demonstrate non-significant trends (p = 0.086) around a local valley at step 18 (approximately 760.0) and subsequent relative peak at step 19 (approximately 774.0), suggesting resistance to further reduction or temporary reversion toward whole milk consumption within the network.

Later steps (20–24) resume significant decreases (slope = -17.45, p = 0.027), transitioning into a final phase (steps 25–29) marked by non-significant trends (p = 0.086) despite continued numerical decline toward terminal values. Local extrema occur at step 26 (valley: ~594.4), step 27 (peak: ~621.0), and step 29 (valley: ~323.5).

Thirty independent simulation runs demonstrate consistent monotonic decline patterns with converging variance, supporting robustness in the modeled social influence and habit formation mechanisms. Inflection points at steps 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 12, 13, 14, 19, 24, 25, 27, and 30 indicate structural transitions in the rate of consumption change, corresponding to calibration parameters governing cognitive dissonance thresholds and susceptibility weightings within the Watts-Strogatz network topology.
