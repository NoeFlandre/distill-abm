The simulation of milk adoption behaviors among British consumers from 1974 to 2005 reveals a three-phase trajectory in the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk varieties. The process begins at step 0 with minimal alternative milk consumption at approximately 5.3 units per person weekly, reflecting the historical market dominance of whole milk in 1974. The mean habit factor for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk begins at 1.0000 and exhibits a trajectory characterized by initial stability followed by sustained growth. This early period shows no statistically significant trend. The most rapid acceleration occurs between steps 10 and 14, achieving a maximum slope of 0.030 units per step. The first regime (steps 0–10) demonstrates rapid acceleration, with the deviation increasing at a rate of 0.063 per step during steps 0-4, reaching approximately 0.25 by step 4, followed by a continued strong growth rate of 0.41 per step through step 9.
