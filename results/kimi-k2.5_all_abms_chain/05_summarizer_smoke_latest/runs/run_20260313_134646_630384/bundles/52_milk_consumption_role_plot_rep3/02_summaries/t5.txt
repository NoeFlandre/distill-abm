Model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005. Individual agents represent adult consumers who begin with preferences reflective of 1974 market conditions. Each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers. Consumption exhibits a steep negative gradient, falling rapidly from baseline levels to approximately 950–1,050 units by step 10.
