The rapid recovery to baseline levels—facilitated by the short regrowth-time of 3 ticks—demonstrates the high resilience built into the grassland system. **Implications for Decision Makers**

The data indicate that the system possesses strong resilience mechanisms, with vegetation recovering rapidly (within 100 steps) from severe depletion events, suggesting that short-term environmental shocks or overgrazing events do not lead to irreversible degradation under current regrowth parameters. However, the initial 300-step period carries the highest risk of resource depletion and potential starvation events for herbivores, indicating that early-phase dynamics require careful monitoring in management applications. The configuration successfully maintains target vegetation levels (~33%) after the initial transient phase, suggesting that the combination of fast grass regrowth (3 ticks), moderate hunting pressure (20 foragers), and the specified herbivore life history constraints creates a sustainable equilibrium that prevents long-term overgrazing while supporting stable prey populations.
