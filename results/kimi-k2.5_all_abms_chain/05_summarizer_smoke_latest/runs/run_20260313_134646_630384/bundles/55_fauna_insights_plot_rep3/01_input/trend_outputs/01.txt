The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands maintains a constant value of 20 throughout the simulation sequence, from step 0 through approximately step 2200, with no observable variation across repeated runs. This temporal invariance stems directly from the model parameterization: hunters are immortal (preventing mortality from energy depletion), do not reproduce, and therefore maintain the initial population size of 20 bands indefinitely without response to environmental conditions or prey availability.

For decision makers: The static hunter population establishes a controlled, unidirectional predation pressure that remains decoupled from prey population dynamics. This configuration allows researchers to isolate the effects of constant hunting mortality rates on megaherbivore extinction probability, excluding feedback loops where prey scarcity might otherwise reduce hunter numbers. When designing experiments, this constancy ensures that any observed changes in extinction outcomes derive specifically from manipulated variables (such as environmental degradation or life history traits) rather than shifting predator-prey demographic coupling.
