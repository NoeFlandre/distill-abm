The total energy available to all forager bands begins at approximately 1,500 to 2,000 energy units, indicating a modest initial energy reserve distributed among the 20 hunters. Over the first 500 time steps, this value declines rapidly to approximately -5,000, with substantial variation across simulation runs during this interval. This initial divergence suggests stochastic differences in early hunting success as foragers encounter the initial population of 250 ungulates, with some bands obtaining more energy from successful kills while others expend movement costs without commensurate returns.

After approximately 500 time steps, the trajectories converge and follow a consistent linear decline. From step 500 through step 2,000, the total energy decreases at a constant rate of approximately 20 energy units per tick, reaching values between -32,000 and -38,000 by the end of the observation period. This linear pattern indicates that hunting success has effectively ceased, and the forager population is accumulating energy debt solely through movement costs. Given that foragers are immortal and do not perish from energy depletion, they continue to accrue negative energy indefinitely.

The transition from variable, rapid decline to uniform linear decrease around step 500 suggests that the ungulate prey population has been depleted below viable hunting thresholds. Once prey density drops sufficiently, foragers can no longer encounter and successfully process animals at rates that offset their energetic expenditures, resulting in the consistent accumulation of energy debt observed in the latter portion of the simulation.
