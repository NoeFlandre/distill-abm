model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . individual agents represent adult consumers who begin with preferences reflective of 1974 market conditions . each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers . over the first ten steps, consumption declines sharply to approximately 950–1000 units, representing a reduction of roughly 62–64% . this rapid decrease indicates that despite high habitual barriers, agents successfully accumulate sufficient motivation to break automatic consumption patterns . initial skepticism toward alternatives quickly erodes as agents process health and environmental information within their social networks . cumulative adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk follows a characteristic S-shaped trajectory across the 32-step simulation period . between steps 5 and 25, the adoption curve enters a phase of rapid acceleration, with cumulative choices increasing steeply from approximately 2,000 to nearly 20,000 . this period represents the mainstream diffusion of low-fat milk . the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents begins at approximately zero . over the first ten steps, consumption increases rapidly and non-linearly, reaching values between 1200 and 1280 units . this acceleration corresponds to the period where early adopter agents overcome the high initial habit strength associated with whole milk consumption . the trajectory exhibits remarkably low variance across simulation runs throughout the entire 32-step period . the overall pattern reflects a behavioral transition where alternative milk consumption evolves from a novel, deliberative choice into an increasingly automatic and entrenched behavior . this persistent plateau indicates that the system has reached a steady-state equilibrium where the population-level misalignment between environmental values and actual consumption behavior remains constant . the aggregate deviation remains fixed due to a dynamic balance between newly aware consumers entering states of high value-action dissonance .
