The irreversibility observed in trajectories dropping to zero implies that once non-learning strategies are abandoned below a threshold, recovery is unlikely without external intervention. The persistence of a substantial non-learning population in approximately one-third of simulations (stabilizing above 25) indicates that under certain conditions, the baseline rule-of-thumb approach remains viable, warranting careful monitoring of early warning indicators that predict which equilibrium path the system will follow.
