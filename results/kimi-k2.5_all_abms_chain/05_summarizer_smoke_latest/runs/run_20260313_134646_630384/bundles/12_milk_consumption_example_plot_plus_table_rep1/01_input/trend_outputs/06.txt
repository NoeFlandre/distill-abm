By step 10, values have fallen to approximately 1000 units, marking the first major inflection point where the rate of decline moderates.

In the intermediate phase between steps 10 and 19, the descent continues but at a reduced rate (window 3 slope -38.13), with the series exhibiting localized peaks and valleys. A brief plateau occurs around step 18-19, where consumption temporarily stabilizes near 760-774 units, suggesting a period of equilibrium where habit persistence and social influence create resistance to further immediate reduction.

The latter portion of the simulation (steps 20-32) demonstrates continued erosion of whole milk consumption but with increased volatility, including oscillations between 594 and 621 units around step 26-27, and between 323 and 335 units toward the conclusion. These fluctuations align with the dominant 15-step oscillation period identified in the series, likely corresponding to the cyclical nature of habit reassessment and cognitive dissonance resolution within the agent population. The final change point at step 32 confirms the establishment of a new steady state at minimal consumption levels, indicating that social norms and alternative preferences have largely displaced whole milk from the simulated dietary patterns.

The high standard deviation (693.08) relative to the mean (1078.20) reflects substantial heterogeneity in abandonment timing across the 30 simulation runs, consistent with the random positioning of agents within the Watts-Strogatz network and variation in individual habit thresholds and social susceptibility parameters. Despite this variance, the monotonic downward progression from the maximum value at inception to the minimum at termination captures the fundamental behavioral shift from incumbent whole milk consumption to alternative varieties driven by the interplay of health perceptions, environmental awareness, and social network effects.
