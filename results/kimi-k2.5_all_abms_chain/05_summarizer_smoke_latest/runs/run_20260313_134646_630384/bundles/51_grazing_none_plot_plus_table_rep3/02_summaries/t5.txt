Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. Individual household initial risk attitudes display heterogeneity across the population. Individual values are distributed across discrete values ranging from approximately -0.11 to 0.18. These individual values remain fixed at their initial assignments throughout the simulation duration.
