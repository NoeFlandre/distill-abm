The count of non-learning households initiates at 20 across all simulation runs. During the first 20 steps, trajectories diverge rapidly, exhibiting substantial variability in outcomes. Several runs demonstrate significant increases, with counts rising to between 30 and 42 households and reaching peak values approximately between steps 15 and 35. Conversely, other trajectories exhibit steep declines, with multiple runs falling below 5 households by step 20 and several approaching or reaching zero by step 40.  

A transitional phase occurs between steps 35 and 50, wherein trajectories that had maintained elevated counts undergo marked reductions, decreasing from highs above 35 down to levels between 23 and 28. Following step 60, the system stabilizes across all runs, though at distinctly different equilibrium levels. By step 100, the final count of non-learning households spans a broad range from 0 to approximately 26, with notable persistent clusters occurring near values of 0, 3, 8, 13, 16, 23, and 26.  

This pattern of divergence indicates strong path dependency in the dynamics of behavioral strategy adoption, suggesting that early stochastic variations determine whether households transition away from the baseline non-learning strategy toward adaptive learning behaviors, or whether the non-learning strategy persists and temporarily dominates the population before partially declining to stable intermediate levels.
