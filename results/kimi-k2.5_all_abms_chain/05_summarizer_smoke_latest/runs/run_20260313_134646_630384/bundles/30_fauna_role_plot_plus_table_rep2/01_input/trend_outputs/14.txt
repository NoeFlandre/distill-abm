Vegetation cover initiates at 33.0% of the landscape, averaged across 40 replicate simulations, and remains statistically stable over the long term, concluding at essentially the same value of 33.0% with a grand mean of 32.66% across 2,312 sampled time steps. The system exhibits moderate variance (standard deviation: 0.95%) driven primarily by transient dynamics in early simulation phases.

During the first annual cycle (approximately ticks 0-365), vegetation undergoes a severe decline from the initial 33.0% to a minimum value of 24.91% observed at tick 365. This represents an absolute reduction of 8.1 percentage points (24.5% relative decline) in available forage. Following this nadir, rapid recovery occurs, with mean vegetation returning to 32.5-33.0% by tick 500.

A second perturbation manifests around tick 750, where vegetation decreases to approximately 29.0%, followed by recovery to baseline levels by tick 1000. After tick 1500, the system stabilizes with negligible long-term drift, fluctuating narrowly around 32.99-33.0% for the remainder of the simulation.

The dynamics display strong oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 63 ticks. Given the temporal calibration of 365 ticks per year, this period corresponds to approximately 63 days, indicating regular seasonal pulses in vegetation availability consistent with the high seasonality parameter (0.8). 

Statistical trend analysis reveals significant monotonic increases during recovery phases following major depletion events (positive Mann-Kendall trends in windows covering ticks 0-1,855), transitioning to slight decreasing trends in the latest phases (ticks 1,856-2,087). Despite these transient dynamics, the net change across the full simulation duration is negligible (-0.0003 percentage points), indicating the vegetation subsystem achieves long-term equilibrium with consumption pressure.

The trajectory indicates that initial megaherbivore populations exert intense grazing pressure during establishment, driving vegetation to annual minimum levels at the end of the first year. The rapid regrowth capability (3-tick recovery time) enables vegetation rebound, and the system eventually stabilizes at the initialized grass proportion of 33%, with ongoing seasonal oscillations reflecting the imposed environmental seasonality.
