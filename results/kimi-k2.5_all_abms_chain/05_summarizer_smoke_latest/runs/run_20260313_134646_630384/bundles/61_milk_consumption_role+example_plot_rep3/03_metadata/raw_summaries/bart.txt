 The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . It captures how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts . Success is measured by the model's ability to reproduce the historical crossover point where skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption surpassed whole milk consumption .  The study was published by the University of California, California, and the National Institute of American Studies . The study is published in the journal "Psychicoprivatomicopraige" and "Pomniapria" (PSC) and "Psychiapria") .  Means quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed per agent begins at approximately zero at step 0, consistent with initial conditions reflecting 1974 market dominance of whole milk . Means of milk consumed begins at about 2,650 units, then declines rapidly over the first 10 steps, falling to roughly 1,000 units by step 10 . Means continue to fluctuate between approximately 1,180 and 1,280 units, exhibiting a temporary decline .  The mean habit factor applied to whole milk choices initiates at a value of 1.00 at step 0, then undergoes an immediate and substantial increase to approximately 1.02 by step 1.0 . By step 10, values across simulation runs range from roughly 1.028 to 1.032 . The monotonic upward trend indicates that habitual tendencies toward whole milk strengthen progressively among agents .  The mean deviation between agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices initiates at approximately 0 at time step 0, indicating strong alignment between consumers' values and their milk consumption choices during the initial period corresponding to 1974 market conditions . From time step 10 onward, the metric exhibits a sustained, nearly linear increase .
