**Mean Value Environmental Deviation**

The mean deviation between agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices initiates at approximately 0 at time step 0, indicating strong alignment between consumers' environmental values and their milk consumption choices during the initial period corresponding to 1974 market conditions. This metric undergoes a sharp increase during the first 5 time steps, rising rapidly to reach approximately 13. This steep ascent suggests an immediate and substantial decoupling between environmental values and consumption behaviors as agents begin deviating from the incumbent whole milk consumption patterns.

Between time steps 5 and 10, the growth rate moderates significantly, with the deviation increasing from approximately 13 to around 13.5. This deceleration reflects a slowing but continued accumulation of misalignment between values and choices as the market transition progresses and social influence mechanisms propagate alternative consumption patterns through the network.

From approximately time step 10 through the conclusion of the simulation at step 32, the metric stabilizes and maintains a relatively constant value between 13.3 and 13.5, exhibiting only minor fluctuations around this equilibrium level. This persistent plateau indicates that the population has reached a steady-state configuration where the average discrepancy between environmental values and consumption choices remains constant, likely reflecting an equilibrium where the justification mechanisms (set to 0.61) and cognitive dissonance processing allow consumers to maintain choices that diverge from their environmental values without further increasing the systematic deviation.

Across all simulation runs, the trajectories demonstrate minimal variance and remain tightly clustered throughout the entire time period. This high consistency suggests that the emergence of this environmental value-behavior gap represents a deterministic and robust outcome of the model's parameterization—including the high initial habit strength for whole milk (10.0), the moderate social susceptibility (0.2), and the specific network connectivity (Watts-Strogatz with parameter 8.0)—rather than stochastic variation in agent interactions.
