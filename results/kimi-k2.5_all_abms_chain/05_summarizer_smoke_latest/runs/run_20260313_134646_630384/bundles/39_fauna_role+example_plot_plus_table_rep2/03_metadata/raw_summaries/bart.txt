 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change . Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances . Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores .  Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius . The number of human foragers remains constant at 20 individuals throughout the entire simulation duration of 2,312 steps . Seasonality at 0.8 creates strong seasonal pulses in resource availability .  Substantial heterogeneity exists across the 80 simulation runs, with a standard deviation of 32.5 and final values ranging from 0 to 120.2 . The median final count of 116.5 exceeds the mean of 100.4, indicating a left-skewed distribution where the majority of simulations resulted in high cumulative kill counts (110–120), while a subset of runs exhibited considerably lower exploitation rates .  The cumulative count of postpartum females killed initiates at zero and undergoes an initial phase of rapid accumulation, reaching approximately half of the terminal maximum within the first 475 ticks . The accumulation rate decelerates markedly through sequential structural transitions at ticks 625 and 850 . From approximately tick 1400 to 1826, the cumulative count stabilizes near its asymptotic maximum of 119 individuals .  The number of male ungulates begins at 125 individuals . Over the course of the simulation, this population undergoes a deterministic decline toward extinction, reaching zero individuals by approximately step 2,310 . The population of female megaherbivores initiates at 125 individuals and undergoes a deterministic decline toward extinction across the simulation duration . The trajectory exhibits three distinct phases: an initial rapid collapse, an intermediate period of decelerated decay with oscillatory dynamics, and a terminal low-density phase characterized by high stochasticity .  The terminal phase, from approximately step 1,000 to the conclusion at step 2,311, is defined by extremely low abundance, typically fewer than 5 individuals . During this interval, the population exhibits oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps, corresponding to seasonal vegetation cycles that create temporary fluctuations in resource availability and demographic rates .  The aggregate energy reserves of the megaherbivore population commence at approximately 16,667 units, reflecting the initial cohort of 250 animals distributed across the grassland landscape . During the initial 350 steps, energy reserves plummet rapidly as the 20 human foragers exploit the abundant prey base, representing the immediate impact of hunting pressure on the naive population .
