Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. Metric exhibits no temporal variation, maintaining an identical value across all 101 time steps with zero standard deviation and variance. The metric represents a fixed baseline attribute assigned to agents at the start of the simulation rather than a dynamically evolving variable. Green biomass exhibited a characteristic trajectory of initial pressure followed by adaptive recovery over the 100-timestep simulation period. A transitional stabilization occurred between timesteps 11 and 21, representing the nadir of pasture conditions under initial grazing pressure. Green biomass attained its maximum value of approximately 60,535 units—marking a net increase of over 34% from initial conditions. The early period (timesteps 0-32) exhibits significant decreasing trends across multiple consecutive windows, indicating consistent improvement in resource management. The middle period shows no significant trend, while late periods demonstrate slight variability but maintain near-zero destocking levels. This plateau represents an equilibrium where roughly 53% of the household population maintains active social learning engagement. Social learning processes diffuse beyond the initially designated E-RO-SL1 agents to encompass a substantial portion of the farming community. Non-learning E-RO households are gradually outcompeted or displaced over time. The population composition is shifting toward learning-capable behavioral types despite the initial increase in baseline strategy adoption.
