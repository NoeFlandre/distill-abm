The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005. It captures how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts. Success is measured by the model's ability to reproduce the historical crossover point where skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption surpassed whole milk consumption. The time series exhibits a three-phase trajectory in mean weekly skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption per agent across the 31-step simulation horizon. At initial (Step 0), consumption registers near 5 units, reflecting the initial market condition in which whole milk maintains dominance through maximal incumbent habit strength. The cumulative count of whole milk selections exhibits approximately linear growth throughout the 32-step. The model produces a consistent selection of approximately 7% per agent per agent. At the start of the 31-year simulation window (1974-2005), the total choices made equals zero. At step 32, the cumulative total reaches approximately 32,000 choices. The cumulative count of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk selections initiates at approximately zero at step 0 and exhibits a consistent upward trajectory. The mean habit factor for whole milk begins at approximately 1.00 at Step 0, indicating a neutral baseline where habit exerts no initial influence on choice. Means of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk initiates at a value of 1.0 at step 0. Means mean health deviation exhibits a three-phase trajectory over the 31-step simulation period. At first, the deviation initiates at 0.0 and undergoes rapid growth, reaching approximately 0.50–0.55 by step 10. At step 10, the metric stabilizes and plateaus, maintaining a relatively constant value between approximately 13.3 and 13.5 through step 32.
