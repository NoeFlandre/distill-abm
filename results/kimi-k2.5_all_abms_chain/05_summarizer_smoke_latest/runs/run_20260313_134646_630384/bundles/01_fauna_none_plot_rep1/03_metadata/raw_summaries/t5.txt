the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the simulation, from step 0 through step 2200 and beyond . the count of active foragers shows no variation from its initial value across all recorded time steps . during the initial phase, the energy decreases rapidly to approximately -5000 units . the metric stabilizes between approximately 115 and 125 total females killed . some runs reach a plateau as early as step 600-800 and maintain constant values thereafter . the saturation at levels approaching 125 indicates that hunters eventually kill most or all available females . trajectories initiate at zero cumulative postpartum female deaths . the cumulative number of prey deaths increases monotonically from zero at initialization . death counts rise steeply, with most simulations accumulating between 200 and 230 deaths by step 500 . by steps 1000 to 1500, the accumulation of deaths largely ceases across most runs . extinction timing reflects stochastic processes in hunter-prey encounters and herd demographic dynamics . trajectories demonstrate high variability in the rate of population decrease and timing of final extinction events . some populations persist until between steps 1,500 and 2,200 before reaching zero . the metric undergoes a steep and rapid decline, with most trajectories falling to values between 2,000 and 4,000 units . several simulation runs drop to near-zero levels within this timeframe . the rate of energy loss slows considerably between steps 1,000 and 1,500 . a rapid recovery phase ensues between time steps 300 and 600 . the system exhibits oscillatory behavior, with vegetated patches declining to 29% before recovering again to 33% . a subsequent, less severe fluctuation occurs around time step 1000-1100 .
