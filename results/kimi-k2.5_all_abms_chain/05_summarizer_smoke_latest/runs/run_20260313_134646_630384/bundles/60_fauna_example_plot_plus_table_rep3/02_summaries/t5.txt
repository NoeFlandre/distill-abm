The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics. The population of human hunter-gatherer bands remains fixed at 20 throughout the entire simulation duration, from the initial time step through approximately 2,300 time steps. This constant value indicates that no births or deaths occur within the hunter population during the experimental period. Cumulative mortality of postpartum female prey begins at zero and increases rapidly during the initial approximately 475 time steps. This early phase exhibits the steepest accumulation rate, with counts rising sharply as hunter agents encounter and target reproductive females bearing dependent offspring. A significant deceleration occurs around step 475, marking the first major transition. A second phase transition occurs around step 1325, followed by the peak reproductive output at step 1702. The system enters a decline phase marked by change points at approximately steps 1850 and 2087. By the conclusion of the simulation, the aggregate mean cumulative birth count has declined to approximately 1.0, representing a substantial reduction from peak values. A transient demographic stabilization occurs between approximately steps 1300 and 1600. The population resumes its terminal decline after step 1600, with numbers dwindling below 5 individuals by step 1800. After step 875, the population drops below 3 individuals and enters a stochastic extinction phase characterized by extreme variability. The combined total energy of male prey animals initiates at approximately 12,500 units and undergoes a progressive collapse to extinction, converging to zero near step 2,284. The initial phase (steps 0–125) witnesses a precipitous decline characterized by steep downward trajectories, superimposed with regular oscillations. Between steps 1,392 and 1,623, the rate of decline moderates considerably. The slope decreases to approximately -3 to -0.07 units per step. From step 1,624 onward, the energy resumes its decline, though at varying rates.
