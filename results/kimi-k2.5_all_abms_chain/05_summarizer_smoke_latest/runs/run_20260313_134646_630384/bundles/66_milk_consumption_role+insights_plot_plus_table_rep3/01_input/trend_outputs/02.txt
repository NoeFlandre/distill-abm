**Implications for Decision Makers**

Market interventions targeting dietary transitions should anticipate a delayed but explosive uptake phase once habit thresholds are breached, followed by an extended period of market turbulence requiring sustained engagement strategies. The mid-cycle plateau (steps 12-26) suggests that early success does not guarantee linear progression; instead, distinct strategies are required for late adopters who respond to consolidated social norms rather than initial health messaging. Furthermore, the oscillatory nature of the middle phase indicates that social conformity mechanisms can temporarily retard growth as agents negotiate conflicting pressures, suggesting that timing of normative messaging must account for these natural market fluctuations to avoid reinforcing temporary resistance periods.
