The cumulative mortality trajectory of the megaherbivore population exhibits a saturating increase over the observed 2,312 time steps (approximately 6.3 years), approaching an asymptote near 235–240 total deaths. During the initial 850 ticks (roughly the first 2.3 years), the system experiences rapid mortality accumulation, with death counts rising steeply from zero to approximately 200 individuals. This phase is characterized by statistically significant monotonic increases across consecutive 232-tick windows, with standardized slopes as steep as 0.346 deaths per tick initially, gradually decelerating to 0.017 by the window spanning ticks 928–1159. The pronounced early mortality likely reflects the high encounter rates between the founding cohort of 250 prey individuals and 20 human foragers, compounded by strong seasonal resource bottlenecks (seasonality parameter 0.8) that exacerbate starvation risk during low-productivity periods.

Between ticks 850 and 1,876, the accumulation rate decelerates markedly, with the series exhibiting distinct change points at roughly 125-tick intervals, suggesting regular mortality pulses superimposed on the saturating trend. By tick 1,876, cumulative mortality reaches its maximum observed value of approximately 240.3 deaths. In the final approximately 436 ticks (from 1,876 to 2,312), the cumulative death count stabilizes and registers a slight decline to 235, oscillating around 237–239 with a dominant periodicity of 63 ticks (approximately two-month cycles). This plateau indicates effective cessation of net mortality accumulation, likely arising from demographic stabilization wherein surviving females recruit offspring—governed by the 730-tick interbirth interval—sufficient to balance ongoing deaths, combined with the depletion of the initial naive cohort and potential adaptive shifts in herd movement patterns. The transition from highly significant increasing trends in early windows to non-significant or slightly decreasing trends in later windows (particularly the decreasing trend in window 1,856–2,087, tau = -0.446) confirms the shift from active mortality accumulation to demographic equilibrium within this initial 6-year period.
