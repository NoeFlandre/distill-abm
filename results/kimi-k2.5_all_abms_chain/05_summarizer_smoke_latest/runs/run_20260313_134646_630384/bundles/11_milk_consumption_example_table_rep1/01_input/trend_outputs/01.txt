The trajectory exhibits distinct phases of varying rates of decrease, punctuated by periods of relative stabilization and minor oscillation.

The initial phase spanning the first five time steps demonstrates a rapid decrease from the initial high baseline. This decline accelerates further between indices 5 and 9, representing the steepest rate of reduction throughout the series, suggesting intense early disposition toward alternative milk varieties driven by initial perceptions of health benefits or environmental considerations among agents.

From indices 10 through 14, the descent moderates but remains steady, indicating continued adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed alternatives as agents adjust their consumption patterns. A notable plateau emerges between indices 15 and 19, where consumption stabilizes temporarily around values between 760 and 774 units. This interruption in the decline likely reflects the temporary influence of strong incumbent habits or social conformity effects reaching an equilibrium state where disposition mechanisms are less frequently triggered among remaining whole milk consumers.

Following this stabilization, a gradual decline resumes between indices 20 and 24, albeit at a considerably reduced rate compared to earlier phases. The period from indices 25 to 30 exhibits volatile fluctuations with values oscillating between approximately 323 and 621 units, marked by minor peaks at indices 27 and 30 and intervening valleys. These late-stage variations suggest ongoing micro-adjustments in agent decision-making as habit strengths weaken and social influence continues to propagate, though with diminishing overall effect on the remaining consumer base.
