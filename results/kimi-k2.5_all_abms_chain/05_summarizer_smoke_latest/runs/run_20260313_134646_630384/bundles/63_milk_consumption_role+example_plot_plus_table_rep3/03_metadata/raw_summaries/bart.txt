 The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . It captures how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts . Each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers in their network . During the initial nine time steps, consumption exhibits a steep monotonic increase, rising rapidly from near-negligible levels to approximately 1,200 units by time step 9 . The mean value health deviation begins at zero, indicating perfect alignment between consumers' held health perceptions and their milk choices at the initial state representing 1974 market conditions .
