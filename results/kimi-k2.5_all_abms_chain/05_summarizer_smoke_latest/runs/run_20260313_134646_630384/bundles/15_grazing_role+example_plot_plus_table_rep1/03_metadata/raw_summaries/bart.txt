 The agent-based model represents smallholder farmers managing private pastures in a grazing system . Each year, farmers observe their pasture condition and neighbors' situations to decide how many animals to keep . The model explores how different learning strategies affect long-term outcomes for both the land and the farmers' livelihoods .  Individual simulation runs display constant but heterogeneous initial risk attitude values that vary across the ensemble, ranging from approximately -0.11 to 0.18 . The mean household risk attitude initiates at a near-neutral level of 0.0074 and increases rapidly during the first third of the simulation . The decline suggests a collective recalibration toward more conservative risk postures .  The mean green biomass across all patches begins at approximately 45,000 units . Within the first four steps, the system exhibits minor fluctuation, reaching a local maximum of roughly 45,261 units before entering a significant declining phase . This early decline suggests that initial stocking rates exceeded immediate pasture carrying capacity, leading to vegetation depletion as grazing pressure intensified .  The mean number of livestock destocked per household begins at zero, then rises sharply to 6.6 animals by the third time step . The trajectory reveals distinct phases of adaptation: an initial crisis response characterized by high variance and rapid change, followed by a convergence toward equilibrium as learning mechanisms take effect .  The count of households engaged in social learning initiates at zero across all simulation replicates . During the first approximately 20 steps, the system exhibits rapid adoption, with statistically significant increasing trends (p<0.001) and slopes exceeding 1.4 households per step in the initial decade . This early phase represents the diffusion of social learning behaviors through the farming community as households begin observing and adopting strategies from within their knowledge radius .  The decline phase reduces the population from its peak to approximately 15 units, representing roughly 25% attrition of the non-learning cohort . The steepest decreases occur between steps 11 and 21, after which the attrition continues at a moderating pace through step 65 .
