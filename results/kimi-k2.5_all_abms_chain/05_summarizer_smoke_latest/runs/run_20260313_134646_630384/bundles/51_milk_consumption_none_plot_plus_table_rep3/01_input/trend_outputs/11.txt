The simulation of milk consumption adoption behaviors from 1974 to 2005 demonstrates a consistent monotonic increase in the aggregate mean deviation between agents' held health values and the health valuations implied by their actual milk choices. At the initial time step, representing the 1974 market baseline dominated by whole milk consumption, the deviation registers at 0.0000, indicating initial alignment between consumer health perceptions and milk selections under conditions of strong incumbent habits (parameterized at 10.0) and limited exposure to alternatives.

During the early phase spanning approximately the first four to six time steps, the metric exhibits rapid acceleration characterized by a Mann-Kendall slope of 0.063, rising sharply to approximately 0.25–0.30. This corresponds to the initial disposition triggering period where agents, positioned within a Watts-Strogatz network topology (parameter 8.0), begin considering low-fat alternatives despite high initial habit strength, creating immediate tension between established consumption patterns and evolving health perceptions (alt-health-mean-initial 1.69).

In the subsequent middle phase from roughly time steps 10 through 24, the rate of increase diminishes substantially, with per-window slopes dropping to between 0.003 and 0.012. During this interval, the deviation metric progresses from approximately 0.50 to 0.58, indicating a temporary stabilization in the divergence between values and choices. This plateau likely reflects the moderating influence of habit formation mechanisms and cognitive dissonance thresholds (0.15) operating alongside social susceptibility effects (0.2), which create resistance to rapid behavioral adjustment despite ongoing social interaction (p-interact 0.3).

The final phase, commencing around time step 25 and continuing through the conclusion at step 32 (representing 2005), shows renewed acceleration with a slope of 0.019, driving the terminal deviation value to 0.7251. This late-stage increase suggests that as social norms intensify within the network and social conformity pressures (0.28) compound with memory-lifetime effects (5.0), agents' choices progressively diverge from their individually held health valuations, resulting in substantial misalignment by the end of the simulation period.

Across thirty replicated simulation runs, trajectories remain consistently increasing without detected peak or valley inflections, though minor heterogeneity appears in the precise timing of transition points between growth phases, with all runs converging toward final deviation values ranging between 0.70 and 0.75. The absence of detected oscillatory peaks, combined with statistically significant monotonic trends across all analysis windows (p=0.027), indicates stable directional dynamics in value-choice misalignment throughout the modeled transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties.
