 The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . It captures how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts . Success is measured by the model's ability to reproduce the historical crossover point where skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption surpassed whole milk consumption .  The simulation tracks cumulative whole milk selections across 33 time steps representing the period from 1974 to 2005 . The rate of accumulation displays distinct phases characterized by an initial acceleration followed by progressive deceleration . The median consumption level across all periods stood near 817 units, substantially below the arithmetic mean of approximately 1080 units . The acceleration phase emerges between 1979 and 1983, where the annual accumulation rate increases substantially to approximately 737 selections . By the final years, the annual selection rate suggests that approximately 94 percent of agents are choosing alternative milk varieties .
