The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to the combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. Unlike traditional approaches that look at populations as uniform groups, this model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances. The model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for the herbivores. Additionally, the model is designed to simulate the effects of hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and animal life history traits on hunter behavior.
