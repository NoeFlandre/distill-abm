**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The rapid saturation of hunting mortality within the first three years indicates that forager populations can exert substantial pressure on megaherbivore stocks quickly, potentially exceeding sustainable yield thresholds before demographic compensation can occur. The presence of strong oscillatory patterns suggests that management or conservation strategies must account for predictable seasonal windows of vulnerability where males face disproportionate risk. The late-phase decline in cumulative kills serves as a critical warning that continued hunting pressure may lead to functional extinction of the male cohort, potentially triggering herd demographic collapse due to reproductive failure long before total population extinction occurs.
