 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change . Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances . Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores .  Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius . The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains absolutely constant at 20 individuals throughout the entire simulation duration of 2,312 time steps .  The cumulative hunting mortality of male ungulates begins at zero and undergoes rapid acceleration during the first approximately 365 time steps . By approximately step 1000 to 1200 (roughly 2.7 to 3.3 years), the accumulation transitions into a plateau phase, with the cumulative count stabilizing near 118 to 120 individuals killed . During this plateau period, the metric exhibits regular oscillatory behavior with a dominant periodicity of approximately 63 steps .  The cumulative mortality of postpartum female ungulates follows a decelerating growth trajectory across the simulation . The accumulation process undergoes distinct kinematic phases marked by specific temporal thresholds . The 63-tick cyclical pattern offers predictable windows for management interventions or hunting moratoriums to maximize conservation effectiveness .  The trajectory reveals a critical tipping point near tick 1700, after which reproductive output enters irreversible decline despite continued seasonal potential . Early investment in population protection before tick 725 appears crucial, as the system demonstrates limited resilience to recover once the decline phase initiates after tick 1700 . For decision makers: The four-decade persistence of prey populations under continuous hunting pressure .  The trajectory reveals four distinct phases of demographic change driven by interaction of human hunting pressure, reproductive biology, and seasonal resource variability . From tick 0 to 475, the population collapses rapidly from 125 to approximately 5 individuals, representing a 96% reduction in the founding female cohort . The trajectory indicates that encounter rates between 20 human foragers and the ungulate population generate hunting mortality that vastly exceeds the maximum sustainable yield .  The population spends the majority of the simulation at very low densities, evidenced by a median value of approximately 1.5 individuals compared to a mean of 25 . The population enters a terminal decline phase after tick 2087, passing through inflection points at 2156 and 2167 before reaching extinction at tick 2311 . The trajectory demonstrates that constant hunter populations of modest size (20 foragers) generate sufficient predation pressure to drive megaherbivore extinction within approximately 6.3 simulation  The total energy available across the entire prey population initiates at approximately 16,667 units and follows a deterministic trajectory toward complete depletion, reaching zero by tick 2,311 (approximately 6.3 years) This pattern indicates that the initial parameter configuration produces predation pressure that exceeds the population's capacity for demographic replacement . Management efforts should prioritize reducing extraction pressure before critical energy thresholds are breached .  The vegetated landscape coverage begins at approximately 33%, consistent with the initial grassland configuration . The vegetation never fully recovers to the initial 33% in subsequent cycles, instead fluctuating between roughly 25% and 33% with a dominant periodicity of approximately 63 time steps (roughly bi-monthly given the daily timestep calibration)
