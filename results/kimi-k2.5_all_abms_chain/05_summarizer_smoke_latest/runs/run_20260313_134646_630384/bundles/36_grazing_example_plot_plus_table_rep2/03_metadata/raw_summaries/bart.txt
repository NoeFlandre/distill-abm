 This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . It is designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems . In the Mann-Kendall analysis, inequality in livestock health across households begins at effectively zero, indicating that all households start with nearly identical livestock health conditions . Over the course of the simulation, inequality increases steadily and consistently across all scenarios, reaching an average value of approximately 0.073 by the final time step .
