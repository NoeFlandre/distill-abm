 The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005, capturing the transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . The primary objective is to replicate the observed historical adoption patterns of milk consumption in Britain, capturing how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts . Success is measured by the model's ability to reproduce the historical crossover point where skimmed milk consumption surpassed whole milk consumption .  Consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at approximately zero to ten units during the initial time steps . Consumption undergoes a rapid exponential increase, rising sharply from near-zero levels to approximately 1200-1250 units . Consumption initiates a second growth phase, ascending from approximately 1250 units to 1480 units . At approximately step 10, the cumulative count reaches roughly 700–800 selections .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents accumulates linearly throughout the simulation period . At the initial time step (step 0), the cumulative count stands at 0 choices . By the final observed time step, the total reaches approximately 32,000 choices . The data reveal a consistent downward trajectory in mean whole milk consumption .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trajectory across all simulation runs . This progression occurs through three distinguishable phases with minimal variance between repeated simulations . This pattern aligns with the historical transition from whole milk dominance to the establishment of reduced-fat milk varieties as standard consumption choices .  The mean value health deviation demonstrates a monotonic upward trajectory across the 31-step simulation period . The temporal dynamics unfold through three distinct phases characterized by varying rates of change . Within the context of the British milk consumption transition (1974-2005), these trends suggest an evolving misalignment between consumers' internal health valuations and their actual consumption behaviors .
