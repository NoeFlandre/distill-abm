 This agent-based model simulates the adoption behaviors of milk consumption among the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It replicates individual preferences and decision factors underlying the historical transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . Decision-making follows a structured sequence involving perception of health and environmental choice characteristics, disposition triggering, scored choice functions weighted by habit strength and social influence, and post-choice evaluation .  Means weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at approximately 5.3 units at step 0, representing negligible adoption at the start of the simulation period corresponding to 1974 . Total whole milk choices begin at zero and accumulate steadily throughout the simulation, reaching approximately 2,195 choices by the final step .  The average cumulative choice count across all time steps settles at 11,157, with a median of 10,679 . By the change point at step 32, the system has accumulated approximately 80 percent of the maximum possible alternative choices . The average weekly consumption of whole milk per person begins at 2,654.81 units and undergoes a substantial decline throughout the simulation period .  The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption begins at 1.0000, representing a neutral baseline where habits exert no modification on choice functions . Within the initial two time steps, the factor increases sharply to approximately 1.0200, reflecting the immediate activation of habit reinforcement mechanisms among agents with strong incumbent preferences . A distinct second growth phase emerges beginning around step 25, with consumption rising steadily from approximately 1,220 units to 1,456 units by the final time step 32 .  The mean value health deviation begins at 0.000 and exhibits a continuous monotonic increase throughout the 33-step simulation . This persistent upward trajectory reflects the model's dynamics where individual habit strength, social susceptibility, and cognitive dissonance thresholds interact to create distinct preference profiles among the 1,000 agents, rather than converging toward a homogeneous equilibrium .  After peaking at step 13, the metric stabilizes into a plateau phase, exhibiting minor oscillations between approximately 13.42 and 13.48 through the conclusion of the simulation at step 32, where it registers 13.4186 . Inflection points at steps 16, 19, 24, 26, 28, and 31 mark subtle adjustments within this stable regime .
