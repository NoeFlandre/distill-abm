The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics. The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the observation period. This stability reflects the parameter configuration where foragers are immortal and do not reproduce, maintaining the initial population of 20 established at the start of the simulation. The total energy available to all forager bands begins at approximately 2000 units across all simulation runs. Atypical trajectory exhibits a pronounced lag phase, maintaining cumulative kills below 10 until approximately step 150. This pattern suggests initial spatial segregation between forager bands and breeding herds in that particular realization. The plateauing dynamics indicate that hunting pressure effectively exhausts the available reproductive female cohort faster than replacement through natality can sustain. The spread in final values, ranging from approximately 225 to 250 deaths, reflects differences in the total number of offspring produced before population collapse. A rapid decline occurs over the first 300 to 500 steps, with counts typically falling below 40 individuals and many runs approaching 20 or fewer males. Some runs maintain 1 to 5 individuals until step 1500 or later. This prolonged persistence aligns with the maximum lifespan parameter of 10,220 ticks and the senescence threshold at 7,300 ticks. The combination of low fecundity, continuous hunting pressure from immortal foragers, and environmental constraints prevents population recovery at low densities. Substantial variance characterizes the extinction trajectories. Some runs plummet to minimal energy by step 400 while others retain 8,000 or more units until step 600. The total energy of all animals begins at approximately 25,000 units across all simulation runs.
