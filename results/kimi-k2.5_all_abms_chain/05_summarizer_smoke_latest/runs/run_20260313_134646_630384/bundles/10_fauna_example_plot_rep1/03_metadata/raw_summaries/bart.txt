 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges . The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics .  The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the observation period . Total energy available to all forager bands begins at approximately 2000 units across all simulation runs . By step 2000, the total energy reaches approximately -30,000 to -32,000 units in most runs, with one run continuing to step 2200 .  The cumulative number of prey deaths starts at zero and increases in a stepwise manner throughout the simulation runs . Some simulations reach their maximum cumulative death count by step 1000, while others continue accumulating sporadic deaths until step 2000 or beyond . The model's life history parameters include a maximum lifespan of 10,220 ticks and a birth-spacing interval of 730 steps .  The number of female animals begins at approximately 120 to 125 individuals across all simulation runs . The rate of decline varies considerably across simulation runs, resulting in a wide range of extinction times . Some populations decline rapidly, reaching near-zero levels within 400 to 600 steps . Others persist considerably longer, with some surviving beyond 2000 steps before declining to extinction .  The total energy stored in male animal populations begins at approximately 12,500 units . It declines precipitously over the first 500 steps, dropping to values ranging between roughly 500 and 5,000 units . During this phase, the steepest trajectories lose over 75 percent of initial energy within the first 300 steps .  Percentage of vegetated landscape patches begins at approximately 33% and immediately undergoes a slight decline to around 32% within the first 100 steps . After step 1000, the majority of simulation runs converge toward zero total energy, with most populations reaching effective extinction or near-extinction levels by step 1200 .
