The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005. It captures how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts. Each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers in their network. The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent in 31-year simulation period from 1974 to 2005 was studied. This trajectory captures the historical market transition from incumbent dominance to alternative milk adoption. The decline exhibits distinct phased dynamics rather than uniform decay. Decision makers should anticipate that initial adoption acceleration (steps 0–9) will naturally slow as laggard segments activate strong habit maintenance mechanisms. Consumption increases from approximately 1,210 units at time step 23 to a terminal value of 1,456.6 units at step 32. As proportion of alternative milk consumers reaches critical mass, social influence shifts from inhibiting to facilitating change. The slower rate compared to initial adoption reflects the diminishing pool of remaining whole milk consumers. The simulation depicts a perfectly linear accumulation of consumer choices over the 1974–2005 period. The aggregate count begins at zero choices and increases at a constant rate of exactly 1,000 decisions per time step. This trajectory produces a mean value of 16,000 choices across the time series, with the median identically positioned at the midpoint of the simulation. The trajectory of whole milk consumption exhibits a pronounced secular decline over the 31-year simulation period, commencing from approximately 2,655 units at time step 0 (representing 1974 market conditions) and terminating near 253 units by time step 32 (2005) The trajectory reflects the aggregate behavioral shift away from the incumbent whole milk option toward alternative varieties. The temporal evolution of the whole milk habit factor reveals a modest but consistent upward trajectory across the entire observation window. The dynamics unfold through distinct phases punctuated by inflection points at time steps 7 and 12. From step 12 onward, the trend continues its steady ascent toward the terminal value of 1.0502 by time step 32.
