Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. The mean household risk attitude begins the simulation at a minimal value of approximately 0.007. This metric undergoes a rapid and sustained increase, reaching a peak value of 0.38 around time step 33. After time step 65, the metric stabilizes around 0.19 for the remainder of the simulation. The system enters a sustained recovery phase that continues through step 100. From step 44 to 54, the biomass increases significantly with strong upward trends. The recovery accelerates notably between steps 44 and 54. This acceleration may reflect the influence of learning processes. The number of households engaging in social learning begins at zero and undergoes a rapid increase during the initial phase of the simulation. This early surge suggests an initial diffusion process where social learning behaviors quickly spread among the population, likely driven by interactions with the initially present E-RO-SL1 agents and early observations of neighbor performance. The count of E-RO households begins at 20.0 at time step 0. It rises slightly to a peak of 21.1 at step 8, suggesting a brief initial period where the baseline non-learning strategy is adopted or retained by a slightly larger cohort. The decline becomes more gradual after time step 45, with the value reaching 16.5 around time step 34 and 15.6 by time step 44.
