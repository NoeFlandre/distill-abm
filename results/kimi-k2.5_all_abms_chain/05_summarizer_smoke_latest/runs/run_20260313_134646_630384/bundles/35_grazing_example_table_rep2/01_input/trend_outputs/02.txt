The mean household risk attitude (r-parameter) begins the simulation at a minimal value of approximately 0.007, indicating that agents initially operate with low sensitivity to uncertainty and risk. Over the first 33 time steps, this metric undergoes a rapid and sustained increase, reaching a peak value of 0.38 around time step 33. This sharp escalation suggests that during the initial establishment phase, households—comprising a heterogeneous mix of E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1 behavioral types—rapidly heighten their uncertainty management parameters in response to early experiences with climatic variability and the challenges of optimizing livestock feeding on private pastures under the specified rainfall conditions.

Following this peak, the mean risk attitude enters a pronounced decline that persists through approximately time step 65. The value decreases steadily from 0.38 to roughly 0.19, reflecting a calibration period where agents adjust their initially elevated risk assessments downward. This reduction likely corresponds to the learning-by-doing and social learning processes described in the adaptive co-management framework, as households accumulate direct experience with vegetation dynamics and observe neighbor performance within the constrained knowledge radius, thereby refining their estimates of actual ecological uncertainty.

After time step 65, the metric stabilizes around 0.19 for the remainder of the simulation, showing no statistically significant trend through time step 100. This plateau indicates that the population has converged toward a steady-state level of uncertainty management, where the feedback mechanisms between pasture biomass conditions, livestock placement decisions, and memory-based learning have equilibrated. The stabilization persists despite ongoing yearly cycles of green biomass updates and reserve biomass observations, suggesting that the social-ecological system has achieved resilience in its decision-making parameters under the fixed behavioral composition and climatic settings.

The trajectory exhibits a dominant oscillatory pattern with a period of approximately 49 time steps, which aligns with the interval between the early risk-attitude peak and the subsequent stabilization phase, reflecting the cyclical nature of adaptive learning and memory updating in response to the annual sequence of ecological and observational events.
