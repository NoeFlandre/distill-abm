The first phase encompasses time steps 0 through 9, characterized by rapid exponential growth. During this initial adoption period, consumption increases steeply from the baseline to approximately 1200–1265 units. Statistical analysis confirms significant monotonic increases throughout this window, with standardized trend statistics reaching maximum values and slope estimates exceeding 112 units per time step.

The second phase, spanning time steps 10 through 24, exhibits a plateau pattern with oscillating dynamics. Local peaks occur at time steps 11 (1265.47), 13 (1240.35), 19 (1221.57), and 21 (1226.86), interspersed with valleys at time steps 12 (1233.62), 18 (1183.39), 20 (1214.46), and 23 (1209.70). This oscillatory behavior suggests dynamic equilibrium between adoption forces and resistance mechanisms, likely reflecting the interplay of social influence, habit maintenance, and cognitive dissonance resolution among agents.

The third phase, from time steps 25 through 32, demonstrates renewed growth culminating in maximum adoption. Consumption increases from approximately 1220 units to a terminal value of 1456.60 units. This final ascent indicates the completion of the market transition, with skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties achieving dominance over whole milk by the 2005 endpoint.
