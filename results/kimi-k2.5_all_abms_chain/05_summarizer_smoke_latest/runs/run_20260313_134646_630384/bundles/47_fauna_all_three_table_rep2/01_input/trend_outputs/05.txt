**Implications for Decision Makers:**
The trajectory indicates that under high seasonality and moderate hunting pressure (20 foragers), postpartum females face disproportionate mortality risk during the first 18 months of simulation time, with cumulative losses reaching levels that likely compromise population viability. The subsequent plateau suggests either a sustainable but fragile equilibrium at reduced population levels or a delayed extinction vortex. Conservation strategies should prioritize protection of calving grounds during the initial exploitation phase (first 475 ticks), as this period determines the long-term trajectory of the reproductive female cohort. The 63-tick oscillation cycle offers a temporal window for targeted intervention, suggesting that seasonal hunting moratoria aligned with peak vulnerability periods could significantly alter cumulative mortality outcomes.
