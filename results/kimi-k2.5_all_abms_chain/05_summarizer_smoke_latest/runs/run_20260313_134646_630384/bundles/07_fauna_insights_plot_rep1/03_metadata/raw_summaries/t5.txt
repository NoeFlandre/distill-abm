the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the population of active foragers maintains a constant value of 20 agents throughout the entire simulation period from step 0 through step 2200 . the metric remains invariant at its initial value with zero deviation across all recorded time steps . this steady-state condition persists uniformly without evidence of growth, decline, or stochastic fluctuation . as early as step 1000, while others continue accumulating kills until beyond step 2000 . accumulated kills range from 20 to 60 females across runs;
