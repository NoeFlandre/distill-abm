**Implications for Decision Makers**

The distinct phase transitions suggest that public health interventions and marketing strategies require temporal calibration: initial efforts must focus on disrupting habitual consumption through information campaigns that alter health perceptions, while mid-period strategies should leverage social network effects and normative messaging to accelerate the adoption cascade. The prolonged linear growth phase (15+ years) indicates that market saturation requires sustained engagement rather than one-time interventions, particularly given the moderate social susceptibility (0.2) and significant social blindness (0.51) parameters that limit the efficiency of broadcast communications compared to targeted network-based approaches. Finally, the presence of persistent oscillations in late-stage adoption suggests that residual market segments may require specialized messaging that addresses cognitive dissonance resolution rather than purely informational appeals.
