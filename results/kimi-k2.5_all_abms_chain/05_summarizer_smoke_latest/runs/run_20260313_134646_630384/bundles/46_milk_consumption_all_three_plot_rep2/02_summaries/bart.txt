The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005. It captures how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts. Each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers in their network. The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent commences at approximately zero units during the initial time step. Consumption undergoes a rapid, approximately linear escalation, surging from near zero to roughly 1,250 units. From step 11 through step 26, the trajectory enters a prolonged plateau phase characterized by oscillation between approximately 1,180 and 1,280 units. The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined begins at 0 and increases linearly to approximately 32,000 by the final time step (step 32) Every agent in the population of 1,000 consumers makes exactly one milk selection decision during each period of the simulation. The mean quantity of whole milk consumed begins at approximately 2,650 to 2,700 units at step 0. The quantity declines sharply and consistently, reaching approximately 950 to 1,000 units by step 10. This steep reduction indicates a rapid transition period where consumers initially shifted away from whole milk toward alternative varieties. By the final years of the simulation, whole milk represents a niche market segment. The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at a baseline value of 1.0, indicating neutral habit strength for alternative milk varieties at the start of the simulation. During the initial five time steps, the metric exhibits minimal growth, fluctuating narrowly around 1.02. The growth rate increases substantially between steps 10 and 25, with the metric rising from 1.08 to approximately 1.46. The mean deviation between agents' held environmental values and the implied environmental values of their milk choices initiates at approximately zero and escalates rapidly during the first five time steps. This initial surge indicates an immediate decoupling of consumer values from actual purchasing behaviors as the population begins transitioning away from whole milk. The sharp acceleration in deviation during the final phase suggests that as markets approach major consumption crossovers, non-health factors become primary drivers of choice.
