 This agent-based model simulates the adoption behaviors of milk consumption among the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It replicates individual preferences and decision factors underlying the historical transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . The simulation integrates theoretical frameworks with empirical data, employing calibration against observed macro-level consumption patterns to validate outcomes .  The authors say whole milk consumption begins at zero and increases continuously throughout the simulation . By step 10, roughly 90 to 95 percent of agents have adopted skimmed or semi-skimmed milk as their regular choice, maintaining this preference consistently through the remainder of the simulation period corresponding to 1984-2005 .  The mean whole milk quantity consumed per agent begins at approximately 2,650 units at step 0, reflecting the initial 1974 market conditions where whole milk dominated consumer preferences . Consumption undergoes a steep decline over the first ten steps, falling to roughly 950 units by step 10 . Consumption continues declining at a reduced rate, ultimately stabilizing near 250 units in the final phase from step 28 to step 32 .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at approximately 1.0, indicating a neutral baseline for alternative milk consumption habits at the start of the simulation . The mean percentage difference between maximum and minimum choice function scores initiates at approximately zero, reflecting initial homogeneity in how agents evaluate milk options at the simulation outset .  The mean deviation between agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices begins at approximately zero . It increases rapidly over the first ten steps, rising sharply to approximately 0.52. The steep accumulation of deviation during this phase suggests that the initial reconsideration of milk choices generates substantial misalignment between . agents' internal health valuations and the health characteristics implied by their actual consumption behaviors .
