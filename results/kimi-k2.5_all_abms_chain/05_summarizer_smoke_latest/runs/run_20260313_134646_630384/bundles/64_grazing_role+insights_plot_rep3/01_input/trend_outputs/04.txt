The trajectories demonstrate three distinct phases: initial degradation (steps 5–30), rapid rehabilitation (steps 30–60), and stabilization (steps 60–100). The minimum values around step 30 correspond to periods of high grazing pressure relative to available forage, while the recovery reflects adaptive stocking decisions by farmers responding to declining pasture conditions. The persistent variation in final outcomes suggests that rainfall variability creates path dependencies where early environmental shocks have lasting effects on system productivity.
