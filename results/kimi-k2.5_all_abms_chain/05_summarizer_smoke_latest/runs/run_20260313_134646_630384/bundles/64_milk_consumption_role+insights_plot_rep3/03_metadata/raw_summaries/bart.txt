 The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . It captures the historical transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . Each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers in their network .  Trend Analysis of Skimmed and Semi-Skimmed Milk Consumption (1974-2005 Simulation) The simulations reveal a four-phase adoption trajectory for alternative milk consumption across 32 time steps . The trajectory reflects the interplay of several model mechanisms: the initial dominance of incumbent habits gives way as agents accumulate motivation to break habits .  Consumption increases from approximately 1,250 units to reach 1,450-1,480 units by step 32 . A secondary growth phase emerges in the final period, with sharp growth between steps 27 and 30 . The consistent rate of increase indicates whole milk consumption reaches a persistent equilibrium level within this parameterization .  The simulation data reveals a pronounced non-linear decline in mean whole milk consumption over the 31-year period . The initial five-step stagnation reflects the high incumbent habit strength parameter (10.0) for whole milk, creating substantial inertia that prevents immediate switching . The average per-agent adoption rate of approximately 80% by the end of the simulation period, indicating substantial but incomplete market transformation .
