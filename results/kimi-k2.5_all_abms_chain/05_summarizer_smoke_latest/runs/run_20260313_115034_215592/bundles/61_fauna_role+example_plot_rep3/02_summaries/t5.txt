The model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape. Each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for the herbivores. The model specifically incorporates sexual dimorphism. Animals-gain-from-food ranges from 0 to 100, set at 1.5, representing energy obtained from grazing. Threshold for starvation mortality, animals-starvation-threshold, ranges between 0 and 100. Human agents follow similar energetic rules. Curves flatten into plateaus with final cumulative counts ranging from approximately 108 to 125 males killed. This indicates that the male subpopulation has been hunted to the point where further kills become energetically unprofitable or demographically limited. Some simulation runs terminate earlier around 1500 steps while others continue to 2000 steps or beyond. At approximately step 750, a synchronized reproductive event occurs across all simulation runs. Cumulative birth counts instantaneously jump to values ranging between 2 and 8 offspring. The most successful run achieves only 10 total births over approximately 2000 time steps (equivalent to roughly 5.5 years) the rate of decline varies substantially across replicates. Some populations collapsing to fewer than 10 individuals by step 400. Others maintain 30–40 individuals until step 500–600. Divergence in extinction trajectories becomes pronounced during this middle phase. The rapid depletion corresponds to the initial hunting pressure exerted by the 20 human foragers operating under optimal foraging strategies. Some trajectories exhibit brief periods of stabilization or minor fluctuations between steps 300 and 600, occasionally rebounding slightly to 4,000 or 5,000 energy units before resuming their downward trend. The decay trajectories exhibit substantial heterogeneity across simulation runs. Some replicates exhaust available animal energy by step 300 to 400, descending to near-zero levels. Others maintain substantial reserves—ranging from 5,000 to 8,000 units—until approximately step 600 to 700.
