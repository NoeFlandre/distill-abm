the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period . the constancy reflects the model configuration where foragers are immortal and do not reproduce, maintaining the initial population of 20 hunters established at the start of the simulation . the cumulative mortality of female prey initiates at zero and undergoes rapid accumulation during the first 375 ticks, reaching approximately 85 individuals . this initial surge corresponds to the deployment of 20 forager agents exploiting the founding prey population of 250 ungulates . the trajectory exhibits inflection points at 1204, 1336, and 1487, suggesting progressive slowing in hunting success . around tick 613, the first inflection point marks the onset of measurable reproductive accumulation . the metric exhibits sustained growth punctuated by regular oscillations with a dominant period of approximately 50 ticks . this trajectory reverses dramatically, with statistically significant decreasing trends dominating from tick 1624 through 2087 . moderates between ticks 600 and 875 entering a prolonged phase of gradual reduction characterized by high volatility . superimposed upon this trend are cyclical fluctuations with a dominant period of approximately 63 ticks . these oscillations generate periodic peaks and troughs as recruitment attempts align with resource availability cycles . temporary demographic recoveries interrupt the decline at two intervals: first between approximately ticks 1160 and 1390 . the final phase from tick 1,624 to 2,284 exhibits terminal decline to zero, representing population collapse and functional extinction of males . the eventual disappearance by tick 2,284, just 28 ticks before the simulation termination indicates that hunting pressure ultimately overwhelmed all demographic compensatory mechanisms . from roughly 800 units to zero by tick 2,311 (approximately 6.3 years), with change points at ticks 875 and 2310 demarcating the final regime shift . the combination of sustained hunter presence, optimal foraging behavior, and environmental seasonality ultimately overwhelms the megaherbivore population's replacement capacity .
