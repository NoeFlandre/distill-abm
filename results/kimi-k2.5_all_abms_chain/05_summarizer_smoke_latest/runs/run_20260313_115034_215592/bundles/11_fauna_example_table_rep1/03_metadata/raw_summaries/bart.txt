 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges . The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics .  The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period . The constancy reflects the model configuration where foragers are immortal and do not reproduce . The aggregate energy available to forager bands initiates at 2000 units and undergoes a monotonic decline over the 2312 time steps of the simulation .  The cumulative mortality of postpartum female prey initiates at zero and undergoes rapid accumulation during the first 375 ticks . This initial surge corresponds to the deployment of 20 forager agents exploiting the founding prey population of 250 ungulates . The trajectory exhibits inflection points at 1204, 1336, and 1487, indicating progressive slowing in hunting success .  The population of megaherbivores begins at 125 individuals and declines rapidly toward extinction, reaching zero by approximately tick 2284 . The trajectory represents a protracted collapse under sustained hunting pressure, with the population spending the majority of the time at very low densities (median abundance ~1.35) punctuated by brief, small-scale recoveries .  The megaherbivore population initiates at approximately 167 individuals and undergoes a prolonged trajectory toward extinction over 2,311 time steps . The trajectory reveals four distinct phases of population change under hunting pressure . The combined energy reserves of the male prey population initiate at approximately 12,500 units and exhibit a sustained decline toward eventual extinction by tick 2,284 .  The total energy stored within the prey population initiates at approximately 16,667 units, representing the collective biomass of the initial megaherbivore herds . From tick 1391 to 1855, the population experiences renewed decline with intermittent stabilization periods indicated by inflection points at 1407, 1470, 1594, 1679, and 1763 .
