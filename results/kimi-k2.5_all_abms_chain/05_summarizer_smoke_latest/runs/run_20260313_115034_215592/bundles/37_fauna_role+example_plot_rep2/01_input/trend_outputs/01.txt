The number of active human forager bands remains constant throughout the simulation, maintaining the initial establishment of 20 agents from step 0 through approximately step 2200. This stability indicates that the population of human hunters experiences no mortality events over the observed time period, which aligns with the parameter configuration that removes death from energy depletion as a demographic process. Without attrition from starvation or other mortality sources, and with no mechanism for hunter recruitment or population growth specified in this configuration, the forager population exhibits zero variance across all time steps. This constant hunting pressure provides a fixed baseline of predation risk for the ungulate population, allowing the simulation to isolate the effects of environmental variability and ungulate life history traits on herd dynamics without the confounding influence of fluctuating human population size.
