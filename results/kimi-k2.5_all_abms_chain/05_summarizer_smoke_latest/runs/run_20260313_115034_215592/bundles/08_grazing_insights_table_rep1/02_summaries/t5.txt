Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. A constant value represents the initial disposition of E-RO-SL1 type agents to deviate from recommended stocking rates. The value near zero indicates that social learning agents began the simulation with minimal inherent tendency. However, decision makers should monitor how these agents adapt over time, as the low initial deviation tendency provides a baseline. By the conclusion of the simulation at timestep 100, the metric reached approximately 121,073 units, substantially exceeding initial conditions. The oscillation patterns superimposed on the overall trend exhibited a dominant periodicity of approximately 49 timesteps. The aggregate livestock holdings undergo a substantial contraction followed by a modest recovery and gradual stabilization over the 100-step simulation horizon. Initially, average livestock levels begin at 90.0 and experience a steep decline during the first 20 steps, with the rate of loss most severe in the earliest phase (steps 0–10). This rapid destocking transitions into a slower decline phase between steps 20 and 37, reaching a minimum value of approximately 41.0 at step 37. Inequality exhibits a steady, statistically significant monotonic increase. Over the subsequent 100 time steps, the Gini coefficient rises gradually. Despite starting from homogeneous conditions, divergence in livestock health outcomes emerges progressively within this private-property grazing system. The number of households engaged in social learning stabilizes at approximately 32. The end value at timestep 100 remains at approximately 53% of the total household population. However, the failure to reach universal adoption suggests the presence of structural barriers or differential suitability of social learning for specific household circumstances. From timesteps 11 through 65, slopes indicate consistent net outflows from the E-RO category. The series stabilizes, oscillating within a narrow range and reaching a minimum value of approximately 14.7 households near timestep 92. Statistical tests reveal no significant directional trends.
