The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics. The population of human hunter-gatherer bands maintains a fixed value of 20 across all 2312 recorded time steps. This absolute stability reflects the model's parameterization where hunters are immortal and do not reproduce. The active forager count remains locked at the initialization value throughout the entire simulation duration. Cumulative number of female prey hunted and killed by forager bands initiates at zero and accumulates to approximately 123 individuals by tick 2312. The trajectory follows a markedly decelerating growth pattern, transitioning from rapid initial accumulation to prolonged plateau with terminal oscillations. The metric enters a growth phase marked by distinct seasonal oscillations with a dominant periodicity of 50 ticks. Sequence peaks occurring at ticks 1073 (2.875), 1214 (3.45) and 1392 (4.3) demonstrate progressive increases in reproductive output. By tick 2312, the cumulative births fall to 1.0, indicating that the vast majority of offspring produced have been lost to mortality processes. Female prey population initiates at 125 individuals and exhibits a sustained decline toward extinction over 2,311 time steps, reaching zero individuals by the conclusion of the observation period. The trajectory is characterized by an initial rapid collapse followed by an extended phase of fluctuating decrease, intermittent temporary recoveries, and final extirpation. The total energy available within the male prey population begins at 12500 units at time step 0 and follows a long-term decline toward extinction. The initial phase from time 0 to 125 exhibits a steep decrease, consistent with acute hunting pressure from the initial forager population of 20 agents operating under optimal foraging constraints. By step 875, aggregate energy has fallen below 1,100 units, transitioning into a regime characterized by extreme scarcity. The system exhibits statistically significant but brief intervals of energy increase between steps 1,392–1,623 and again between step 1,856–2,087, where slight positive trends emerge.
