The population of human hunter-gatherer bands maintains a fixed value of 20 across all 2312 recorded time steps, showing no deviation from initial conditions. This absolute stability reflects the model's parameterization where hunters are immortal and do not reproduce, effectively creating a constant predator pressure scenario. With no energy depletion deaths occurring and no recruitment of new hunters, the active forager count remains locked at the initialization value throughout the entire simulation duration. Consequently, the per capita hunting pressure and spatial distribution of foragers remain unchanged, providing a static baseline against which prey population dynamics can be measured without confounding variation in predator density.
