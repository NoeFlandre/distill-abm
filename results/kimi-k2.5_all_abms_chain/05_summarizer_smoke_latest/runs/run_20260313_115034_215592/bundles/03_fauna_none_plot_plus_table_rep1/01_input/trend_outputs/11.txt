The total energy of male animals exhibits a deterministic downward trajectory from an initial value of 12,500 energy units, ultimately reaching complete depletion at approximately step 2,284 across all 40 simulation replicates. The decline follows a nonlinear pattern characterized by three distinct phases: an initial rapid decrease, a prolonged intermediate phase with decelerating reduction rates, and a terminal convergence to zero.

In the initial phase spanning approximately the first 350 steps, the aggregate energy plummets sharply from 12,500 to roughly 4,000 units, representing a 68% reduction. This phase includes a minor oscillatory plateau around step 65, where values temporarily stabilize near 11,600 before resuming descent. Statistical change points at steps 125 and 350 mark transitions in the rate of decline, with the steepest reduction occurring in the first window (slope of -14.8 energy units per step), followed by an accelerated decline in the second window (slope of -28.1 units per step) between steps 232 and 463.

The intermediate phase, extending from approximately step 350 to step 875, shows continued degradation but with markedly reduced rates (slopes ranging from -10.7 to -0.8 units per step). During this interval, recorded values cluster between 4,000 and 1,000 units, with substantial inter-run variability emerging. Inflection points at steps 316, 453, 516, 759, and 824 indicate frequent adjustments in the curvature of the decline trajectory, coinciding with localized peaks and valleys in the range of 3,800 to 4,000 units.

The terminal phase, beginning around step 875, is characterized by values below 1,000 units approaching asymptotic zero. Mann-Kendall analysis reveals that while windows 1 through 6 (covering steps 0-1,391) show statistically significant monotonic decreases (tau values ranging from -1.0 to -0.477), later windows exhibit mixed behavior including a significant increasing trend in window 7 (steps 1,392-1,623, tau = 0.548) and weak fluctuations near zero in subsequent windows, reflecting stochastic dynamics of small remnant populations.

Superimposed on this long-term decline are regular oscillations with a dominant period of 63 steps, occurring consistently across indices 55 through 150 and beyond. These cyclic variations, with amplitudes diminishing as the base level decreases, likely correspond to seasonal energy fluctuations or demographic cycles synchronized to the model's seasonal parameters (seasonality set to 0.8 with 365 ticks per year).

The mean energy across all steps is 1,984 units, though the median of 134.5 indicates right-skewed distribution due to the prolonged terminal tail. Standard deviation of 3,478 reflects both the high initial values and the increasing dispersion across replicates during the extinction process, with some populations persisting until step 2,300 while others reach depletion by step 600-800. The final extinction event occurs at step 2,310 according to change point analysis, with complete energy depletion recorded by step 2,312.
