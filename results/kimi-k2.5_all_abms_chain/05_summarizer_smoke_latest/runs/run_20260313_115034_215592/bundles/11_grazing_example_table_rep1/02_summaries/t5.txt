Model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making. The model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions. The average household risk attitude across the population begins at approximately 0.007. This metric increases rapidly, ascending to a maximum value of approximately 0.38. Following this peak at step 33, the average risk attitude enters a prolonged decline, decreasing steadily through approximately step 65 to values around 0.27. Behavioral adaptation fosters long-term ecological improvement. The average total livestock per household begins at 90 animals. Over the first 20 time steps, the population undergoes a sharp decline, dropping rapidly as households destock. Inequality in healthy livestock distribution initiates at effectively zero. Over the 100-step simulation period, this metric exhibits a consistent upward trajectory, culminating at approximately 0.073. Initial increases occur gradually, with inflection points appearing at steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, and 37. The number of households adhering to the E-RO behavioral strategy begins at 20. An initial increase occurs during the first eight time steps, reaching a maximum of approximately 21 households. The population undergoes a sustained decline lasting from roughly time step 11 through step 65.
