The total energy contained within the megaherbivore population begins at approximately 25,000 energy units across all simulation runs. Over the first 500 time steps, this metric undergoes a precipitous decline, with most trajectories falling to values between 0 and 8,000 units by step 500. This rapid decrease reflects intensive exploitation pressure from the initial cohort of 20 human foragers operating with food storage capabilities and immortal status.

During the interval between steps 300 and 600, several trajectories exhibit temporary stabilization or minor oscillations, with energy levels plateauing between 5,000 and 8,000 units before resuming their downward trajectory. These intermittent pauses likely reflect periods where hunter effort temporarily decreases due to accumulated energy stores reaching satiation thresholds, or brief episodes where surviving animals capitalize on seasonal vegetation pulses to rebuild energy reserves.

Between steps 500 and 1,000, the remaining populations continue their decline toward zero, with the majority of runs reaching negligible energy levels (below 1,000 units) by step 1,000. Substantial variation exists in the exact timing of collapse: some trajectories reach effectively zero by step 400, while others persist with low but measurable energy levels extending out to steps 1,500 through 2,000. This divergence in crash timing suggests high sensitivity to stochastic encounter rates between hunters and prey once population densities drop below critical thresholds.

By step 2,000, all simulation trajectories converge toward zero total energy, indicating complete or near-complete depletion of the megaherbivore population. The coefficient of variation increases dramatically during the middle phase (steps 300-800), indicating that while the initial decline is highly consistent across runs, the system's behavior becomes increasingly unpredictable as populations approach critical minimum viable densities. The inevitable convergence to zero across all runs suggests that with immortal hunters possessing food storage and a starting prey-to-hunter ratio of 12.5:1, the system deterministically progresses toward megafaunal extinction regardless of stochastic variation in the collapse trajectory.
