**Implications for decision makers:** The trajectory suggests an approximate six-year window to extinction under current parameters, indicating that interventions must be implemented during the early decline phase to prevent irreversible collapse. The brief recovery observed in year four represents a potential intervention window where management actions could capitalize on natural recruitment pulses, though the overall pattern indicates that current hunting intensity exceeds the demographic replacement capacity of the male cohort. Additionally, the 63-tick seasonal oscillation suggests that management strategies synchronized with environmental cycles may optimize short-term outcomes, but seasonal timing alone appears insufficient to counteract the prevailing extinction pressure.
