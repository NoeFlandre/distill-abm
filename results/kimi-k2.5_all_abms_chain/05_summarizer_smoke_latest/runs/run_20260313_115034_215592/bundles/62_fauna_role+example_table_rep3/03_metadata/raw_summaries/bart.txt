 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change . Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances . Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores .  The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period of 2,312 ticks . The cumulative hunting mortality of male megaherbivores begins at zero and undergoes rapid accumulation during the initial 625 time steps, characterized by steeply declining rates of increase . The trajectory of energy depletion is characterized by an accelerating rate of decline .  The cumulative mortality of postpartum female ungulates initiates at zero and undergoes rapid accumulation during the initial phase . The series reaches a maximum value of approximately 123.7 kills near step 1826, after which it exhibits a slight declining trend through the remainder of the simulation, concluding at 123.0 by step 2312 .  The number of births among the prey population commences at zero and remains negligible through approximately the first 600 ticks, corresponding to the lag period before the initial cohort of 250 ungulates reaches reproductive maturity . Around tick 725, the system undergoes a critical transition marked by the first significant change point, after which birth rates enter a sustained growth phase .  The female megaherbivore population begins at 125 individuals and undergoes a deterministic collapse to extinction by tick 2311 . The trajectory is characterized by an initial phase of rapid decline, followed by extended low-density oscillations, culminating in irreversible extinction after approximately 6.3 years of simulated time . Males (30 energy units) relative to females, combined with the foragers' immortality and food storage capabilities, creates sustained predation pressure independent of prey density .  The total energy available within the male ungulate population initiates at 12,500 units and follows a complex, oscillating trajectory toward complete extirpation, reaching zero at tick 2,284 . The terminal phase (post-tick 2,000) exhibits final attrition to collapse of the male subpopulation under sustained predation pressure .  The combined energy stored within the prey population initiates at approximately 16,667 units, reflecting the starting cohort of 250 animals . This aggregate energy reservoir undergoes a precipitous decline throughout the first 350 time steps, contracting to below 4,200 units by tick 385 . This initial collapse is characterized by steep monotonic decrease punctuated by high-amplitude oscillations .  The dynamics exhibit a dominant oscillation period of approximately 63 time steps . The simulation concludes with coverage at approximately 33%, essentially unchanged from its starting value . This equilibrium indicates that the rapid vegetation regrowth mechanism successfully balances consumption pressures from the megaherbivore population .
