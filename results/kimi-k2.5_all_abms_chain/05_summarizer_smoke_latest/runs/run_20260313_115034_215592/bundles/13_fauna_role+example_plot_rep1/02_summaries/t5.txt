The model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape. Each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for the herbivores. The model specifically incorporates sexual dimorphism. Animals-gain-from-food ranges from 0 to 100, set at 1.5, representing energy obtained from grazing. Threshold for starvation mortality, animals-starvation-threshold, ranges between 0 and 100. Movement costs energy, with animals-movement-cost ranging from 1 to 10. Between steps 500 and 1000, the rate of increase decelerates substantially across all runs. Cumulative kill counts rise from their step-500 values to ranges between approximately 100 and 125. The slowing accumulation rate suggests diminishing returns as the male population becomes depleted. Trajectories level off as early as step 600–800, while others continue to show slow, incremental increases until approximately step 1500–2000. The final plateau indicates that the pool of postpartum females available to hunters has been effectively exhausted, or that the remaining individuals have become inaccessible. The majority of simulation runs conclude with zero males between steps 1000 and 1500. Some runs exhibit prolonged persistence, with small populations of 1 to 5 individuals surviving until approximately step 2200 before finally reaching zero. Demographic stochasticity can allow small groups of males to evade hunters for extended periods before eventual extinction. From 800 time steps onward, the total energy of male animals approaches zero. Most simulation runs reach complete depletion between 1,000 and 1,500 steps. Trajectories maintain trace amounts of energy until approximately 2,000 to 2,200 steps before ultimately declining to zero - a prolonged tail represents isolated surviving individuals or small groups of males. Global asymptotic approach to zero energy demonstrates that the prey populations undergo systematic collapse rather than achieving stable coexistence with human hunters. The percentage of vegetated landscape patches begins at approximately 33%, consistent with the initial grass coverage parameter. This initial stabilization period reflects the establishment of grazing pressure from the founding herbivore population of 250 individuals as they begin consuming vegetation across the landscape.
