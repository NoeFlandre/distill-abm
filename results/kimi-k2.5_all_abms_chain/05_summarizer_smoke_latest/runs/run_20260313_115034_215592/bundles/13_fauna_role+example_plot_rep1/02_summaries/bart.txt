Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances. Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores. Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius. The number of human foragers remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period. Total energy available to the forager population begins at approximately 1,500 to 2,000 energy units across all simulation runs. The cumulative number of female prey animals killed by human foragers exhibits a characteristic saturation pattern across all simulation runs. During the initial phase, roughly spanning the first 200 to 500 steps, the count increases rapidly in a near-linear fashion, rising sharply from 0 to approximately 80–100 individuals. By step 1,000, the range narrows to approximately 110–125 cumulative kills, indicating that differential early hunting success becomes less relevant. The number of female prey animals begins at approximately 125 individuals at the start of the simulation. By step 1000, the majority of populations have reached zero, while a subset maintains small numbers ranging from approximately 5 to 15 individuals. In the most rapid decline scenarios, the female population collapses to near zero levels within approximately 400 to 400 steps. The total megaherbivore population begins at 250 individuals across all simulation replicates. A consistent downward trajectory emerges immediately, with populations declining rapidly during the initial 500 time steps. By step 1000, the majority of replicates have reached extinction or near-extinction states, with fewer than 5 individuals remaining. The total energy contained within all prey populations initiates at approximately 25,000 energy units across simulation runs. During the initial phase spanning roughly the first 300 to 500 time steps, this aggregate energy undergoes a precipitous decline. By step 500, most trajectories have diminished to below 5,000 units, with numerous simulations approaching minimal energy states near zero. The coupled herbivore-vegetation system has achieved a resilient oscillatory state where seasonal depressions in grass availability are regularly compensated by rapid regrowth within the spatial constraints of the initial vegetation pattern.
