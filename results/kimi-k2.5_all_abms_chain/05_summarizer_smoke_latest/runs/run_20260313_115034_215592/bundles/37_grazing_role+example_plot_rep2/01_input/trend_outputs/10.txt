The number of households following the E-RO (non-learning) strategy begins at 20 across all simulation runs, consistent with the initial parameter configuration. During the first 10 to 20 time steps, the trajectories diverge rapidly: several simulations exhibit sharp increases, with counts rising to between 30 and 42 households by approximately step 15, while other simulations show immediate declines to fewer than 10 households. This early divergence suggests that stochastic rainfall variability and initial neighborhood configurations create strong path dependencies for non-learning agents.

Between steps 20 and 40, the simulations maintain distinct trajectories. One simulation sustains elevated counts of 38-40 E-RO households through step 40 before undergoing a gradual decline to approximately 23-24 by step 100. Other simulations stabilize at intermediate levels: for instance, one trajectory fluctuates between 25-27 households from step 40 onward, while another settles in the range of 16-19 households. Several simulations exhibit continuous decline during this middle phase, with counts dropping below 10 households.

From step 60 to 100, the variability across simulations persists but with reduced volatility. Multiple simulations converge to low abundance levels: one trajectory reaches zero by step 65 and remains there, indicating complete exit of E-RO households from that system realization, while others stabilize at counts of 2-5, 8-10, or 12-15 households. By the final time step, the distribution of outcomes spans from 0 to approximately 26 households, with clusters observed around 0, 3, 8, 13, and 24-26.

This high variance in outcomes indicates that the persistence of non-learning households is highly sensitive to environmental stochasticity and local dynamics. The collapses to zero or near-zero levels in several simulations suggest that without adaptive learning mechanisms, rule-of-thumb decision-makers struggle to cope with the significant rainfall variability (rain-std = 100) and are vulnerable to exit from the grazing system. Conversely, the maintenance of E-RO populations at or above initial levels in other simulations suggests that under certain favorable environmental sequences or neighbor configurations, the baseline strategy can achieve long-term persistence despite the absence of active learning.
