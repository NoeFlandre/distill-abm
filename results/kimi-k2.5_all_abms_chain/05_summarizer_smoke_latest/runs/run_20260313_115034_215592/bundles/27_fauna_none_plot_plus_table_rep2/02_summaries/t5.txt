The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics. The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20. Initial foragers set to 20 and both mortality and recruitment eliminated from the agent dynamics. The hunter population functions as a static experimental parameter rather than a responsive agent population. The cumulative number of females killed begins at zero across all simulation runs. Between approximately steps 1000 and 1500, the rate of increase decelerates markedly. This phase reflects active hunting pressure as foragers encounter and pursue female prey. The cumulative mortality of megaherbivore prey follows a sigmoidal trajectory over the simulation duration. Mortality accumulates rapidly during the initial approximately 500 steps (roughly 1.4 years), rising sharply to reach between 200 and 230 cumulative deaths. This phase contains distinct inflection points at steps 125, 250, 375, and 500, marking discrete transitions in mortality rates. The variability in extinction timing varies from 400 steps to over 2200 steps across individual simulation runs. The population follows a persistent decreasing trajectory with a mean abundance of 16.48 individuals. Initial phase from step 0 to 475 exhibits the most rapid population reduction. The aggregate energy of male animals initiates at 12,500 units and undergoes a protracted decline toward extinction over approximately 2,300 time steps. Between steps 125 and 350, the rate of decline intensifies significantly, with statistical slopes reaching approximately -28 units per step. The metric undergoes a steep decrease from its maximum value. Between steps 350 and 500, the descent temporarily moderates. Localized fluctuations occurring between 3,700 and 4,100 units. A secondary rapid decline occurs between steps 500 and 625.
