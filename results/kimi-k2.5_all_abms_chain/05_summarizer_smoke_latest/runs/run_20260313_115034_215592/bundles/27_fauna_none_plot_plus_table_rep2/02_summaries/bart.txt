The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges. The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics. The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period. Total energy available to forager bands decreases continuously and monotonically from an initial value of 2000 to a terminal value of approximately -38230 over 2312 simulation steps. The cumulative number of females killed begins at zero across all simulation runs. During the initial approximately 1000 time steps, the metric exhibits a steep, stepwise increase. This phase reflects active hunting pressure as foragers encounter and pursue female prey. Between approximately steps 1000 and 1500, the rate of increase decelerates markedly. By approximately step 1000 to 1200, the cumulative kill count approaches asymptotic levels. The population of male animals initiated at 125 individuals across all 40 simulation replicates and followed trajectories of progressive depletion toward elimination. The collapse occurred in distinct phases demarcated by change points at steps 125, 250, 375, 500, 625, and 875. The aggregate mean descended from 125 to 0, reaching complete extinction by step 2284. The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model was created by 20 immortal human hunters. The aggregate energy of male animals initiates at 12,500 units and undergoes a protracted decline toward extinction over approximately 2,300 time steps. The initial phase (0–125 steps) demonstrates rapid depletion from maximum values. By tick 2311, the population reaches zero, indicating complete extinction of the prey species. The total energy of female animals initiates at approximately 12,500 energy units. Over the course of 2,311 steps (approximately 6.3 years) this metric undergoes a continuous decline, ultimately reaching zero, which indicates complete extinction of the female population. The percentage of landscape patches vegetated with grass initiates at approximately 33.0002%, consistent with the initial grass proportion parameter of 0.33%. During the first 176 steps, the metric remains relatively stable near this initial value before entering a period of decline. At step 365, the percentage undergoes a precipitous drop to the recorded minimum of 24.9108%, representing a decline of roughly 8.1 percentage points.
