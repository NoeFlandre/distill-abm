Summary Summary Summary After step 40, the system enters a degradation phase characterized by rapid increase in the number of animals per household. This degradation phase reaches minimum values ranging between 80,000 and 88,000 depending on the specific simulation trajectory, with most runs stabilizing near 82,000 to 85,000 around year 30 to 40. For decision makers, this variability implies that promoting exclusive reliance on traditional non-adaptive grazing practices may lead to unstable community structures, with some realizations resulting in near-complete loss of this population segment while others maintain moderate representation. Interventions supporting adaptive learning mechanisms may be necessary to ensure more predictable household persistence in variable rangeland environments.
