**Implications for Decision Makers:**

The data reveals a system characterized by high resilience but periodic instability. While the 3-tick regrowth rate enables rapid recovery from severe depletion events, the recurrent crashes—particularly the initial deep trough at step 250—indicate that temporary bottlenecks in fodder availability are inevitable even with relatively modest herbivore populations (250 individuals). Decision makers should recognize that fast vegetation regrowth does not prevent acute resource scarcity events, which could trigger starvation mortality (threshold 25) or force energy-expensive long-distance movement by herds. The secondary oscillations around step 700 suggest that seasonal environmental variation creates predictable windows of resource stress that may compound with demographic factors such as calving intervals. Management strategies should account for these cyclical vulnerabilities, potentially by monitoring vegetation thresholds around steps 250 and 700 to anticipate population stress, rather than assuming stable resource availability based on high average green patch percentages.
