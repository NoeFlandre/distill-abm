the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 . from initialization at step 0 through approximately step 2200, there is no observable fluctuation, increase, or decrease in the hunter population . however, hunting pressure remains constant on a per-capita basis . the asymptotic plateau at approximately 120-125 cumulative deaths corresponds to the near-complete depletion of the female segment of the prey population . the saturation of the mortality counts indicates that foragers hunt females to effective extinction, after which continued accumulation of female kills ceases . the cumulative number of prey deaths increases monotonically throughout the simulation period across all repeated runs . the temporal progression exhibits three distinct phases: an initial rapid accumulation phase spanning roughly the first 500 time steps . a transitional slowing phase between approximately 500 and 1000 time steps, where the rate of increase diminishes substantially as values approach 200 to 240 . between steps 500 and 1000, populations that persist continue to decline, with energy values typically falling to ranges between 0 and approximately 5,000 units . by step 1500, most populations had reached complete or near-complete extinction (0-5 animals), though isolated runs maintained small remnant populations of 1-3 individuals persisting until approximately step 2000 . all simulation runs show total female animal energy converging at or very near zero . the combined total energy of prey animals begins at approximately 25,000 units at step 0. During the initial phase, the metric undergoes a steep and consistent decline . most replicates lose between 60% and 80% of initial energy, falling to values ranging from approximately 5,000 to 10,000 .
