This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model, designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems. While the original RAGE model focused on common property grazing with mobile households, this extension applies to smallholder farming contexts where pastures are private property and households remain stationary on their patches. The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes central to adaptive co-management. It implements three new behavioral types: E-RO (baseline behavior), E-RO-SL1 (social learning), and E-LBD (learning-by-doing). Agents are characterized by heterogeneity in uncertainty management, represented by an r-parameter (household-risk-att) that captures factors such as sensing uncertainty, information noise, and risk attitude. The model comprises two main sub-models: Vegetation dynamics and Household decision-making.
