 The agent-based model represents smallholder farmers managing private pastures in a grazing system . Each year, farmers observe their pasture condition and neighbors' situations to decide how many animals to keep . The model explores how different learning strategies affect long-term outcomes for both the land and the farmers' livelihoods .  The mean household risk attitude across the population begins at approximately zero (0.0074), indicating that initial stocking decisions align closely with formal recommendations . During the first thirty-three steps, the system undergoes a rapid and statistically significant increase, reaching a maximum value of 0.3814 . This upward trend suggests that households initially adopt increasingly conservative strategies, potentially understocking their pastures .  The mean green biomass begins at 45000 units at the outset of the simulation . During the initial 4 time steps, it exhibits a marginal increase to approximately 45261, followed by a sustained decline reaching a minimum value of approximately 42057 at step 27 . After step 55, the growth rate moderates progressively through step 98, indicating the system is converging toward a steady-state equilibrium with significantly higher biomass than initial conditions .  Livestock holdings plus cumulative destocking per household begins at 90 animals, reflecting the initial endowment for each farming family . Over the first 20 time steps, this metric declines sharply and significantly, dropping by approximately 50 percent as households engage in aggressive destocking . This rapid reduction reflects the immediate ecological pressure exerted by the initial high stocking rate of 90 animals .  The diffusion of social learning among households initiates from zero and undergoes rapid acceleration within the first 7 steps, reaching approximately 20 households on average by step 20 . Growth rates decelerate substantially after step 28, with notable inflection points at steps 30, 33, and 38 marking progressively slower adoption as the system approaches equilibrium .  The average population settles at approximately 14.7 households, representing a net reduction of roughly 26% from the initial cohort . The researchers say the persistence of non-learning households is highly sensitive to specific sequences of environmental conditions or stochastic events . They say static, rule-of-thumb decision strategies are less viable than adaptive approaches .
