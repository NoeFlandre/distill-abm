**Implications for Decision Makers**

The pronounced initial lag of approximately two years before measurable recruitment highlights the extreme vulnerability of slow-reproducing megaherbivore populations to overharvesting before they can replace losses. The growth-collapse pattern demonstrates that hunting pressure thresholds exist beyond which populations cannot sustain themselves despite initial reproductive buffering. The high variance across simulations ( peaking at 5.6 but ending at 1.0) suggests that extinction outcomes are probabilistic rather than deterministic, requiring precautionary management approaches that account for worst-case scenarios rather than mean expectations. The identification of critical change points at roughly years 2, 3.6, and 5.1 (assuming 365 steps/year) provides discrete time horizons for conservation intervention before irreversible demographic collapse occurs.
