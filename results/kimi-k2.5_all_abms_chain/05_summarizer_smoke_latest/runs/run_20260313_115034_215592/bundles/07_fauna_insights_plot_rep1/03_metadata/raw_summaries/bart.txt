 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges . The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics .  The population of active foragers maintains a constant value of 20 agents throughout the entire simulation period from step 0 through step 2200 . No variation in forager count is observed at any temporal interval . Predation pressure operates as a static variable independent of simulation dynamics .  The cumulative count of females killed begins at zero across all simulation runs and exhibits a three-phase pattern of accumulation . By step 200, accumulated kills range from 20 to 60 females across runs; by step 500, values diverge further, spanning approximately 80 to 120 females killed. By step 800, approximately half of the simulation runs have stabilized, while the remainder continue gradual increases .  These dynamics reflect the optimal foraging behavior of hunter agents who actively target postpartum females due to their favorable energetic return . The rapid saturation at approximately 100-120 kills suggests that the initial population of reproductive females (estimated at roughly 125 individuals) is effectively exhausted through hunting pressure within approximately 500-1000 time steps (1.4 to 2.7 years)  The number of male prey animals begins at approximately 125 individuals across all simulation runs . Over the course of the simulations, this population undergoes a continuous monotonic decline, ultimately reaching effective extinction (zero or near-zero individuals) In the slowest declining runs, small numbers of males persist until approximately 1500-2200 individuals .  Given the calibration of 365 ticks per year, this extinction dynamic unfolds over approximately 4 to 5.5 years . The most rapid phase of depletion occurs within the first 300 to 500 steps, during which the majority of simulations plummet from initial values to below 2,000 units . A minority of runs exhibit prolonged persistence, maintaining small populations of 1 to 5 individuals .  Researchers: Persistent hunting pressure drives female megaherbivore populations toward inevitable demographic collapse . By the conclusion of the observation period, all simulation runs have converged to zero or near-zero total prey energy, with complete collapse occurring in all observed trajectories . Decision makers should recognize that the current hunter density and foraging efficiency create a "extinction vortex"
