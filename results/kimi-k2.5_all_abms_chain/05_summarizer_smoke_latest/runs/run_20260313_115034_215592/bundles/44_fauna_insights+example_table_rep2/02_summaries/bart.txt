The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges. The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics. The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period. This value shows no deviation from the initial time step through to the conclusion of the observation window, maintaining perfect stability with zero variability across all recorded ticks. The trajectory demonstrates that a cohort of 20 immortal foragers can exhaust the harvestable female megafauna population within approximately five years under the specified life-history and environmental parameters. The cumulative number of births within the prey population initiates at zero and remains negligible through approximately the first 700 time steps. The terminal phase indicates the operation of an extinction vortex where demographic stochasticity and Allee effects overwhelm recovery potential once abundance falls below viability thresholds.
