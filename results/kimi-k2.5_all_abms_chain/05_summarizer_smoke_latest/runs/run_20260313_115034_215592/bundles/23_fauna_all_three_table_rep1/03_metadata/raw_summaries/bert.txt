the megafaunal hunting pressure model ( mhpm ) is an agent - based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to the combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. the human foragers operate according to dynamic optimal foraging theory, meaning they constantly evaluate whether pursuing a particular animal is worth the energy expenditure based on the animal's food value, the difficulty of processing it, and how much energy the hunter currently has stored. the model can simulate seasonal environments where grass availability fluctuates throughout the year, and users can control the proportion of the landscape that initially contains vegetation as well as how quickly that vegetation returns after being eaten. maximum - lifespan ranges from 0 to energy surpluses. three boolean switches control vegetation behavior : grass - growth is set to true, enabling vegetation regrowth ; keep - initial - grass - pattern is set to true, restricting
