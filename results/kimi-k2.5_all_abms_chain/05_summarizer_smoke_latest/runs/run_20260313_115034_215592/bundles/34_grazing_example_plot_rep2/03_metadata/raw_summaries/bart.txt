 This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . It is designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems . The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes .  The mean household risk attitude begins at values clustered near zero across all simulation runs . During the initial phase from steps 0 to 20, the metric undergoes rapid divergence, with trajectories spreading across a range from approximately -0.2 to 0.8 . The mean reserve biomass initiates at approximately 90,000 units .  The average number of livestock destocked begins at approximately zero and rises sharply within the first 5 time steps, reaching peak values around 7.5 . The recovery likely reflects the dominance of vegetation regrowth mechanisms over grazing pressure, possibly enhanced by adaptive learning behaviors adjusting livestock placement strategies as households observe declining pasture conditions .  The Gini coefficient measuring the distribution of healthy livestock across households initializes at 0.000 . During the first 40 steps, inequality increases gradually across all simulation trajectories . This early phase reflects initial differentiation in household outcomes as agents apply heterogeneous decision-making strategies .  The population of E-RO households initiates at 20 across all simulation runs . During the initial 20 time steps, rapid divergence occurs as the count spreads across a broad spectrum, reaching maxima of approximately 40-42 households in several runs . This early volatility indicates intense competition and diffusion dynamics among behavioral strategies .
