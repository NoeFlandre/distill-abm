model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . it introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making . the model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions . the distribution encompasses negative values, reflecting risk-averse orientations, and positive values, indicating risk-seeking propensities . the metric captures fixed initial parameters characterizing household uncertainty management and risk attitudes at the outset . trajectories spread across a range from approximately -0.2 to 0.8 . values experience a short period of initial vegetation growth before significant grazing commences . by step 35, biomass undergoes a sustained decline as livestock consumption impacts pasture reserves . most simulations bottom out near 40,000–42,000 while a minority of runs maintain higher levels around 44,000–45,000 . by step 100, most trajectories converge to values between 0 and 0.8, with a small subset of simulation runs stabilizing near 1 . the variance across simulation runs is pronounced in the first 30 steps . some runs maintain destocking levels above 2 until step 25 while others drop below 0.5 by step 15 . the average cumulative number of livestock destocked across households begins at zero and rises rapidly during the first 20 time steps . between time steps 20 and 40, the trajectories diverge significantly . some simulation runs exhibit a marked deceleration, with cumulative destocking stabilizing at values between approximately 58 and 85 for the remainder of the 100-step period .
