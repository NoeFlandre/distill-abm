The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics. The number of human hunter-gatherer bands active in the simulation remains constant at 20 throughout the entire temporal sequence. This value shows no deviation from its initial state across all observed time steps. The initial population of 20 foragers established at the simulation's outset persists indefinitely without attrition, recruitment, or fluctuation.
