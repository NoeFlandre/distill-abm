 This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . It is designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems . The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes .  The average reserve biomass begins at approximately 90,000 units . During the initial 20 time steps, the biomass undergoes a marked decline across all simulation runs . The prevalence of high positive deviations indicates a systemic tendency toward overstocking, while the subset of runs exhibiting severe understocking suggests that some learning trajectories lead to overly conservative strategies .  The metric begins at approximately 90 units at step 0 . A rapid decline occurs over the first 20 steps, with values falling to roughly 45-50 by step 20 . The decline continues more gradually until reaching a minimum of approximately 40 units between steps 35 and 45 . From approximately step 40 through step 100, the system stabilizes at low destocking levels .  The data tracks the diffusion of social learning behaviors across 60 households over 100 time steps across multiple simulation runs . The Gini coefficient measuring inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock begins at 0 across all simulations, indicating perfect equality in initial livestock endowments . During the first 20 time steps, inequality remains minimal with values staying below 0.015 as all households maintain similar herd health status . During step 20, inequality begins to gradually increase, with Gini coefficients rising to the range of 0.  A subset of scenarios exhibits plateau behavior, stabilizing around 58 to 60 head shortly after step 40 and maintaining this level through step 100 . Majority of trajectories demonstrate sustained, approximately linear accumulation of destocked animals throughout the entire 100-step period, with slopes varying from moderate increases to steep escalations .
