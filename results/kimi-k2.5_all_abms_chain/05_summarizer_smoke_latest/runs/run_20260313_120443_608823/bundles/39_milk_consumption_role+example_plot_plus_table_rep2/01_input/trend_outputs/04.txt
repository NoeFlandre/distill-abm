The cumulative count of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk selections begins at zero and exhibits monotonic growth throughout the 33 time steps, terminating at approximately 25,644 total choices. During the initial phase spanning roughly the first ten steps, the accumulation accelerates substantially, with the rate of increase rising from approximately 201 choices per step to over 927 choices per step. This early acceleration likely corresponds to the period where agents' cognitive processing of health and environmental attributes began overcoming the strong initial habit strength associated with whole milk consumption, combined with the gradual activation of social influence mechanisms within the Watts-Strogatz network structure.

Following this establishment period, the growth transitions to a sustained linear regime from approximately step 10 through step 29, maintaining a consistent slope near 935 choices per step. This steady linear accumulation suggests that the system reached a diffusion equilibrium where the probability of social interactions and susceptibility to influence generated a constant velocity of habit-breaking and alternative adoption across the population of 1000 agents.

Statistical confirmation across 30 simulation runs reveals low variance throughout most of the series, indicating robust reproducibility of these dynamics. The consistent upward trend across all examined time windows, with Kendall's tau values of 1.000 and significant Mann-Kendall statistics, confirms the reliability of the monotonic increase, though deviations become slightly more pronounced in the final steps.

In the terminal phase, specifically between steps 27 and 32, minor oscillatory patterns emerge alongside a detected change point at step 32 and an inflection point at step 31. These late-stage dynamics may reflect the cumulative nature of the metric approaching population-level saturation, or the finite memory lifetime creating boundary effects as the simulation concludes, potentially constraining the continued linear extrapolation of adoption choices as the system approaches the 2005 endpoint.
