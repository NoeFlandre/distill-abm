The data indicate that inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households emerges consistently throughout the 100-step simulation period, beginning from a state of perfect equality (Gini coefficient approximately 0.000) when all 60 households initialize with 90 animals each. The aggregate metric increases monotonically to a terminal value of 0.0726, with statistical significance confirmed across all rolling time windows (p < 0.001). 

The temporal dynamics exhibit distinct phases. During the initial phase (approximately steps 0–20), inequality accumulates gradually, remaining below 0.015 for most trajectories. Notable inflection points occur at steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, and 37, indicating successive accelerations in the rate of divergence. A structural shift is detected at step 51, followed by another at step 71, marking transitions to regimes of more rapid inequality growth. By step 60, the central tendency reaches approximately 0.030–0.045, after which trajectories diverge substantially across the 30 simulation runs. 

In the final phase (steps 71–100), the distribution of outcomes spreads widely, with terminal values ranging from approximately 0.030 to 0.095. This heterogeneity across runs reflects the compounding effects of spatial variation in pasture productivity, stochastic rainfall (mean 200, standard deviation 100), and the three distinct decision-making strategies employed by household groups (baseline rule-of-thumb, learning-by-doing, and social learning). Oscillation indices concentrated between steps 78 and 93, with a dominant period of 49 steps, suggest cyclic fluctuations in inequality levels superimposed on the upward trend during the late simulation phase. 

The median value of 0.0229 and mean of 0.0302 across the full time series indicate that inequality remains moderate for the majority of the simulation, with significant divergence occurring primarily in the latter third of the time horizon as differential management outcomes compound across the risk-heterogeneous agent population.
