Across the ensemble of repeated simulations, trajectories demonstrate high consistency with minor variations, suggesting that both model variants employing different disposition mechanisms produce convergent macro-level consumption patterns despite potential micro-level behavioral differences.

**Implications for Decision Makers:**

The distinct two-phase adoption pattern—rapid initial growth followed by a prolonged plateau and renewed late-stage increase—indicates that public health interventions targeting dietary transitions require multi-decade persistence. The extended plateau spanning approximately 15 steps (equivalent to roughly 15 years in the historical timeline) demonstrates that entrenched consumption habits and social inertia can stall progress for significant periods even after initial rapid adoption. Decision makers should anticipate these stagnation phases and avoid premature termination of health promotion campaigns, as the final growth phase suggests that sustained messaging and social norm activation eventually yields substantial additional gains among resistant populations. Resource allocation should favor long-term, consistent engagement strategies over short-term intensive bursts to overcome the cumulative effects of habit strength and cognitive dissonance thresholds identified in the model parameters.
