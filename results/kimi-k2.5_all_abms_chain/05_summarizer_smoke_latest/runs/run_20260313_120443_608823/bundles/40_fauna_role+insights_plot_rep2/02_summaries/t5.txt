The model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape. Each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for the herbivores. The model specifically incorporates sexual dimorphism. Animals-gain-from-food ranges from 0 to 100, set at 1.5, representing energy obtained from grazing. Threshold for starvation mortality, animals-starvation-threshold, ranges between 0 and 100. Movement costs energy, with animals-movement-cost ranging from 1 to 10. The linear decline suggests that without external supplementation or behavioral adaptation, the energy reservoir would theoretically deplete indefinitely. Decision makers should note that sustainable coexistence may require reduced hunter numbers, expanded foraging radii beyond the current setting of 6 patches. By step 1000, the vast majority of replicates have ceased accumulating additional female kills. The narrow clustering of terminal values around 110-125 kills suggests convergence toward a hard ceiling representing the effective extirpation of the female subpopulation. This saturation point corresponds to the elimination of nearly the entire breeding female population from the system. Cumulative births remain at zero for an initial latent period of approximately 730 steps. This quiescence reflects the biological constraint that females in the initial cohort of 250 animals require approximately two years to produce their first offspring. The trajectories exhibit substantial heterogeneity in both magnitude and duration. Pronounced variation in collapse timing suggests short-term population stability does not indicate long-term viability. The population initiates at approximately 125 male animals and undergoes a monotonic decline throughout the temporal domain, reaching zero individuals in all replicates by step 2,200. Approximately half of the simulation runs have reached total male energy levels below 500, with many approaching zero. A small number of runs show persistence of minimal male energy (under 500 units) extending to approximately step 2,000-2,200 before final depletion. The simulations consistently demonstrate rapid collapse of megaherbivore population energy under the specified hunting pressure. At initialization (step 0), total prey energy begins at approximately 25,000 units across all replicates. A steep decline characterizes the first 300 to 500 time steps, with energy decreasing from 25,000 to values between 5,000 and 8,000. Data indicate megaherbivore populations and vegetation resources can achieve long-term stability even under substantial hunting pressure. Rapid vegetation recovery rate (3-day regrowth) is critical for system resilience. Short-term monitoring may misleadingly suggest ecosystem collapse.
