The cumulative mortality of postpartum female ungulates initiates at zero and follows a saturating trajectory characterized by three distinct phases. During the initial approximately 500 ticks, rapid accumulation occurs, with the metric rising sharply to values between 60 and 100 individuals across simulation runs. This transitions into a deceleration phase extending to roughly tick 1000, wherein the rate of increase diminishes considerably as the series approaches asymptotic levels.

Beyond tick 1000, the cumulative count stabilizes in a plateau phase, with individual simulation runs reaching terminal values ranging between approximately 100 and 122 postpartum females. The mean cumulative mortality across the 40 simulation series reaches 117 individuals by tick 2312, with the maximum observed value of 119 occurring at tick 1826. The overall temporal mean stands at 102.44, reflecting the extended initial period of low cumulative mortality.

Statistical trend analysis confirms significant monotonic increases throughout the early and middle phases, with Kendall's tau values of 1.000 in the first two windows (ticks 0–463), gradually declining to 0.732 by window 5 (ticks 928–1159) and 0.174 by window 6 (ticks 1160–1391). Window 7 (ticks 1392–1623) shows no statistically significant trend, indicating the onset of saturation. While window 8 (ticks 1624–1855) resumes slight increasing tendencies, window 9 (ticks 1856–2087) exhibits a statistically significant decreasing trend, likely reflecting stochastic variation around the asymptotic maximum rather than actual reduction in the cumulative count.

The series exhibits periodic oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks, concentrated in the late phase between ticks 2170 and 2265. Structural change points occur at ticks 475, 625, and 850, marking transitions in accumulation dynamics. Multiple inflection points at ticks 245, 262, 285, 1180, 1311, 1394, 1404, 1485, and subsequent intervals indicate shifts in the rate of increase. Substantial variability exists across the 40 simulation runs regarding both the timing of saturation and final cumulative totals, with some runs plateauing near tick 1000 while others continue accumulating until approximately tick 2000.

**Implications for decision makers**: The consistent saturation of postpartum female mortality at levels between 100 and 119 individuals suggests that hunting pressure under these parameter configurations rapidly depletes the reproductive segment of the population, with the majority of cumulative mortality occurring within the first 500–1000 ticks (approximately 1.4–2.7 simulation years). The substantial variation in final mortality levels across runs indicates high sensitivity to stochastic processes, complicating predictive management. The cessation of new postpartum female mortality after the plateau phase implies either functional extirpation of this demographic from hunting pressure or a population restructuring that makes these individuals unavailable to hunters, representing a critical threshold for population viability that management strategies must address before the rapid accumulation phase concludes.
