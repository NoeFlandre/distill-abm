The simulation of mixed behavioral populations (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under private property grazing conditions with stationary households reveals distinct phases in destocking dynamics over 100 yearly time steps. At initialization, the average destocking begins at 0.0 livestock units. An immediate sharp escalation occurs within the first three time steps, reaching a maximum average destocking of 6.61 livestock units at time step 3, indicating substantial initial livestock removal as the 60 households adjust from the initial herd size of 90 animals per household toward sustainable levels.

Following this initial peak, a pronounced declining trend emerges through approximately time step 30, characterized by significant oscillations. Secondary peaks occur at time step 9 (3.37), time step 12 (2.74), time step 17 (1.47), time step 19 (1.38), and time step 22 (1.24), with corresponding valleys at time step 8 (3.33), time step 11 (2.47), time step 16 (1.45), time step 18 (0.90), and time step 21 (0.84). These oscillations reflect year-to-year adjustments as heterogeneous households with varying risk attitudes (initial r-parameter of 0.5) and learning capabilities adapt their stocking decisions to biomass availability under variable rainfall conditions (mean 200, standard deviation 100).

A significant change point occurs around time step 30, marking the transition from the high-variability adjustment phase to a stabilization period. Between time steps 30 and 60, destocking continues to decline but at a decelerating rate, with values dropping below 1.0 livestock unit and exhibiting reduced amplitude fluctuations. The rolling trend analysis confirms significant decreasing trends throughout the early periods (time steps 0-32), with slopes steepening from -0.381 to -0.163 and then moderating to -0.060, before transitioning to no significant trend between time steps 33-43.

From approximately time step 60 onward, the system stabilizes at low destocking levels. By the final time step 100, the average destocking reaches 0.31 livestock units, with most individual simulation trajectories clustering between 0 and 1.0. The median destocking across the entire simulation period is 0.31, substantially lower than the mean of 0.96, indicating the strong right-skew caused by the high initial destocking events. In the final phase (time steps 66-76), a slight increasing trend emerges at very low absolute levels (slope of 0.001), suggesting minor adaptive adjustments as the learning mechanisms (both learning-by-doing and social learning) continue to operate among the surviving households with differentiated behavioral strategies.

Overall, the destocking metric demonstrates a transition from an initial crisis period requiring substantial livestock liquidation to a relatively stable state with minimal forced destocking, suggesting that the mixed learning strategies enable households to converge toward sustainable herd sizes appropriate for the private property pasture conditions within approximately 40-60 time steps.
