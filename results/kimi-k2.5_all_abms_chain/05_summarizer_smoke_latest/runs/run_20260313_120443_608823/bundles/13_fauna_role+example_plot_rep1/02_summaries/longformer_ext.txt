The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to the combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. Unlike traditional approaches that look at populations as uniform groups, this model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances. The model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for the herbivores. Additionally, the model is designed to simulate the effects of hunting pressure and environmental conditions on the environment. The primary goal is to understand which combinations of hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and animal life history traits lead to population collapse versus stable coexistence. The model specifically incorporates sexual dimorphism—meaning males and females can have different sizes and nutritional values to hunters—along with realistic herd movement patterns and reproductive biology including pregnancy, nursing, and interbirth intervals.
