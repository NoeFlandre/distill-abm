 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change . Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances . Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores .  Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius . The number of active human forager bands remains constant at 20 throughout the observed simulation period spanning from step 0 to approximately step 2,312 . The aggregate energy available to human foragers begins at 2000 units and declines continuously throughout the simulation, terminating at approximately -38,200 units after 2310 time steps .  The cumulative number of female megaherbivores killed by human foragers begins at zero and undergoes a rapid accumulation phase during the initial 500 time steps . By step 1876, the series reaches its maximum mean value of approximately 120 individuals, representing roughly 48 percent of the initial ungulate population assuming equal sex ratios .  The cumulative mortality of postpartum female ungulates initiates at zero and undergoes rapid accumulation during the initial approximately 475 time steps . The rate of increase decelerates markedly around step 625, with a significant transition toward saturation evident by step 850 . This saturation pattern indicates that nursing females face disproportionate extinction risk under sustained hunting pressure .  The male ungulate population initiated at 125 individuals and followed a deterministic trajectory toward extinction across all 40 simulation replicates . By approximately step 500, cumulative deaths reach roughly 200 individuals, representing approximately 80 percent of the initial population of 250 ungulates . By step 1500 and continuing through the terminal observations beyond step 2000,  cumulative deaths asymptotically approach 235 to 240 individuals .  The female megaherbivore population initiates at 125 individuals and follows a deterministic trajectory toward extinction over the course of 2,312 time steps . Conservation interventions must target the initial 500-step window when decline rates are steepest and deterministic; after step 875, stochastic extinction dynamics dominate .  The total energy contained within the male animal population initiates at approximately 12,500 energy units, and undergoes a precipitous decline throughout the simulation period . The trajectory is characterized by distinct phases of depletion, marked by change points at steps 125, 350, 500, 625, and 875 .  The combined energetic reserves of the prey population initiate at approximately 25,000 units, representing the aggregate biomass of the founding ungulate cohort . Within the first 350 time steps, this metric undergoes a precipitous collapse, declining by over 75% . Within step 1,000, most trajectories have fallen below 2,00 units, and by step 2,312 all simulation iterations converge to complete energetic exhaustion .
