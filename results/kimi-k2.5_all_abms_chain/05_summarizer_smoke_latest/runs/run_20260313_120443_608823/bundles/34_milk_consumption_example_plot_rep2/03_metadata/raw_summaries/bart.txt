 This agent-based model simulates the adoption behaviors of milk consumption among the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It replicates individual preferences and decision factors underlying the historical transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . Decision-making follows a structured sequence involving perception of health and environmental choice characteristics, disposition triggering, scored choice functions weighted by habit strength and social influence .  Consumption trajectories demonstrate low variance across the repeated simulation runs throughout the entire time series, with only minor deviations visible during the rapid growth phase between steps 5 and 10 . The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined increases linearly from 0 at the initial step to 32,000 by step 32 . The cumulative count of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices begins at approximately zero at the start of the simulation .  The mean weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per person begins at approximately zero at step 0, consistent with the 1974 market baseline where whole milk dominated . From step 10 to step 25, the rate of decline moderates considerably, with consumption decreasing gradually from roughly 1,000 units to approximately 600 units . This slower reduction reflects the progressive weakening of incumbent habits .  The mean habit factor applied to the skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choice function commences at 1.0 at step 0, indicating a neutral baseline where habitual associations with these alternative varieties have not yet developed . The mean deviation between agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices begins at 0 and increases monotonically throughout the simulation period .  The mean deviation between agents' held environmental values and the implied environmental values of their milk choices begins at approximately zero and undergoes a rapid increase over the first 10 time steps before stabilizing for the remainder of the simulation . From step 10 through step 32, the metric remains nearly constant at approximately 13.4, suggesting that the system reaches a steady-state equilibrium .
