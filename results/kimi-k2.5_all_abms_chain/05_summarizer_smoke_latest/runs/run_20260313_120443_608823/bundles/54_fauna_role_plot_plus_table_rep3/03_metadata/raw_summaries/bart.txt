 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change . Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances . Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores .  Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius . Two critical behavioral switches control hunter mortality and storage: imortal-foragers is set to True, meaning hunters do not die from energy depletion, and food-storage allows hunters to accumulate energy surpluses .  The cumulative mortality trajectory of female ungulates exhibits a pronounced saturating pattern across the 40 simulation replicates, evolving through three distinct dynamical phases over the 2,312-tick observation window . The maximum observed cumulative mortality reaches approximately 120 individuals near tick 1876 (5.1 years), approaching the expected initial male population size .
