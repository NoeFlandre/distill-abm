model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . it introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making . the model aims to compare the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) under various ecological and climatic conditions . a subset of trajectories experiences sharp declines, with some dropping precipitously from moderate positive values to negative territory, reaching as low as -0.4 . this critical transition affects only a portion of the simulated households, while others maintain their previously established levels . the biomass enters a rapid recovery phase between steps 40 and 70, increasing sharply from approximately 40,000-47,000 to values ranging between 56,000 and 64,000 . this recovery coincides with the activation of learning processes—learning-by-doing (E-RO-SL1) . the destocking rate exhibits a pronounced downward trajectory from step 5 through approximately step 40, declining from values above 6 to predominantly below 2 . the oscillations indicate boom-bust cycles in biomass availability as agents adjust their strategies in response to stochastic climate forcing . average cumulative number of destocked livestock begins at zero across all simulations and increases rapidly during the initial 20 time steps . trajectories diverge considerably, with the spread between the lowest and highest values increasing monotonically through the remainder of the simulation .
