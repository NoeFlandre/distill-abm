Inflection points at steps 7, 28, 30, 33, 38, 57, and 60 delineate transitions between phases of differing adoption velocity. The diffusion process demonstrates strong path dependency, with individual runs diverging significantly from the mean and from each other, suggesting that stochastic factors or initial condition variations create persistent differences in final adoption levels.

For decision makers, these dynamics indicate that social learning interventions in private grazing systems generate rapid initial engagement, but face substantial barriers to universal adoption, typically saturating at just over half of the household population. The pronounced variability across runs implies that achieving higher adoption rates requires identifying and addressing contextual factors that inhibit the remaining households from engaging, as the natural diffusion process leaves significant portions of the population unaffected even after extended periods.
