The number of active hunter agents remains fixed at 20 individuals throughout the entire simulation, from the initial step through all recorded time steps. This value shows zero variance across the temporal sequence, with no detectable increases, decreases, or fluctuations occurring at any point in the modeled period. The static population level directly corresponds to the parameter configuration establishing an initial forager count of 20 combined with immortality, which eliminates mortality from energy depletion or other causes, while simultaneously removing reproductive capacity that could generate recruitment. Consequently, the human hunting pressure operates as a constant demographic baseline rather than a dynamic variable, maintaining exactly 20 active foragers regardless of environmental conditions, prey availability, or hunting energetics.

For decision makers, this configuration isolates the effects of fixed hunting intensity on megaherbivore extinction dynamics by eliminating predator-prey demographic feedback loops. Any observed prey population declines or extinctions can be attributed solely to the interaction between constant hunting pressure and prey life history traits without confounding variables from changing hunter densities. However, this design choice assumes away realistic scenarios where hunter populations might contract following resource depletion or expand during periods of prey abundance, potentially limiting the model's applicability to contexts where human demographic flexibility significantly influences extinction trajectories.
