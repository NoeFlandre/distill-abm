model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . individual agents represent adult consumers who begin with preferences reflective of 1974 market conditions . each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers . consumption decreases rapidly and steeply, falling by approximately 1650 units to reach levels around 1000 by Step 10 . in the second phase (Steps 10-20), the rate of decline moderates considerably . consumption falls to terminal values between 250 and 300 units within a few steps . cumulative count increases at a constant rate of approximately 900–950 choices per step, reaching roughly 25,000–26,000 by step 32 . the model maintains a steady-state adoption rate where habitual whole milk consumption persists among a resistant subpopulation that does not fully convert within the simulation horizon . the habit factor transitions into a phase of steady, gradual increase . by step 5, values range between approximately 1.023 and 1.027 . at the temporal midpoint around step 15, values cluster between roughly 1.028 to 1.033 . trajectories exhibit increasing divergence over time . between step 0 and approximately step 3, the deviation undergoes a sharp increase, rising rapidly to approximately 0.5 . this phase of rapid divergence slows between steps 3 and 5, where the metric increases more moderately to roughly 0.58–0.6 . after this transition, the deviation never decreases, indicating a persistent and continuous diversification of choice function scores among agents over time .
