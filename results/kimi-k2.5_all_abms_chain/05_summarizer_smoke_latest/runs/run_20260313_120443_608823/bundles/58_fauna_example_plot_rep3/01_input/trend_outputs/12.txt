The total energy of the female animal population initiates at approximately 12,000 to 12,500 units, reflecting the aggregated energetic value of the initial prey cohort including any nursing offspring. During the first 400 to 500 steps, this metric declines precipitously across all simulation runs, falling to values ranging between roughly 500 and 4,000 units depending on the specific stochastic trajectory. This initial collapse corresponds to the onset of intensive foraging activity by hunter agents operating under optimal foraging constraints, exploiting high-density prey patches before landscape depletion forces increased search costs.

Following this initial drop, the decline rate moderates considerably between steps 400 and 800, with several runs exhibiting transient stabilization or modest increases of several hundred units. These interruptions in the downward trend likely represent successful reproductive pulses—consistent with the model's two-year interbirth interval and high seasonality settings—where new offspring increase the collective biomass and energy value of female herds, temporarily offsetting harvest losses and natural mortality.

Subsequent to step 800, the total energy resumes its decline toward extinction, though individual runs display markedly different persistence times. By step 1,000, most populations have diminished to less than 1,000 units, yet a subset maintains measurable energy reserves extending to step 1,500 and beyond, with the longest persisting runs reaching approximately step 2,100 before depletion. 

The simulations exhibit low variance during the initial 200 steps while hunting pressure uniformly depletes the founding population, but divergence increases significantly through the middle phase as stochastic differences in herd movement patterns, grass patch utilization, and hunter encounter rates compound. Ultimately, all trajectories converge toward zero total energy by the end of the observation period, indicating that under the specified parameters of immortal foragers, restricted grass regrowth, and fixed reproductive intervals, the female prey population cannot sustain positive energy balance indefinitely against persistent hunting pressure.
