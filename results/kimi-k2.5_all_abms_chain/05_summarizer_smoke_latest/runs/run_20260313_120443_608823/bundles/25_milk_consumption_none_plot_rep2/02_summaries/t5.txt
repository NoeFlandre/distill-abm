Agents represent adult consumers positioned randomly within an information environment. Each starting with milk choices reflecting the 1974 market dominance of whole milk versus skimmed varieties. All 1,000 agents exist within a unidirectional social network based on the Watts-Strogatz topology. A temporary decline to roughly 1,180 occurs around step 18, followed by a recovery to the 1,200–1,250 range by step 20. Cognitive dissonance mechanisms—with a threshold of 0.15—and habit formation processes (memory-lifetime of 5) temporarily slow the adoption rate as the system reaches a critical mass of adopters. Metric initiates at zero total choices at step 0 and accumulates at a constant rate of approximately 1,000 choices per time step. By step 10, the total reaches approximately 10,000 choices; by step 20, approximately 20,000 choices; and by step 30, approximately 30,000 choices. The accumulation continues through the final time steps, ultimately reaching roughly 32,000 total choices by step 32. The extended plateau spanning roughly fifteen time steps represents a maturity phase where adoption slows as most receptive consumers have already switched. The final upward trend from step 26 onward suggests a late-stage adoption wave, potentially driven by accumulated social norm effects. The metric continues to increase but at a substantially attenuated, approximately linear rate, progressing from roughly 0.58 to approximately 0.75. The rate of increase during this latter phase is markedly shallower than the initial growth period, suggesting a slowing but continuing differentiation in agent choice evaluations.
