The megafaunal hunting pressure model (mhpm) is an agent - based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to the combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. The human foragers operate according to dynamic optimal foraging theory, meaning they constantly evaluate whether pursuing a particular animal is worth the energy expenditure based on the animal's food value, the difficulty of processing it, and how much energy the hunter currently has stored. The model can simulate seasonal environments where grass availability fluctuates throughout the year, and users can control the proportion of the landscape that initially contains vegetation as well as how quickly that vegetation returns after being eaten. Maximum - lifespan ranges from 0 to 12, 000 ticks, set at 10, 220, while age - of - senescence ranges from 0 to 10, 000, set at 7, 300, marking when age - related mortality increases. Food - value - males ranges from 5 to 100, set at 30, while food - value - females ranges from 5 to 100, set at 27, representing the energy hunters gain from consuming each sex. The effort required to process carcasses differs by sex, with processing - cost - males ranging from 0 to 20, set at 6, and processing - cost - females ranging from 0 to 20, set at 3, representing the energy expended to butcher and prepare the meat. Two critical behavioral switches control hunter mortality and storage : imortal - foragers is set to true, meaning hunters do not die from energy depletion, and food - storage is set to true, allowing hunters to accumulate energy surpluses. Three boolean switches control vegetation behavior : grass - growth is set to true, enabling vegetation regrowth ; keep - initial - grass - pattern is set to true, restricting regrowth to patches that were initially vegetated ; and seasonality at 0.
