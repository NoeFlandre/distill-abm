The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges. The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics. The number of active hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period. Total energy of forager bands begins at approximately 2,000 units and exhibits a biphasic decline across all simulation replicates. After an initial period of approximately 500 steps, energy gains from hunting effectively cease, consistent with prey depletion or extinction events. The cumulative count of postpartum female prey killed initiates at zero and increases monotonically throughout the observation period. The steep initial rise reflects intensive hunting pressure targeting females during the early phase of the simulation when prey are abundant. The asymptotic plateau at approximately 120-125 cumulative deaths corresponds to the near complete depletion of the female segment of the prey population. The number of male animals begins at approximately 125 individuals across all simulation runs. The rate of decline is steepest during the initial 500 steps, with the population falling from roughly 125 to below 20 females in most trajectories. By approximately step 500, populations across most runs had diminished to between 0 and 50 animals, representing a decline of roughly 80-100% from initial conditions. By step 1000, the majority of simulation runs have reduced female populations of 5 or fewer individuals. The data demonstrate that under the specified parameter configuration, hunting pressure from 20 immortal foragers drives the male ungulate population toward extinction across all stochastic realizations. Under these settings, the system does not achieve equilibrium but proceeds toward complete depletion of the male prey energy pool. Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model shows that prey population consistently collapses toward functional extinction. The percentage of vegetated landscape patches initiates at approximately 33%, consistent with the grass-proportion parameter setting of 0.33. The rapid initial decline suggests that hunting pressure immediately exceeds the demographic replacement capacity of the herds.
