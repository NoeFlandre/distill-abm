This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model. It is designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems. The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes central to adaptive co-management. The mean household risk attitude (r-parameter) across the population begins at a minimal value near 0.007 during the initial timesteps. The trajectory reflects the collective adaptation process where households adjust their uncertainty management parameters through learning-by-doing and social learning mechanisms. Reserve biomass initiates at approximately 90,000 units and undergoes a pronounced decline during the first 26 time steps, reaching a minimum value of roughly 84,114 units. The simulation begins with an initial average livestock holding of 90 animals per household at year 0. A sharp decline occurs during the first 37 years, with livestock numbers falling rapidly to reach a minimum of 41 animals by year 37. The system enters a stabilization phase between years 37 and 55, where livestock numbers fluctuate narrowly around 41 to 47 animals as households adjust to sustainable stocking levels. During the first 10 time steps, this count rises to a peak of approximately 21 households around time step 8.
