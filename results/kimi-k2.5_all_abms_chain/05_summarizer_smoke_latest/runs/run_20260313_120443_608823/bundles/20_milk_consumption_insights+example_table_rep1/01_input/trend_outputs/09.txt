**Implications for Decision Makers**

These dynamics suggest that habit-based public health campaigns targeting dietary transitions require long-term investment horizons, as meaningful habit formation exhibits a significant five-step lag phase before generating measurable acceleration. The persistent gap between final alternative-milk habit levels and incumbent whole-milk habits implies that eradicating traditional consumption patterns may require complementary interventions beyond simple repetition-based strategies. Furthermore, the oscillations observed in later stages indicate that maintenance phases must account for periodic vulnerability windows where established habits may destabilize, necessitating sustained reinforcement mechanisms to prevent regression to dominant incumbent behaviors.
