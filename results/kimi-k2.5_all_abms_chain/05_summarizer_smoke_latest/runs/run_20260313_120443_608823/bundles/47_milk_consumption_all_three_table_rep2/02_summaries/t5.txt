Model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005. Individual agents represent adult consumers who begin with preferences reflective of 1974 market conditions. Each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers. The trajectory exhibits distinct phases of erosion interspersed with temporary stabilization periods. The initial contraction (steps 0 through 9) demonstrates accelerated decline rates, with the steepest descent occurring between steps 5 and 9. A critical plateau emerges between steps 15 and 19, where consumption stabilizes temporarily around 760 to 774 units. The inflection points detected at time steps four, twelve, sixteen, and twenty-two demarcate transitions between these distinct regimes of growth, stasis, and renewed expansion. The trajectory demonstrates a classic innovation diffusion pattern: prolonged initial acceleration followed by a turbulent middle period of market contention, ultimately resolving into widespread adoption dominance.
