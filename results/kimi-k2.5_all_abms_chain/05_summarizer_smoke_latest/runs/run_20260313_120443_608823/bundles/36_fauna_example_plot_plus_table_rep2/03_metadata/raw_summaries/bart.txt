 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges . The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics .  The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period of 2,312 time steps . The total energy available to forager bands begins at 2000 and undergoes a continuous, monotonic decline throughout the simulation, reaching approximately -38229 by step 2312 .  During the initial period from step 0 to approximately step 500, the number of females killed increases rapidly and monotonically, with significant inflections around steps 245 and 285 indicating strong acceleration in hunting mortality . The number of postpartum females killed commences at zero and increases rapidly during the initial phase, reflecting immediate hunting pressure by forager agents on reproductive females . As the simulation progresses, the rate of increase decelerates markedly .  The population of female megaherbivores begins at 125 individuals and undergoes a persistent decline toward extinction over the course of the simulation . The system approaches an asymptotic maximum around step 1876, reaching approximately 240.33 cumulative deaths . The trajectory is characterized by a monotonic decrease with high variance in extinction timing .  The total prey population begins at approximately 167 individuals and declines continuously to extinction over the course of the simulation, reaching zero individuals by step 2311 . The most rapid decrease occurs within the first 500 time steps, during which the population plummets from its initial value to levels below 50 individuals . By step 1000, population levels have fallen to near-zero in most trajectories, with values typically remaining below 3 individuals .  The population spends most of its existence at critically low energy levels following the initial crash, with sustained high energy only at the onset of the simulation . A steep decline characterizes the first 350 time steps, with aggregate energy falling by roughly 75% to approximately 4,100 units . The final change point occurs at step 2,310, where the total energy reaches zero and remains there, indicating complete extinction of the female population .
