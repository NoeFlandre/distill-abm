 The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . Each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers in their network . Success is measured by the model's ability to reproduce the historical crossover point where skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption surpassed whole milk consumption . This acceleration aligns with initial shifts in health perceptions and the beginning of social influence propagation through the network structure .
