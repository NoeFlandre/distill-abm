The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It simulates a simplified grassland environment represented as an abstract 211×211 patch matrix forming a square universe that wraps at the edges. The model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions, human hunting pressure, and animal life history traits influence extinction dynamics. The number of human hunter-gatherer bands active in the simulation remains constant at 20 throughout the entire temporal sequence. This constancy reflects the implementation of immortal forager mechanics within the model parameters. The aggregate energy available to forager bands begins at 2000 units and undergoes a continuous, pronounced decline throughout the simulation, terminating at approximately -38229 units. The metric reaches its maximum value of approximately 123.7 at time step 1826, entering a brief plateau phase characterized by minimal net accumulation. This peak represents the maximum sustainable cumulative hunting pressure on female prey under the specified parameters of maximum lifespan 10220 ticks and senescence beginning at 7300 ticks. The trajectory of megaherbivore herds initiates at zero and follows a sigmoid trajectory characterized by rapid early acceleration, subsequent deceleration, and ultimate stabilization near an asymptotic maximum. After the peak at step 1673, the trend reverses decisively, with statistically significant decreasing trends documented from time step 1624 through 2087. The population of female prey initiates at 125 individuals and undergoes a precipitous decline during the first approximately 300 to 475 time steps. The system exhibits oscillatory dynamics with a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps throughout the decline. The median population level of approximately 1.3 individuals indicates that the system spent the majority of its time at critically low densities following the initial collapse. The combined energy of the prey population begins at approximately 16,667 units, representing the initial biomass of 250 ungulate herds distributed across the landscape. During the first approximately 350 time steps, the total prey energy undergoes a steep decline, dropping sharply from initial levels. The trajectory follows a pronounced negative trend characterized by distinct phases of varying decline rates and intermittent stabilization periods. The population reaches zero energy at time step 2,284 and remains extinct through the conclusion of the simulation. Following step 875, the system enters a terminal decline phase characterized by increasingly severe fluctuations but a consistent downward trajectory. Brief stabilization attempts occur around steps 1390-1620 and 1850-2090, where the rate of decline temporarily slows or slightly reverses. By step 2311, the total prey energy reaches zero, indicating complete megaherbivore extinction.
