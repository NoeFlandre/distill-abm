The mean weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent initiates at approximately zero units during Steps 0–1, consistent with the 1974 market baseline where whole milk dominated consumer preferences. Across repeated simulation runs, trajectories demonstrate consistent qualitative patterns with quantitative variations typically bounded within 50–100 units, particularly during transition phases, indicating robust replication of adoption dynamics despite stochasticity in individual agent interactions.

**Implications for Decision Makers**

The distinct four-phase trajectory—initial dormancy, rapid take-off, extended plateau, and late-stage acceleration—demonstrates that dietary transitions follow non-linear paths that require differential intervention strategies at different temporal stages. The pronounced plateau phase spanning approximately 14 steps suggests that early adoption metrics may indicate false saturation; therefore, health promotion campaigns require sustained, long-term commitment rather than short-term interventions to overcome habit persistence and cognitive dissonance thresholds. The secondary growth phase further indicates that maintaining communication infrastructure and social norm messaging beyond initial adoption peaks remains critical for maximizing market transformation, as significant adoption potential may remain latent within the population even after apparent stabilization. Decision makers should anticipate resistance periods lasting roughly half the total simulation duration and design policies that reinforce social influence mechanisms continuously to bridge the gap between early adopters and the remaining reluctant population.
