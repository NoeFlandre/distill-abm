Agents represent adult consumers positioned randomly within an information environment. Each starting with milk choices reflecting the 1974 market dominance of whole milk versus skimmed varieties. All 1,000 agents exist within a unidirectional social network based on the Watts-Strogatz topology. Between Steps 1 and 10, the metric exhibits a steep acceleration, rising sharply from near-zero levels to approximately 1,200–1,280 units. This rapid increase reflects the initial diffusion of alternative milk choices through the Watts-Strogatz social network. A secondary growth phase commences after Step 25 with consumption escalating from approximately 1,250 units to reach 1,450–1,480 units by Step 32. This final value represents roughly 80 percent of all possible milk selections across the 1,000-agent population over the simulation period. Multiple simulation runs demonstrate highly consistent trajectories with minor stochastic variation. Both model variants—threshold-based and probability-based disposition mechanisms—produce comparable cumulative adoption curves. The initial rapid growth (steps 0-10) likely represents the "innovator" and "early adopter" segments of the population responding to health information. The subsequent 15-step plateau indicates a period of resistance among the majority population, where habit strength and social network effects create inertia against further adoption. The linear growth pattern for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption experiences an initial lag phase before entering a prolonged period of steady strengthening. The high consistency across simulations supports confidence in the reliability of these projected trends. Perceptions and actual consumption behaviors may diverge significantly and increasingly over time. Cognitive dissonance alone may be insufficient to trigger behavioral alignment with health beliefs without additional intervention mechanisms. The deviation registers approximately 0, indicating close alignment between agents' held environmental values and their initial milk selections dominated by whole milk.
