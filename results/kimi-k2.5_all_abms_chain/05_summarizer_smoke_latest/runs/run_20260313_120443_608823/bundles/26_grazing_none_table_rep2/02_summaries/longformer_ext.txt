This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model, designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems. While the original RAGE model focused on common property grazing with mobile households, this extension applies to smallholder farming contexts where pastures are private property and households remain stationary on their patches. The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes central to adaptive co-management. It implements three new behavioral types: E-RO (baseline behavior), E-RO-SL1 (social learning), and E-LBD (learning-by-doing). Agents are characterized by heterogeneity in uncertainty management, represented by an r-parameter (household-risk-att) that captures factors such as sensing uncertainty, information noise, and risk attitude. The model comprises two main sub-models: Vegetation dynamics and Household decision-making. During the initial phase (timesteps 0–10), this population exhibits a modest increase, reaching a maximum of approximately 21.1 households around timestep 8. This early peak suggests a transient period where static risk attitudes initially suffice before the full effects of environmental variability manifest. The E-RO population declines to approximately 15 households around timestep 11.Following this peak, a pronounced decline commences between timesteps 11 and 21, during which the number of E-RO households falls steeply from roughly 20 to approximately 16. This steep drop indicates that non-learning agents begin experiencing higher rates of failure or exit as they confront the stochastic rainfall conditions (mean 200, standard deviation 100) without adaptive capacity.
