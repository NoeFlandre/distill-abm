Between approximately steps 5 and 25, all simulation runs exhibit a rapid, step - like increase in the count of households with social learning, with adoption occurring in discrete increments rather than gradually. This high variability suggests that stochastic factors or initial conditions significantly influence whether social learning becomes nearly universal or remains limited to a minority of households, despite all runs starting from identical parameter configurations. During the initial phase spanning approximately steps 0 to 20, all simulation runs exhibit synchronous rapid accumulation of mean household destock totals, rising uniformly from initial values of 0 to between 60 and 80 animals per household. This moderate trajectory indicates systems experiencing ongoing but manageable grazing pressure, where households must periodically destock to cope with seasonal biomass fluctuations but maintain viable herd cores. A third subset exhibits steep continuous growth throughout the simulation period, reaching values between 140 and 173 by step 100 — exceeding the initial herd endowment by 55 to 92 percent. This pattern reveals that while initial destocking of roughly 65 - 75 percent of initial stock is inevitable across all behavioral configurations, climate realizations, and learning strategies, long - term stability depends critically on stochastic rainfall sequences and the effectiveness of adaptive decision - making processes. The existence of stable trajectories demonstrates that sustainable private property grazing is achievable with learning - based management, but the prevalence of rising trajectories indicates that without appropriate support, many households face chronic vulnerability requiring repeated asset liquidation that depletes productive capital. During the initial phase (steps 0 - 10), the trajectories exhibit immediate and extreme divergence : in several runs, the count of non - learning households surges rapidly to peaks between 38 and 42, while in other runs, the count plummets sharply to values between 0 and 10.
