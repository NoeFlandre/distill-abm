 This agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE (Resilience of Agent-based Grazing Systems) model . It is designed to simulate feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in grazing systems . The model introduces learning capabilities to agent decision-making, specifically incorporating learning-by-doing and social learning processes .  The average deviation from recommended livestock numbers begins at a negligible level (approximately 0.007) in the initial time steps, indicating near-perfect adherence to recommendations . The reserve biomass exhibits a pronounced U-shaped trajectory over the 100-timestep simulation period, characterized by an initial degradation phase followed by sustained recovery .
