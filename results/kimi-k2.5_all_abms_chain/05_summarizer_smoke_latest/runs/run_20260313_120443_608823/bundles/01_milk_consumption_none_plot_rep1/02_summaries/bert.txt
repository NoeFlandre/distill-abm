This agent - based model simulates the adoption behaviors of milk consumption among the british public from 1974 to 2005, replicating individual preferences and decision factors underlying the historical transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi - skimmed varieties. The primary objective is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence interact within individual decision - making processes regarding milk choice, with the main outcome metric being average weekly consumption per person for each milk type. Decision - making follows a structured sequence involving perception of health and environmental choice characteristics, disposition triggering, scored choice functions weighted by habit strength and social influence, and post - choice evaluation where agents may adjust future decisions. Given the fixed population of 1, 000 agents, this linear accumulation indicates that each agent executes a milk choice decision approximately once per time step throughout the entire simulation timeframe spanning 1974 to 2005.
