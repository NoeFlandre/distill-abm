The total energy contained within the megaherbivore population exhibits a deterministic trajectory toward collapse under sustained hunting pressure, beginning at approximately 16,667 units on average across replicates (with individual simulation runs initiating at roughly 25,000 units) and declining to complete exhaustion by tick 2,311. 

A phase of rapid depletion dominates the first 500 ticks, characterized by steep monotonic declines with statistical slopes reaching -36.0 units per tick between ticks 232-463, and remaining strongly negative at -24.95 units per tick in the initial window. Superimposed on this decline are transient oscillatory peaks occurring at tick 385 (4,102 units), 388 (4,092 units), 402 (3,995 units), 408 (3,962 units), 433 (3,764 units), and 440 (3,709 units), reflecting demographic responses to seasonal resource fluctuations with a dominant periodicity of approximately 63 ticks. A secondary cluster of diminished peaks appears around ticks 708-730, reaching only 869-1,083 units, indicating the first year of suppression has severely constrained population energetic capacity.

Change points at ticks 350, 500, and 625 demarcate transitions to progressively slower decline rates. Between ticks 500 and 1,000, the degradation continues but at attenuated velocities (slopes of -10.96 to -0.96 units per tick), with aggregate energy typically remaining below 5,000 units and many replicates approaching zero. The period from tick 928 to 1,391 shows the slowest rate of decline (-0.069 to -0.963 slopes), representing a protracted terminal phase where small population remnants persist.

Despite the overall downward trajectory, the system exhibits brief demographic resilience in later stages, with statistically significant increasing trends detected between ticks 1,392-1,623 (positive slope) and 1,856-2,087 (positive slope of 0.000). However, these temporary recoveries prove insufficient to reverse the collapse. Change points at ticks 875 and 2,310 bracket the final extinction sequence. By tick 2,311, all energetic stores are depleted to zero, representing total megaherbivore population failure approximately 6.3 years after simulation initiation. The substantial discrepancy between the mean energetic value of 2,368.8 units and the median of 146.4 units across the full temporal series reflects the highly skewed distribution, where high initial states dominate early time steps while prolonged near-zero conditions characterize the terminal two-thirds of the simulation duration.
