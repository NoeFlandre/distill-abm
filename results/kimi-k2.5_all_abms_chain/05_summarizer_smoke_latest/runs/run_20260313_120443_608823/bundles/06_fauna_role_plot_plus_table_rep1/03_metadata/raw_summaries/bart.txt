 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change . Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances . Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores .  Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius . The number of hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 agents throughout the entire simulation duration of 2,312 ticks . Strongest growth occurs between ticks 1,392 and 1,623, with a slope of 0.007 births per tick and high significance (tau = 0.784) Earlier growth windows (ticks 696-927 and 928-1,159) show steep increases .  The simulations consistently resulted in the extinction of female megaherbivore populations, initiating from a cohort of 125 individuals and declining to zero across all 40 replicate runs . The median population size across the entire simulation period is approximately 1.35 individuals, with a mean of 21.03, indicating that the population spent the majority of the simulation at low densities .  The aggregate trajectory of total energy stored in male ungulate populations initiates at 12500 energy units and undergoes a deterministic collapse to complete extinction by tick 2284 . The median population size across the full simulation duration is 1.48 individuals, reflecting the extended period during which small remnant populations persist at densities highly vulnerable to stochastic extinction .  Initial phase (ticks 0–500) demonstrates a precipitous decrease with steep negative slopes ranging from approximately -22.6 to -26.7 energy units per tick . During this period, aggregate female energy plummets from 12,500 to roughly 2,200 by tick 375, representing a loss of over 82% of the initial energy reserve . Superimposed on this decline is a cyclical oscillation with a dominant period of approximately 63 ticks, consistent with strong seasonal resource fluctuations  The percentage of vegetated landscape patches exhibits distinct temporal dynamics across the 2,312 time steps of the aggregated simulation series . For the first approximately 150 time steps, the vegetation cover undergoes minor adjustments while maintaining levels near 32% . The recovery is not monotonic but exhibits oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps .
