the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the simulation period, from the initial time step through approximately step 2,200 . the static population size reflects the model configuration where foragers are immortal and do not reproduce, thereby maintaining the initial population count indefinitely . cumulative count of postpartum females killed initiates at zero and increases monotonically throughout the simulation period . some simulation runs stabilize as early as 600 to 800 steps, while others continue accumulating kills through 1,500 steps or beyond . despite these temporal differences, final cumulative values concentrate primarily in the 115 to 125 range, with occasional runs terminating at lower asymptotes around 100 to 110. the population of male prey animals begins at approximately 125 individuals . over the first 400 to 500 time steps, the population undergoes a rapid decline, decreasing to roughly 10 to 40 individuals depending on the specific simulation run . this slowing decline continues but at a decelerating rate, with most simulation runs reaching 10 or fewer males by step 1000 . 20 initial foragers and the specified environmental and life history parameters generate unsustainable hunting pressure that overwhelms reproductive rates and population recovery capacity . temporary plateaus observed during the collapse sequence likely represent intermittent reproductive events or periods of reduced encounter rates between hunters and prey . the total energy of male animals begins at approximately 12,500 energy units at step 0. This metric declines rapidly over the first 500 steps . landscape patches initiate at approximately 33%, consistent with grass-proportion parameter setting of 0.33 . a severe depletion event occurs, with the percentage of green patches plummeting to minima ranging from approximately 23% to 30% across different simulation runs .
