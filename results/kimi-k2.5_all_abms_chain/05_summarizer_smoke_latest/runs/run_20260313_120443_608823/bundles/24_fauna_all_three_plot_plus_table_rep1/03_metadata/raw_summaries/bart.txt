 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change . Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances . Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores .  Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius . The number of active human foragers remains constant at 20 individuals throughout the entire simulation period spanning 2,312 time steps .  The trajectory reveals that male megaherbivore populations face critical vulnerability within the first two years of hunting pressure, with approximately 60% of eventual total mortality occurring by step 625 . Conservation interventions must be implemented before this inflection point, as delayed action leaves insufficient breeding males to maintain population viability . Managers should therefore prioritize monitoring male abundance specifically rather than total population counts .  The trajectory indicates that approximately 80% of cumulative female mortality occurs within the first 1000 time steps (approximately 2.7 years), suggesting a narrow critical window for intervention before demographic collapse . The pronounced seasonal oscillation of 63 ticks indicates predictable periods of elevated vulnerability that could inform targeted protection measures during high-risk seasons .  The male megaherbivore population commences at 125 individuals and undergoes a deterministic decline to extinction, reaching zero individuals by step 2,284 . The trajectory exhibits distinct phases: an initial rapid collapse between steps 0 and 390, where the population falls precipitously from 125 to approximately 25 individuals . By step 1,000, population counts have declined to fewer than 10 males in the majority of simulation replicates .  The aggregate megaherbivore population begins at approximately 250 individuals, representing the initial carrying capacity with sexual dimorphism and mixed-age structure . By step 2312, approximately 6.3 years into the simulation, the population reaches complete extirpation across all replicates . Conservation strategies must address both the absolute number of hunters and the seasonal timing of exploitation .  Management strategies should consider reducing hunter numbers below this threshold or implementing seasonal hunting restrictions that align with the 63-tick oscillatory cycle to prevent the steep decline rates observed in the critical first 463 ticks . The total energy content aggregated across female ungulate populations commences at approximately 12,500 units, representing the collective energetic reserves of the founding female cohort distributed across the landscape grid .  The dynamics are characterized by regular oscillations with a dominant period of approximately 63 steps . The percentage of green patches begins at approximately 33.0% and maintains this baseline throughout the simulation, concluding at roughly 32.998% with a long-term mean of 32.656%. The system exhibits remarkable stability around this level .
