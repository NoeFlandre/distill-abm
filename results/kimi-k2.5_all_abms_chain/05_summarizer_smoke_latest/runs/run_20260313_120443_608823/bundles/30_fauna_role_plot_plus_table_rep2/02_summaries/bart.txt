Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based simulation designed to explore how megaherbivore populations respond to combined pressures of human hunting and environmental change. Model treats individual animals and human hunters as autonomous agents making decisions based on their immediate circumstances. Model operates on a 211 by 211 patch grid representing a grassland landscape, where each patch can contain vegetation that serves as food for herbivores. Foragers-movement-cost ranges from 0 to 10, set at 1.0, and distance hunters can perceive and pursue prey is determined by foraging-radius. Two critical behavioral switches control hunter mortality and storage: imortal-foragers is set to True, meaning hunters do not die from energy depletion. The number of human foragers remains constant at the initial value of 20 individuals throughout the entire simulation period of 2,312 ticks. The cumulative mortality of female ungulates exhibits a saturating growth pattern across the 2,312 time steps of the simulation series. Most simulations plateauing between 110 and 125 males killed. The preferential hunting of males, driven by their higher food value (30 units versus 27 for females) contributes to the eventual depletion of the male segment of the population. Credential mortality data reveal a three-phase pattern characterizing the extinction trajectory of the megaherbivore population under persistent hunting pressure. The data indicate that the combined pressures of immortal forager agents (who do not die from energy depletion) and environmental stress drive the prey population toward near-extinction within the first five to six years. Mann-Kendall trend tests confirm statistically significant monotonic decreases throughout the first 1,391 ticks, with steepest decline rates occurring in the earliest intervals. The trajectory exhibits oscillatory components with a dominant periodicity of approximately 63 ticks (roughly bimonthly cycles), consistent with the strong seasonality parameter (0.8) driving pulsed resource availability. By tick 475, the cross-run mean population had fallen to low single digits. The population reached complete extinction across all simulation replicates, with the final recorded value being zero. This pattern demonstrates that under the configured parameter regime, the megaherbivore population collapses from 250 individuals to extinction within a timeframe well short of the maximum simulation duration of 73,000 ticks. The population enters a terminal phase of protracted low-energy persistence. By step 2,310, all replicates converge to zero energetic biomass, indicating complete population extinction. Some populations exhausting energy reserves by step 500 while others persist beyond step 500. This variation arises despite identical initial conditions. All replicates converged to zero energy storage, indicating deterministic extinction of the megaherbivore population under the parameterized hunting pressure of 20 immortal foragers with energy storage capabilities operating in a seasonal grassland environment. The system exhibits moderate variance (standard deviation: 0.95%) driven by transient dynamics in early simulation phases.
