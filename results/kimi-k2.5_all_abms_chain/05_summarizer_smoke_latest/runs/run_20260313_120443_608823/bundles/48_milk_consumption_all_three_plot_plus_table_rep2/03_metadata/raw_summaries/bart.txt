Success is measured by the model's ability to reproduce the historical crossover point where skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption surpassed whole milk consumption .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at 5.29 units and undergoes a distinct three-phase evolution over 32 time steps, terminating at 1456.60 units . The distinct phases of decline suggest that intervention strategies should be temporally targeted . Policy measures aimed at accelerating the initial decline phase (steps 0-10) would likely yield the highest marginal returns .
