model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . individual agents represent adult consumers who begin with preferences reflective of 1974 market conditions . each agent processes information about health and environmental characteristics of milk options, weighs these against their habitual tendencies, and responds to social influence from other consumers . between Steps 1 and 10, consumption declines rapidly to approximately 1,000 units . this decrease reflects the initial phase of market transition where health perceptions about alternative milk varieties begin to overcome established consumption habits . from step 10 to step 20, consumption continues to fall, though with increased variability including discrete drops . habit strength assigned to whole milk (incumbent-initial-habit: 10.0) effectively anchors consumer behavior to the incumbent option . variance across simulation runs remains negligible during the first five time steps, after which the trajectories progressively diverge, achieving a spread of approximately 300 to 400 total choices by the final time step . the mean quantity of whole milk consumed begins at approximately 2,650 to 2,700 units at step 0, representing the market dominance of entire milk in 1974 . over the first 10 steps, the quantity declines sharply and consistently, reaching approximately 950 to 1,000 units by step 10 . this period shows a slower rate of decline as the remaining whole milk consumers appear more resistant to switching . the mean factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at a baseline value of 1.0, indicating neutral habit strength for alternative milk varieties at the start of the simulation . by time step 10, the value reaches approximately 1.08, marking the onset of accelerated habit formation . initial divergence within the first five time steps suggests market segmentation emerges almost immediately following the introduction of health and environmental information about alternative milk products . the continued, sustained growth in deviation throughout the 31-year period implies that the consumer landscape becomes increasingly fragmented over time rather than consolidating around unified preferences .
