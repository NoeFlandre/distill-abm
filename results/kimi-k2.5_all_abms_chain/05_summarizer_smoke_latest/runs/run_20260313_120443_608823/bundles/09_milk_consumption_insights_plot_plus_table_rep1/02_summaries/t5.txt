Agents represent adult consumers positioned randomly within an information environment. Each starting with milk choices reflecting the 1974 market dominance of whole milk versus skimmed varieties. All 1,000 agents exist within a unidirectional social network based on the Watts-Strogatz topology. The model indicates that dietary behavior change follows a non-linear trajectory with distinct phases of rapid adoption, temporary stabilization, and continued gradual decline. The mid-simulation plateau around steps 18–19 indicates that habitual consumption patterns and social network effects create substantial resistance requiring sustained intervention to overcome. Whole milk maintains a stable niche market of approximately 6–8% of consumers even decades into the transition period. The trajectory suggests market stabilization with a small, resistant whole milk segment. This metric represents the aggregate number of times agents selected alternative milk varieties. The steepest negative slope occurs within steps 5–9, indicating an acceleration of abandonment behavior following the initial adoption phase. Between steps 10 and 14, the rate of decline moderates considerably, though remaining statistically significant, as the values descend from approximately 1,100 to 900 units. A secondary decline emerges between steps 20–24, characterized by a significant decreasing trend that reduces values from roughly 775. The mean habit factor for whole milk initiates at 1.000 and increases monotonically throughout the simulation period, terminating at 1.050. The factor subsequently advances at a steady rate, reaching roughly 1.030 by step 10, 1.040 by step 20 and approximately 1.045 by step 25. Habit formation for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk operates as a cumulative, self-reinforcing process that builds momentum approximately one-third through the simulation period before gradually stabilizing. The 5-10 step immature phase represents a critical window where alternative disposition mechanisms must compensate for weak habitual drivers. The final phase from steps 25 through 32 shows renewed acceleration with a slope of 0.019 (steps 25-29), pushing the deviation from approximately 0.60 to the terminal value of 0.7251. This late-stage intensification indicates that as the simulation approaches the 2005 endpoint, agents' milk choices increasingly misalign with their personal health valuations.
