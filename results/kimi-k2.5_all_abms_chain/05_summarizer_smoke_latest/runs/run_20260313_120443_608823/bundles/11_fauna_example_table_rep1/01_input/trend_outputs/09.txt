This initial rapid attrition reflects intense hunting pressure, as females represent high-efficiency prey targets due to their lower processing costs relative to males and additional caloric value when nursing.

Following this collapse, the descent moderates between ticks 600 and 875, entering a prolonged phase of gradual reduction characterized by high volatility. Superimposed upon this trend are cyclical fluctuations with a dominant period of approximately 63 ticks, consistent with seasonal environmental forcing given the model's high seasonality setting. These oscillations generate periodic peaks and troughs as recruitment attempts align with resource availability cycles, with local maxima occurring around ticks 730, 820, and 893.

Temporary demographic recoveries interrupt the decline at two intervals: first between approximately ticks 1160 and 1390, and again between ticks 1856 and 2087, where the population exhibits weak positive momentum. These transitory increases likely correspond to successful breeding pulses following the two-year birth-spacing interval, allowing brief population growth before hunting pressure again drives numbers downward.

Despite these intermittent recoveries and seasonal oscillations, the underlying trajectory remains negative. The population reaches critical thresholds at approximately ticks 1410 and 2194, ultimately collapsing to zero by the simulation's conclusion. The median population level of one individual throughout the simulation indicates that for the majority of the temporal sequence, the female cohort operated at critically low densities, rendering the population vulnerable to stochastic extinction events.
