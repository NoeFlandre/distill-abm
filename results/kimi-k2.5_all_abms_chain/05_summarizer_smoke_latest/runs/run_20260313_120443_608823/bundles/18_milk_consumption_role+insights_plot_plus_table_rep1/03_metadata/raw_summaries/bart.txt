 The model simulates the shifting landscape of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005 . It captures how individual decision-making factors collectively drive macro-level market shifts . Success is measured by the model's ability to reproduce the historical crossover point where skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption surpassed whole milk consumption .  Consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk demonstrates a three-phase adoption trajectory over the 1974-2005 simulation period . The dynamics reflect the interplay of habit maintenance mechanisms, threshold-based behavioral change, and social network effects . For decision makers, the pattern indicates that market transformation strategies should anticipate diminishing returns over time .  The authors say the model generates sustained incumbent consumption throughout the 31-year period . During the first five time steps (window 0–4), the growth rate averages 70.8 choices per step, increasing to 76.2 during steps 5–9 .
