This agent-based model simulates the adoption behaviors of milk consumption among the British public from 1974 to 2005. It replicates individual preferences and decision factors underlying the historical transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties. The simulation integrates theoretical frameworks with empirical data, employing calibration against observed macro-level consumption patterns to validate outcomes. From 1974 to 2005, skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent exhibits a complex adoption trajectory characterized by three distinct phases with superimposed oscillatory behavior. At the initial time step in 1974, consumption stands at approximately 5.3 units, reflecting the historical dominance of whole milk during this period. Average weekly quantities per person plummeting from approximately 2,655 units initially to roughly 253 units by termination. The median cumulative count of milk selections follows a strictly linear increasing trajectory across the simulation timeframe. Average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk began at approximately 5.3 units per person at the outset of the simulation period representing 1974. The trajectory exhibited significant upward momentum through time step 9, driven by the interaction of health perception shifts, environmental awareness, and early social influence propagation through the Watts-Strogatz network topology. The simulation tracks the temporal evolution of cognitive dissonance in health-related milk choices across the British population from 1974 to 2005. The simulation reveals a consistent trajectory of increasing heterogeneity in consumer choice evaluation frameworks over the 32-year transition period. At the outset of the simulation (1974), the deviation registers at 0.0000, indicating perfect alignment between agents' health beliefs and their milk choices. Over the subsequent 32 time steps, this metric exhibits a monotonically increasing trend. The mean deviation between agents' held environmental values and the implied environmental values of their milk choices exhibits a distinct two-phase trajectory over the simulation period. An oscillatory component with a dominant periodicity of approximately 15 time steps is a cyclical variation that manifests through indices 18–20 and 27–31.
