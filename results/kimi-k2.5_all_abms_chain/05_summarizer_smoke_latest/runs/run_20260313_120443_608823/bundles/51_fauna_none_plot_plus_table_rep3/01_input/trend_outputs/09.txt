This phase exhibits statistically significant monotonic declines with steep negative slopes, reducing the population to critical levels within the first 600 steps.

The second phase, extending from approximately step 475 to step 875, is marked by continued decline but at attenuated rates, with the population diminishing from roughly 20 individuals to fewer than 5. The final phase comprises an extended persistence period at very low densities, generally maintaining between 0 and 2 individuals, with a median population value of 1.0 across all time steps. During this prolonged terminal phase, additional statistically significant but minor fluctuations occur, including brief increasing trends between steps 1,160-1,391 and 1,856-2,087, though these represent stochastic noise at extinction thresholds rather than recovery. The rate of change slows considerably, approaching zero slope, until the final extinction event occurs variably across runs, with the longest persistence extending to step 2,310.

Statistical evidence indicates inflection points at steps 305, 740, 833, and 1,309, marking transitions in the rate of decline, while structural change points at steps 475, 600, and 875 delineate shifts between different regimes of population dynamics.
