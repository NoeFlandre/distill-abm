Agents represent adult consumers positioned randomly within an information environment. Each starting with milk choices reflecting the 1974 market dominance of whole milk versus skimmed varieties. All 1,000 agents exist within a unidirectional social network based on the Watts-Strogatz topology. The cumulative total of whole milk choices initiates at approximately zero. The trajectory maintains a consistent rate of accumulation of roughly 70 selections per step throughout the entire period. Inter-run variation remains relatively constrained but increases gradually over time, with a spread of 300 total choices observed. Consumption drops sharply from 600-650 units to 300-400 units by step 28. Trajectories remain closely clustered, indicating consistency in the overall dynamic pattern. The greatest variation between runs appears around step 10, where values range from approximately 950 to 1,100 units, and during the transition around steps 25-28. Between steps 10 and 32, the value rises consistently from roughly 1.08 to approximately 1.58 or 1.6. This pattern indicates that habit formation for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk remained negligible during the early years of the simulation period, followed by a sustained strengthening of habitual preference throughout the subsequent two decades. From step 10 through step 32, the deviation stabilizes, maintaining values consistently between 13.3 and 13.5 with minimal variation across simulation replicate replicates. This trajectory reflects the modelled transition from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties.
