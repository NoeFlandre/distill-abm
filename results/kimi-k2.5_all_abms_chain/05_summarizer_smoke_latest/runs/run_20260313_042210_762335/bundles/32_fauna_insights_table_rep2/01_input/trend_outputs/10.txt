**Implications for Decision Makers**

The deterministic extinction within approximately six years, despite initial population viability, indicates that the combination of constant hunter presence (immortal foragers with energy storage capabilities), high environmental seasonality, and conservative ungulate life history traits (extended birth spacing and senescence) creates unsustainable predation pressure. Transient demographic recoveries observed during the oscillatory decay phase may generate false signals of population resilience; consequently, management interventions must occur prior to the onset of oscillatory collapse to prevent inevitable extinction. The dominant 63-tick cycle highlights critical seasonal bottlenecks where population vulnerability peaks, suggesting targeted protection during specific seasonal windows may be necessary to interrupt the extinction trajectory.
