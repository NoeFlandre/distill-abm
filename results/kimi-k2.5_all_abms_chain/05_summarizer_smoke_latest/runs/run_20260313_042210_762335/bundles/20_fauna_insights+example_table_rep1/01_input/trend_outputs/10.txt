**Implications for Decision Makers**

Intervention thresholds for hunted megaherbivore populations must account for the rapid initial decline phase; once populations fall below approximately 10% of initial abundance, recovery becomes improbable despite temporary stabilization. The prolonged low-density phase represents an extinction vortex where stochastic processes dominate, indicating that conservation measures implemented after the initial crash are likely ineffective. The 63-tick oscillation periodicity suggests that seasonal protection measures timed with reproductive cycles could theoretically extend population persistence, though the eventual extinction outcome under current parameters indicates that such measures require concurrent reduction in hunter numbers or mortality rates to prevent ultimate population collapse.
