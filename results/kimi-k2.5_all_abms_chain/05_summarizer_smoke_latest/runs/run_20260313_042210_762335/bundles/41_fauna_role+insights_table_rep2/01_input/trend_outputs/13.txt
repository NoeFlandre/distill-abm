**Implications for Decision Makers**

The multi-phase nature of the collapse indicates that conservation interventions face diminishing returns over time. The first simulated year represents a critical window where population trajectories are most responsive to mitigation efforts; once aggregate energy falls below approximately 4,000 units, demographic momentum toward extinction becomes increasingly irreversible despite temporary stabilization periods. The brief recovery episodes in years four and five suggest that prey populations retain residual resilience that could theoretically be exploited for recovery if hunting pressure were substantially reduced during these specific intervals, though the model indicates such windows close rapidly. Finally, the persistent 63-tick oscillation cycle implies that seasonal restrictions on hunting during low-energy periods (valleys) could potentially extend population persistence, whereas sustained pressure throughout seasonal cycles accelerates collapse.
