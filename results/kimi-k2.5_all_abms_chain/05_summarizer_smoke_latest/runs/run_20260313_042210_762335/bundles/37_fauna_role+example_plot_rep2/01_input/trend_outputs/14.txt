The percentage of landscape patches containing vegetation initiates at approximately 33%, consistent with the initial grass coverage parameter. Within the first 100 time steps, this metric declines to approximately 32% and stabilizes, reflecting the establishment of grazing pressure from the initial herbivore population.

The system subsequently exhibits pronounced annual cyclical dynamics, with sharp declines and rapid recoveries occurring at intervals of approximately 365 time steps. During each cycle, vegetated cover drops from baseline levels near 32.5% to minima ranging between 29% and 31%, though the first cycle around time step 350 exhibits greater variability across runs, with some trajectories falling to approximately 23-24%. These declines are followed by rapid recovery within approximately 50 time steps, restoring vegetation to pre-decline levels before the subsequent seasonal downturn. This pattern repeats consistently at time steps corresponding to years 2 through 6 (approximately steps 700, 1050, 1400, 1750, and 2100).

After approximately 1200 time steps, simulation trajectories exhibit increasing divergence across runs in terms of baseline vegetation levels and the magnitude of seasonal fluctuations. While all runs maintain the annual cyclical pattern, some stabilize at slightly higher baseline levels whereas others maintain lower baselines, with varying amplitudes of seasonal decline. This divergence likely reflects accumulated stochastic variation in herbivore population dynamics and grazing pressure across multiple annual cycles. Throughout the entire temporal range, the strong seasonal oscillations persist, indicating that vegetation dynamics remain tightly coupled to the seasonal environmental forcing parameter.
