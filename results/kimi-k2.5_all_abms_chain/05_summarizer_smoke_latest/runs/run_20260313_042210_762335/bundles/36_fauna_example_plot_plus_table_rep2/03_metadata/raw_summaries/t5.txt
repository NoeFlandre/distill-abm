the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period of 2,312 time steps . starting at the initial value of 20, the population exhibits no variation over time, maintaining exactly 20 agents with zero variance across all 40 simulation runs . this stability reflects the model configuration in which hunters are initialized at a fixed density of 20, do not reproduce, and are immortal, thereby preventing mortality events from depleting their numbers . cumulative mortality of female megaherbivores initiates at zero and exhibits distinct phases of accumulation over the course of the simulation . by step 500, the cumulative count reaches roughly 100 to 120 individuals, representing a substantial proportion of the total eventual mortality . this pattern reflects diminishing hunting returns as the female prey population becomes increasingly depleted and difficult to locate . the cumulative mortality of the megaherbivore population originates at zero and follows a trajectory characterized by rapid initial accumulation, progressive deceleration, and eventual stabilization near 235 to 240 deaths . by step 500, the cumulative count approaches 200 to 230 individuals, driven by intensive hunting pressure from the fixed population of 20 immortal foragers operating under dynamic optimal foraging constraints . between steps 1160 and 1391, a brief period of slight increase occurs . values rising marginally from near-zero levels, potentially indicating temporary reductions in hunting efficiency or stochastic survival of reproductive individuals . by step 2311, the population reaches zero, indicating complete extinction of female animals . median energy value of approximately 134.53 across all time steps indicates that for most of the simulation duration, the male population operates at severely reduced biomass levels compared to the initial state . over the first 375 time steps, this metric undergoes a precipitous decline, falling to roughly 2,200 units . this transitional phase likely indicates reduced hunting efficiency as prey density decreases . vegetated landscape patches initiate at 33.0% and remains concentrated near this maximum value throughout the simulation . a severe depletion event occurs precisely at time step 365 (equivalent to one annual cycle), where coverage collapses to 24.91% . following this annual minimum, the system undergoes rapid recovery, returning to baseline levels of 32.5–33.0% .
