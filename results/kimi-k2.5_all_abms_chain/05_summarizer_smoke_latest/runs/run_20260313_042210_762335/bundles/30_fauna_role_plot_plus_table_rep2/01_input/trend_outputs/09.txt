Across all 40 replicate runs, the female megaherbivore population underwent complete extinction, declining from an initial cohort of 125 individuals to zero by tick 2311. The trajectories exhibit pronounced temporal heterogeneity in collapse dynamics, with extinction timing varying substantially across runs—some populations collapsed within 300-500 ticks while others persisted until beyond tick 2000.

The decline proceeded through distinct phases marked by changes in velocity. The initial phase (approximately ticks 0-475) featured the steepest descent, with statistical trends indicating slopes of -0.227 to -0.245 per tick and highly significant decreasing trajectories (tau approaching -1.0). By tick 475, the cross-run mean population had fallen to low single digits. Moderating decline rates characterized the interval between ticks 475-875, with slopes diminishing to -0.061 and then -0.019, reflecting a transition from rapid collapse to protracted attrition.

A transient stabilization emerged between ticks 1160-1391, where the aggregate series registered a statistically significant but modest increasing trend (tau=0.093), temporarily interrupting the overall collapse before resuming decline between ticks 1392-1855. A second brief increasing window occurred between ticks 1856-2087 (tau=0.453), though starting from very low baseline values.

Superimposed on the secular decline, the series exhibits oscillatory dynamics with a dominant periodicity of approximately 63 ticks, consistent with seasonal breeding cycles interacting with the 0.8 seasonality parameter and 730-tick birth-spacing interval. These oscillations manifest as minor fluctuations around the declining trend, with peak mean values during the attrition phase reaching only 6.5 individuals at tick 730.

The population distribution across time is highly right-skewed, with a mean of 16.48 but a median of 1.0, indicating that for the majority of the simulation duration, most runs maintained only 0-1 surviving females. Final extinction across all replicates occurred by tick 2311, with the last inflection points in population dynamics registered between ticks 2156-2194 preceding the terminal zero state.
