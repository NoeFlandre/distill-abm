the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human hunter-gatherer bands remained constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period . the aggregate trajectory of total forager energy across 40 simulation runs exhibits a monotonic, statistically significant decline over 2312 steps . the accumulation rate drops by an order of magnitude to approximately 0.006 to 0.02 females per step . the aggregate series reaches its maximum value of approximately 124 females killed around step 1826 . following this peak, the metric exhibits mild fluctuations and a slight decreasing trend through step 2087 . the metric achieves local maxima of approximately 2.875 at step 1073, 3.45 at step 1214, and 4.3 at step 1392, before reaching the global maximum value of 5.6 around step 1673 . following step 1624, the trend reverses to a statistically significant decrease (slope of -0.005) that persists through step 2087, with the cumulative count declining from its peak toward terminal values . between steps 500 and 1000, the decline continues but at a slower rate, with populations decreasing from roughly 20–40 individuals to fewer than 10 . minor temporary fluctuations occur at these low levels, including brief increases in some windows . the final extinction occurs across all runs by step 2310 . the terminal phase, beginning around step 875, is characterized by values below 1,000 units approaching asymptotic zero . later windows exhibit mixed behavior including a significant increasing trend in window 7 (steps 1,392-1,623, tau = 0.548) and weak fluctuations near zero in subsequent windows, reflecting stochastic dynamics of small remnant populations . from step 875 through step 1,391, the system persisted at low energy levels with minimal average decline
