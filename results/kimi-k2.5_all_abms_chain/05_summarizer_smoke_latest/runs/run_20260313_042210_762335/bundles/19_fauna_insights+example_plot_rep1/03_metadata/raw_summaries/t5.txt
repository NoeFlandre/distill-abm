the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments testing hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . it integrates environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the model aims to examine how interactions between environmental conditions and human hunting pressure influence extinction dynamics . the number of active human hunter agents remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period, from the initial step through approximately step 2200 . this stability reflects the parameter configuration where foragers are immortal and do not reproduce, resulting in a static population size that neither declines due to mortality nor increases through recruitment . a fundamental limit imposed by either prey population dynamics or hunter foraging constraints under the specified parameter configuration . the self-limiting nature of the accumulation curve suggests that male megaherbivore populations are vulnerable to rapid depletion under the modeled hunting intensity . fewer than ten total cumulative births across approximately 5.5 years confirm low reproductive output characteristic of the megafaunal life history strategy . extinction risk assessments must explicitly account for demographic stochasticity, as modest variations in early mortality or herd spatial distribution can produce order-of-magnitude differences . male animal population initiates at approximately 125 individuals and follows a deterministic trajectory toward extinction across all simulation runs . in the initial interval from step 0 to approximately step 500, the population experiences rapid depletion, falling sharply from 125 to below 20 individuals . this steep decline suggests immediate and sustained hunting pressure exceeding the population's capacity for reproduction and survival . the majority of trajectories converge near zero by step 1,200, though several runs exhibit extended persistence tails where 1-5 individuals survive beyond step 2,100 . this variance emerges from the interaction between the fixed hunting pressure from immortal foragers, demographic stochasticity in herd reproduction constrained by 730-tick birth spacing intervals and spatial heterogeneity in the 211211 wrapped environment . the current configuration produces an unsustainable system where hunting pressure overwhelms prey population viability . the temporal variation in collapse (ranging from 500 to 2,200 steps) indicates that while stochastic factors may delay extinction, they cannot prevent it under these parameters settings .
