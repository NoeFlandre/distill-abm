The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands maintains a constant value of 20 throughout the simulation period, beginning at time step 0 and persisting unchanged through time step 2,312. This absolute stability—with zero variance across all 40 replicated runs—reflects the model's configuration that prevents hunter mortality from energy depletion and omits demographic processes such as reproduction or recruitment. Without mechanisms for population increase or decrease, the initial cohort of 20 foragers remains static regardless of environmental conditions or foraging success.

Implications for decision makers: This fixed hunter density provides a controlled experimental baseline for isolating the effects of sustained hunting pressure on megaherbivore population dynamics, though it removes the ecological feedback loops where hunter populations might typically fluctuate in response to prey availability, environmental stress, or demographic stochasticity.
