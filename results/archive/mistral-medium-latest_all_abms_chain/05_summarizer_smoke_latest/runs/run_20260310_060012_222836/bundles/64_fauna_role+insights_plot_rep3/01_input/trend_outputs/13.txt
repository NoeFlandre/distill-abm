The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent trend across simulations. Initially, the total energy of all animals is approximately 25,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period, followed by a sharp decline starting around 500 steps. By approximately 1,000 steps, the total energy of all animals drops significantly to around 5,000 units. This rapid decrease continues, and by around 2,000 steps, the total energy stabilizes near zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy among the animals. This trend suggests a rapid and severe decline in the energy reserves of the prey population, likely due to a combination of hunting pressure, environmental factors, and internal dynamics of the animal population. For decision-makers, this implies that under the given parameters, the prey population faces a critical energy crisis leading to potential extinction or severe depletion within a relatively short timeframe. Understanding these dynamics can help in evaluating the sustainability of hunting practices and the resilience of the prey population under different environmental conditions.
