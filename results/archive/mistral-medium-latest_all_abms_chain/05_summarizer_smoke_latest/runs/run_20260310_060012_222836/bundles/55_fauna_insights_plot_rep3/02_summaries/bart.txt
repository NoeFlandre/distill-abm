 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history . The landscape is a simplified grassland environment with patches of grass, ungulates, and human hunters . The environment can be controlled by adjusting grass density and regrowth time .  The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages, followed by a gradual leveling off as time progresses . For decision makers, this trend suggests that initial hunting efforts are highly effective but may lead to diminishing returns over time .  The data from repeated simulations of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of male animals over time . For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the male animal population is not sustainable over the long term .  Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a rapid decline in energy of female animals in the model . Trend suggests that female animals' energy reserves are being depleted quickly at first, likely due to high initial hunting pressure and environmental factors . For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters and conditions, the male animal population experiences a rapid and sustained reduction in energy .
