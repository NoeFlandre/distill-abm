The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and rapid decline over time. Initially, the total energy of all animals starts at approximately 25,000 units. Within the first 500 steps, there is a steep decline in total energy, dropping to around 5,000 units. This rapid decrease continues more gradually, reaching near-zero levels by approximately 2,000 steps. Beyond this point, the total energy of all animals remains close to zero for the remainder of the simulation. This trend indicates a significant reduction in the energy available within the prey population over time, suggesting a potential decline in the prey population or their energy reserves. For decision-makers, this implies that under the given parameters and conditions, the prey population's energy resources are being depleted rapidly, which could lead to potential extinction or severe reduction in the population if the trends continue unabated.
