The total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations shows a consistent and rapid decline from an initial value of 250 animals. The decline begins immediately and continues steeply until around 1000 steps, where the number of animals drops to near zero. By approximately 1500 steps, the number of animals stabilizes at zero, indicating complete extinction. This trend suggests that under the given parameter settings, the prey population is unable to sustain itself, leading to a swift and total collapse. For decision makers, this implies that the current configuration of parameters, which includes factors such as hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and animal demographics, results in an unsustainable scenario for the prey population. Adjustments to parameters such as initial prey numbers, food values, processing costs, and environmental factors may be necessary to explore scenarios where the prey population can achieve equilibrium rather than extinction.
