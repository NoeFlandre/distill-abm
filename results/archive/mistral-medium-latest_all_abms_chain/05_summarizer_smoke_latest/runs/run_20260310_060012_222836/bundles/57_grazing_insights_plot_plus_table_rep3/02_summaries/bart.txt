 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The mean, median, standard deviation, and variance are all consistent with a constant value of 0.0074 . The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates no trend across all time windows, reinforcing the lack of change over time . The average household risk attitude in the simulation exhibits distinct phases over the 100 steps .  The mean destock of households shows a sharp initial increase, peaking at approximately 6.61 livestock at step 3 . This is followed by a rapid decline to around 3.33 livestock by step 8 . The oscillation analysis indicates a dominant period of 49 time steps, suggesting cyclical patterns in livestock numbers that could be important for long-term planning and management .  The number of non-learning households fluctuates slightly but remains relatively stable around a mean of approximately 15 households . The statistical analysis indicates significant decreasing trends in the early phases of the simulation . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing policies or strategies that account for the initial volatility and subsequent stabilization in household behaviors .
