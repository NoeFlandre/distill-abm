The decline continues more gradually until around time step 37, where the metric reaches its minimum value of approximately 41. After reaching the minimum, there is a gradual increase in livestock numbers, stabilizing around a value of 47.5 by the end of the simulation at time step 100. This recovery and stabilization phase suggests that households have adapted their strategies to better align with environmental conditions and resource availability. The presence of significant decreasing trends in the early phases followed by increasing trends indicates a shift from initial stress responses to more sustainable management practices. For decision makers, the initial rapid decline highlights the importance of preparing for short-term adjustments in livestock management. The subsequent stabilization suggests that long-term strategies can lead to a balanced and sustainable livestock population, emphasizing the need for adaptive management practices.
