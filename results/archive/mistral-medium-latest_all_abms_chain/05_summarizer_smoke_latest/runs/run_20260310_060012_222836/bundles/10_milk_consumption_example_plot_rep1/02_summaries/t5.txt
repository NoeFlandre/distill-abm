agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero . this slow increase indicates a cautious adoption phase . the rate of increase accelerates significantly after this point . the mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 . it remains relatively constant for the first few steps, indicating a minor shift in habits . by the 15th step, there is a slight increase, suggesting a significant change in habits.
