 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 30 steps . At step 0, the total choices made are 0, reflecting the initial state of the simulation . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,600 units . It declines rapidly within the first 10 steps, reaching around 1,000 units . This sharp decline could be attributed to a significant shift in consumer preferences and behaviors favoring alternative milk types .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 . It remains relatively constant for the first few steps, indicating a stable initial habit factor among the agents . Around the 5th step, there is a slight increase, suggesting a minor shift in habits . Following this, the habit factor continues to rise gradually until approximately the 10th step where it begins to increase more rapidly .
