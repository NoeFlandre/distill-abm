 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  There is no variance or fluctuation observed in the data, indicating a stable and unchanging initial risk attitude among households . The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis reveals significant increasing trends in the early phases (0-32 time steps) followed by significant decreasing trends (33-65 time steps).  The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at 0.0, indicating perfect equality . The trend shows a rapid initial decline in destocking, followed by a more gradual reduction and stabilization over time . The presence of several peaks and valleys in the early time steps indicates variability and instability in the households' ability to manage their livestock under changing conditions .  The number of non-learning households starts at 20 and exhibits a slight increase in the early time steps, peaking at around 21.1 at step 8 . The trend shows significant decreasing phases, particularly between steps 11 to 65 . The dominant period of oscillation is around 49 steps, indicating cyclical patterns in the data .
