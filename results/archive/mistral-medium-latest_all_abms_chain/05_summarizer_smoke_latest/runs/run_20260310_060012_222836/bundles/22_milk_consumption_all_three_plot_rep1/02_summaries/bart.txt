 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at a low value close to zero . Over the first 10 time steps, there is a rapid increase in the consumption, reaching approximately 1,200 units . The trend shows that the market has reached a new equilibrium . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing more effective strategies for promoting healthier or more sustainable consumption patterns .  The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,600 units at the beginning of the simulation . Over the first 10 steps, there is a rapid decline in the mean whole milk quantity, dropping to around 1,000 units . This sharp decrease suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior .  The slow start followed by a rapid increase underscores the need for targeted interventions and consistent messaging to encourage the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . The consistent upward trend suggests that the initial habits formed earlier are being reinforced over time, leading to a stronger preference for these milk types .
