agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. time step 20 to 30, the consumption continues to decrease gradually, reaching approximately 500 units . this gradual decline suggests a continued but slower transition away from whole milk, possibly due to sustained social influences . the rapid initial decline suggests that targeted campaigns or policy changes can have a substantial impact in the short term . the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,600 units . this high initial value indicates a strong preference or habit for whole milk consumption among the agents at the beginning of the simulation . over the first 10 steps, there is a rapid decline in the mean whole milk quantity, dropping to around 1,000 units, indicating a significant shift in consumer behavior . between step 20 and step 30, the mean habit factor continues to rise steadily, ultimately reaching a value close to 1.6 . this sustained increase indicates that the habit of consuming skimmed and semi-skimmed milk is becoming deeply ingrained among the agents . the consistent upward trend suggests that the initial habits formed earlier are being reinforced over time . it suggests that interventions aimed at promoting environmentally friendly choices should consider the strong impact of social networks and established habits on consumer decisions . social networks should also consider the impact of habits and habits on consumers .
