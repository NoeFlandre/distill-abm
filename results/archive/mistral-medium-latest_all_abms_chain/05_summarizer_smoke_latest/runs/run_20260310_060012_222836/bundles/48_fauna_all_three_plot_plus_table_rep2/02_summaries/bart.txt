 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters, and the model provides summary statistics such as the number of animals in the environment over time .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains at this level until around 725 time steps . The rate of deaths slows down significantly after this initial period, indicating that the most susceptible individuals are hunted early on . For decision-makers, this trend highlights the critical vulnerability period for postpartum females and the importance of early intervention if conservation efforts are to be effective .  The total energy of male animals begins at 12,500 units and exhibits a rapid decline within the first 500 time steps . The decline continues steadily, albeit at a slower rate, until around 1000 time steps, where the number of animals drops to near zero . For decision-makers, this trend underscores the importance of considering the impacts of hunting pressure and environmental factors on prey populations, particularly females .  After 1,000 time steps, the total energy levels gradually approach zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of the female prey population's energy reserves . For decision-makers, these trends underscore the critical need to consider the sustainability of hunting practices and the ecological balance within the modeled environment . Understanding these dynamics can inform conservation efforts and management practices aimed at sustaining prey populations in the face of hunting and environmental challenges .
