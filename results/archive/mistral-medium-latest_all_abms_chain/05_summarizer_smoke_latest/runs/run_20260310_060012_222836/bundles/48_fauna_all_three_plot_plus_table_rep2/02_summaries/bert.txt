the megaherbivore hunting pressure model ( mhpm ) is an agent - based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores. the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters.  the model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix. the number of human hunter - gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant at 20 throughout the entire duration of the simulation. the number of males killed stabilizes around 120 after approximately 1876 steps, with minor fluctuations observed thereafter. this information can be crucial for developing strategies to manage hunting pressures and ensure the long - term sustainability of prey populations. the cumulative number of postpartum females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly in the first 500 time steps, reaching approximately 110. the rate of increase slows down significantly after this initial period, indicating that the most susceptible individuals are hunted early on. this initial surge in deaths can be attributed to the initial interactions between the prey and human hunters, as well as natural mortality factors. the steep rise indicates a high initial predation rate and possibly high natural mortality due to the initial conditions of the simulation. the number of female prey animals in the simulation starts at 125 and exhibits a rapid decline within the first 500 time steps. the total number of animals in the simulation starts at approximately 167 and exhibits a rapid decline over the initial 500 time steps. however, these periods of stabilization are short - lived, and the overall trend continues to decline towards extinction. the simulation ends with the total energy of male animals approaching zero, indicating a near - complete depletion of the male animal population. this period of slower decline indicates a possible adjustment phase where the remaining female animals adapt to the environmental pressures. this high initial value indicates a healthy and abundant population of prey animals at the beginning of the simulation. this decline could be attributed to increased grazing pressure or environmental factors affecting grass regrowth.
