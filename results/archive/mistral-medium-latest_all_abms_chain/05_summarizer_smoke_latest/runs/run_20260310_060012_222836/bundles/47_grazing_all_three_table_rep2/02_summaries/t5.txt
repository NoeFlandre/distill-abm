agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis reveals significant increasing trends in deviation during the initial phases (0-32 time steps), followed by significant decreasing trends (33-65 time steps) and eventually stabilizing with no significant trends (66-98 time steps). this pattern highlights the dynamic nature of household decision-making and the impact of learning and adaptation over time . the trend generally decreases over time, with significant decreasing trends identified in the early phases . there is a brief period of no significant trend (33-43 and 55-65 time steps), suggesting a stabilization phase where the number of livestock destocked remains relatively constant . for decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial adaptive strategies to mitigate rapid destocking . the dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps suggests some cyclical patterns in the destocking process, possibly linked to periodic environmental or economic conditions affecting livestock management . this trend implies a gradual shift away from the E-RO strategy among households .
