 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  In simulations, the mean household risk attitude starts at a neutral value of 0.5 and 0.17, and the lowest value is around -0.1.1 . During the first 20 steps of the simulation, there is a noticeable divergence in the risk attitudes among households . This divergence indicates that households are adapting their risk attitudes based on initial observations and interactions within the model .  The initial rapid decline in the average total number of livestock suggests a significant adjustment period where households may be adapting to initial conditions or external factors . The rapid initial adjustment period highlights the importance of flexible and responsive strategies in the early stages of management . For decision makers, these trends highlight the potential for increasing inequality in livestock distribution .  Initially, from step 0 to around step 20, there is a steep rise in the mean destock total, indicating that households are destocking livestock at a high rate . As the system stabilizes, the slower rate of destocking suggests that . households are adapting to the conditions and finding a sustainable balance .
