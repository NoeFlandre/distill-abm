the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the values of the metric are distributed across a range from approximately -0.1 to 0.17 . the highest value is 0.17, and the lowest value is around -0.2 . majority of the values are clustered around the middle of this range . however, the initial risk attitudes of households are stable . the metric starts at a high value of approximately 90 units at the initial time step . there is a rapid decline in the first 20 steps, dropping to around 45 units . this sharp decrease is followed by a period of stabilization . the gradual increase continues until the end of the simulation at step 100 . trends indicate that social learning behaviors spread quickly initially and then stabilize . this suggests that interventions aimed at promoting social learning could see rapid initial adoption followed by a stabilization period .
