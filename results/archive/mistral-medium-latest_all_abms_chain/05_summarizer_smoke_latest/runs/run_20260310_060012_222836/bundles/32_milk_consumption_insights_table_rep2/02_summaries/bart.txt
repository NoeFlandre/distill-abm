 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices . The average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent in the simulation shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time .  Starting from an initial value of 0, the total number of milk choices rose steadily to reach approximately 25,643.83 by the end of the simulation . The mean value of the series is 11,157.35, with a median of 10,679.43, suggesting a right-skewed distribution . Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . There is a period of stabilization, where consumption values oscillate around a high mean of approximately 1220 units, with minor fluctuations and no significant trend . The dominant period of oscillation is around 15 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in the consumption behavior .  The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005 . The consistent increase in the mean percentage difference implies that over time, the disparity in choice function scores among agents grows, which could indicate increasing diversity in agent preferences or behaviors .
