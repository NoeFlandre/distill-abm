The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents in the simulation shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the average consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units per week per person. This value experiences a significant drop in the early time steps, decreasing rapidly. By the mid-point of the simulation, the consumption has reduced to around 773.96 units, indicating a substantial decline. The trend continues to decline, albeit with some fluctuations and minor peaks and valleys. Notable peaks occur at around 773.96 units and 620.98 units, while valleys are observed at approximately 760.00 units and 594.35 units. Towards the end of the simulation, the consumption further reduces to a low of about 253.10 units. The data exhibits several inflection points, suggesting periods where the rate of decline changes. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant decreasing trends in multiple segments of the time series, reinforcing the overall downward trajectory. The dominant period of oscillation is 15 time steps, indicating some periodic fluctuations within the general decline. For decision makers, this trend suggests a significant shift away from whole milk consumption over the simulated period, which could be influenced by the model's parameters such as health perceptions, social influences, and habit formation. This insight may be crucial for understanding consumer behavior and planning market strategies accordingly.
