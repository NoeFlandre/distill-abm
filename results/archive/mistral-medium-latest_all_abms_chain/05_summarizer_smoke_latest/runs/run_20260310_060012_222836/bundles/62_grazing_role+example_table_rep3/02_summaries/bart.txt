 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model aims to evaluate the long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types and the impact of various ecological and climatic parameters on the system .  Simulation shows periods of strong oscillation, particularly around the early to mid-time steps, suggesting dynamic adjustments and reactions among the households . The dominant period of oscillation is around 49 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in the adjustment behaviors of the households. This overall pattern suggests a system that initially experiences a reduction in biomass due to grazing but then recovers .  The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households starts at zero and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . The number of households following the E-RO strategy starts at 20.1 at around the 8th time step . By time step 7, approximately 10 households have adopted social learning, indicating an early and swift uptake of this behavior . The dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps suggests cyclical patterns in the adoption rate .
