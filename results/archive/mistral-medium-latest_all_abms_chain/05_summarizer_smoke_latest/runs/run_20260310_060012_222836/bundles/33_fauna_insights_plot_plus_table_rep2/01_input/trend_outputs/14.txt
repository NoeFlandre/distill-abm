The percentage of green patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable. Around the 365th tick, there is a notable decline to a minimum of about 24.91%. Following this drop, the percentage of green patches rapidly increases, stabilizing again around 33%. This pattern of stability, sharp decline, and rapid recovery repeats periodically throughout the simulation. The key inflection points occur around the 150th, 300th, 475th, 725th, and 2310th ticks, indicating significant changes in the trend. The dominant period of oscillation is approximately 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the availability of green patches. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends initially, followed by periods of decreasing trends, highlighting the dynamic nature of the landscape's vegetation coverage. For decision makers, these trends suggest periodic fluctuations in the availability of fodder for prey animals, which could influence the survival and movement patterns of both prey and hunters in the model.
