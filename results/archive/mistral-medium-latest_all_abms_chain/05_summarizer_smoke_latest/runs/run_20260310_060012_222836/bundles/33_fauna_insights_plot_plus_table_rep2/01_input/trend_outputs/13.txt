The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high initial value indicates a large amount of energy collectively held by the prey animals at the beginning of the simulation. As the simulation progresses, there is a rapid decline in the total energy, particularly noticeable in the early stages. By around 500 steps, the total energy has dropped significantly to approximately 5,000 units. This sharp decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, and by around 1,000 steps, the total energy further reduces to about 1,000 units. Beyond this point, the decline becomes more gradual, eventually approaching zero as the simulation nears its end at 2,312 steps. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes and tau values across different segments of the simulation timeline. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points suggests periods where the rate of decline varies, but the overall trajectory remains downward. The oscillation summary indicates some periodic fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the dominant decreasing trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters and conditions of the MHPM, the energy reserves of the prey population are consistently depleted over time, which could lead to potential extinction or severe reduction in the prey population if the simulation were to continue under the same conditions.
