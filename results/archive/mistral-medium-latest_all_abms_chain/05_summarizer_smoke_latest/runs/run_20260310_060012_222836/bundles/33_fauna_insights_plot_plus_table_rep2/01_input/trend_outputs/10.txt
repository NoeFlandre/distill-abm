The total number of prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and significant decreasing trend over time. Initially, the number of animals starts at approximately 166.67 and rapidly declines. The most substantial drop occurs within the first 2000 steps, where the number of animals decreases sharply. This rapid decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, until around 7000 steps, where the number of animals approaches zero. Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks, but the overall trend remains a steady decline. Key points in the simulation show slight increases or stabilizations, but these are short-lived and do not significantly alter the downward trajectory. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant Mann-Kendall test results indicating consistent declines across various segments of the simulation. The implications for decision-makers suggest that under the given parameters and conditions, the prey population is highly susceptible to extinction pressures, emphasizing the need for careful consideration of hunting behaviors, environmental factors, and demographic variables in conservation strategies.
