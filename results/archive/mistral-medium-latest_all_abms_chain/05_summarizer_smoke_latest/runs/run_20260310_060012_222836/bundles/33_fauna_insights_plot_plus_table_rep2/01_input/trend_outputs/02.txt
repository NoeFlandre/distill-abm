The total energy of foragers in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy starts at 2000 units. In the early stages, there are minor fluctuations, with peaks and valleys occurring around similar values, such as a peak at 1989.925 units and a valley at 1987.65 units. However, these fluctuations are minimal and the overall trend remains a steady decline. By around 400 steps, the energy level drops to approximately -2005.95 units, indicating a sharp decrease. This downward trend continues uniformly, with the energy levels reaching around -23807 units by 1594 steps and further declining to approximately -37713 units by 2284 steps. The decline persists, culminating in a final energy level of -38233 units at the end of the simulation period. The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decrease, with significant negative slopes observed in all rolling Mann-Kendall tests across different segments of the simulation. The change points identified at steps 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 mark significant stages in the energy decline, but the overall trend remains uniformly downward. For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the energy status of foragers deteriorates continuously over time, which could imply increasing difficulties in sustaining hunting activities and potentially leading to critical energy shortages among forager bands.
