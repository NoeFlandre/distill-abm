 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The temporal units are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters, such as the number of prey items taken, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and animals in the environment over time .  The number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation . For decision-makers, these trends imply that initial hunting pressure is intense but eventually stabilizes . Understanding these dynamics can help in assessing the impact of different parameters on the prey population and in making informed decisions regarding conservation strategies .  Key change points are observed at approximately 125, 250, 375, 500, and 875 steps, marking significant shifts in the rate of decline . The dominant period of oscillation is around 63 steps, indicating periodic fluctuations in the number of male animals . Under the given parameters, male animals face a rapid and significant decline, stabilizing at very low numbers .  The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time . For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, the energy reserves of the prey population are consistently depleted over time, leading to a potential extinction scenario .
