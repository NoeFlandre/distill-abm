 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices . The data suggests a consistent reduction in whole milk consumption over the observed timeframe .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the entire simulation period . This consistent upward trend indicates a continuous and uniform adoption of milk consumption behaviors among the agents . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents begins at approximately 2654.81 units . There is a rapid decline in consumption within the initial time steps, dropping significantly . This sharp rise indicates a swift shift in consumer preferences .  The mean factor applied to the skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choice function due to habit starts at a value of 1.0000 . Initially, there is no significant trend observed in the first few time steps . The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across all segments of the simulation .
