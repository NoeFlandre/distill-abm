 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent in the simulation demonstrates distinct trends over time . The data also exhibits some oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 15 time steps, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the rate of decline . For decision makers, these trends highlight a significant shift away from whole milk consumption .  Initially, the number of times agents chose these milk types started at zero . There was a rapid increase in adoption, with the final value reaching approximately 25,643.83 . The mean number of choices across the simulation period was about 11,157.35, with a median of 10,679.43 .  Understanding these dynamics can help in designing more effective and timely interventions to promote healthier milk consumption habits . For decision makers, this trend implies a growing influence of habit on the choice of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time, which could be leveraged in marketing strategies and public health campaigns .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages, reaching approximately 13.4756 by the 13th time step . For decision makers, the consistent increase in mean deviation implies a growing discrepancy between perceived health values and actual milk choices .
