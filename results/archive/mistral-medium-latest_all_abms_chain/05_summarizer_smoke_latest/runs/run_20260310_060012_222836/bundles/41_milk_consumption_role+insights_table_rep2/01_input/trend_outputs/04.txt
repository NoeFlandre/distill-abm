The simulation results indicate a consistent and significant upward trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk by agents over the observed time period. Initially, the number of times agents chose these milk types started at zero. However, there was a rapid increase in adoption, with the final value reaching approximately 25,643.83. The mean number of choices across the simulation period was about 11,157.35, with a median of 10,679.43, indicating a skewed distribution towards higher values in the later stages of the simulation. The standard deviation of 8,241.32 and variance of 67,919,370.31 suggest substantial variability in the adoption rates over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results consistently show significant increasing trends across various segments of the simulation, with high tau values and significant p-values, reinforcing the upward trajectory in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The detection of a dominant oscillation period of 15.00 and strong oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation suggest some periodic fluctuations in the adoption rates. The inflection point at index 31 and the change point at index 32 mark significant shifts in the trend dynamics. For decision makers, these trends highlight the growing preference for healthier milk options over time, driven by the model's parameters such as social influence, health perceptions, and habit formation. This shift underscores the importance of considering these factors in strategies aimed at promoting healthier dietary choices.
