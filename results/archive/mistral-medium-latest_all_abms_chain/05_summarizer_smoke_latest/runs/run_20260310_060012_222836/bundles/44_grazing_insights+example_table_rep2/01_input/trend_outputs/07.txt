The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at a value of 0.0, indicating perfect equality. Over the course of the simulation, the Gini coefficient exhibits a consistent increasing trend, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 by the end of the 100 time steps. This trend suggests a gradual increase in inequality among households in terms of healthy livestock distribution. The consistent increase is statistically significant throughout the entire duration of the simulation, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results, which show significant increasing trends in each decade of time steps. The change points detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100 indicate notable shifts in the rate of increase in inequality. The dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps suggests a cyclical pattern in the data, although the overall trend remains upward. For decision-makers, this trend implies that over time, disparities in livestock distribution among households are likely to grow, which could have implications for resource allocation and support strategies aimed at promoting equity in livestock distribution.
