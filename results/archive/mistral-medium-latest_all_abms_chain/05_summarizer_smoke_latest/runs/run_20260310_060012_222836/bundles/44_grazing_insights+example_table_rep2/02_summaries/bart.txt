 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  There are no significant trends or changes detected in any segment of the simulation, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results . The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents begins at a low value of approximately 0.0074 . This metric shows a rapid increase in the early stages, reaching a peak value of about 0.3814 around the 33rd time step .  The average number of livestock destocked across all households begins at zero and exhibits a sharp increase, peaking significantly at the third time step with a value of 6.6144 . The trend generally decreases over time, with significant decreasing trends identified in the initial phases (0-10, 11-21, 22-32, and 44-54 time steps)  The number of E-RO households declines steadily, reaching a low point of approximately 14.6667 at time step 92 . The dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps suggests some cyclical patterns in the destocking process . For decision-makers, this trend implies a steady reduction in livestock numbers over time, which could have significant implications for household livelihoods .
