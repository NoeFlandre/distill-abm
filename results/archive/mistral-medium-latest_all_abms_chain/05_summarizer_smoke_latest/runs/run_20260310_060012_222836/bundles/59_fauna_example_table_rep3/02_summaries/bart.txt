 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history . The landscape is a simplified grassland environment with patches of grass, ungulates, and human hunters .  The energy levels of the forager bands show a continuous and significant decrease, indicating a persistent negative energy balance influenced by the costs of hunting and movement outweighing the energy gains from successful hunts . Overall, the cumulative number of male prey hunted stabilizes at a high value, indicating sustained hunting pressure throughout the simulation . The simulation exhibits periodic oscillations with a dominant period of 63 time steps .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model began with a rapid increase in the early stages of population growth and adaptation within the model . Initially, the birth count remains relatively low, reflecting the early phases of growth, peak reproductive success, and subsequent stabilization . The model shows strong oscillations with a dominant period of 50 time steps, suggesting regular fluctuations in the birth rate .  The total number of prey animals in the simulation starts at approximately 167 and exhibits a rapid decline over the initial time steps . This sharp decrease could be attributed to the initial high hunting pressure and the immediate energy consumption by the male prey population . After the 875th time step, the energy levels continue to decrease but at a much slower rate . The simulation ends with the prey population reaching zero, indicating complete extinction .  The total energy available in prey animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of the prey population . The percentage of landscape patches that are vegetated begins at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable throughout the simulation . The simulation exhibits minor fluctuations, with peaks and valleys showing minimal prominence, suggesting a consistent and stable landscape vegetation coverage .
