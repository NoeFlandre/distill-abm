 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The temporal units are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters, and the model provides summary statistics such as the number of prey items taken and the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass . The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population exhibits several distinct trends . For decision makers, these trends highlight critical periods where intervention might be necessary to prevent high mortality rates . In the early stages of the simulation, up to around 500 steps, there is a gradual decline in the number of animals . This decline continues steeply until approximately the 1,000-step mark, where the total energy drops to around 2,000 units .  Percentage of green patches starts at approximately 32% and remains stable for a short period . Around 200 steps, there is a sharp decline to about 24% indicating a significant reduction in vegetated patches . This decline is followed by a rapid recovery to around 32% by approximately 500 steps .
