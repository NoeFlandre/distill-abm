The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent downward trend over the simulation period. Initially, the total energy of foragers is around 0. As the simulation progresses, the total energy gradually decreases, reaching approximately -5000 by around 500 steps. This decline continues steadily, with the total energy dropping to about -10000 by 1000 steps. The trend persists, and by 1500 steps, the total energy is around -20000. Towards the end of the simulation, at around 2000 steps, the total energy of foragers reaches approximately -35000. This continuous decline in the total energy of foragers suggests that the energy expenditure due to movement costs and possibly reduced hunting success outweighs the energy gained from consuming prey. For decision makers, this trend indicates that the current parameters may not be sustainable for the foragers in the long term, potentially leading to a scenario where foragers may face energy deficits. This insight could prompt a review of the parameters related to energy costs and gains, such as processing costs, food values, and movement costs, to ensure the viability of the forager population within the model.
