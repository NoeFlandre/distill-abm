agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. from steps 5 to 20, the consumption continues to decrease but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 700 units . this phase indicates a period of adjustment where agents are gradually solidifying their new habits and preferences . the long-term adoption of new milk types is influenced by habit formation and social conformity . the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined exhibits a linear growth trend over the 30 steps of the simulation . by step 10, approximately 10,000 choices have been made . this upward trend continues consistently, reaching around 20,000 choices by step 20 . over the first 10 time steps, there is a sharp decline in the mean whole milk quantity . the mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the 30 steps of the simulation . the initial slow increase indicates a growing reinforcement of these habits over time, likely due to repeated consumption and positive reinforcement mechanisms within the model. the mean value health deviation continues until the end of the simulation at step 30, where it reaches approximately 0.65 . this final phase suggests a minor but consistent adjustment in health perceptions, possibly due to ongoing social interactions and the reinforcement of habits .
