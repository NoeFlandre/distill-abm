 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . Model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice . The ABM is designed to replicate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 .  Simulation of the agent-based model shows the trends in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption over a series of steps . By the final step, the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent reaches approximately 253 units .  The mean and median values of the total choices made over the entire period are both 16,000, indicating a symmetric distribution of values around the center . The minimum value remains at 0 throughout the initial time step, while the maximum value reaches 32,000 at the final time step . The dominant period of oscillation is 15 time steps, with strong oscillations observed towards the end of the time series .  The mean habit factor for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk shows a clear trend over the simulation period . No peaks or valleys are detected, but there are inflection points at indices 7 and 12, suggesting subtle changes in the rate of increase . The data exhibits a dominant oscillation period of 15 steps, with several strong oscillation indices identified .  The deviation stabilizes around 13.4 from step 13 onwards, with minor fluctuations . The peaks and valleys in the data are very close in value, suggesting a stable plateau after the initial rise . The inflection points and the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicate that the significant changes occur primarily in the early steps .
