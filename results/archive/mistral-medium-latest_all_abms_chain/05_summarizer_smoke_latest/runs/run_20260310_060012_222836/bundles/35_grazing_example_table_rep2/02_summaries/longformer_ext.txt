The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system. The extension adds learning capabilities to agents, focusing on learning-by-doing and social learning, which are central to adaptive co-management. The model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors. The primary goals are to evaluate the long-term social-ecological performance of different behavioral types and to study the diffusion of behavioral strategies in a heterogeneous population.
