 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  Average degree of deviation from recommended number of livestock for all extension agents begins at a low value of approximately 0.0074 . No significant peaks or valleys, and the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms the absence of any trends across all time segments . System's stability is further evidenced by the lack of significant change points and the consistent values observed at various indices .
