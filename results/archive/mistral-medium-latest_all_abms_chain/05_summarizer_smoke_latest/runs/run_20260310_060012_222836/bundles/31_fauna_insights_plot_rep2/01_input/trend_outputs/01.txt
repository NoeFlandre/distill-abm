In the simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM), the number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the entire duration of the simulation. Initially, the number of foragers is set to 20, and this value does not change over the 73000 ticks of the simulation. This stability indicates that the parameters and conditions set for the model do not lead to any significant changes in the number of active hunter-gatherer bands. For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given conditions and parameter settings, the population of human hunter-gatherer bands is in a stable equilibrium, neither increasing nor decreasing over time. This could imply that the environmental and social factors influencing the hunter-gatherer population are balanced, leading to a steady state. Understanding this stability can help in assessing the long-term sustainability of the hunter-gatherer lifestyle under the simulated conditions.
