 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with relative spatial scale .  The total energy of foragers begins at a value of 0 and shows a gradual decline over time . The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands exhibits a rapid increase initially, followed by a gradual plateau . Understanding this stability can help in assessing the long-term sustainability of the hunter-gatherer lifestyle .  Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations show a consistent trend in the total number of prey animals over time . For decision-makers, these trends imply that initial conditions and early interactions significantly impact mortality rates . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions or conservation strategies to mitigate high early mortality rates and promote long-term stability in prey populations .
