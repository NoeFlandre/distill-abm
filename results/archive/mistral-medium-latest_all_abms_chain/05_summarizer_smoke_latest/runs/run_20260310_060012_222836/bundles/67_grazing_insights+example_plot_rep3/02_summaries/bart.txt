 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The mean household risk attitude starts with a value of approximately 0.5 across all simulations . Some households exhibit an increase in risk attitude, while others show a decrease, dropping to values as low as -0.2 .
