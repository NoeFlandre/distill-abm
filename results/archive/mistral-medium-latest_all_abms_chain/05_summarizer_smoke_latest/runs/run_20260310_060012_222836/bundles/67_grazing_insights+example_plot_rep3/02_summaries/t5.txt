the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the mean household initial risk attitude is constant throughout the simulation . the lowest value is around 0.17, indicating a segment of households with a relatively higher initial risk attitudes . this distribution implies that decision-makers need to consider a wide spectrum of risk dispositions when designing interventions . the stabilization period suggests that long-term planning can achieve a balance between livestock needs and pasture sustainability . over the first 20 time steps, there is a rapid decline in the total livestock, dropping to around 45 . this sharp decrease suggests an initial period of adjustment where households may be destocking livestock due to limited pasture resources . the destocking rate slows down and the curves begin to flatten . by the end of the simulation, the mean destock total ranges between approximately 70 and 170 across different runs . the increasing variability over time highlights the importance of adaptive strategies to account for diverse household behaviors and conditions .
