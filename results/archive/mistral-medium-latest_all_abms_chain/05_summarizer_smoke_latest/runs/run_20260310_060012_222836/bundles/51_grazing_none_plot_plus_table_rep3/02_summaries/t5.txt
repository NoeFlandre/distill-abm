the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation period . the initial value is 0.0074, and this value does not change across all time steps, maintaining a consistent trend . a mean, median, standard deviation, and variance all reflect this stability . Initially, the destocking value starts at 0 and quickly rises to a peak of approximately 6.61 at step 3 . this peak is followed by a steep decline to around 3.37 by step 9 . by step 30, the values have significantly reduced and remain relatively stable .
