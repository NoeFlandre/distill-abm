 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The mean household risk attitude in the simulation begins with a low initial value of approximately 0.0074 . There is no variation in the mean household initial risk attitude, with a mean, median, standard deviation and variance all reflecting this stability . The mean reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps .  The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . The trend remains consistently upward, with significant change points detected at steps 51, 71, and 100 . The data shows a consistent increase in social learning adoption among households .
