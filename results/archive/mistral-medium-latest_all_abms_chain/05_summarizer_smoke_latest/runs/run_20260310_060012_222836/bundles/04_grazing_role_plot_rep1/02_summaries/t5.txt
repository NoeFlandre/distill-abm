agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . at step 100, the mean reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . the metric representing the average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches shows distinct trends . a slight decline in the biomass followed by a more pronounced decrease until around step 40, where the biomass reaches its lowest point . by the 100th step, the Gini coefficient values range approximately between 0.03 and 0.09 . this suggests that, over time, the distribution of healthy livestock becomes unequal among households in the simulated grazing system .
