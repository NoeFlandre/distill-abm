 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The mean value of whole milk choices across all time steps is around 1130.88, with a median of 1158.97, indicating a relatively balanced distribution of values throughout the simulation period . The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent increasing trend in the adoption behavior . The total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 25,643.83 at the final time step .  The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . The trend is characterized by a continuous increase, with no detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady growth pattern . The trends indicate a strong overall increase in the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, reflecting the model's underlying mechanisms of social influence, habit formation, and perceived health benefits .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at 0 and increases rapidly in the first few steps . This initial increase indicates a rapid divergence between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices . By around the 5th step, the deviation reaches approximately 12 . The deviation continues to increase but at a slower rate, stabilizing around the value of 13.4 by the 13th step .
