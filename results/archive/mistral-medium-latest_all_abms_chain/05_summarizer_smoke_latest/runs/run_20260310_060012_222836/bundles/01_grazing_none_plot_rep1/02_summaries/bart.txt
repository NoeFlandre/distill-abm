 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The mean household risk attitude starts at a baseline value of approximately 0.5 for all households . The values do not change over time, suggesting that the initial risk attitude is a fixed parameter for each household throughout the simulation period . The mean reserve biomass in the agent-based model exhibits distinct trends over the 100 steps of the simulation .  The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at 0 for all simulations, indicating an initially equal distribution of livestock among households . By step 20, the values of the Gini . coefficient range between approximately 0.01 and 0.025 . This indicates a growing disparity in livestock distribution among households.
