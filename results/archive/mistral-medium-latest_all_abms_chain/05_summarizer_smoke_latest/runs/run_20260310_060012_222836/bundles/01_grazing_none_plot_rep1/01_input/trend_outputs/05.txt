The simulations of the agent-based model show distinct trends in the mean livestock and destock of households over 100 steps. Initially, the mean livestock starts at a high value of approximately 90. This value sharply declines within the first 10 steps, dropping to around 45. Following this steep decline, the mean livestock continues to decrease gradually, reaching its lowest point of roughly 40 by step 40. After this point, the trend reverses, and the mean livestock begins to increase steadily. By step 100, the mean livestock reaches a value of approximately 60. The initial sharp decline suggests a rapid adjustment phase, followed by a period of stabilization and gradual recovery in livestock numbers.
