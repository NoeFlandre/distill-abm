 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . By step 25, the consumption rate has decreased to approximately 600 units . The overall pattern indicates a consistent reduction in whole milk consumption over time, reflecting the model's parameters and the agents' evolving preferences and behaviors .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . The mean and median values of the total choices are both 16,000, suggesting a symmetric distribution of choices over time . The dominant oscillation period and strong oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation indicate some variability in the adoption rates .  Trend analysis indicates a consistent increase in the habit factor across all rolling windows, with a significant positive slope and a Kendall tau value of 1.000 . The dominant oscillation period of 15 steps suggests a cyclical pattern in the rate of change, potentially linked to periodic social interactions or habit reinforcement cycles .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at 0.0000 and increases rapidly within the first few steps . This rapid increase indicates a significant initial divergence between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices . After this sharp rise, the deviation stabilizes around 13.4302, with minor fluctuations .
