agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at 5.29 units . this initial value indicates that at the beginning of the simulation, the consumption is minimal among the agents . over the first few time steps, there is a rapid increase in consumption, reaching around 1265.47 units by the 11th time step . the rolling Mann-Kendall test results confirm this increasing trend across all time windows, with significant positive slopes . the dominant oscillation period and strong oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation indicate some variability in the adoption rates, possibly due to the complex interplay of the various parameters influencing agent behavior . the mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at 1.0000 . this initial phase is followed by a more gradual and steady increase over the remaining steps . the habit factor reaches its maximum value of 1.0502 at the final step of the simulation . the deviation continues to rise but at a much slower pace, reflecting a period of gradual adjustment . between steps 20 and 25, the deviation remains relatively stable, hovering around 0.6 . this stability suggests a temporary equilibrium where the agents' perceptions and choices are relatively aligned . the simulation ends with the mean value health deviation near 0.725 .
