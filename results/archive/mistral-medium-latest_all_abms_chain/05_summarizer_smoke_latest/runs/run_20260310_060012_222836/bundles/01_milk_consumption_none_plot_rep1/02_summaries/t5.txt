agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . the total number of choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over the time steps . starting from 0 choices at step 0, there is a rapid decline in the quantity of whole milk consumed within the first 10 steps, dropping to around 1000 units . after step 20, the decline continues at a slower rate, reaching approximately 600 units by step 25 . the deviation continues to rise but at a slower rate, stabilizing around a value of 0.55 by step 10 . after step 25, there is a gradual increase in the mean value health deviation, reaching around 0.65 by step 30 . this final phase indicates a renewed divergence between the agents' perceptions and their actual milk choices .
