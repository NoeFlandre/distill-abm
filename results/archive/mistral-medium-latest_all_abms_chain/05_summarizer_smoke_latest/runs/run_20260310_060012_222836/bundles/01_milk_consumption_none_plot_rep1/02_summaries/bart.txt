 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model explores how perception, habits, and social influence affect individual decision-making processes regarding milk choices . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The total number of choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over the time steps . Starting from 0 choices at step 0, the total choices rises steadily, reaching approximately 32,000 choices by step 30 . The mean whole milk quantity consumed by agents starts at approximately 2600 units at the initial time step . There is a rapid decline in the quantity of whole milk consumed within the first 10 steps, dropping to around 1000 units .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero . It then rapidly increases and reaches a value of approximately 13 by around the 5th step . After this rapid increase, the deviation stabilizes and remains relatively constant around the value of 13 . This indicates that initially, there is a significant change in the environmental values implied by the agents' milk choices .
