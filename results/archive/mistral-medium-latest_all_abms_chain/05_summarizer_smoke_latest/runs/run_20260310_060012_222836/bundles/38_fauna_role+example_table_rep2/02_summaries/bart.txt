 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration . Starting at 20 bands, the value does not change at any point, maintaining a steady state from the beginning to the end of the simulation . The trend continues to rise gradually, with minor fluctuations, until it stabilizes around the 7000 time step mark . The stabilization indicates that the hunting pressure and the prey population have reached a form of equilibrium .  Statistical analysis shows a consistent decreasing trend in the number of male prey animals throughout the simulation . The number of female prey animals in the simulation starts at 125 and exhibits a rapid decline in the initial phase . This sharp decrease continues consistently over the first 927 time steps . The decline continues with some fluctuations until the end of the simulation, where the female prey population reaches zero .  The Mann-Kendall test shows significant decreasing trends in multiple segments, with brief periods of stabilization or minor increases . The overall trend continues to decline, albeit at a slower rate . The energy levels eventually approach zero as the simulation nears its end at 73,000 time steps, indicating the near-complete depletion of the female prey population's energy reserves .
