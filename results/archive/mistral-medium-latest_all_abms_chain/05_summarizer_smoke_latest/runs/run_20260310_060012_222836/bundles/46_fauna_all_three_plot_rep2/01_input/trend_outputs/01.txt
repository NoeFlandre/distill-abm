The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the simulation, starting and ending at 20. This stability suggests that the parameters governing the hunter-gatherer dynamics, such as food availability, movement costs, and energy requirements, are balanced in a way that sustains a consistent number of bands. For decision makers, this implies that under the given conditions, the environment can support a stable population of hunter-gatherer bands without leading to an increase or decrease in their number. This could be useful for understanding the carrying capacity of the environment and the sustainability of hunter-gatherer lifestyles under similar ecological and demographic conditions.
