 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters, and the model provides summary statistics such as the number of animals in the environment over time .  The number of male animals in the simulation starts at approximately 120 and exhibits a rapid decline within the first 500 steps . The low variance among simulation runs indicates consistent hunting patterns and similar outcomes across different simulations . For decision makers, understanding these trends can provide insights into the factors influencing population growth and stability .  The total energy of all animals in the simulation begins at approximately 25,000 units . It declines rapidly within the first 500 steps, dropping to around 50 animals . By around 1000 steps, the number of animals further decreases to near zero, indicating a critical threshold where the population struggles to sustain itself . This trend underscores the importance of sustainable hunting practices and habitat conservation to prevent the rapid depletion and potential extinction of vulnerable species .  The percentage of green patches recovers again and stabilizes around 32% for the remainder of the simulation . This suggests a resilient grass regrowth mechanism that maintains landscape vegetation despite continuous grazing pressure . The temporary dips in green patch percentage underscore the need to monitor grazing pressure and ensure regrowth rates are sufficient .
