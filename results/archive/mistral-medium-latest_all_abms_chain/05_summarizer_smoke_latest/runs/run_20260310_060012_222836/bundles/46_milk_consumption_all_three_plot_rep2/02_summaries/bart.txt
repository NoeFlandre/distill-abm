 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value, close to zero . This initial low consumption indicates that the majority of agents initially prefer whole milk . The overall trend indicates a successful shift in consumer behavior from whole milk to skimmed milk, driven by a combination of health awareness, social influence and habit formation .  The linear trend suggests that the rate at which agents make choices remains constant throughout the simulation period . For decision makers, this implies a predictable and steady pattern of consumer behavior, which can be useful for forecasting and planning purposes . The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at a low value, close to zero . This sharp rise suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior .  At the beginning of the simulation, there is no variability in the agents' preferences or behaviors regarding milk consumption . The mean value health deviation starts at a value close to zero, indicating that agents' health perceptions are closely aligned with their milk consumption choices . The rapid rise in the deviation increases rapidly, reaching approximately 0.55 . The subsequent stabilization suggests that after a certain point, consumer preferences become more entrenched and less susceptible to change .
