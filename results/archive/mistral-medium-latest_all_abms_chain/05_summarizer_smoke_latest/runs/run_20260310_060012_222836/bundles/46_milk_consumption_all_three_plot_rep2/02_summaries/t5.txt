agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. after time step 25, there is a slight decline followed by a minor increase around time step 30 . this variability could be due to external factors or minor adjustments in agent behavior . the final stabilization around 500 units indicates a new baseline for whole milk consumption . the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases linearly over the 30 time steps, reaching a value of approximately 32,000 . for decision makers, this implies a predictable and steady pattern of consumer behavior, which can be useful for forecasting and planning purposes . the mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of approximately 1.0 . this initial value indicates that, at the beginning of the simulation, the habit factor does not significantly influence the choice of skimmmed or semi smmed milk . over the first 10 steps, there is a slight increase in the means habit factor, reaching around 1.1 . trends in the mean value health deviation highlight the interplay between social influence, habit formation, and health perceptions in shaping milk consumption behaviors . understanding these trends can help decision-makers design targeted interventions to bridge the gap between perceived health values and actual consumption choices .
