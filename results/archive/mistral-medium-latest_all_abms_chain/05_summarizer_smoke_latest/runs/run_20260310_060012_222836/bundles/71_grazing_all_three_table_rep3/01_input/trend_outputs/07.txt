The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at a value of 0.0, indicating perfect equality. Over the course of the simulation, the Gini coefficient exhibits a consistent increasing trend, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 at the final time step. This trend suggests a gradual increase in inequality among households in terms of healthy livestock distribution. The mean value of the Gini coefficient across all time steps is 0.0302, with a median of 0.0229, indicating that while the inequality is generally low, there is a slight skew towards higher inequality values as the simulation progresses. The standard deviation of 0.0239 and variance of 0.0006 further support this observation, showing some variability in inequality levels over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend throughout the entire simulation, with consistent positive tau values and significant p-values across all time windows. This persistent increase in inequality implies that certain households are progressively accumulating more healthy livestock compared to others. The detected change points at time steps 51, 71, and 100 indicate notable shifts in the rate of inequality increase, suggesting key moments where the dynamics of livestock distribution among households may have undergone significant changes. The dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps and the strong oscillation indices towards the latter part of the simulation suggest periodic fluctuations in the inequality levels, possibly due to recurring patterns in household decision-making or environmental factors. For decision-makers, the steady increase in inequality highlights the need for interventions aimed at promoting more equitable distribution of resources and supporting households that may be at a disadvantage in accumulating healthy livestock. Understanding the factors contributing to these change points and oscillations could provide insights into the underlying mechanisms driving inequality and inform targeted policies to mitigate these trends.
