 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters, and the model provides summary statistics such as the number of animals in the environment over time .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains constant until around 700 time steps . The trend shows low variance across simulation runs, implying consistent predation patterns under the given parameters . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of monitoring and managing predation pressures and environmental conditions to maintain a stable prey population .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model shows that the animal population in the model struggles to sustain itself, ultimately leading to extinction . For decision-makers, this indicates that under the given parameters, the current environmental and hunting conditions are unsustainable . The trends indicate that the initial conditions and parameters of the model lead to rapid and substantial depletion of the energy reserves of the prey population .  Green patches quickly recovers to around 32% by time step 500 . This recovery suggests a period of regrowth and regeneration of grass patches . The percentage of green patches remains relatively stable again until around time step 1000, where another sharp decline is observed . This second decline could indicate another phase of increased grazing or environmental stress .
