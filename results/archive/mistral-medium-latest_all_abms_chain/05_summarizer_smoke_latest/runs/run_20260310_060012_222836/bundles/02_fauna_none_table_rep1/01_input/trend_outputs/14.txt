The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows relatively stable dynamics in the percentage of vegetated landscape patches over time. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33%. Throughout the simulation, this value exhibits minor fluctuations but generally remains close to the initial value. The minimum value observed is around 24.91% at time step 365, indicating a slight decrease in vegetated patches at this point. The mean percentage of vegetated patches across the simulation is approximately 32.66%, with a median value of 32.97%, suggesting that the data is relatively symmetric around the mean. The standard deviation is about 0.95, indicating low variability in the percentage of vegetated patches. The simulation exhibits several peaks and valleys, but these are not prominent, indicating minor oscillations around the mean value. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates periods of both increasing and decreasing trends, but these trends are minimal and not substantial. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the vegetation coverage, albeit with low amplitude. Overall, the percentage of vegetated patches remains relatively constant throughout the simulation, with minor fluctuations and low variability.
