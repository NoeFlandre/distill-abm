The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear trend in the population of male prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 and exhibits a consistent decreasing trend throughout the simulation. The most significant declines occur in the early stages, with steep drops observed up to around 2,310 ticks. The population experiences several peaks and valleys, indicating periodic fluctuations. Notable peaks in the male prey population occur at approximately 730 ticks with a value of 10.54, 709 ticks with a value of 8.85, and 803 ticks with a value of 6.76. Corresponding valleys are observed closely before these peaks, such as at 708 ticks with a value of 8.70 and 729 ticks with a value of 7.97. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates significant decreasing trends in the early phases, transitioning to no significant trend in the later phases of the simulation. The population eventually declines to zero by the end of the simulation at 2284 ticks. The dominant period of oscillation is around 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the population dynamics. The inflection points and change points further highlight the dynamic shifts in the population trend, marking significant transitions in the rate of decline.
