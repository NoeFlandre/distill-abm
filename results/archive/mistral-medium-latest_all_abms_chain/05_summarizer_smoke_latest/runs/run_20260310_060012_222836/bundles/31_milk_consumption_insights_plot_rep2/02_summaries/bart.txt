 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over the 30 steps of the simulation . The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents shows distinct trends over the simulated time steps . The trend suggests a growing preference for skimmed milk over time among the agents .  The mean choice function deviation starts at a value of 0 at step 0, indicating that initially, there is no difference in the choice function scores among agents . Over time, the consumption habits for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk become more ingrained in the population, reflecting a shift in consumer behavior towards these milk types .
