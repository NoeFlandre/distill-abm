agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . the average weekly consumption of each milk type starts at approximately 2600 units at the initial time step . the total number of choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over the 30 steps of the simulation . this suggests a growing preference for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time among the agents . for decision makers, this implies that interventions or factors influencing the adoption of healthier milk options are effective and gain momentum over time . by step 15, the habit factor reaches approximately 1.2, and it continues to rise, albeit at a slightly slower pace . this trend suggests that over time, the consumption habits for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk become more ingrained in the population, reflecting a shift in consumer behavior towards these milk types .
