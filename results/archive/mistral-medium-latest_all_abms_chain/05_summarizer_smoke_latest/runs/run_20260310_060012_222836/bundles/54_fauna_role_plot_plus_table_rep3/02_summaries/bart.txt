 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The temporal units are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration of the simulation .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation, followed by a gradual stabilization . Initially, the number of postpartum females killed rises sharply from 0 to approximately 117 over the first 2000 steps . The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test shows significant increasing trends in early phases .  The total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time . The model's parameters and dynamics suggest that factors such as hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and animal demographics contribute to this consistent decline .  The percentage of green patches in the landscape, representing the availability of grass for prey animals, exhibits several notable trends over the simulation period . The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in various segments of the time series, indicating a continuous reduction in the total energy of all animals .
