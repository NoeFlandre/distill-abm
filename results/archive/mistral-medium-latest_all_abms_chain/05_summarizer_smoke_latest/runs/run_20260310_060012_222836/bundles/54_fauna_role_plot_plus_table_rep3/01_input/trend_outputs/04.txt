The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the cumulative count reaches approximately 100 females killed. Following this initial phase, the rate of increase slows down significantly. Between 500 and 1500 steps, the cumulative number of females killed continues to rise but at a much slower pace, eventually stabilizing around 120 females killed. After reaching this plateau, the cumulative count exhibits minor fluctuations but generally remains stable, indicating a state of equilibrium. The statistical analysis reveals several key points, including notable change points at approximately 125, 375, 475, 625, 850, 1600, and 2310 steps, which mark shifts in the rate of increase. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early phases, transitioning to a significant decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation period. The oscillation summary suggests a dominant period of 63 steps, with strong oscillations observed towards the latter part of the simulation. Overall, the dynamics of female prey hunting show an initial rapid increase followed by a stabilization phase, reflecting the interactions between hunting pressure and prey population dynamics within the model.
