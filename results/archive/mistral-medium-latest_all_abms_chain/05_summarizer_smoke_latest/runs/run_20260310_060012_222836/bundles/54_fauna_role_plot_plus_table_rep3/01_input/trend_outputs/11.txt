The total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy of male animals starts at a high value of 12500 units. This high energy level is maintained briefly before a rapid decline begins. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has decreased significantly to approximately 4000 units. This downward trend continues, with the total energy of male animals gradually approaching zero. By around 2284 time steps, the total energy of male animals reaches zero and remains there for the rest of the simulation. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various intervals. The initial phase shows a steep decline, followed by a more gradual decrease as the energy levels approach zero. The model's parameters and dynamics suggest that factors such as hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and animal demographics contribute to this consistent decline in the total energy of male animals.
