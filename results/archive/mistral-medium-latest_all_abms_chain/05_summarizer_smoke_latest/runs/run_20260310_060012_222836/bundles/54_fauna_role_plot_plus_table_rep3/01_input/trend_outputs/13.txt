The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a pronounced decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has dropped significantly to roughly 5,000 units. This steep decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, until about 1,000 time steps, where the total energy stabilizes around 1,000 units. Beyond this point, the decline becomes more gradual, with the total energy approaching near-zero values by the end of the simulation at 73,000 time steps. Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks, but the overall trend remains a consistent decrease in total energy. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in various segments of the time series, indicating a continuous reduction in the total energy of all animals.
