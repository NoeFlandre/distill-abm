The following is a summary of the results of the study:The average amount of reserve biomass in the grazing system shows distinct trends over the simulated time steps. Initially, the reserve biomass starts at 90,000 units. In the early stages, there is a decreasing trend in biomass, reaching a minimum value of approximately 84,114 units around the 26th time step. This initial decline suggests a period where the grazing pressure or environmental conditions might be leading to a reduction in biomass. Following this low point, the system exhibits a recovery phase. From around the 33rd time step onwards, there is a consistent and significant increasing trend in reserve biomass. This upward trend continues, with the biomass reaching its peak at approximately 121,073 units by the 100th time step. The increasing trend is particularly notable for its consistency and significance, indicating a robust recovery and growth in biomass over the latter part of the simulation period. The dominant period of oscillation is around 49 time steps, suggesting cyclical patterns in biomass levels, possibly due to seasonal or periodic factors influencing the grazing system. The model also incorporates a risk parameter to represent the risk attitudes of social learning agents under the given parameters, which could inform strategies for pasture management The number of households following the E-RO strategy, which represents non-learning agents, starts at approximately 6.7, peaking significantly at time step 3 with a value of 6.6144. This peak is followed by a rapid decline, reaching a local minimum at time step 8 with a value of 3.3333. Subsequent fluctuations are observed, with notable peaks at time steps 9, 12, 17, 19, 22, and 26, and valleys at time steps 11, 16, 18, 21, 23, and 25. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a significant decreasing trend in the initial phases (time steps 0-10, 11-21, and 22-32), followed by a period of no significant trend (time steps 33-43 and 55-65), and a slight increasing trend towards the end (time steps 66-76). The dominant period of oscillation is around 49 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the destocking rates. The trend analysis indicates a gradual shift away from the E-RO strategy over time, which may indicate a transition towards other behavioral types or strategies among households.
