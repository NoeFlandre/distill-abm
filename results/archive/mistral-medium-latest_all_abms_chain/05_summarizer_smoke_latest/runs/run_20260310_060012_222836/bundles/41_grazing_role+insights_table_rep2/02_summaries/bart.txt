 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model aims to evaluate the long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types and the impact of various ecological and climatic parameters on the system .  The average amount of available green biomass across all patches shows distinct trends over the 100 time steps of the simulation . The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households shows a distinct trend . The dominant period of oscillation is 49 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the biomass dynamics .  The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . For decision makers, these trends highlight the initial volatility in livestock destocking, followed by periods of stabilization and slight recovery . Understanding these patterns can inform strategies to mitigate the impacts of resource insufficiency and enhance the resilience of pastoral systems .  The number of households following the E-RO strategy, which represents non-learning agents, starts at 20 and exhibits a general decreasing trend over the 100 time steps . Understanding these trends can help in planning adaptive management strategies to mitigate the impacts of increasing destocking rates .
