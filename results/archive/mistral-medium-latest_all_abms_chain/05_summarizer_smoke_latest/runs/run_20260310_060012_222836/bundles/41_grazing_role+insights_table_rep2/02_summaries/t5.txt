agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the average amount of reserve biomass in the grazing system shows distinct trends over the simulated time steps . Initially, the reserve biomass starts at 90,000 units . in the early stages, there is a decreasing trend in biomass, reaching a minimum value of approximately 84,114 units around the 26th time step . the average number of destocked livestock across all households exhibits notable trends over the simulated time steps . Initially, the destocking rate starts at zero and experiences a sharp increase . this peak is followed by a rapid decline, reaching a local minimum at time step 8 with a value of 3.3333 . the slopes for subsequent decades of time steps are 1.466, 0.687, and so on . change points are detected at time steps 25, 60, and 100 . this suggests a continuous and accelerating need for destocking over time .
