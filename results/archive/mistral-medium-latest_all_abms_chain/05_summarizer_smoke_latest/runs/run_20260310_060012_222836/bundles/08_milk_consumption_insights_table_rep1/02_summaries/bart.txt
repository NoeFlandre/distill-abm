 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices . The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent in the simulation shows distinct trends over time .  Simulation results show a consistent and significant increasing trend in the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined over the observed time period . Trend is characterized by a continuous increase without any detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady adoption or preference for whole milk among the agents . For decision makers, these trends suggest a steady and predictable increase in milk consumption choices over time .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the observed period . Initially, the mean quantity of milk consumed is very low, starting at approximately 5.29 units . This is followed by a rapid increase in consumption, reaching around 1265.47 units by the early stages of the simulation .  The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005 . For decision makers, this implies that interventions or policies aimed at influencing milk consumption behaviors may need to account for growing diversity in consumer preferences and the factors influencing their choices over time .
