 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  There is no observable trend or variation in the data, indicating a stable and unchanging initial risk attitude among households . The mean household risk attitude starts at a low value of approximately 0.0074 . It then increases rapidly over the first 20 time steps, reaching a peak value of 0.3814 at time step 33 . The decline after the peak suggests a period of adjustment where households refine their strategies based on accumulated experience and social learning .  The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at 0.0000, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of livestock among households . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 steps, reaching approximately 30 households . This initial rapid increase suggests that households quickly adopt social learning behaviors as they begin to interact and observe their neighbors .  System exhibits strong oscillations, particularly between steps 8 and 24, and around steps 42 to 44, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the number of non-learning households . Dominant period of oscillation is approximately 49 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in the data . No significant trends observed from steps 66 to 98 .
