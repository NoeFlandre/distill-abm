the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation period . the value is consistently 0.0074 from the beginning to the end of the 100 steps . there is no variation or trend observed in the data, as indicated by the statistical analysis . the trend analysis reveals several phases: an initial sharp decrease, a period of stabilization at the lowest point, followed by a gradual and consistent increase . the oscillation summary indicates a dominant period of 49 time steps, suggesting cyclical patterns in livestock numbers . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across all time windows . the inflection points and change points suggest some variations in the rate of increase, but the overall direction remains positive . for decision makers, this trend implies that households are increasingly destocking livestock .
