The total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at approximately 167 and declines rapidly within the first 500 time steps. This initial decline can be attributed to the immediate interaction between human hunters and prey, where the initial high density of prey makes them more susceptible to hunting pressure. The number of animals continues to decrease steadily, reaching near zero around 2,300 time steps. This steady decline reflects the continuous hunting pressure and possibly the environmental constraints that limit the prey population's ability to recover. There are minor fluctuations and brief increases in the number of animals at certain points, such as around 730 and 800 time steps, which could be due to temporary reductions in hunting pressure or slight recoveries in the prey population. However, these increases are not sustained, and the overall trend remains a decline towards extinction. The simulation ends with the number of animals near zero, indicating that the prey population has been nearly eradicated under the given conditions. For decision makers, these trends highlight the potential for rapid declines in prey populations under sustained hunting pressure and the difficulty of population recovery once a certain threshold of decline is reached.
