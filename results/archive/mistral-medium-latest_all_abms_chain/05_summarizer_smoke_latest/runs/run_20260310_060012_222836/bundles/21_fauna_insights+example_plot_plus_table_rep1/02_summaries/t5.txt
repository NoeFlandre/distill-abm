the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) has 29 parameters that can be adjusted to test various hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . it considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism . the total energy of foragers begins at 2000 units and exhibits a consistent downward trend throughout the simulation . by around 400 time steps, the energy level drops to approximately -2000 units . the decline continues uniformly, reaching about -15000 units by the end of the simulation at 2312 time steps . the cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains unchanged until around 700 time steps . this initial period of no births could be attributed to the initial conditions of the simulation, where the population might be stabilizing or adjusting to the environment . following this period, there is a gradual increase in births, reaching a peak of approximately 5.6 births at around 1700 time steps. this decline could suggest that the population has reached a carrying capacity or that environmental factors are limiting further growth  the number of animals continues to decrease steadily, reaching near zero around 2,300 time steps . this steady decline reflects the continuous hunting pressure and possibly the environmental constraints that limit the prey population's ability to recover . however, these increases are not sustained and the overall trend remains a decline towards extinction . change points mark periods where the rate of energy decline shifts . the initial rapid decline suggests early measures to reduce hunting pressure or enhance prey survival could significantly impact the long-term sustainability of the prey population .
