 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history . The landscape is a simplified grassland environment with patches of grass, ungulates, and human hunters .  Total energy of foragers begins at 2000 units and exhibits a consistent downward trend throughout the simulation . Foragers are not able to sustain their energy levels over time, which could lead to potential issues in maintaining the forager population in the long term . Understanding these dynamics can help in managing hunting practices and conserving prey populations .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at approximately 167 and declines rapidly within the first 500 time steps . The number of births begins to decline gradually, stabilizing at around 1 birth per time step towards the end of the simulation . Understanding these patterns can help in making informed decisions about conservation efforts, hunting regulations, and habitat management to ensure the sustainability of the prey animal population .  Total energy of male animals begins at 12,500 units and exhibits a rapid decline over the initial 500 time steps . This sharp decrease suggests a high rate of energy expenditure or loss among the male population . Around the 500-step mark, the rate of decline slows, indicating a possible stabilization in the factors affecting the energy levels of the male animals . This could imply that the remaining males have adapted to the environmental conditions or that the hunting pressure has decreased .  The stability in the percentage of green patches suggests a balanced interaction between grazing by ungulates and grass regrowth . This suggests that the environment can sustain the current population of herbivores without significant degradation . Decision-makers should be cautious of potential seasonal variations or events that could temporarily disrupt this balance .
