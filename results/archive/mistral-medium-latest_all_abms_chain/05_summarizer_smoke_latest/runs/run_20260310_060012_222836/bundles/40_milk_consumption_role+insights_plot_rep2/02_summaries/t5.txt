agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. between time step 15 and time step 25, the consumption of whole milk remains relatively stable, fluctuating slightly around 800 units . this stability suggests that the agents have reached a new equilibrium in their consumption patterns, balancing their habits, social influences, and perceptions . a slight decline followed by a minor increase, but the overall trend remains relatively flat with minor fluctuations . by the thirtieth step, the total number of choices reaches approximately 25,000 . this trend indicates a growing preference for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk over time . the mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the 30 steps of the simulation . by step 10, the factor has increased to around 1.1 . this trend indicates that agents are increasingly influenced by their habits when choosing whole milk . for decision makers, interventions aimed at changing milk consumption patterns may need to account for the strengthening habit factor . environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero and rapidly increases within the first five steps, reaching a value of approximately 13 . after this rapid increase, the deviation stabilizes and plateaus around the value of 13 for the remainder of the simulation period up to 30 steps .
