 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . Simulations show a consistent and linear upward trend in the total number of whole milk choices made by agents over the simulation . Understanding the underlying factors driving this consistent preference for whole milk can help in designing strategies to promote more environmentally friendly milk choices .  The total number of choices made by all agents combined exhibits a linear growth trend over the observed time steps . This trend indicates a growing preference for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk over time . Understanding these dynamics can help in strategizing marketing efforts, product placements, and public health campaigns to further encourage the adoption of healthier milk choices .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the 30 steps of the simulation . For decision makers, this trend suggests that interventions aimed at promoting skimmed milk should consider the growing influence of habit formation .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero and rapidly increases within the first five steps, reaching a value of approximately 13 . This sharp rise indicates that initially, there is a significant misalignment between the agents' environmental values and the environmental implications of their milk choices . After the initial adjustment period, the system reaches a steady state .
