The simulation results indicate a gradual increase in the number of households engaging in social learning over the observed time steps. Initially, no households were engaged in social learning. The adoption of social learning began to rise steadily from the early time steps, showing a consistent upward trend. By time step 63, the number of households participating in social learning peaked at approximately 32. Throughout the simulation, the mean number of households engaged in social learning was around 27, with a median of 31, indicating that more than half of the time steps had values at or near this level. The standard deviation of approximately 8.92 suggests some variability in the number of households adopting social learning at different time steps. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend in social learning adoption across various segments of the simulation period. The inflection points and change points suggest periods where the rate of increase in social learning adoption experienced notable shifts. The oscillation summary indicates periodic fluctuations in the adoption rates, with a dominant period of 49 time steps, highlighting intervals of stronger oscillatory behavior.
