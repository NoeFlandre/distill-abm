the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration of the model run . the model shows distinct trends in the average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents over the course of 100 timesteps . key change points in the trend are identified at timesteps 20, 55, and 100 . the average number of destocked livestock across all households exhibits notable trends over the simulated time steps . following this peak, there is a rapid decline to 3.3333 by time step 8 . the model shows strong oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 49 time steps . the cumulative number of livestock destocked starts at 0 and reaches a maximum of approximately 96.78 by the end of the simulation .
