The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of -0.0000, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock. Over time, the coefficient gradually increases, reaching a value of 0.0726 by the end of the simulation at time step 100. This trend suggests a growing disparity in the distribution of healthy livestock among households. The mean value of the Gini coefficient over the entire period is 0.0302, with a median of 0.0229, indicating that the values are generally low but show a slight skew towards higher inequality as time progresses. The standard deviation of 0.0239 and variance of 0.0006 indicate relatively low variability in the data. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent and significant increasing trend across all time windows, with significant p-values and high Kendall tau coefficients. Change points are detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100, suggesting notable shifts in the trend at these points. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 49 time steps, with strong oscillations observed towards the latter part of the simulation. Overall, the data indicates a steady and significant increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households over the simulation period.
