The number of households following the E-RO strategy, which represents non-learning agents, starts at 20 and exhibits a general decreasing trend over the 100 timesteps. Initially, there is a slight increase, reaching a peak of approximately 21.1 at timestep 8. Following this peak, the number of E-RO households begins to decline steadily. By timestep 20, the count drops to around 17.5, marking a significant change point in the trend. This downward trend continues, with minor fluctuations, until around timestep 45, where another change point is observed. From this point onward, the decline becomes more gradual, and the number of E-RO households stabilizes somewhat, ending at approximately 14.7 by the final timestep. The data shows strong oscillations particularly in the early and middle timesteps, indicating periods of more pronounced changes in the number of E-RO households. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms these trends, showing significant decreasing trends in the initial phases and no significant trends in the later phases, suggesting a stabilization in the number of E-RO households towards the end of the simulation period.
