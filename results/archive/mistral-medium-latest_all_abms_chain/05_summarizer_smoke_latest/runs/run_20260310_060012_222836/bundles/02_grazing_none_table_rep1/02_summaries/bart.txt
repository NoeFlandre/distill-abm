 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  Simulation of agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents over the course of 100 timesteps . Initial risk attitude of social learning agents showed a stable and unchanging pattern throughout the simulation .  Initially, the destocking rate starts at zero and experiences a sharp increase, peaking significantly at time step 3 . Subsequent fluctuations are observed with smaller peaks and valleys . The trend continues to show a general decrease in destocking rates with intermittent minor increases . Overall, the data indicates a steady and significant increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households .  The number of E-RO households reaches a peak of approximately 21.1 at timestep 8.1 . The data shows strong oscillations particularly in the early and middle timesteps . The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms these trends, showing significant decreasing trends in the initial phases .
