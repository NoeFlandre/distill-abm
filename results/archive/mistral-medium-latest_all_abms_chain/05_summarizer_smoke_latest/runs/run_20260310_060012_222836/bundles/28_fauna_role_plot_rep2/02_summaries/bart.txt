 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration . Initially, the number of foragers is set at 20, and this value does not change over the course of the simulation .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations show a consistent trend in the total number of prey animals over time . Initially, the number of male animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline . By around 2000 steps, most simulations show that the prey population has either reached zero or is very close to it .
