The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations show a consistent trend in the total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the total number of animals starts at approximately 250, which aligns with the set value for the initial number of prey. In the early stages, up to around 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of animals. This steep decrease suggests a high rate of predation or mortality early in the simulation. By around 1000 steps, the number of animals has generally dropped to below 50, indicating a significant reduction in the prey population. Beyond 1000 steps, the decline continues but at a slower rate, with the number of animals gradually approaching zero. By approximately 2000 steps, most simulations show that the number of animals has either reached zero or is very close to it. This trend suggests that under the given parameters, the prey population tends to go extinct within this timeframe. The simulations indicate that the combination of hunting pressure, environmental factors, and demographic parameters leads to a consistent pattern of prey population decline and eventual extinction.
