The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation period, maintaining a value of approximately 0.0074 from the beginning to the end of the 100 steps. This indicates that there is no change in the average initial risk attitude of social learning agents over time. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, showing no significant trends or variations in the data. The mean, median, standard deviation, and variance are all consistent with a value of approximately 0.0074, and the minimum and maximum values also remain at this level. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results further support the absence of any significant trends across different time intervals. For decision makers, this implies that the initial risk attitude of households does not fluctuate, suggesting a steady disposition towards risk among social learning agents throughout the simulation period.
