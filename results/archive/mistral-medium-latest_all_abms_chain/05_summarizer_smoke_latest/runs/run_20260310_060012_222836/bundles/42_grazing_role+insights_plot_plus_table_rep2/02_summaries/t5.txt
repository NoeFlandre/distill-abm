agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the mean reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . Initially, the biomass starts at approximately 90,000 units . this decreasing trend is statistically significant in the initial phases, indicating a period of biomass reduction . the trend continues to decrease, with notable values of approximately 2.47 at step 11 and 1.45 at step 16 . by step 27, the mean destock value reduces to about 0.51 . after step 33, the trend stabilizes with no significant trends observed in some intervals . the data exhibits strong oscillations with a dominant period of 49 time steps . change points are detected at time steps 25, 60, and 100, marking significant shifts in the trend . for decision-makers, these trends imply that destocking is an ongoing process that accelerates initially and then stabilizes .
