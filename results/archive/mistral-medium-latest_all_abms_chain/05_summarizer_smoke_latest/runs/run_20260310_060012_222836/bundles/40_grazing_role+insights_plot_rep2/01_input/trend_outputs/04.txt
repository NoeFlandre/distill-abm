The average green biomass across all patches exhibits a consistent trend across all simulations. Initially, the mean green biomass starts around 45,000 units. This value remains relatively stable for the first 20 steps. Following this period, there is a noticeable decline in green biomass, reaching a low point of approximately 42,000 units around the 40th step. After this decline, the green biomass begins to recover steadily and continues to increase, surpassing the initial values and reaching around 62,000 units by the 100th step. This trend suggests an initial stability in biomass, followed by a period of decline, and then a significant recovery phase. For decision makers, this trend indicates that while there may be initial stability in pasture resources, periods of decline can occur, but adaptive management strategies can lead to recovery and even improvement in biomass levels over time.
