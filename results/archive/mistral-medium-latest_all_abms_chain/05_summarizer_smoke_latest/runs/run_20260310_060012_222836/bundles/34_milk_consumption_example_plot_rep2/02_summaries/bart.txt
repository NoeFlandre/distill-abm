 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  At step 0, the total number of choices made is 0, reflecting the initial state of the simulation . By step 30, agents have made 32,000 milk choices, suggesting a shift in consumer behavior . The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,600 units .  The mean value health deviation starts at a value close to zero and increases rapidly within the first 10 steps, reaching approximately 0.45 . This rapid rise indicates a quick divergence in the choice function scores among agents as they evaluate their milk consumption options . After steps 10 and 20, the rate of increase slows down, indicating a period of adjustment where agents are refining their perceptions and choices .
