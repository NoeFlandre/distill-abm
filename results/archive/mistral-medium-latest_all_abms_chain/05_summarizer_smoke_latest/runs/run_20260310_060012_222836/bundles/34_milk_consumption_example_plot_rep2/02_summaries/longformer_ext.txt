The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The model explores how perception, habits, and social influence affect individual decision-making processes regarding milk choices. This shift could be attributed to the growing awareness of health and environmental benefits associated with skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. As the simulation progresses beyond the tenth step, the rate of increase in skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices accelerates, indicating a more rapid adoption of these milk types. This acceleration could be due to the cumulative effects of social influence, habit formation, and the evaluation of previous choices.
