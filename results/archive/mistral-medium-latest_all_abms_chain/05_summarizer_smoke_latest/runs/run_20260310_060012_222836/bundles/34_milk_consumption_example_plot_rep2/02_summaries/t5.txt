agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero . around the fifth step, there is a noticeable increase in the number of choices made . this shift could be attributed to the growing awareness of health and environmental benefits . the habit factor continues to rise gradually until about the 10th step . the rapid increase continues until around the 15th step, where it begins to rise more rapidly . this suggests a growing adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among agents .
