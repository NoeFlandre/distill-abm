The minor fluctuations around the plateau indicate small variations in the hunting success or prey availability but do not significantly alter the overall trend.

The standard deviation of 31.10 and variance of 967.08 suggest some variability in the number of females killed across different simulations, but the overall pattern remains consistent. 

For decision-makers, these trends suggest that the initial phase of hunting leads to a rapid reduction in the female prey population, followed by a stabilization phase where the hunting pressure and prey availability reach a form of equilibrium.
