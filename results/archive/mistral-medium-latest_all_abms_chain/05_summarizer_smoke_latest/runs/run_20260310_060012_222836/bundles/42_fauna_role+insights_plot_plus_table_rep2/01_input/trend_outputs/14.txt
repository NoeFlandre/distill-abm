Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable for a brief period. Around time step 150, there is a noticeable decline in the percentage of green patches, reaching a minimum of about 24.91% at time step 365. This decline indicates a significant reduction in available fodder, which could be due to increased grazing pressure or environmental changes affecting grass regrowth.

Following this decline, there is a gradual recovery phase where the percentage of green patches increases again, stabilizing around 32.97% by time step 725. This recovery suggests a period of regrowth and potential reduction in grazing pressure, allowing the grass patches to recover. The landscape then maintains a relatively stable percentage of green patches with minor fluctuations until the end of the simulation.

Key change points are observed at time steps 150, 300, 475, and 725, indicating significant shifts in the dynamics of green patch availability. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results show periods of increasing and decreasing trends, with significant changes in the percentage of green patches. For instance, from time steps 0 to 695, there are increasing trends, followed by a period of stability and minor fluctuations.

The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 time steps, suggesting regular cyclic patterns in the availability of green patches. This cyclical behavior could be influenced by seasonal variations in grass regrowth or periodic changes in grazing patterns.

For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of understanding the dynamics of fodder availability and the factors influencing grass regrowth.
