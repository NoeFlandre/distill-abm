 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The temporal units are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant at 20 throughout the entire duration .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows distinct trends over time . Initial phase of hunting leads to a rapid reduction in the female prey population, followed by a stabilization phase where the hunting pressure and prey availability reach a form of equilibrium . For decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of monitoring and managing hunting pressures and environmental conditions to ensure the sustainability of the prey population . Understanding these dynamics can help in developing strategies to balance ecological interactions and prevent potential extinctions  The number of animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time . The trend continues to show a gradual decline, with occasional minor fluctuations, but the overall trajectory remains downward . For decision-makers, this trend indicates that under the given parameters, the animal population is not sustainable .  The total energy of all animals in the simulation starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units . The trend of decreasing energy persists, albeit at a slower rate, and by 2,000 time steps, the total energy approaches near-zero values . The percentage of green patches in the landscape, representing the availability of fodder for prey animals, exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period .
