The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear trend over time. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the cumulative number reaches approximately 80 females killed. Following this initial phase, the rate of increase begins to slow down, indicating a deceleration in the hunting rate. By around 1000 steps, the cumulative number of females killed stabilizes around 120. This stabilization suggests that the hunting pressure reaches a plateau, where the number of females killed does not significantly increase further. The data indicates that the system approaches an equilibrium state where the hunting rate balances with other factors in the model, such as prey availability and environmental conditions. The trend remains relatively stable with minor fluctuations around the plateau value for the remainder of the simulation steps, indicating a steady-state in the hunting dynamics.
