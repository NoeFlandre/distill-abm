The total energy of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of 12500 units. Initially, there is a rapid decline in the total energy, dropping sharply to around 4000 units by approximately 500 steps. This steep decrease indicates a significant reduction in the energy levels of male prey animals early in the simulation. Following this initial drop, the decline continues but at a slower rate, with the total energy gradually decreasing to near zero by around 2000 steps. Throughout this period, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks and valleys, but the overall trend remains a steady decline. The statistical analysis indicates that the energy levels are consistently decreasing across different segments of the simulation, with significant downward trends noted in the rolling Mann-Kendall test. The change points and inflection indices suggest shifts in the rate of decline at various steps, but the dominant pattern remains a reduction in total energy. The oscillation summary indicates some periodic variations, but these are minor compared to the overall decreasing trend. By the end of the simulation, the total energy of male animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy resources among the male prey population.
