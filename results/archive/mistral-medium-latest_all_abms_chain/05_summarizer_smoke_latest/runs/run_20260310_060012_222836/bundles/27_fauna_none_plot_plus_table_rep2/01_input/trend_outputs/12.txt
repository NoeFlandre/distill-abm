The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of 12500 and exhibits a rapid and consistent decline over time. Initially, the energy levels drop steeply, indicating a significant reduction in the energy available within the female prey population. This trend continues with a sharp decrease until around 500 steps, where the rate of decline begins to slow down. By approximately 2310 steps, the energy levels approach zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy within the female prey population. Throughout this period, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks and valleys, but the overall trend remains a steady decline. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant Mann-Kendall test results across various intervals, showing a consistent negative slope. The model parameters and the dynamics of the simulation suggest that factors such as hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and animal demographics contribute to this observed trend.
