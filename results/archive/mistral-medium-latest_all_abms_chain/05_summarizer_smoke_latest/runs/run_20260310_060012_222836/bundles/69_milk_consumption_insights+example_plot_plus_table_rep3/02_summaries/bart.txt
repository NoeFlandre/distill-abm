 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model explores how perception, habits, and social influence affect individual decision-making processes regarding milk choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person, using both theoretical grounding and empirical data for calibration against observed macro-level data .  The mean number of whole milk choices is approximately 1131, with a median of 1159, indicating a central tendency towards higher values as the simulation progresses . By the final time step, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 25,644, showing a substantial shift from whole milk to healthier alternatives . The trends observed in the simulation provide valuable insights for decision-makers .  The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2654.81 units . The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . By around the 5th time step, the consumption has risen significantly, reflecting a growing acceptance and adoption among the agents . The overall trend indicates a significant shift in milk consumption preferences over the simulated period .  Trend analysis shows no significant trend in the initial steps, followed by a consistent increasing trend from the fifth step onwards . This suggests that once the initial inertia is overcome, the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk becomes a sustained and accelerating trend . The mean habit factor continues to rise steadily, reaching a value of 1.5879 by the final step .
