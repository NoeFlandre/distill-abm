 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The positive initial risk attitudes range up to approximately 0.17, indicating a tendency to take more risks . The negative risk attitudes go down to approximately -0.1 . These values do not change over the 100 steps of the simulation . Households with increasing risk attitudes stabilize around higher values, while those with decreasing risk attitudes .  Simulations of the agent-based model show a general trend of increasing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households over time . Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value close to zero, indicating a relatively equal distribution of livestock among households . By step 20, the number of households engaging in social learning ranges from about 18 to 48 .  The number of non-learning households starts at a value of 20 . This number quickly increases, showing a sharp rise to around 35 households by step 10 . After step 40, there is a noticeable decline in the number, dropping to around 25 by step 50 . Beyond step 50, the number continues to decrease gradually, with minor fluctuations .
