agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at 5.29 . this initial value suggests that at the beginning of the simulation, the consumption of these milk types is minimal, likely due to the strong initial habit for whole milk consumption and low initial perceptions of the health and environmental benefits of alternative milk types . consistent increase without any detected peaks or valleys highlights a smooth transition rather than abrupt changes . the dominant oscillation period of 15.00 towards the end of the simulation suggests some periodic fluctuations in the rate of adoption . for decision-makers, this trend implies a consistent and predictable pattern in consumer behavior . consumption levels exhibit some fluctuations but generally stabilize around a high mean value of approximately 1220.21 units . these fluctuations indicate periods of slight decreases and increases in consumption, which could be due to varying social influences, market conditions, or temporary shifts in consumer preferences . trend highlights potential for targeted marketing and public health campaigns to significantly alter consumption patterns over time . deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and increases rapidly in the early time steps . this indicates that over time, there is a growing discrepancy between what agents believe about the health benefits of their chosen milk type and the actual health implications . the deviation reaches a maximum value of 0.7251 at the final time step .
