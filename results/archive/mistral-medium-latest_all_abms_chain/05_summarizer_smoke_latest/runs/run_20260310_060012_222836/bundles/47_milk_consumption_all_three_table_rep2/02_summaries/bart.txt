 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value of 5.29.29 . The consumption then increases rapidly, indicating a significant shift in consumer behavior . For decision makers, this trend indicates a clear and sustained move away from whole milk over the simulated period, which could inform strategies related to market adjustments, health campaigns, or supply chain management .  The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents begins at approximately 2654.81 units and exhibits a general decreasing trend throughout the simulation period . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial habit strength and the impact of health and environmental perceptions in driving long-term changes in consumer behavior .  The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit starts at a value of 1.0000 . Over the course of the simulation, this factor exhibits a gradual and consistent increase . For decision makers, this trend underscores the importance of health and environmental perceptions, social influence, and habit formation in driving consumer behavior .
