This trend indicates a growing adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time. The steady increase in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk can be attributed to the influence of health perceptions, social interactions, and habit formation among the agents. The inflection point at index 31 suggests a significant shift in the adoption rate, possibly indicating a tipping point where social influence and health awareness collectively drive a more rapid change in consumer behavior. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results consistently show significant increasing trends across different time segments, reinforcing the steady growth in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The dominant oscillation period of 15.00 and strong oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation suggest some variability in the adoption rates, potentially due to the complex interplay of social networks and individual decision-making processes.
