agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. by the end of the simulation, the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent drops to approximately 253.10 units . this significant reduction over the simulation period highlights a major shift away from whole milk consumption . for decision makers, these trends imply interventions or changes in consumer perceptions and social influences can have a substantial impact on milk consumption habits . approximately 25,644 choices by the final time step . this trend suggests a widespread and sustained adoption of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk over the simulated period . for decision-makers, interventions targeting these parameters could be highly effective in promoting healthier milk choices among the public . the mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a baseline value of 1.0000 . this trend indicates a gradual increase in the influence of habit on the consumption of whole milk over time . the standard deviation is relatively low, and the variance is 0.0001 . rapid initial divergence suggests early interventions could have a substantial impact on shaping long-term consumption habits . the steady increase and eventual stabilization of choice function deviation indicate that consumer preferences continue to evolve, but eventually reach a stable state . this could inform strategic planning in public health and marketing campaigns.
