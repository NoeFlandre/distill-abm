 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value, close to zero . Initially, at step 0, the total number of whole milk choices starts at 0 . By step 5, the number of these choices begins to rise more noticeably, reaching approximately 2,500 . The trend in the data shows a gradual increase in skimmed milk choices over a series of steps, indicating a shift in consumer behavior over time .  Initially, at time step 0, the total number of choices made is 0.0 . By time step 10, approximately 10,000 choices have been made . The linear nature of this growth indicates a constant rate of choices being made by the agents . Overall, the data suggests a significant shift away from whole milk consumption .  The mean value environmental deviation in the agent-based model of milk consumption behaviors exhibits a rapid initial increase followed by stabilization . Overall, the trend shows an initial rapid adjustment, followed by a period of stabilization, and a final increase in the deviation, reflecting the dynamic interplay between health perceptions and consumption behaviors .
