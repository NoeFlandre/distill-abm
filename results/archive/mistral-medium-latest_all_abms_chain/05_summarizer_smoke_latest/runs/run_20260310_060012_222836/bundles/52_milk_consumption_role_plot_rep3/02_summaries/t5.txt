agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. from steps 15 to 25, the consumption of whole milk continues to decrease gradually, reaching approximately 600 units . this gradual decline suggests a slower but steady shift away from whole milk, possibly due to long-term changes in social norms, health perceptions, or environmental concerns . the total number of choices made by all agents combined in the simulation exhibits a linear growth trend over the observed time steps . by time step 0, approximately 10,000 choices have been made . this upward trend continues consistently, reaching around 20,000 choices by the time step 20 and 30,000 choices by timestep 30 . from step 5 to step 10, the deviation continues to increase but at a slower rate, reaching around 0.6 . this period indicates a stabilization phase where the agents' choice functions are becoming more distinct over time, but the rate of diversification slows down as the simulation progresses .
