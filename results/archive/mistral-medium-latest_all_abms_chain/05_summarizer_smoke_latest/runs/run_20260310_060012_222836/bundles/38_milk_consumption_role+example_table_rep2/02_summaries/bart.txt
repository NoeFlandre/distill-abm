 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at a very low value of 5.29.29 . The overall trend is a steady decrease in whole milk consumption . This trend reflects a significant shift away from whole milk, likely due to sustained changes in consumer habits and social influences favoring alternative milk types .  The data shows a strong and steady increase in the total number of choices, with no detected peaks, valleys, or inflection points . The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across all time steps . The oscillation summary suggests a dominant period of 15 time steps, with strong oscillations detected towards the end of the simulation period .  The mean factor reaches its maximum value of 1.5879 at the final time step, indicating a substantial rise in the habit factor throughout the simulation period . Trend analysis reveals several inflection points and change points, notably around the fifteenth and thirty-second time steps, which may indicate shifts in the rate of increase .  The dominant oscillation period of 15.00 further supports the presence of periodic adjustments in the agents' choices, contributing to the observed stability in the mean deviation . From time steps 10 onwards, there is no significant trend, suggesting that the system has reached a relatively stable state with high deviation values .
