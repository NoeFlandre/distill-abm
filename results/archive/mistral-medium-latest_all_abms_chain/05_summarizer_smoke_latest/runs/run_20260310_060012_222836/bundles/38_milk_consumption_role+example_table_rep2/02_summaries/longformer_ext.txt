An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole. In this context, the ABM is designed to replicate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The overall trend is a steady decrease in whole milk consumption, with periods of stabilization and minor fluctuations, ultimately indicating a substantial reduction in preference for whole milk over time.
