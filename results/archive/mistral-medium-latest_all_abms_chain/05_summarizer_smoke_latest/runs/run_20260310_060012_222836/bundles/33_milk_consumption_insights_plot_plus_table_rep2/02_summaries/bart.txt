 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on health and environmental characteristics modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The total number of milk choices made by agents in the simulation shows a consistent increasing trend over the time steps . The mean value of the total choices is around 11,157.35, with a median of 10,679.43, indicating a right-skewed distribution . For decision makers, these trends suggest a growing preference for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk .  Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . This is followed by a rapid increase in consumption, reaching around 1220 units by the 10th step . This sharp rise indicates a renewed or intensified adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . For decision makers, these trends suggest that initial interventions or changes in the model have a substantial impact on the parameters parameters . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing policies or strategies that effectively influence milk consumption  The mean value environmental deviation starts at 0 and rapidly increases in the early steps . This trend suggests that over time, the agents' health perceptions increasingly deviate from the health values implied by their milk choices . For decision makers, this implies a growing misalignment between perceived health benefits and actual consumption patterns .
