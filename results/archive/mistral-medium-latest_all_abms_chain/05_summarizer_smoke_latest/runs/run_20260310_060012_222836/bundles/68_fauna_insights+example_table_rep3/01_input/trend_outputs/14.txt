The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) begins at approximately 33% and exhibits minor fluctuations throughout the simulation. The initial value of 33.0002% slightly decreases to a minimum of 24.9108% at time step 365, indicating a temporary reduction in vegetated patches. Following this decline, the percentage of vegetated patches gradually recovers and stabilizes around the initial value, ending at 32.9979%. The model shows several minor peaks and valleys, suggesting periodic oscillations in vegetation coverage. These oscillations have a dominant period of 63 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in vegetation regrowth and consumption. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals significant increasing trends in the early phases of the simulation, followed by decreasing trends in later phases, suggesting dynamic changes in vegetation coverage over time. The identified change points at time steps 150, 300, 475, 725, and 2310 indicate notable shifts in the trend of vegetated patches. For decision makers, these trends imply that while the overall vegetation coverage remains relatively stable, there are periodic fluctuations and significant change points that may require attention. Understanding these patterns can help in managing the landscape to ensure a consistent food supply for the prey animals, thereby influencing the overall dynamics of the ecosystem.
