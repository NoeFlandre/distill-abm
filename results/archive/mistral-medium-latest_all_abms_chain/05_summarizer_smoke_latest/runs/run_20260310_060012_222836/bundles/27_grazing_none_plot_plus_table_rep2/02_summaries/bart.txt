 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the mean household risk attitude over 100 time steps . The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households shows a distinct trend . The mean reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the 100 steps of the simulation .  The average number of livestock destocked across all households in the model shows a consistent increasing trend over the 100 time steps . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages . Over time, the distribution of healthy livestock becomes increasingly unequal among households .  The overall trend indicates a significant reduction in the number of non-learning households over time, with a stabilization phase in the latter half of the simulation period . The initial increase is followed by a consistent decrease and eventual stabilization, reflecting changes in household behaviors over the simulated time steps .
