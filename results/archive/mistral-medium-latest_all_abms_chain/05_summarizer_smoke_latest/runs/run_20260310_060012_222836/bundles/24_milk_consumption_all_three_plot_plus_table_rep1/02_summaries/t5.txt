agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. by the end of the simulation, the average weekly consumption of whole milk stabilizes at around 253.10 units . this pattern suggests that while the overall trend is a decline in whole milk consumption, there are phases where the rate of decline slows down or stabilizes temporarily . the presence of strong oscillations at specific indices further supports the idea of periodic fluctuations in consumption behavior. the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches about 10,000 by the 15th time step and continues to climb . the steady increase throughout the simulation suggests a robust and continuous adoption process influenced by ongoing social interactions, habit formation, and positive perceptions of health and environmental benefits . from time steps 10 to 20, consumption stabilizes, fluctuating slightly around a mean value of approximately 1,220 units . after time step 20, there is a gradual increase in consumption, reaching a peak of around 1,456.60 units by the end of the simulation . this gradual increase suggests a steady, long-term adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . the deviation reaches its maximum value of approximately 0.75 at step 32 . the trend shows a consistent increase in deviation throughout the simulation . p-values suggest a steady divergence in choice behaviors over time .
