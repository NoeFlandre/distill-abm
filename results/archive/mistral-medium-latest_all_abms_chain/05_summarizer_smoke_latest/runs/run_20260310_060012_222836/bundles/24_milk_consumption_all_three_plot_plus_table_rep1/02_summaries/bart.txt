 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . Model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice . The ABM is designed to replicate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at approximately 5.29 units per agent . The consumption then increases rapidly, reaching around 1220.21 units by the 11th time step . This sharp rise suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior, likely driven by increasing awareness and social influence favoring skimmed .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps, reaching a final value of 32,000 . The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation, with significant results across all time step intervals . For decision makers, this trend implies a steady and predictable pattern in consumer behavior .  The statistical analysis shows significant increasing trends in the early and late phases of the simulation . This pattern highlights the initial rapid adoption, followed by a stabilization period, and then a gradual increase as the new consumption behavior becomes more entrenched . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing effective strategies for promoting healthier or more environmentally friendly consumer choices .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and increases rapidly in the first few steps . For decision makers, the rapid initial increase in deviation highlights the importance of early interventions to align consumer choices with environmental values . The stabilization phase indicates that once habits are formed, changes become more gradual and less responsive to interventions . Therefore, policies aimed at influencing milk consumption choices should be implemented early .
