The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, there is a rapid increase in the number of males killed, starting from zero and rising sharply. This steep increase continues until around 500 steps, where the rate of increase begins to slow. By approximately 1000 steps, the cumulative number of males killed reaches a plateau, stabilizing around 120 males. This plateau is maintained with minor fluctuations until the end of the simulation at 2312 steps. Key values include a maximum of 120.1667 males killed at step 1876 and a mean value of approximately 100.3948 males killed across the simulation period. The data shows significant increasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation, followed by periods of stability and minor decreases towards the end. The model parameters and dynamic interactions between hunters and prey contribute to these observed trends, reflecting the complex socio-natural system being simulated.
