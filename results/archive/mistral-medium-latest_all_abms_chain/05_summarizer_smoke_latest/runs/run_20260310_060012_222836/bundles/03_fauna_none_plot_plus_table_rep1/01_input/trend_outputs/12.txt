The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This high initial value rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has decreased significantly to approximately 2,000 units. The rate of decline then slows down, but the overall trend remains a steady decrease. Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks and valleys, but these do not significantly alter the overall downward trajectory. By the end of the simulation at 2,311 time steps, the total energy of female animals approaches zero. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various segments of the time series. The model parameters and the dynamics of hunter behavior, prey demographics, and environmental factors collectively contribute to this observed trend in the total energy of female animals.
