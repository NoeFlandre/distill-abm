The simulation results indicate a consistent and significant upward trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the observed period. Initially, the number of times agents chose these milk types started at zero. This is followed by a steady increase, reaching a peak of approximately 25,643.83 at the final time step. The data shows a continuous growth pattern without any detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a steady shift in consumer behavior towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various segments of the timeline, with significant slopes indicating a robust and sustained change. The inflection point and change point at the final time step further emphasize the continuous nature of this trend. The dominant oscillation period and strong oscillation indices towards the end of the timeline suggest some variability in the rate of adoption, but the overall trajectory remains upward. For decision makers, this trend implies a clear and sustained shift in consumer preferences towards healthier milk options, which could inform marketing strategies, product development, and public health initiatives.
