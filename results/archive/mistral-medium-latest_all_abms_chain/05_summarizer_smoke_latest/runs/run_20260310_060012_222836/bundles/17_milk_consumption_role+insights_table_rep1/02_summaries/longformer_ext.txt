An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole. In this context, the ABM is designed to replicate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice. The ABM is designed to be used to assess the impact of the change in consumer behavior on the system as a whole.The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer. These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. The key parameters and their settings are as follows:1.1. **Memory-Lifetime**: This parameter determines how long an agent remembers past experiences.
