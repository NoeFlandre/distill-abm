agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. specific peaks are observed at time steps 19, 27, and 30 with values of approximately 773.96, 620.98, and 335.18 units respectively . valleys are noted at time step 18, 26, and 29 with values around 760.00, 594.35, and 323.46 units respectively. these fluctuations suggest periods of temporary stabilization or minor resurgence in whole milk consumption . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various segments of the timeline . the inflection point and change point at the final time step further emphasize the continuous nature of this trend . this trend implies a clear and sustained shift in consumer preferences towards healthier milk options . the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis for time steps 25-29 shows a significant increasing trend with a slope of 48.544 . this late-stage increase could be influenced by the cumulative effects of social susceptibility and conformity, as well as the justification and cognitive dissonance parameters . the mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents in the simulation starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the time steps . the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a significant increasing trend across various segments of the time series .
