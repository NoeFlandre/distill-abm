 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The simulation of average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent shows a clear trend of increasing adoption over time . Initial rapid increase in consumption highlights a successful adoption phase, likely driven by effective marketing, social influence, or perceived health benefits . Understanding these phases can help in planning targeted interventions and marketing strategies to sustain and potentially increase market share .  Simulation results indicate a consistent and linear increase in the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined over the observed time steps . The data shows a continuous growth pattern without any detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a steady shift in consumer behavior towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various segments of the timeline .  The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit exhibits a consistent and gradual increasing trend throughout the simulation period . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial perceptions and social influences in driving the adoption of healthier or more environmentally friendly products .  The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a significant increasing trend across various segments of the time series . The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 15.00, with strong oscillations observed at several indices . Understanding these trends can help in designing targeted interventions or marketing strategies to address specific consumer segments .
