The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Starting from an initial value of 0.0000, the deviation steadily rises, reaching a final value of 0.7251. The mean deviation across the entire period is 0.4965, with a median of 0.5584, indicating that the values are generally centered around these figures. The standard deviation of 0.1702 and variance of 0.0290 suggest moderate variability in the data. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals significant increasing trends across various segments of the simulation, with consistent positive slopes ranging from 0.003 to 0.063. This indicates a persistent and statistically significant increase in the deviation over time. The detection of a dominant oscillation period of 15.00 suggests cyclical patterns within the data, although the overall trend remains upward. The presence of multiple inflection points and strong oscillation indices further supports the notion of periodic fluctuations within the increasing trend. For decision-makers, this implies that over time, there is a growing discrepancy between the health values agents attribute to different milk types and the health implications of their actual milk choices. This trend could inform strategies aimed at aligning consumer perceptions more closely with the health outcomes of their choices, potentially through targeted education or marketing campaigns.
