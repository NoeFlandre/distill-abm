agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the mean reserve biomass in the grazing system starts at approximately 90,000 units . over the first 20 time steps, there is a slight decline, followed by a more pronounced decrease until around time step 30, where the biomass reaches a low point of about 85,000 units. this initial decline could be attributed to the initial pressure exerted by the livestock as they begin to utilize the available biomass . by step 40, the Gini coefficient ranges between approximately 0.015 and 0.045 . this indicates a high level of inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households at the end of the simulation period . by step 100, the rate of increase in the gini coefficient starts to vary more widely among simulations, with some showing a rapid increase .
