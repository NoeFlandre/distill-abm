 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . The extension focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, which are central to adaptive (co-)management strategies . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households starts at approximately 90 . The mean green biomass starts at about 45,000 units, followed by a slight decline in the initial 20 time steps . The number of destocked livestock then declines rapidly within the first 20 steps, dropping to around 2 . This sharp decline suggests that households are quickly adapting to the initial conditions, possibly by adjusting livestock numbers .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase within the first 20 steps of the simulation . This initial phase indicates a quick adoption of social learning behaviors among households . By around 20 steps, the number with social learning stabilizes across different simulation runs, with values ranging between approximately 12 and 54 households .
