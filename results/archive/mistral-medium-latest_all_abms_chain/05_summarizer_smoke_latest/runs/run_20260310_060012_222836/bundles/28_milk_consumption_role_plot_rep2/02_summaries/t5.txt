agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. between steps 15 and 25, the consumption of whole milk remains relatively stable, fluctuating slightly around 700 units . this stability suggests that the agents have reached a new equilibrium in their milk consumption habits, with the influence of social norms, health perceptions, and environmental concerns being balanced out . the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation, followed by a stabilization and a subsequent gradual rise towards the end of the observed period . within the first five steps, there is a sharp increase, with the mean amount rising steeply to approximately 1200 units . this final increase suggests a renewed shift in consumption behaviors, potentially driven by cumulative social influences . the deviation starts at a low value close to zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early steps of the simulation . by around the fifth step, the deviation reaches approximately 0.3 . this initial phase indicates a significant shift in the agents' perceptions and their milk choices .
