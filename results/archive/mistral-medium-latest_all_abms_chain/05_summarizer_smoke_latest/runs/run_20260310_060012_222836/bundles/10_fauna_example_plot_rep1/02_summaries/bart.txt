 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history . The landscape is a simplified grassland environment with patches of grass, ungulates, and human hunters .  Total number of males killed starts at zero and increases rapidly over the first 500 time steps . Total energy of foragers reaches approximately -40,000 by the 2000-step mark, indicating a gradual reduction in the energy status of the foragers . The number of postpartum females killed begins to plateau, showing a slower and more gradual increase .  The total number of animals starts at 250 and declines rapidly over the first 500 time steps . The number of male animals in the simulation starts at approximately 125 and remains relatively stable for the first 100 steps . There is some variance across simulation runs, particularly noticeable after the initial 500 steps . This suggests that while the initial reactions to hunting and environmental pressures are predictable, the system's complexity introduces more variability over time .
