 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value, close to zero . After time step 25, there is a slight decline followed by another stabilization around 500 units, indicating a further reduction in whole milk consumption but at a slower pace . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial interventions to rapidly shift consumption habits .  The total number of choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases linearly over the 30 steps . The linear trend suggests that the rate at which agents make choices remains constant throughout the simulation period . For decision makers, this implies a predictable and steady pattern of consumer behavior .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 at the beginning of the simulation . The steady increase throughout the simulation highlights the persistent influence of habit on whole milk consumption . For decision makers, this trend implies that interventions aimed at changing milk consumption habits need to be robust and sustained to overcome strong initial habits .  Environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero, indicating that initially, there is a strong alignment between the agents' environmental values and their milk choices . This alignment rapidly increases within the first five steps, reaching a value of approximately 13 . After this rapid increase, the deviation stabilizes and plateaus around the value of 13 for the remainder of the simulation .
