agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. after time step 25, there is a slight decline followed by another stabilization around 500 units . this suggests that while whole milk consumption has significantly reduced, it still maintains a baseline level among some consumers . for decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial interventions to rapidly shift consumption habits . gradual but steady increase in the adoption of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk underscores the potential for long-term shifts in consumption patterns . the linear trend suggests that the rate at which agents make choices remains constant throughout the simulation period . for decision makers, this implies a predictable and steady pattern of consumer behavior . the mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 . this initial value indicates that there is no initial habit bias towards skimmmed milk at the beginning of the simulation . from step 10 to step 15, the habit factor continues to rise steeply, reaching approximately 1.2 . after step 25, the mean value health deviation begins to increase again, reaching approximately 0.7 by step 30 . this final increase indicates a renewed divergence between health perceptions and actual choices, possibly due to cumulative social influences or long-term habit formation .
