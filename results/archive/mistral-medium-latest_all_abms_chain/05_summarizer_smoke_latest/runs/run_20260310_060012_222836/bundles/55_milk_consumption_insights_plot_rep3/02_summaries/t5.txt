agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at a low value close to zero . in the initial steps, from 0 to around 5, there is a gradual increase in the number of choices . this initial phase indicates a slow adoption rate where more agents are choosing . the rate of increase accelerates significantly, showing a steep upward trend . habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the 30-step period . Initially, the habit factor starts slightly above 1.0 . around step 5, the rate of increase accelerates, indicating a significant growth phase .
