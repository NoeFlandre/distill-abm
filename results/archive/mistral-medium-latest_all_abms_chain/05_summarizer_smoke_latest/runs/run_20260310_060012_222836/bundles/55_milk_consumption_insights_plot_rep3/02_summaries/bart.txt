 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined shows a linear and consistent upward trend over the 31 steps, which represent the time period from 1974 to 2005 . For decision makers, these trends imply that the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk may initially face resistance but can rapidly gain traction, eventually stabilizing at a higher consumption level .  Understanding this trend can help in designing long-term strategies that leverage habit reinforcement to encourage sustained consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . For decision makers, this implies that interventions aimed at promoting healthier milk choices may benefit from considering the growing influence of habit formation over time .
