 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The metric representing the mean household risk attitude in the agent-based model exhibits distinct trends over the 100 steps of the simulation . Some households show an increasing risk attitude, rising sharply to values as high as 0.8, while others exhibit a decline . After step 40, the trends stabilize somewhat, with households maintaining their respective risk attitudes . This period of stabilization indicates households have settled into their adaptive behaviors based on their initial learning and observations .  The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at a value of 0 for all simulations, indicating an initial state of perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households . Within the first 10 steps of the simulation, there is a noticeable increase in the number of non-learning households, reaching a peak of around 40 households . The number of households engaging in social learning stabilizes, with most simulations showing values ranging from approximately 6 to 18 households .
