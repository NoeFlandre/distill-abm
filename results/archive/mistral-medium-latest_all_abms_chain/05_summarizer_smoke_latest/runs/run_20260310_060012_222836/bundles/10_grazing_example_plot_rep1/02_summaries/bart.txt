 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The mean household risk attitude starts at a neutral value of approximately 0.5.16, while the lowest value stays constant at approximately -0.1.1 . In the initial 20 time steps, there is a rapid divergence in risk attitudes among households . This divergence suggests that households are adapting their risk attitudes based on initial observations and interactions within the model . The final risk attitudes reflect a diverse range of household behaviors, with some maintaining high-risk attitudes .  The Gini coefficient for total livestock healthy starts at 0 across all simulations, indicating an equal distribution of healthy livestock among households at the beginning . After step 30, the destocking rate fluctuates slightly but generally remains low and stable, close to 1. The low variance in the latter part of the simulation indicates consistent behavior across different simulation runs .
