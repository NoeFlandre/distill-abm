the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation . the highest value remains steady at around 0.16, while the lowest value stays constant at approximately -0.1 . other values within this range also exhibit no change over the 100 time steps . the mean destock of households starts with a high value around 7, indicating a significant initial reduction in livestock . between steps 10 and 30, the destocking rate continues to decrease but at a slower pace, stabilizing around a value of 1. This period indicates a phase of adjustment where households are fine-tuning their livestock numbers . with minor fluctuations around the 20 mark until the end of the simulation at step 100 . the simulation is based on a simulation of the 20-year-old's position .
