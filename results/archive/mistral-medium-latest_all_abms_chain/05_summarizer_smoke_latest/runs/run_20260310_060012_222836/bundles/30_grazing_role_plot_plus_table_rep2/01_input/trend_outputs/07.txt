The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the 100 time steps. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of approximately 0.0, indicating a relatively equal distribution of healthy livestock among households. As time progresses, the Gini coefficient gradually increases, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 at the final time step. This indicates a growing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock over time. The trend is consistently increasing throughout the entire period, with no detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a steady and continuous rise in inequality. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across all time windows, with significant positive slopes and tau values of 1.000, indicating a strong and consistent upward trend. The change points detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100 suggest notable shifts in the rate of increase in inequality. The dominant period of oscillation is around 49 time steps, with strong oscillation indices observed in the later stages of the simulation. This indicates some periodic fluctuations in the rate of increase in inequality, particularly in the latter half of the time steps.
