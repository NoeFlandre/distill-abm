 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, which are central to adaptive (co-)management strategies .  The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households, starts at 90.0000 . The average number of destocked livestock across households exhibits a sharp initial increase, peaking at approximately 6.61 livestock at the third time step . The overall trend indicates an initial sharp decline in livestock numbers, a period of stability, and then a gradual recovery .  The average cumulative number of destocked livestock across all households in the grazing system shows a consistent increasing trend over the 101 time steps . The trend is consistently increasing throughout the entire period, with no detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a steady and continuous rise in inequality . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly in the initial steps .
