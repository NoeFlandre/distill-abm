 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows a clear trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time . Initially, the average weekly consumption of these milk types per agent starts very low at 5.29.29 . The most substantial growth occurs early in the simulation, with the average consumption rising sharply .  The mean number of choices across all time steps is 11,157.35, with a median of 10,679.43, suggesting a right-skewed distribution . The standard deviation of 8,241.32 and variance of 67,919,370.31 indicate substantial variability in the data, reflecting diverse adoption rates over time . The data reflects a clear and sustained increase in the preference for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the agents .  The simulation captures the dynamic process of milk consumption, highlighting initial rapid adoption, a middle phase of stabilization with periodic fluctuations, and a final phase of renewed increase in consumption . The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period .  The metric consistently increases over time, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test . The slopes of these increasing trends vary, indicating different rates of increase in deviation over time . Inflection points are noted at time steps 7, 12, and 28, indicating changes in the rate of increase .
