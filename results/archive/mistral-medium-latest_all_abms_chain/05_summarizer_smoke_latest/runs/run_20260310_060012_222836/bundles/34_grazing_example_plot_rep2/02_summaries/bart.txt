 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The mean household risk attitude starts with a value of approximately 0.5 across all simulations . The highest mean household initial risk attitude is around 0.17, while the lowest is around -0.1 . This distribution suggests that there are varying levels of risk attitudes among households, but each household's risk attitude does not change over the course of the simulation .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase within the first 20 time steps . The Gini coefficient for total livestock healthy starts at 0 across all simulations, indicating an equal distribution of healthy livestock among households at the beginning .  This stabilization suggests that a balance has been reached in the population, with a consistent proportion of households maintaining non-learning behaviors despite the presence of learning strategies .
