the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation . the majority of the values are clustered around specific points, indicating that different groups maintain distinct and steady risk attitudes over time . after time step 40, there is a gradual increase in the metric, reaching approximately 60 units by time step 100 . this upward trend indicates a recovery phase where households might be successfully adapting their livestock management practices . the variance among simulation runs increases slightly after the initial rapid decline, suggesting that different households or strategies lead to varying outcomes over the long term . around step 10, there is a noticeable increase in the number of non-learning households, peaking at approximately 40 households by step 20 . this rise could be attributed to initial interactions and observations among households .
