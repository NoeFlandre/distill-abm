 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model aims to evaluate the long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types and the impact of various ecological and climatic parameters on the system .  The average amount of reserve biomass across all patches in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the 100 time steps of the simulation . The data shows strong oscillations with a dominant period of 49 time steps, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the reserve biomass levels . The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households shows distinct trends .  The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households in the grazing system model exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the entire simulation period of 100 time steps . The number of households engaging in social learning shows a consistent increase over the simulation period . Overall, the trend indicates a growing adoption of social learning strategies among households over time .  The dominant period of oscillation is 49 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in the data . Key change points are observed at time steps 20 and 45, marking shifts in the trend dynamics . The stabilization in the latter part of the simulation indicates a possible equilibrium reached within the system .
