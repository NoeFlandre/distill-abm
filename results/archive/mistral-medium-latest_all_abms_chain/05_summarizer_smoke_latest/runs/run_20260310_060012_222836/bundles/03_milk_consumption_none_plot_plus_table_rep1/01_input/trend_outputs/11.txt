The mean value health deviation in the agent-based model simulating milk consumption behaviors shows a consistent increasing trend over the time steps. Initially, the deviation starts at 0.0000 and rises sharply in the early steps, reaching approximately 0.5 by step 10. This rapid increase slows down as the steps progress, with the deviation continuing to grow but at a more gradual rate. By step 32, the deviation reaches its maximum value of 0.7251. Throughout the simulation, there are several inflection points indicating changes in the rate of increase, notably around steps 4, 6, 16, 19, 22, 28, and 31. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across various segments of the time steps, with consistent positive slopes. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 15 steps, with strong oscillations detected at several indices, particularly towards the latter part of the simulation. Overall, the mean value health deviation demonstrates a clear upward trajectory, reflecting changes in agents' perceptions and choices regarding milk consumption over time.
