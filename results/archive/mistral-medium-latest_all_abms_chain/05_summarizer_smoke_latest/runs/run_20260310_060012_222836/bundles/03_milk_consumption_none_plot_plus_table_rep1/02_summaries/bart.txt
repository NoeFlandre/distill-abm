 The model aims to simulate the consumption of whole and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others . The data shows a clear and consistent trend in the initial steps, followed by a period of stabilization .  The data shows a significant increasing trend in all rolling Mann-Kendall intervals . The inflection point occurs near the end of the simulation, suggesting a shift in the rate of adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2654.81 units and exhibits a general decreasing trend over time .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.5879 at the final step, indicating a substantial overall increase over the simulation period . The data shows a dominant oscillation period of 15 steps, with strong oscillations noted at several points towards the latter half of the simulation . Overall, the mean value health deviation demonstrates a clear upward trajectory, reflecting changes in milk consumption over time .
