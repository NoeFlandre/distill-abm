 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, which are central to adaptive (co-)management strategies .  An initial period of increasing deviation from the recommended number of livestock, the system undergoes a correction phase where the deviation decreases and eventually stabilizes, indicating a potential adaptation or learning process among the agents . The mean reserve biomass in the grazing system starts at an initial value of 90,000 units . The overall trend demonstrates an initial decline in biomass followed by a robust and sustained recovery .  The average cumulative number of destocked livestock across all households in the grazing system model shows a consistent increasing trend over the 100 time steps . The data shows a continuous increase in inequality, with no detected peaks or valleys, implying a steady rise without significant fluctuations . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages .
