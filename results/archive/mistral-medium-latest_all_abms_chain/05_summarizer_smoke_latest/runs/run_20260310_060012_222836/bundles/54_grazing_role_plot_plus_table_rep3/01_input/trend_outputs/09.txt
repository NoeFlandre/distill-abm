The average cumulative number of destocked livestock across all households in the grazing system model shows a consistent increasing trend over the 100 time steps. Initially, the destocking starts at zero and rapidly increases in the early stages, with a steep slope indicating a high rate of destocking. This initial phase is followed by a gradual decrease in the rate of increase, as evidenced by the declining slopes in subsequent time intervals. The mean destock total starts at 0 and reaches approximately 96.78 by the end of the simulation period. Key inflection points are observed around time steps 45, 47, 53, 56, 57, 62, 67, 72, 74, and 75, suggesting notable changes in the rate of destocking during these periods. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation, with significant p-values across all intervals. Change points are detected at time steps 25, 60, and 100, marking significant shifts in the destocking pattern. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 49 time steps, with strong oscillations observed around specific intervals, indicating periodic variations in the destocking rates. Overall, the destocking process exhibits a clear upward trajectory with varying rates of increase, reflecting the dynamic interactions between households, pastures, and livestock within the adaptive grazing system.
