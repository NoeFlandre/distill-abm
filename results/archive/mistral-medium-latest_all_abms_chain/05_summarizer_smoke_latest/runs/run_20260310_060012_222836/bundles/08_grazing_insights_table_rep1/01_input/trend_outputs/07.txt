The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of 0.0000, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock. Over time, the inequality gradually increases, reaching a value of 0.0726 at the final time step. The mean value of the Gini coefficient across all time steps is 0.0302, with a median of 0.0229, suggesting that the distribution is slightly skewed towards lower inequality values. The standard deviation of 0.0239 indicates some variability in the inequality levels over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend in inequality across all time windows, with a consistent slope of approximately 0.001. Change points are detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100, suggesting notable shifts in the trend at these points. The dominant period of oscillation is 49 time steps, with strong oscillation indices observed towards the latter part of the simulation. For decision makers, the increasing trend in inequality suggests a growing disparity in the distribution of healthy livestock among households. This trend may indicate the need for interventions or policies aimed at promoting more equitable distribution of resources and outcomes in the common property grazing system.
