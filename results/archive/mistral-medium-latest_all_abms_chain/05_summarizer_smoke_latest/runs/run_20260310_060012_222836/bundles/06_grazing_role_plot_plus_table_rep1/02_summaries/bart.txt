 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . The extension focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The simulation demonstrates an initial increase in risk-taking behavior, followed by a decrease and eventual stabilization . The mean household risk attitude exhibits strong oscillations, particularly noticeable between steps 20 to 35 . The dominant period of these oscillations is around 49 steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the risk attitude adjustments by households . The overall mean value across the entire simulation period is 4846, with a median of 9.5663 .  System shows strong oscillations initially, which diminish over time, indicating a stabilization in the number of destocked livestock as the simulation progresses . The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at a value of 0, indicating perfect equality . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation .  The statistical analysis indicates significant decreasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation . The dominant period of oscillation is around 49 steps, suggesting some cyclical behavior in the data . The overall mean number of non-learning households across the simulation is approximately 16.37, with a standard deviation of about 1.92 .
