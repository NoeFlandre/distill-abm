 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The extension focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, which are central to adaptive (co-)management strategies . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making .  The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households starts at 90.5% . The variability in risk attitudes decreases over time, indicating a convergence towards more uniform risk attitudes among households as the simulation progresses . The average amount of green biomass across all patches starts at approximately 45,000 units .  The trend persists with a consistent upward trajectory, reflecting an ongoing divergence in household livestock health . By the final time step of 100, the Gini coefficient reaches approximately 0.0726, signifying a substantial inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly .
