The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, there is a rapid increase in the number of males killed, starting from zero and rising steeply. This sharp increase continues until around 500 steps, where the cumulative count reaches approximately 80 males killed. Following this initial phase, the rate of increase begins to slow down, indicating a transition period. Between 500 and 1000 steps, the cumulative number of males killed continues to rise but at a more gradual pace, reaching around 100 males killed. Beyond 1000 steps, the trend stabilizes, and the cumulative count plateaus, fluctuating slightly but generally maintaining a steady state around 120 males killed. This plateau persists for the remainder of the observed time steps, indicating that the rate at which males are killed has balanced out with other dynamic factors in the model. The overall trend suggests an initial phase of rapid hunting followed by a stabilization phase where the number of males killed levels off.
