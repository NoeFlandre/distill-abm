 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with relative spatial scale . Beyond 1000 steps, the trend stabilizes, and the cumulative count plateaus, fluctuating slightly but generally maintaining a steady state around 120 males killed .
