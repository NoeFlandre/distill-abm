The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the simulation. Initially, the number of bands is set at 20 and this value does not change over the entire duration of the simulation, which spans 73000 ticks. This stability suggests that the parameters governing the hunter-gatherer dynamics, such as energy costs, food values, and movement costs, are balanced in a way that sustains the initial number of bands without leading to any increase or decrease. For decision makers, this implies that under the given conditions and parameters, the system reaches a stable equilibrium where the number of hunter-gatherer bands is maintained. This could be useful for understanding the sustainability of human populations in similar ecological settings and for predicting long-term outcomes of human-environment interactions.
