The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. From the onset, there is a rapid decline in energy levels, with notable values such as approximately 1989.925 units at the second tick and around 1263.3 units at the 55th tick. This downward trend continues steadily, with the energy levels dropping to negative values relatively early in the simulation. For instance, by the 394th tick, the energy level is around -2005.95 units. The decline persists with increasing negative values, reaching approximately -23807 units by the 1594th tick and further dropping to around -37713 units by the 2284th tick. The lowest energy level recorded is -38233 units at the 2310th tick, marking the end of the simulation. Throughout the simulation, the energy levels of the forager bands consistently decrease without any periods of recovery or stabilization. The statistical analysis confirms this continuous decline, with significant decreasing trends identified in all rolling Mann-Kendall tests across various segments of the simulation. The change points and inflection indices further support the observation of a steady decline in energy levels, indicating a persistent and significant reduction in the total energy available to the forager bands over time.
