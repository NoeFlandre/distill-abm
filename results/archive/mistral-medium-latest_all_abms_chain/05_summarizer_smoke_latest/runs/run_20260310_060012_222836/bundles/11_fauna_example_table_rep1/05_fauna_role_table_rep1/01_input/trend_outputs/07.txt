The cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the death count starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages, with significant growth observed up to around 1,159 ticks. During this phase, the death count rises sharply, indicating a high rate of mortality among the prey population. This trend is supported by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis, which shows strong increasing trends with high tau values and significant p-values. Specifically, the death count increases with slopes ranging from 0.346 to 0.017 in the early phases, reflecting a rapid accumulation of deaths. By around 1,159 ticks, the death count reaches approximately 233, which is close to the median value of the entire simulation period. Following this initial rapid increase, the trend stabilizes, and the death count exhibits a period of no significant trend between approximately 1,160 and 1,391 ticks. During this phase, the death count fluctuates around the median value, indicating a temporary equilibrium in the mortality rate. After this stable period, there is a slight resurgence in the increasing trend from around 1,392 to 1,855 ticks, although this trend is less pronounced compared to the initial phase. The death count continues to rise but at a much slower rate, with a slope of approximately 0.003. Towards the end of the simulation, from around 1,856 ticks onwards, the trend shifts to a decreasing pattern. This phase is characterized by a slight decline in the death count, indicating a reduction in the mortality rate among the prey population. The death count reaches its peak values around 240 at several points, notably around 1,876 ticks, before experiencing minor fluctuations and slight decreases. Throughout the simulation, the death count exhibits some periodic oscillations, with a dominant period of approximately 63 ticks. These oscillations are particularly noticeable towards the end of the simulation, suggesting cyclic variations in the mortality rate. Overall, the cumulative death count in the MHPM simulation demonstrates an initial rapid increase, followed by a period of stabilization, a slight resurgence, and finally a decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation period.
