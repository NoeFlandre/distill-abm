The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear trend over the simulation period. Initially, the count starts at zero and begins to increase steadily. The early phase of the simulation, up to around 1,160 ticks, shows a rapid and significant increase in the number of females killed, with the slope of the increase being quite steep. This trend continues but starts to moderate around 1,160 ticks, where the rate of increase begins to slow down. By approximately 1,624 ticks, the trend shows a more gradual increase, indicating a slowing accumulation rate. The peak value of females killed is observed around 1,826 ticks, reaching approximately 123.6667. Following this peak, there is a slight decrease or stabilization in the cumulative count, indicating a potential equilibrium or a reduction in the hunting rate. The simulation data shows several inflection points and change points, suggesting periods where the rate of change in the cumulative count shifts. Notably, the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in various segments of the simulation, with a shift to a decreasing trend observed towards the end of the simulation period. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the hunting dynamics. Overall, the data suggests an initial rapid hunting phase followed by a gradual approach to equilibrium or saturation in the cumulative number of female prey animals hunted.
