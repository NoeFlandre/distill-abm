The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows relatively stable dynamics over the simulation period. Initially, the percentage of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33% and remains close to this value throughout the simulation, ending at around 32.998%. The mean percentage of vegetated patches is 32.6558%, with a median of 32.9696%, indicating a slight skew in the data distribution. The standard deviation is relatively low at 0.9503, suggesting minimal variability around the mean. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, indicating a notable dip in vegetation coverage at this point. The data series exhibits several peaks and valleys, but these fluctuations are minimal, with prominence values close to zero, indicating minor variations. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals periods of both increasing and decreasing trends in vegetation coverage. Initially, there are significant increasing trends in vegetation coverage up to tick 1623, followed by decreasing trends from tick 1624 onwards. The oscillation analysis identifies a dominant period of 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in vegetation coverage. For decision-makers, the overall stability in vegetation coverage implies a relatively balanced interaction between grazing by megaherbivores and grass regrowth. However, the slight dip at tick 365 and the identified change points may warrant further investigation to understand the underlying causes and potential impacts on the ecosystem dynamics.
