The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, with small peaks and valleys observed around the initial values. For instance, slight increases are noted at early time steps, such as an increase to approximately 1989.925 and 1263.30. However, these are followed by immediate declines. The overall trend is a steady and significant decrease in energy levels. By the midpoint of the simulation, the energy levels have dropped substantially, reaching values around -23807. This downward trend continues, with the energy levels plummeting further to approximately -38233 by the end of the simulation. The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decrease, with all rolling Mann-Kendall tests indicating significant negative trends across different segments of the simulation. The change points analysis also supports this, identifying key time steps where the rate of decrease shifts, but consistently remains negative. The oscillation summary indicates some periodic fluctuations towards the end of the simulation, but these are minor compared to the overall decreasing trend. For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters, the energy available to forager bands is not sustainable over the long term, leading to a continuous decline. This could imply that the current hunting and movement costs, along with the energy gains from prey, are insufficient to maintain the energy levels of the foragers, potentially leading to their eventual inability to sustain hunting activities.
