 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at 45,000 units . The average number of livestock destocked starts at zero, indicating no initial removal of livestock . There is a sharp increase in the early time steps, peaking significantly at the third time step . This initial spike suggests a rapid response to immediate resource limitations or environmental stressors affecting the households . The dominant period of oscillation around 49 time steps suggests cyclical patterns in household decision-making .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 10 time steps, reaching approximately 15 households . The average cumulative number of livestock that have been destocked across all households starts at 0 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . The overall trend shows a significant increase in the adoption of social learning behaviors .
