 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It focuses on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The agent-based model simulates the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The trends reflect the dynamics of consumer behavior influenced by perception, habits, and social interactions within the context of the model . There are no detected peaks, valleys, or inflection points, reinforcing the steady and uniform increase in milk choices over time .  The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005 . Overall, the simulation demonstrates an initial rapid adoption phase, followed by a stabilization period with minor fluctuations, and a final phase of increased consumption . The dominant period of oscillation is around 15 time steps, suggesting cyclic patterns in the consumption behavior of the agents .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a rapid increase in the early time steps, reaching a value of approximately 13.4756 by the 13th time step . Following this rapid increase, the mean deviation stabilizes around a high value, fluctuating slightly but remaining close to 13.4302 for the remainder of the simulation .
