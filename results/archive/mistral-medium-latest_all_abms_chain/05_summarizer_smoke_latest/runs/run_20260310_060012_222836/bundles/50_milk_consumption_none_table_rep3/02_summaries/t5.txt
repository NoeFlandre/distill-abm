agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . agent-based model simulating the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 shows a consistent increasing trend in the total number of times agents chose whole milk . Initially, the value starts at 0 and steadily rises over the time steps, reaching a maximum of approximately 2194.57 at the final time step . the simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time . Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . there is a rapid increase in consumption during the early time steps, with significant growth observed up to around the 9th time step . the mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 . as time progresses, the deviation gradually increases, reaching a maximum value of 0.7251 at the end of the simulation period .
