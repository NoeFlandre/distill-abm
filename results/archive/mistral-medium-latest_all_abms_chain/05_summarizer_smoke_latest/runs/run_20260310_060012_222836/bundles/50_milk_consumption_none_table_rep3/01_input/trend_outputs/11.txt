The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the mean deviation starts at 0.0000, indicating that agents' health values perfectly align with their milk choices at the beginning of the simulation. As time progresses, the deviation gradually increases, reaching a maximum value of 0.7251 at the end of the simulation period. Throughout the simulation, the mean deviation exhibits a steady rise, with several inflection points detected at various time steps, suggesting changes in the rate of increase. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across different segments of the simulation, with significant positive slopes indicating a continuous rise in the mean deviation. Additionally, there are strong oscillations detected in the latter half of the simulation, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the rate of deviation increase. Overall, the trend indicates that over time, the alignment between agents' health values and their milk choices diverges, reflecting changes in perception, habits, and social influence affecting their decision-making processes.
