The data from the repeated simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the number of female prey animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of female prey animals has decreased to about 80. The decline continues, and by approximately 1000 steps, the number of female prey animals drops to around 20. Beyond this point, the number of female prey animals continues to decrease gradually, approaching zero by around 2000 steps. This trend indicates a significant reduction in the population of female prey animals over the course of the simulation, suggesting a potential extinction event influenced by the various socio-natural dynamics and parameters set in the model.
