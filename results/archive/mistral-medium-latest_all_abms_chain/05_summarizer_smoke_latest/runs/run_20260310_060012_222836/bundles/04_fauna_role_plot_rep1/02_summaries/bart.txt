 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration of the simulation . The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent downward trend .  The data from the repeated simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of male animals over time . The simulations suggest that under the given parameters, the male animal population experiences a rapid decline followed by a more gradual reduction, eventually leading to near extinction .
