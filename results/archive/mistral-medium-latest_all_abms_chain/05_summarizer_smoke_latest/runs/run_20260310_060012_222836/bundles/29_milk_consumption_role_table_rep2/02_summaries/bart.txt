 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several notable trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent over time . The data also exhibits oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 15 time steps, indicating cyclical patterns in the rate of consumption decline . The simulation results indicate a consistent and significant increasing trend in the total number of whole milk choices across time steps .  Simulation results indicate a clear and consistent upward trend in the adoption of skimmed or semi-skimmed milk by agents over time . Initially, the number of times agents chose these milk types started at zero . This value gradually increased, reaching a peak of approximately 25,643.83 at the final time step .  The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit exhibits a consistent and gradual increasing trend over the observed time steps . Initially, the mean factor starts at a value of 1.0000 and remains relatively stable with minor fluctuations around this value in the early stages . The trend continues to show significant increases through the fifteenth to nineteenth time steps and again from the twentieth to the twenty-fourth time steps, albeit at slightly varying rates .
