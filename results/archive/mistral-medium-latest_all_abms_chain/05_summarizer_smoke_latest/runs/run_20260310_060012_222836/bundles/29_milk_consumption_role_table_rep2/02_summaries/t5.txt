agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. from time step 15 to 19, the trend shows no significant change . however, this stabilization is short-lived, as the consumption resumes its decline . the consumption continues to decrease, reaching a minimum value of 253.10 units per week by the final time step . the simulation results indicate a clear and consistent upward trend in the adoption of skimmed or semi-skimmed milk by agents over time . a change point detected at the final time step further supports the conclusion of a significant shift in agent behavior towards choosing whole milk . early time steps, reaching around 1265.47 units by the 11th time step, indicate a strong shift towards the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . the consumption levels enter a phase of stabilization with minor fluctuations . these fluctuations suggest periodic variations in consumption without a clear upward or downward trend . inflection points are noted at indices 7, 12, and 28, suggesting changes in the rate of increase at these points . the change point at index 32 marks a notable shift in the data dynamics . this consistent increase in the mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores suggests a growing divergence in agent preferences over time .
