 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The simulation of the agent-based model reveals distinct trends in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption over time . The overall trend is a significant and steady decline in the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent . The rolling Mann-Kendall test results show a consistent increasing trend across all analyzed intervals .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows the trends in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption over a series of steps, representing the time evolution of consumer behavior . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents in the simulation starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units and exhibits a general decreasing trend over time .  The mean habit factor for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk starts at a baseline value of 1.0000 . Initially, there is no significant trend observed in the early steps, with values slightly fluctuating around the initial value . The data shows strong oscillations in certain periods, particularly around steps 17 to 30, suggesting periodic variations in the habit factor .  The mean value environmental deviation in the agent-based model simulating milk consumption behaviors shows distinct trends over the observed time steps . Initially, the deviation starts at 0.0000 and rapidly increases, reaching approximately 13.4756 by the 13th time step . This pattern indicates that the agents' environmental values and their implied values by milk choices quickly align and then maintain a consistent deviation .
