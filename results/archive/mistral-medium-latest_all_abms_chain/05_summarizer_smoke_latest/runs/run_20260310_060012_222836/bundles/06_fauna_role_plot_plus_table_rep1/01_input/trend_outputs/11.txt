The total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12500 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has dropped significantly to approximately 4000 units. The decline continues steadily, with notable inflection points around 316, 453, and 516 time steps, indicating periods where the rate of decline changes. By 1000 time steps, the total energy of male animals is further reduced to around 1000 units. The trend continues to show a decrease, albeit at a slower rate, with the energy levels approaching zero as the simulation progresses towards 2000 time steps. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various intervals. The model's parameters, such as high initial energy values and the costs associated with hunting and movement, contribute to this observed trend. The interaction between hunters and prey, along with environmental factors, drives the continuous reduction in the total energy of male animals over time.
