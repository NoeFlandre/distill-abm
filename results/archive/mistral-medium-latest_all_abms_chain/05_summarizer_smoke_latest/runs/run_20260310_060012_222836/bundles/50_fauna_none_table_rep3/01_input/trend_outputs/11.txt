The combined total energy available in the population of male prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear overall decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12500 units. This high initial value is followed by a rapid and significant decrease in the early stages of the simulation. The decline continues steadily, with notable change points around ticks 125, 350, 500, 625, and 875, indicating shifts in the rate of energy decrease. Despite the overall downward trend, there are periods of minor fluctuations and temporary increases in energy levels. For instance, between ticks 1392 and 1623, there is a significant increasing trend, albeit with a modest slope. Additionally, there are several peaks and valleys observed around ticks 377 to 408, where the energy levels show minor oscillations. The dominant period of these oscillations is approximately 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern within the broader decline. The energy levels eventually approach zero by the end of the simulation at tick 73000, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy in the male prey population. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms the significant decreasing trends across various segments of the simulation, with only a few segments showing temporary increases or stable periods.
