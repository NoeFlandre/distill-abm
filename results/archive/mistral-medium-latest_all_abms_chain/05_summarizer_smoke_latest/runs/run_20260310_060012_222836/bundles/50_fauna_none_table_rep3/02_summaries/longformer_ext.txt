The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction. It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history. The model allows users to manipulate parameters to observe how different conditions interact to influence extinction or equilibrium outcomes. The landscape is a simplified grassland environment with patches of grass, ungulates, and human hunters. The model uses 'ticks' as temporal units, which can be scaled to any temporal granularity. The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with relative spatial scale. The model is interactive, allowing users to manipulate variables to test a wide range of hypotheses related to extinction in a socio-natural system. Hunters in the model follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, making decisions based on environmental feedback, their energy status, prey food value, prey density, and the cost of hunting. The change points identified at various intervals (e.g., 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, 2310) indicate moments where the rate of decline shifts, but the overall direction remains consistently downward.
