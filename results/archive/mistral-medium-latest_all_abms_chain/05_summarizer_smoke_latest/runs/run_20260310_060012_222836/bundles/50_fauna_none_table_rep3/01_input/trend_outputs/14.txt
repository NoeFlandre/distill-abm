The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows relatively stable dynamics in the percentage of vegetated landscape patches over time. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33%. Throughout the simulation, the mean percentage of vegetated patches is around 32.66%, with a slight decrease observed over time, ending at roughly 32.998%. The minimum value observed is about 24.91% at an early time step, indicating a brief period of reduced vegetation cover. The model exhibits minor fluctuations, with several peaks and valleys that are very close in value, suggesting minimal variability in vegetation cover. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates periods of both increasing and decreasing trends in vegetation cover, with significant changes detected at various intervals. Notably, there are periods of increasing vegetation cover early in the simulation, followed by phases of decreasing cover. The dominant oscillation period is around 63 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in vegetation dynamics. Overall, the vegetation cover remains relatively stable with minor oscillations, reflecting the interplay between environmental factors and agent behaviors within the model.
