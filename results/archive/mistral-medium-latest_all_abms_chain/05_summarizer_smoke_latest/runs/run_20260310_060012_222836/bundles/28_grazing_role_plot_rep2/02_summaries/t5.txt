agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the average reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . Initially, the mean reserve biomass starts at approximately 90,000 units . within the first 10 time steps, there is a sharp decline in the number of livestock, dropping to around 45 . the Gini coefficient gradually increases, reflecting growing inequality in livestock distribution . by the 40th step, the inequality continues to rise, with values typically ranging between 0.015 and 0.045 . this indicates a significant increase in inequality over time, suggesting certain households accumulate a disproportionately higher number of healthy livestock compared to others .
