The average green biomass across all patches, accounting for reductions due to grazing, exhibits a consistent trend across all simulations. Initially, the mean green biomass starts at approximately 45,000 units. This value remains relatively stable for the first 20 steps. Following this period, there is a noticeable decline in the mean green biomass, reaching a low point of around 40,000 units by step 40. After this decline, the mean green biomass begins to increase steadily. This upward trend continues until around step 80, where the mean green biomass stabilizes at approximately 60,000 units. From step 80 to step 100, the mean green biomass remains relatively constant, indicating a plateau in the system. This trend suggests an initial stability in biomass, followed by a period of decline, and then a recovery phase leading to a new stable state at a higher biomass level.
