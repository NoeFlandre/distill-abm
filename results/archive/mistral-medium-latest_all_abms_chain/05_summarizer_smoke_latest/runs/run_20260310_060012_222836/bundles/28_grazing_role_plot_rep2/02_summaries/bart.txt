 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . The extension focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The average green biomass across all patches, accounting for reductions due to grazing, exhibits a consistent trend across all simulations . The trends suggest an initial period of adjustment followed by a rapid recovery and stabilization of the biomass reserves in the grazing system . The metric representing the average total number of livestock across all households exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps .  Simulations show a variety of trends in the adoption of social learning among households over time . The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households over 100 time steps . This indicates a significant increase in inequality over time, suggesting that certain households accumulate a disproportionately higher number of healthy livestock compared to others .
