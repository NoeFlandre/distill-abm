The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent downward trend over the course of the simulation. Initially, the total energy of foragers starts at approximately 0. As the simulation progresses, the total energy gradually decreases. By around 500 steps, the total energy of foragers drops to approximately -5000. This decline continues steadily, reaching about -10000 by 1000 steps. The trend persists, with the total energy decreasing further to around -20000 by 1500 steps. As the simulation approaches 2000 steps, the total energy of foragers falls to approximately -35000. This continuous decline in total energy suggests that the foragers are consistently expending more energy than they are gaining, likely due to the costs associated with movement and processing prey, coupled with the energy values gained from hunting.
