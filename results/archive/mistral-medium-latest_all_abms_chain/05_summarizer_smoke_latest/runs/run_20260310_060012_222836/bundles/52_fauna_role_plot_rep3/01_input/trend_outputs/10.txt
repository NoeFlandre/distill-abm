The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations show a consistent trend in the total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the number of animals starts at approximately 250, reflecting the set value for the initial number of prey. In the early stages of the simulation, up to around 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the total number of animals. This steep decrease suggests a high rate of predation or mortality early on, potentially due to the combined effects of human hunting pressure and environmental factors. By around 1000 steps, the number of animals has significantly reduced, with most simulations showing fewer than 50 animals remaining. Beyond this point, the decline continues but at a slower rate, with the number of animals gradually approaching zero. By approximately 2000 steps, nearly all simulations show that the total number of animals has reached zero or is very close to it. This indicates that, under the given parameters, the prey population tends to go extinct within this timeframe. The consistent pattern across multiple simulations suggests that the model parameters lead to a predictable outcome of prey extinction within the simulated environment.
