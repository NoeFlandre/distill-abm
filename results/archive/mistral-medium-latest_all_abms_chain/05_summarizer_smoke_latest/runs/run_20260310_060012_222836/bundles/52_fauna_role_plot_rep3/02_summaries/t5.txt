the model incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . the model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events or equilibrium conditions . key parameters of the model include: - **init-foragers**: Represents the initial number of human hunters . the model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges . the temporal units are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . Initially, the number of foragers is set at 20 and this value does not change over the course of the 73000 ticks . the cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . between 500 and 1500 steps, there is a rapid increase, rising steeply from zero to around 150 deaths . this phase indicates a high mortality rate among prey population, likely due to a combination of initial predation pressure from human hunters . by approximately 500 steps, the total energy of male animals drops significantly to around 2,000 units . this downward trend continues more gradually, with the energy further decreasing to near zero by 2,000 steps . following this steep decline, the energy levels gradually approach zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of female prey population's energy reserves .
