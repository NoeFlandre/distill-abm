 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . The model explores how perception, habits, and social influence affect individual decision-making processes regarding milk choices . Trend is characterized by a continuous increase without any detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a uniform adoption pattern . For decision makers, trends imply a growing preference for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the British public over the studied period .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows a general increasing trend in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over the time period from 1974 to 2005 . For decision makers, this trend implies a significant shift away from whole milk consumption over the simulated period . Understanding these dynamics can help in strategizing interventions or policies aimed at influencing milk consumption behaviors .  The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005 . For decision makers, these trends imply a growing divergence in the choice function scores among agents over time, reflecting increasing diversity in the factors influencing milk consumption choices .
