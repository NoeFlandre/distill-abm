The simulation results indicate a consistent and significant increasing trend in the total number of times agents chose whole milk over the observed time period. Starting from an initial value of 0, the metric rises steadily, reaching a final value of approximately 2194.57. The trend is characterized by a continuous increase without any detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a uniform adoption pattern. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various segments of the time series, with significant positive slopes ranging from approximately 64 to 76. This indicates a robust and steady rise in the consumption of whole milk over time. The inflection points at indices 1, 7, and 31 suggest slight changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. The dominant oscillation period of 15.00 and strong oscillation indices towards the end of the series imply some periodic fluctuations, but these do not disrupt the overall increasing trend. For decision makers, this trend suggests a growing preference or habit formation towards whole milk among the agents, which could be influenced by the model parameters such as habit threshold, social susceptibility, and network characteristics. Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions or strategies to influence milk consumption behaviors effectively.
