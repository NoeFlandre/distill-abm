By the final step, step 31, the total number of whole milk choices is slightly above 2000.

The steady increase in the total number of whole milk choices over time suggests that, despite the introduction and growing popularity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, whole milk remained a significant choice among consumers throughout the period. This trend indicates that habits, social influence, and perceptions of health and environmental characteristics collectively contributed to the sustained consumption of whole milk. For decision-makers, this implies that interventions aimed at reducing whole milk consumption need to consider the strong role of habit and social influence in consumer choices. Understanding these dynamics can help in designing more effective strategies to promote healthier or more environmentally friendly milk alternatives.
