 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others .  The number of choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over the 30 steps of the simulation . The total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices increases steadily over time . The mean whole milk quantity consumed by agents starts at approximately 2700 units at the initial time step . There is a sharp decline in consumption within the first 10 steps, dropping to around 1000 units .  The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption starts at a value of 1.00 and quickly rises to approximately 1.02 within the first few steps . The influence of habit on the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk grows stronger over time, suggesting that as consumers become more accustomed to these milk types, their consumption habits become more entrenched .
