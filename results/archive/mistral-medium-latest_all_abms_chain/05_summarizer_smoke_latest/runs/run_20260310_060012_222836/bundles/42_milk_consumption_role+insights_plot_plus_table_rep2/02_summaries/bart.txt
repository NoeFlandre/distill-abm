 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent in the simulation exhibits distinct phases over the time steps analyzed . For decision makers, these trends suggest a substantial shift away from whole milk consumption . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions or marketing strategies that align with consumer behavior trends towards healthier or more environmentally friendly milk choices .  The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents in the simulation shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time . For decision-makers, this trend indicates a steady and predictable increase in consumer engagement with milk choices, which could inform strategies related to market supply, consumer education, and product availability .  The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period . This pattern suggests that initial adoption is rapid, followed by a stabilization phase, and then a final push towards higher consumption levels . Understanding this trend can help in designing strategies that effectively address habit formation and potentially shift consumption patterns towards healthier alternatives .  The mean value health deviation in the agent-based model simulating milk consumption behaviors shows a clear and consistent increasing trend over the time steps analyzed . The steady increase in deviation over time indicates that consumer preferences continue to evolve, albeit at a slower pace, emphasizing the need for ongoing engagement and adaptation of strategies to meet changing consumer needs .
