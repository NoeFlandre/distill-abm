The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, the mean habit factor starts at 1.0000 and gradually rises to 1.0502 by the end of the simulation. The overall mean value of the habit factor across all time steps is 1.0359, with a median of 1.0376, indicating a slight upward trend in the data. The standard deviation is relatively low at 0.0110, suggesting that the values do not deviate significantly from the mean. The variance is also low at 0.0001, reinforcing the stability of the trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent increasing trend across all time windows, with a significant p-value of 0.027, confirming the upward trajectory of the habit factor. There are no detected peaks or valleys, but inflection points are noted at indices 7 and 12, suggesting subtle changes in the rate of increase. The dominant period of oscillation is 15.00, indicating a cyclical pattern in the data. The habit factor for whole milk consumption steadily increases, reflecting a growing influence of habit on the choice of whole milk over time. This trend suggests that as agents become more accustomed to consuming whole milk, their habit strengthens, making them more likely to continue choosing whole milk. For decision-makers, this implies that interventions aimed at changing milk consumption habits may need to account for the strengthening of existing habits over time. Understanding this trend can help in designing strategies that effectively address habit formation and potentially shift consumption patterns towards healthier alternatives.
