The mean value health deviation in the agent-based model simulating milk consumption behaviors shows a clear and consistent increasing trend over the time steps analyzed. Initially, the mean value health deviation starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a rapid increase in the early steps, reaching approximately 0.5584 by around the 10th step. This rapid increase is followed by a more gradual rise, with the deviation continuing to grow steadily. By the final step, the mean value health deviation reaches a peak of 0.7251. Throughout the simulation, the deviation values exhibit a consistent upward trajectory, with no detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady increase without significant fluctuations. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various segments of the time steps, with significant positive slopes. The inflection points identified at steps 4, 6, 16, 19, 22, 28, and 31 suggest subtle changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. The dominant oscillation period of 15.00 and the strong oscillation indices towards the latter part of the time steps indicate some periodic variations, but these do not disrupt the overall increasing trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that over time, the agents' health perceptions increasingly deviate from the values implied by their milk choices, suggesting a growing misalignment between perceived health benefits and actual consumption behaviors. This insight could inform strategies aimed at aligning consumer perceptions more closely with healthier choices, potentially through targeted education or marketing campaigns.
