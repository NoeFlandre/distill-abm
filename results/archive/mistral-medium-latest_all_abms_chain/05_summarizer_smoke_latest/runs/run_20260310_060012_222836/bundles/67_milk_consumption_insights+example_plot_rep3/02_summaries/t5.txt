agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . the steady increase in whole milk choices implies that, despite the influence of various factors such as perception, habits, and social influence, the overall trend in milk choice behavior is consistent . for decision-makers, this trend suggests a predictable pattern of decision-making among the agents, which could be useful for forecasting and planning purposes . the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed choices starts at zero and increases gradually over the first 5 steps . the mean habit factor for whole milk starts at a value of 1.00 and rapidly increases to approximately 1.02 within the first few steps . the habit factor continues to rise steadily over the subsequent steps, reaching around 1.03 by step 10 . this gradual increase indicates a strengthening of habits related to whole milk consumption among the agents . from step 20 to step 25, the deviation remains relatively stable . this stability suggests a temporary equilibrium where the agents' perceptions and choices are relatively balanced . the final increase indicates a renewed divergence between perceived health values and the values implied by milk choices .
