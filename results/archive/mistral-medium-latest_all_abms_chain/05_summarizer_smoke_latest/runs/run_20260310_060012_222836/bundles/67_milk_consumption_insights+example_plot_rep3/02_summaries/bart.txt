 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model explores how perception, habits, and social influence affect individual decision-making processes regarding milk choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person, using both theoretical grounding and empirical data for calibration against observed macro-level data .  Total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases linearly over the 30 steps of the simulation, reaching a value of approximately 32,000 . For decision makers, this trend suggests a predictable pattern in consumer behavior regarding whole milk choices, which could be useful for forecasting and planning purposes .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 for the first few steps, indicating that initially, habits have a minimal influence on the choice of skimmed milk . Around step 5, there is a slight increase, suggesting that habits begin to play a more significant role in milk choice around step 5 .
