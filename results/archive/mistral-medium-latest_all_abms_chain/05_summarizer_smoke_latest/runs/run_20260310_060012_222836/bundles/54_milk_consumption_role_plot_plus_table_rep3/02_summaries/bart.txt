 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption over time . The data also shows some minor oscillations, particularly with a dominant period of 15 steps, indicating some variability in the consumption patterns . Initial rapid adoption phase is followed by a period of stabilization and a subsequent gradual increase .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined exhibits a consistent and linear increasing trend over the 33 time steps . Starting from an initial value of 0, the total choices made increase uniformly, reaching a final value of 32,000 . The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent increasing trend across all time windows .  The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period . Over the course of 33 time steps, this value gradually increases, reaching a final value of 1.0502 . The trend analysis indicates consistent increasing patterns across different segments of the simulation, with significant positive slopes in each segment .
