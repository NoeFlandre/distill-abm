the megaherbivore hunting pressure model ( mhpm ) is an agent - based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores. the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters.  the model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix. the number of human hunter - gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation duration. the number of males killed continues to increase steadily, reaching around 116 by the 1, 000th time step. the cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands starts at zero and increases rapidly in the initial time steps. the cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains at this level until around 700 time steps. by around 1, 000 time steps, the cumulative number of deaths begins to plateau, indicating a stabilization in the death rate. the decline continues more gradually until around 1000 steps, where the number of male animals stabilizes around 1. by the end of the simulation, the number of female prey animals approaches zero, indicating a near - complete extinction event. the total number of animals in the simulation starts at approximately 167 and declines rapidly over the first 500 time steps. the simulation ends with the total energy of male animals near zero, indicating a near - complete depletion of the male animal population. following this rapid decline, the rate of energy loss slows down, and the energy levels continue to decrease more gradually. from the 625th to the 875th time steps, the energy levels show a more gradual decline, stabilizing around 500 units. throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations in the total energy levels, particularly noticeable around the 700 to 800 time steps. however, these fluctuations are relatively small and do not significantly alter the overall trend of declining energy levels.
