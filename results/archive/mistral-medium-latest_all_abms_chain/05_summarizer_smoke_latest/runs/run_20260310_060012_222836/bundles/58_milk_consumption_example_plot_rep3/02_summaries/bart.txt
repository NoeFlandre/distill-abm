 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  At step 0, the total number of choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 30 steps . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,600 units . It declines rapidly over the first 10 steps, reaching around 1,000 units . This rapid rise suggests a swift adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk by the agents .  By the 30th step, the mean habit factor reaches a value of approximately 1.6, showing a consistent upward trend throughout the simulation . This trend suggests a growing preference or habit formation towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time among the agents .
