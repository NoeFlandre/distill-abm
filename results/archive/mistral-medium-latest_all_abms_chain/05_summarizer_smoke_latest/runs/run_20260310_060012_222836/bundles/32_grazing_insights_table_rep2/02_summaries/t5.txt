the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration of the model run . there were no fluctuations, peaks, or valleys in the data, indicating a complete lack of variability . a single period in the oscillation summary further reinforces the stability of this metric . cyclical patterns also imply the need for periodic review and adjustment of grazing practices to sustain biomass levels . the average number of livestock, including healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, shows a distinct trend over the simulated time steps . the most rapid increases occur early in the simulation, with the rate of increase slowing down as the number of households engaging in social learning approaches its peak . for decision makers, this trend indicates a growing adoption of social learning strategies among households over time .
