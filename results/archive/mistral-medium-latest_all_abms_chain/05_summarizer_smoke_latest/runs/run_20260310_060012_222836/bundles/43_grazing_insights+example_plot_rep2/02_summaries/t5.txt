the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the mean household risk attitude starts with a value of approximately 0.5 across all simulations . over the first 20 time steps, there is a rapid divergence in risk attitudes among households . some households exhibit an increase in risk attitude, reaching values as high as 0.8, while others show a decrease, dropping to values as low as -0.2 . the initial decline in biomass highlights the importance of managing initial grazing pressures to avoid overgrazing . the subsequent recovery and stabilization suggest adaptive management practices can lead to sustainable pasture use over time . the mean destock total of households starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 steps, reaching approximately 50 . this initial rapid increase indicates that households are quickly reducing their livestock numbers early in the simulation, possibly due to initial conditions or immediate reactions to environmental or economic factors .
