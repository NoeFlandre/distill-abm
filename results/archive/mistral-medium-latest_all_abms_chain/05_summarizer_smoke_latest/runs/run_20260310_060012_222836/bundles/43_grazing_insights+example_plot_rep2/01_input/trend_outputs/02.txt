By around 20 time steps, the risk attitudes begin to stabilize, with distinct groups forming based on their risk attitudes. This stabilization suggests that households have settled into different behavioral strategies based on their initial adaptations. Throughout the remaining time steps, from 20 to 100, the risk attitudes remain relatively stable within these formed groups. There are minor fluctuations, but the overall trends do not show significant changes. This stability implies that the households have reached a steady state in their risk attitudes, likely influenced by their learning experiences and social interactions within the model.
