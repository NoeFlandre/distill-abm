 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the entire simulation period . The initial value of zero suggests that at the beginning of the simulation, no agents had chosen these milk types . As the simulation progresses, the number of choices increases rapidly, reaching a peak of approximately 25,643.83 at the final time step . This trend suggests a significant shift in milk consumption preferences among the agents .  Consumption levels exhibit minor fluctuations throughout the late 1980s and early 1990s, with peaks and valleys suggesting periodic variations in consumer preferences or external influences . Overall trend indicates a strengthening influence of habit on the choice of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time .  Trend analysis shows a significant increasing trend in the initial time steps, followed by periods with no significant trend . The valleys, although slight, are observed at time steps 18, 20, 26, 29, and 31 . This suggests that the initial phase of the simulation is characterized by a rapid adjustment in the agents' choices, leading to a high deviation .
