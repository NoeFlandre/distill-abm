The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the deviation starts at 0.0000 and gradually rises to reach a maximum value of 0.7251 by the end of the simulation. The mean deviation across all time steps is 0.4965, with a median of 0.5584, indicating that the central tendency of the data is well-represented by these measures. The standard deviation of 0.1702 and variance of 0.0290 suggest moderate variability around the mean. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent increasing trend across various segments of the simulation, with significant positive slopes ranging from 0.063 to 0.003, all showing significant p-values. This consistent increase suggests that over time, agents' health perceptions and their actual milk choices increasingly diverge. The detection of a dominant oscillation period of 15.00 and several strong oscillation indices imply periodic fluctuations in the deviation, although the overall trend remains upward. The presence of multiple inflection points further supports the notion of periodic variations within the broader increasing trend. For decision-makers, this trend indicates a growing misalignment between perceived health values and actual consumption behaviors over time, which could inform strategies aimed at aligning consumer perceptions more closely with healthier choices.
