 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several key trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent over the observed time period . These trends suggest that interventions or factors influencing consumer behavior have a substantial impact on reducing whole milk consumption . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing policies or campaigns aimed at promoting healthier or alternative milk choices .  Simulation results indicate a consistent and significant upward trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk by agents over the observed time period . For decision makers, these trends suggest a growing preference or habit formation towards whole milk among the agents over time . The consistent increase also suggests that social influence and habit formation play crucial roles in shaping consumer choices .  The mean factor applied to the skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choice function due to habit shows a clear increasing trend over time . For decision makers, these trends suggest an initial rapid adoption phase followed by a stabilization period, with minor fluctuations and a slight increase towards the end . This implies that interventions or marketing efforts might be most impactful during the early stages to accelerate adoption . This consistent increase suggests that over time, agents' health perceptions and their actual milk choices increasingly diverge .
