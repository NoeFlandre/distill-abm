agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . endall analysis indicates significant increasing trends in the early and later stages of the simulation . the simulation ends with the highest consumption value of approximately 1456.60 units . this indicates a growing adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time . the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at a very low value of 5.29 units . this initial value suggests that at the beginning of the simulation period in 1974, the consumption of these milk types was minimal among the British public . the consumption then increases rapidly, indicating a significant shift in consumer preferences and behaviors . the initial value of 0.0000 suggests that agents start with similar choice function scores, but as the simulation progresses, their choices begin to differ more significantly . the maximum value of 0.7508 at the final time step underscores the increasing divergence in agent choices by the end of the simulation .
