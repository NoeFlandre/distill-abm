The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at 0, indicating perfect equality. Over the course of 100 time steps, the Gini coefficient increases steadily, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726. This trend suggests a gradual rise in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households. The increase is consistent and significant throughout the entire period, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results, which show a continuous increasing trend with high statistical significance (p=0.000) across all time windows. The inflection points and change points indicate notable shifts in the rate of increase, particularly around time steps 51, 71, and 100. The dominant period of oscillation suggests some cyclical patterns in the later stages of the simulation. For decision makers, this trend implies that over time, disparities in livestock health among households are likely to grow, which could have implications for resource allocation and support strategies aimed at maintaining equity in livestock health across households.
