agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at a very low value of 5.29 . the consumption then exhibits a rapid and significant increase over the early time steps, reaching a peak of approximately 1265.47 around the 11th time step . peaks are observed at time steps 13, 21, and 19, with values around 1240.35, 1226.86, and 1221.57, respectively . the dominant period of oscillation towards the end of the simulation highlights the strong and consistent preference for alternative milk types . the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the entire simulation period, reaching a final value of 32,000 . the standard deviation is relatively low at 0.0110, suggesting a slight upward shift in the central tendency over time . inflection points are noted at indices 7 and 12, suggesting subtle changes in the rate of increase at these points . the simulation identifies a dominant oscillation period of 15.00 . deviation dynamics suggest a rapid divergence between the agents' environmental perceptions and their actual milk consumption choices . follows this rapid increase, the mean deviation stabilizes around the value of 13.4302, with minor fluctuations . the dominant period of oscillation is 15 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the agent's decision-making process .
