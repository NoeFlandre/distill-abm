The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulation exhibits several notable trends and patterns over the observed time steps. Initially, the percentage of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable throughout the simulation, ending at around 33% as well. The mean percentage of vegetated patches across the simulation is approximately 32.66%, with a median value of 32.97%, indicating a slight skew in the data distribution. The standard deviation of 0.95 and variance of 0.90 suggest minor fluctuations around the mean. The minimum value observed is approximately 24.91% at time step 365, indicating the lowest point of vegetation coverage. The data series shows multiple peaks and valleys, with peaks reaching up to approximately 32.9957% and valleys dipping slightly to around 32.9942%. These minor fluctuations suggest a relatively stable vegetation coverage with slight periodic variations. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates several phases of increasing and decreasing trends. Initially, from time steps 0 to 1623, the vegetation coverage shows a significant increasing trend, albeit with varying strengths. This is followed by a decreasing trend from time steps 1624 to 2087. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 time steps, with strong oscillation indices observed at regular intervals, indicating periodic fluctuations in vegetation coverage. The change points identified at time steps 150, 300, 475, 725, and 2310 suggest shifts in the underlying dynamics of vegetation coverage. For decision makers, the overall stability in vegetation coverage with minor periodic fluctuations implies a relatively balanced interaction between the prey animals and the grass regrowth dynamics. The identified change points and trends can help in understanding the underlying mechanisms driving vegetation coverage and in making informed decisions regarding the management of the landscape and the parameters influencing the model.
