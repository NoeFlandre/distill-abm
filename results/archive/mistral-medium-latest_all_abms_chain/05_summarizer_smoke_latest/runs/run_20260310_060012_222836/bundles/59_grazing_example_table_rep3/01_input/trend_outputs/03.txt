After this low point, the reserve biomass begins a steady and consistent increase. This upward trend is marked by several phases of significant growth, particularly noticeable from the 33rd time step onwards. The biomass continues to rise, reaching its peak at approximately 121,072.59 units by the final time step. The overall trend indicates a recovery and subsequent growth in biomass, suggesting that the grazing system stabilizes and possibly benefits from adaptive management practices over time. The system shows strong oscillations particularly between the 10th and 20th, and 41st to 49th time steps, indicating periods of fluctuation in biomass levels.
