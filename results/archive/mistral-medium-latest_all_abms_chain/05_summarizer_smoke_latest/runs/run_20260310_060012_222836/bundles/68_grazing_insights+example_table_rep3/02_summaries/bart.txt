 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents begins at a low value of approximately 0.0074 . This metric shows a rapid increase in the early stages, reaching a peak value of around 0.3814 at around the 33rd time step . Following this peak, the deviation metric exhibits a general decreasing trend, indicating that households are gradually aligning their livestock numbers closer to the recommended values over time .  The average number of livestock destocked across all households begins at zero and exhibits a sharp increase, peaking significantly at 6.6144 by the third time step . The trend generally decreases over time, with smaller peaks and valleys indicating periodic adjustments . Understanding these patterns can inform policies aimed at supporting household resilience and sustainable livestock management .  The number of households following the E-RO strategy begins at 20 and exhibits minor fluctuations in the initial time steps . There is a slight increase peaking at 21.1 at time step 8, followed by a general decreasing trend . The dominant period of oscillation is around 49 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the data .
