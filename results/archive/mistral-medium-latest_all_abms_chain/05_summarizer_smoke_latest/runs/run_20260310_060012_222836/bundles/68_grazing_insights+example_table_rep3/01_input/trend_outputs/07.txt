The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at a value of 0.0, indicating perfect equality. Over the course of the simulation, the Gini coefficient increases steadily, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 at the final time step. This trend suggests a gradual increase in inequality among households regarding the distribution of healthy livestock. The increase is consistent and significant throughout the entire simulation, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results, which show a continuous increasing trend with high significance across all time windows. The presence of several inflection points and strong oscillation indices towards the latter part of the simulation suggests some variability in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. For decision makers, this implies that over time, disparities in livestock distribution among households are likely to grow, which could have implications for equity and resource allocation strategies in the community.
