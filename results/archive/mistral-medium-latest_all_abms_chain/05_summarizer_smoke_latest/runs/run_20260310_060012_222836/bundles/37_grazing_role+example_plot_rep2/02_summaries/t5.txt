agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . from time step 40 to 60, the risk attitudes of most households converge towards a neutral or slightly positive value, around 0.2 to 0.4 . this convergence suggests a period of adjustment where households are balancing their risk attitudes based on accumulated experiences and possibly social learning . the final time steps show that while there is some variability, the overall risk attitudes do not exhibit drastic changes . this period indicates a phase of adjustment where households are fine-tuning their livestock numbers in response to the ecological and climatic conditions . after 40 time steps, the mean destock of households fluctuates slightly but generally remains low and stable, hovering around 1 livestock or less . the low variance in the latter part of the simulation indicates consistent behavior across different simulation runs . from 20 to 100 steps, the number of non-learning households remains relatively constant . this stability suggests that after the initial variability, the system settles into a more predictable pattern .
