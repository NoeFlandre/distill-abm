 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . The extension focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, which are central to adaptive (co-)management strategies . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The trends observed suggest an initial adjustment period followed by a recovery and stabilization phase, reflecting the adaptive management strategies and the resilience of the grazing system . The mean destock of households starts with a high value of approximately 7 livestock at the initial time step . The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, starts at approximately 90 across all households .  Total livestock healthy starts at 0 for all simulations, indicating an initial state of perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households . Over the first 20 time steps, the Gini coefficient increases gradually, suggesting a slow divergence in livestock health among households . This period could be characterized by the emergence of disparities due to varying adaptive strategies and learning capabilities .
