The average cumulative number of destocked livestock across all households in the grazing system model exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the entire simulation period. Starting from an initial value of 0, the metric steadily rises, reaching a final value of approximately 96.78 at the end of the simulation. The increase is characterized by a rapid initial rise, with a slope of 4.956 in the first 10 time steps, followed by a gradual slowing in the rate of increase. The slopes for subsequent decades of time steps are 1.466, 0.687, and so on, indicating a decelerating yet persistent upward trend. The model identifies several inflection points, suggesting subtle changes in the rate of destocking over time. Notably, change points are detected at time steps 25, 60, and 100, marking significant shifts in the trend. The data also exhibits strong oscillations with a dominant period of 49 time steps, indicating cyclical patterns in the destocking behavior. Throughout the simulation, the trend remains consistently increasing, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, which shows significant increasing trends across all time windows. This suggests a continuous and steady accumulation of destocked livestock over the simulated period.
