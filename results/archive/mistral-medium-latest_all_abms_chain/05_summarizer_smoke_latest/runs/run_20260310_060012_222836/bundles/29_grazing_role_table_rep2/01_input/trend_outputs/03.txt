The average amount of reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps. Initially, the reserve biomass starts at a value of 90,000 units. In the early stages, from time step 0 to 10, there is a significant decreasing trend, with the biomass dropping to around 84,114 units by time step 26, which is the lowest point observed. This initial decline is followed by a period of stabilization and slight recovery. From time step 33 onwards, the reserve biomass begins to increase steadily. This increasing trend continues consistently through to the end of the simulation at time step 100, where the biomass reaches its peak value of approximately 121,073 units. The overall pattern indicates an initial decline in biomass, followed by a robust and sustained recovery phase. The system shows strong oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 49 time steps, suggesting cyclical patterns in biomass levels. Key inflection points are noted around time steps 8 and 48, marking significant changes in the trend dynamics. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these trends, highlighting significant decreases early on and consistent increases in the latter part of the simulation.
