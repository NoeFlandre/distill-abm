agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a very low value, close to zero . over the first 10 time steps, there is a rapid increase in consumption, reaching approximately 1,000 units . this sharp rise indicates a significant shift in consumer behavior, likely driven by initial exposure and early adoption influenced by environmental perceptions . from time step 20 to 30, the mean quantity of whole milk consumed remains relatively stable, fluctuating slightly but generally maintaining a level around 700 units . this stability suggests that the agents have reached a new equilibrium in their consumption habits . initial differences in choice functions emerge quickly, but the system takes more time to reach a stable state with consistent deviations . the deviation starts at a value of 0.0, indicating that initially, there is no deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices . between steps 10 and 20, the rate of increase in deviation slows down, with the mean value health deviation stabilizing around 0.5 .
