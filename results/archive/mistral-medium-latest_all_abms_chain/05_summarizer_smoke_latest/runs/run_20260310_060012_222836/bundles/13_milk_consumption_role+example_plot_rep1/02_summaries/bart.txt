 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value, close to zero . This initial state reflects the low adoption of these milk types at the beginning of the simulation period in 1974 . Over the first 10 time steps, there is a rapid increase in consumption, reaching approximately 1,000 units . This sharp rise indicates a significant shift in consumer behavior .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at a low value close to zero . It then increases rapidly within the first 10 steps, reaching approximately 1,200 units . After step 25, there is a gradual increase in the mean quantity, reaching around 1,400 units by step 30 . This suggests that the agents have reached a new equilibrium in their consumption habits, with whole milk being less dominant .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at a low value near zero . The trend shows an initial rapid increase in deviation, followed by a period of stabilization, and a final slight increase, reflecting the dynamic nature of agents' decision-making processes influenced by social interactions .
