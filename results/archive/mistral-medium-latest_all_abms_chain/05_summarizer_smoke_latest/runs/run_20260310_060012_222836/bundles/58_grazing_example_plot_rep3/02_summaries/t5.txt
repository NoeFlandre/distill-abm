the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the mean household initial risk attitude is constant throughout the simulation . the majority of the values are clustered around the middle of this range . this indicates that the initial risk attitudes of households do not change . the final 30 time steps show a consistent level of mean livestock plus destock . the mean destock of households starts at a value of 0 and rapidly increases to a peak of approximately 7 within the first few time steps . this sharp rise indicates an initial period of significant livestock reduction across households, likely due to immediate resource constraints or initial conditions of the simulation . after the 35th step, there is a noticeable decline in the count of non-learning households, dropping to around 25 by the 50th step . this decline could be attributed to a shift in household strategies or external factors influencing the adoption of learning behaviors.
