The total number of animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at an initial value of 250, as set by the parameter init-prey. In the early stages of the simulation, up to approximately 500 steps, there is a noticeable decline in the total number of animals. This decline is relatively steep, indicating a high rate of reduction in the animal population. By around 500 steps, the total number of animals has decreased to roughly 50, reflecting an 80% reduction from the initial population.\n\nFollowing this initial steep decline, the rate of decrease in the animal population begins to slow down. Between 500 and 1000 steps, the population continues to decline but at a more gradual pace. By 1000 steps, the total number of animals is further reduced to around 10. This phase suggests that while the population is still decreasing, the rate of decline has lessened compared to the initial phase.\n\nBeyond 1000 steps, the decline in the animal population continues to slow down even more. The population approaches near-zero values as the simulation progresses towards 2000 steps. This trend indicates that the animal population is nearing extinction, with very few animals remaining in the environment.\n\nFor decision makers, these trends highlight the significant impact of the initial conditions and parameters on the animal population dynamics. The steep initial decline suggests that early interventions or adjustments to parameters such as hunting pressure, food availability, or mortality rates could be crucial in influencing the trajectory of the animal population. The gradual slowdown in the rate of decline after the initial steep drop indicates that while the population is still decreasing, the factors affecting the population might be interacting in a way that mitigates the rate of decline over time. Understanding these dynamics can help in designing strategies to manage and conserve animal populations in similar ecological settings.
