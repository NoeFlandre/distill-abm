 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The temporal units are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration of the simulation .  The number of deaths in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period . Understanding these patterns can help in managing wildlife populations and predicting future trends in animal demographics . For decision-makers, these trends highlight critical periods where intervention might be necessary to prevent high mortality rates .  The total number of animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at an initial value of 250, as set by the parameter init-prey . In the early stages of the simulation, up to approximately 500 steps, there is a noticeable decline in the animal population . The steep decline in energy levels suggests that current hunting pressures and environmental conditions may not be sustainable for the long-term survival of male animals .  Initially, the total energy is approximately 25,000 units . Within the first 500 steps, there is a steep decrease in total energy . This trend suggests a rapid depletion of energy resources among the prey animals . For decision-makers, this indicates that under the given parameters, the prey population's energy resources are quickly exhausted .
