The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid decline over time. Initially, the total energy is approximately 25,000 units. Within the first 500 steps, there is a steep decrease in total energy, dropping to around 5,000 units. This sharp decline continues more gradually until about 1,000 steps, where the total energy stabilizes near zero. Beyond 1,000 steps, the total energy remains consistently low, approaching zero and maintaining this level for the remainder of the simulation. This trend suggests a rapid depletion of energy resources among the prey animals, likely due to a combination of hunting pressure, environmental factors, and internal dynamics such as mortality and movement costs. For decision-makers, this indicates that under the given parameters, the prey population's energy resources are quickly exhausted, which could lead to extinction or severe population decline. This insight may prompt further investigation into the sustainability of current hunting practices and environmental conditions.
