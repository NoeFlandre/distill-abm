 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value, close to zero . Initially, the number of whole milk choices is low, reflecting the initial conditions where agents might be exploring different options or have not yet formed strong habits . Around the 5th step, there is a rapid increase in the consumption . This sharp rise continues until approximately the 10th time step, where the average consumption reaches around 1,200 units .
