agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a very low value, close to zero . this initial phase persists for a brief period, indicating that initially, the majority of agents predominantly consume whole milk . after this rapid increase, the consumption stabilizes and fluctuates slightly around the 1,200 to 1,300 unit range until about the 25th time step . between time steps 10 and 20, the decline continues but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 750 units . this period indicates a phase of adjustment where agents are gradually adapting to new habits or preferences . the mean quantity of whole milk consumed remains relatively stable, fluctuating slightly but generally maintaining a level around 500 units. the mean value health deviation starts at a value close to zero, indicating that initially, the agents' health perceptions are closely aligned with their milk choices . between steps 10 and 15, the rate of increase slows down . this gradual increase suggests a more stable phase where agents are gradually aligning their perceptions with their consumption habits .
