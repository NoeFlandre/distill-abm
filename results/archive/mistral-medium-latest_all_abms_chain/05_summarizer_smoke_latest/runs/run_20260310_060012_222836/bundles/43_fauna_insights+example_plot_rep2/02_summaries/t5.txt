the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) has 29 parameters that can be adjusted to test various hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . it considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism . the total energy of foragers begins at 0 and remains relatively stable for the first 500 time steps, indicating a period of equilibrium where the energy gained from hunting balances the energy expended . this stability suggests that the initial conditions of the environment provide sufficient resources to maintain their energy levels . the steady decline in energy levels indicates that the forager is expending more energy than they are gaining from hunting, leading to a gradual depletion of their energy reserves . at around 800 time steps, there is a sudden increase in the number of births, reaching approximately 8 births . this sharp rise suggests that the population has reached a stage where reproductive conditions are favorable, possibly due to sufficient food availability and stable environmental conditions . after 1200 time steps there is another significant increase, reaching up to 10 births by around 1500 time steps . the second rise could be due to further improvements in environmental conditions or a reduction in predation pressure . the rapid initial decline and subsequent stabilization near zero suggest that without intervention, the animal population could face extinction . this information can guide conservation efforts and policy-making to mitigate the factors leading to such a decline . the total number of animals in the simulation starts at 250 and declines rapidly over the first 1000 steps . around the 500th time step, there is a sharp decline in the percentage of green patches . this decline could be attributed to an increase in grazing pressure as the ungulate population grows or as environmental conditions change, reducing the grass regrowth rate .
