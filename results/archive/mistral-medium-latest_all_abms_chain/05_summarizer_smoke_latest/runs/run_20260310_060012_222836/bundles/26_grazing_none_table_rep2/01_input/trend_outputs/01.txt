The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration of the model run. The value was consistently 0.0074 from the beginning to the end of the 100 timesteps. There were no fluctuations or trends observed in the data, as indicated by the statistical analysis showing no significant trends in any of the rolling Mann-Kendall tests. The lack of variance and the consistent values at all key time steps suggest a stable and unchanging initial risk attitude among the social learning agents. This stability implies that, under the given model parameters and conditions, the agents' disposition to deviate from the recommended number of livestock did not vary over time.
