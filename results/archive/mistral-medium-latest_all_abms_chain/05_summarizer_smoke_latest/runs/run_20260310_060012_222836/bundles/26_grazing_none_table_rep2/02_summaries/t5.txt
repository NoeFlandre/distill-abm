the agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model's context involves smallholder farmers making decisions about livestock placement on their privately-owned pastures based on observations of pasture states and neighbor behaviors . the average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration of the model run . there were no fluctuations or trends observed in the data, as indicated by the statistical analysis showing no significant trends in the rolling Mann-Kendall tests . the model exhibits a consistent increasing trend in livestock numbers . by the end of the simulation at timestep 100, the average total number of livestock across all households is 47.5472 . there is evidence of strong oscillations in certain intervals . the model identifies change points at time steps 25, 60, and 100, marking notable shifts in the trend's behavior . the dominant period of oscillation is around 49 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern .
