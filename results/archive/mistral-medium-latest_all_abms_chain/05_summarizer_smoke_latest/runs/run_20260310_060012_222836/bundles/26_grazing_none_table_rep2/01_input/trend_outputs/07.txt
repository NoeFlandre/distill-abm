The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the entire simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of -0.0000, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock. Over time, the inequality gradually increases, reaching a value of 0.0726 at the final time step. Throughout the simulation, the Gini coefficient shows a steady rise, with no detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a continuous and uniform increase in inequality. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across all time windows, with significant positive trends and increasing slopes. Change points are detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100, indicating notable shifts in the rate of increase in inequality. Additionally, there is evidence of strong oscillations towards the latter part of the simulation, with a dominant period of 49 time steps. These oscillations suggest periodic fluctuations in the rate of change of inequality, although the overall trend remains upward.
