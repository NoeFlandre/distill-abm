 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  Simulation of agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents over the course of 100 timesteps . No significant trends in any of the rolling Mann-Kendall tests .  The average number of livestock destocked across all households in the simulation begins at zero and exhibits notable fluctuations in the early time steps . The number of households engaging in social learning exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period . The Gini coefficient shows a steady rise, with no detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a continuous and uniform increase in inequality .  The number of E-RO households starts at 20 and experiences a slight increase, peaking at 21.1 at time step 8 . The dominant period of oscillation is around 49 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in the data . Key change points are identified at time steps 20 and 45 .
