 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes . The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at 0 for all simulations, indicating an initially equal distribution of livestock among households . Initially, the mean destocking rate of households starts at a high value, with some households destocking up to 7 livestock . By step 20, the number of households engaging in social learning ranges widely from approximately 10 to 40 households . This trend implies that households achieve a more sustainable balance in managing their livestock .  Trend suggests majority of destocking decisions occur early in the timeline, with households adjusting their livestock numbers significantly at the beginning and then maintaining a more stable livestock population as the simulation progresses . For decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of understanding the factors that influence the adoption and persistence of non-learning strategies .
