 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters, and the model provides summary statistics such as the number of prey items taken, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and animals in the environment .  The total number of animals in the simulation starts at 250, which is the initial number of prey set in the model . Over the first 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of male animals, dropping to around 60 . This sharp decrease could be attributed to the combined effects of hunting pressure from human hunters and natural mortality factors . The number of births fluctuates but generally trends upwards .  The total energy of male animals begins at approximately 12,000 units . It declines rapidly over the first 500 time steps, reaching around 2,000 . Beyond 1,000 steps, the total energy gradually approaches zero, suggesting a continued decline in the male animal population . The variability among simulation runs increases after the initial 100 steps .
