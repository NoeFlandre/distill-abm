agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . households with high risk attitudes remain around 0.7 to 0.8, those with moderate risk attitudes stay around 0.4 to 0.6, and those with low or negative risk attitudes maintain values around 0.2 or lower . trends indicate that household risk attitudes can significantly diverge over time based on adaptive learning and interactions . the mean destock of households starts at a high value of approximately 7 livestock per household . this initial high value indicates a significant immediate response to initial conditions, where households may be adjusting to the starting parameters of the simulation . over the first 20 steps, there is a rapid decline in the mean . destock, dropping to around 2 livestock . per household, this sharp decline suggests that households are quickly adapting to the conditions . over 40 time steps, the destocking rate continues to rise steadily but at a slower pace, reaching approximately 120 livestock by 100 time steps . this long-term trend suggests a sustained response to chronic conditions, which could be due to persistent environmental factors .
