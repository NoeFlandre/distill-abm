The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy of foragers starts at a value of 2000 units. This value rapidly declines, showing a steep negative slope from the beginning. By around 231 ticks, the energy level drops to approximately -11,070 units, indicating a rapid depletion of energy reserves among the foragers. This trend continues with varying slopes but remains consistently negative. Around 464 ticks, the energy level further decreases to about -8,823 units, and by 695 ticks, it drops to approximately -15,729 units. The decline persists, reaching around -18,536 units by 927 ticks and approximately -19,585 units by 1159 ticks. The energy levels continue to decrease, hitting around -20,011 units by 1391 ticks and maintaining a similar rate of decline thereafter. By the end of the simulation at 2310 ticks, the total energy of foragers reaches its lowest point at approximately -38,233 units. The consistent and significant decreasing trend in the total energy of foragers suggests that the energy expenditure due to hunting and movement costs outweighs the energy gained from consuming prey. This implies that the foragers are not able to sustain their energy levels over time, leading to a continuous decline. For decision-makers, this trend indicates that the current parameters and dynamics of the model may not support a sustainable energy balance for the foragers, potentially leading to their eventual depletion if the simulation were to continue. Adjustments to parameters such as food value from prey, movement costs, or initial energy reserves might be necessary to achieve a more stable or sustainable energy balance for the foragers.
