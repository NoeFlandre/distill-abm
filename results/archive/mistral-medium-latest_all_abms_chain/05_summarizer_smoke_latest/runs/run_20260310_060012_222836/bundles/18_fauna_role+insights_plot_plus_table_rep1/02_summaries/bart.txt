 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters, and the model provides summary statistics such as the number of prey items taken, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, the amount of animals in the environment over time .  Initial rapid increase suggests a high initial hunting pressure or vulnerability of female prey, which stabilizes as the system reaches an equilibrium . For decision-makers, these trends highlight critical periods in the simulation where reproductive activity peaks and declines . Understanding these patterns can inform strategies related to conservation efforts, hunting regulations, and environmental management to ensure the sustainability of the prey population .  The number of female prey animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend over time . For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, the male animal population is not sustainable and tends towards extinction .  The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time . For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters and conditions of the MHPM, the population of female prey animals experiences a continuous and significant reduction in total energy .
