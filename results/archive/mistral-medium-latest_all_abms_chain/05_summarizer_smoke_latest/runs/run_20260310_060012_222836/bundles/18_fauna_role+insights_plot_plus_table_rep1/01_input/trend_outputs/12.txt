The total energy of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This high initial energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy of female animals drops to approximately 2,000 units, indicating a significant reduction. This downward trend continues, albeit at a slower rate, with the energy levels gradually approaching zero. By the end of the simulation period, the total energy of female animals reaches zero, indicating complete depletion. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various segments of the time series. The presence of multiple change points and inflection indices further supports the observation of a consistent decline in energy levels. For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters and conditions of the MHPM, the population of female prey animals experiences a continuous and significant reduction in total energy, ultimately leading to their complete depletion. This insight could inform strategies related to conservation efforts, hunting regulations, and environmental management to mitigate such outcomes in real-world scenarios.
