The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the grazing system model remains constant throughout the simulation period. The value is consistently 0.0074 from the beginning to the end of the 100 time steps. There is no variation or trend observed in the data, as indicated by the statistical measures such as mean, median, standard deviation, and variance, all of which reflect this stability. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms the absence of any significant trends across different segments of the time series. Additionally, the oscillation summary suggests some periodic behavior, but the prominence values indicate that these oscillations are negligible. For decision makers, this implies that the initial risk attitude of social learning agents does not change over time, suggesting a stable disposition towards risk among these agents throughout the simulation. This stability could be a crucial factor in understanding the long-term behavior and decision-making processes of households in the grazing system.
