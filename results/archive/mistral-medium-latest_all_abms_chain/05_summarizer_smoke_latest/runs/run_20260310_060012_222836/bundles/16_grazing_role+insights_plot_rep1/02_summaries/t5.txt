agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the mean reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . initial decline in biomass suggests a period where the biomass is being utilized more rapidly than it is being replenished . this recovery starts around step 20 and continues until approximately step 60 . the Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at 0 for all simulations, indicating an initial equal distribution of livestock among households . by step 40, the inequality continues to rise, with values spreading further apart, typically between 0.030 and 0.060 . this trend suggests that certain households accumulate more livestock, potentially leading to disparities in wealth and resources . tends to favor learning strategies over time, leading to a more balanced distribution of behavioral types among households . this indicates the importance of promoting adaptive strategies to enhance resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems .
