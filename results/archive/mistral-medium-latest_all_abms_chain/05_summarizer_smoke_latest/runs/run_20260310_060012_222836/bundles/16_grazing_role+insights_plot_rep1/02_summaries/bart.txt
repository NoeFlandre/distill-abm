 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . The extension focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, which are central to adaptive (co-)management strategies . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The average green biomass across all patches exhibits a consistent trend across all simulations . Initial decline in biomass highlights the importance of monitoring and managing grazing activities to prevent overutilization of resources . This information can guide policies and practices aimed at maintaining the long-term health and productivity of the grazing system .  Simulations show a variety of trends in the adoption of social learning among households over 100 time steps . The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households exhibits a rapid increase during the initial time steps, followed by a gradual leveling off . For decision-makers, these trends suggest that the adoption . of social . learning practices can vary widely based on initial conditions and parameters . Understanding the factors driving early destocking can help in designing policies that promote sustainable livestock management .
