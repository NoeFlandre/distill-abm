 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at 45,000 units . The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households starts at 90 . The overall trend indicates a recovery and growth phase in the biomass after the initial depletion .  The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households begins at zero and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . The number of households following the E-RO strategy, which represents non-learning agents, begins at 20.1 . The trend indicates a strong and steady adoption of social learning behaviors within the community over time .
