agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the dominant period of oscillation is around 49 time steps . the average amount of reserve biomass available across all patches begins at 90,000 units . this initial decrease could be attributed to immediate consumption and utilization of biomass by livestock as households begin their grazing activities . inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households starts at 0.0000, indicating perfect equality . over the course of the simulation, the Gini coefficient exhibits a consistent increasing trend, reaching a value of 0.0726 at the final time step . the trend indicates a strong and steady adoption of social learning behaviors within the community .
