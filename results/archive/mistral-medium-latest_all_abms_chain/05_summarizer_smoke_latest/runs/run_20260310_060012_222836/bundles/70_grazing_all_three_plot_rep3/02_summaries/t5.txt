agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . by time step 40, most households' risk attitudes converge to a range between 0.2 and 0.4 . this convergence suggests a collective adaptation towards a moderate risk attitude . for decision makers, the initial rapid divergence highlights the importance of early interventions to guide household risk attitudes towards sustainable practices . the initial rapid decline underscores the need for robust initial strategies to manage livestock during early periods of environmental or economic stress . the subsequent stabilization and gradual increase suggest that long-term planning and adaptive management can lead to recovery and growth in livestock numbers . the destocking continues at a steady but slower pace, with the mean destock total reaching approximately 100 livestock by 100 steps . the variance among simulation runs increases slightly over time, indicating that households are continuously adapting their livestock management strategies in response to ongoing conditions . for decision makers, this trend highlights the importance of initial conditions and the potential for rapid shifts in household strategies .
