The total energy of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of approximately 12,000 units. This initial high energy level is maintained for a brief period before a rapid decline begins. By around 500 steps, the total energy of male animals drops significantly to about 2,000 units. This sharp decrease continues more gradually until approximately 1,000 steps, where the energy levels stabilize around 0 units. Beyond 1,000 steps, the total energy of male animals remains consistently low, approaching zero and maintaining this level through to the end of the simulation at 2,000 steps. This trend indicates a rapid initial reduction in the energy levels of male animals followed by a stabilization at minimal energy levels.
