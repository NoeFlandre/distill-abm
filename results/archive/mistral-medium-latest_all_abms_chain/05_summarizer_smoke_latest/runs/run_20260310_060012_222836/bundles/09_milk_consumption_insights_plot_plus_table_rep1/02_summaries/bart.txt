 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It focuses on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined shows a consistent and linear increasing trend over the 33 steps, starting from 0 and reaching 32,000 at the final step . For decision makers, this trend suggests a growing preference for skimmed or semi-skimmed milk over time, influenced by the model's parameters such as social susceptibility, habit formation, and network interactions .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk shows a consistent increasing trend over the 33 steps of the simulation . For decision makers, these trends suggest that initial adoption of healthier milk options can be rapid given the right conditions, but sustained and increased consumption may require ongoing reinforcement .
