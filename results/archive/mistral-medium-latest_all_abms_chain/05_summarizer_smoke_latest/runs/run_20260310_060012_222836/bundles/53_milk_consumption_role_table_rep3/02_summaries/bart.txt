 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent over time . The data also exhibits some oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 15 time steps, indicating periodic fluctuations around the overall downward trend .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent and steady increase in the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined over the time period from 1974 to 2005 . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents in the simulation exhibits a clear decreasing trend over time . Overall, the simulation demonstrates a clear and significant shift in consumer preference towards skimmed or semi-skimmed milk .  The simulation shows an initial rapid adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, followed by a stabilization period with minor fluctuations, and a slight increase towards the end . The dominant period of oscillation is 15.00, with several strong oscillation indices suggesting periodic fluctuations in consumption levels during the stabilization phase .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early time steps, reaching a value of approximately 13.4756 . The data shows several peaks and valleys, suggesting minor oscillations around the stabilized mean deviation . Inflection points identified at time steps 16, 19, 24, 26, 28 and 31 indicate changes in the trend direction .
