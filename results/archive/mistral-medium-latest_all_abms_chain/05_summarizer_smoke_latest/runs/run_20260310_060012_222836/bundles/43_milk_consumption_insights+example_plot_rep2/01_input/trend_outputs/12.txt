After this rapid increase, the deviation stabilizes around a value of 13 from step 10 onwards, indicating a steady-state where the agents' choices and environmental values reach a form of equilibrium. The low variance across simulation runs suggests that the model's behavior is consistent and predictable after the initial transient phase.
