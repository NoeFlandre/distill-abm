 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . Model explores how perception, habits, and social influence affect individual decision-making processes regarding milk choices . Model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others .  The number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases linearly over the 30 steps . At each step, the total number of choices made increases by approximately 1,000, reaching 31,000 choices . For decision makers, this trend suggests that the adoption of whole milk follows a predictable pattern, which could be useful for forecasting and planning purposes .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 . Initially, habits related to milk consumption do not change significantly . Early interventions may have a higher impact due to the rapid initial habit formation phase . For decision makers, this trend implies that interventions aimed at changing milk consumption habits need to be robust and sustained .
