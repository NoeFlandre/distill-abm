 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on habits, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  Simulation results indicate a consistent and significant increasing trend in the total number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk over the entire period from 1974 to 2005 . Initially, the adoption of these milk types starts at zero, reflecting the initial conditions of the model where agents presumably began with a preference for whole milk . The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a persistent increasing trend throughout the simulation .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time . The data also exhibits oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 15 time steps, indicating periodic fluctuations in the consumption patterns .  The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005 . Initially, the deviation starts at 0.0000 and gradually rises to 0.7251 by the end of the simulation .
