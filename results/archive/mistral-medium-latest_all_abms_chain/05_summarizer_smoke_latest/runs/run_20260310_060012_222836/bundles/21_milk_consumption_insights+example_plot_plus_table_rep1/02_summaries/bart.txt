 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It focuses on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The overall trend indicates a strong and sustained shift towards the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the simulation period . For decision makers, this trend suggests that interventions aimed at promoting healthier milk choices can have a substantial impact over time .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 5.29 units at the beginning of the simulation period . The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of understanding consumer behavior shifts and the impact of health perceptions on product adoption .  The mean choice function deviation starts at 0.0000 and increases rapidly in the initial steps, reaching approximately 0.6 by step 5 . This rapid increase indicates a quick divergence in the choice function scores among agents as they evaluate and adjust their milk consumption choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . The trend shows a consistent increase in deviation throughout the simulation, with no detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady divergence in choice behaviors .
