The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction. It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history. The model allows users to manipulate parameters to observe how different conditions interact to influence extinction or equilibrium outcomes. The landscape is a simplified grassland environment with patches of grass, ungulates, and human hunters. The model uses 'ticks' as temporal units, which can be scaled to any temporal granularity.
