The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear trend in the population dynamics of male prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 male prey animals. The population exhibits a consistent decreasing trend throughout the simulation, ultimately reaching zero by the end of the observed period. The mean number of male prey animals across the simulation is approximately 21, with a median of about 1.35, indicating a skewed distribution with many low values as the population declines. The standard deviation is 36.04, reflecting significant variability in the population size at different time steps. The population experiences several peaks and valleys, particularly notable around the early time steps such as 730, 709, and 803, where temporary increases and decreases in population are observed. However, these fluctuations are relatively minor compared to the overall downward trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant decreasing trends in the early phases of the simulation, with periods of no significant trend observed later on, suggesting phases of stabilization before the population continues to decline. Change points are identified at several key time steps, including 125, 250, 375, 500, 625, 875, and 2310, marking significant shifts in the rate of population change. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 time steps, indicating cyclical patterns in the population dynamics, though these oscillations are overshadowed by the overall decline. The simulation ultimately shows a complete extinction of male prey animals by the final time step.
