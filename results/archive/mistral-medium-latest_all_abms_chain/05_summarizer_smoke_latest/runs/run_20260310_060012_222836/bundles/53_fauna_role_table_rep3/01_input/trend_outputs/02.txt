The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, with small peaks and valleys around the initial value. For instance, there is a slight peak at around 1989.925 units and a small valley at approximately 1987.65 units. However, these initial variations are minimal compared to the overall trend. As the simulation progresses, the energy levels begin to decline more steeply. By around the 394th tick, the energy level drops to approximately -2005.95 units, marking a notable decrease from the starting point. This downward trend continues with increasing severity. By the 1594th tick, the energy level reaches around -23807 units, and by the 1826th tick, it further declines to about -28403 units. The most significant drop is observed towards the end of the simulation, with the energy level plummeting to -38233 units by the 2310th tick. The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decrease, with all rolling Mann-Kendall tests indicating significant decreasing trends across different segments of the simulation. The change points identified at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 further emphasize the continuous decline in energy levels. The oscillation summary indicates a dominant period of 63 ticks, suggesting some periodic fluctuations within the overall decreasing trend, but these oscillations do not alter the primary downward trajectory. The energy levels of the forager bands are consistently decreasing, reflecting a continuous decline in their energy status influenced by hunting success and movement costs throughout the simulation.
