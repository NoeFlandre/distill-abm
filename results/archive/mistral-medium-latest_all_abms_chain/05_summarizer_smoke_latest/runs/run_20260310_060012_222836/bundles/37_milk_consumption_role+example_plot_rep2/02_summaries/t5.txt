agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at a very low value, close to zero . this initial state reflects the low adoption of these milk types in 1974 . over the first 10 time steps, there is a rapid increase in consumption, reaching approximately 1,000 units . between time steps 10 and 20, the decline continues but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 800 units . this period indicates a phase of adjustment where agents are gradually adapting to new preferences and social influences . the simulation ends with the mean whole milk quantity slightly below 700 units, indicating a sustained reduction in whole milk consumption . the deviation continues to increase gradually, eventually approaching a value close to 0.7 . this gradual increase indicates ongoing but slower adjustments in agents' choices, possibly due to continuous social interactions and habit formations .
