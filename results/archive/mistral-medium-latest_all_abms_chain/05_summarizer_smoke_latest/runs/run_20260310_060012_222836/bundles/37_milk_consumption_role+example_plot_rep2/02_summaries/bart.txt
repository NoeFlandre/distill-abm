 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . Model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice . The ABM is designed to replicate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value, close to zero . Over the first 10 time steps, there is a rapid increase in consumption, reaching approximately 1,000 units . By around the 15th time step, the consumption stabilizes at around 1,200 units . The overall trend demonstrates a clear shift from whole milk to skimmed milk .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at a low value near zero . It then increases rapidly over the first 10 steps, reaching approximately 1,200 units . After step 25, there is a noticeable increase in the mean quantity, rising to around 1,400 units by step 30 . The simulation ends with the mean whole milk quantity slightly below 700 units .  The mean value environmental deviation stabilizes over time, reflecting a balance between individual preferences and social influences in the agents' decision-making processes . The low variance across simulation runs suggests that the model's behavior is consistent . The simulation ends with the mean value health deviation stabilizing around 0.7 .
