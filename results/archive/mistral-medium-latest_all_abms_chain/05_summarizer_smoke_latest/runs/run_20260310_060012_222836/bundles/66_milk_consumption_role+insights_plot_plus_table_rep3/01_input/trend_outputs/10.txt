This rapid increase suggests a significant diversification in the agents' choice functions early in the simulation period.

Following this initial rapid increase, the rate of deviation growth slows down, but the deviation continues to rise steadily. This steady increase indicates a continuous but slower diversification in the agents' choice functions as the simulation progresses.

Towards the end of the simulation, the deviation stabilizes, reaching a value of 0.7508 by step 32. This stabilization suggests that the agents' choice functions have diversified to a certain extent and then plateaued, indicating a balance in the diversity of milk consumption choices among the agents.

The statistical analysis reveals that the trend of increasing deviation is consistent and significant throughout the simulation, with no detected peaks or valleys, indicating a smooth and continuous increase in deviation. The inflection points at steps 7, 12, and 28 suggest changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward.

For decision-makers, these trends imply that initial interventions or influences have a substantial impact on diversifying consumer choices.
