 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period . Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . This is followed by a rapid increase in consumption, reaching around 1220.21 units by the 11th step . The rapid increase underscores the impact of initial marketing efforts or social influences in driving adoption . For decision-makers, the steady increase in whole milk choices highlights the enduring preference  The simulation of the agent-based model reveals a clear and consistent trend in the consumption of whole milk over time . The data shows no peaks, valleys, or inflection points, suggesting a steady and predictable increase in the total number of choices made by agents . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing more effective public health campaigns and policy measures to promote healthier dietary choices . This insight could be valuable for understanding consumer behavior patterns and planning marketing or intervention strategies accordingly .
