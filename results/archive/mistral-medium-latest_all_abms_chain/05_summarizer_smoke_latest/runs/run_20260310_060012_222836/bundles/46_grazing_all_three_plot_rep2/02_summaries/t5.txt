agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the mean reserve biomass in the grazing system starts at approximately 90,000 units . this initial rise could be attributed to the initial growth phase of vegetation, where the biomass accumulates due to favorable conditions at the start of the simulation . over the first 20 time steps, there is a rapid decline in the mean destocking of households, dropping to around 2 . this sharp decrease indicates that households are quickly adapting to the available resources . the count of non-learning households starts with a high variability . this initial variability suggests a diverse set of strategies being employed by households at the beginning of the simulation . the majority settle into a range between 5 and 30 households, indicating a consolidation of strategies among the households .
