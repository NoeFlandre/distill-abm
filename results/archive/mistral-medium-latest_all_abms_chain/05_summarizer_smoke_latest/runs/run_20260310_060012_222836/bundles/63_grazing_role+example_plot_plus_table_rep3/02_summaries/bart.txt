 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . It simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households, starts at 90.9 per cent . The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches starts at approximately 45,000 units . The overall trend indicates an initial depletion followed by a robust recovery phase, leading to a higher biomass level than the starting point .  The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at 0.0, indicating perfect equality . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 time steps, reaching approximately 30 households . The overall trend shows a continuous increase in inequality in livestock health distribution among households .
