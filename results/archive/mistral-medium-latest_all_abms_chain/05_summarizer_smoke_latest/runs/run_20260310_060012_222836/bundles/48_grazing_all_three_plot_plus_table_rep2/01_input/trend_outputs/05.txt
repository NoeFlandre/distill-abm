This sharp decrease could be attributed to initial adjustments in household management strategies or environmental factors impacting livestock survival rates. Following this decline, the livestock numbers begin to recover gradually. From time step 44 onwards, there is a consistent, albeit slow, increase in livestock numbers. This trend continues steadily through to the end of the simulation at time step 100, where the average livestock number stabilizes around 47.55. The initial rapid decline followed by a gradual recovery suggests that households may initially struggle with managing their livestock but eventually adapt and improve their strategies over time. For decision makers, this indicates the importance of supporting households during initial periods of adjustment to prevent drastic losses and to promote long-term stability and growth in livestock numbers.
