agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the mean reserve biomass in the grazing system starts at approximately 90,000 units . Initially, there is a slight increase, reaching around 90,522 units by the third time step . after this low point, the biomass has increased significantly and continues to rise, albeit at a slower rate, for the remainder of the simulation . by the 27th time step, the destocking rate stabilizes around 0.5 livestock . there is no significant trend observed between time steps 33-43 and 55-65, suggesting periods of stability . the initial sharp increase highlights the importance of preparing for significant initial adjustments in livestock management . the number of households following the E-RO strategy starts at 20 . the count initially increases slightly, reaching a peak of approximately 21.1 at around 8 time steps . this initial rise could be attributed to the initial conditions and setup of the simulation .
