agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . from time step 60 to 100, the trends remain relatively constant with minimal variations . the mean reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . by step 60, the mean green biomass reaches around 110,000 units . by the 100th step, the Gini coefficient stabilizes around 0.090, indicating a significant increase in inequality over time . the trend suggests that, as the simulation progresses, certain households accumulate more healthy livestock while others accumulate less, leading to a more unequal distribution .
