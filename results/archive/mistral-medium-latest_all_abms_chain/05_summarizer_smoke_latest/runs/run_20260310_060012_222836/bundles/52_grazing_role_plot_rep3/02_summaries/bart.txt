 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . The extension focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The mean reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . The metric representing the average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, exhibits a consistent upward trend over the 100 steps of the simulation . The overall trend suggests an initial dip in biomass followed by a robust recovery and growth phase .  The data from repeated simulations of the agent-based model show distinct patterns in the average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households over 100 time steps . By step 20, the number of households engaging in social learning ranges widely across simulations, from as low as approximately 10 households to as high as around 40 households .
