agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . the total number of whole milk choices starts at zero and increases steadily over the 30 steps . this linear growth suggests a consistent and predictable pattern in the adoption behavior . the initial steps show a gradual increase, indicating that agents are slowly beginning to incorporate whole milk into their choices . rapid initial decline suggests that interventions or campaigns aimed at reducing whole milk consumption can be highly effective in the short term . however, the gradual stabilization indicates that complete replacement of whole milk may be challenging . skimmed milk is a gradual process that accelerates once a critical mass of agents begins to change their habits . the rapid increase phase highlights the importance of social influence and positive evaluations in driving widespread adoption .
