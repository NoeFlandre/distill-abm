The total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero and increases gradually over the first 5 steps. This initial slow increase could be attributed to the initial adoption phase where a small number of agents begin to consider and switch to skimmed or semi-skimmed milk. The rate of increase accelerates significantly between steps 5 and 15, indicating a rapid adoption phase where more agents are influenced by social interactions, perceived health benefits, and environmental considerations. By step 15, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 5,000. This rapid increase suggests a tipping point where the social influence and habit formation mechanisms gain momentum, leading to a widespread shift in milk preferences. From step 15 to step 30, the growth continues but at a slower, more linear rate, eventually reaching around 25,000 choices. This phase indicates a maturation period where the majority of agents have already adopted the new milk types, and the remaining increases are due to the final holdouts or new agents entering the system. The trend shows low variance across simulation runs, suggesting a consistent pattern of adoption influenced by the model's parameters. For decision makers, this trend implies that interventions aimed at promoting healthier milk choices can expect a gradual initial uptake followed by a rapid adoption phase, eventually leading to a widespread change in consumption habits. Understanding these phases can help in planning and resource allocation for public health campaigns.
