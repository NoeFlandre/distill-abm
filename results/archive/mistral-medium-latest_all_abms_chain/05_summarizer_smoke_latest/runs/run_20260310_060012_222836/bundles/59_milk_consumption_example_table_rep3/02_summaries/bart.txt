 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It focuses on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the entire simulation period . The trend shows a linear growth pattern, with a steady increase of 1,000 choices per time step, suggesting a predictable and systematic decision-making process influenced by the model's parameters . Towards the end of the simulation, the consumption of whole milk continues to decrease, ultimately reaching a low of approximately 253.10 units .  The overall trend demonstrates a significant shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the 32-year period, driven by health perceptions and social dynamics . The data shows a steady increase in the influence of habit on the choice of milk over time .  The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a significant increasing trend in the initial time steps . Inflection points, where the trend changes direction, are noted at time steps 16, 19, 24, 26, 28, and 31 . The dominant oscillation period is 15.00, indicating a cyclical pattern in the deviations over time . The simulation ends with the mean deviation near 13.4186 .
