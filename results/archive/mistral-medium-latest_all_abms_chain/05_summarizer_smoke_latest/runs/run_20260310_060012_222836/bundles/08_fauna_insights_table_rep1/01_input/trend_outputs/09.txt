The number of female prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulation exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 and gradually declines to zero by the end of the simulation period. The decline is marked by several phases of varying rates of decrease, with notable change points around time steps 475, 600, and 875, where the rate of decline shifts. Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and peaks, such as at time steps 730, 710, and 803, but these do not alter the overall downward trajectory. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms the significant decreasing trend in multiple intervals, with only brief periods of slight increase or stability. The dominant period of oscillation is around 63 time steps, indicating some regularity in the minor fluctuations. For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the female prey population is not sustainable and heads towards extinction. This insight could be crucial for understanding the dynamics of megaherbivore populations under similar simulated conditions and for evaluating the impact of various parameters on population sustainability.
