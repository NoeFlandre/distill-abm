The combined total energy available in all prey animals within the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulation exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This value rapidly declines in the early stages of the simulation, with significant drops observed within the first few hundred ticks. For instance, notable peaks and valleys are observed around ticks 385 to 440, where energy values fluctuate between approximately 3,700 and 4,100 units. Despite these fluctuations, the overall trend remains a steady decline. By the midpoint of the simulation, the energy levels have significantly reduced, with values around 1,083 units at tick 730. The decreasing trend continues, albeit at a slower rate, with occasional minor increases and decreases. Towards the end of the simulation, the total energy available in prey animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy resources within the prey population. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend, with multiple significant periods of decline identified through the rolling Mann-Kendall test. The implications for decision-makers suggest that under the given parameters, the prey population's energy resources are consistently depleted over time, which could inform strategies related to conservation efforts, hunting regulations, and environmental management to prevent such depletion in real-world scenarios.
