the megaherbivore hunting pressure model ( mhpm ) is an agent - based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores. the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters.  the model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix. the number of human hunter - gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation period. the cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands starts at 0 and remains constant for a brief period. by around 1000 steps, the cumulative number of postpartum females killed stabilizes, fluctuating slightly around 120. between 1000 and 1500 time steps, the number of births fluctuates but generally trends upwards, reaching around 10 births by 1500 time steps. around the 100th time step, the number of deaths begins to increase rapidly, reaching approximately 150 deaths by the 500th time step. beyond 1000 time steps, the number of male animals continues to decrease gradually, eventually approaching zero by around 2000 time steps. the simulation ends with the number of female animals nearing zero, indicating a potential extinction event or a severe reduction in the population. this high initial value indicates a healthy and abundant population of male prey at the beginning of the simulation. by around 1, 500 steps, the total energy of male animals approaches zero, indicating that the population has been significantly depleted. following this initial drop, the total energy continues to decrease but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 1, 000 units by 1, 000 steps. around time step 200, there is a sharp decline in the percentage of green patches, dropping to approximately 26 % by time step 500.
