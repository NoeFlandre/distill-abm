 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The temporal units are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters, and the model provides summary statistics such as the number of animals in the model's environment .  The number of deaths among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains low for the first 100 time steps . After 1500 time steps, the number of births stabilizes and remains constant at around 10 births until the end of the simulation . This stabilization suggests that the population has reached an equilibrium, where births are balanced by deaths, and the population size remains relatively constant .  The total energy of male animals begins at approximately 12,000 units at the beginning of the simulation . The energy levels remain relatively stable for a brief period, suggesting that the initial conditions provide sufficient resources for the male animals to maintain their energy levels . By around 1,500 steps, the energy levels of male prey are near zero, indicating that the male population has been completely eradicated .
