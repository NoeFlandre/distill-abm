 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model, which simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The average reserve biomass in the grazing system shows distinct trends over the simulated time steps . For decision makers, these trends imply that initial deviations from recommended livestock numbers can be significant but tend to correct over time . The overall positive trend in the latter part of the simulation suggests that long-term sustainability is achievable with proper management practices .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly in the initial steps . By step 7, there is a noticeable increase, and by step 28, the number of social learning has risen significantly . The trend continues to rise steeply until around step 40, where the rate of increase starts to slow down . Inflection points and change points indicate key transitions in rate of adoption .
