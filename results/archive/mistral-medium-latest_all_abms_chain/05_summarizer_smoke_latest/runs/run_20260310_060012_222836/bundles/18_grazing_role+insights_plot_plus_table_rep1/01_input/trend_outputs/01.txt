The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation period, with a value of approximately 0.0074. This indicates that there is no change in the average initial disposition of households to deviate from the recommended number of livestock over time. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, showing no significant trends or variations in the data. The lack of peaks, valleys, and significant inflection points further supports the constancy of the risk attitude. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results consistently indicate no trend across different time segments of the simulation. This stability in risk attitude suggests that, under the given model parameters and conditions, households maintain a consistent approach to risk throughout the simulation period. For decision-makers, this implies that interventions or changes in the system may be necessary to influence household risk attitudes, as the current setup does not lead to any adaptive changes in risk behavior over time.
