 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The mean whole milk quantity consumed by agents starts at approximately 2700 units at the initial time step . There is a sharp decline in the quantity of whole milk consumed within the first 10 steps, dropping to around 1000 units . This rapid decrease indicates a significant shift in consumption patterns early in the simulation . The mean habit factor for whole milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the time steps observed .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero . It then rapidly increases, reaching a value of approximately 12 by the 5th step . After step 25, there is a slight upward trend, and by step 30, the deviation reaches approximately 0.7 and stabilizes .
