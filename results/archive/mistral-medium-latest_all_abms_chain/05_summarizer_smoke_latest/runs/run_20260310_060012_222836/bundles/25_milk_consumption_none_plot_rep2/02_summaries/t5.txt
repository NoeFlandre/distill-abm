agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . by the 10th step, the number of choices has risen significantly, reaching approximately 5000 . this upward trend persists, and by the 20th step the total reaches about 15000 choices . the rate of increase starts to level off slightly but continues to rise steadily . the deviation approaches a value slightly above 0.7, indicating a trend towards stabilization as the steps progress . the difference in choice function scores among agents increases rapidly at first and then stabilizes over time, reflecting the evolving decision-making processes .
