 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model explores how perception, habits, and social influence affect individual decision-making processes regarding milk choices . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  Simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent and steady increase in the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined over the time period from 1974 to 2005 . The trend is characterized by a continuous upward trajectory without any detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a consistent adoption pattern . The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various segments of the time series, with significant slopes ranging from approximately 64 to 76 .  The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents in the simulation starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the time steps . Notable peaks during this phase include values around 1265.47, 1240.35, and 1226.86 units . The mean quantity rises again, reaching a maximum value of approximately 1456.60 units by the final time step .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a rapid increase in the early time steps, reaching a value of approximately 13.4756 by the 13th time step . Following this rapid increase, the mean deviation stabilizes around a high value, fluctuating slightly but remaining close to 13.4302 for the remainder of the simulation .
