 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history . The landscape is a simplified grassland environment with patches of grass, ungulates, and human hunters .  Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent decreasing trend throughout the simulation period . Initial rapid increase suggests a high hunting pressure at the beginning of the simulation, which gradually stabilizes as the system reaches an equilibrium . For decision makers, these trends imply that initial hunting pressure is intense but eventually stabilizes, indicating a potential equilibrium in predator-prey dynamics within the model's parameters .  Understanding these patterns can inform strategies for managing wildlife populations and assessing the sustainability of hunting practices .
