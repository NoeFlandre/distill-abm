The percentage of green patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable. Around the 365th step, there is a notable decline to about 24.91%, indicating a significant reduction in vegetated patches. Following this drop, the percentage of green patches gradually recovers, showing a steady increase. By approximately the 725th step, the percentage of green patches stabilizes again around 33%. Throughout the remainder of the simulation, the percentage of green patches fluctuates slightly but remains close to this stabilized value, with minor oscillations observed at regular intervals. The dominant period of these oscillations is around 63 steps. The trends suggest that the landscape undergoes a significant disturbance around the 365th step, followed by a recovery phase and eventual stabilization. This pattern implies that the ecosystem within the model has a resilience mechanism that allows it to recover from disturbances and maintain a relatively stable state of vegetation coverage over time. For decision makers, this indicates that while the ecosystem can withstand and recover from significant disturbances, monitoring and managing the factors leading to such disturbances are crucial for maintaining the overall health and stability of the ecosystem.
