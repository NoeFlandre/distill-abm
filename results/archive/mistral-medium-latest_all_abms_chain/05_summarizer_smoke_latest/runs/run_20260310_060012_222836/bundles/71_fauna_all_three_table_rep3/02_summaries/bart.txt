 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant at 20 throughout the entire duration .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at 0 and exhibits a gradual increase over time . The dominant period of oscillation towards the end of the simulation suggests periodic variations in hunting success or prey availability . For decision-makers, these trends suggest that the prey population undergoes significant growth phases followed by stabilization or decline . Understanding these patterns can help in making informed decisions regarding conservation efforts, habitat management and the impact of human activities on wildlife populations .
