The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear increasing trend over time. Initially, the count starts at zero and gradually rises, reflecting the ongoing hunting activities of the foragers. The early phase of the simulation, up to around 1,159 ticks, shows a steady and significant increase in the number of females killed, with the rate of increase being more pronounced in the initial ticks and gradually tapering off. The mean number of females killed over the entire simulation period is approximately 104.56, with a median of 119.06, indicating that the latter half of the simulation period has a higher concentration of values. The maximum value reached is around 123.67 at approximately 1,826 ticks, after which the trend stabilizes with minor fluctuations. The model identifies several change points, notably around 125, 375, 475, 625, 850, 1600, and 2310 ticks, suggesting shifts in the rate of increase. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in various segments, with the most substantial increases occurring in the early phases. Towards the end of the simulation, from around 1,856 ticks onwards, there is a slight decreasing trend, although the overall number of females killed remains high and relatively stable. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 ticks, indicating a cyclical pattern in the latter part of the simulation. This detailed trend analysis provides insights into the dynamic interactions between foragers and prey within the model's parameters.
