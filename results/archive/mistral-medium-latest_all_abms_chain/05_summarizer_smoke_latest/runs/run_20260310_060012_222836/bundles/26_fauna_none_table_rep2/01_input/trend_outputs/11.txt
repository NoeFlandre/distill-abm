The combined total energy available in the population of male prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear overall decreasing trend over time. Initially, the energy level starts at 12,500 units. This high value is followed by a rapid decline in the early stages of the simulation. By around tick 65, the energy level drops to approximately 11,608 units, marking the first significant decrease. This downward trend continues, with notable values around 4,002 units at tick 377 and further reductions to approximately 3,986 units by tick 380. The decline persists, albeit at a slower rate, with energy levels fluctuating around the 3,975 unit mark between ticks 385 and 393. Throughout the simulation, the energy levels exhibit several minor peaks and valleys, indicating periods of slight recovery followed by further declines. For instance, there are small peaks at ticks 388, 390, and 393, with values around 3,975, 3,959, and 3,944 units respectively. However, these minor recoveries are short-lived, and the overall trend remains a steady decrease. By the latter stages of the simulation, the energy levels approach zero, ultimately reaching an energy level of 0 units by tick 2,284. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend, with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various segments of the simulation. The model exhibits strong oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks, indicating regular intervals of minor fluctuations within the overall declining trend. The change points identified at ticks 125, 350, 500, 625, 875, and 2,310 further highlight the segments where significant shifts in the rate of decline occur.
