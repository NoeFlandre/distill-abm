The percentage of green patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33%. This value remains relatively stable until around 365 steps, where a notable decline occurs, reaching a minimum of about 24.91%. Following this drop, the percentage of green patches rapidly increases and stabilizes around 32.97% by approximately 730 steps. This stabilization is maintained with minor fluctuations for the remainder of the simulation period up to 2312 steps. The data shows several peaks and valleys, indicating minor oscillations around the stabilized value. The dominant period of these oscillations is approximately 63 steps. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early phases (0-1623 steps) followed by significant decreasing trends in the later phases (1624-2087 steps). The change points identified at 150, 300, 475, 725, and 2310 steps suggest shifts in the underlying dynamics of the system. For decision makers, these trends imply that the landscape's vegetation undergoes initial stability, a significant decline, and subsequent recovery to a new equilibrium. The minor oscillations around the equilibrium suggest periodic variations in vegetation cover, which could be influenced by seasonal factors or interactions between herbivores and vegetation regrowth dynamics.
