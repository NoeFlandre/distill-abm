 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The mean, median, standard deviation, and variance are all consistent with a flat line, indicating no fluctuations or deviations from the initial value . The rolling Mann-Kendall test results consistently show no trend across different time intervals, reinforcing the lack of change in the risk attitude .  The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at 0.0, indicating perfect equality . The overall trend shows a significant reduction in the number of livestock being destocked over time . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 time steps, reaching approximately 30 households .  The implications for decision-makers include recognizing the gradual reduction in non-learning households over time, which may reflect the influence of adaptive strategies and learning behaviors diffusing through the population . Understanding these trends can help in evaluating the effectiveness of different behavioral strategies and their long-term impacts .
