 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20 throughout the entire simulation duration .  The overall trend highlights the intense initial hunting pressure on postpartum females, followed by a stabilization phase where the rate of hunting success plateaus . The number of male animals in the simulation starts at 125 and declines rapidly over the first 500 time steps . The statistical analysis shows significant decreasing trends in the early phases of the simulation .  The total energy of male animals begins at 12,500 units and declines rapidly in the first 500 time steps . The decline rate slows down between 250 and 750 time steps, with the number of animals decreasing more gradually . After 750 time steps, the decline continues at a slower pace, with minor fluctuations . Throughout the simulation, there are periods of significant decrease interspersed with brief periods of stabilization or minor increases .  The system exhibits minor fluctuations, with small peaks and valleys, indicating periodic variations in grass regrowth and consumption . The overall trend shows a slight decreasing pattern in the latter part of the simulation . This decline could be attributed to seasonal variations or increased grazing pressure from the megaherbivores .
