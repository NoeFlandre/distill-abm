agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the mean reserve biomass in the grazing system starts at approximately 90,000 units . it initially shows a slight increase, reaching a peak around 92,000 units by the 10th time step . after this peak, there is a notable decline in the reserve biomass, dropping to around 85,000 units by 20th time . by around 40 time steps, the Gini coefficient shows a more noticeable rise . this suggests that disparities in livestock health distribution are becoming more pronounced among households . the trend continues steadily until approximately 60 time steps . some simulations show a steeper rise than others .
