 This agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . The extension focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . The model is applied to smallholder farmers' decision-making, where each pasture is the private property of the household placed on it .  The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, starts at approximately 90 across all households . The mean destock of households starts at a value of zero, then increases rapidly, reaching a peak of approximately 7 livestock at around 5 time steps .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 time steps of the simulation . The majority of the simulations show a clustering of households . This stratification could be due to different thresholds and interactions among households, leading to varied diffusion rates .
