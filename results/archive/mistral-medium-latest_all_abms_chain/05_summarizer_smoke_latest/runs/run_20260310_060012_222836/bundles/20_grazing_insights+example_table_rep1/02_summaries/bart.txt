 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents begins at a low value of approximately 0.0074 . This metric shows a rapid increase in the early stages, reaching a peak value of around 0.3814 by the 33rd time step . Following this peak, there is a general decreasing trend observed from time step 33 onwards, indicating a gradual convergence towards the recommended livestock numbers as households refine their strategies and gain more experience .  The average number of livestock destocked across all households begins at zero and exhibits a sharp increase, peaking significantly at 6.6144 by the third time step . Following this peak, there is a notable decline to 3.3333 by the eighth time step, indicating a period of adjustment as households adapt to the resource constraints . The subsequent gradual increase suggests that interventions or policies supporting recovery could be beneficial in the long term .  The number of E-RO households decreases steadily, reaching a value of approximately 14.7 by the end of the simulation at time step 100 . The most notable decline occurs between time steps 11 and 65, where significant decreasing trends are observed . Post time step 65, the trend stabilizes with no significant changes .
