 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . Model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice . The ABM is designed to replicate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . At the start of the simulation, the consumption of these milk types is minimal, reflecting a strong preference for whole milk among the agents . As the simulation progresses, the rate of increase in whole milk choices starts to stabilize, showing a steady upward trend .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases linearly over the 33 time steps, reaching a final value of 32,000 . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2654.81 units . This high initial value indicates a strong preference or habit for whole milk consumption among the agents at the beginning of the simulation .  The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption begins at a baseline value of 1.0000 . It then exhibits a consistent upward trend throughout the simulation period . The trend continues to rise consistently through subsequent steps, reflecting an increasing propensity among agents to choose skimmed and semi-skimmed milk due to habit formation .  Trend analysis shows a consistent increasing pattern in the mean choice function deviation throughout the simulation . This indicates a continuous but gradual diversification in agents' choices as they interact and influence each other within the social network . Decision-makers can expect a rapid initial adjustment in consumer behavior towards environmental values, followed by a stable period where further changes are minimal .
