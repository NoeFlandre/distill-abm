An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole. In this context, the ABM is designed to replicate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice.                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                                       The first 300 time steps of the simulation show a steady increase in the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, with periods of stabilization and minor fluctuations with minor peaks and valleys. For instance, there are notable peaks at around 1240.35 units and 1226.86 units, and valleys at approximately 1233.62 units and 1214.46 units. These fluctuations indicate periods of varying adoption rates, possibly due to the complex interplay of social influence, habit formation, and cognitive dissonance among agents. The simulation ends with the mean consumption near 1456.60 units, indicating a substantial shift towards the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. This final value suggests that over time, a significant portion of the population has adopted alternative milk types, reflecting a major shift in consumer behavior. This acceleration could be due to the cumulative effects of social influence and the weakening of initial habits, as agents become more susceptible to the perceived benefits of skimmed or semi-skimmed milk. The simulation ends with the total number of choices for skimmed or semi-skimmed milk reaching approximately 25,644, showing a substantial shift in consumer behavior over the simulated period.
