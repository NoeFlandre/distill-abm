agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at a very low value of 5.29 . this initial value suggests that at the start of the simulation, the adoption of whole milk is minimal . by around the 4th time step, the consumption reaches approximately 530 . over the first 300 time steps, there is a steady and significant increase in the adoption of skimmed or semi-skimmed milk . this trend suggests that agents are gradually shifting their preferences, likely influenced by the parameters governing health perception, social influence, and habit formation . by around the 100th time step, the mean consumption reaches approximately 1265.47 units . this peak suggests a critical mass of agents have started to adopt alternative milk types, driven by social influence and changing perceptions . the mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores begins at 0.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation . the metric rises rapidly in the initial time steps, reaching approximately 0.6729 by the middle of the simulation, and continues to increase steadily, culminating at 0.7508 by the final time step . this suggests that agents are becoming more differentiated in their preferences and behaviors as the simulation progresses .
