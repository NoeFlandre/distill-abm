 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  There is no variance or fluctuation in the data, indicating a consistent and unchanging initial risk attitude among households . The mean reserve biomass in the grazing system starts at approximately 90,000 units . The overall pattern reflects an initial period of rapid adaptation followed by a stabilization phase, with minor oscillations as households fine-tune their strategies .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 steps, reaching approximately 30 households . The overall trend shows an initial rapid adoption followed by a stabilization phase, reflecting the diffusion of social learning behaviors within the household population . The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at 0.0 .
