The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households starts at 90. It declines rapidly over the first 20 time steps, reaching a value of approximately 47. This initial decline could be attributed to the households adjusting to initial conditions and possibly destocking livestock as part of their adaptive strategies. The metric continues to decrease at a slower rate until around time step 37, where it reaches its minimum value of approximately 41. After this point, the number of livestock gradually increases, indicating a recovery phase where households might be adapting better to the environmental and social conditions. By the end of the simulation at time step 100, the average total number of livestock stabilizes around 47.5. The trend analysis shows a significant decreasing trend in the early time steps, followed by a period of no significant trend, and then a significant increasing trend starting from time step 44 onwards. The presence of strong oscillations, particularly between time steps 5 to 20 and 39 to 42, suggests periodic adjustments in livestock numbers as households respond to varying conditions.
