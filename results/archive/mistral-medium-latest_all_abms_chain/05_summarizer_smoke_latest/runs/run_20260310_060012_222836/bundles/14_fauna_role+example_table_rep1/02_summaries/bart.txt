 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration .  The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a strong increasing trend in the early phases, which gradually weakens and transitions to a period with no significant trend, followed by a slight decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation . This pattern suggests that the initial hunting pressure is high but tapers off as the simulation progresses, potentially due to a reduction in the availability of postpartum female prey .  The number of female prey animals in the simulation starts at 125 and exhibits a rapid decline in the initial phase . The rate of decline slows down after this initial phase, suggesting a shift in the dynamics affecting the population . Throughout the simulation, there are several minor peaks and valleys, indicating small-scale fluctuations in the population. These fluctuations suggest periodic variations in the . population dynamics, potentially influenced by seasonal changes .  The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates a consistent decreasing trend in energy levels across various segments of the simulation . The simulation ends with the energy levels approaching zero, suggesting a near-complete depletion of the female prey population's energy reserves by the final tick .
