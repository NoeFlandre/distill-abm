 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction . It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history . The landscape is a simplified grassland environment with patches of grass, ungulates, and human hunters . Ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values and processing costs  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear trend in the cumulative number of postpartum females killed over time . Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero and remains relatively low for the first few hundred steps . The trend suggests that initial hunting pressure can be quite intense, but over time, the system may reach a stable state where the rate of hunting levels off .  The data from repeated simulations of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of male animals over time . This trend suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the male animal population is not sustainable and tends towards extinction .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid decline from an initial value of approximately 12,000 units . This sharp decrease occurs within the first 500 steps, where the energy levels drop to around 2,000 . This trend indicates a rapid initial depletion of energy resources among female animals . For decision makers, this suggests that early interventions are critical in influencing the long-term sustainability of female animal populations within the model .
