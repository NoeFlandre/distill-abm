The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations exhibits a consistent and pronounced decline over time. Initially, the total energy of all animals starts at approximately 25,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period before a sharp decline begins around 500 steps. By approximately 1,000 steps, the total energy of all animals drops significantly to around 5,000 units. This downward trend continues more gradually, with the total energy stabilizing near zero around 2,000 steps and remaining there for the rest of the simulation. This trend suggests a rapid depletion of energy resources among the animals, potentially indicating high predation pressure, limited food availability, or other environmental stressors leading to a significant reduction in the energy reserves of the animal population. For decision-makers, this implies that under the given parameters, the animal population faces severe challenges in maintaining energy levels, which could lead to population collapse or extinction. This insight could guide further investigations into the factors contributing to this decline and inform strategies to mitigate such outcomes in real-world scenarios.
