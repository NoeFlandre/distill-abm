The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid decline from an initial value of approximately 12,000 units. This sharp decrease occurs within the first 500 steps, where the energy levels drop to around 2,000 units. Following this steep decline, the rate of energy reduction slows significantly, with the energy levels gradually tapering off and approaching near-zero values by around 2,000 steps. This trend indicates a rapid initial depletion of energy resources among female animals, likely due to high hunting pressure or environmental factors, followed by a prolonged period of stabilization where the remaining energy is minimal. For decision makers, this suggests that initial conditions and early interventions are critical in influencing the long-term sustainability of female animal populations within the model.
