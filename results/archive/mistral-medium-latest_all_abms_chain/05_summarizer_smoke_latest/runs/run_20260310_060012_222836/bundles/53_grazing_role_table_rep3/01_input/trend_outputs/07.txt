The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation timeline. Over time, the inequality gradually increases, reaching a value of 0.0726 at the final timestep. The standard deviation of 0.0239 and variance of 0.0006 indicate low variability in the inequality values over time. Change points are detected at timesteps 51, 71, and 100, suggesting notable shifts in the trend at these points. Additionally, there is evidence of strong oscillations in the latter part of the simulation, with a dominant period of 49.00 timesteps, indicating cyclical patterns in the inequality dynamics. Overall, the simulation results suggest a gradual and consistent increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households, with some periodic fluctuations in the latter stages.
