The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households shows distinct trends over the simulated time steps. Initially, the average number of livestock starts at 90.0000. There is a significant decreasing trend in the early time steps, with the average number of livestock dropping sharply. The most pronounced drop is observed around time step 37, where the average number of livestock reaches its minimum value of 41.0089. Following this low point, the trend shifts. From time step 44 onwards, there is a consistent increasing trend in the average number of livestock. This increasing trend is significant and continues through to the end of the simulation, although the rate of increase slows down over time. The inflection points at time steps 3, 46, and the change points at 20, 55, and 100 mark notable shifts in the trend dynamics. The oscillation analysis suggests a dominant period of 49 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in the data. The strong oscillation indices further support the presence of periodic fluctuations in the average number of livestock. Overall, the simulation shows an initial sharp decline in livestock numbers followed by a gradual and steady recovery.
