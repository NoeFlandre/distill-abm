agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the average reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . from time step 55 onwards, the decreasing trend continues more gradually, and the deviation values stabilize, showing no significant trend towards the end of the simulation . the destocking rate starts at 0.0000, indicating a perfectly equal distribution of healthy livestock among households . by the third time step, the value drops to 3.3333, followed by another peak at 3.3733 in the ninth time step . the rolling Mann-Kendall test shows significant decreasing trends in the initial phases . key change points are detected at time steps 25, 60, and 100 . the model exhibits strong oscillations with a dominant period of 49 time steps . inflection points are observed at several time steps, including 45, 47, 53, 56, 57, 62, 67, 72, 74, and 75 .
