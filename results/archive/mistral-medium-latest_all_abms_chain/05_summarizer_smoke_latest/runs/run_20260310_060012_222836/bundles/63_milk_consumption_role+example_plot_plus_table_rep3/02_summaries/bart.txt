 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . At the beginning of the simulation, the consumption of these milk types is minimal, reflecting a strong preference for whole milk among the agents . The trend from start to finish shows a clear and consistent decline in whole milk consumption .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . This consistent increase reflects a systematic rise in decision-making events regarding milk consumption over the simulated period . The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2654.81 units . This value declines rapidly within the first few time steps, indicating a sharp reduction in whole milk consumption .  The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation . Initially, there is a rapid increase in the habit factor within the first few steps . This increase becomes more pronounced and consistent from step 15 onwards, indicating a shift in agents' habits towards a higher preference for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . The trend is characterized by a continuous upward trajectory with no detected peaks or valleys .  The mean value of environmental deviation starts at 0 and increases rapidly in the first few steps, reaching approximately 13.47 by step 13 . The stabilization occurs after step 13, with minor fluctuations around the mean value . This stability indicates a period of equilibrium where the agents' health perceptions and their milk choices are largely aligned . The dominant period of oscillation is 15 steps, indicating a regular pattern of minor adjustments .
