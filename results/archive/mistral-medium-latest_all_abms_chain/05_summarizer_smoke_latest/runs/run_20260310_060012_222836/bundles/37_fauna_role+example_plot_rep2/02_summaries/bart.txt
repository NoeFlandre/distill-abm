 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . Hunters and animals move and interact based on the set parameters, and the model provides summary statistics such as the number of animals in the environment over time .  The total number of deaths among the prey animal population starts at 0 and increases rapidly within the first 500 time steps, reaching approximately 200 deaths . By around 1000 steps, the number of female animals drops to about 20, indicating a significant reduction in the female population . The number of male animals continues to decrease gradually, approaching near zero by around 2000 time steps .  The simulation ends with the number of animals close to zero, suggesting that the population has not been able to sustain itself under the given conditions . Throughout the simulation, there is some variability in the trajectories of different runs, but the overall trend remains consistent . The total energy of male animals starts at approximately 12,000 units .
