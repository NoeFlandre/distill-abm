agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins at a very low value of 5.29 units . this initial value suggests that interventions targeting health awareness and social norms could be pivotal in shaping consumer behavior towards healthier milk choices . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across all time windows . the dominant oscillation period of 15.00 and strong oscillations towards the end suggest some periodic fluctuations in the adoption rate . for decision-makers, these trends imply a clear and steady shift towards healthier milk options, driven by underlying consumer preferences and social influences . consumption levels exhibit minor fluctuations but generally stabilize around a high mean value of approximately 1220.21 units . fluctuations observed in the data, such as the valleys and peaks around the 12th to 23rd time steps, indicate periods of slight declines and recoveries in consumption . the dominant period of oscillation being around 15 time steps suggests a cyclical pattern in consumer behavior . trend is characterized by a series of significant increases, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test . the dominant period of oscillation is 15.00, with strong oscillations noted at several indices, indicating periodic fluctuations in the rate of divergence . for decision makers, this trend implies that over time, the factors influencing milk choice become more varied and complex .
