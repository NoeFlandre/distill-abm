 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at a very low value of 5.29 units . Over the initial time steps, there is a rapid increase in consumption, indicating a significant shift in consumer behavior . By the 32nd time step, the consumption reaches a peak of 1456.5991 units . This trend highlights a major transition in milk consumption patterns, likely driven by changing perceptions, social influences, and habit formation .  Total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the entire simulation period, reaching a final value of 32,000 . For decision-makers, these trends imply a clear and steady shift towards healthier milk options, driven by underlying consumer preferences and social influences .  The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit starts at an initial value of 1.0000 . Over the course of the simulation, this factor exhibits a gradual and consistent increase . The dominant period of oscillation around 15 time steps indicates a cyclical pattern in the habit factor's influence .  Researchers found a growing discrepancy over time between perceived health values and the actual health implications of agents' milk choices . This insight could be crucial for targeting marketing strategies and understanding the evolving landscape of consumer preferences . For decision makers, this trend implies that over time, the factors influencing milk choice become more varied and complex, leading to greater diversity in consumer behavior .
