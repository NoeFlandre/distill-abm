The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to megaherbivore extinction. It considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and life history. The model allows users to manipulate parameters to observe how different conditions interact to influence extinction or equilibrium outcomes. The landscape is a simplified grassland environment with patches of grass, ungulates, and human hunters. The significant and steady decrease in energy levels implies that the foragers are struggling to maintain their energy balance, which could be due to a variety of factors such as declining prey availability, increasing environmental challenges, or heightened competition. For decision makers, this trend highlights the potential unsustainability of the current foraging strategies and environmental conditions, suggesting a need for interventions to support forager energy levels or to mitigate the factors contributing to this decline.
