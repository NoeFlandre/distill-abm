 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The data shows a clear and steady increase in the total number of whole milk choices over the simulation period . No peaks or valleys are detected, but there are inflection points at steps 1, 7, and 31, indicating changes in the rate of increase . The dominant period of oscillation is 15 steps, with strong oscillations noted towards the end of the series .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk shows a clear trend over the simulation period . The trend analysis reveals that the habit factor experiences a continuous and significant upward trend after the initial steps, reflecting a growing preference or habit formation towards skimmed milk over time . The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a significant increasing trend across all segments of the time series .
