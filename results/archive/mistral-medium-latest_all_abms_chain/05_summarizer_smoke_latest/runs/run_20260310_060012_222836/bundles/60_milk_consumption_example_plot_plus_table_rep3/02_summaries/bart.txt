 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It explores how perception, habits, and social influence affect individual decision-making processes regarding milk choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others .  Total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . Means quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2654.81 units . Average number of choices reached 32,000 at the final time step .  The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . Initially, there is a rapid increase in the habit factor within the first few steps, suggesting a quick adjustment phase where agents rapidly develop habits towards whole milk consumption . The trend analysis shows a consistent increase across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, with significant positive slopes .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at 0 and increases rapidly within the first few steps . By around the 5th step, the deviation reaches approximately 12, showing a steep initial adjustment period . The deviation remains relatively constant from around the 10th step to the end of the simulation at the 33rd step .
