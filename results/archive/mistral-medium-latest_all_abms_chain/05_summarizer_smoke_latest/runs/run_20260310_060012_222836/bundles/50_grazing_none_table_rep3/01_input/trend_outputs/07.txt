The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the entire simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of approximately 0, indicating a relatively equal distribution of healthy livestock among households. Throughout the simulation, the Gini coefficient gradually increases, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 by the end of the simulation period. This trend suggests a growing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock over time. The increase is steady and significant, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results, which show a consistent and significant increasing trend across all time intervals. There are several inflection points detected at various time steps, indicating changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. Additionally, there are strong oscillations detected towards the latter part of the simulation, suggesting some periodic variations in the rate of change of inequality. The dominant period of these oscillations is around 49 time steps. The change points identified at time steps 51, 71, and 100 further highlight significant shifts in the trend dynamics. Overall, the simulation results indicate a clear and consistent trend towards increasing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households.
