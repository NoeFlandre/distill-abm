 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making, which are linked through a yearly sequence of processes .  The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents in the simulation exhibits distinct trends over the 100 timesteps . There were no fluctuations or trends observed in the data, as indicated by the statistical analysis showing no significant trends in any of the rolling Mann-Kendall tests .  The overall mean number of livestock across the simulation is 48.7946, with a median of 47.3122, reflecting a slight right skew in the distribution of values . Initially, the destocking rate starts at zero and experiences a significant spike, reaching a peak of approximately 6.61 at time step 3 . The trend analysis indicates a general decreasing trend in destocking rates during the initial phases, specifically from time steps 0-10, 11-21, and 22-32 .  The model exhibits strong oscillations, particularly around the mid-range time steps, with a dominant period of 49 time steps . Key change points are observed at time steps 25, 60, and 100, suggesting notable shifts in the rate of destocking at these points .
