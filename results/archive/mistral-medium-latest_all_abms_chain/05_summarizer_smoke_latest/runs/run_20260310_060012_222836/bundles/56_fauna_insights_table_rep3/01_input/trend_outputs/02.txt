The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, with slight peaks and valleys around the initial value. For instance, at tick 2, the energy level is approximately 1989.925 units, and at tick 55, it is around 1263.3 units. However, these early variations are relatively small compared to the overall trend.  As the simulation progresses, the energy levels continue to decline steadily. By tick 394, the energy level drops to about -2005.95 units, marking a significant decrease from the initial value. This downward trend persists, with energy levels reaching approximately -23807 units by tick 1594 and further decreasing to around -28403 units by tick 1826. The most substantial drops are observed towards the end of the simulation, with energy levels plummeting to -38233 units by the final tick, 2310. The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decrease, with all rolling Mann-Kendall tests indicating significant decreasing trends across different segments of the simulation. The change points identified at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 further emphasize the continuous decline in energy levels. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 ticks, but the overall trend is overwhelmingly downward. For decision-makers, this trend suggests that the forager bands are experiencing a continuous decline in their energy status, which could be attributed to factors such as hunting success rates, movement costs, and environmental conditions within the model. This insight may prompt further investigation into the parameters influencing forager energy levels and potential adjustments to mitigate the observed decline.
