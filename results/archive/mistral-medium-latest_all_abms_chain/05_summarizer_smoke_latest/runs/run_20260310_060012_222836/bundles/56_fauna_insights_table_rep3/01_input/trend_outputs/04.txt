The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear increasing trend over time, starting from 0 and reaching a peak value of approximately 123.67. The initial phase of the simulation shows a rapid increase in the number of females killed, with significant growth observed up to around 1,855 ticks. This period is characterized by a strong upward trend, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, which shows significant increasing trends with high tau values and slopes. After reaching the peak, the trend stabilizes, and a slight decrease is observed towards the end of the simulation period. The model shows several inflection points and change points, indicating shifts in the rate of increase. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 ticks, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the hunting patterns or prey availability. The implications for decision-makers include understanding the dynamics of hunting pressure on female prey, which can inform strategies for conservation and management of megaherbivore populations.
