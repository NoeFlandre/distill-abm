The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear increasing trend over time. Initially, the count starts at zero and gradually rises, reaching a peak value of 119 around the mid-point of the simulation timeline. This phase is characterized by a steep slope, suggesting a high rate of hunting success or pressure. As the simulation progresses beyond the mid-point, the rate of increase slows down, and the trend stabilizes, indicating a potential equilibrium or saturation point in hunting activity. Towards the end of the simulation, there is a slight decrease in the cumulative count, suggesting a minor reduction in hunting pressure or success. Key inflection points and change points highlight significant shifts in the trend dynamics, suggesting underlying changes in the model's parameters or interactions. For decision-makers, these trends imply that hunting pressure on postpartum females is initially high but stabilizes over time, which could inform strategies related to conservation efforts or hunting regulations.
