The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulation exhibits relatively stable dynamics over the observed time period. Initially, the percentage of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33% and remains close to this value throughout the simulation, ending at around 32.998%. The mean percentage of vegetated patches is 32.6558%, with a median of 32.9696%, indicating a slight skew in the data distribution. The standard deviation is 0.9503, suggesting minor fluctuations around the mean. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at time step 365, which is a notable deviation from the initial value. The data shows several peaks and valleys, but these variations are minimal, with prominence values close to zero, indicating minor oscillations. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals several periods of increasing and decreasing trends, but these changes are not substantial. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 time steps, with strong oscillation indices observed at regular intervals. The change points identified do not correspond to significant shifts in the overall trend, reinforcing the stability of the vegetated landscape patches. For decision makers, this stability suggests that the environmental conditions governing grass regrowth and consumption by prey animals are in a steady state, implying that the current parameter settings maintain a balanced ecosystem within the model. This balance could be useful for testing hypotheses related to the long-term sustainability of the landscape under given conditions.
