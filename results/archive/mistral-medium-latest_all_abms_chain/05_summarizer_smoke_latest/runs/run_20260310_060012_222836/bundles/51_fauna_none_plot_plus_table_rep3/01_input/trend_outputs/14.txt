The percentage of green patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33%. There is a notable decline in the percentage of green patches around the 365th time step, reaching a minimum value of about 24.91%. Following this decline, the percentage of green patches gradually increases and stabilizes around 32.97%. Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, but the overall trend remains relatively stable after the initial recovery period. The statistical analysis indicates several significant change points and periods of increasing and decreasing trends, but the overall variation remains minimal with a standard deviation of approximately 0.95. The dominant period of oscillation is around 63 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the percentage of green patches. The model parameters and interactions between hunters, prey, and the environment contribute to these observed trends.
