 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It focuses on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics, modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  Total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . No peaks, valleys, or inflection points are detected, suggesting a smooth and uninterrupted trend in the adoption behavior . The total number of choices reached 32,000 at the final time step, indicating a continuous and linear growth in decision-making events .  The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.0000 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . The mean value of the habit factor across all time steps is 1.0359, with a median of 1 .0376, indicating a consistent upward trend . The overall trend shows a significant shift from low to high consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk driven by the complex interplay of personal habits, social influence, and perceived benefits .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at 0 and increases rapidly in the first few steps . This rapid increase suggests that initially, there is a significant misalignment between the agents' environmental values and their milk choices . The deviation stabilizes around 13.4 from step 13 onwards, indicating that the system reaches a steady state .
