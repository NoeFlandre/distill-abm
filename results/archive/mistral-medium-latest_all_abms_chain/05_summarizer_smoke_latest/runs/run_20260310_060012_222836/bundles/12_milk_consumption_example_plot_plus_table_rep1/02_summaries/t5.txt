agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from 1974 to 2005 . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . the total number of whole milk choices starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . the initial increase is gradual, reflecting the initial stages of habit formation and social influence . as time progresses, the rate of increase remains steady . between the tenth and fifteenth time steps, the consumption quantity stabilizes around 1200 to 1300 units, indicating a plateau where the majority of agents have adopted the new milk types and the rate of new adoptions slows down . this stabilization phase suggests that the agents have reached a new equilibrium in their consumption habits . the deviation continues to rise more gradually, reaching around 0.6 by step 10 . this stabilization suggests that agents have settled into their new consumption patterns, with only minor adjustments being made . from step 28 to step 32, there is a slight increase, reaching 0.7251 .
