 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where they can influence and be influenced by others .  The mean whole milk quantity consumed by agents starts at approximately 2700 units at the initial time step . There is a rapid decline in consumption within the first 10 steps, dropping to around 1000 units . This sharp decrease is followed by a more gradual decline, with the consumption stabilizing around 800 units between steps 10 and 20 . The mean habit factor for whole milk exhibits a steady and continuous increase .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero and rapidly increases within the first few steps . By around step 5, the deviation reaches approximately 12.5 and continues to rise, albeit at a slower rate, until it stabilizes around step 10 . This trend indicates that initially, there is a significant divergence between the agents' environmental values and their milk choices .
