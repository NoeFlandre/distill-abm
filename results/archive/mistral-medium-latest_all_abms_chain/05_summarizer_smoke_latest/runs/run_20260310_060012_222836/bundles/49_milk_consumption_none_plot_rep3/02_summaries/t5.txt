agent-based model aims to simulate the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . the model compares two variants with different mechanisms for how agents consider their choices . agents represent adult consumers who make milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics . the total number of choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over the time steps . starting from 0 choices at step 0, the total numbers of choices rises steadily and reaches approximately 32,000 choices by step 30 . a rapid decline in consumption within the first 10 steps, dropping to around 1000 units . the deviation starts at a value close to 0 at the initial time step . in the early stages, up to around step 5, there is a rapid increase in the deviation, reaching approximately 0.35 . this initial phase indicates a significant divergence between the agents' perceived health values and their health values implied by their milk choices .
