The mean value environmental deviation in the agent-based model simulating milk consumption behaviors shows a rapid initial increase, stabilizing quickly within the first few steps. Initially, the deviation starts at 0 and rises sharply, reaching approximately 13.4 by step 13. This rapid increase indicates that agents quickly adjust their environmental perceptions in response to their milk choices. After this initial adjustment period, the deviation stabilizes and remains relatively constant around the value of 13.4 for the remainder of the simulation steps, indicating a steady state where agents' perceptions align closely with their consumption choices. The statistical analysis shows that the initial increase is significant, with strong increasing trends in the early steps, followed by periods of no significant trend, suggesting that the system reaches equilibrium quickly. The minor fluctuations around the steady state are not significant, indicating stability in the agents' environmental perceptions over time. For decision makers, this trend suggests that interventions aimed at changing consumer behavior regarding milk choices may see rapid initial adjustments in consumer perceptions, followed by a stabilization period where further changes are minimal.
