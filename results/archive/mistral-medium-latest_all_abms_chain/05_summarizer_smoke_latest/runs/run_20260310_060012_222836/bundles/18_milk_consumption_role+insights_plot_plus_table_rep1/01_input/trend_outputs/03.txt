The simulation results indicate a consistent and significant increasing trend in the total number of whole milk choices made by agents over the 33 time steps. Starting from an initial value of 0, the total number of whole milk choices rises steadily, reaching a final value of approximately 2194.57. The mean value across all time steps is 1130.88, with a median of 1158.97, suggesting a right-skewed distribution. The standard deviation of 654.69 and variance of 428622.51 indicate substantial variability in the data. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across all time windows, with significant p-values and high Kendall tau coefficients, indicating strong monotonic trends. The slope values, ranging from approximately 64 to 76, further emphasize the steady increase in whole milk choices. Inflection points are detected at indices 1, 7, and 31, suggesting changes in the rate of increase at these points. Additionally, the data exhibits strong oscillations towards the end of the time series, with a dominant period of 15.00 and strong oscillation indices identified at time steps 27, 28, 29, and 30. For decision-makers, these trends suggest a growing preference for whole milk over time among the agents, influenced by the parameters set in the model such as habit formation, social influence, and initial perceptions of health and environmental impacts. The consistent increase and the identified inflection points can help in understanding key periods where interventions or further studies might be beneficial to explore the underlying causes of these trends.
