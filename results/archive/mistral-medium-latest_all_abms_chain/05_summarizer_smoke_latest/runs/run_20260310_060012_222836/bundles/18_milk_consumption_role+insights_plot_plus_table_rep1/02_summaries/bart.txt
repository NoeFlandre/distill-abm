 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  Simulation of the agent-based model reveals distinct trends in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption over time . For decision-makers, these trends highlight the effectiveness of factors influencing consumer behavior away from whole milk . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing strategies to influence consumer behavior effectively, particularly in the context of health and environmental perceptions .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined shows a consistent and linear increasing trend over the 33 steps of the simulation . For decision makers, this implies a predictable and steady increase in milk choices over time, which could inform strategies related to milk consumption patterns and market planning .  These trends suggest that initial adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk can be rapid once certain thresholds are met, followed by a period of stabilization . Understanding these phases can help in designing interventions or marketing strategies to encourage the adoption of healthier milk options .  The mean value environmental deviation in the agent-based model shows a rapid initial increase, stabilizing quickly within the first few steps . The trend suggests that the agents' health perceptions increasingly deviate from the implied values of their milk choices over time . Understanding the factors contributing to this divergence can help in designing more effective strategies to guide consumer behavior towards desired outcomes .
