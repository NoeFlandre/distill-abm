agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . this initial value indicates that at the beginning of the simulation, the consumption of these milk types is minimal, reflecting the initial conditions where whole milk is predominantly consumed . over the first few time steps, there is a rapid increase in consumption . after this peak the consumption slightly decreases and stabilizes around 1220 units, indicating a period of equilibrium . the number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices exceeds 15,000 . this shows a substantial shift in consumption patterns early adopters make . the trend remains strongly positive, with the number nearing 20,000 by the 25th time step . the final value suggests a significant adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk by the end of the simulation period . there are several notable points of inflection and minor peaks and valleys, indicating periods of temporary stabilization . however, the overall trajectory shows a clear movement towards increased consumption of skimmmed milk . the mean habit factor for whole milk starts at a baseline value of 1.0000 . between steps 10 and 20, the mean value health deviation stabilizes, fluctuating slightly around 0.6 . the deviation begins to increase again, albeit at a slower pace, reaching approximately 0.65 by step 25 . this final increase suggests agents are further refining their choices .
