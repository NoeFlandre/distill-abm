The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a baseline value of 1.0000. Initially, there is a slight increase to 1.0205 within the first few steps, followed by a minor dip to 1.0167. This initial fluctuation stabilizes quickly, indicating a brief period of adjustment in consumer habits. From this point onward, the habit factor exhibits a consistent and steady increase. By the midpoint of the simulation, the habit factor reaches approximately 1.2578, reflecting a growing preference and habit formation towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the agents. The trend continues to rise, showing significant and steady growth in the habit factor. By the end of the simulation, the mean habit factor peaks at 1.5879, indicating a strong establishment of habits favoring skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The overall trend demonstrates a clear and continuous upward trajectory in the habit factor, suggesting that over time, the agents increasingly incorporate skimmed and semi-skimmed milk into their regular consumption patterns. The statistical analysis confirms this increasing trend with significant Mann-Kendall test results across various segments of the simulation, reinforcing the steady growth in habit formation.
