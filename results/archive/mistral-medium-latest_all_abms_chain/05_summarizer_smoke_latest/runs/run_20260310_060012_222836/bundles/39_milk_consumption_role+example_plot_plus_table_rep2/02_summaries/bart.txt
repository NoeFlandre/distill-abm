 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . At the beginning of the simulation, the consumption of these milk types is minimal, reflecting the initial conditions where whole milk is predominantly consumed . Over the first few time steps, there is a rapid increase in skimmed milk consumption . This sharp rise continues until around the 10th time step, where the consumption reaches approximately 1265.47 units .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,654.81 units . This quantity declines rapidly in the initial steps, reaching around 816.85 units by the mid-point of the simulation . The overall trend is characterized by a steady and significant increase, reflecting the successful simulation of a widespread behavioral shift .  The statistical analysis reveals significant increasing trends in the early and late stages of the simulation, reflecting the complex dynamics of consumer behavior . The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption starts at a baseline value of 1.0000. It then exhibits a rapid increase within the first few time steps, indicating an initial strong influence of habit on the choice of whole milk .  The mean value of environmental deviation starts at 0 and increases rapidly in the first few steps . This rapid increase indicates a significant initial divergence between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices . The deviation stabilizes around this value, showing minimal fluctuations and maintaining a steady state .
