 RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The extension focuses on incorporating learning capabilities into the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making .  The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households starts at 90.0 . The average amount of green biomass across all patches begins at approximately 45,000 units . The overall trend indicates an initial period of biomass reduction followed by a robust recovery and growth phase, suggesting a resilient vegetation system capable of regeneration .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 time steps, reaching approximately 30 households . This initial rapid increase suggests that households quickly adopt social learning behaviors as they begin to interact and observe their neighbors . By time step 60, the mean destock total reaches around 80, indicating a continued but more gradual reduction in livestock . The overall trend indicates a strong initial uptake of social learning followed by a gradual increase and eventual stabilization .
