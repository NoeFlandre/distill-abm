 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The overall trend reflects the impact of various factors such as social influence, health perceptions, and habit formation on the consumption patterns of whole milk over the simulated period . The trend in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent shows distinct phases .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time shows a rapid initial increase followed by stabilization and a subsequent gradual rise . This trend indicates a significant reduction in the consumption of whole milk over the simulated period, reflecting a shift in consumer behavior influenced by the various parameters and interactions within the agent-based model .  The mean value environmental deviation in the agent-based model starts at a low value close to zero . It then rapidly increases within the first few steps, reaching a value of approximately 12 by step 5 . After this initial sharp rise, the rate of increase slows down, and the deviation stabilizes around 13.5 by step 15 .
