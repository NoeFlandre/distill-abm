 The MHPM is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . It incorporates various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate parameters to establish socio-natural dynamics and observe how these interactions influence extinction events .  The model's landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix . The temporal units are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant at 20 throughout the entire duration of the simulation .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains at this level until around 700 time steps . After this initial period of no births, the number of deaths begins to increase steadily . The rate of increase in deaths slows down significantly after this initial phase, suggesting a reduction in the availability of postpartum females or a shift in hunting focus to other prey types .  The total number of animals in the simulation starts at approximately 166.67 and exhibits a rapid decline over the initial 500 time steps . Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks, but the overall trend remains a steady decline towards extinction . The simulation ends with the total energy of female animals at zero, suggesting that the population could not sustain itself under the given conditions .  The percentage of green patches in the landscape starts at approximately 33% . This initial value represents the baseline proportion of vegetated patches available for grazing by the prey animals . The minimum value observed is approximately 24.91% at around 365 time steps, indicating a slight reduction in the availability .
