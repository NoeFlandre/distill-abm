 An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents to assess their effects on the system as a whole . The model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . These agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . The rapid initial decline highlights the significant impact of introducing healthier alternatives and the role of social influence in consumer choices . The stabilization phases suggest that while initial changes can be swift, long-term behavior modification requires sustained efforts . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing strategies to promote healthier or more environmentally friendly products effectively .  The trend in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents shows a significant decline over the simulated time steps . For decision makers, this trend suggests a steady engagement of the agents in the decision-making process over time . The linear increase implies that the parameters influencing the agents' choices, such as social interactions, habit formation, and perceptions of health and environment, are consistently affecting agents' behaviors without significant fluctuations or disruptions .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the 30 steps of the simulation . Understanding the key time steps and the corresponding habit factors can help in designing targeted interventions that align with the stages of habit formation among consumers .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero and rapidly increases within the first few steps . By around the fifth step, the deviation reaches approximately 13.5 and then gradually stabilizes . By step 25, there is a slight upward trend in the mean value health deviation, reaching approximately 0.65 by step 30 .
