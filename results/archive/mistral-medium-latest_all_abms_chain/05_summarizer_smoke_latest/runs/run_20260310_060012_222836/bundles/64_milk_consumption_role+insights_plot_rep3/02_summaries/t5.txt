agent-based model (ABM) aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . the model consists of 1,000 agents, each representing an adult consumer . these agents are part of a social network and make decisions based on various parameters that influence their milk choice. the rapid initial decline highlights the significant impact of introducing healthier alternatives and the role of social influence in consumer choices . the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . this period of rapid increase continues until approximately the tenth time step . trend in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents shows a significant decline over the simulated time steps . Initially, the mean whole milk quantity starts at approximately 2,600 units . this high initial value reflects the strong initial habit strength for whole milk consumption among the agents . the mean habit factor for whole milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the observed time steps . within the first few steps, there is a rapid increase, reaching around 1.02 by the fifth step . this steady upward trend persists, and by the 30th step, the means habit factor reaches a value close to 1.05. the mean value health deviation starts at a low value close to zero . by step 5, the deviation reaches approximately 0.3 . this initial phase indicates a significant shift in the agents' health perceptions as they begin to adopt new milk types .
