The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of approximately 25,000 units. This initial high energy level is maintained for a brief period before a rapid decline begins. By around 500 steps, the total energy has decreased significantly to about 5,000 units. This sharp decline continues, and by approximately 1,000 steps, the total energy of all animals drops to near zero. After this point, the total energy remains at or near zero for the remainder of the simulation, indicating that the energy levels of the animals have been depleted and are not recovering. This trend suggests a rapid and sustained reduction in the total energy of the prey animals within the model's environment.
