The data from the repeated simulations of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of male animals over time. Initially, the number of male animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of male animals has decreased to roughly 80. The decline continues, and by 1000 steps, the number of male animals drops to about 20. Beyond 1000 steps, the number of male animals continues to decrease gradually, approaching zero around 1500 steps. After 1500 steps, the number of male animals remains at or near zero for the remainder of the simulation. This trend indicates a significant reduction in the male animal population over time, eventually leading to near extinction within the simulated environment.
