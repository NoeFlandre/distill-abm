agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . the model aims to evaluate long-term social-ecological consequences of different household behavioral types . the mean reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . in the early stages, up to approximately step 20 there is a noticeable decline in the mean reserves biomass, reaching a low point of around 85,000 units . this initial decrease suggests a period of increased graze activity or reduced biomass growth, possibly due to the initial setup and adaptation phase . the Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at 0 for all simulations . by the 40th step, the inequality continues to rise, with values ranging from about 0.030 to 0.060 . this trend persists and becomes more pronounced by the 60th step . trends suggest that the number of non-learning households tends to decrease over time . the stabilization at different levels indicates that certain conditions or parameters might lead to a persistent presence . others result in their near-complete disappearance .
