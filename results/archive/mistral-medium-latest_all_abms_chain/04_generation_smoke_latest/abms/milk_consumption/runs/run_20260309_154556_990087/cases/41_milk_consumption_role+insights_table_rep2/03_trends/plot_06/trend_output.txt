The simulation results indicate a clear and consistent decline in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents over the observed time period. Initially, the average consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units. This value experiences a significant and steady decrease, reaching a low of approximately 253.10 units by the end of the simulation. The trend analysis reveals several phases of significant decline, particularly notable in the early and middle stages of the simulation. For instance, from the beginning to around one-fifth of the simulation period, there is a sharp decrease in consumption, followed by a more moderate decline. The rolling Mann-Kendall test highlights significant decreasing trends in multiple segments, underscoring the overall downward trajectory. Key inflection points and change points suggest periods where the rate of decline varies, but the overarching trend remains consistently downward. The presence of peaks and valleys indicates some fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the overall trend. For decision makers, this trend suggests a substantial shift away from whole milk consumption over time, which could be influenced by the model parameters such as social susceptibility, habit threshold, and initial health perceptions. This insight is crucial for stakeholders in the dairy industry and public health sectors to understand the evolving consumer preferences and to strategize accordingly.
