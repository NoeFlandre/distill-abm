Between time steps 15 and 19, the rate of decline slows down, and the trend becomes less pronounced, as indicated by the non-significant Mann-Kendall test result. This suggests a period of stabilization where the rate of decrease in whole milk consumption plateaus temporarily. 

Following this stabilization, the downward trend resumes, albeit at a slower pace. The mean whole milk quantity continues to decrease, with minor fluctuations observed around time steps 19, 27, and 30. These fluctuations are marked by small peaks and valleys, indicating brief periods of slight increases or stabilizations in consumption before the overall downward trend continues. 

By the end of the simulation, at time step 32, the mean whole milk quantity reaches its lowest point at approximately 253.10 units. This substantial reduction from the initial value highlights a significant shift in consumption patterns over the simulated period. 

The statistical summary also reveals several inflection points and change points, indicating key moments where the rate of change in consumption patterns shifts.
