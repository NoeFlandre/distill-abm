The mean value health deviation in the agent-based model simulating milk consumption behaviors among British consumers from 1974 to 2005 shows a clear trend of increasing deviation over time. Initially, the mean value health deviation starts at 0.0000. In the early steps, there is a rapid increase in deviation, reaching approximately 0.3 by step 5. This upward trend continues, albeit at a slower pace, with the deviation value reaching around 0.5 by step 10. The growth rate of deviation further decreases but remains positive, with the deviation value stabilizing around 0.6 between steps 15 and 25. Towards the end of the simulation, there is a slight increase, culminating in a final deviation value of 0.7251 at step 32. Throughout the simulation, the mean value health deviation consistently increases, indicating a growing discrepancy between the agents' health perceptions and their actual milk consumption choices. The inflection points at steps 4, 6, 16, 19, 22, 28, and 31 suggest changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various segments of the simulation, with significant positive slopes. The dominant oscillation period of 15.00 and strong oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation suggest some periodic fluctuations in the rate of deviation increase, but the primary trend is a steady rise in mean value health deviation.
