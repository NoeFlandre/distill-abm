The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent over time. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29 units. In the early stages, there is a rapid and significant increase in consumption, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, which indicates a strong increasing trend with a high Kendall tau coefficient and significant p-values during the initial time steps. Specifically, from time steps 0 to 9, the consumption increases sharply with a slope of approximately 140 units per time step. 

Following this initial surge, the consumption stabilizes and enters a phase of no significant trend. From time steps 10 to 24, the rolling Mann-Kendall test shows no significant trend, indicating a period of relative stability in consumption levels. During this phase, the consumption values oscillate around a mean of approximately 1220 units, with several peaks and valleys identified. Notable peaks occur at time steps 11, 13, and 21, with values around 1265.47, 1240.35, and 1226.86 units respectively. Corresponding valleys are observed at time steps 12, 20, and 23, with values around 1233.62, 1214.46, and 1209.70 units respectively. 

Towards the end of the simulation, there is another significant increasing trend. From time steps 25 to 29, the rolling Mann-Kendall test again indicates a strong increasing trend with a slope of approximately 48.54 units per time step. The consumption reaches its maximum value of 1456.60 units at the final time step, marking a substantial rise from the initial value. 

The data also exhibits strong oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 15 time steps, suggesting regular fluctuations in consumption levels throughout the simulation. The inflection points and change points further highlight the dynamic nature of the consumption patterns, with notable changes in the trend occurring at specific time steps.
