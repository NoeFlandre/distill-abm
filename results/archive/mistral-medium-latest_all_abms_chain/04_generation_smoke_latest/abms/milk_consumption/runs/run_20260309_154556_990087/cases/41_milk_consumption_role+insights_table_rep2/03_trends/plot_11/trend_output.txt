The mean deviation between the agents' held health values and the implied values by their milk choices exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Starting from an initial value of 0.0000, the mean deviation gradually rises to 0.7251 by the end of the simulation. The mean value over the entire period is 0.4965, with a median of 0.5584, indicating a slight right-skew in the distribution. The standard deviation of 0.1702 and variance of 0.0290 suggest moderate variability around the mean. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals significant increasing trends across various segments of the simulation, with varying slopes indicating different rates of increase. Notably, the initial phase shows a steeper increase with a slope of 0.063, which gradually flattens out in subsequent phases. The detection of inflection points at indices 4, 6, 16, 19, 22, 28, and 31 suggests changes in the rate of increase at these points. The oscillation summary indicates a dominant period of 15.00, with strong oscillations observed towards the latter part of the simulation. For decision makers, the consistent increase in mean deviation implies a growing discrepancy between perceived health values and actual milk choices over time. This trend suggests that agents may be increasingly influenced by factors other than health perceptions, such as social norms or habits, which could be critical for designing interventions aimed at aligning health perceptions with consumption behaviors.
