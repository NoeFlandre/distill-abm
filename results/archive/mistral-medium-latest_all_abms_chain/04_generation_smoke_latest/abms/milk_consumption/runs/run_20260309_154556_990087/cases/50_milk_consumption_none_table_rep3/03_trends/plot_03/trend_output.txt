The agent-based model simulating the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 shows a consistent increasing trend in the total number of times agents chose whole milk. Initially, the value starts at 0 and steadily rises over the time steps, reaching a maximum of approximately 2194.57 at the final time step. Throughout the simulation, there are no detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady and continuous increase without any significant fluctuations or declines. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various segments of the time series, with significant positive slopes and tau values of 1.000, indicating a strong and consistent upward trend. The inflection points at indices 1, 7, and 31 suggest slight changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. The oscillation summary indicates a dominant period of 15, with strong oscillation indices towards the end of the series, suggesting some periodic behavior in the later stages of the simulation. The change point at index 32 marks the final significant shift in the trend, coinciding with the maximum value observed.
