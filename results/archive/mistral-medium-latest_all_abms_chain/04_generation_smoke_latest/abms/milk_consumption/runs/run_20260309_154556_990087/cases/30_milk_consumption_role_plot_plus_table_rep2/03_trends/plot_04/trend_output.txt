The simulation results show a clear trend of increasing adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time. Initially, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices is zero, indicating that all agents start by consuming whole milk. As the simulation progresses, the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk begins to rise gradually. By the end of the simulation, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 25,643.83. This indicates a significant shift in consumer behavior towards healthier milk options over the simulated time period. The trend is consistently increasing, with no detected peaks or valleys, suggesting a steady and continuous adoption process. The inflection point at index 31 and the change point at index 32 indicate a significant shift in the rate of adoption around these time steps. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms that the trend is consistently increasing across different segments of the simulation, with significant slopes ranging from 201.463 to 935.933. The dominant oscillation period of 15.00 and strong oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation suggest some periodic fluctuations in the adoption rate, but the overall trend remains strongly upward. This consistent increase in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk reflects the influence of the model parameters, such as social susceptibility, habit threshold, and social conformity, which collectively drive the agents towards healthier milk choices over time.
