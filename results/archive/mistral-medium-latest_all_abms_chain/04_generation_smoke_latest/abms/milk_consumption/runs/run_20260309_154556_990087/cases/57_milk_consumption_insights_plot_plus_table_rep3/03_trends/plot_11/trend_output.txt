The mean value health deviation in the agent-based model simulating milk consumption behaviors shows a clear increasing trend over the time steps analyzed. Initially, the mean value health deviation starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a rapid increase in the early steps, reaching approximately 0.5 by step 10. This initial phase indicates a significant shift in the agents' perception of health values related to their milk choices. From steps 10 to 20, the rate of increase slows down, suggesting a stabilization period where the agents' health perceptions are adjusting more gradually. After step 20, the mean value health deviation continues to rise but at a slower and more consistent rate, eventually reaching a peak of 0.7251 by the final step, 32. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across all segments of the time steps, with significant positive slopes. The inflection points identified at steps 4, 6, 16, 19, 22, 28, and 31 indicate key moments where the rate of change in health deviation values shifts, reflecting potential changes in the agents' decision-making processes. The dominant oscillation period of 15.00 suggests cyclical patterns in the agents' health perceptions, possibly influenced by social interactions and habit formations. For decision makers, these trends imply that interventions aimed at influencing milk consumption behaviors should consider the initial rapid shift in perceptions and the subsequent stabilization period, where changes occur more gradually. Understanding these dynamics can help in designing strategies that effectively target the different phases of perception and habit formation in consumers.
