The simulation of the agent-based model reveals a clear and consistent downward trend in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents over time. Around the middle time steps, the rate of decline slows down, and there are intervals where the trend is not statistically significant, suggesting periods of stabilization or slower rates of change. Despite these intervals, the overall trajectory remains downward. Towards the later stages of the simulation, the consumption of whole milk continues to decrease, reaching a minimum value of approximately 253.10 units by the end. Throughout the simulation, there are several peaks and valleys, indicating minor fluctuations in the consumption rates, but these do not deviate substantially from the overall declining trend.
