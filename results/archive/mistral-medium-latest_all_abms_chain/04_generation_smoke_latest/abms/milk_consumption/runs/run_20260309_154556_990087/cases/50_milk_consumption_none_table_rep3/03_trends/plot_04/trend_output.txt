The simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent and significant increasing trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the observed time period from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the number of times agents chose these milk types started at zero. The adoption rate increased steadily and significantly throughout the simulation, with notable acceleration in the early time steps. The mean number of choices for skimmed or semi-skimmed milk across the simulation period is approximately 11,157, with a median of around 10,679. The standard deviation of 8,241 indicates considerable variability in the adoption rates over time. The maximum value reached by the end of the simulation is 25,643. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across various segments of the simulation, with significant slopes indicating rapid adoption rates. There are strong oscillations detected towards the latter part of the simulation, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the adoption behavior. The inflection point and change point at the end of the simulation indicate a significant shift in the adoption pattern, marking a stabilization phase after a period of rapid increase.
