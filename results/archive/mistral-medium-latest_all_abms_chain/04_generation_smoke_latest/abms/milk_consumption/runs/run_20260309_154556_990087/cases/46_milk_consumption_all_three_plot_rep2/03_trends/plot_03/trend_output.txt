The total number of whole milk choices starts at zero and increases steadily over the 30 time steps. This linear growth indicates a consistent and predictable pattern in the adoption behavior of whole milk among the agents. The initial increase from zero to approximately 500 choices by the fifth time step suggests a rapid initial uptake. This could be attributed to the strong initial habit formation for whole milk, as indicated by the high Incumbent-Initial-Habit parameter. As the simulation progresses, the number of whole milk choices continues to rise, reaching around 1500 by the fifteenth time step and approaching 2000 by the thirtieth time step. The steady increase throughout the simulation suggests that, despite the influence of other parameters such as social susceptibility and cognitive dissonance, the habit formation and initial preferences play a significant role in the agents' choices. For decision makers, this trend implies that initial habits and perceptions are crucial in shaping long-term consumption patterns. Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions that aim to shift consumption behaviors towards healthier or more sustainable options.
