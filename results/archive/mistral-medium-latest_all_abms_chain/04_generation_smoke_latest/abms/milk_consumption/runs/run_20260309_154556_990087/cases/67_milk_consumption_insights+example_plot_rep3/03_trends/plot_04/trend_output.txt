The total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero and increases gradually over the first 5 steps. This initial slow increase suggests a cautious adoption phase where only a few agents begin to consider and switch to skimmed or semi-skimmed milk. Following this, there is a rapid increase between steps 5 and 15, where the total number of choices rises sharply from approximately 1,000 to around 15,000. This period indicates a significant shift in consumer behavior, likely driven by growing awareness and social influence among the agents. After step 15, the rate of increase starts to slow down, showing a more gradual upward trend until step 30, where it plateaus near 25,000 choices. This deceleration and eventual plateau suggest that the majority of agents who are inclined to switch have already done so, and the market is reaching saturation. The low variance across simulation runs indicates a consistent pattern of adoption behavior among the agents, implying predictable consumer responses to the factors influencing their milk choices. For decision makers, this trend highlights the importance of initial awareness campaigns and social influence in driving the adoption of healthier milk options. The plateau phase suggests that continued efforts might yield diminishing returns, indicating a need for targeted strategies to reach the remaining segment of the population.
