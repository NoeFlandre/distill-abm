By around the 15th time step, the consumption stabilizes at around 1,200 units, suggesting that a substantial portion of the population has integrated skimmed and semi-skimmed milk into their regular consumption habits. Between the 15th and 25th time steps, the consumption remains relatively stable with minor fluctuations, indicating a period of equilibrium where the adoption rate balances out with habitual consumption. After the 25th time step, there is another noticeable increase in consumption, reaching approximately 1,400 units by the end of the simulation period in 2005.
