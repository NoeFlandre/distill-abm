The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several key trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29. This value rapidly increases in the early stages of the simulation, indicating a swift adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the agents. By the early time steps, the consumption quantity sees a significant rise, reaching notable values such as 1265.47 at one of the peaks. 

The trend analysis shows distinct phases in the adoption process. In the initial phase, from time steps 0 to 9, there is a strong and significant increasing trend in the consumption of alternative milk types. This is evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, which indicates a significant upward trend with high tau values and positive slopes. Specifically, the slope values of 112.802 and 139.015 in the early phases highlight the rapid increase in consumption. 

Following this initial surge, the model enters a phase of stabilization. From time steps 10 to 24, the trend analysis shows no significant trend, suggesting that the consumption levels have plateaued. During this period, the consumption values oscillate around a mean value, with several peaks and valleys identified. For instance, peaks are observed at time steps 11, 13, and 21 with values around 1265.47, 1240.35, and 1226.86 respectively. Similarly, valleys are noted at time steps 12, 20, and 23 with values around 1233.62, 1214.46, and 1209.70 respectively. 

Towards the later stages of the simulation, from time steps 25 to 29, there is a resurgence in the increasing trend of alternative milk consumption. This phase is marked by a significant upward trend as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, with a slope of 48.544. The consumption continues to rise, reaching the maximum value of 1456.60 at the final time step of 32. 

The oscillation summary indicates a dominant period of 15 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the consumption behavior of the agents. The strong oscillation indices further support the presence of periodic fluctuations in the consumption values. 

Overall, the simulation captures the dynamic process of milk consumption adoption, highlighting initial rapid adoption, a middle phase of stabilization with periodic fluctuations, and a final phase of renewed increase in consumption.
