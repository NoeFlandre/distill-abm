The total number of times agents chose whole milk starts at zero and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. This steady rise suggests that agents are gradually adopting whole milk over time, potentially influenced by initial habits and social interactions. The trend is characterized by a significant and continuous increase, with the metric reaching its peak at the end of the simulation, indicating a strong cumulative adoption of whole milk. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across all time steps, with significant positive slopes and tau values of 1.000, highlighting a robust and consistent upward movement. The presence of strong oscillations towards the end of the simulation suggests periodic fluctuations in the adoption rate, which could be due to varying social influences or habit reinforcement cycles. For decision makers, this trend implies that interventions aimed at reducing whole milk consumption may need to be sustained and adaptive to counteract the strong cumulative adoption patterns observed.
