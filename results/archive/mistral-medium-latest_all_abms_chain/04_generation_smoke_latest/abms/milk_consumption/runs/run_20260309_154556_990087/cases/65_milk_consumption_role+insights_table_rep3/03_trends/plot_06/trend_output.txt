The simulation results indicate a clear and consistent decline in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents over the observed time period. Initially, the average consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units. This value experiences a significant and steady decrease, reaching a low of 253.10 units by the end of the simulation. The trend analysis reveals several phases of significant decline, particularly notable in the early and middle stages of the simulation. For instance, from the start to around one-fifth of the way through the simulation, there is a sharp decrease in consumption, followed by periods of continued decline interspersed with brief phases of stabilization. Key inflection points and peaks and valleys in the data suggest moments of temporary stabilization or slight recovery in consumption, but these are followed by continued downward trends. The rolling Mann-Kendall test highlights significant decreasing trends in multiple segments, underscoring the overall downward trajectory. For decision makers, this trend suggests a strong shift away from whole milk consumption over time, which could be influenced by the model parameters such as social susceptibility, habit threshold, and initial health perceptions of alternative milk types. This insight may be crucial for stakeholders in the dairy industry to understand the evolving consumer preferences and to strategize accordingly.
