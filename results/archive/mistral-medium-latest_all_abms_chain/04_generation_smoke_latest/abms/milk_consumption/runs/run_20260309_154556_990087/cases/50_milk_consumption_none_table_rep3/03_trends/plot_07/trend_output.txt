The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units. There is a rapid increase in consumption during the early time steps, with significant growth observed up to around the 9th time step, where the mean consumption reaches approximately 1220.21 units. This period is marked by a strong increasing trend, as indicated by the Mann-Kendall test results, which show significant positive slopes and high tau values. Following this rapid increase, the consumption values exhibit a period of stabilization with minor fluctuations. During this middle phase, from around the 10th to the 24th time steps, there is no significant trend, and the consumption values oscillate around a mean of approximately 1220 units. Notable peaks and valleys are observed during this phase, with peaks around the 11th, 13th, and 21st time steps, and valleys around the 12th, 20th, and 23rd time steps. Towards the end of the simulation, there is another increasing trend starting around the 25th time step, leading to a final consumption value of approximately 1456.60 units at the 32nd time step. This final phase indicates a renewed increase in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The dominant period of oscillation is around 15 time steps, suggesting cyclic patterns in the consumption behavior of the agents. Overall, the simulation demonstrates an initial rapid adoption phase, followed by a stabilization period with minor fluctuations, and a final phase of increased consumption.
