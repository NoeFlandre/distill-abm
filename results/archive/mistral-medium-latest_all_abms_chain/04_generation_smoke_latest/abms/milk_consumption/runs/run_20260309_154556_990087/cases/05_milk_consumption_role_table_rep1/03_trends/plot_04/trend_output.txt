Additionally, the oscillation summary reveals a dominant period of 15.00, with strong oscillations detected in the latter part of the time series, indicating periodic fluctuations in the adoption behavior. Overall, the data reflects a clear and sustained increase in the preference for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the agents, driven by the underlying parameters of the model such as social influence, habit formation, and health perceptions.
