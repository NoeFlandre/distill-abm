The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at a value of 1.00 and experiences a rapid increase within the first few time steps, reaching approximately 1.02. This initial rise indicates a quick adjustment period where agents rapidly form habits around whole milk consumption. Following this initial spike, the habit factor continues to increase steadily over the remaining time steps, reaching values slightly above 1.05 by the end of the simulation period. This gradual increase suggests a consistent reinforcement of the habit over time, likely due to repeated consumption and social influence reinforcing the behavior. The variance among the simulation runs is relatively low initially but becomes more pronounced as time progresses, indicating increasing variability in how habits are formed and reinforced among different agents. For decision makers, this trend implies that interventions aimed at changing milk consumption habits need to be robust and sustained, as habits become more entrenched over time. Early interventions may have a higher impact due to the rapid initial habit formation phase.
