The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the habit factor starts at a value of 1.0000 and gradually increases to 1.0502 by the end of the period. The trend is characterized by a steady rise without any significant peaks or valleys, indicating a smooth and continuous change. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across various segments of the time series, with a consistent slope of 0.001 and significant p-values, suggesting a statistically significant upward movement. The presence of inflection points at indices 7 and 12 suggests slight changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 15.00, indicating some periodic fluctuations within the general increasing trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that the habit factor for whole milk consumption has been gradually strengthening over time. This could suggest that despite changes in consumer preferences and the introduction of alternative milk types, the habitual consumption of whole milk has persisted and even slightly increased among the British public. This insight could be crucial for understanding the resilience of whole milk consumption habits and for designing interventions aimed at promoting healthier or more environmentally friendly milk choices.
