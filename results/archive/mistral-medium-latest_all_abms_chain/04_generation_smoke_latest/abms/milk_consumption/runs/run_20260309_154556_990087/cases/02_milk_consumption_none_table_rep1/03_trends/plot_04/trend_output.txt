The simulation results indicate a consistent and significant increasing trend in the total number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk over the entire period from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the adoption of these milk types starts at zero, reflecting the initial conditions of the model where agents presumably began with a preference for whole milk. The adoption rate increases steadily and significantly over time, with notable acceleration in the early years. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals several phases of significant increase, each with a tau value of 1.000, indicating strong upward trends. For instance, from time steps 0 to 4, the slope of increase is approximately 201.463, which accelerates to around 736.583 from time steps 5 to 9, and further to about 927.217 from time steps 10 to 14. This trend continues with similar slopes through subsequent time steps, indicating a robust and continuous rise in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. By the end of the simulation period, the total number of choices for these milk types reaches approximately 25,643.83. The model also identifies a dominant oscillation period of 15 time steps, suggesting some periodic fluctuations in the adoption rates, although the overall trend remains strongly upward. The inflection point at time step 31 and the change point at time step 32 mark significant shifts in the adoption dynamics, potentially indicating a stabilization or maturation phase in the adoption process. This trend reflects the underlying mechanisms of the model, where agents' choices are influenced by health perceptions, social interactions, and habit formation, leading to a gradual but steady shift towards healthier milk options over the simulated period.
