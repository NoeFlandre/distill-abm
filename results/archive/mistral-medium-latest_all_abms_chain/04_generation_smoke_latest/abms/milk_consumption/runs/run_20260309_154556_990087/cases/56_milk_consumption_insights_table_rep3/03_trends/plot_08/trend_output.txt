The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005. Starting at an initial value of 1.0000, the factor gradually rises to 1.0502 by the end of the period. The trend is characterized by a steady increase, with no significant peaks or valleys detected throughout the time series. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a continuous increasing trend across all time windows, with a consistent slope of 0.001 and significant p-values, suggesting a persistent upward movement in the habit factor for whole milk. The dominant period of oscillation is 15.00, indicating some periodic fluctuations within the overall increasing trend. The implications for decision makers suggest that the habit factor for whole milk consumption has been gradually increasing over the years, which could indicate a strengthening habit formation among consumers despite potential shifts towards other milk types. This trend might be useful for understanding consumer behavior and designing interventions or marketing strategies.
