The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units and experiences a gradual decline. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, with small peaks and valleys around the initial values. For instance, slight increases are observed at indices 2 and 55, with values of approximately 1989.925 and 1263.300 units, respectively. However, these are followed by immediate decreases, indicating an overall downward trajectory. 

As the simulation progresses, the rate of energy decline becomes more pronounced. By index 394, the energy level drops to around -2005.950 units, marking a significant decrease from the initial value. This downward trend continues steadily, with notable energy levels at indices 1594 and 1826, where the values are approximately -23807.000 and -28403.000 units, respectively. The most substantial declines are observed towards the end of the simulation, with the energy level reaching its lowest point of -38233.000 units at index 2310. 

The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decrease, with all rolling Mann-Kendall tests indicating significant downward trends across different segments of the simulation. The change points identified at indices 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 further emphasize the continuous decline in energy levels. The oscillation summary suggests a dominant period of 63 ticks, but the strong oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation do not alter the overall decreasing trend. 

For decision-makers, these trends imply that the energy dynamics of forager bands are heavily influenced by the model's parameters, leading to a continuous depletion of energy resources over time. This could suggest that under the given conditions, forager bands face significant challenges in maintaining their energy levels, potentially leading to critical implications for their survival and hunting success.
