The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the number of postpartum females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the cumulative count reaches approximately 100. Following this initial phase, the rate of increase slows down significantly, indicating a deceleration in the hunting and killing of postpartum females. By around 1000 steps, the cumulative count stabilizes around 117, with minor fluctuations observed thereafter. The data shows that the maximum value reached is 119, which occurs around the 1826th step. The mean and median values of the cumulative count are approximately 102.44 and 116.86, respectively, indicating that the values tend to cluster around the upper limit of the observed range. The statistical analysis reveals several change points and inflection indices, suggesting shifts in the dynamics of the system. Notably, the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early phases, transitioning to periods with no significant trend and eventually to a slight decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation. The oscillation analysis identifies a dominant period of 63 steps, with strong oscillations observed towards the latter part of the simulation. Overall, the dynamics suggest an initial rapid exploitation phase followed by a stabilization period with minor fluctuations in the number of postpartum females killed.
