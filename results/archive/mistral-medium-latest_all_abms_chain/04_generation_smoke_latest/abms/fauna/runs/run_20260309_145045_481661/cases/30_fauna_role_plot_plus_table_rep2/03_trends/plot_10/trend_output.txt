The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear trend of decreasing total number of animals over time. Initially, the number of animals starts at approximately 166.67 and exhibits a steep decline within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 steps, the number of animals has significantly reduced, continuing a downward trend with some fluctuations. The mean number of animals across the simulation is approximately 25.01, with a median of 1.48, indicating that the majority of the time steps have a very low number of animals. The standard deviation of 44.90 and variance of 2015.71 suggest considerable variability in the number of animals at different time steps. The minimum number of animals reaches zero by the end of the simulation, indicating complete extinction in some scenarios. Peaks and valleys in the data suggest minor temporary recoveries and subsequent declines in the animal population, but these are not sustained. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant decreasing trends throughout most of the simulation, with brief periods of non-significant changes or slight increases. Change points identified at 125, 475, 625, 875, and 2310 steps mark significant shifts in the rate of decline. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 steps, indicating cyclical patterns within the data, though the overall trend remains a decline towards extinction.
