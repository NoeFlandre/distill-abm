The number of foragers remains constant throughout the simulation, starting and ending at a value of 20. This stability indicates that the population of human hunter-gatherers is neither increasing nor decreasing over time. For decision-makers, this suggests a steady-state scenario where the factors influencing the number of foragers, such as birth rates, mortality rates, and possibly migration, are balanced. Understanding this stability is crucial for assessing the long-term sustainability of the hunter-gatherer lifestyle within the given environmental and social parameters.
