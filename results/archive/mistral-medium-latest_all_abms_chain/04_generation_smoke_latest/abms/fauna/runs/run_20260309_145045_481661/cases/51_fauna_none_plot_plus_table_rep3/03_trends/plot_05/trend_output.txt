The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of postpartum females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the rate of increase begins to slow down. By approximately 1000 steps, the cumulative number of postpartum females killed reaches around 117, which is close to the maximum value observed. After this point, the trend stabilizes, and the number of postpartum females killed fluctuates slightly around this maximum value, indicating a plateau. The statistical analysis shows that the mean number of postpartum females killed is approximately 102.44, with a median of 116.86, suggesting that the data is slightly skewed towards lower values. The standard deviation is around 30, indicating some variability in the number of postpartum females killed across different simulations. The minimum value remains at zero, while the maximum value reaches 119. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation, with a shift to no significant trend and a slight decreasing trend towards the end. Change points are identified around steps 475, 625, 850, and 2310, marking shifts in the trend dynamics. The oscillation summary suggests a dominant period of 63 steps, with strong oscillations observed towards the end of the simulation series.
