The total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This initial energy level rapidly decreases within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has dropped significantly to approximately 4,000 units. This downward trend continues, albeit at a slower rate, reaching around 2,000 units by 1,000 time steps. The decline persists, and by 1,500 time steps, the total energy of male animals approaches near-zero levels, indicating a substantial reduction in the energy available within the male animal population. Throughout the simulation, the energy levels exhibit minor fluctuations but maintain an overall decreasing trend until stabilizing near zero. The statistical analysis reveals several key points of inflection and change points, indicating shifts in the rate of energy decline. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms significant decreasing trends in various segments of the time series, with occasional brief periods of increase or stabilization. The dominant oscillation period is identified as 63 time steps, suggesting periodic fluctuations within the overall declining trend.
