The number of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at 125 and exhibits a consistent decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the decline is steep, with the number of male animals dropping rapidly. This trend continues, albeit at a slower rate, as the simulation progresses. By around 2,284 ticks, the number of male animals reaches zero and remains there for the rest of the simulation. The statistical analysis reveals several key points and inflections, indicating periods where the rate of decline changes. Notably, there are significant decreasing trends in the early phases of the simulation, followed by periods of less pronounced decline and brief intervals of no significant trend. The oscillation analysis indicates periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of approximately 63 ticks, suggesting some cyclical behavior in the decline pattern. For decision makers, these trends highlight the rapid initial impact on male animal populations under the given model parameters, followed by a gradual stabilization towards extinction. This information can be crucial for understanding the dynamics of hunting pressure and its long-term effects on prey populations.
