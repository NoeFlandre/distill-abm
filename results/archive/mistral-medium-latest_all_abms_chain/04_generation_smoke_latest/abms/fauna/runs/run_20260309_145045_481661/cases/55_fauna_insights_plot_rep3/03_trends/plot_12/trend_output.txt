The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid decline initially, followed by a more gradual decrease over time. At the start of the simulation, the total energy is approximately 12,000 units. Within the first 500 steps, there is a steep drop to around 2,000 units. This sharp decline continues more gradually until about 1,000 steps, where the total energy stabilizes around 500 units. Beyond 1,000 steps, the energy levels continue to decrease at a slower rate, eventually approaching near-zero values by around 2,000 steps. This trend suggests that the female animals' energy reserves are being depleted quickly at first, likely due to high initial hunting pressure and environmental factors. The gradual stabilization indicates a possible equilibrium where the remaining female animals are either less accessible to hunters or have adapted to the environmental conditions. For decision-makers, this implies that initial conditions and early interventions are critical in influencing the long-term survival and energy reserves of female animals in the model.
