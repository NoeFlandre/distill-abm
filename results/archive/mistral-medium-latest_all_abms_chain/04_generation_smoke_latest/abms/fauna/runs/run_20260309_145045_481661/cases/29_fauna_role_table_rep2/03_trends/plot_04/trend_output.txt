The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear trend over the simulation period. Initially, the count starts at zero and shows a rapid increase. This increasing trend is statistically significant and consistent across several time intervals, with the rate of increase being most pronounced early in the simulation. Specifically, from the beginning up to around the 1159th tick, the trend is strongly increasing, with the slope of the increase being steepest in the earliest phases (ticks 0-463) and gradually becoming less steep over time. The mean value of the cumulative count is approximately 104.56, with a median of 119.06, indicating that the data is somewhat skewed towards lower values. The maximum value reached is 123.67 around the 1826th tick, after which the trend starts to stabilize. From tick 1856 onwards, there is a slight but significant decreasing trend, suggesting a minor decline in the cumulative count of hunted females towards the end of the simulation. The data also shows some periodic oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks, particularly noticeable towards the latter part of the simulation. These oscillations, however, do not significantly deviate from the overall stabilized cumulative count, which hovers around the maximum value observed. The inflection points and change points identified in the data further corroborate the phases of rapid increase, stabilization, and slight decline in the cumulative count of hunted female prey.
