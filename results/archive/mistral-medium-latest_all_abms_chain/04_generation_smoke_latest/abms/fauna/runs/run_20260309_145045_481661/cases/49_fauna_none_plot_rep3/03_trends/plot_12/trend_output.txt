The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid decline from an initial value of approximately 12,000 units. This sharp decrease occurs within the first 500 steps, where the total energy drops to around 2,000 units. Following this steep decline, the rate of decrease slows significantly, and the total energy continues to diminish more gradually. By around 1,000 steps, the total energy of female animals stabilizes near zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy reserves in the population. This trend suggests a rapid initial impact on the female animal population, followed by a prolonged period of low energy levels.
