The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the rate of increase begins to slow down. By approximately 1000 steps, the cumulative number of females killed reaches around 120, indicating a significant hunting impact early in the simulation. After this point, the rate of increase further diminishes, and the curve starts to plateau, suggesting a stabilization in the number of females killed. The cumulative number of females killed eventually stabilizes around 123, with minor fluctuations observed in the later stages of the simulation. Key time steps highlight the rapid initial increase, the slowing rate around 500 steps, and the plateauing effect after 1000 steps. The statistical analysis indicates significant increasing trends in the early phases, followed by a period of stabilization and minor oscillations towards the end of the simulation.
