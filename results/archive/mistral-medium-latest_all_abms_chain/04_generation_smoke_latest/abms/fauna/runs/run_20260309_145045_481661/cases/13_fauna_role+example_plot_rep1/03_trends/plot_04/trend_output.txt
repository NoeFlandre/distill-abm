The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands starts at zero and remains constant for a brief period. This initial period of inactivity is followed by a rapid increase in the number of females killed, reaching approximately 100 by around 500 time steps. This sharp rise indicates an initial phase where hunting pressure is significantly impacting the female prey population. After this rapid increase, the rate of females killed begins to plateau, stabilizing around 120 females killed. This plateau suggests that the hunting pressure reaches a point where the remaining female prey population is either more challenging to hunt or has reached a level of equilibrium with the hunting activities. The simulations show low variability during the initial phase, but as time progresses, slight deviations become more noticeable, indicating varying dynamics in hunting success and prey availability across different simulation runs.
