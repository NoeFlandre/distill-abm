The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy starts at 2000 units. This value rapidly declines, reaching approximately -2000 units by around 400 steps. The decline continues steadily, with the total energy dropping to about -15000 units by 1000 steps and further decreasing to around -25000 units by 1500 steps. By the end of the simulation at 2312 steps, the total energy of foragers reaches approximately -38229 units. The trend analysis indicates a continuous and significant decrease in energy levels, with no periods of recovery or stabilization observed. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this decreasing trend across all segments of the simulation, with significant negative slopes and tau values close to -1, indicating a strong and consistent downward trend. The implications for decision-makers suggest that under the given parameters, the energy status of foragers deteriorates continuously, which could lead to potential challenges in sustaining hunting activities and overall survival in the long term.
