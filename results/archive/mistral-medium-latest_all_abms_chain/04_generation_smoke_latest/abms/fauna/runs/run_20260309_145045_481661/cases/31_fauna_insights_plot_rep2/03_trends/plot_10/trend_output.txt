The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations show a consistent trend in the total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the total number of animals starts at approximately 250. Within the first 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of animals, dropping to around 100. This sharp decrease continues more gradually until about 1000 steps, where the number of animals stabilizes around 20 to 50. Beyond 1000 steps, the decline becomes more gradual, and the number of animals continues to decrease slowly, approaching near-zero levels by around 2000 steps. For the remainder of the simulation, the number of animals remains very low, close to zero. This trend suggests that under the given parameters, the prey population experiences a rapid initial decline followed by a slower decrease until near extinction. For decision-makers, this indicates that the current settings of the model lead to a significant reduction in prey population over time, which could be critical for understanding the factors contributing to megaherbivore extinction and evaluating the impact of different parameters on prey survival.
