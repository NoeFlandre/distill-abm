The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, with slight increases and decreases in energy levels. For instance, there is a small peak at around 1263.3 units and a subsequent valley at approximately 1256.9 units. However, these initial variations are relatively minor compared to the overall trend. As the simulation progresses, the energy levels begin to decline more steeply. By around the midpoint of the simulation, the energy levels have dropped significantly, reaching values such as -23807 units. This downward trend continues, with the energy levels plummeting further to -38233 units by the end of the simulation. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms this consistent decrease, with significant negative slopes and tau values close to -1 across all analyzed intervals. The change points identified at various intervals (e.g., 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, 2310) indicate moments where the rate of decline shifts, but the overall direction remains consistently downward. The oscillation summary suggests some periodic fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the dominant decreasing trend. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 ticks, but these oscillations do not significantly alter the overall downward trajectory of the energy levels.
