The number of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent declining trend across all simulations. Initially, the number of male animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of male animals drops to roughly 80. The decline continues, and by 1000 steps, the number of male animals further reduces to about 20. Beyond 1000 steps, the number of male animals continues to decrease gradually, approaching near extinction levels by around 2000 steps. This trend suggests a significant pressure on the male animal population, likely due to a combination of hunting pressure and environmental factors. For decision-makers, this indicates that under the current model parameters, the male animal population is not sustainable in the long term, highlighting the need for potential interventions or adjustments in hunting practices or environmental management to ensure the survival of the species.
