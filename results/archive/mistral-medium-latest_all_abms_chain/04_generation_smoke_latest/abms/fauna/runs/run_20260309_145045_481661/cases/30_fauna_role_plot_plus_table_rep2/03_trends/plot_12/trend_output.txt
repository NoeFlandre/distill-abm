The total energy of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has dropped significantly to approximately 2,000 units. The decline continues more gradually after this point, with the total energy approaching zero around 2,000 time steps and remaining there for the rest of the simulation. Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks and valleys, but the overall trend remains a steady decrease in total energy. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various segments of the time series. The dominant period of oscillation is relatively short, indicating frequent but small fluctuations around the overall decreasing trend. The change points identified further support the phases of rapid decline followed by a more gradual decrease in total energy.
