The combined total energy available in the population of female prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12500 units. This high energy level rapidly declines in the early stages of the simulation. Throughout the simulation, the energy levels continue to decrease, albeit at varying rates. Notable change points occur around time steps 375, 500, 625, and 875, where the rate of decline shifts. The most significant drop is observed towards the end of the simulation, culminating in a complete depletion of energy to 0 units by the final time step of 2311. The data also shows some minor oscillations with a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps, indicating periodic fluctuations in the energy levels. However, these oscillations do not significantly alter the overall decreasing trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms the significant decreasing trend across various segments of the simulation, with only brief periods of slight increase or stabilization, particularly around time steps 1160-1391 and 1856-2087. Despite these minor fluctuations, the overarching trend remains a steady decline in the total energy available in the population of female prey animals.
