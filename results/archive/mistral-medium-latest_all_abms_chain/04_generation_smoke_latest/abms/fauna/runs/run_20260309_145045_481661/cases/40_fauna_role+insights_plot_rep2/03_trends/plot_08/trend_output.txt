The number of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent declining trend across all simulations. Initially, the number of male animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of male animals drops to about 80. The decline continues, and by approximately 1000 steps, the number of male animals further reduces to around 20. Beyond 1000 steps, the number of male animals continues to decrease, approaching near extinction levels close to zero by around 2000 steps. This trend suggests a significant pressure on the male animal population, likely due to a combination of hunting pressure, environmental factors, and demographic dynamics. For decision makers, this trend indicates that under the current model parameters, the male animal population is not sustainable in the long term. This insight could inform conservation strategies, hunting regulations, and environmental management practices aimed at preserving megaherbivore populations.
