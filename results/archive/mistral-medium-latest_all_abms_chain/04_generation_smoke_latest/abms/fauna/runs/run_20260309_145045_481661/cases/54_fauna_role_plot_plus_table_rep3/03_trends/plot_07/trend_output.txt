The cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the number of deaths starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the cumulative deaths reach approximately 150. Following this initial surge, the rate of increase in the number of deaths begins to slow down, indicating a deceleration in the mortality rate. By around 1000 steps, the cumulative deaths stabilize at approximately 230, marking a plateau in the trend. This plateau continues with minor fluctuations, maintaining a relatively constant number of deaths until the end of the simulation at 2312 steps, where the cumulative deaths are around 235. The statistical analysis reveals several key points of inflection and change points, notably around 375, 500, and 625 steps, which correspond to shifts in the rate of increase in deaths. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early phases of the simulation, followed by periods of no significant trend and a slight decreasing trend towards the end. The oscillation analysis suggests a dominant periodicity of 63 steps, indicating regular fluctuations in the number of deaths around the plateau phase. Overall, the dynamics of the cumulative number of deaths reflect an initial rapid increase, a subsequent deceleration, and a final stabilization with minor oscillations.
