The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at zero and exhibits a consistent increasing trend over time. The initial phase shows a rapid increase, reaching approximately 119 by around 1,850 time steps. This rapid rise suggests an initial abundance of female prey and high hunting activity by foragers. Following this, the rate of increase slows down, indicating a potential reduction in the availability of female prey or a stabilization in hunting success. The cumulative count peaks at around 123.67 at approximately 1,851 time steps, after which it shows minor fluctuations but generally stabilizes. The minor oscillations observed towards the end of the simulation suggest periodic variations in hunting success or prey availability, possibly due to environmental factors or changes in prey behavior. The overall trend indicates that while hunting pressure remains consistent, the availability of female prey may decrease over time, leading to a plateau in the cumulative count. For decision-makers, this trend highlights the importance of considering long-term sustainability in hunting practices and the potential need for conservation measures to maintain prey populations.
