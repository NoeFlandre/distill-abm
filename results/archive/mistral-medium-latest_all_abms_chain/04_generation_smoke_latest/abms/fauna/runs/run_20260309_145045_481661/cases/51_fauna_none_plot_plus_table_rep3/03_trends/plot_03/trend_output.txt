The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, there is a rapid increase in the number of males killed, starting from zero and rising steeply. This phase of rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the number of males killed reaches approximately 60. Following this, the rate of increase begins to slow down, and by around 1000 steps, the number of males killed stabilizes around 116. This stabilization indicates a plateau where the cumulative number of males killed remains relatively constant, fluctuating slightly around this value. The data shows minor oscillations and inflection points, but the overall trend remains stable with no significant increases or decreases. The statistical analysis confirms these observations, with significant increasing trends detected in the early phases and no significant trends or a slight decreasing trend in the later phases. The model parameters and environmental conditions contribute to these dynamics, reflecting the interactions between human hunting behavior, prey demographics, and environmental factors.
