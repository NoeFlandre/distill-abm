The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy starts at a value of 2000 units. This value rapidly declines, showing a steep negative slope from the very beginning. By around 2310 ticks, the total energy reaches its minimum value of approximately -38233 units. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a continuous and significant decrease in energy levels across all segments of the simulation, with no periods of increase or stabilization. Key change points identified at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 indicate moments where the rate of energy decline shifts, but the overall direction remains consistently downward. The oscillation summary suggests some periodic fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the overall decreasing trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, the energy status of foragers deteriorates continuously, which could lead to potential extinction scenarios or significant behavioral changes in the forager population. The consistent decline in energy suggests that the current hunting and movement costs, along with the energy gains from prey, are not sustainable over the long term.
