By the 1160th tick, the rate of increase starts to slow down, indicating a shift towards stabilization. Following this plateau, there is a brief period of slight increase before a slight decrease is observed from the 1856th tick onwards. The overall pattern suggests that the cumulative count of hunted postpartum females reaches a near-equilibrium state after an initial period of rapid increase. The dominant oscillation period identified in the data is relatively short, indicating minor fluctuations around the stabilized value.
