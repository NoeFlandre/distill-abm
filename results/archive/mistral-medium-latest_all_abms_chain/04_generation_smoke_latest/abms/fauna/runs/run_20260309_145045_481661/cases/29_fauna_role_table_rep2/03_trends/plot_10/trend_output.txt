The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear overall decreasing trend in the total number of prey animals over time, starting from an initial value of approximately 166.67 and declining to zero by the end of the simulation. The decline is steep initially, with significant drops in the number of animals within the first few time steps. The mean number of animals throughout the simulation is approximately 25, with a median of about 1.48, indicating that for the majority of the simulation, the number of animals is quite low. The standard deviation of 44.90 and variance of 2015.71 suggest substantial variability in animal numbers across different time steps. The minimum value of zero animals is reached at the final time step, while the maximum value of approximately 166.67 occurs at the beginning. Several peaks and valleys are observed at various time steps, but these fluctuations are relatively minor compared to the overall downward trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant decreasing trends in multiple segments of the simulation, with only a few segments showing increasing trends or stability. Change points are identified at time steps 125, 475, 625, 875, and 2310, marking significant shifts in the rate of decline. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 time steps, with strong oscillations observed at regular intervals. Despite these oscillations, the overarching trend remains a steady decline in the prey population, ultimately leading to extinction.
