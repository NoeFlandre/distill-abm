The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the cumulative count reaches approximately 80 females. Following this, the rate of increase begins to slow down, and the cumulative count gradually approaches an asymptote. By around 1000 steps, the cumulative number of females killed stabilizes around 120, indicating a plateau in the hunting activity. This plateau persists with minor fluctuations, maintaining a relatively constant number of females killed until the end of the simulation at 2000 steps. The data shows that the initial phase of rapid increase is followed by a period of stabilization, suggesting that the hunting pressure reaches a balance with the available prey population or other limiting factors in the model.
