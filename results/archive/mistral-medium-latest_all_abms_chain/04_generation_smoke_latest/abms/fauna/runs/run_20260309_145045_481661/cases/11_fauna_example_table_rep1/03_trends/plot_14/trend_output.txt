The percentage of landscape patches that are vegetated begins at approximately 33% and exhibits minor fluctuations throughout the simulation period. The initial value slightly decreases to around 24.91% at the 365th time step, which could be attributed to the initial grazing pressure from the prey animals. Following this decline, the percentage of vegetated patches gradually recovers and stabilizes around the initial value of 33%. The simulation shows several minor peaks and valleys, indicating small-scale oscillations in the vegetation cover. These oscillations have a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the vegetation regrowth and grazing dynamics. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates several phases of increasing and decreasing trends, with significant changes in the vegetation cover. Notably, there are increasing trends in the early phases of the simulation, followed by a shift to decreasing trends around the 1624th time step. The change points identified at various time steps further highlight the dynamic nature of the vegetation cover over the simulation period. Overall, the vegetation cover remains relatively stable with minor fluctuations, reflecting the balance between grazing by prey animals and the regrowth of grass.
