The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid increase initially, followed by a gradual leveling off. At the beginning of the simulation, the number of females killed starts at zero. Within the first 500 steps, there is a steep rise in the number of females killed, reaching approximately 100. This rapid increase continues until around 1000 steps, where the cumulative number of females killed stabilizes around 120. Beyond this point, the rate of increase slows significantly, and the number of females killed remains relatively constant, fluctuating slightly around the 120 mark for the remainder of the simulation. This trend suggests an initial period of high hunting activity followed by a stabilization phase where the rate of hunting plateaus.
