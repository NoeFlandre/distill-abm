The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12500 units. This high initial value indicates a large population of female animals with substantial energy reserves at the beginning of the simulation. The energy levels drop sharply in the early stages, with a significant decline observed within the first few hundred time steps. This rapid decrease suggests a high rate of energy consumption or loss among the female animals, potentially due to factors such as hunting pressure, environmental changes, or high metabolic costs. As the simulation progresses, the rate of decline in total energy slows down, but the overall trend remains downward. By the end of the simulation, the total energy of female animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy reserves within the population. This trend implies that the female animals are facing significant challenges in maintaining their energy levels, which could be due to a combination of high processing costs, limited food availability, and other environmental stressors. For decision-makers, this trend highlights the critical need to understand and mitigate the factors contributing to the rapid depletion of energy reserves in female animals to prevent potential extinction scenarios.
