The simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the total number of animals starts at 250, which is the set value for the initial number of prey. In the early stages of the simulation, up to around 500 steps, there is a gradual decline in the number of animals. This decline continues more steeply until approximately 1000 steps, where the number of animals drops to around 50 or fewer. Beyond this point, the number of animals continues to decrease but at a slower rate, eventually approaching zero around 2000 steps and remaining there for the rest of the simulation. This trend suggests that under the given parameters, the prey population experiences a significant decline and ultimately faces extinction within the simulated timeframe. For decision-makers, this indicates that the current set of parameters leads to a scenario where the prey population is not sustainable, highlighting the need for potential adjustments in parameters related to hunting pressure, environmental factors, or animal demographics to achieve a more balanced ecosystem.
