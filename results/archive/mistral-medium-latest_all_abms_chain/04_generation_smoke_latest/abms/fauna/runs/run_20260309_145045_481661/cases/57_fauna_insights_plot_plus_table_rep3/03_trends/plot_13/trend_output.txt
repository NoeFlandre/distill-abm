The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high initial energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred steps, indicating a sharp drop in the energy reserves of the prey animals. By around 500 steps, the total energy has decreased significantly to roughly 4,000 units. This rapid decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, until around 1,000 steps, where the total energy stabilizes at lower values, fluctuating slightly but generally maintaining a downward trajectory. By the end of the simulation at 2,312 steps, the total energy of all animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy reserves among the prey population. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various segments of the simulation. The presence of multiple change points and inflection indices further supports the observation of a consistently declining trend with occasional minor fluctuations. For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the prey population's energy reserves are rapidly and consistently depleted over time, which could lead to potential extinction or severe reduction in the prey population if the conditions remain unchanged.
