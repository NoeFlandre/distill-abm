The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 and exhibits a steady decrease throughout the simulation period. The trend is predominantly decreasing, as evidenced by multiple significant decreasing trends in the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis. Key time steps indicate notable changes, such as the early rapid decline observed in the first few hundred ticks, followed by a more gradual decrease. Peaks and valleys in the data suggest minor fluctuations, but the overall trajectory remains downward. The population eventually reaches zero by the end of the simulation period at tick 73000. The analysis also identifies several change points and inflection indices, indicating shifts in the rate of decline. Despite some periods of slight increase, the dominant trend is a significant and consistent reduction in the female prey population.
