The total energy of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and rapid decline over time. Initially, the total energy is around 12,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period, indicating a stable and abundant energy state among the male animals at the start of the simulation. However, after approximately 100 steps, the total energy begins to decrease sharply. By around 500 steps, the total energy drops to about 2,000 units, marking a significant reduction. This steep decline continues, and by 1,000 steps, the total energy levels off near zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy among the male animals. The trend suggests a rapid and sustained reduction in the energy reserves of male animals over the course of the simulation. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters and conditions, the male animal population experiences a severe and swift decline in energy, which could be critical for understanding the dynamics of megaherbivore extinction and the impact of various factors such as hunting pressure and environmental changes.
