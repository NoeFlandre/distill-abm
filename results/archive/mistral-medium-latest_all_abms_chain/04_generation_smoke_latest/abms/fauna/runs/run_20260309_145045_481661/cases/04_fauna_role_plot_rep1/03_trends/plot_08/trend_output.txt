The data from the repeated simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of male animals over time. Initially, the number of male animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of male animals has decreased to about 80. The decline continues, and by approximately 1000 steps, the number of male animals drops to around 20. Beyond this point, the number of male animals continues to decrease gradually, approaching zero around 2000 steps. This trend indicates a significant reduction in the male animal population over time, with the most substantial decline occurring within the first 1000 steps. The simulations suggest that under the given parameters, the male animal population experiences a rapid decline followed by a more gradual reduction, eventually leading to near extinction.
