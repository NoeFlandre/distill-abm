The total energy of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12500 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few time steps, indicating a sharp drop in the energy reserves of male animals. By around 500 time steps, the energy levels have decreased significantly to approximately 4000 units. This rapid decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, and by around 1000 time steps, the energy levels are further reduced to about 2000 units. The trend continues with the energy levels gradually approaching zero as the simulation progresses towards 2000 time steps. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes and tau values across different time windows, indicating a consistent and significant decline in energy levels. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points suggests periods where the rate of decline varies, but the overall trend remains downward. The oscillation summary indicates some periodic fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the overall decreasing trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given model parameters, the energy reserves of male animals are consistently depleted over time, which could be critical for understanding the dynamics of prey populations under hunting pressure and environmental conditions.
