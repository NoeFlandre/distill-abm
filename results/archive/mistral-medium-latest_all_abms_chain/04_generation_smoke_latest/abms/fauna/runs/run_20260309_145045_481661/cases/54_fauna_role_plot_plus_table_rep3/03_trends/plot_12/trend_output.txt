The total energy of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy of female animals drops significantly to approximately 2,000 units. This steep decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, until around 1,000 time steps, where the total energy stabilizes to lower values, generally under 1,000 units. Beyond 1,000 time steps, the energy levels continue to decrease gradually, approaching near-zero values as the simulation progresses towards 2,312 time steps. The statistical analysis indicates a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation, with no periods of increase or recovery in the total energy levels of female animals. The dominant periodic oscillations observed are relatively minor compared to the overall downward trend.
