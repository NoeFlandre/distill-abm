The total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent declining trend across all simulations. Initially, the total energy of male animals starts at approximately 12,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period, suggesting a stable initial population with ample resources. Around 500 steps, the total energy begins to decline sharply, indicating a significant reduction in the male animal population or their energy reserves. By approximately 1,000 steps, the total energy drops to around 2,000 units, marking a critical phase where the population experiences substantial stress. Beyond 1,000 steps, the decline continues but at a slower rate, eventually approaching near-zero levels around 2,000 steps. This trend suggests a gradual extinction or severe depletion of male animals over time. For decision-makers, this trend highlights the potential impact of hunting pressure and environmental factors on the sustainability of male animal populations. It underscores the need for careful management of hunting practices and environmental resources to prevent the collapse of animal populations.
