The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the count starts at zero and shows a rapid increase in the early time steps. After this initial phase, the rate of increase slows down, and the cumulative count stabilizes around the value of 117. This stabilization suggests a balance or equilibrium in the hunting dynamics, where the number of postpartum females hunted plateaus. Towards the end of the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, but the overall trend remains relatively stable with slight oscillations. The dominant period of these oscillations is around 63 ticks, indicating a cyclical pattern in the hunting dynamics. The key time steps highlight significant changes in the trend, particularly around ticks 475, 625, and 850, where the rate of increase shifts.
