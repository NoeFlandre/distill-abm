The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands exhibits a rapid increase initially, followed by a gradual leveling off. This rapid increase continues until around 1000 steps, where the cumulative number of females killed stabilizes around 120. Beyond this point, the rate of increase slows significantly, and the cumulative number of females killed plateaus, indicating that the rate at which females are being killed has decreased and is approaching an equilibrium. This trend suggests that early in the simulation, hunting pressure is high, but as time progresses, the rate of hunting stabilizes, potentially due to a balance being reached between the number of hunters and the availability of female prey.
