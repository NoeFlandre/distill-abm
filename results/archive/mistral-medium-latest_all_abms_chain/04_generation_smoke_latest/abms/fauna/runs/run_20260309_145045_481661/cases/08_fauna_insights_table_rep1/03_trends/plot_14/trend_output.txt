The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits relatively stable dynamics over the simulation period. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains close to this value throughout the simulation, ending at around 32.998%. The mean percentage of vegetated patches is approximately 32.656%, with a median of 32.970%, indicating a slight skew in the data distribution. The standard deviation of 0.950 and variance of 0.903 suggest low variability in the percentage of green patches over time. The minimum value observed is 24.911% at time step 365, which is notably lower than the initial value, indicating a temporary reduction in vegetated patches. The data series shows several peaks and valleys, but these fluctuations are minimal, with prominence values close to zero, suggesting minor oscillations around the mean. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates several periods of increasing and decreasing trends, but these changes are not substantial. The dominant oscillation period is 63 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern in the vegetation coverage. For decision makers, the stability in vegetation coverage suggests a resilient grassland environment under the given model parameters. The slight fluctuations and temporary reductions in vegetated patches may indicate periods of higher grazing pressure or environmental stress, but the overall stability implies a balanced interaction between the prey animals and their food resources.
