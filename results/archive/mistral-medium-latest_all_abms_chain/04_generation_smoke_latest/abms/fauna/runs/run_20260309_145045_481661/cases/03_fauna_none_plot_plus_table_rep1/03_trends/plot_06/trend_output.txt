The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct phases over time. Initially, there is a period of no births, followed by a gradual increase starting around 725 time steps. This increase accelerates significantly, reaching a peak around 1702 time steps with a value of 5.6 births. Following this peak, there is a noticeable decline in the number of births, which stabilizes around 1850 time steps. After this stabilization, the number of births fluctuates slightly but remains relatively constant until the end of the simulation. Key change points in the trend occur at approximately 725, 1325, 1850, 2050, and 2310 time steps, indicating shifts in the dynamics of the birth rate. The model shows periods of significant increase in births followed by a decrease and eventual stabilization, reflecting the complex interactions within the socio-natural system being simulated.
