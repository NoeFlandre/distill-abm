The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear trend of decreasing total number of animals over time. Initially, the number of animals starts at approximately 166.67 and exhibits a rapid decline within the first few hundred steps. By around 500 steps, the number of animals has significantly reduced, continuing a downward trend with some fluctuations. The rate of decline slows down after approximately 1000 steps, with the number of animals stabilizing at very low values close to zero by around 2000 steps. Throughout the simulation, there are minor peaks and valleys, indicating small temporary increases and decreases in the animal population, but the overall trend remains a steady decline. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various segments of the simulation. The dominant period of oscillation is around 63 steps, suggesting some cyclical patterns in the data, but these oscillations do not alter the overall declining trend. The simulation effectively reaches near-zero animal population by the end, indicating a scenario where the megaherbivores are driven to extinction under the given parameters and conditions.
