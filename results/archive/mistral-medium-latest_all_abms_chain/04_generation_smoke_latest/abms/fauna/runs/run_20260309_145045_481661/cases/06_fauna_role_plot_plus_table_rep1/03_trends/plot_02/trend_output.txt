The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy starts at 2000 units. Over time, this value steadily declines, reaching approximately -38229 units by the end of the simulation. The trend is characterized by a continuous and significant decrease in energy levels, with no periods of recovery or increase. Key time steps highlight this downward trajectory, with notable values at specific indices such as -2005.95 at index 394, -23807 at index 1594, and -28403 at index 1826. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend, with significant negative slopes and tau values across all rolling Mann-Kendall tests. The change points and inflection indices further emphasize the consistent decline in energy levels. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 units, but the overall trend remains a steady decrease in total energy of foragers, reflecting the ongoing energy expenditure and lack of sufficient energy gain to sustain the foragers' energy levels.
