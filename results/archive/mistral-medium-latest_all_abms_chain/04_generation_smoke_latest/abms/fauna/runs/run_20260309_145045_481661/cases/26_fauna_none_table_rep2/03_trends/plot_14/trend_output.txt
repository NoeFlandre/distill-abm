The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows relatively stable dynamics in the percentage of vegetated landscape patches over time. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33%. Throughout the simulation, this value exhibits minor fluctuations but generally remains close to the initial value. The minimum percentage of vegetated patches occurs at tick 365, with a value of approximately 24.91%, indicating a slight decline from the initial state. Following this decline, the percentage of vegetated patches gradually increases and stabilizes around the initial value. The mean percentage of vegetated patches over the entire simulation period is approximately 32.66%, with a median value of 32.97%, suggesting a slight skew towards lower values. The standard deviation of 0.95 indicates minor variability around the mean. The simulation data shows several peaks and valleys, but these are relatively minor and do not represent significant deviations from the overall trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates periods of both increasing and decreasing trends in the percentage of vegetated patches, but these changes are minimal and not substantial. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the vegetation dynamics. Overall, the percentage of vegetated patches in the landscape remains relatively stable throughout the simulation, with minor fluctuations and a slight initial decline followed by a gradual return to the initial percentage.
