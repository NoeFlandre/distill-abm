The total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and rapid decline over time. Initially, the number of animals starts at around 250. Within the first 500 steps, there is a steep decrease in the total number of animals, dropping to approximately 50. This trend continues, and by around 1000 steps, the number of animals further reduces to near zero. Beyond 1000 steps, the number of animals remains very low, approaching extinction. This trend suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the prey population is not sustainable and rapidly declines towards extinction. For decision-makers, this indicates that the current settings of the model, which include factors such as hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and animal demographics, lead to a scenario where the prey population cannot sustain itself. This insight could be crucial for understanding the dynamics of megaherbivore extinction and the impact of various factors on their survival.
