The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred steps, dropping to approximately 2,232 units by step 376. Throughout the simulation, the energy levels continue to decrease, albeit at varying rates. Notable points include a series of small peaks and valleys around steps 376 to 410, where energy values fluctuate slightly but remain within a narrow band. The overall trend remains a steady decline, with significant drops observed at various inflection points such as steps 299, 472, and 506. By the end of the simulation at step 2311, the total energy of female animals reaches zero. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend with significant negative slopes in multiple rolling Mann-Kendall tests, indicating a consistent reduction in energy levels over time. The model also identifies several change points and periods of oscillation, but the dominant pattern remains the steady decline in total energy.
