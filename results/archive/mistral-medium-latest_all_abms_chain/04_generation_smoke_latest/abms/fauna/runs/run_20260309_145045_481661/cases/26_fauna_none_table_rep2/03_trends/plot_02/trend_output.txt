The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, with slight peaks and valleys, such as a peak at 1989.925 units and a valley at 1987.65 units. However, these initial variations are relatively small compared to the overall trend. As the simulation progresses, the energy levels continue to decline steadily. By the midpoint of the simulation, the energy levels have dropped significantly, reaching values around -23807 units. This downward trend persists, with notable points such as -28403 units and -37713 units, indicating a continuous and significant reduction in energy availability. Towards the end of the simulation, the energy levels reach their lowest points, bottoming out at approximately -38233 units. The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decrease, with all rolling Mann-Kendall tests indicating significant decreasing trends across various segments of the simulation. The model does not exhibit any periods of recovery or stabilization in energy levels, suggesting a persistent decline influenced by the interplay of hunting behavior, environmental conditions, and other model parameters.
