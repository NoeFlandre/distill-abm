The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid decline from an initial value of approximately 12,000 units. This sharp decrease occurs within the first 500 steps, where the total energy drops to around 2,000 units. Following this steep decline, the rate of decrease slows significantly, with the total energy gradually approaching zero over the subsequent steps. By around 2,000 steps, the total energy of female animals stabilizes near zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy reserves in the female animal population. This trend suggests a rapid initial impact on the female animal population, followed by a prolonged period of gradual decline, ultimately leading to a state of minimal energy reserves. For decision-makers, this implies that interventions aimed at preserving the energy reserves of female animals would need to be implemented very early in the timeline to be effective.
