The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a pronounced decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at approximately 16,666.67 units. This high initial value rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has decreased significantly to roughly 5,000 units. This downward trend continues, albeit at a slower rate, with the total energy further reducing to about 1,000 units by 1,000 time steps. Beyond this point, the decline becomes more gradual, and the total energy approaches near-zero values as the simulation progresses towards 2,312 time steps, ultimately reaching zero.

The statistical analysis reveals several key points of inflection and change points that mark shifts in the rate of decline. Notable change points occur around 350, 500, 625, and 875 time steps, indicating periods where the rate of energy decrease undergoes significant changes. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms that the overall trend is consistently decreasing across various segments of the simulation, with significant negative slopes indicating a steady reduction in total energy.

Additionally, there are minor oscillations detected with a dominant period of 63 time steps, suggesting some periodic fluctuations in the total energy levels. However, these oscillations are relatively minor compared to the overall decreasing trend. The peaks and valleys identified in the data are closely spaced and do not significantly deviate from the general downward trajectory, reinforcing the consistent decline in total energy over time.
