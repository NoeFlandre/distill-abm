The number of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations exhibits a consistent declining trend across all runs. Initially, the number of female animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of female animals drops to about 60. The decline continues more gradually, reaching approximately 20 female animals by around 1000 steps. Beyond this point, the number of female animals continues to decrease at a slower rate, approaching near-zero values by around 2000 steps. This trend suggests a significant reduction in the female animal population over time, which could have implications for the overall sustainability of the prey population in the model. For decision-makers, this trend indicates that under the current model parameters, the female prey population is not sustainable in the long term, which could lead to potential extinction events. This insight may prompt further investigation into the factors contributing to this decline and the exploration of different parameter settings to achieve more stable population dynamics.
