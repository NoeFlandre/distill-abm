The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a relatively stable trend over the simulation period, with minor fluctuations. Initially, the percentage of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33.0002% and ends very close to this value at 32.9979%. Throughout the simulation, the mean percentage of vegetated patches is around 32.6558%, with a median of 32.9696%, indicating that the data distribution is fairly symmetric and centered around the mean. The standard deviation of 0.9503 and variance of 0.9030 suggest low variability in the data. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, indicating a notable dip in vegetation coverage at this point. The peaks and valleys identified in the data are very close in value, with peaks around 32.9957% and valleys around 32.9942%, suggesting minor oscillations. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates several periods of increasing trends followed by decreasing trends, with significant changes in the trend direction at various points. The dominant oscillation period is 63 ticks, indicating a cyclical pattern in vegetation coverage. For decision makers, the stability in vegetation coverage suggests a resilient grassland environment within the model parameters. However, the slight dip at tick 365 and the identified change points may warrant further investigation to understand the underlying causes and potential impacts on the megaherbivore population and hunting dynamics.
