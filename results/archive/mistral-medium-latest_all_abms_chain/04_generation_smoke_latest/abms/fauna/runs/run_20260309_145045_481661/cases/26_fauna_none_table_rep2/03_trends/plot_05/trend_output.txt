The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a generally increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, the count starts at zero and gradually rises, reflecting the early stages of hunting activity. The most significant increases occur in the early phases of the simulation, with a steep rise observed up to around 2,310 ticks, where the count reaches approximately 117. This period is marked by a consistent and significant upward trend, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis, which shows strong increasing trends with high tau values and significant p-values. After this point, the rate of increase slows down, and the cumulative count stabilizes around the value of 117, with minor fluctuations. The peaks and valleys identified in the data are relatively close in value, indicating a plateau in the cumulative count of hunted postpartum females. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 ticks, suggesting some periodic variability in the hunting success rate, although these oscillations are minor compared to the overall trend. The change points identified at ticks 475, 625, 850, and 2310 mark significant shifts in the trend dynamics, with the most notable change occurring around tick 2310, where the trend shifts from increasing to a more stable state. Overall, the simulation shows an initial rapid increase in the hunting of postpartum females, followed by a stabilization phase with minor periodic fluctuations.
