After 1,000 time steps, the total energy of male animals continues to decline gradually, eventually approaching zero by around 2,300 time steps. This trend suggests a continuous depletion of energy reserves, leading to the potential extinction or severe reduction in the population of male animals. The simulation shows significant variability in energy levels across different runs, particularly after the initial rapid decline, indicating diverse outcomes based on varying interactions within the model. For decision-makers, these trends highlight the critical initial period where interventions could be most effective in preserving the energy reserves and population of male animals.
