The simulations of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend across multiple runs. Initially, the total number of animals starts at approximately 250. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of animals has decreased significantly to about 100. The decline continues, and by approximately 1000 steps, the number of animals drops to around 10. Beyond this point, the number of animals continues to decrease gradually, approaching zero as the steps progress towards 2000. The trend indicates a rapid initial decline in the animal population followed by a more gradual reduction, eventually leading to near extinction.
