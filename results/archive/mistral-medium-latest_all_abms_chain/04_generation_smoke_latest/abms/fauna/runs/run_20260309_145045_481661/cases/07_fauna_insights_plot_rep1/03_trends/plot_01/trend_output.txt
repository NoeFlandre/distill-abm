The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration of the simulation. Initially, the number of foragers is set at 20 and this value does not change over time. This stability suggests that the parameters governing the foragers' behavior and the environmental conditions are balanced in such a way that the number of active forager bands neither increases nor decreases. For decision makers, this implies that under the given conditions and parameters, the system maintains a steady state in terms of the number of forager bands, which could be useful for understanding the equilibrium dynamics in the context of megaherbivore extinction and human hunting pressure.
