The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear overall decreasing trend in the total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at approximately 166.67 animals. This number steadily declines throughout the simulation, reaching zero by the final time step. The decline is not uniform, with several notable phases and minor fluctuations. Early in the simulation, there is a rapid decrease in the prey population, followed by a more gradual decline. Around the mid-point of the simulation, there are several small peaks and valleys, indicating minor oscillations in the population. For instance, small peaks are observed at time steps 730, 709, and 803, with values around 11.37, 9.28, and 7.42 animals respectively. These peaks are followed by slight declines, creating minor valleys. Despite these fluctuations, the overall trend remains a steady decrease. The simulation also identifies several change points where the rate of decline shifts, notably at time steps 125, 475, 625, and 875. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 time steps, indicating a regular interval of minor population fluctuations amidst the overall decline. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms significant decreasing trends throughout most of the simulation, with brief periods of slight increase or stabilization. Ultimately, the prey population trends towards extinction by the end of the simulation period.
