The total energy of foragers in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and steady decline over time. Initially, the total energy starts at 2000 units. In the early stages, there are minor fluctuations, but the overall trend is a continuous decrease. By around 500 steps, the energy level drops to approximately -5000 units. This downward trend persists steadily, reaching around -15000 units by 1000 steps. As the simulation progresses further, the energy levels continue to decline, hitting approximately -25000 units by 1500 steps. By the end of the simulation at 2310 steps, the total energy of the foragers plummets to about -38233 units. The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decrease, with significant negative trends observed in all rolling Mann-Kendall tests across different segments of the simulation. The change points identified at steps 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 mark notable points in the energy decline, but the overall trend remains uniformly downward throughout the simulation period.
