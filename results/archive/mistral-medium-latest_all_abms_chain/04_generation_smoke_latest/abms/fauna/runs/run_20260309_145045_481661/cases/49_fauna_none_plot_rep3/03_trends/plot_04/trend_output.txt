The data from the repeated simulations of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a clear trend in the cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands over time. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero. As the simulation progresses, there is a rapid increase in the number of females killed, which begins to level off as it approaches approximately 120 females. This trend is observed consistently across the simulations. By around 500 steps, most simulations have reached close to 100 females killed. Beyond this point, the rate of increase slows down significantly, and the number of females killed stabilizes around 120. This stabilization suggests that the system reaches a dynamic equilibrium where the rate of hunting females balances with other factors in the model, such as reproduction rates, movement patterns, and environmental conditions. The simulations indicate that after an initial period of rapid hunting, the number of females killed plateaus, reflecting a steady-state in the interaction between hunters and prey within the given parameters of the model.
