The data from the repeated simulations of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of male animals over time. Initially, the number of male animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of male animals has decreased to about 80. The decline continues, and by 1000 steps, the number of male animals drops to roughly 20. Beyond 1000 steps, the number of male animals continues to decrease gradually, approaching zero around 2000 steps. This trend indicates a significant reduction in the male animal population over time, with a steep decline in the initial phases followed by a more gradual decrease as the number of male animals nears extinction. For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the male animal population is not sustainable over the long term, highlighting the potential impact of hunting pressure and environmental factors on the population dynamics of male animals.
