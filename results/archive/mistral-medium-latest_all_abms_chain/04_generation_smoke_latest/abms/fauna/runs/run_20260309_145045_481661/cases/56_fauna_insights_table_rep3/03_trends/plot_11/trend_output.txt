The combined total energy available in the population of male prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. After this initial sharp decline, the rate of decrease slows down, with the energy levels continuing to drop but at a much slower pace. Between ticks 1160 and 1391, the trend remains decreasing but with a less steep slope of -0.155. There is a brief period of increase in energy levels between ticks 1392 and 1623, where the trend reverses slightly with a positive slope of 0.091. However, this increase is short-lived, and the overall trend resumes its decreasing pattern. The energy levels eventually approach zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy in the male prey population by the end of the simulation. The presence of several peaks and valleys suggests some fluctuations in the energy levels, but these are relatively minor compared to the overall decreasing trend. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 ticks, indicating some regularity in the fluctuations, but these oscillations do not significantly alter the overall downward trajectory. The key change points identified at ticks 125, 350, 500, 625, 875, and 2310 mark significant shifts in the rate of decline, but the overall trend remains consistently downward. For decision makers, these trends suggest that under the given parameters, the male prey population experiences a significant and sustained reduction in energy levels over time.
