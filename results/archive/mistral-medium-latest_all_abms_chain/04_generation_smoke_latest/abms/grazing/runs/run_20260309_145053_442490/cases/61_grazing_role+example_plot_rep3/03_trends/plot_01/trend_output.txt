The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation, indicating that the initial disposition of social learning agents to deviate from the recommended number of livestock does not change over time. The values of the mean household initial risk attitude are distributed across a range from approximately -0.1 to 0.17. This distribution suggests a diversity in the initial risk attitudes among the households at the beginning of the simulation. The constancy of these values over the 100 time steps implies that the initial risk attitudes are set parameters that do not evolve or adapt during the simulation period. This static nature of risk attitude could be a deliberate design choice to observe the impact of fixed initial risk dispositions on the overall system dynamics without the influence of temporal changes in risk attitude.
