The average number of livestock destocked across all households in the simulation exhibits notable trends over the 100 timesteps. Initially, the destocking rate starts at zero and experiences a sharp increase, peaking significantly at timestep 3 with a value of 6.6144. Following this peak, there is a general decreasing trend with several smaller peaks and valleys. Key peaks occur at timesteps 9 (3.3733), 12 (2.7406), 17 (1.4661), and 19 (1.3828), while notable valleys are observed at timesteps 8 (3.3333), 11 (2.4717), 16 (1.4450), and 18 (0.9028). The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant decreasing trends in the initial phases (timesteps 0-10, 11-21, and 22-32), followed by periods of no significant trend and a slight increasing trend towards the end (timesteps 66-76). The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 49 timesteps, suggesting cyclical patterns in the destocking rates. The simulation ends with a relatively low destocking rate of 0.3111 at timestep 100. For decision makers, these trends highlight the initial volatility in livestock destocking, followed by a stabilization period. The cyclical patterns suggest periodic stressors or recovery phases in the system, which could inform adaptive management strategies to mitigate the impacts of these fluctuations.
