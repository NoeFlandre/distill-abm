The trend in the average number of livestock destocked across households shows a sharp initial peak followed by a rapid decline and eventual stabilization. After the initial sharp decline, the trend stabilizes with minor fluctuations and no significant trends from timesteps 33-43 and 55-65. A slight increasing trend is observed from timesteps 66-76. The implications for decision-makers include the need to address the initial high destocking rates, possibly through interventions aimed at resource management or support for households during early timesteps. The stabilization phase suggests that households may adapt over time, but continuous monitoring and support could be beneficial to maintain low destocking rates.
