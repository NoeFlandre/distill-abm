Conversely, other households exhibit a decreasing trend, with values dropping to as low as -0.4, indicating a higher risk aversion. Around the 40-step mark, the trends stabilize, and households' risk attitudes converge into distinct clusters. These clusters maintain relatively stable risk attitudes for the remainder of the simulation. The implications for decision-makers include recognizing the variability in risk attitudes among households, which can influence livestock management strategies and adaptive co-management practices.
