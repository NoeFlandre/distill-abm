The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation period. The values of the risk attitude vary among different households, with some households exhibiting a positive initial risk attitude, some a neutral attitude, and others a negative attitude. The positive risk attitudes range up to approximately 0.17, indicating a tendency to take more risks. The neutral risk attitude is around 0, showing no particular inclination towards risk-taking or risk-aversion. The negative risk attitudes go down to approximately -0.1, indicating a tendency to avoid risks. These varying initial risk attitudes reflect the diverse dispositions of households towards deviating from the recommended number of livestock, which is a critical aspect of their decision-making process in the grazing system.
