The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant at 0.0074 throughout the entire simulation period. There is no variation or trend observed in the data, as indicated by the consistent values across all time steps and the statistical measures showing a mean, median, minimum, and maximum all at 0.0074 with no standard deviation or variance. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms the absence of any significant trends across different segments of the simulation. This stability suggests that the initial disposition of social learning agents to deviate from the recommended number of livestock does not change over time under the given model parameters. For decision makers, this implies that the risk attitude of social learning agents is highly consistent and predictable, which could be useful for planning and managing grazing systems under similar conditions.
