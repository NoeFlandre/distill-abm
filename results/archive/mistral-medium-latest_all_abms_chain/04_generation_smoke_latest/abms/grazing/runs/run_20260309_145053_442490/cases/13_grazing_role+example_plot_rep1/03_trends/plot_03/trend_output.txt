The mean reserve biomass in the grazing system starts at approximately 90,000 units. Over the first 20 time steps, there is a slight decline, followed by a more pronounced decrease until around time step 30, where the biomass reaches a low point of about 85,000 units. This initial decline could be attributed to the initial grazing pressure exerted by the livestock as they begin to utilize the available biomass. After this low point, the mean reserve biomass begins to increase steadily. This recovery phase continues until around time step 60, where the biomass levels off and stabilizes at approximately 120,000 units. The stabilization at this higher level suggests that the system has reached a new equilibrium where the biomass production matches the grazing pressure. There is some variability among the simulation runs, particularly noticeable between time steps 30 and 60, indicating differences in how quickly the system reaches equilibrium across different simulations. This variability could be due to differences in the adaptive strategies of the households and the stochastic nature of ecological processes such as rainfall and biomass growth.
