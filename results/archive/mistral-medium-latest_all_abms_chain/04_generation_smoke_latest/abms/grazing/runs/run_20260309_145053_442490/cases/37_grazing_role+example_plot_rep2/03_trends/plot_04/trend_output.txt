The mean green biomass starts at approximately 45,000 units. Over the first 20 time steps, there is a slight decline, followed by a more pronounced decrease until around the 40th time step, where the biomass reaches a minimum value of about 42,000 units. This initial decline could be attributed to the immediate grazing pressure exerted by the livestock at the beginning of the simulation, as the animals consume the readily available green biomass. After this initial drop, the mean green biomass begins to increase steadily. This recovery phase continues until around the 80th time step, where the biomass levels off and stabilizes at approximately 62,000 units. The increase in biomass can be interpreted as the vegetation's response to reduced grazing pressure or favorable climatic conditions that promote regrowth. The stabilization phase suggests that the system has reached a dynamic equilibrium where the rate of biomass consumption by livestock is balanced by the rate of biomass regrowth. Throughout the simulation, there is some variability among the different runs, but the overall trend remains consistent across all simulations.
