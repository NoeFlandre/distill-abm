The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households shows distinct trends over the simulated time steps. Initially, the number of livestock starts at 90 and exhibits a decreasing trend. This decline is steep at first, with a significant drop in the early time steps, reaching a minimum value of approximately 41 at around time step 37. This period of decrease is statistically significant and consistent, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis. 

Following this low point, the trend shifts, and the number of livestock begins to increase gradually. This increasing trend starts around time step 44 and continues through to the end of the simulation at time step 100. The rate of increase varies, with a more pronounced rise initially, which then tapers off to a more gradual increase in later time steps. The change points detected at time steps 20, 55, and 100 mark significant shifts in the trend dynamics. 

The oscillation analysis indicates a dominant period of 49 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the livestock numbers. The strong oscillation indices further support the presence of periodic fluctuations within the data. Overall, the system demonstrates an initial decline in livestock numbers followed by a recovery phase, with the latter part of the simulation characterized by a steady increase in livestock numbers.
