This sharp decline could be attributed to initial adjustments in livestock management practices as households adapt to the environmental and social conditions of the model. Following this decline, the metric shows a gradual increase, stabilizing around a value of 47.5 by the end of the 100 time steps. This stabilization suggests that households have adapted their livestock management strategies to better suit the prevailing conditions, achieving a more sustainable balance. The initial rapid decline followed by a gradual increase and stabilization provides insights for decision-makers on the importance of adaptive management practices in maintaining livestock populations under varying conditions.
