The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the entire simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of -0.0000, indicating no inequality. Throughout the simulation, the coefficient gradually increases, reaching a final value of 0.0726 at the end of the 100 time steps. The mean value of the Gini coefficient over the simulation period is 0.0302, with a median of 0.0229, suggesting a slight right-skewed distribution. The standard deviation of 0.0239 and variance of 0.0006 indicate low variability in the data. Change points are detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100, suggesting notable shifts in the trend at these points. Additionally, there is evidence of strong oscillation towards the latter part of the simulation, with a dominant period of 49.00 and strong oscillation indices observed from time step 78 onwards. This suggests periodic fluctuations in the inequality levels during the later stages of the simulation.
