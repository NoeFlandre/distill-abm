The average green biomass across all patches exhibits a consistent trend across all simulations. Initially, the mean green biomass starts at approximately 45,000 units. By step 40, the biomass has recovered to its initial levels and continues to rise. This upward trend accelerates, and by step 60, the biomass reaches approximately 55,000 units. The growth continues, albeit at a slower rate, and by step 100, the mean green biomass stabilizes around 62,000 units. 

For decision makers, this trend suggests that the grazing system, under the given parameters, initially experiences a slight reduction in biomass likely due to grazing pressure. However, the system demonstrates resilience and the ability to recover and even exceed initial biomass levels over time. This recovery and growth phase indicates that the adaptive management strategies, including learning-by-doing and social learning, may contribute positively to the long-term sustainability and productivity of the grazing system. The stabilization of biomass at higher levels towards the end of the simulation period suggests that the system reaches a new equilibrium, which could be beneficial for the livelihoods dependent on these pastures.
