The Gini coefficient for the distribution of healthy livestock across households starts at a value of 0, indicating perfect equality. Over the course of 100 time steps, the Gini coefficient exhibits a consistent increasing trend, reaching a maximum value of approximately 0.0726. This indicates a gradual increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households. The mean value of the Gini coefficient over the entire period is 0.0302, with a median of 0.0229, suggesting that while there is an overall increase, the values are generally low, implying relatively minor inequality throughout the simulation. The standard deviation of 0.0239 and variance of 0.0006 indicate some variability in the inequality levels over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results confirm a significant increasing trend across all time segments, with consistent positive tau values and significant p-values. Change points are detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100, suggesting notable shifts in the rate of increase in inequality at these points. The inflection indices and oscillation summary suggest some periodic fluctuations, particularly in the latter half of the time steps, but the overall trend remains one of increasing inequality. This trend reflects the dynamics of household decision-making and the impact of various behavioral types and learning strategies on livestock distribution over time.
