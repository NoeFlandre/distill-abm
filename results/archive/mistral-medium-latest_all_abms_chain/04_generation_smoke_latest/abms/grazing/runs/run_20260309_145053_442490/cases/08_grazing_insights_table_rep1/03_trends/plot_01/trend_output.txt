The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration of the model run. The value was consistently 0.0074 from the beginning to the end of the 100 timesteps. There was no variation or trend observed in the data, as indicated by the statistical analysis showing a mean, median, standard deviation, and variance all equal to 0.0074 and 0.0000 respectively. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirmed the absence of any significant trends across all time windows. The implications for decision makers are that the initial risk attitude of social learning agents did not change, suggesting a stable disposition towards risk among these agents throughout the simulation period. This stability could be useful for predicting long-term behavior and planning interventions in the context of adaptive co-management.
