Key change points are detected at time steps 25, 60, and 100, suggesting notable shifts in the rate of destocking at these points. The model exhibits strong oscillations with a dominant period of 49 time steps, indicating periodic fluctuations in the rate of destocking. Inflection points are observed at several time steps, including 45, 47, 53, 56, 57, 62, 67, 72, 74, and 75, suggesting changes in the concavity of the trend. Overall, the data indicates a steady and significant increase in the cumulative number of livestock destocked, reflecting the adaptive management strategies and behavioral dynamics of the households in response to the ecological and climatic conditions simulated in the model.
