The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the 100 time steps. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at 0.0, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock. As time progresses, the Gini coefficient gradually increases, reflecting a growing inequality among households. By the final time step, the Gini coefficient reaches approximately 0.0726, suggesting a noticeable but not extreme level of inequality. Throughout the simulation, the trend is consistently upward, with no detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady increase in inequality. The inflection points and change points identified at various intervals suggest shifts in the rate of increase, but the overall direction remains consistently upward. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across all time intervals, with significant statistical evidence supporting the continuous rise in the Gini coefficient. The dominant period of oscillation towards the latter part of the simulation indicates some periodic variations, but these do not alter the overall increasing trend. This analysis suggests that over time, the distribution of healthy livestock becomes increasingly unequal among households in the model.
