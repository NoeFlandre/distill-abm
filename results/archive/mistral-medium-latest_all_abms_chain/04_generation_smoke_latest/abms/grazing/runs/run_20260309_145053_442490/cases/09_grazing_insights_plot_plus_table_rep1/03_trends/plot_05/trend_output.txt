The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households shows a distinct trend over the simulated time steps. Initially, the mean livestock count starts at 90. This high initial value sharply decreases in the early stages, reaching a minimum value of approximately 41 around time step 37. This significant drop indicates a period of substantial destocking or livestock reduction among households. Following this low point, the trend reverses, and the livestock numbers begin to gradually increase. The increase is steady but slow, indicating a recovery phase where households are possibly rebuilding their livestock numbers. By the end of the simulation period, the mean livestock count stabilizes around 47.5. The trend analysis reveals several phases: an initial sharp decrease, a period of stabilization at the lowest point, followed by a gradual and consistent increase. The inflection points at time steps 3 and 46 mark significant changes in the trend direction. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these observations, showing significant decreasing trends initially, followed by periods of no trend, and eventually significant increasing trends. The oscillation summary indicates a dominant period of 49 time steps, suggesting cyclical patterns in livestock numbers. For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of understanding the dynamics of livestock management under varying conditions. The initial sharp decline suggests a critical period where interventions might be necessary to support households. The subsequent recovery phase indicates resilience and adaptive strategies among households, which could be studied and potentially enhanced through targeted policies.
