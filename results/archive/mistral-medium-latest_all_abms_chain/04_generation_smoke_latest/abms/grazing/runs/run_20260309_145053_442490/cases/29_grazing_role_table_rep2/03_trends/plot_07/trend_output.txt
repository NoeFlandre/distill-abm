The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of approximately 0, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock. Over time, the inequality gradually increases, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 by the end of the simulation at time step 100. The mean value of the Gini coefficient across all time steps is 0.0302, with a median of 0.0229, suggesting a slight right-skewed distribution. The standard deviation of 0.0239 and variance of 0.0006 indicate relatively low variability around the mean. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across all time windows, with a consistent tau value of 1.000 and significant p-values, indicating a strong and steady increase in inequality. Change points are detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100, suggesting notable shifts in the trend at these points. Additionally, there is evidence of strong oscillation indices towards the latter part of the simulation, indicating periodic fluctuations in the rate of increase in inequality. Overall, the data suggests a steady and significant increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households over the simulation period.
