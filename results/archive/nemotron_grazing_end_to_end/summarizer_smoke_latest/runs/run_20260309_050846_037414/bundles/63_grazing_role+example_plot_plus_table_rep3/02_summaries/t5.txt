agent-based model focuses on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . the original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . this extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity . key goals include evaluating long-term social-ecological performance of behavioral types . the data reveals sustained stability in mean initial risk attitudes across all 100 simulation runs . values consistently cluster between -0.11 and 0.18 across 30 household groups . the central tendency (mean) remains close to 0.05-0.0 . the mean reserve biomass exhibits a pronounced upward trajectory across all simulation runs, beginning at 80,000–90,000 units and rising steadily to exceed 120,000 units by the final timestep . the average across 30 runs reveals consistent long-term gains, with mean variability decreasing by 5% after step 80 . the total livestock destocking across households begins at approximately 6 livestock units per household in the initial steps, reflecting the immediate pressure from a large initial herd size (90 animals per household) the metric remains relatively stable within a narrow range (0.4-0.6) for the remainder of the 100 timesteps . destock levels plateau slightly at 30–35, coinciding with stochastic biomass recovery periods during improved rainfall phases . the linear expansion from 60–100 steps (destock values 60–167) reflects compounding pasture degradation: households progressively reduce herd sizes to maintain survival .
