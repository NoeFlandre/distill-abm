 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The average household risk attitude exhibits distinct temporal phases of exploration, adaptation, and equilibrium, driven by stochastic climate shocks and behavioral learning . The data reveals sustained stability in mean initial risk attitudes across all 100 simulation runs, with values consistently clustered between -0.11 and 0.18 across 30 household groups . The highest variance clusters center around 0.07-0.09, suggesting this represents the most stable equilibrium point for adaptive strategies .  The simulation reveals a dynamic interplay between environmental uncertainty, behavioral adaptation, and social learning . Risk-sensitive strategies (risk-mode?=True) and homogeneous behavioral types constrain resource recovery, as agents default to E-RO decision-making without social or learning-driven adaptations . The mean reserve biomass exhibits a pronounced upward trajectory across all simulation runs .  Gini coefficient quantifying livestock health inequality begins near zero at the simulation's outset . A gradual increase emerges within the first 10–20 years, with some runs showing accelerated growth after environmental perturbations . By step 50–60, abrupt rises occur, driven by agent responses to pasture collapse and herd size adjustments under risk-sensitive decision-making .  E-RO strategy's lack of learning mechanisms aligns with its lack of behavioral shifts . Non-herd dynamics persist because agents never adjust grazing intensity or timing despite changing environmental conditions . The stable equilibrium reflects the original RAGE model's assumptions of stochastic pastoralist decisions fixed to preconceived grazing rules .
