The average mean destock of households begins at 6.0 during the first time step, after which it declines rapidly over the first 20 steps. This sharp decline likely reflects initial adaptive herd size adjustments in response to resource constraints and stochastic climate shocks. As the simulation progresses, a plateau emerges between steps 50 and 100, with values stabilizing within a range of 0.4 to 1.0 across replicates. This equilibrium suggests ongoing resource limitations but stabilized herd sizes under adaptive management. The absence of pasture retirement mechanisms (resting?=False) combined with homogenous initial conditions (homog-behav-types?=True) likely prevents complete resolution of destocking pressures. The risk parameter (risk-att-init=0.5) enabled in the model parameters appears to maintain moderate destocking levels within the learning strategy replicates (number-E-RO-SL1=20 and number-E-LBD=20), as indicated by the reduced volatility in later steps.
