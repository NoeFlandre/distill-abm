In nearly all cases (columns 2–31), the initial risk attitudes hover between 0.0375 and 0.1167, clustering tightly around 0.05–0.06. This suggests a near-uniform risk tolerance among agents in most simulations, likely reflecting the influence of the dynamic risk attitude threshold (risk-att-init: 0.5) constrained between 0.5 and 0.9. The first column (-0.02833) deviates significantly, indicating a unique case where agents started with a risk-averse disposition. The initial risk-averse parameter (risk-att-init) is set to 0.5, but the lower values may result from dynamic adjustments during enrollment. No clear temporal trends emerge within individual simulations, as all lines in the plot remain nearly horizontal across 100 steps, confirming stability in risk attitudes. These results imply that, under homogeneous behavioral settings (homog-behav-types?), agents self-organize into predictable risk profiles, which could inform resource allocation strategies in risk-management decision-making.
