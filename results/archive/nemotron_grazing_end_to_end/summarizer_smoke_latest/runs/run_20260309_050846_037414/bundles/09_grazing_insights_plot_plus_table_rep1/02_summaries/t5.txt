agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . the context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . social learning (E-RO-SL1) is inactive due to homog-behav-types? . initial risk attitudes hover between 0.0375 and 0.1167, clustering tightly around 0.05–0.06 . by 100 steps, the sustained trajectories converge to three distinct levels of mean green biomass . this separation emerges from initial environmental conditions, particularly pastureland quality disparities . analysis suggests baseline management strategies disproportionately affect long-term outcomes . the lack of active social learning (social-susceptibility=0) and disabled strategy switching (SL2-strategy-switch?=False) prevents agents from adapting to emerging inequalities . inequality in livestock outcomes suggests necessary portfolio diversification in risk management tools for rangeland communities . adaptive strategies could dominate long-term outcomes in tightly regulated systems . in more open or variable contexts, adaptive strategies may dominate . policymakers believe that traditional practices may persist .
