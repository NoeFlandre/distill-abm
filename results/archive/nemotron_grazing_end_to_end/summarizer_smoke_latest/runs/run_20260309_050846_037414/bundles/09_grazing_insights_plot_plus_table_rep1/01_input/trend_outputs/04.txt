The divergence between these regimes grows over time, with the steepest slope occurring in the third decade. By 100 steps, the sustained trajectories converge to three distinct levels of mean green biomass, with temporal smoothing evident in the final two decades as oscillations fade. This separation emerges from initial environmental conditions, particularly pastureland quality disparities. Analysis suggests baseline management strategies disproportionately affect long-term outcomes, with its critical window revealing sustainability thresholds under uncertain environmental conditions.
