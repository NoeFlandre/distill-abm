 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The data reveals persistent patterns in the average initial risk attitudes of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) across simulated populations . Values cluster predominantly between 0.04 and 0.14, reflecting predominantly mild to moderate risk-seeking tendencies . The median value remains near neutral (close to zero), suggesting initial herd size decisions occur approximately at the recommended baseline . The total number of livestock destocked across all households begins at zero at the initial timestep and exhibits a non-linear increase, accelerating sharply after approximately 12 timesteps . The trajectory reflects coupled social-ecological feedbacks: early destocking pressures stem from baseline herd size (**livestock-init**=90) and resource constraints .
