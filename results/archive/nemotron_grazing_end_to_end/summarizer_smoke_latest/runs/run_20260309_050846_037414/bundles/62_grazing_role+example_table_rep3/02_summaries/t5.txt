agent-based model focuses on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . the original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . this extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity . key goals include evaluating long-term social-ecological performance of behavioral types . the data reveals persistent patterns in the average initial risk attitudes of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) across simulated populations . values cluster predominantly between 0.04 and 0.14, reflecting predominantly mild to moderate risk-seeking tendencies . between timesteps 5–15, deviations show clustering around -0.1 to -0.3 across most households . this persistent trend correlates with the **risk-att-init=0.5** setting, which favored cautious herd management in uncertain climates . the total number of livestock, including both healthy and destocked animals, remains constant at 90 for the first 10 timesteps . the trajectory exhibits fluctuations, particularly pronounced in the latter half of the simulation, which align with the stochastic climate shocks introduced by high rainfall variability (rain-mean=200, rain-std=100) adoption rates ranging from 9 to 17 households, while timestep 20 peaks at 20–22 in some simulations . adoption rates stabilize between 40–50 households across runs, suggesting a tipping point where social learning becomes self-reinforcing . the spread across simulation runs reveals heterogeneous adoption trajectories . absence of learning behaviors (homog-behav-types?=True) and social-susceptibility (set to 0.0) suggest external factors like risk-averse herd management decisions and pasture stress combinations drive these oscillations .
