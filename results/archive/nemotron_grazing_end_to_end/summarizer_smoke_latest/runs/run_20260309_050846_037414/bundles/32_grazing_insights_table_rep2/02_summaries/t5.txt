agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . the context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . data reveals variability in initial risk attitudes across households . values fluctuate between approximately -0.11 and 0.18 . most individuals exhibit moderate risk attitudes, clustering near zero . risk-seeking tendencies suggest heterogeneous risk preferences . biomass levels drop below 90,000 in all runs, with extreme cases reaching 86,000-83,000 by timestep 100 . no simulations demonstrate resilience or recovery, suggesting systemic susceptibility to degradation under static decision-making . destocking rates fluctuate between 4.0 and 7.0 across replicates, with no consistent directional trend . subsequent timesteps (t 12) show sharper variability, with values dipping as low as 2.533 (timestep 24), suggesting transient climatic shocks or adaptive responses . data reveals a gradual increase in the number of households engaged in social learning across timesteps, with values remaining consistently zero until timestep 19 . the trend shows intermittent fluctuations but no significant decline after peaking, reaching a peak of 48 households by timestep 29 .
