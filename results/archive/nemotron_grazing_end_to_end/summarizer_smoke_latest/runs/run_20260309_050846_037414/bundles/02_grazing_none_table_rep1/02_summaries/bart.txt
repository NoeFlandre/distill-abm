 The agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . The context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . The model evaluates long-term outcomes like pasture biomass, livestock survival, and household resilience .  The model focuses on E-RO under homogeneous conditions with climatic variability, though parameters are ready for heterogeneous comparisons . Strategy switching (SL2-strategy-switch?) is disabled, preventing agents from adopting other types . Social learning (E-RO-SL1) is inactive due to homog-behav-types?.
