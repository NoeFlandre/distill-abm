The model simulates 100 years ('timesteps') of data for 60 homogeneous E-RO households under climatic variability (200±100 annual rainfall). The Gini coefficient trends reveal three key patterns: 1) Early-time inequality acts as a baseline of moderate homogeneity, 2) Mid-game variability shows sensitivity to climate-driven shocks, and 3) Late-game convergence emerges despite external variability.

Key observations: Initial conditions showed low but non-zero inequality (average Gini ~0.00027), reflecting inherent risk variability in E-RO agents' initial stocking decisions. By time step 48 (48/100), inequality surged to a peak of 0.0066-0.0073, suggesting compounding disparities from compounded climatic adversity. Final time steps (18-20) converge toward renewed homogeneity (Gini ~0.0039-0.0052), demonstrating resilience through repeated selective pressure. Notably, the ‘.13’ and ‘.20’ time steps show distinct temporal dynamics - the former records the historical maximum inequality, while the latter demonstrates recovery momentum.

Contextually, this mirrors E-RO agents' tendency to reinforce cautious strategies under uncertainty. However, the observed divergence-amplification pattern contrasts with original RAGE findings about satisficing thresholds.
