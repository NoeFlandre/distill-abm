agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . the context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . the model focuses on E-RO under homogeneous conditions with climatic variability . mean risk attitudes cluster around 0.075, 0.045, and 0.0125, suggesting households highly inclined to take risks (low risk aversion). biomass remained constant at 90,000 units, indicating a steady-state equilibrium . a gradual decline began at timestep 6 (90,180), continuing through timesteps 7–10 . this suggests early signs of resource pressure . the model simulates 100 years of data for 60 homogeneous E-RO households under climatic variability (200100 annual rainfall) from year 1 onward, households uniformly destocked 6.0 livestock annually, establishing a stable baseline . this plateau persisted through Year 4 during which animated fluctuations emerged . early-period destocking (e.g., 6.0 by timestep 10–15) reflects baseline adjustments as agents under E-RO (baseline) strategies initially respond to pasture biomass dynamics .
