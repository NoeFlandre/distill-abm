At timestep 28, a marked phase shift occurs (mean = 20.61, range = 17.83-20.58 by timestep 31), potentially reflecting adaptive threshold effects in risk-sensitive decision-making or social learning spillover among E-RO-SL1 agents. This plateau phase is followed by progressively increasing destocking rates: 17.9 → 19.23 (timestep 29-31), 24.43 → 26.05 (timestep 41-42), and 47.95 → 48.1 (timestep 57-58), demonstrating cascading behavioral adjustments.  
  
The terminal plateau (timestep 100+) stabilizes around 172, suggesting system convergence to a new equilibrium. This trajectory aligns with the simulated climate stress (global-rain?=True with σ=100) exacerbating overgrazing, compounded by E-LBD agents' experiential learning and E-RO-SL1 strategy diffusion. The absence of resting mechanisms (resting?=False) and high initial herd size (livestock-init=90) appear critical in triggering these adaptive cascades, particularly after cross-power-curve thresholds in biomass depletion.  
  
These findings underscore the model's capacity to replicate emergent dynamics from heterogeneous behavioral interactions under environmental stochasticity, with learning strategies mediating delayed but self-reinforcing system responses. Deestocking patterns correlate temporally with parameter-driven constraints on resource regeneration (gr1=0.5, gr2=0.1) and decision architecture, particularly risk-att-init=0.5 operating under satisficing-free decision rules.
