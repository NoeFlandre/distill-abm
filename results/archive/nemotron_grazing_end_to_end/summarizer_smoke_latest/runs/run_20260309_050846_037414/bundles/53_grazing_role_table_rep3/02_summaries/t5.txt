agent-based model focuses on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . the original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . this extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity . key goals include evaluating long-term social-ecological performance of behavioral types . the data illustrates fluctuations in the average risk attitude of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) over 30 simulated time steps . values generally cluster between -0.11 and +0.18 . households 22-28 exhibit synchronized increases in deviations between timesteps 100–200 . the absence of pasture rest mechanisms and high initial herd sizes (90 livestock/agent) likely amplify stress on grazing resources, exacerbating deviations . simulations span 100 timesteps under conditions of stochastic rainfall (mean=200, std=100), uniform behavioral types (all E-RO, baseline agents without learning) and high initial herd sizes (90 livestock per household) despite identical initial conditions, variability emerges by timestep 6 (ranging 5.67–6.48) . data reveals systematic patterns in household adoption of social learning . no households engaged in social learning, indicating an initial phase dominated by individual decision-making without observational adaptation . a notable increase occurs at timestep 12 (11 households), establishing a rising trend that culminates in a stabilization phase by timesteps 28–29 . rebounds from 0 to 20 in 15–20 timesteps suggest climate variability alone cannot eradicate E-RO strategies . learning-capable agents may emerge post-crisis . the data underscores a trade-off between behavioral rigidity and environmental resilience .
