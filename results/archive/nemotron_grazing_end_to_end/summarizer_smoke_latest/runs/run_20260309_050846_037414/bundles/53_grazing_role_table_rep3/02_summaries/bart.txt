 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The data illustrates fluctuations in the average risk attitude of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) over 30 simulated time steps . It suggests agents prioritize stability over risk-taking, likely influenced by pasture management pressures and climatic unpredictability .
