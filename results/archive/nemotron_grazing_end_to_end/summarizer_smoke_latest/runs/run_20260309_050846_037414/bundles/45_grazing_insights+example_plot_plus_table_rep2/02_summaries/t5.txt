agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . the context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . social learning (E-RO-SL1) is inactive due to homog-behav-types? . the model focuses on E-RO under homogeneous conditions with climatic variability . despite dynamic environmental conditions, risk attitudes remain statistically unchanged throughout all 100 timesteps . the mean green biomass begins at 45,000 across all simulations but declines initially, reaching 40,000–42,000 by step 30 due to localized overgrazing and uneven resource distribution . by step 50, biomass undergoes a sharp increase, peaking at 62,000–64,000 by step 100, with slight variability between runs . the recovery is gradual, with no significant reversals after step 60 . the absence of strategy heterogeneity in this run precludes analysis of social learning dynamics . by step 100, the majority of simulations show Gini coefficients between 0.02 and 0.06, with some reaching as high as 0.07 . this suggests that long-term outcomes are sensitive to stochastic climatic shocks . the absence of strategy switching (SL2-strategy-switch?=False) and fixed behavioral type homogeneity (homog-behav-types?=True) explain why E-RO remains dominant despite climate shocks . the data also suggests potential tipping points, as some runs exhibit rapid decline before stabilizing .
