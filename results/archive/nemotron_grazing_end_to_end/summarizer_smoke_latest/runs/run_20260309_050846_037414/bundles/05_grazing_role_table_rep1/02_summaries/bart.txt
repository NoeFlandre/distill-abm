 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The data reveals a heterogeneous distribution of initial risk attitudes among social learning agents (E-RO-SL1), with values ranging from -0.11 (strong risk aversion) to 0.18 (risk-seeking) The data suggests initial conditions for long-term resilience are likely to act as potential leverage points for management interventions .  E-RO-SL1 agents (n=20) demonstrate rapid divergence in some simulations, suggesting social learning implementation (despite social-susceptibility=0) or individual risk sensitivity (risk-att-init=0.5) influencing herding strategies . Risk-aware decision-making manifests in herd reductions (-0.28) during low biomass states, yet incomplete conformity to optimal carrying capacities . The data reveals a distinct two-phase pattern in destocking trends over the 100-year simulation period .
