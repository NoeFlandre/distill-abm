The Gini coefficient for total livestock health begins near zero at the start of the simulation and increases consistently over time. At the onset (early time steps), all households exhibit similar scores (values <0.01), indicating low inequality. Gradual divergence emerges after ~20 time steps, with values rising to ~0.02–0.03 by steps 40–60. By ~80 steps, inequality accelerates, reaching ~0.05–0.07, with sharper rises observed in runs featuring higher variance in adaptive behaviors. The final time steps (90–100) show stark clustering, with values exceeding ~0.06 in some simulations. This pattern suggests learning-aware agents (E-LBD, E-RO-SL1) and risk-sensitive strategies increasingly diverge from traditional agents (E-RO) under stochastic climate shocks (rain-mean: 200, rain-std: 100) and high initial grazing pressures (livestock-init: 90). The rising inequality reflects feedbacks between social learning (know radius: 1.0), behavioral adaptation, and resource variability.
