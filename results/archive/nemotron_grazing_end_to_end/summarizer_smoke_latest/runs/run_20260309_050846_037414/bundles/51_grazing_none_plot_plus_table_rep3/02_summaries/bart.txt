 The agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . The context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . The model evaluates long-term outcomes like pasture biomass, livestock survival, and household resilience .  The model focuses on E-RO under homogeneous conditions with climatic variability . Social learning (E-RO-SL1) is inactive due to homog-behav-types?. Strategy switching (SL2-strategy-switch?) is disabled, preventing agents from adopting other types . The mean initial risk attitude values show minimal variation across households .  All 60 households show steady biomass increase over time, with the exception of a few columns showing minor fluctuations but no declines . This suggests biomass approaches a stable equilibrium after 100 years, despite environmental shocks .
