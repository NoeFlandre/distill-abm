 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The simulation reveals critical insights about adaptive herd management in stochastic grazing systems . Simulation length in years allows observation of long-term systemic patterns . The mean initial risk attitude of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) begins at 0.5 at timestep 0 . Initial herd sizes create immediate overgrazing pressure, leading to rapid biomass decline . Gini coefficient for 'Total Livestock Healthy' increases steadily from nearly 0 to over 0.09 across all simulations . Early inequality growth corresponds with the equilibrium establishment of pasture resource allocation patterns among households . By step 50-60, rates of inequality acceleration diverge significantly among behavioral types .  The E-RO behavioral group's count exhibits a population trajectory characterized by cyclical resilience and gradual decline, mirroring the dynamics of non-learning agents in an environment with stochastic climate regimes . The extinction phase begins around step 70, with households dropping below 20 units and persisting in a near-collapse asymptote .
