The simulation tracks mean green biomass across 100 annual steps under homogeneous E-RO behavioral conditions, with rainfall variability (mean=200, std=100) driving ecological dynamics. All runs begin at 45,000 biomass units (step 0), reflecting initial pasture stability. A consistent decline phase emerges between steps 30–40, with biomass dropping to ~40,000–42,000 across runs. This trough represents ecological stress from climatic variability and overgrazing. By steps 60–70, biomass begins to recover, reaching ~61,000–62,000 by step 90. Variability decreases after step 40 as runs align toward convergence, ending at ~63,000–64,000 by step 100.

Three distinct phases characterize the trajectory: **1)** Pre-stress equilibrium (steps 0–20: minor fluctuations ~44,500–45,000), **2)** Stress period (steps 25–60: sharp decline followed by partial recovery), and **3)** Post-stress recovery (steps 65–100: gradual return to ~64,000). The sharpest declines occur between steps 30–40, with some runs (e.g., mean-green-biomass.21) bottoming at ~40,500, while others rebound faster. By step 80, all runs stabilize above 58,000, suggesting management strategies mitigate long-term damage despite short-term variability. The model's focus on homogeneous E-RO agents without social learning mechanisms emphasizes intrinsic risk aversion (risk-att-init=0.5) as a resilience factor. Decision-makers should prioritize adaptive stocking rates during climatic variability to leverage natural recovery capacity, but results highlight the need for social learning (E-RO-SL1) in heterogeneous populations to address variability in rebound rates. The data table reveals run-specific deviations in recovery trajectories, underscoring stochasticity in ecological systems even under identical parameters.
