 The agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . The context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . The model evaluates long-term outcomes like pasture biomass, livestock survival, and household resilience .  The model focuses on E-RO under homogeneous conditions with climatic variability, though parameters are ready for heterogeneous comparisons . The mean household risk attitude begins at approximately 0.08 across all agents at step 0, reflecting initial conservative stocking decisions under the risk mode parameter . Decision-makers should consider integrating resting thresholds or adaptive stocking limits to mitigate long-term resource collapse .
