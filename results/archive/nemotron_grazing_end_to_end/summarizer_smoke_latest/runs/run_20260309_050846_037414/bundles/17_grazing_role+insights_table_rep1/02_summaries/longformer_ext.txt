Appears to be an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities. The original model was designed to analyze the feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems. This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity, allowing exploration of adaptive management and strategy diffusion. Key goals include evaluating long-term social-ecological performance of behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, E-RO-SL1), studying the implications of uncertainty management, and analyzing how learning strategies propagate in populations.
