 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The data reveals heterogeneous risk attitudes among households across the simulation timeline, with average risk attitudes fluctuating around a narrow range . Column 9 exhibits the highest positive risk attitudes (up to 0.18), while column 5 shows the most pronounced negative values (0.11), reflecting adaptation to environmental pressures .  The data reveals a gradual, non-linear increase in mean-reserve-biomass across all patches in the grazing system over 100 simulated years . This trajectory suggests initial overgrazing pressure from households destabilizes pastures early, but structural recovery emerges as biomass regeneration rates outpace consumption . For decision-makers, this underscores the importance of balancing grazing pressure with climate factors and agents' dynamic risk responses in sustainable pasture management .  The data reveals a gradual decline in mean livestock numbers (including destocked animals) across households over time, starting from an initial uniform value of 90.0 livestock per household . The rising biomass trend indicates that adaptive decision-making under uncertainty can yield sustainable outcomes . But the plateau at 64,000 units suggests system-level constraints .  Simulations show near-zero inequality (-6.66e-16 to 1.37e-4), suggesting almost perfect equilibrium in resource access at the outset . By simulations 3–4, inequality remains minimal (up to 2.02e- 4), reflecting stable outcomes under the baseline ‘E-RO’ behavioral strategy . Some simulations (columns 5–8) show slight declines (-5e-5), while later time steps exhibit localized increases .
