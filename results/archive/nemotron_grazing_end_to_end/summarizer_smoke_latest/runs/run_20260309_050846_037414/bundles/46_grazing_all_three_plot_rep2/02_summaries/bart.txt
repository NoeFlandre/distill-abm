 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The mean initial risk attitude of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) begins at approximately 0.5, reflecting a moderate balance between risk-tolerant and risk-averse behaviors . The metric stabilizes between 0.45–0.48 from timesteps 60–100, indicating a learned equilibrium where agents adjust herd sizes (livestock-init=90) to align with slow pasture recovery rates during dry cycles .
