The mean initial risk attitude (y-axis) of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) remains constant at the nominal risk tolerance value of 0.5 across all timesteps. This indicates no temporal adaptation in risk disposition despite stochastic rainfall (mean=200, std=100) and grazing stressors. The E-RO-SL1 agents' inability to modify their initial risk attitude—due to fixed **risk-att-init**=0.5 and disabled social learning mechanisms (**social-susceptibility**=0, **SL2-strategy-switch?**=False)—suppresses variability in risk management strategies. Despite active **risk-mode?**=True enabling risk-aware herd size adjustments, the agents' baseline disposition to uncertainty-tolerance (moderate risk-seeking) persists unchanged. This flat trajectory highlights the static nature of risk tolerance in this simulation setup, contrasting with adaptive learning-by-doing (E-LBD) strategies where behavioral updates would be expected.
