The Gini coefficient values show a clear trend of increasing inequality in livestock health across households over the 100-step simulation period. Initially, all scenarios begin with near-zero values (0.000–0.001), reflecting uniform initial conditions. By mid-simulation steps (around step 50), values rise significantly across all conditions, reaching mid-range Gini coefficients (0.030–0.060). The most pronounced inequality occurs in scenarios with heterogeneous behavioral types (e.g., columns 25–28, values up to 0.093), where differences in strategy adoption and social learning mechanisms create distinct herd outcomes. Environmental variability (rainfall parameters) exacerbates disparities, particularly in scenarios with combined E-RO and E-LBD strategies (columns 19–20). Strategies emphasizing social learning (columns 29–30) mitigate extreme inequality compared to purely behavioral approaches, suggesting institutional support for equitable knowledge sharing could stabilize herd outcomes. The data indicates that long-term climate-resilient policies should prioritize intervention thresholds (e.g., resting-threshold adjustments) to curb resource-driven inequities.
