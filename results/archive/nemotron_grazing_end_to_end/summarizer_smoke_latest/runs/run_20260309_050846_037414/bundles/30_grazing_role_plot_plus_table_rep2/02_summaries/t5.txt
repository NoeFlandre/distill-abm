agent-based model focuses on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . the original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . this extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity . key goals include evaluating long-term social-ecological performance of behavioral types . the data table contains 40 simulation runs, each reporting the initial risk attitudes of 29 households . the energy attitude varies from very risk-averse (r -0.11) to risk-seeking (r-parameter). agents with learning capabilities (E-LBD, E-RO-SL1) diverge from risk-neutral TRAD agents . agents reduce herd sizes earlier (step 30) due to negative experience feedback . agent rely on local social learning (knowledge-radius=1.0) model demonstrates adaptive behavioral heterogeneity under uncertainty (risk-aware decisions with global precipitation shocks) sustains pastoralist resilience, exceeding single-strategy baselines by 30% in final biomass . biomass increases by 10-20% (45,000 55,000), with plateauing after step 60 . adaptive strategies (E-RO-SL1 social learning, E-LBD trial-and-error learning) stabilize grazing practices . the plateau values are lower than initial levels due to persistent climate uncertainty (rainfall variability) and legacy effects of early resource depletion . the model's risk-sensitive decision framework manifests in gradual herd concentration patterns feasible by step 100 when normalized values exceed 0.30 across most households . the data aligns with the RAGE model extension's hypothesis that heterogeneous learning behaviors combined with environmental uncertainty generate long-term inequality trajectories .
