 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The data table contains 40 simulation runs, each reporting the initial risk attitudes of 29 households . These runs feature static energy dynamics across all households during timesteps . The energy attitude varies from very risk-averse to strongly risk-seeking . The mean energy attitude across 29 households is 0.04, with standard error 0.0033 . The model features global rainfalls with annual mean 200 and standard deviation 100 .  The model captures emergent feedbacks between pasture degradation, risk perception, and adaptive decision-making . Social and ecological boundaries are met while managing stochasticity within domain-specific constraints .  All 60 households in a pasture-based system experienced identical destocking (6.0), suggesting synchronized resource depletion . This aligns with parameters: high livestock-init (90 animals/household) and global rainfall variability (rain-mean=200, rain-std=100) High pasture stress likely triggered immediate pasture stress, forcing households to cull livestock rapidly .  At early stages (timesteps 0–12), destocking remains minimal (0–13 animals), with all households maintaining similar low levels . Acceleration occurs between timesteps 15–30, with values reaching 18–24 animals as households respond to environmental pressures . At final timestep (99–100) destocking totals reach 69–171 animals, suggesting systemic collapse or strategic herd reductions in response to persistent stressors .
