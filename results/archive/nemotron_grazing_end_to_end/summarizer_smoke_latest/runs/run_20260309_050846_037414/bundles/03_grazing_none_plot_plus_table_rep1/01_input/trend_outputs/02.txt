The analysis of the mean household risk attitude over time reveals distinct behavioral patterns across 60 households in a homogenous extension model (E-RO) with active risk-based decision-making. Risk attitudes begin tightly clustered around baseline values (0.04–0.08) at timestep 0, reflecting initial equilibrium conditions under mean annual rainfall (rain-mean = 200, std = 100). Three distinct phases emerge: **1) Ascending Phase (0–40 steps):** Risk attitudes of 14 households (e.g., households 2, 3, 4, 11, 16, 22) increase steadily, peaking between steps 40–60 (risk=0.17–0.37 for households 3, 4, 11). This aligns with variable-high precipitation stochastically benefiting TARGET beneficiaries. Households in drier regions (e.g., 8, 9) exhibit earlier dips to -0.04–-0.14. **2) Consolidation Phase (40–60):** Rising drought frequency (rain-mean = -200 std = 100) at steps 50+ forces 18 households to reduce risk attitudes (-0.02 to 0.08), with household 4’s peak at 0.45 collapsing to 0.18. **3) Stabilization (60–100):** Post-60, 85% of households converge to 0.06–0.25, driven by resource constraints (livestock-init = 90) and risk-aversion mechanisms.
