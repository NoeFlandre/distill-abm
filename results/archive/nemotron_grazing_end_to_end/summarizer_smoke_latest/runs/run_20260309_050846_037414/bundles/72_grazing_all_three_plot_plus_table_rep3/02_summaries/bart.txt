 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The average initial risk attitude of social learning agents in the population demonstrates stability across simulation steps . The findings imply that in pasture-based systems with fixed behavioral strategies, risk management policies should target initial risk calibration rather than relying on adaptive behavioral adjustment .  Final values cluster between 0.18 and 0.44, with persistent high-risk attitudes potentially straining grazing systems due to overgrazing pressures . Risk-averse agents (households 21-30) hover near 0.22, possibly underutilizing pasture resources . For policymakers, this suggests that risk-neutral constrained pasture management could mitigate long-term system stress .
