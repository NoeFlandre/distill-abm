Terminal values cluster between 0.070–0.097 across all runs, signaling perverse outcomes where high-risk environments and learning strategies amplify disparities. This trajectory mirrors observable patterns in real-world grazing systems where pastoralists with access to adaptive strategies (via social learning or risk tolerance) maintain higher herd health, exacerbating health wealth inequality over time. The nonlinear growth pattern—accelerating in mid-simulation before plateauing—implies attaining limits tied to environmental stochasticity and strategy entrenchment.
