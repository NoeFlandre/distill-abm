 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The average household risk attitude begins with low variability across households, showing gradual increases in risk tolerance for agents . The average risk attitude rises steadily, peaking before plateauing by 40–0.5 by step 40 . Households exhibit sharper initial increases, but declining by step 60, suggesting convergence toward a risk-averse equilibrium .  The overall population stabilizes between steps 60–80, with mean risk attitude ranging 0.12–0.38, reflecting a balance between learning-by-doing (E-LBD: 20 agents) and socially learned risk aversion . The mean reserve biomass begins at 90,000 units and undergoes gradual fluctuations over the 27 steps .
