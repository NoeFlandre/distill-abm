 The agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . The context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . The model evaluates long-term outcomes like pasture biomass, livestock survival, and household resilience .  The model focuses on E-RO under homogeneous conditions with climatic variability . The total livestock and destocked households begin at approximately 90 and undergo an immediate, steep decline to 40-42 within the first 20 time steps . This initial decline and subsequent recovery likely reflect agents' annual stocking decisions interacting with climatic variability . By Step 70, biomass stabilizes near 62,500, indicating equilibrium between grazing pressure and climatic fluctuations .  The total number of earthquakes recorded in the region starts at 5,000 then it declines rapidly over the first 400 time steps . The total seismic activity follows the same pattern, starting at 10,000 events and declining steadily . This steady decline reflects a systematic reduction in seismic energy over time, as the energy distribution within the tectonic plates becomes more uniform .
