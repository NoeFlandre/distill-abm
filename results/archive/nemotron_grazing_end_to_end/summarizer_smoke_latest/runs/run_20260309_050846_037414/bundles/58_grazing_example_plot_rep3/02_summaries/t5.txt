agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . the context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . social learning (E-RO-SL1) is inactive due to homog-behav-types? . the mean household risk attitude starts at approximately -0.1 for all households at time step 0, reflecting initial cautious livestock stocking decisions . by time step 40, the majority of lines plateau near 0.5 with minor fluctuations . all trajectories asymptotically approach equilibrium between 118,000–128,000 units . the absence of social learning (homog-behav-types? = True) prevents strategic coordination, maintaining reliance on patch-specific resource . variable runs increases progressively after time step 50, with destocking rates diverging between 0 and 0.7 households in later steps . this divergence may stem from compounding effects of environmental stochasticity and household-specific risk attitudes . adoption stabilizes within a range of 30–50 households by step 50–60 . some simulations with higher risk attitudes exhibit earlier and more sustained social learning adoption . the variability in timing and magnitude across runs underscores the influence of stochastic elements .
