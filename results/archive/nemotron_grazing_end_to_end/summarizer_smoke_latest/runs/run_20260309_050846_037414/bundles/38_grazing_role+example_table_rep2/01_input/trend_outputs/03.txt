The total reserve biomass across all patches begins uniformly at 90,000 units, increases to 90,180 units by the second timestep, and peaks at 90,427 units by the third timestep. After this initial growth phase, biomass trajectories diverge significantly across runs. In many simulations, biomass declines steadily from the fourth timestep onward, reaching values between 88,000 and 89,000 units by the final timestep. Notable exceptions show modest stabilization or growth in specific runs peaking above 90,500 units near the midpoint, followed by gradual degradation. The extinction of certain grazing patterns and stochastic climate shocks amplify depletion in later stages. These declines reflect unmitigated grazing pressure from homogeneous behavioral strategies and climate instability, consistent with high livestock init stressing pastures and absence of adaptive behaviors in the simulated agent population. Recovery phases are absent in the majority of trajectories, aligning with the model’s emphasis on systemic resource limitations under constrained decision-making frameworks.
