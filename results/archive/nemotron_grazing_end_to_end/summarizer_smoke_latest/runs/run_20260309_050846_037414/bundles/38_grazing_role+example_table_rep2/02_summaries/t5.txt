agent-based model focuses on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . the original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . this extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity . key goals include evaluating long-term social-ecological performance of behavioral types . the average initial risk attitude of social learning agents exhibits minimal temporal variation, with values consistently clustering around 0.075 to 0.045 across the observed period . this pattern suggests a relatively stable risk disposition among learning agents, likely influenced by learning agents . the total reserve biomass across all patches begins uniformly at 90,000 units, increases to 90,180 units by the second timestep . after this initial growth phase, biomass trajectories diverge significantly across runs . extinction of certain grazing patterns and stochastic climate shocks amplify depletion in later stages . from timestep 40 to 60, the mean stabilizes around 45.7–50.3, suggesting a partial recovery during periods of higher rainfall or reduced herds . the final phase (timesteps 80–100) shows convergence toward a lower equilibrium between 40.0 and 46.8, where populations stabilize at a level consistent with the degraded but regenerating pasture base . this upward trajectory corresponds to agents transitioning from fixed rule-based behavior to adopting social learning (E-RO-SL1) through interactions within the **knowledge-radius** . variability in peak values suggests stochastic influences from rainy-day annual rainfall (**rain-std**=100) and randomized pasture responses, which may disrupt feeding patterns and create situational opportunities for behavioral adaptation . in approximately 10 of 25 simulation runs, the number of E-RO households remains constant at 20 throughout all time steps, suggesting stable behavioral dominance under baseline conditions . five simulations show early resilience, followed by abrupt declines typically reaching 15-20 households by the final time step .
