This pattern suggests that while short-term fluctuations in biomass are influenced by climatic variability (e.g., rainfall cycles) and initial resource allocation (e.g., livestock stocking decisions), long-term stabilization is driven by the system’s inherent resilience. Subtle variations in early steps reflect stochastic weather dynamics and agent decisions under the E-RO strategy, but the absence of behavioral adaptation (e.g., strategy switching or social learning) results in a systematic return to baseline biomass, consistent with Agente based simulation parameters (e.g., fixed rainfall characteristics, no resting, and absence of learning mechanisms).
