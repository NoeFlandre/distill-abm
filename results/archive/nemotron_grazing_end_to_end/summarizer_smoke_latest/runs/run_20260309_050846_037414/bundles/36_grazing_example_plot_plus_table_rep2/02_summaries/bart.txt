 The agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . The context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . The model evaluates long-term outcomes like pasture biomass, livestock survival, and household resilience .  The total number of earthquakes recorded in the region starts at 5,000 then it declines rapidly over the first 400 time steps . The total seismic activity follows the same pattern, starting at 10,000 events and declining steadily . The mean green biomass across all patches begins at 45,000 and remains relatively stable until step 20 .  The total livestock + destock metric initiates at 90 households across all simulations, reflecting the initial uniform animal stocking rate . The mean destock of households begins at approximately 0.0 across all households in the first time step, transitioning rapidly to a uniform value of 6.0 by step 2 . The Gini coefficient measuring inequality in livestock health across households starts near zero and increases steadily over 100 timesteps .  The long-time the agents were on the field, the larger the average value for destocked houses over 100 timesteps (years) would become . The values start at 0.5 destock cows and households for years 0 to 50 . From year 50 onwards,  there is a noticeable acceleration of the process . It might be due to added population pressure and ecological limits .
