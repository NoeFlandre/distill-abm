The graph shows social learning adoption in a simulated 60-household grazing system over 100 years. Early adoption (5-15 years) was uneven, with most runs (4) showing maximal adoption (44-48 households) by year 15. One run (yellow) achieved this by year 12, while orange and brown runs took 20-25 years. By year 60, all simulations plateaued at 36-48 households, indicating stable social learning regimes. The minimum (20 households) took 30 years in the light blue line, while the maximum (48) was achieved by year 15 in two runs (purple and orange), though total convergence occurred by year 60. Social learning adoption appears strongly tied to initial population size, learning mechanisms, and stress factors, suggesting social learning accelerates under environmental pressure but remains moderated by resource abundance thresholds.
