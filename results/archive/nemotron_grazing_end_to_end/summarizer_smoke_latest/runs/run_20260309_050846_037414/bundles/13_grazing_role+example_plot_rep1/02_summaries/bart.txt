 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The mean initial risk attitude of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) begins at 0.5 across all simulation runs . From time steps 0–30, the metric exhibits minimal deviation . This period aligns with simulation’s early grazing dynamics, where pastures remain relatively healthy . High values strain pasture resources immediately .  Simulation tracks mean reserve biomass across time steps, revealing growth patterns conditioned by stochastic rainfall and agent behavior . Biomass plateaus around step 50-60 (approximately 115,000) before declining steeply by step 90, suggesting systemic overextraction under persistent dry conditions . E-RO-SL1 agents display erratic trends, with risk attitudes oscillating between 0.2 and 0.8 depending on neighbors’ strategies within knowledge-radius=1.6 .  The number of earthquakes recorded in the region starts at 5,000 then it declines rapidly over the first 400 time steps . The total seismic activity follows the same pattern, starting at 10,000 events and declining steadily . This steady decline reflects a systematic reduction in seismic energy over time, as the energy distribution within the tectonic plates becomes more uniform .  The mean destocking total of households begins at approximately 160 per household at the simulation start (step 0), reflecting the maximum initial destocking capacity . Destocking values undergo a rapid decline to around 80 between steps 0–20, driven by strict pasture restoration rules (resting?=False, resting-threshold=0.1) and stochastic rainfall stress .
