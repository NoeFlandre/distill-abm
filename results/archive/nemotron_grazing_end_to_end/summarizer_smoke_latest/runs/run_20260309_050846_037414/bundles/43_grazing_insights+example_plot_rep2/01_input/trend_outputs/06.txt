By the 20th step, the average destock stabilizes below 2 households per timestep, suggesting partial adaptation to environmental limits. The final ~30 timesteps show plateauing destock rates near 0.5-1 household, indicating equilibrium between livestock demand and pasture biomass. This equilibrium suggests households achieved sustainable stocking levels through learning-by-doing (E-LBD agents) or social learning (E-RO-SL1 agents), though the homogeneous behavior constraint (homog-behav-types?=True) limits cross-type comparisons. The fixed resting-threshold (0.1) and disabled resting behavior created persistent grazing pressure, requiring continuous stocking adjustments.
