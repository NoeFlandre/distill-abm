agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . the context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . the model focuses on E-RO under homogeneous conditions with climatic variability . the mean household initial risk attitude remains constant across all time steps (from 0 to 100), showing no change over time . this indicates that the average risk attitude of households does not vary during the simulation . all simulations stabilize at step 90 with uniform green biomass levels approximately 62,000 to 64,000 units . the total number of livestock + destock starts at 90 households, then it declines rapidly over the first 20 steps . this initial decline could be attributed to the depletion of vegetation due to overexploitation . standardized Earth simulations handle baseline environmental pressures effectively at first . lack of adaptive behavioral mechanisms exacerbates disparities over time . policymakers might prioritize interventions that promote knowledge-sharing systems or flexible resource management protocols to mitigate long-term inequalities .
