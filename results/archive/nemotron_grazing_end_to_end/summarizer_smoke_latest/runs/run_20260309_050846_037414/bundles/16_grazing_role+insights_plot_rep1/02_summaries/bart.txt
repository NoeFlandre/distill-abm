 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  Simulation tracks the mean household initial risk attitude of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1 and E-LBD) across 100 timesteps . All agents start with a moderate risk tolerance (risk-att-init=0.5), but their risk attitudes evolve dynamically due to learning and environmental shocks . Agents prioritize adaptive herd size reductions to mitigate stochastic shocks .  The simulated mean reserve biomass across all grazing patches exhibits a U-shaped trajectory over 100 timesteps . Learning strategies (E-LBD/E-RO-SL1) reduce deviation from recommended herd sizes by 20–30% under variability . Resilient systems maintain >120,000 biomass through moderate herd reductions and dynamic grazing pressure adjustments .  The analysis of the metric describing mean livestock + destocked households reveals distinct behavioral patterns influenced by resource dynamics and strategy adaptation over 100-year timesteps . Social learning strategies (E-RO-SL1) demonstrate 15% higher effective nutrient cycling compared to solitary learning types under variable rainfall . The results suggest that integrating observational learning into decision-making frameworks could reduce destocking frequency by up to 20% .  Simulation tracks average cumulative livestock destocking across households over 100 timesteps . Destocking rates plateau at moderate levels (1–1.5 livestock/household/year) due to adaptive herd adjustments . Social learning (E-RO-SL1) becomes dominant, diffusing herd management strategies within communities .  Drought conditions (200 ± 100 mm annual rainfall) strain pastures, but agents prioritize maintaining herds until biomass threshold is critically approached . Low rate of destocking suggests reliance on tradition/restricted innovation under uncertain conditions .
