agent-based model focuses on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . the original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . this extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity . key goals include evaluating long-term social-ecological performance of behavioral types . the simulation tracks the mean household initial risk attitude of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1 and E-LBD) across 100 timesteps . all agents start with a moderate risk tolerance (risk-att-init**) E-RO agents (fixed-rule) exhibit no learning-related adaptation, maintaining a consistently high risk attitude (0.5–0.7) throughout, reflecting static responses to environmental stressors . simulated droughts (rain-std=100, rain-mean=200) but recover as stochastic rainfall patterns reduce pressure . trajectories with learning-capable agents (E-LBD, E-RO-SL1) demonstrating faster biomass rebound due to adaptive grazing management . by Step 85-100, biomass stabilizes between 58,000-63,000 for 90th percentiles . 10th per centiles remain below 55,000, revealing intrinsic system vulnerability to stochastic rainfall shocks (global-rain?=True, rain-std=100) initial destocking spikes occur between 0–10 steps due to combination of high starting herd sizes and pasture depletion . biomass regeneration rates (gr1=0.5 during wet seasons, gr2=0.1 during dry seasons) and stochastic climate shocks drive temporal variability . the simulation tracks the average cumulative livestock destocking across households over 100 timesteps . early trends (steps 1–10) suggest slow adoption, likely due to initial learning curve challenges . between steps 20–40, acceleration in adoption coincides with heightened herd management uncertainty . E-RO agents exhibit herd collapse at 70–80 timesteps due to inflexibility . they delay destocking but eventually align with dominant responses via localized learning . model emphasizes role of initial conditions in destabilizing systems under climate stress .
