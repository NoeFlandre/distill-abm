The simulation shows dynamic adjustments in household risk attitudes across 100 timesteps, reflecting strategic adaptations to pasture sustainability and climate uncertainty. Early timesteps (0-10) demonstrate initial divergence, with some households adopting higher risk attitudes (e.g., Household 5's attitude increases from 0.075 to 0.1375 by step 9), while others remain cautious. By step 20, several households experience sharp declines in risk attitude, particularly those initially adopting high-risk strategies (Household 15 drops from 0.1816666 to 0.05499999 at step 40 after peaking at 0.6583333 at step 19). This pattern suggests cumulative resource constraints from initial high herd sizes (90 livestock units) and no satisficing behavior (satisficing-threshold=0), leading to herd overcommitment that collapses under variable rainfall shocks (rain std=100).   Furthermore, the 40-60 timesteps reveal heterogeneous recovery trajectories. Households with moderate risk tolerance (e.g., Househouse 7's mean attitude fluctuates between 0.045 and 0.1641) show cyclical adjustments consistent with learning-by-doing mechanisms (E-LBD behavioral type), while socially learning households (E-RO-SL1) exhibit sharper declines mid-simulation (Househouse 22's attitude drops from 0.1183333 at step 49 to 0.04583333 at step 61). By timesteps 80-100, risk attitudes stabilize in a bifurcated pattern: a subset of households maintains elevated risk tolerance (Househouse 18 settles at 0.24999999) suggesting persistent optimism in managing dry-season biomass constraints (gr2=0.1), while most converge to negative values (-0.02 to -0.19), indicating systemic herd downsizing. These trends align with the model's design to test behavioral strategy diffusion under resource uncertainty, where heterogeneous responses emerge from interactions between intrinsic risk parameters (risk-att-init=0.5) and environmental feedbacks. The absence of converging strategies among extension agents (all initialized as E-RO) despite homogeneous initial conditions (-0.0 to 0.18) highlights both the complexity of adaptive learning dynamics and limitations of static behavioral initialization in multi-agent systems. Mean attitudes below zero in final steps may reflect institutional constraints not explicitly modeled, warranting further validation against observed farmer decision patterns.
