The Mean Household Initial Risk Attitude for social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) initially stabilizes at approximately 0.5 across all timesteps, reflecting their baseline tolerance for risk before climate shocks and adaptive learning influence decision-making. No significant divergence from this baseline is observed throughout the simulation. Over the 100-year period, minor fluctuations occur in response to stochastic rainfall variability and pasture stress, but the average value remains tightly clustered around 0.5. By timestep 100, the mean risk attitude exhibits a slight upward tendency (~0.52), suggesting partial adaptation to climatic uncertainty through observational learning and risk-sensitive adjustments. However, this minor shift is insufficient to counteract the persistent environmental variability (rain-std=100) and livestock pressure (livestock-init=90), which maintain systemic grazing stress. The flat trajectory underscores the resilience of E-RO-SL1 agents' baseline risk disposition despite dynamic environmental and social feedbacks.
