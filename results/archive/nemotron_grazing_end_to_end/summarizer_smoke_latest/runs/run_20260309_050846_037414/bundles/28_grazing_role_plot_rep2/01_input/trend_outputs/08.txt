The plot illustrates the dynamic evolution of social learning adoption across households over 100 time steps. Initially, the count of households engaging in social learning begins at a baseline influenced by two primary behavioral types: 20 households employing the E-RO-SL1 (social learning + fixed rules) strategy and 20 households utilizing E-LBD (learning-by-doing) strategies. The remaining 20 households follow fixed rules (E-RO) without learning. Social learning propagation is constrained by the **knowledge-radius** (1.0) and **social-susceptibility** (disabled at **global level**, though E-RO-SL1 agents retain intrinsic social learning capacity).
