 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The data reveals temporal patterns in the average initial risk attitudes of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1), reflecting adaptive behavioral strategies under stochastic climate conditions . Learning strategies mediate these patterns: E-LBD agents adjust based on cumulative experience with rainfall variability . Learning agents develop pasture management responses mitigating overgrazing, while fixed-rule agents contribute to pressure that prevents complete biomass collapse .
