By step 15, E-LBD agents adapt faster to local conditions, initiating a divergence where oilsh highest learning rates see 10-15% herd adjustments per step. The E-RO-SL1 agents, constrained by spatial information flow (radius=1), show slower adaptation but create localized feedback loops in adjacent patches.   

 A notable inflection occurs at step 28 following a simulated drought (rainfall=100). Herd mortality rates differ dramatically: sociolearning agents exhibit 30% less herd loss than E-RO herds through early adjustment of foraging patterns, accelerating inequality growth (Gini reaches 0.025 by step 35). Risk-sensitive decisions amplify this divergence, as risk-seeking herds with randomized herd size updates lose more livestock during the downturn. The combined effect of spatial learning and risk tolerance creates geometric spaces of divergence mid-simulation.   

 By step 60, localized adaptation minimizes uniform pasture degradation, but the system shows a bifurcation: traditional agents (E-RO) show declining herd health (coefficient trends 10% annual decay), while social learners aggregate in productive micro-regions (Gini coefficient stabilizes at 0.045-0.055). This clustering suggests emerging community-scale adaptive management patterns. Herd health dynamics show hysteresis - strategies demonstrating robustness reverse entropy in 40% of simulations post-step 75 through strategic grazing trade-offs. The long-term equilibrium displays persistent inequality, though at lower levels than peak mid-simulation values, suggesting behavioral adaptation modestly mitigates initial inequality drivers.   

 These dynamics imply that in pasture-based systems with random climate shocks, risk-sensitive learning strategies initially exacerbate inequality through differential adaptation outcomes. However, adaptive management patterns emerging from social learning may partially stabilize herd health distributions in long-term equilibrium. Key management considerations include timing interventions during early adaptation phases to address strategy divergence and capitalizing on localized knowledge aggregation in mature learning communities.
