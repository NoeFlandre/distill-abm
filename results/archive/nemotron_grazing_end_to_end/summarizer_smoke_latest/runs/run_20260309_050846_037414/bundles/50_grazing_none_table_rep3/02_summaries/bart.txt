 The agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . The context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . The model evaluates long-term outcomes like pasture biomass, livestock survival, and household resilience .  The model focuses on E-RO under homogeneous conditions with climatic variability . The data reveals consistent patterns in the mean initial risk attitudes of agents across multiple simulations . Spurious negative values (-0.04 to -0.11) appear in a subset of simulations, indicating minimal risk-seeking behavior .  The absence of social learning (E-RO-SL1 disabled via homog-behav-types?) limits strategy diffusion, resulting in heterogeneous risk attitudes without structured knowledge-sharing . By mid-simulation (e.g., 20–30 years), values fluctuate more widely, with some households exhibiting negative risk attitudes .
