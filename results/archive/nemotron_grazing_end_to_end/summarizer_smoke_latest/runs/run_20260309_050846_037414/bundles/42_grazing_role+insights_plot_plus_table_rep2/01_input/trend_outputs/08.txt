The analysis reveals a consistent upward trend in households adopting social learning (SL) over time, with stabilization near the simulation end. Initially, adoption is minimal (4-8 households by step 13), but a sharp increase occurs between steps 16-25, peaking at ~48 households by final step 29. Variability exists across simulations: some show gradual adoption (e.g., 6->12->24 households sequentially), while others demonstrate abrupt transitions (e.g., 19->27->36 households). The E-LBD (20 agents) and E-RO-SL1 (20 agents) behavioral types demonstrate distinct adoption trajectories, with E-RO-SL1 agents reaching deeper stabilization via social network effects despite disabled social susceptibility. This suggests learning strategies, particularly social learning, become widely adopted even in resource-constrained common property systems with stochastic climate stresses (rain-std=100), highlighting its resilience to environmental uncertainty. The results imply that individual learning behaviors catalyzed by E-LBD agents may enable broader strategy diffusion, offering insights for policymakers designing community adaptation programs in uncertain grazing contexts.
