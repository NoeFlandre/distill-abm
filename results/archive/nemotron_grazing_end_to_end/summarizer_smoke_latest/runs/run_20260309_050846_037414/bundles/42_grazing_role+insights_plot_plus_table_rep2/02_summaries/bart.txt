 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  An analysis of the data table reveals that the .mean initial risk attitude** (r-parameter) of social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) remains statistically stable across all 54 steps, oscillating within a narrow range . The stability implies that social learning dynamics (e.g., peer influence within **knowledge-radius**=1.0) and risk sensitivity do not induce systemic shifts in average risk tolerance .  The analysis of mean reserve biomass trends reveals a systematic decline in pasture conditions across the simulation's 100-year time frame . Social learning buffers against systematic risk, while risk-sensitivity causes divergent responses to random perturbations . Strategists should consider training programs that combine peer-based learning with adaptive risk management frameworks .
