The plot and data table show the mean green biomass trajectories across 100 timesteps for 30 simulation runs under homogeneous behavioral conditions (E-RO agents). Initially, biomass remains stable (~45,000 units) in all runs until timestep 20, after which a divergence emerges. Most runs exhibit a sharp decline until ~timestep 40, followed by recovery trajectories. Notably, runs labeled 'mean-green-biomass.21' and 'mean-green-biomass.11' demonstrate the steepest recovery phase, rebounding to >60,000 biomass by timestep 80. This contrasts with runs like 'mean-green-biomass.0' and 'mean-green-biomass.1,' which remain depressed (~40–45,000) throughout. The recovery phase accelerates between timesteps 50–80 in the fastest-recovering runs, with biomass increasing at ~700 units timestep (45,000 to 60,000 in 30 steps). The divergence suggests that while all agents adopt E-RO (satisficing-only) strategies, variations in initial conditions (e.g., 'global-rain? = True' with rain variability) or model parameters (e.g., 'satisficing-trials = 21') influence the speed and degree of biomass recovery. Decisions to conserve pasture—a factor implied by 'satisficing-threshold = 0'—appear critical, as runs with faster recovery show sustained grazing reductions post-decline. These dynamics highlight the model's sensitivity to parameter-coercive interactions, suggesting that even uniform behavioral strategies can yield divergent ecological outcomes depending on context-specific factors.
