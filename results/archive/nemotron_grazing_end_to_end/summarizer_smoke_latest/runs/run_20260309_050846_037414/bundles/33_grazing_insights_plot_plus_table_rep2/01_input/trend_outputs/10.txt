The data indicates that under homogeneous conditions (homog-behav-types? = True), the number of non-learning (E-RO) households remains constant at 20 across all time steps (1–100), as enforced by the parameter 'number-E-RO' and the exclusion of other behavioral types. Simulations with heterogeneous behavioral type distributions (homog-behav-types? = False) show initial variability in E-RO counts, with transient spikes up to 40 households and troughs near 0, followed by stabilization at 20. This reflects E-RO strategy adoption dynamics in mixed populations. When learning mechanisms (E-LBD, E-RO-SL1) are active, non-learning households decrease temporarily due to strategy switching, but rebound as parameter settings (e.g., social-susceptibility = 0) re-establish dominance. The stabilizing effect at 20 households across all scenarios underscores the importance of homogeneous behavioral type enforcement for consistent long-term outcomes in pastoral resource management.
