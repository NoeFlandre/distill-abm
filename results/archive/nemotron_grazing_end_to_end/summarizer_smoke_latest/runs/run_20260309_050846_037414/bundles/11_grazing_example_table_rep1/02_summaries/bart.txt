 The agent-based model extends the original RAGE model to study how behavioral types and learning mechanisms influence social-ecological dynamics in a common property grazing system . The context involves 60 households managing pastures on private property, with agents deciding how many livestock to stock annually . The model evaluates long-term outcomes like pasture biomass, livestock survival, and household resilience .  Social learning (E-RO-SL1) is inactive due to homog-behav-types? The model focuses on E-RO under homogeneous conditions with climatic variability, though parameters are ready for heterogeneous comparisons . The absence of social learning amplifies individual decision-making biases, as agents cannot align practices through observation .  The total number of livestock (including both healthy and destocked animals) across all households declines steadily over time . This is followed by more pronounced decreases, with livestock numbers falling below 60 by the 70th step and stabilizing near 40–42 by the final time steps . The decline reflects the cumulative impact of environmental variability, enabled by fixed rainfall, disrupting vegetation growth dynamics .  The number of livestock destocked by households begins at zero and remains stable for the first 20 time steps . From steps 21 to 40, a gradual increase occurs, reaching approximately 14 across households . This phase aligns with period of stabilizing modulo climate shocks and agent decision-making dynamics under homogeneous conditions .
