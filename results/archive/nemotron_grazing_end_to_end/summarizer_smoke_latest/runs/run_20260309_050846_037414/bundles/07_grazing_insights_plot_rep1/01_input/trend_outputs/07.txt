Initial years (steps 0-20) demonstrate near-equal distribution of healthy livestock across households (Gini < 0.005), reflecting similar starting conditions. A critical divergence emerges between steps 20-40, where Gini values rapidly rise to 0.02-0.04 across simulations, indicating early-stage inequality emergence. By steps 40-60, trends split into two pathways: conservative trajectories maintain moderate inequality (Gini 0.04-0.06) while progressive pathways show accelerating divergence (Gini >0.06), reaching up to 0.085 by step 100. The highest inequality outcomes correlate with reduced rainfall stability (evidenced by rainfall std=100), suggesting climatic variability exacerbates resource competition. Notably, scenarios with stronger social influence (E-RO-SL1) maintain lower Gini values (<0.07) through knowledge-sharing about grazing thresholds, while pure E-RO strategies without social learning exhibit higher inequality.
