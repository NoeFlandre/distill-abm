Implications and suggestions: 
Initial herding behavior weakens when the grazing land drops to approximately 41 after about 8 years of grazing. Waiting for the land to regenerate to higher levels early in the season would allow for more grazers as well as a more permitting environment for adequate regrowth and prevent exhaustion of the pasture and risk to animal survival with higher growth rates simultaneously helping. Having behavioral types control or adapt to the herd density and grazing trends during pasture depletion may be beneficial for long-term land productivity. This could indicate an opportunity for behavioral strategies to better manage the timing and magnitude of grazing improvements given those social dynamics and climate conditions.
