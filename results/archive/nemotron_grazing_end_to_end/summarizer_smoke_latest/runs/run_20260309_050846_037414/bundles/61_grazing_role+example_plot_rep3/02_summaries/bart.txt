 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  The mean household initial risk attitude starts at 0.5 and remains approximately constant across all 100 time steps . The average deviation from optimal livestock numbers begins at a high positive value in early time steps, reflecting initial overstocking pressures from livestock-init=90 herd sizes . Herd size adjustments driven by risk-aware decision-making show dampened responses to climate shocks .  The report describes the dynamics of livestock herds and the addition of livestock to residential areas in an agent-based model focused on smallholder farmers’ decision-making in pasture-based systems . The total number of livestock per household starts at 90 units (the maximum value indicated by the **livestock-init** parameter), representing a high initial stocking level that places immediate pressure on available pasture resources .  The Gini coefficient tracking inequality in livestock health across households evolves from near-zero initial equality to pronounced inequality over the 100-timestep horizon . The total number of households engaged in social learning (household-SL being true) begins near zero, rising sharply in the first 10 steps as initial conditions trigger adoption . By step 20, adoption plateaus for all strategies, with values stabilizing between 60–80 households across runs .  The total number of households following the non-learning E-RO strategy starts around 20 and declines to approximately 10 by the end of the simulation . Risk-sensitive decisions (**risk-att-init**=0.5) likely moderate herd adjustments during climatic shocks but are insufficient to overcome the limitations of the **E-RO** baseline strategy .
