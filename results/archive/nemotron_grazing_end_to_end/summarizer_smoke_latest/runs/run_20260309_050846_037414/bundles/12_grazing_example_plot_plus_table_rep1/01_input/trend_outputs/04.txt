Summary Description of Green Biomass Trends:   The average green biomass begins at 45,000 units and maintains this level for the first 40 steps, indicating initial system stabilization. From steps 40 to 100, biomass gradually increases, reaching approximately 64,000 units in the later stages of the simulation. However, this growth is not uniform across all simulation runs: late-stage deviations in biomass outcomes become more pronounced. This divergence among runs suggests heightened sensitivity to environmental variability (e.g., stochastic rainfall patterns with a standard deviation of 100) and agent decision-making patterns. The lack of social learning mechanisms and strategy diversity in this homogeneous baseline setup appears insufficient to mitigate environmental fluctuations at later stages. Notably, the absence of strategy-switching behavior prevents agents from adapting their stocking decisions in response to repeated droughts or abundance, limiting collective responses to extreme conditions. Biomass trends align with parameterized grass growth rates (gr1 = 0.5, gr2 = 0.1) and livestock herd pressures under these homogeneous conditions.
