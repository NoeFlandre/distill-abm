 The agent-based model is an extension of the RAGE model, focusing on simulating smallholder farmers' decision-making in pasture-based systems with learning capabilities . The original model was designed to analyze feedbacks between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in common property grazing systems . This extension adds heterogeneity in behavioral strategies (e.g., learning-by-doing, social learning) and introduces risk sensitivity .  An extended RAGE model has been used to study the dynamics of risk attitudes among social learning agents in the extended model . The initial risk attitudes (measured as `r-parameter` in the range [0–1.0, 1.0) exhibit no clear directional trend . This suggests agents repeatedly adjust their risk tolerance in response to environmental and social feedbacks .
