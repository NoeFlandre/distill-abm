The plot illustrates the dynamics of mean green biomass across 100 model years under homogeneous E-RO behavioral type settings. All simulations show a consistent pattern: biomass begins near 45,000 units, declines sharply to ~40,000 units by steps 30-40, rises rapidly to peak values of ~60,000-62,000 units between steps 60-80, and then stabilizes between 55,000-63,000 units. This cyclical pattern reflects sustained interaction between grazing pressure and vegetation regrowth processes. Post-peak stabilization values vary based on rainfall realizations, with simulations experiencing lower mean rainfall showing reduced long-term biomass stability. No significant divergence occurs between simulations, indicating E-RO agents consistently generate similar management trajectories under homogeneous conditions. The threshold-based grazing adjustments (satisficing-trials = 21) appear to auto-regulate grazing intensity after initial overexploitation, enabling recovery through endogenous behavioral adjustments rather than external interventions like resting.
