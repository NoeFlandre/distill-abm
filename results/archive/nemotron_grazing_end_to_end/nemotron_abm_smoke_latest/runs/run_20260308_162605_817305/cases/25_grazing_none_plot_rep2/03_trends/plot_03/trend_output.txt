The plot shows aggregated reserve biomass post 10-year trials of an extension to the RAGE model modifying decision strategies for livestock stocking. All trials begin with approximately 90,000 biomass units before experiencing estimated peak post ~25 iterations, creating a temporary V-shape pattern followed by stabilization around 115,000 units. Early simulation steps exhibit high variability between repetitions, with patterns converging as models learn through iteration. The first 30 simulation steps show estimated biomass fluctuations of -25% to +20% from initial levels, driven by impulsive livestoking managed through risk attitude settings. Ranier peaks around median simulation remaining suggest effective stocking wariness compensates for moderate rainfall fluctuations (avg 200mm/year, std 100mm) within fixed climate parameters. Post-stabilization biomass levels align with calculated carrying capacities for given livestock survival contributions : median ¬effectiveness in maintaining precarious grazing land productivity.
