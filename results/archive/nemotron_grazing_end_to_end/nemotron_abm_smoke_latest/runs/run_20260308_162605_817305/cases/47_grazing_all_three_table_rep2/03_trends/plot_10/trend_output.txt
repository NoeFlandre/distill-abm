The data indicates that the number of households following the E-RO (non-learning, fixed rule-based) strategy remains constant at 20 across all 30 time steps in most simulations. However, induced variations in later time steps (e.g., 23, 27, 32 households at specific steps) suggest measurement artifacts or data formatting inconsistencies rather than true behavioral shifts. These anomalies do not align with the model's design, where E-RO agents are non-learning and should remain stable. The persistent baseline behavior of E-RO households reflects their rigid decision-making, as they cannot adapt to environmental changes (e.g., stochastic rainfall, pasture dynamics) or social learning opportunities. This underscores their vulnerability in stochastic systems where flexibility is critical for long-term resilience. Decision makers should note that reliance on fixed strategies may exacerbate risks under climate uncertainty, as these agents cannot adjust herd sizes, pasture use, or adaptive practices in response to shocks like droughts or rainfall variability.
