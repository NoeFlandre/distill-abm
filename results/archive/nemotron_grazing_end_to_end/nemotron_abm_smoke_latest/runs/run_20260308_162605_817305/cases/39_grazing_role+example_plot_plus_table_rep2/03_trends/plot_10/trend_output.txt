The total count of non-learning households (E-RO strategy) begins at approximately 20–25 across most simulation runs and experiences a decline over time. This decline is not linear, showing fluctuations in certain time steps. For example, in some runs, the count increases transiently (e.g., from 20 to 27 by time step 12) before decreasing again. By the final time step, most runs stabilize near 10–20 households. Variance in the early time steps (e.g., steps 1–20) is moderate, but deviations between runs become minimal in the later time steps (steps 80–100), suggesting convergence toward equilibrium. These trends align with the model’s stochastic rainfall (average=200, std=100) and pasture dynamics (gr1=0.5, gr2=0.1), which create resource scarcity and grazing pressure. The decline may reflect households exiting the system or transitioning strategies due to environmental stress or risk aversion, as risk tolerance (risk-att-init=0.5) could drive adaptive herd size adjustments. The stabilization in later steps suggests that system shocks (e.g., pasture degradation or climate variability) reach a steady-state balance under the given parameters.
