The Mean Reserve Biomass exhibits a dynamic pattern over 100 steps, beginning at ~89,000 units. After an initial 5-step period of slight growth, biomass declines sharply to ~82,000 units between steps 5–20, likely reflecting overgrazing pressures from homogeneous E-RO agents stretching resources under variable rainfall (rain-mean=200, rain-std=100). From step 20–50, biomass recovers exponentially, reaching ~117,000 units by step 50, suggesting adaptive adjustments in livestock stocking decisions. This plateau persists with minor fluctuations, indicating stabilization at a sustainable equilibrium (~117,000±2,000 units). The sharp recovery contrasts with the gradual slope between steps 5–20, highlighting nonlinear agent strategy adaptation to climatic shocks. Notably, two simulations peak slightly above the plateau (~118,000 units at step 60), potentially reflecting aggressive stocking strategies mediated by risk-att-init=0.5. The ~35,000-unit increase from the trough (~82k) to plateau (~117k) demonstrates system resilience despite homogeneous attractor-driven decision-making, with the resting-threshold=0.1 and satisficing-trials=21 enabling threshold-based resource reallocation without invoking SL2-strategy-switch or social learning mechanisms.
