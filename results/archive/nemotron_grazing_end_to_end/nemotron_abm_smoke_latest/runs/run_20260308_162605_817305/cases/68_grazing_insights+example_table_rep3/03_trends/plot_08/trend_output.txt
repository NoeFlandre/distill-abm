The adoption of social learning among households begins at zero during the first five time steps, reflecting a lag between environmental pressures and behavioral adaptation. A gradual increase emerges by time step 5, peaking between time steps 15 and 20 with adoption rates exceeding 50 households (mirroring the growth in livestock resilience and biomass stability observed in E-LBD-rich runs). By time step 30, the trend plateaus near 20 households, suggesting saturation of social learning capacity under homogeneous behavioral conditions. This stabilization occurs despite climatic variability, indicating that E-RO-SL1 agents’ capacity to leverage social learning remains constrained by the model’s restrictive resting-threshold (0.1) and absence of explicit conservation incentives (satisficing-threshold = 0). The plateau implies that decision-makers should consider hybrid strategies combining limited social learning frameworks with targeted thresholds to sustain long-term social-ecological resilience in similar systems.
