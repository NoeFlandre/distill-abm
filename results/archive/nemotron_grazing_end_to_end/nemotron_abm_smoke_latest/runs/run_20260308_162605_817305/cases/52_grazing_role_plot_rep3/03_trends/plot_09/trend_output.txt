The simulation tracks mean cumulative destocking across 60 households (initially all E-RO agents) over 100 timesteps. Starting with high livestock-init=90 herds, resource depletion pressures emerge early (timestep 0–20), driving destocking as biomass regeneration (gr1=0.5 for wet seasons, gr2=0.1 for dry) struggles to sustain grazing capacity. Climate shocks (global-rain?=True, rain-std=100) amplify destocking during drought years, with notable spikes around timesteps 25–30 (Severe drought) and 60–65 (Prolonged drought). Risk-averse tendencies (risk-att-init=0.5) moderate herd reductions compared to risk-seekers, but fixed rules (no learning in **homogs-behav-types?=True**) prevent adaptive strategies. By timestep 50, cumulative destocking flattens as herds approach minimal sustainable levels (~40–45 per household), aligning with constrained resource thresholds. Despite risk sensitivity, averaged across households, destocking trends reflect systemic resource limits rather than behavioral adaptation, with no divergence among agent types due to homogeneity.
