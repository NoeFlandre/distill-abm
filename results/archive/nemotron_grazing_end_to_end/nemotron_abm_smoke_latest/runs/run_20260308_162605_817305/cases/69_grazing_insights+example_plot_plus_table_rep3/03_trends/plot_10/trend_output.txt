The number of households consistently following the non-learning E-RO strategy fluctuates initially but stabilizes around 20 by the final time steps across nearly all scenarios. Most simulations show initial increases (27-39 households) peaking within the first 10-15 steps, particularly in cases where counts surpassed 35. By ~20 steps, numbers drop below 25 in nearly all cases but rebound, reaching 25-30 households between 40-50 steps. The final phase (60-100 steps) exhibits minimal change, with 18-22 households remaining. A subset of runs (e.g., 23-25 households) achieves equilibrium where tiny fluctuations (-2 to +3 households) persist but do not cross 20. Notably, stress peaks correlate with abrupt declines (e.g., 39 to 14 households) that later recover, suggesting sensitivity to intermediate dynamics. The absence of long-term directional trends implies non-learning agents maintain stable resilience under homogeneous conditions despite climatic variability, aligning with the goal of evaluating E-RO strategy robustness.
