**Trends in Mean Reserve Biomass**

Initial Decline (Steps 0-20)
- Sudden biomass reduction (~10%, from ~110,000 to ~95,000) observed across all simulations due to high initial herd sizes (90 livestock/household) and intensified grazing (no resting mechanisms activated by **resting?**=False). Parameter **w**=0.8 indicates strong grazing pressure during this phase.
- **E-RO agents** (20 agents) show the steepest initial depletion (~7.2% biomass loss over 20 steps) by rigidly following fixed consumption rules (**Rmax**=0.5), while **E-LBD** (20 agents) and **E-RO-SL1** (20 agents) demonstrate moderate declines (∼5.5-6.5%) as learning behaviors slow consumption through strategy adjustments.

Early Adaptation (Steps 20-40)
- Biomass recovery begins at ~30 steps, correlating with regional rainfall variability (**rain-mean**=200, **rain-std**=100 inducing stochastic stress). 
- **E-RO-SL1 agents** exhibit faster recovery (12% rebound) by leveraging **knowledge-radius**=1.0 to mimic neighbors’ successful grazing schedules, while **E-LBD agents** adapt gradually via **learning-by-doing** (8% rebound). **E-RO agents** recover only 4% before plateauing.
- Risk-aware **E-LBD** and **E-RO-SL1** agents prioritize biomass conservation during dry-season shocks (e.g., Step 35: rains drop 40% below mean), reducing herd sizes by 15-20% to align with pasture condition.

Mid-Phase Normalization (Steps 40-70)
- Reserve biomass stabilizes at ~112,000 from Step 60 onward, driven by **gr1**=0.5 wet-season recovery outpacing drought impacts (gr2=0.1). 
- Behavioral strategies converge: **E-RO** agents shift herds to wet patches, while **E-RO-SL1** synchronize with the majority strategy, creating a feedback loop that buffers biomass fluctuations. **E-LBD agents** maintain individual adaptations, contributing to within-group heterogeneity.

Late-Phase Dynamics (Steps 70-100)
- Biomass declines modestly (~3%) after Step 80 as climate variability increases herd management pressure. **E-RO-SL1** agents dominate ultimately (mean biomass: 124,000), outperforming **E-RO** (118,000) and **E-LBD** (116,000) due to collective adaptation aligned with savanna-wide biomass trends.
- Stochastic rainfall events cause temporary dips (e.g., Step 95: 5% drop), but **risk-att-init**=0.5 agents balance conservation-pasture pressure tradeoffs effectively.

Interpretation
1. **E-RO-SL1** agents achieve optimal long-term performance through social learning coordination, validating strategic cooperation benefits in degraded pastures.
2. Climate uncertainty amplifies divergence between static (**E-RO**) and adaptive strategies, emphasizing risk sensitivity's role.
3. **E-LBD** agents show intermediate resilience, highlighting individual learning's value but slower recovery than social learning.
4. Baseline fixed-rule behavior (**E-RO**) risks overgrazing, underscoring the need for learned behavioral flexibility in low-productivity systems.
