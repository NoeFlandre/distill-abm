Biomass initiates near 90,000 units in all runs before declining sharply during the first 40 steps, reaching a trough around steps 20-40. The steepest declines occur when grazing pressure from high initial herd sizes (90 livestock/household) exceeds regenerative capacity, particularly in runs with stochastic rainfall creating compounding stress. By step 40, biomass rebounds in most runs, accelerating after step 60 to stabilize between 120,000-130,000 units. This delayed recovery aligns with pasture regeneration cycles (gr1=0.5 for wet, gr2=0.1 for dry) offsetting earlier overexploitation. Variance increases initially (<2,000 units between run quantiles by step 20) but decreases as systems converge to management equilibria post-step 60, suggesting self-regulation of grazing pressure despite high-risk tolerance (risk-att-init=0.5) and lack of formal rest protocols.
