The data on E-RO households across timesteps shows a clear long-term decline in their prevalence, starting from 20 households at timestep 0 and dropping to values between 1–2 in the final timesteps (42–55). Initial stability is observed up to ~timestep 20–25, where counts fluctuate between 13–23 before accelerating downward. This trend suggests that non-learning agents (E-RO) gradually lose relative fitness compared to learning-capable behavioral types (E-LBD, E-RO-SL1) within the same simulation setup. The presence of global rainfall variability (rain-mean=200, rain-std=100) introduces environmental stress, likely disproportionately affecting E-RO agents who cannot adapt strategy parameters based on outcomes or social learning. Their decline mirrors expectations in dynamic systems where risk-averse or rule-bound behaviors fail to respond to resource scarcity or climatic shocks. However, the low absolute counts (e.g., dropping to single digits) indicate that E-RO may face near-extinction risks in multi-generational simulations without intervention, underscoring the importance of integrating adaptive decision-making into smallholder management practices for sustainable livelihoods under uncertainty. The homogeneous behavioral-type initialization (all agents starting as E-RO with parameters override?) appears inconsistent with the observed diversification of behavioral types in later timesteps, suggesting either model-specific emergent dynamics or potential parameter misinterpretation in the simulation.
