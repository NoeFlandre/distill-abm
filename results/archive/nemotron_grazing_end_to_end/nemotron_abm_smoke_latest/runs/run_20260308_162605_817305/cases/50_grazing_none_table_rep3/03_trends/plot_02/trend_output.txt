The data reveals dynamic patterns in household risk attitudes across the 100-year simulation period, reflecting adaptive behaviors under climatic variability. Early time steps (e.g., first 5–10 years) show moderate to low risk attitudes (mean values: 0.01–0.075), indicating cautious stocking decisions likely influenced by the baseline E-RO strategy's risk-averse calibration. By mid-simulation (e.g., 20–30 years), values fluctuate more widely, with some households exhibiting negative risk attitudes (e.g., -0.1117), suggesting increased conservatism possibly linked to negative rainfall shock impacts on pasture biomass. Later stages (years 50–100) display greater variability, with mean values ranging from -0.089 to 0.36, implying diversified adaptation strategies emerging despite homogeneous behavioral types.
