The gini_coef_total_livestock_healthy metric exhibits a distinct temporal pattern across the simulation time steps. During the initial phase (timesteps 0-23), the values remain approximately zero, indicating near-homogeneous distribution of healthy livestock across households. A marked increase in inequality begins at timestep 24, with values ranging from 1.91E-4 to 3.42E-4, peaking at timestep 27 with a value of 3.42E-4. This represents a 179% increase compared to the baseline. The peak is followed by a gradual decline to 2.42E-4 at timestep 29, suggesting partial stabilization of distribution. Notably, the metric shows periodicity with increasing amplitude up to timestep 27, followed by attenuation. Environmental stochasticity, particularly through rainfall variability (std=100) combined with homogeneous E-RO behavioral strategy adoption, appears to drive these dynamics. The observed patterns suggest that while baseline assumptions predict homogeneous outcomes, neither environmental variability nor risk-averse preferences (risk_att-init=0.5) prevent the emergence of temporary inequality. The temporal pattern indicates resilience of the system's equity after three simulation timesteps, despite sustained climatic stress and absence of social learning mechanisms.
