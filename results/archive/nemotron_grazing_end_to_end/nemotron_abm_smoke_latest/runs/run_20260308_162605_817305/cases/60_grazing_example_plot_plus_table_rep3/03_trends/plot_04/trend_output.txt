The total mean green biomass across patches begins at approximately 45,000 units and remains nearly constant for the first 20-30 time steps. This initial stability suggests a baseline equilibrium under the current parameter settings. Starting around step 40, all runs show a rapid upward trajectory, with biomass values increasing by approximately 1,000-2,000 units per step until reaching stabilization near 62,000 units between steps 80 and 100. This recovery phase correlates with the model's climatic variability parameters (rain-mean=200, rain-std=100), which likely drive vegetation regrowth despite continuous grazing (resting?=False). The convergence of all 29 simulation runs implies robustness of this trend to initial conditions and stochastic elements. Notably, the disabled strategy-switching (SL2-strategy-switch?=False) and homogeneous behavioral types (homog-behav-types?=True) appear to constrain inter-run variability, as no deviation in long-term outcomes is observed across simulations. While the specific behavioral configurations (E-RO, E-LBD, E-RO-SL1) could not be differentiated in this setup, the model's focus on climatic impact over behavioral strategy is evident in the consistent biomass recovery pattern across runs.
