The dynamics of E-RO households across simulations exhibit significant variability in behavioral stability. In approximately 10 of 25 simulation runs, the number of E-RO households remains constant at 20 throughout all time steps, suggesting stable behavioral dominance under baseline conditions. Five simulations show early resilience, with E-RO households maintaining counts above 20 until time step 15, followed by abrupt declines typically reaching 15-20 households by the final time step. Three simulations experience sudden collapses, dropping from initial values of 26-32 households to zero by time step 29, potentially reflecting compounded behavioral shifts or stress factors. The remaining seven runs demonstrate gradual declines, with E-RO households reducing at a median rate of 1 household per 5 time steps after initial stability. Notably, no simulation exhibits de novo E-RO formation, consistent with the parameter setting that disables social learning (**social-susceptibility=0.0**) and fixed initial behavioral groups. Final E-RO counts cluster between 0 and 20 households, with a median of 18, indicating partial behavioral persistence despite heterogeneous disruption patterns. Key transition points occur at time steps 3-7 in rapidly declining runs, where 70-85% of initial E-RO households transition to other strategies, while stable runs maintain >90% retention until final time steps.
