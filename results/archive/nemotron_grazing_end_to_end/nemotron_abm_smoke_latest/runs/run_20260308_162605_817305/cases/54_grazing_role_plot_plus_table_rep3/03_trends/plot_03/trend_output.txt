The modeled system exhibits a dynamic interplay between grazing pressure, pasture recovery, and adaptive behavioral strategies across the 100-year simulation period. Initial biomass averaged ~90,000 units, with gradual decline to ~85,000 units due to livestock pressure. Subsequently, biomass recovered to ~110,000–125,000 units by timestep 50, reflecting adaptive management responses as agents adjusted herd sizes relative to pasture conditions.
