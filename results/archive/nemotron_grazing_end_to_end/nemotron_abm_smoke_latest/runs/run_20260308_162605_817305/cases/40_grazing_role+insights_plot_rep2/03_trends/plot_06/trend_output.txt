The plot depicts the average number of livestock removed due to insufficient resources across all households over 100 simulation years. Initial destocking is statistically high and relatively consistent across all agents at Year 0. The average number of destocked livestock then appears to fluctuate until about Year 20 before stabilizing with no significant changes. 

The fluctuations in the initial years could be attributed to the high initial herd sizes and the stochastic climate shocks induced by global rainfall variability. As the simulation progresses, the destocking levels start to converge, suggesting an adaptation or adjustment in the agents' behavior in response to the environmental constraints and the risk-aware decision-making mechanism enabled in this model run. The long-term stability in destocking numbers could be interpreted as a sign of the system reaching a steady state, where the strategies employed by the households effectively manage the trade-offs between herd size and environmental carrying capacity. 

From a decision-making perspective, the initial high destocking and the subsequent stabilization could imply that households may need to carefully manage their herd sizes and grazing strategies in response to climate variability and other uncertainties. Agents that employ learning strategies, particularly those that include social learning (E-RO-SL1) or learning by doing (E-LBD), might be better equipped to adapt to changing conditions and moderate the stress on pastures, leading to more sustainable outcomes. The presence of climate variability, as reflected in the simulation, underscores the importance of factoring in environmental uncertainty when designing pasture-based systems. In agricultural policy design, this suggests that supporting farmers' risk management skills and promoting adaptive strategies could be beneficial for long-term sustainability and food security. Including the risks associated with climate variability and promoting diversification of income sources may also be crucial to foster adaptive behaviors in the face of environmental uncertainty.
