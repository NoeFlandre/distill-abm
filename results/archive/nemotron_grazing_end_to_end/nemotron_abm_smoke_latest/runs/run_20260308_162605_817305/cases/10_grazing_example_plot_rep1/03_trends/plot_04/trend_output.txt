The simulation demonstrates a phased dynamic in mean green biomass across all runs. Initially, biomass declines between time steps 15–40 from approximate starting values of 45,000–50,000 down to 40,000–45,000. This likely reflects immediate resource pressure as agents stock livestock annually, leveraging the fixed rainfall variability (rain-mean = 200, rain-std = 100) that destabilizes vegetation growth. The recovery phase begins around time steps 40–60, with most trajectories surging to 60,000–63,000 by steps 60–80. This surge correlates with the satisficing-threshold (set to 0) activating conservation behaviors when biomass drops below a critical level, compounded by social learning effects enabled by knowledge-radius = 1. The final stabilization phase (steps 80–100) at 60,000–63,000 suggests equilibrium between grazing pressure and regenerative capacity, with rain-std = 100 and risk-att-init = 0.5 enabling adaptive decision-making under uncertainty. Notably, all runs converge in later phases, indicating robust outcomes under homogeneous E-RO behavioral types despite stochastic environmental variation. The absence of resting? = False and intrinsic-preference = 0 suggests that explicit conservation rules, not intrinsic preferences, drive recovery, while risk-mode? = True facilitates responsiveness to climatic shocks.
