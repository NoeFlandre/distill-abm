The mean reserve biomass exhibits three distinct phases of fluctuation. Initially, biomass remains stable at 90,000 across all patches for the first six time steps, suggesting an equilibrium state under baseline grazing pressure from E-RO agents. At time step 6, biomass rises to 90,180, followed by a gradual increase to ~90,528 by step 10, coinciding with the emergence of behavioral heterogeneity.  

Following this, biomass oscillates narrowly between 90,427–90,553 until step 28, with minor declines (e.g., 89,318 at step 29), reflecting stochastic climate impacts (rain-std=100) and risk-sensitive herd management. The final time steps show a sharp 40% increase (89,318 → 128,000), potentially indicating adaptive responses to prior biomass stress, such as reduced grazing pressure from learning agents (E-LBD, E-RO-SL1) or improved pasture recovery (gr1=0.5). However, the abrupt recovery exceeds initial herd capacity (livestock-init=90), suggesting either model-specific dynamics or compensatory feedbacks in the extension model's strategy adoption.
