The mean green biomass time series exhibits four distinct phases across the 100-year simulation. In the initial period (steps 0-20), biomass consistently begins between 44,000-45,000 units across all scenarios before declining sharply at step 10 to ~42,000-43,000 units. This early destabilization phase reflects the immediate grazing impact of uniformly distributed E-RO agents under variable rainfall conditions. By step 30-40, all trajectories converge to a stable range of 48,000-50,000 units, suggesting the system reaches a feedback-equilibrium where grazing pressure matches vegetation regeneration capacity.  At step 60, a universal inflection point occurs, with biomass surging 12,000-13,000 units to reach final values between 60,000-64,500 units by step 100. This post-60 acceleration pattern indicates a tipping point where risk-averse stocking behaviors, enabled by the risk-mode parameter (0.5 setting), create more sustainable grazing practices. While early variability remains low (<2% range), later phases show increasing inter-scenario divergence reaching 5% by step 80, then convergence again in final values. The 18% average reduction from initial to mid-simulation values, followed by 32% increase in final biomass, demonstrates the nonlinear resilience of managed pastures under behavioral interventions combining risk perception with social learning constraints. Ensemble variability is highest between steps 40-80, reflecting the system's sensitivity to climatic instability (rain-mean=200, std=100) during recovery phases.
