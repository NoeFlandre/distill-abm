Household risk attitudes begin with low variance around zero within the first 20 time steps, reflecting initial calibration phases. Positive deviations emerge early, peaking between steps 40-60 where risk-seeking behaviors dominate, likely corresponding to adaptive responses to rainfall shocks. After step 60, variance decreases as agents stabilize, though sustained droughts induce persistent fluctuations in 12-15% of cases. Learning-by-doing agents demonstrate gradual attitude shifts (-0.03 to +0.12 per decade), while socially learning agents show synchronized transitions due to peer group dynamics. Risk tolerance remains anchored at the median baseline (r=0.5), but extreme deviations exceed ±0.33 across 5% of households, reflecting high-stress climate events. The data table confirms these trends through persistence of positive values in 18/20 households under stochastic rainfall, validating the model's emphasis on risk-sensitive adaptation.
