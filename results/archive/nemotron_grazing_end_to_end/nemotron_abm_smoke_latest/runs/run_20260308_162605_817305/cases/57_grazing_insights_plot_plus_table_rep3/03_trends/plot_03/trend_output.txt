The simulations show that under homogeneous conditions with fixed climatic variability, the mean reserve biomass consistently increases over the 100-year timeframe, with trajectories diverging into distinct patterns. After this initial phase, stabilization occurs as trajectories converged closely around 120,000-128,000 units.   \n\nKey observations include:   \n1. Stabilization phases demonstrate resilience to climatic shocks, as biomass levels rebound to ~120,000-125,000 units despite rainfall variability.   \n3. Trajectory paths vary dramatically in intermediate periods, with some fluctuations exceeding ∼30% of maximum reserve biomass, reflecting systemic nonlinearities in stock-keeping responses.   \n\nCritical thresholds appear at ∼90,000 units, where competitive lifting strategies (risk-averse stocking) become viable, and ~110,000 units, where resource constraints lead to equilibrium dynamics.
