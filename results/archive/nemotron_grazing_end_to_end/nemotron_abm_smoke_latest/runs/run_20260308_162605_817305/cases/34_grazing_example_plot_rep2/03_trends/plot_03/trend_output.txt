The graph depicts the mean reserve biomass of the agent-based model, reflecting the condition of biomass in the model's grazing system over 100 time steps for 60 households. The y-axis represents the mean reserve biomass, whereas the x-axis represents the number of steps. We observe that the biomass for all E-RO simulations rides around 90000, from there, it makes a steep slope and finally converges at 130000 from step 55 onwards. All the simulations start at around 90000 where 5 to 10 people have dropped below 80000. The curves sample a degree of heterogeneity across iterations, but none come close to depicting a monotonic ascent throughout. The biomass for nearly all simulations far below 130000 reaches a constant value between step 70 and 90. Most of the simulations depart with substantial variance within the first 50 time steps before stabilizing.
