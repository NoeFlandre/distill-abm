The data reveals a consistent downward trend in mean reserve biomass over the 100-year simulation period under homogeneous E-RO (baseline) behavioral strategies. Initial biomass levels begin at approximately 90,000 units, with slight temporal variation across simulations. Within the first 20-30 timesteps, biomass stability is observed in some runs, followed by rapid declines in later years. By timestep 25, biomass levels drop below 90,000 in all runs, with extreme cases reaching ~86,000-83,000 by timestep 100. The magnitude of decline correlates with cumulative climatic variability, as higher rainfall instability (rain-std = 100) amplifies pressure on pasture recovery. No simulations demonstrate resilience or recovery, indicating systemic susceptibility to degradation under static decision-making.  

Decision-makers should note that homogeneous, non-adaptive strategies (e.g., E-RO) fail to sustain biomass in variable climates, suggesting urgency for integrating learning mechanisms (LBD, SL) to improve resource management outcomes.
