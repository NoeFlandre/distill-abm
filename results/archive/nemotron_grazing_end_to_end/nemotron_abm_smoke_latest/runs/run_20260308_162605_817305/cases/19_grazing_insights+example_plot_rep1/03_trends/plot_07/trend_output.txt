The Gini coefficient for total livestock health across all households generally increases over time, indicating rising inequality in livestock health outcomes. Initially, at step 0, all households have equal livestock health (Gini = 0). By step 20, divergence begins, with some households accumulating higher livestock health while others stagnate. A notable acceleration in inequality occurs between steps 40–60, where the metric peaks in many runs, suggesting critical turning points linked to climate variability and resource stress. By step 100, the Gini coefficient reaches 0.09–0.1 in most simulations, reflecting persistent disparities. Subtle inflection points near step 70 in some runs may correlate with climatic events (e.g., prolonged rainfall deficits or surpluses) amplifying strategic decision-making consequences. These trends imply that homogeneous E-RO agents, constrained by inactive resting and fixed behavioral thresholds, exhibit delayed but compounding inequities as environmental variability interacts with static decision rules. The results underscore the sensitivity of household outcomes to stochastic climate interactions under uniform strategies.
