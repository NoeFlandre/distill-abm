In the repeated simulations, the count of households with social learning (household-SL = true) starts at 0 at step 0 and undergoes an initial rapid increase, reaching approximately 10–12 households by step 10 and around 20–25 by step 20. By step 30–40, the count rises further to approximately 30–35, then plateaus or stabilizes around 40–42 households by step 40–50, maintaining a relatively stable level for the remaining steps. The trend shows a steep ascent in the early stages (first ~40 steps) followed by a plateau, with slight variations in plateau height across simulation runs. This suggests that under the homogeneous behavioral type setup (homog-behav-types? = True), the adoption of social learning accelerates quickly in the initial steps, likely due to agents’ ability to observe and learn from neighbors within the knowledge-radius (1 patch) and the presence of social learning behaviors (E-RO-SL1 agents), but eventually reaches a stable adoption level as the system stabilizes. For decision makers, this indicates that in similar homogeneous settings, social learning adoption is rapid but reaches a plateau, which could inform strategies for promoting social learning adoption in resource management contexts where behavioral homogeneity exists.
