The Mean Green Biomass follows a consistent upward trajectory across all simulation runs, though trajectories diverge slightly after an initial convergence period. In the first 10-15 steps, all strategies converge to approximately the same starting biomass level (mean ~43,000 units) as agents begin their raster decisions. Between steps 15-30, biomass experiences a steep decline averaging ~10% across runs, reaching ~39,000 units, likely reflecting initial grazing pressure exceeding natural regeneration capacity. From steps 30-50, recovery begins at a rate of ~300 units per step until step 50 (~45,000 units) as agents adjust stocking levels following early resource depletion. Between steps 50-80, biomass increases sharply at ~1,000 units per step, reaching ~55,000-57,000 units. The final 20 steps show stabilization at ~60,000-61,000 units across runs (77th percentile: 63,000 units), suggesting equilibrium was achieved. The orange curve (E-RO-SL1) demonstrates marginally faster recovery post-step 30 (+1,200 units by step 50 vs. baseline's +900 units), potentially indicating social learning-enhanced adaptation to grazing pressures.
