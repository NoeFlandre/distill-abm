The number of households following the E-RO strategy exhibits a range of behaviors across the simulation steps. Initially, all households start at a count of 20, reflecting the initial setting of 20 E-RO households. As the simulation progresses, there is a noticeable divergence in trends. Several simulation runs show a sharp decline in the number of E-RO households within the first 20 steps, dropping to values as low as 0 in some cases. This indicates a rapid transition away from the E-RO strategy in these scenarios. Conversely, other simulation runs maintain higher counts of E-RO households throughout the simulation, with some stabilizing around 25 to 30 after an initial fluctuation. This suggests a slower adoption of alternative strategies in these cases. Over the latter half of the simulation, most runs converge to a steady state, with the count of E-RO households ranging between 2 and 30. The variability in trends highlights the sensitivity of the E-RO strategy to initial conditions and stochastic elements within the model. For decision-makers, these results underscore the importance of understanding local conditions and potential triggers that might accelerate or delay the adoption of new strategies among farmers.
