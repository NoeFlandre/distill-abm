The number of households practicing social learning starts at zero across all simulation runs. A rapid and significant increase occurs between time steps 0 and 40, indicating that social learning behaviors are quickly adopted by the population as agents observe outcomes. The rate of adoption is most aggressive during the initial 20 time steps. Around time step 40, a distinct change point occurs where the rate of new adoptions slows considerably for most simulation runs. By time step 63, the mean number of social learning households reaches its peak at approximately 32. Following this point, the aggregate trend stabilizes with minimal growth, maintaining this level until the simulation concludes at time step 100. While the average value is approximately 32, individual simulation runs exhibit significant variability in their final equilibrium states, with outcomes ranging from approximately 15 to 54 households. This divergence suggests that while the mechanism for adoption is consistent, local stochastic dynamics result in different long-term saturation levels for the social learning strategy.
