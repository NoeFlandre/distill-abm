The number of households engaging in social learning begins at zero for all simulation runs at the initial time step. A rapid increase occurs between time steps 5 and 25, where the count rises sharply. By time step 40, the number of households reaches a plateau for the majority of runs. The final counts at time step 100 vary significantly across simulations, stabilizing in a range between 16 and 54. No further increases in the number of socially learning households are observed after time step 60. This trajectory suggests that the adoption of social learning behavior is driven by early interactions, likely facilitated by the knowledge-radius of 1, allowing information to propagate through the network of 60 households. The variance in final counts indicates that stochastic elements in the simulation lead to different saturation levels of social learning adoption among the households.
