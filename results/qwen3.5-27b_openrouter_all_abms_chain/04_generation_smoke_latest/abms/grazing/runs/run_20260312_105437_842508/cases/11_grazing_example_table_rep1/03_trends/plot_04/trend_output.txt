The average available green biomass across all patches begins at 45,000 units at the start of the simulation. An initial decline is observed, with the metric reaching a minimum value of approximately 42,057.04 at time step 27. This initial reduction likely corresponds to a period where grazing pressure temporarily exceeded the immediate regrowth capacity of the pasture. Following this low point, the system undergoes a distinct shift, entering a phase of sustained growth. Statistical analysis confirms a significant increasing trend establishing itself from around time step 33 onwards. By the final recorded time step of 100, the biomass recovers and surpasses initial levels, peaking at approximately 60,535.28. This long-term recovery suggests that after an initial adjustment period, the interaction between the learning agents and the pasture conditions results in a net accumulation of vegetation, potentially due to behavioral adaptations within the heterogeneous population.
