The simulation tracks the count of smallholder households adhering to the E-RO behavioral strategy, characterized as non-learning agents. At the start of the simulation period, this metric stands at 20.0 households. During the initial phase from time step 0 to 10, the population of E-RO adopters experiences a statistically significant upward movement, peaking at 21.1 households at time step 8. Subsequently, the trend reverses sharply. Between time step 11 and time step 65, a sustained and significant decrease is observed across multiple consecutive periods, indicating a systematic shift in farmer behavior. During this decline, the count drops to approximately 15.57 households by time step 44. This reduction reflects a transition away from the baseline non-learning approach, likely as farmers adopt strategies involving self-learning or social learning facilitated by the extension model. Following time step 65, the rate of change diminishes, and the count stabilizes with no statistically significant trend observed through the end of the simulation at time step 100. The metric reaches its lowest point of 14.67 households at time step 92 before settling at 14.70 by the final time step. Overall, the number of households retaining the E-RO strategy decreases from 20.0 to 14.7 over the full simulation duration, illustrating a long-term preference shift among the farming community towards learning-integrated management styles.
