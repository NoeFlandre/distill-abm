The mean household risk attitude begins near 0 at the start of the simulation and increases rapidly over the first 20 time steps, reaching values between 0.2 and 0.7. This initial rise suggests a period of heightened risk-taking, likely as farmers adjust to the model conditions and respond to initial pasture conditions. Following this initial phase, risk attitudes diverge into stable bands. The highest bands maintain risk attitudes between 0.6 and 0.8 for the duration of the simulation, while lower bands fluctuate or decline. Specifically, a subset of households shows a sharp decline in risk attitude beginning around time step 40, dropping to negative values by time step 60. These declines result in final risk attitudes ranging from -0.1 to -0.35. Conversely, households in the upper bands maintain their high risk posture throughout the simulation. This divergence indicates that while some farmers stabilize at high risk levels, others react strongly to accumulated experience or environmental feedback by adopting a significantly more conservative risk posture over time.
