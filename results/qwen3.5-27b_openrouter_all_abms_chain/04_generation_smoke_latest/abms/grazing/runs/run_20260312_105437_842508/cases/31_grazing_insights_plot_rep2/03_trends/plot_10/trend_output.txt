The number of households following the E-RO strategy exhibits considerable variability across simulations. At the beginning of the simulations, most scenarios start with 20 households adhering to this non-learning strategy. By time step 20, some simulations show a sharp increase, reaching up to 40 households, while others experience a decline, dropping to as low as 5 households. Between time steps 20 and 40, there is a noticeable divergence in trends: some simulations show sustained high numbers around 30-40 households, whereas others continue to decline, stabilizing around 5-15 households. After time step 40, the number of households following the E-RO strategy tends to stabilize in most simulations, with some maintaining levels between 20-25 households and others settling at lower values around 5-10 households. The overall trend suggests that the initial increase in the adoption of the E-RO strategy is transient, with long-term stabilization reflecting the interplay between learning mechanisms and non-learning behaviors. For decision-makers, this indicates that while initial conditions can lead to a temporary surge in non-learning strategies, over time, the adoption of learning strategies becomes more prevalent, potentially leading to more adaptive and sustainable management of pastures.
