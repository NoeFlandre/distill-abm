The average total number of livestock, including both healthy and destocked animals, begins at 90 units at the initial time step. This value declines rapidly, dropping below 50 units by time step 15 and reaching an overall minimum of approximately 41.0 units around time step 37. This initial sharp decrease suggests a period of high destocking or mortality as the system adjusts from the initial state, potentially driven by the risk attitudes of the baseline E-RO behavioral type in the presence of uncertain rainfall. Following this trough, the trend reverses significantly. Between time steps 45 and 60, the values rise, with the mean climbing back toward 50 units. By time step 100, the metric stabilizes at approximately 47.5 units. The data exhibits notable divergence in its later stages; while the aggregate mean follows a U-shaped trajectory, the individual simulation runs show increasing separation. Some trajectories stabilize at higher levels near 60 units, while others remain lower around 41 to 42 units. This bifurcation likely reflects the heterogeneous risk dispositions and the varying success of learning strategies—specifically learning-by-doing and social learning—in adapting herd sizes to the stochastic rainfall conditions.
