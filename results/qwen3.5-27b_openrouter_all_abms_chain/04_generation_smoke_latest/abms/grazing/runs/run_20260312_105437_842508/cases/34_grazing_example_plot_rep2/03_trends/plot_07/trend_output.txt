The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock begins at zero across all simulation runs, indicating an initial equality in the distribution of healthy livestock among the households. In the first phase of the simulation, from step 0 to approximately step 40, the coefficient shows a gradual and low-rate increase, with values remaining below 0.02. This suggests that the introduction of learning behaviors and risk parameters initially allows for the redistribution of livestock without significantly diverging from the initial equitable state. A distinct change in the trend occurs around step 40, marking the onset of a more rapid divergence. Between steps 40 and 60, the Gini coefficient accelerates sharply for most simulation runs, rising from approximately 0.02 to a range between 0.04 and 0.06. This phase reflects a period where the interaction of heterogeneous risk attitudes and behavioral types (E-RO, E-LBD, E-RO-SL1) amplifies the differences in herd sizes, likely driven by varying responses to the stochastic rainfall environment. In the final phase, from step 60 to 100, the coefficient continues to rise but exhibits significant variance in the rate of increase. By step 100, the values span a wide range from approximately 0.03 to over 0.09. The higher end of this range indicates a scenario where inequality has become pronounced, with a subset of households accumulating a disproportionately large share of healthy livestock compared to others. The lower end represents trajectories where the disparity grew more slowly but still resulted in a departure from the initial equality.
