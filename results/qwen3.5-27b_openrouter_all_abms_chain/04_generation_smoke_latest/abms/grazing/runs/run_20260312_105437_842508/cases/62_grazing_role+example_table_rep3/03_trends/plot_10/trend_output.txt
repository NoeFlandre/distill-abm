The population of households adhering to the baseline E-RO strategy, representing agents without learning capabilities, initiates at 20. During the early simulation phase, a transient increase occurs, peaking at 21.1 households at time step 8. Following this initial fluctuation, the metric enters a prolonged period of decline. Rolling trend analysis indicates a statistically significant decrease from time step 11 through time step 65, driven by negative slopes and p-values below 0.01. The count descends steadily from the initial baseline, reaching a minimum value of 14.6667 at time step 92. By the end of the simulation at time step 100, the number of E-RO households stabilizes at 14.7. In the final interval from time step 66 to 98, statistical tests show no significant trend, implying the system has converged toward a new steady state. This trajectory indicates that over the 100-year grazing period, the static non-learning approach loses prevalence. This reduction likely reflects the competitive advantage of learning-based strategies, such as experience-based or social learning, which allow farmers to better adapt livestock management to changing pasture conditions compared to the fixed baseline behavior.
