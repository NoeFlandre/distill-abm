The simulation monitors the number of households engaging in social learning across a population of 60 agents over 100 time steps. Initially, at time step 0, no households are engaged in social learning, resulting in a count of zero. Over the course of the simulation, the number of participating households exhibits a significant and consistent increasing trend. From time step 63 to the end of the simulation at time step 100, the value remains stable at this maximum level. The mean number of households engaging in social learning across the entire period is approximately 27.43. Inflection points detected at time steps 7, 28, 30, 33, 38, 57, and 60 indicate specific moments where the rate of change in adoption shifts. By the conclusion of the simulation, slightly more than half of the total household population is engaged in social learning, demonstrating a clear progression from no engagement to a stabilized state of widespread adoption within the heterogeneous agent population.
