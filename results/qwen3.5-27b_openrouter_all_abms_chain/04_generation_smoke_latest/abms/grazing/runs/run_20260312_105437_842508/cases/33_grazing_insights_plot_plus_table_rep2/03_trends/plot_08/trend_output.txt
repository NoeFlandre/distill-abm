The number of households practicing social learning started at zero and showed a consistent upward trend across the simulation duration. Initially, the growth was rapid, with inflection points observed at time steps 7, 28, 30, 33, 38, 57, and 60, indicating significant changes in adoption rates. The count reached a peak of approximately 32 households by time step 63, which represented about 53% of the total households. After reaching this peak, the adoption rate stabilized, maintaining a level close to the maximum. The statistical analysis confirms a strong and significant increasing trend throughout the simulation, with no significant decrease or oscillation patterns detected. For decision-makers, this indicates that once social learning strategies are introduced, they can quickly gain traction and stabilize within the community, suggesting the importance of initial support and training for such strategies.
