This initial decrease likely reflects the immediate impact of livestock grazing on pasture resources as the system adjusts to the presence of animals. The data shows a slight rebound at time step 3, with a value of 90,522.74, suggesting a transient stabilization before the subsequent decline.

Following the minimum at step 26, the reserve biomass exhibits a pronounced and consistent upward trend. This recovery phase begins around time step 33, where the rate of increase accelerates significantly. The biomass values rise steadily through the remainder of the simulation, culminating in a maximum value of 121,072.59 at the final time step, step 100. This sustained increase indicates a shift in the system's dynamics, likely driven by farmers adjusting their herd sizes to sustainable levels or improved resource management strategies emerging over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms a significant increasing trend starting from time step 33 and persisting through the end of the simulation. By the conclusion of the simulation period, the reserve biomass has not only recovered from its initial dip but has grown substantially above its starting level, suggesting the grazing system has reached a stable, high-yield equilibrium.
