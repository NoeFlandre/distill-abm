The average available green biomass across all patches exhibits a distinct trajectory over the 100-timestep simulation period. Starting at 45,000 units, the metric experiences an initial decline, reaching a minimum value of approximately 42,057 at timestep 27. Statistical analysis indicates a significant decreasing trend during the early adjustment phase between timesteps 11 and 21, suggesting an initial period of high grazing pressure or suboptimal livestock management strategies among the farmer population. Following the trough at timestep 27, the system stabilizes briefly before transitioning into a sustained recovery phase. A significant inflection occurs around timestep 49, marking a shift toward positive growth. From this point onward, the biomass levels rise consistently, culminating in a peak value of 60,535 at the final timestep 100. Rolling trend analyses confirm a statistically significant increasing trend from timestep 33 through the end of the simulation, with the steepest rate of recovery observed between timesteps 44 and 54. The overall mean biomass across the simulation is approximately 51,529, with a standard deviation of 8,006, indicating moderate variability relative to the trend. The dynamics suggest that initial grazing strategies led to pasture degradation, but subsequent behavioral adjustments or learning mechanisms allowed for significant regrowth, ultimately restoring the pasture to a state exceeding the initial carrying capacity. Periodic fluctuations were observed with a dominant oscillation period of approximately 49 timesteps, potentially reflecting cyclical learning adjustments or environmental feedback loops within the farming community.
