In the initial phase, from time step 0 to about time step 20, the biomass levels exhibit a modest decline, reaching values near 43,000 units in most simulation runs. This decline may be attributed to increased grazing pressure as households adjust their livestock numbers to the initial pasture conditions. By time step 20 to 40, the green biomass stabilizes around 43,000 units, with some runs showing minor fluctuations, likely reflecting varying livestock management practices among agents. A significant upward trend emerges from time step 40 to 60, with the biomass increasing rapidly to exceed 60,000 units. This sharp rise suggests a period of reduced grazing pressure, possibly due to behavioral adaptations by households or favorable ecological conditions such as increased rainfall. From time step 60 onwards, the growth rate of the green biomass slows, stabilizing between 61,000 and 64,000 units by the end of the simulation at time step 100. This stabilization indicates that the system has reached a new equilibrium, balancing vegetation regeneration with grazing activity. Throughout the simulation, there is notable variation across different runs, with deviations becoming more pronounced after time step 60. This variability highlights the influence of heterogeneous agent behaviors and stochastic factors, such as rainfall fluctuations, on the long-term dynamics of green biomass.
