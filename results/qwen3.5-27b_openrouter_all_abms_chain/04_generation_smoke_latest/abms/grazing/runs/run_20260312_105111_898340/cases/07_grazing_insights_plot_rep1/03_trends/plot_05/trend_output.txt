The average total number of livestock per household, including both healthy and destocked animals, starts at 90 for all agents at timestep 0. Over the first 20 time steps, the average livestock count drops steeply across all simulations, reaching approximately 50 by timestep 20. From timestep 20 to 40, the values stabilize further, with most trajectories falling to a low between 40 and 45 around timestep 35. After timestep 40, a divergence in trends emerges: while several simulation runs maintain low or slightly increasing livestock levels between 40 and 50, others show a recovery starting around timestep 45. Two distinct higher trajectories rise noticeably from this point onward, stabilizing by timestep 80 at values around 59 and 62. The remaining simulations plateau between 41 and 51 by the end of the 100 time steps. These patterns suggest that while initial conditions lead to rapid destocking or reduced herd sizes, a subset of agent configurations exhibit a delayed recovery phase, maintaining or rebuilding herd sizes over time.
