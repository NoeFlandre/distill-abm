The cumulative mortality curve exhibits a distinct logistic growth pattern, characterized by an initial phase of exponential increase followed by a deceleration toward an asymptotic plateau. In the early simulation period, the total number of deaths rises sharply. A significant change in the rate of accumulation occurs around the 1200-step mark, where the trajectory transitions from a steep ascent to a much flatter incline. The data indicates an initial mean accumulation slope of approximately 0.35 deaths per step, which decreases substantially to 0.017 by the period between 928 and 1159 steps. By the 1876-step mark, the mean cumulative mortality reaches its peak value of approximately 240.3 individuals. In the final phase of the observed series, the trend stabilizes; statistical analysis reveals a period of no significant trend (p=0.127) between steps 1624 and 1855, and a subsequent period of significant decreasing trend in the slope (p=0.000) from step 1856 to 2087, confirming the approach to a saturation limit near 235 to 240 cumulative deaths.
