Following this initial plateau, a significant surge occurs, with the aggregate birth count rising steadily to a peak value of 5.6 around the 1700 time step. This post-peak decline and stabilization suggest that as the population matures, reproductive rates are checked by environmental constraints or carrying capacity limits, a critical dynamic for managers aiming to prevent over-exploitation.
