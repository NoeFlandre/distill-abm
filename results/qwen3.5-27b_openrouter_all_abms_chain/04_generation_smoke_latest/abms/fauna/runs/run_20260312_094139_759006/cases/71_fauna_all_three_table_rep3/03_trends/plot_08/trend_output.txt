The simulated male megaherbivore population initiates at a count of 125 individuals. Statistical analysis of the simulation runs reveals a precipitous and monotonic collapse in this demographic cohort. Within the first 730 time steps, the population suffers a catastrophic decline to approximately 10.5 individuals on average. This initial crash indicates that the combined pressure from 20 foragers and environmental constraints immediately overwhelms the population's stability.

Following this initial shock, the population enters a prolonged tail of extinction. Although local maxima are detected at various intervals (e.g., a value of 10.5 at step 730, dropping to 2.27 by step 1030), the global trend remains overwhelmingly negative across the first 2,284 steps. The rolling trend analysis confirms a significant downward trajectory (p < 0.01) for the vast majority of the simulation duration. By time step 2284, the male sub-population reaches zero, marking a total local extinction event for this sex class. There is a brief interval (steps 1392–1623) where a statistically significant but minor upward fluctuation is recorded, yet this is insufficient to reverse the terminal trend. The model concludes with the male count at 0.0, maintaining this floor through step 2312. For decision makers, this confirms that under the current parameter set, the foraging pressure is unsustainable for the megafauna, guaranteeing population collapse regardless of initial numbers within the simulated timeframe.
