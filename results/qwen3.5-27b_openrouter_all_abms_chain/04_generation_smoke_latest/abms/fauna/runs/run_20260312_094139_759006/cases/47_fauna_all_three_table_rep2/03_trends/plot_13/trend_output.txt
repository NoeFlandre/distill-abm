The cumulative energy reserves of the megaherbivore population begin at a maximum of approximately 16,667 units at the onset of the simulation. Statistical analysis confirms a significant downward trajectory, with the aggregate energy declining steadily until it reaches zero units by the final time step. Notable structural changes in the rate of depletion are identified at time steps 350, 500, 625, and 875, interrupting the descent with varying degrees of acceleration. Despite minor oscillations characterized by a dominant period of roughly 63 steps, likely reflecting seasonal cycles, the overall trend remains unidirectional toward total depletion. By the 2311th step, the energy level is zero, marking functional extinction. For resource managers, this trajectory confirms that the current predation pressure is unsustainable under the modeled environmental conditions. The system fails to stabilize at a non-zero equilibrium, suggesting that without reducing forager pressure or increasing carrying capacity, the population faces inevitable collapse.
