The mean consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at 0 and increases sharply over the first 10 time steps, reaching approximately 1,200. This rapid rise suggests a strong initial adoption phase, possibly driven by agents' initial perceptions of health benefits and environmental concerns, which are key factors in the model. Around time step 11, the rate of increase slows, stabilizing at a consumption level between 1,200 and 1,300. This plateau may indicate a balance between habit strength and the influence of social norms, as agents' choices become more entrenched. A slight dip occurs around time step 19, where consumption drops to about 1,180, potentially reflecting temporary shifts in individual dispositions or social interactions. The consumption level rises again after time step 28, climbing to approximately 1,400 by time step 32. This final increase could be attributed to a cumulative effect of social influence and evolving perceptions, reinforcing the shift towards healthier milk options. The variability across simulation runs remains relatively low, indicating consistent dynamics in agents' decision-making processes under the given parameter settings.
