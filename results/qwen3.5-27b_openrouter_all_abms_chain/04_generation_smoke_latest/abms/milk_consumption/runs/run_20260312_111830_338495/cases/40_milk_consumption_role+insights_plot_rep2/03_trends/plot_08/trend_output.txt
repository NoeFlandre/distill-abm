The mean habit factor for whole milk exhibits a consistent upward trajectory across all simulation runs. At the initial step, the value stands at 1.0, indicating no habitual influence on purchasing decisions. By step 2, there is a sharp increase to approximately 1.02, suggesting that early consumer behavior begins to be influenced by habit formation. From step 2 onward, the habit factor grows steadily, reaching values between 1.04 and 1.05 by step 32. This gradual increase implies that habitual consumption of whole milk becomes increasingly entrenched over time. The relatively narrow spread of values across runs highlights the robustness of this trend, indicating that habit formation is a strong and consistent driver of whole milk purchasing behavior in the model. For decision-makers, this underscores the challenge of shifting entrenched consumer habits, particularly in markets where traditional products like whole milk have long-standing loyalty. Strategies to alter consumer behavior would need to account for the reinforcing nature of habit strength over time.
