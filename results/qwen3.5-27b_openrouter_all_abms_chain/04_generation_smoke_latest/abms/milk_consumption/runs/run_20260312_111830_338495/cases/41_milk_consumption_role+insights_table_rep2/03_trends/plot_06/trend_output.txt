The average quantity of whole milk consumed per agent exhibits a marked downward trajectory across the 33 time steps analyzed. Initial consumption at time step 0 is recorded at 2654.81 units, establishing a high baseline consistent with strong incumbent habits. By time step 32, this value declines to 253.10 units, indicating a near-total market shift away from whole milk. Statistical validation via rolling Mann-Kendall tests confirms a significant decreasing trend from time steps 0 to 14, characterized by steep negative slopes. However, periods of statistical non-significance occur between time steps 15 to 19 and 25 to 29, indicating temporary plateaus where habit strength likely buffered against further decline. Specific inflection points identified at indices 5, 13, 16, 19, 24, 25, 27, and 30 denote shifts in the rate of consumption loss, suggesting alternating phases of rapid adoption and behavioral resistance driven by social norms and health perceptions. The minimum value occurs at the final index 32. For decision makers, this trajectory underscores that while entrenched habits initially protect market share, prolonged exposure to alternative health narratives and social influence eventually overcomes inertia, necessitating long-term strategic patience during periods of apparent stability.
