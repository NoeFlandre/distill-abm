From time step 10 onwards, the deviation stabilizes around 13.5 and remains consistent through the end of the simulation at step 32. This plateau implies that the population reached a steady state where the gap between actual choices and environmental values became constant. The convergence of multiple simulation runs indicates robustness in this outcome regardless of initial stochastic variations. For decision-makers, this persistent deviation implies that even with environmental awareness, external pressures like habit formation and social influence may dominate long-term consumption behaviors, suggesting that interventions to promote sustainable milk choices must address these deeper behavioral drivers.
