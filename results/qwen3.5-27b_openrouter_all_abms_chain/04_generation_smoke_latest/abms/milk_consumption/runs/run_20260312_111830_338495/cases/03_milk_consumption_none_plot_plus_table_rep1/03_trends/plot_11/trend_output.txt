The mean value health deviation, representing the average discrepancy between agents' internal health perceptions and the health values implied by their milk choices, exhibits a consistent and significant upward trajectory across all thirty simulation runs. From time step 1 to time step 10, the deviation rises rapidly in a steep curve, reaching approximately 0.51 by time step 10. Following this initial growth phase, the rate of increase slows considerably between time steps 11 and 25, where the metric plateaus slightly, hovering between 0.54 and 0.60. A second phase of sharper acceleration occurs starting around time step 26, driving the deviation upwards significantly. The simulation concludes at time step 32 with a final aggregate mean value of approximately 0.7251. This trend suggests that over the course of the simulated period, agents' consumption choices increasingly diverged from their initial health perceptions, likely reflecting the complex interplay of habit, social influence, and the shift in milk varieties towards skimmed and semi-skimmed options.
