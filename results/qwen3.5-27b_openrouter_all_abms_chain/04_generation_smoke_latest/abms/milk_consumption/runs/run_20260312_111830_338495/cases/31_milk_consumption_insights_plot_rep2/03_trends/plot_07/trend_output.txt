The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents shows a distinct upward trend over the simulation steps. Starting from near zero at the initial step, consumption rapidly increases, reaching approximately 1,200 units by step 10. This surge suggests a strong initial adoption phase driven by factors such as health and environmental perceptions, habit formation, and social influence. Between steps 10 and 25, the growth rate slows, with the quantity stabilizing around 1,200 to 1,250 units, indicating a period of consolidation where consumption levels plateau. A secondary increase is observed after step 25, with consumption rising again to surpass 1,400 units by step 32. This renewed growth may reflect cumulative effects of social norms, habit reinforcement, or evolving agent evaluations of milk choices. The overall trajectory implies that adoption behaviors are influenced by both immediate decision factors and long-term systemic dynamics within the model.
