The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore. It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival. The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape. The cumulative number of male prey animals killed by forager bands exhibits a consistent pattern of rapid increase followed by a plateau across all simulation runs. The rate of decline remains constant throughout the simulation, indicating a continuous depletion of energy resources. The trend suggests a dynamic interaction between human predation and the resilience of the megaherbivore population. Total energy of male animals begins at approximately 12,000 units at the start of the simulation. By step 2000, male animals are consistently zero across all simulation runs, indicating the extinction of the male population within this timeframe. Female prey animals experience a rapid decline, reaching values as low as 10 by approximately step 500. The population stabilizes near zero, with minimal fluctuations observed in remaining steps up to 73000.
