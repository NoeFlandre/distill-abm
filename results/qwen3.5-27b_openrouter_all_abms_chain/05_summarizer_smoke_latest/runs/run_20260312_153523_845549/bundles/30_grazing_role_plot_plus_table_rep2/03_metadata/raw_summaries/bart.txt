 This simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors . The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock . The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time .  The statistical analysis confirms a significant decreasing trend in the first window (timesteps 0-10) and a strong, significant increasing trend persisting from timestep 33 through the end of the simulation . The overall pattern indicates a transition from initial instability to a prolonged period of recovery and sustained increase in green biomass .  The number of households adhering to the E-RO (non-learning) strategy begins at 20.1 . In the initial phase, specifically between time steps 0 and 10, there is a transient increase, with the value rising to a maximum of 21.1 at time step 8 . The value settles at 14.7, representing a net decrease of approximately 5.3 households .
