RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock begins at a minimal level of 0.0074 at the start of the simulation. This initial adherence suggests that households align closely with extension guidelines. The average available green biomass across all patches begins at 45,000 units. By time step 100, the average reserve biomass reaches a maximum of 121,072 units. The lowest point is reached at time step 37, where the average livestock count bottoms out at 41.01 units. This period of contraction likely corresponds to the initial adjustment of the three behavioral types to the private property regime under conditions of rainfall variability and heterogeneous risk attitudes. By the final time step of 100, the average total livestock reaches 47.55 units. The average cumulative number of livestock destocked per household begins at zero and increases monotonically throughout the simulation, reaching a final value of 96.78 at time step 100. The rate of destocking is most pronounced during the initial period, with a steep slope of 4.96.
