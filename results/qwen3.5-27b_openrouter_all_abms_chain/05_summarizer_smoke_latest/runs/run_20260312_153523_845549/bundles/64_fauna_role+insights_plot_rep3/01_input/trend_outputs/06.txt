The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population exhibits a step-like increase, with significant jumps occurring between approximately 800 and 1000 steps. During this period, multiple simulations show a rise in the number of births, with values reaching up to 10 in some cases. After this initial surge, the cumulative number of births stabilizes, with different simulations showing varying levels of births, ranging from 0 to 10. This suggests that the initial conditions and environmental factors during the early stages of the simulation significantly influence the reproductive success of the megaherbivore population. For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of early intervention strategies to manage hunting pressure and environmental degradation, which could have long-term effects on the sustainability of the megaherbivore population.
