the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . the model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . despite mechanisms for food storage and immortal foragers, the energy costs associated with movement and hunting exceed the energy gains from prey . this suggests that socio-natural dynamics configured are unsustainable for the long-term viability of the hunting bands . the simulation tracks the cumulative tally of male prey animals removed by forager bands over 2312 time steps . forager bands, configured with 20 initial agents, rapidly exploit the available population of postpartum females before reaching a saturation point . the transition from rapid growth to a stable plateau implies that the hunting pressure reaches an equilibrium with the prey's reproductive capacity and mobility . environmental conditions influence both prey movement and forager success rates . the female prey population initiates at 125 individuals and follows a consistent downward trajectory, reaching a count of zero by the final recorded step . the mean abundance drops below 7 individuals by tick 730, resulting in the local extinction of the female breeding stock within the observed timeframe. a second, weaker period of stabilization appears between ticks 1,856 and 2,087 . despite these intervals, the overall statistical trend remains significantly decreasing . the dominant oscillation period identified at 63 ticks reflect the influence of seasonal environmental cycles on resource availability .
