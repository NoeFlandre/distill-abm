The number of households engaging in social learning exhibits a rapid upward trend from the initial time steps, stabilizing at varying levels across different simulation runs. This divergence continues to widen until time step 40, where the count stabilizes within a broader range, typically between 30 and 54 households. The stabilization suggests that the adoption of social learning strategies reaches a saturation point in most runs, with some variability indicating differing degrees of strategy diffusion.
