RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The mean household risk attitude values begin near 0.0 at the start of the simulation and exhibit a rapid increase for nearly all trajectories within the first 20 to 30 time steps. For decision makers, this stability implies that initial risk dispositions are set as fixed parameters and do not adapt dynamically. Policymakers should consider this static nature when designing interventions targeting risk behavior in similar models. The number of households following the E-RO strategy exhibits considerable variability over time across different simulation runs. The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0.000, indicating an equal distribution among all households at the initial step. The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households increases over the 100 time steps, with variations among households.
