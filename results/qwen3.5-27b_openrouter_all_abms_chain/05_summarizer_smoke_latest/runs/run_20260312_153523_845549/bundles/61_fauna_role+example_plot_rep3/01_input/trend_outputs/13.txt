The total energy of all animals within the simulation begins at approximately 25,000 units at the initial time step. This value undergoes a sharp and rapid decline over the first 400 steps, falling to an average of roughly 5,000 units by the 400th step. This initial steep trajectory indicates a high rate of energy removal from the system, driven by predation events and metabolic costs of the forager and prey agents. Following this initial phase, the rate of decline moderates, though a secondary period of accelerated loss occurs between steps 400 and 700, where the total energy drops further toward near-zero levels. By approximately step 1,200, the total energy across the population stabilizes at or near zero and remains there for the remainder of the simulation. This final plateau suggests the complete collapse of the megaherbivore biomass, leaving no remaining energy stored in the prey population.
