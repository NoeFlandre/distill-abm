The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. The simulated trajectory of average weekly consumption for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk reveals a non-linear adoption pattern over 33 time steps. From time step 25, the growth trajectory resumes, culminating in a peak value of 1456.59 at time step 32. The steady volume of choices suggests that consumer engagement with milk purchasing remains constant over time. The mean quantity consumed initiates at a baseline of 5.29 and escalates to 1456.5991 by the final time step, reflecting a comprehensive transition in consumer behavior. The initial stability suggests agents are anchored by their initial whole milk preferences. The subsequent sustained increase reflects the gradual modification of habits through evaluation and social influence, making the alternative options more habitual over time.
