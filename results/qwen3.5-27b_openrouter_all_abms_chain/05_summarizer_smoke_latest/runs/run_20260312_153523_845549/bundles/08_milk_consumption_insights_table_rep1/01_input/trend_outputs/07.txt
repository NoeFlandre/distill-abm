The simulation results illustrate a profound structural shift in milk consumption, characterized by a massive aggregate increase in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The mean quantity consumed initiates at a baseline of 5.29 and escalates to 1456.5991 by the final time step, reflecting a comprehensive transition in consumer behavior. Trends are non-linear, marked by distinct phases. A statistically significant upward trajectory is confirmed in the early stages (indices 0-9). Subsequent periods show varying degrees of stability and oscillation. A dominant oscillation period of approximately 15 steps is identified, visible through a sequence of peaks and valleys. Notable local maxima occur at index 11 (1265.47) and 13 (1240.35), with a subsequent resurgence of positive growth in the final recorded window (indices 25-29). Key inflection points marking changes in growth velocity are observed at indices 4, 12, 13, 16, 19, 22, 27, and 30. For decision-makers, the data implies that shifting from whole milk to alternatives is a complex, wave-like process rather than a linear progression. The significant early adoption followed by volatility suggests that social influence and habit parameters create sensitivity to network effects, which must be factored into strategies aiming to influence dietary choices.
