The simulation monitors the average available green biomass across all patches over 100 time steps. At the onset of the simulation, the biomass level is recorded at 45,000. During the initial phase, the pasture undergoes a period of depletion, reaching a minimum value of 42,057 at timestep 27. Statistical trend analysis confirms a significant decreasing trajectory specifically between timesteps 11 and 21, indicating that grazing pressure initially outpaced grass regeneration. Following this valley, the system stabilizes briefly before entering a sustained recovery phase. A distinct shift occurs around timestep 50, marking a change point where the trend becomes consistently positive. From timestep 33 onwards, significant increasing trends are observed, with the steepest growth rate occurring between timesteps 44 and 54. By the conclusion of the simulation at timestep 100, the average green biomass reaches a maximum of 60,535. This final value represents a substantial increase over the initial conditions, suggesting that the adaptive management strategies employed by the households eventually alleviated grazing pressure and facilitated significant pasture regeneration.
