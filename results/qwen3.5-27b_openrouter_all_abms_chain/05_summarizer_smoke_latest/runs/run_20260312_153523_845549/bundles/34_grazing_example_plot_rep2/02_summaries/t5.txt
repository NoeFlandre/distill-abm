The RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant throughout the 100 time steps. The values range from approximately -0.11 to 0.18. Each simulation run maintains its initial value without change over the entire duration of the simulation. The average number of destocked livestock declines steadily, reaching values close to 1 by time step 30. The rate of decline slows down as time progresses, stabilizing around 1 between time steps 40 and 100. This stabilization reflects a balance between livestock management practices and resource availability over time.
