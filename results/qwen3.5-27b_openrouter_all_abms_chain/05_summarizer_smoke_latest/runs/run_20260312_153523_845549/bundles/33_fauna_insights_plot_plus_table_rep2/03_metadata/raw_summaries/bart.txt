 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs .  The cumulative number of female prey killed exhibits a steady upward trend, starting at zero and reaching a plateau near 120 individuals after approximately 2000 steps . By time step 1250, the cumulative count stabilizes, hovering around a plateau of 118 to 120 for the remainder of the simulation period . Decision makers should consider these cyclical patterns when planning conservation strategies .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model suggests that hunting pressure combined with environmental seasonality results in rapid population decline with no recovery or equilibrium . For decision makers, this trajectory implies that the interaction of hunting pressure and environmental parameters creates an unstable dynamic where the reproductive capacity of the female herd cannot buffer against mortality rates, leading to rapid extinction .  The combined total energy within the megaherbivore population begins at approximately 16,667 units and follows a consistent, sharp downward trajectory, reaching zero by the final time step . The data indicates that under the specified parameters, the population cannot sustain its biomass, leading to complete depletion .
