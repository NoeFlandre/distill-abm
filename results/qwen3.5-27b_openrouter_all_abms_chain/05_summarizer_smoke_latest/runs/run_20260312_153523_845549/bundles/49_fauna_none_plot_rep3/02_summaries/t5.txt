The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The cumulative number of female prey animals killed by forager bands increases rapidly from the start. Between steps 500 and 1000, the rate of increase slows, with the cumulative count stabilizing around 110 to 120 females. A few simulations show slightly lower plateaus around 110-115 females, while others extend marginally higher, up to approximately 126 females. By step 500, the population further diminishes, reaching close to 50 animals in most simulations. This rapid decrease occurs across multiple simulation runs, indicating a high sensitivity to the initial conditions or parameters of the model. By step 2000, the total energy levels for male prey animals approach zero.
