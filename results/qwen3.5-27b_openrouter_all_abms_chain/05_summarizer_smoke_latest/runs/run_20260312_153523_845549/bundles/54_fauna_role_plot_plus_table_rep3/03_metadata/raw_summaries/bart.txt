 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape .  The energy trend is characterized by a strong downward slope, indicating a continuous depletion of available energy resources for the forager bands . This decline suggests that under the given model parameters, the foragers are unable to sustain their energy levels over time, likely due to a combination of environmental constraints and hunting costs .  The simulation demonstrates a robust trajectory toward the extinction of male megaherbivores across all 40 runs . The total prey population exhibits a rapid, monotonic decline from an initial mean of approximately 166.67 to an absolute zero . By tick 2284, the minimum index recorded in the dataset, the male population reaches 0.0 in the aggregate mean .  At the commencement of the simulation (tick 0), the aggregate mean energy across the population series stands at 12,500.0 units . The metric demonstrates a steep and rapid decline immediately following this start, characterized by a statistically significant negative trend (slope -22.616) during the first 230 ticks . By step 2311, the total energy reaches zero, indicating the total loss of the megaherbivore population .
