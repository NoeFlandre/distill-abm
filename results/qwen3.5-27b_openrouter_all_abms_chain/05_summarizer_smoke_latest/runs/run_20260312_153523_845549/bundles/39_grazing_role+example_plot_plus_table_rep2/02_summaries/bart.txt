This simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time. The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at 45000.00 at time step 0.00. In the initial phase, the biomass levels fluctuate slightly but exhibit a general downward trend, reaching a local low near time step 4 with a value of approximately 45261.37. The decline becomes more pronounced and statistically significant between time steps 11 and 21, with a steep downward slope of -120.458, leading to the minimum recorded value of 42057.04 The number of households practicing social learning starts at zero across all simulation runs. The data indicates a clear bifurcation in behavior, where about half the population eventually utilizes social information to guide grazing decisions, while the other half does not.
