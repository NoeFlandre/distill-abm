The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. Between time steps 20 and 30, consumption fluctuates slightly. From time step 30 onwards, there is a noticeable drop again, bringing consumption down to approximately 300 units by the end of the simulation. This final decline could indicate a renewed shift in consumer behavior or external factors influencing milk choices. The mean habit factor for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk begins at 1.0 at the initial step, indicating no influence of habit at the start of the simulation. From step 10 to step 28, the consumption levels fluctuate within a range of roughly 1,180 to 1,260. This trajectory indicates a swift initial market entry phase for lower-fat milk varieties, followed by a prolonged period of plateau where consumption stabilizes. Implied values of milk types resolve into a steady state of deviation within the first 10 time steps. This suggests that the decision-making model parameters result in agents quickly settling into an unstable behavior pattern where environmental considerations are consistently separated from their actual consumption choices.
