The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. Satisficing-threshold: Range from 0.0 to 100.0. We set it to 0. Social-susceptibility: range from 0 to 1.0. The mean reserve biomass begins at approximately 90,000 units across all simulation runs. This reduction indicates an initial period of high grazing pressure or low regeneration rates relative to consumption. From step 60 onwards, the rate of increase decelerates and the biomass levels plateau, stabilizing between 110,000 and 128,000 units by step 100. Over the first 40 steps, there is a slow and gradual increase in the Gini coefficient, with values reaching approximately 0.01 to 0.02. This early phase suggests minimal differentiation in livestock health outcomes among households. By step 100, the coefficient spans a range from approximately 0.03 to 0.06.
