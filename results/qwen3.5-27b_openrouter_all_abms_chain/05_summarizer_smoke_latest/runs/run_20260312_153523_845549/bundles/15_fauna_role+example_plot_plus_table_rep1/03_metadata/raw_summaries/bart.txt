 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival .  The cumulative number of male prey animals killed by forager bands begins at zero and increases steadily over the initial phase of the simulation . Initial energy levels show negligible variation in the first 100 steps, but divergence increases as the simulation progresses . The negative energy trajectory suggests that the aggregate cost of foraging activities consistently exceeds the energy obtained from prey acquisition . The mean birth count across all simulations hovers around 2.17, with a maximum observed value of 5.6 births reached near step 1673 . The population eventually stabilizes at zero, indicating a total extinction event within the simulated timeframe .
