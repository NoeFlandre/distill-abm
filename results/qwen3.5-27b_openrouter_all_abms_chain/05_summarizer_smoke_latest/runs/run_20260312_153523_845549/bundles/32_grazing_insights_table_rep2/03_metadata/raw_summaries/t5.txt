the RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . it implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences . the average initial risk attitude of social learning agents remains constant at 0.0074 across the entire 101 time step duration . the value is identical at every recorded instance from time step 0 to 100, showing zero variance and no trend . this stability indicates that the baseline risk disposition assigned at initialization does not change during the simulation . the adaptive management model suggests that the mixed population of behavioral types initially responds to uncertainty by reducing stock, before stabilizing and rebuilding assets as learning processes take effect . the average number of livestock removed due to insufficient resources begins at zero and rises sharply in the initial phase, peaking at 6.61 at time step 3 . secondary peaks occur at step 9 with a value of 3.37 and at time 12 with 2.74, though these remain lower than the initial maximum . the rate of increase is highest early in the simulation, with a slope of 4.956 between time steps 0 and 10, before diminishing to approximately 0.311 . the rapid initial destocking suggests a significant adjustment phase for herd management .
