Within the context of the RAGE model extension, the E-RO strategy represents a baseline behavior lacking learning mechanisms. The reduction in household numbers suggests that non-learning agents face higher risks of livelihood failure or exit under the specified conditions of high rainfall variability. Since strategy switching is disabled, this decline reflects attrition rather than behavioral change. For decision makers, these trends highlight the vulnerability of static management strategies in uncertain environments. Investing in learning capabilities for households may be necessary to ensure long-term persistence and stability of the grazing system.
