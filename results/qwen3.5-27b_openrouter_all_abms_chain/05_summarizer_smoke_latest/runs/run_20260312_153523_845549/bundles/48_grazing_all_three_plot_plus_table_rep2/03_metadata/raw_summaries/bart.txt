 Simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors . The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time . The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock .  The average total number of livestock, including destocked animals, starts at 90 and declines sharply over the first 20 time steps . The average amount of actual available green biomass starts at 45000, rises slightly to 45261 by time step 4, and then declines steadily . This initial decrease is followed by a period of low values, reaching a minimum of 42057 at time step 27, likely due to increased grazing pressure .
