Simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. In this version, each farmer stays on their own plot of land. They decide how many animals to keep based on how the grass is doing and what others are doing. The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. The average number of livestock destocked across all households begins at 0 at the initial step, then rapidly increases to a peak between 6.0 and 7.5 within the first 5 to 10 time steps. The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero for all simulation runs. By step 100, the simulations stabilize at varying high levels, generally ranging between 55000 and 63000. From steps 10 to 40, the counts show considerable fluctuation. Some lines stabilize between 20 and 30, while others decline steadily. By the final step at 100 the counts range from 0 to approximately 30, indicating a wide variation in the persistence of the E-RO strategy among households.
