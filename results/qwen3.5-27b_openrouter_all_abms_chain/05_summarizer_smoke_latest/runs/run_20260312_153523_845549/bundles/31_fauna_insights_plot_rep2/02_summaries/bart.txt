The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs. The observed dynamics suggest that early-stage hunting strategies significantly impact male prey populations, with diminishing returns as time progresses. Decision makers should consider the timing and intensity of initial hunting efforts, as they critically influence prey population dynamics and resource availability. The number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed increases sharply during the initial phase of the simulation, reaching approximately 120 by step 600. Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model shows a consistent decline in prey population. Trend implies that the current parameters, including hunting behavior and environmental factors, lead to unsustainable prey populations. Decision makers should consider the impact of hunting strategies and environmental conditions on the energy availability of female prey animals to ensure sustainable population dynamics.
