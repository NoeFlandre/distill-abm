The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. Consumption rises sharply, reaching approximately 1,200 by time step 10. Growth rate slows between time steps 10 and 27, where consumption fluctuates around 1,200 to 1,250. This period may reflect a balance between habit formation and social influence. From time step 27 onwards, there is another significant increase, with the quantity reaching approximately 1450 units by time step 32. From step 1 to step 32, the factor exhibits a steady upward trend, reaching values between 1.03 and 1.05. This gradual rise indicates a continuous reinforcement of habitual consumption of whole milk over time. The deviation continues to grow but at a slower rate, reaching 13.5 by the 10th time step. From this point onward, the mean value environmental deviation stabilizes, maintaining a value close to 13.5 throughout the remaining duration of the simulation.
