The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals killed by forager bands increases rapidly from the start. By step 1000, the majority of simulations stabilize, with the cumulative count ranging between 110 and 125 females killed. Some simulations exhibit slower initial accumulation, with values below 20 killed at step 100, eventually converging with the larger trend by step 500. A few simulations showed minor fluctuations between steps 1000 and 2000, but these values remained close to zero. Overall, the data indicates a consistent and rapid decline in the female prey population over time. By step 500, the population further diminishes, reaching close to 50 animals in most simulations.
