The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. Between steps 15 and 19, the rate of change stabilizes, suggesting a period where habit persistence or existing social norms temporarily buffer against further substitution. From step 25 to step 29, the trend significance weakens before the metric reaches its minimum at step 32. Minor oscillations are observed in the final time steps, indexed between 27 and 31, which may reflect adjustments in agent decision-making as the simulation nears completion. The consistent significant upward trend across all rolling windows confirms a robust directional shift in consumption behavior, mirroring the historical substitution of whole milk by lower-fat alternatives. Norms eventually override initial resistance, leading to widespread adoption of alternative milk choices by the end of the modeled period.
