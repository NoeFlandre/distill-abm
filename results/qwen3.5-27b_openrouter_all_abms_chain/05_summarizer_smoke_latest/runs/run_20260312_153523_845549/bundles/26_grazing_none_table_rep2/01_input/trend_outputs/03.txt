The average reserve biomass followed a distinct U-shaped trajectory over the 100-timestep simulation, starting at 90,000 and declining to a minimum of 84,114.07 at timestep 26. This initial depletion suggests an early period of grazing pressure exceeding immediate regeneration. Following this nadir, the system entered a sustained growth phase, with the metric climbing consistently to a maximum of 121,072.59 by timestep 100. Statistical trend analysis confirms a significant negative trend for the first 21 timesteps, transitioning into a strong positive trend from timestep 33 onwards. This recovery phase aligns with the model's learning modules, where agents of types E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1 adjusted their strategies through learning-by-doing and social interaction. The long-term increase indicates that the collective adaptation of the heterogeneous household population shifted the system towards a more sustainable equilibrium, allowing the pasture biomass to accumulate significantly beyond initial levels.
