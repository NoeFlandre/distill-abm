The mean reserve biomass exhibits a distinct U-shaped trajectory over the 100-year simulation horizon. Starting at 90,000 units, the biomass decreases significantly during the first 26 time steps. Statistical analysis confirms a decreasing trend with high significance from time step 0 to 21. The system reaches its lowest point at time step 26, recording a minimum value of 84,114. Following this minimum, the trend shifts toward recovery. Between time steps 33 and 98, the indicators show a consistently significant increase. The steepest rate of recovery occurs between time steps 44 and 54, where the slope accelerates substantially compared to earlier periods. By the final time step 100, the biomass reaches its peak value of 121,073, surpassing the initial condition by over 30,000 units. This pattern suggests that the mixed population of farmers, including those learning from experience and neighbors, initially exerts pressure that depletes reserves. However, adaptive behaviors eventually align with carrying capacity, allowing the pasture to regenerate robustly. Decision makers should anticipate an initial period of resource depletion before observing long-term improvement, implying that short-term support mechanisms may be necessary to sustain farmers through the early adaptation phase.
