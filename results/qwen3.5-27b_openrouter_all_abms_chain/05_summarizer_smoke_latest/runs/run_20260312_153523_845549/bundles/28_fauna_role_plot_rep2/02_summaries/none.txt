The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival. The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape.

Context:
The model simulates a socio-ecological system containing human foragers and sexually dimorphic megaherbivore herds. It incorporates principles of optimal foraging theory, where hunters make decisions based on energy costs and food value. The environment is dynamic, featuring grass growth and seasonal changes.

Goals:
The primary goal is to determine conditions under which human hunting pressure, combined with environmental constraints, leads to the extinction of megafauna. By manipulating parameters, users can test scenarios of environmental degradation and predation intensity.
