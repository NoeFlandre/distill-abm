The cumulative number of deaths among the prey population exhibits a consistent pattern across all simulation runs. Over the initial 500 steps, the death count rises steeply from zero to approximately 150, indicating a high rate of mortality during this early phase. Following this rapid increase, the rate of death slows substantially. Between 500 and 1000 steps, the cumulative deaths rise more gradually, reaching between 220 and 230 in most runs. After 1000 steps, the cumulative death counts stabilize significantly, plateauing between 225 and 250 for the remainder of the simulation period up to 2000 steps. The final cumulative death counts approach the initial population size of 250, suggesting that in the majority of scenarios, the prey population is driven to extinction or near-extinction levels within the first 1000 steps.
