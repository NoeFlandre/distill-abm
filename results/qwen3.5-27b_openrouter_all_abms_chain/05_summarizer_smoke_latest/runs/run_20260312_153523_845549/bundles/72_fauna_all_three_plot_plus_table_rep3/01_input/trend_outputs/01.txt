The number of active human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant at 20 throughout the entire duration of the study. This stability indicates that the initial population of foragers was neither increased through reproduction nor decreased through mortality. The lack of change suggests that the model configuration for this specific output was set to treat foragers as an immortal and static pressure variable. For decision makers and modelers, this implies that under the current parameter set, human population dynamics are decoupled from environmental feedback loops, meaning the extinction risk for megaherbivores is driven purely by the constant rate of predation from this fixed number of agents rather than a fluctuating human population.
