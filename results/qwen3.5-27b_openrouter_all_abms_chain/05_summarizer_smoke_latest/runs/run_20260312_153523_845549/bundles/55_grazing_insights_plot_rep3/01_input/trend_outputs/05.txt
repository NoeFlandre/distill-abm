The average total number of livestock per household begins at an initial value of 90. A sharp decline occurs during the first 40 time steps, reaching a minimum value ranging between 40 and 42 for all simulation runs. Following this nadir, the trajectories diverge based on the behavioral type of the households. The households utilizing the E-LBD and E-RO-SL1 learning strategies exhibit a sustained recovery, with livestock numbers increasing between time steps 40 and 70 to stabilize at higher levels between 50 and 62. Conversely, the baseline E-RO households show minimal recovery, stabilizing at lower levels between 41 and 47. These patterns indicate that the implementation of learning mechanisms, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, enables households to more effectively rebuild their livestock assets following initial resource constraints compared to static baseline strategies.
