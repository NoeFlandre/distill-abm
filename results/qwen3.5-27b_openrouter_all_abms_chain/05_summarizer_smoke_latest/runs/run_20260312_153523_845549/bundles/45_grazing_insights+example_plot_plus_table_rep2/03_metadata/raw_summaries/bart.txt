 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average household risk attitude in the simulated population begins near zero, indicating an initial state of neutrality or balanced risk perception among agents . The average reserve biomass in the grazing system begins at approximately 90,000 units and experiences an initial decline during the first 26 time steps . The transition from high volatility in the early phase to a stable, moderate risk profile in the later phase indicates a system adaptation where agents refine their strategies .  The average number of livestock destocked by households begins at zero and spikes sharply in the initial steps, reaching a maximum average of 6.61 at step 3 . The data exhibits a significant shift in trend around step 30, after which variability decreases and the rate of destocking slows considerably . Decision makers should consider the timing of interventions to maximize adoption rates, focusing on the initial phase of the simulation .  The number of households following the non-learning E-RO strategy begins at 20 at the start of the simulation . By step 20, the average count drops to 17.5, and by step 30, it falls to approximately 16.5 . This decline indicates that over time, environmental feedbacks or social dynamics within the model cause households to abandon the E- RO strategy .
