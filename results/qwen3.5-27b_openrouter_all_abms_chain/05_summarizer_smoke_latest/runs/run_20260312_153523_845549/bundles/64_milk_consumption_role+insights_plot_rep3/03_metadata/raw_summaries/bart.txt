 The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined exhibits a consistent linear increase over time . The mean whole milk quantity consumed by agents in the agent-based model exhibits a clear downward trend over the simulated time steps, reflecting a significant shift in consumer behavior . By step 30, the consumption reaches around 1400 units, indicating a sustained preference shift towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk varieties .  The mean value health deviation starts at 0.0 at step 0 and increases steadily over time . From step 5 to step 10, the deviation increases more gradually, reaching around 13.2, as agents settle into new purchasing patterns influenced by habit strength, social norms, and perceived environmental characteristics .
