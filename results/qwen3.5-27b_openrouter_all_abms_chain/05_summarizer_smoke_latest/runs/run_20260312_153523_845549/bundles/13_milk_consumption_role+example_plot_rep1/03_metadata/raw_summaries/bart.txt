 The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms .  The total number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk starts at 0 and increases steadily throughout the simulation . This steady increase reflects the cumulative nature of decision-making events within the agent population, where each agent makes a selection in every time step .  The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents begins at 0.0 . The metric rises sharply to approximately 0.58 by time step 5 . This suggests that habit reinforcement becomes more pronounced as agents integrate health perceptions and social influences into their decision-making processes .
