The total number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk starts at zero and increases steadily over the simulation period. This increase is nearly linear, reaching approximately 25,000 choices by the 32nd time step. The low variance across simulation runs indicates that the trends are robust, suggesting that the mechanisms driving the adoption of skimmed or semi-skimmed milk are consistent across different iterations of the model. The steady rise in choices could be attributed to the growing influence of social norms and the habit formation around healthier milk choices, which are key factors in the decision-making process of the agents. For decision makers, this trend implies that interventions aimed at promoting healthier milk choices, such as public health campaigns or social influence strategies, can lead to a sustained and predictable increase in the adoption of these choices over time.
