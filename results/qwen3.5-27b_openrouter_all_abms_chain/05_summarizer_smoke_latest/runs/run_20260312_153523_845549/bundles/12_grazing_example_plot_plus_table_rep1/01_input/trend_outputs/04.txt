The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches starts at 45000 and experiences a slight initial increase before declining. This decline continues through the first 27 time steps, reaching a minimum value of 42057.04. This initial decrease could be attributed to the active grazing pressure from the diverse household types (E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1) on the pastures as they adjust their livestock numbers without an initial resting phase. Following this trough, the biomass undergoes a sustained period of growth. A significant inflection occurs around step 9, followed by a steady increase that becomes statistically significant starting from step 33. The growth accelerates notably between steps 44 and 54, with the average biomass surging. By the final time step, step 100, the biomass reaches a maximum value of 60535.28, representing the highest level of pasture availability recorded in the simulation. This long-term recovery and growth indicate that the collective livestock management strategies eventually resulted in a reduction of grazing pressure relative to the vegetation's carrying capacity, allowing for significant biomass accumulation.
