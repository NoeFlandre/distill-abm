 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice . An agent's milk choice function is modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at 0 and remains constant for the first time step . From step 1, there is a sharp increase in consumption, reaching approximately 1,200 by step 10 . The trend reflects a long-term transition from whole milk to other milk types, consistent with the model's aim to replicate historical shifts in milk consumption preferences .  At the final recorded time step of 32, the mean habit factor ranges between 1.045 and 1.056 across the various runs . Data indicates a persistent strengthening of the habit factor for whole milk consumption throughout the simulation period . The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents starts at 0.0 at step 0 .
