The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. By step 2000, most simulations show no further increase in the cumulative count. This suggests that hunting pressure on males has stabilized or that the remaining male population is either too sparse or too costly to hunt under given socio-natural conditions. Between 500 and 1000 time steps, the rate of decline slows, and the number of male animals stabilizes at values between 5 and 20. This gradual decline could be attributed to the combined effects of sustained hunting pressure and natural mortality as the population ages. The decline continues more gradually from 400 to 800 time steps. The total energy stabilizes at very low levels, hovering near zero. This stabilization suggests that the population of female animals has been reduced to a minimal number or that the surviving individuals are operating at the lower end of their energy threshold.
