 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs . Ungulates are sexually dimorphic, reproduce, age, and move as herds . Trend analysis confirms a significant increasing pattern from the start until time step 1855, after which the rate of accumulation shifts . The output indicates a dominant oscillation period of 63 ticks, suggesting recurring seasonal or cyclical patterns in hunting success . The peak value indicates the maximum reproductive capacity achieved before environmental or predation constraints become limiting .
