The aggregate adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk alternatives exhibits a robust and monotonic increase throughout the simulation period. Starting from a baseline of zero choices at the initial time step, the cumulative count of alternative milk selections rises continuously to reach approximately 25,644 by the final observation at index 32. Statistical trend analysis reveals significant upward momentum across all observed intervals, with the rate of adoption accelerating notably from an initial slope of roughly 201 per period to a sustained slope exceeding 935 in the middle and later stages. This acceleration suggests that as agents overcome initial habit inertia, represented by the high incumbent habit parameter, social influence and health perceptions drive a compounding shift in preferences. In the final phase, specifically between indices 27 and 31, observations indicate minor oscillations in the growth rate, potentially reflecting market stabilization or variance in social interaction dynamics as the alternative options approach saturation. For stakeholders, these trends highlight the efficacy of long-term interventions targeting health perception and social norms, as the results confirm that sustained exposure to these drivers effectively erodes established habits and generates significant cumulative market share shifts over time.
