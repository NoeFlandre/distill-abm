RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The mean household risk attitude for the 60 households starts at an initial value of 0.5. Within the first 20 time steps, there is a sharp divergence where the majority of households increase their risk attitudes. This sharp increase is likely due to initial resource scarcity, as households with high livestock counts face immediate resource constraints. The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at approximately 45,000 units. The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock begins near zero at time step 0, indicating an initial state of nearly perfect equality in livestock distribution among the 60 households. Between time steps 20 and 100, the mean destocking stabilizes within a lower range, oscillating between 0 and 1.5, with noticeable fluctuations. Variability among simulation runs increases over time, indicating that individual household trajectories diverge as they respond differently to resource constraints and decision-making processes. The number of households following the E-RO strategy begins with an average of 20 households at the start of the simulation. Within the first 5 time steps, significant divergence is observed across simulation runs. The lower bound remains relatively flat after step 50, indicating that some households may stabilize their herd sizes earlier.
