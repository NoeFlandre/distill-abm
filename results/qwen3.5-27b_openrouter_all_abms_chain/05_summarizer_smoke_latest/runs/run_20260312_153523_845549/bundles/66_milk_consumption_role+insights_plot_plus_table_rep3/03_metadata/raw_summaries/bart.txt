 The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms .  Simulation data indicate a steady upward trend in the cumulative number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk over the course of the study period . The total number of milk choices made by all agents consistently increased over the simulation period, starting from zero at the initial step and reaching 32,000 by the final step .  The mean percentage difference in choice function scores among agents exhibits a consistent upward trajectory over the simulation period . The mean deviation between agents' held health values and the implied values of their milk choices begins at zero at the start of the simulation . For decision-makers, this data suggests that promoting healthier milk options can lead to sustained changes in consumer behavior over time .  The simulated population becomes a stable characteristic of the simulated population's behavior . The behavior of a simulated population can be seen to be stable in the real world . The simulation is a simulation of a population with a population of 1,000 simulated animals .
