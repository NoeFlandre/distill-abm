RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The mean household risk attitude values begin near 0.0 at the initial step and exhibit a general upward trend during the first 20 steps. The average reserve biomass across all patches initially stands at approximately 90,000 units at the start of the simulation. By step 100, the trajectories stabilize within a final range between 111,000 and 128,000. Trend indicates a progressive increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households over the simulation period. The number of households following the E-RO strategy exhibits considerable variability across the simulation runs. For many runs, the count declines sharply, particularly between the initial step and step 30.
