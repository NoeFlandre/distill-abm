The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households showed a consistently increasing trend over the 100 time steps. It began at zero and grew to approximately 96.8 by the end of the simulation, with a mean value of 75.5. The median final value was 81.2, and the standard deviation was 20.3, indicating some variation among the series. Statistical tests confirmed significant increasing trends throughout all evaluated windows (p=0.000), with slopes decreasing over time, suggesting a deceleration in the rate of destocking. Early growth was rapid, with a high initial slope of 4.96 per time step, which reduced to a stable rate of approximately 0.31 per time step by the end of the run. The data revealed specific periods of changing dynamics at time steps 25, 60, and 100, as well as oscillatory patterns around steps 22-26, 49-59, and 79-80. The dominant oscillation period was identified at 49 time steps. This trajectory indicates that farmers progressively reduced their herd sizes throughout the simulation period, with the intensity of destocking being highest in the initial phase before settling into a steady state of gradual reduction.
