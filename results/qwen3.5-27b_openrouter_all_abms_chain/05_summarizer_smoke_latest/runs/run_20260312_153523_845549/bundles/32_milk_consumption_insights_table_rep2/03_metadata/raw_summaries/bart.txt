 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent demonstrates a pronounced long-term increase . The aggregate measure of total whole milk selections demonstrates a persistent upward trend across the 33 time steps of the simulation . This trajectory reflects the successful historical substitution of whole milk with reduced-fat alternatives over the thirty-year period .  The simulation demonstrates a pronounced downward trajectory in the average weekly consumption of whole milk per person across 33 time steps . Within the model of British milk consumption between 1974 and 2005, this pattern implies that all 1,000 agents consistently register a choice event at each time step . The mean habit factor applied to the whole milk choice function shows a steady linear increase throughout the simulation .  The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents begins at 0.0000 at the initial time step . Over the 33 time steps, the value demonstrates a consistent upward trajectory . Oscillatory behavior becomes apparent in the later stages, particularly between time steps 17 and 30 .
