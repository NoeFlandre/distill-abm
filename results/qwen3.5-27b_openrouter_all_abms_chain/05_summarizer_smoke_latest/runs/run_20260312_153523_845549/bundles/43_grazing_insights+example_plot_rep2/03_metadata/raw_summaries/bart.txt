 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The mean household risk attitude for extension agents starts at an initial value of 0.5 at step 0 . After step 15, the risk attitude remains relatively stable, fluctuating within a narrow range between 0.7 and 0.8 for the remainder of the simulation . The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches begins at approximately 45,000 units .  The average number of livestock destocked by households begins near zero and rises sharply in the first 5 time steps, peaking at approximately 7.5 . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero in the initial steps and increases rapidly over the first 20 time steps . The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0 across all simulation runs at time step 0 .  The number of households following the E-RO strategy exhibits diverse trajectories across simulation runs . Early intervention and resource management are critical for sustaining livestock herds, authors say . Decision-makers should consider these patterns to tailor support and policies that promote sustainable livestock management across different household types .
