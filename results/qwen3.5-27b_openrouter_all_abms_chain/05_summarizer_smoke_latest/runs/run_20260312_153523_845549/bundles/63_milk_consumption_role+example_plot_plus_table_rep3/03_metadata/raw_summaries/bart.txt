 The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties . Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents starts at 0.0000 and increases steadily to 32000 by the final time step . The average value across the simulation period is 1130.88, with a median of 1158.97, suggesting a central tendency close to the midpoint of the simulation . The Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across multiple windows .  The mean habit factor applied to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices begins at an initial value of 1.0.0 . The rate of growth is relatively consistent, as indicated by a low standard deviation of 0.0110 across the matched series . The rise reflects the cumulative effect of repeated purchasing behavior reinforcing the preference for these lower-fat alternatives .  This stabilization suggests that the consumer population reached a state where milk choices became consistently misaligned with their environmental values, remaining steady as habits and social influences solidified these decisions over the simulated timeframe .
