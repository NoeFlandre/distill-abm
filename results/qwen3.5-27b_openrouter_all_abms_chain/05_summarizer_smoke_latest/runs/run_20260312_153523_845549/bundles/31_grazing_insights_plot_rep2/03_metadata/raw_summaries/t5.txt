the RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . it implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences . the mean household initial risk attitude across all social learning agents (E-RO-SL1) remains constant throughout the 100 simulation time steps . the value is uniformly distributed around the mean of 0.5, as indicated by the initial parameter setting . this static behavior suggests that initial dispositions to deviate from recommended livestock numbers remain unchanged . in the final phase of the simulation, the mean destocking rate becomes nearly constant for most simulations, settling at values between 0.2 and 1.0 . the system adapts over time, leading to a stabilization of herd management strategies and reduced loss due to insufficient resources .
