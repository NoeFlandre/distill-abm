The average total number of livestock per household, including both healthy and destocked animals, starts at 90 for all agents at timestep 0. Over the first 20 time steps, the average livestock count drops steeply across all simulations, reaching approximately 50 by timestep 20. From timestep 20 to 40, the values stabilize further, with most trajectories falling to a low between 40 and 45 around timestep 35. After timestep 40, a divergence occurs: some household groups show a gradual recovery, with livestock numbers rising between 45 and 50 by timestep 60 and stabilizing thereafter between 48 and 52 through timestep 100. In contrast, other groups remain at lower levels, fluctuating between 40 and 43. The two highest trajectories reach stable values near 60 and 62 by timestep 80, maintaining these levels through the end of the simulation. These trends suggest that, while most households reduce their livestock numbers early on, certain learning behaviors may allow specific groups to partially recover herd sizes in the later stages of the simulation.
