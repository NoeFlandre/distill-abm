The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based simulation. It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival. The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape. The cumulative number of male prey animals killed by forager bands exhibits a consistent pattern of rapid increase followed by a plateau across all simulation runs. Foragers consistently expend more energy than they acquire through hunting and foraging under the given model conditions. By step 2000, the male prey population is effectively extinct in most simulation runs, suggesting that the hunting pressure has reached a limit. Total energy available in the male megaherbivore population exhibits a consistent and rapid decline across all simulation runs. The female prey population stabilizes at or near zero in most runs by step 1100. A few simulation runs show slightly delayed extinction, with female populations remaining non-zero until approximately step 2300, where the count reaches 1 before extinction.
