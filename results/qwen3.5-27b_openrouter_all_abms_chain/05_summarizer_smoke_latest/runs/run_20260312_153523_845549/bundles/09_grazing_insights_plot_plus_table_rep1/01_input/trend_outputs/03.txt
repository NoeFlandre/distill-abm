At the start of the simulation, the average reserve biomass was recorded at 90,000 units. Over the initial phase, specifically between the 0th and the 26th time step, the biomass condition deteriorated, reaching a minimum of approximately 84,114 units at the 26th time step. This decline was statistically significant in the early period. A turning point occurred around the 33rd time step, marking the beginning of a sustained and significant recovery. The biomass levels rose consistently through the subsequent phases, reaching a peak of 121,072 units by the final time step (100). The recovery phase exhibited a strong increasing trend with an average slope of over 200 units per time step in the early recovery period, which eventually stabilized as the biomass levels plateaued between 111,000 and 121,000 units towards the end of the simulation. This trajectory indicates that the system shifted from an initial state of degradation to a state of high ecological resilience and improved biomass storage by the end of the observed period.
