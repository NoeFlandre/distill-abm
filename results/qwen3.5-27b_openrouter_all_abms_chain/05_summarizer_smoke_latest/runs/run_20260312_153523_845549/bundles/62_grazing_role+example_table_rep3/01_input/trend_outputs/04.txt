The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches initiates at 45,000 units. During the early phase of the simulation, the biomass undergoes a reduction, reaching a minimum value of 42,057 at time step 27. This initial decline corresponds to a period where grazing consumption likely outpaced regenerative growth, as households established their livestock herds based on initial resource assessments. The trajectory shows a significant decreasing trend between steps 11 and 21. Subsequent to this trough, the system exhibits a marked recovery. An increasing trend becomes evident starting at time step 33, continuing consistently through the final time step. An inflection point near step 49 marks a consolidation of this upward momentum. By time step 100, the biomass reaches a peak of 60,535, surpassing the initial value. This long-term upward trajectory suggests that the adaptive management behaviors of the farmers eventually reduce grazing pressure sufficiently to allow for substantial pasture regeneration and accumulation of green biomass over the simulation period.
