Simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time. The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0.0 at timestep 1 and rises steadily to 0.0726. The average number of livestock destocked by households begins at zero, rises sharply to a peak of approximately 6.6 during the third time step, and then declines consistently. The trend suggests that inequality in healthy livestock holdings among households grows consistently over time. The number of households adhering to the E-RO (non-learning) strategy begins at 20.0 at the start of the simulation. During the initial phase, specifically up to time step 10, the trend is slightly increasing, reaching a peak of 21.1 at time step 8. The decline indicates a shift away from non-learning strategies, likely as households adapt based on social influence.
