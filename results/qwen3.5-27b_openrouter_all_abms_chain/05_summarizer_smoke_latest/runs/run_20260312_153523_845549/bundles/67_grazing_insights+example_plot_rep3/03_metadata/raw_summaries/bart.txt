 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  Risk-attitude parameter is fixed at the beginning of the simulation, as it serves as a baseline for agents' decision-making under uncertainty . For decision makers, this indicates that the heterogeneity in risk attitudes is pre-defined and static, highlighting the importance of understanding initial agent behavior in shaping long-term outcomes .  The average number of livestock destocked by households begins near zero and rises sharply in the first 5 time steps, peaking at approximately 7.5 . For decision-makers, this trend suggests that initial resource constraints necessitate rapid adjustments in livestock numbers, followed by a recovery phase as strategies are refined . The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero across all simulation runs .  The number of households following the E-RO strategy starts at 20 in the simulation . By step 10, the count ranges from approximately 20 to 40, with some simulation runs peaking around 40 households . The trend underscores the importance of adaptive management strategies for maintaining social-ecological balance .
