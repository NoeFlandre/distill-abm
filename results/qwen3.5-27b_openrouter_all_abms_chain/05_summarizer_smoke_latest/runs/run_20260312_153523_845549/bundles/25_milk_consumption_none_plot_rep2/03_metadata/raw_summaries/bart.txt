 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice . An agent's milk choice function is modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  At step 0, the mean weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent is 0 . A rapid increase begins immediately and continues through step 10, where consumption reaches approximately 1200 units . The data shows a linear increase in the total number of whole milk choices over time .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at approximately 1.0 at the initial time step . The data shows a slight divergence among simulation runs over time, indicating increasing variance in the habit factor . The trend indicates that the diversity in choice function scores among agents increases rapidly at the beginning of the simulation .
