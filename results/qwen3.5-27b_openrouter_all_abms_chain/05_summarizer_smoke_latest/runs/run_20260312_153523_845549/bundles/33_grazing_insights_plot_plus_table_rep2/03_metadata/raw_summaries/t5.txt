the RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . it implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences . the average initial risk attitude of social learning agents remains constant throughout the 100-step simulation period, holding a steady value of 0.0074 . this metric, which represents the initial disposition of these agents to deviate from the recommended number of livestock, shows no upward or downward trend . the average household risk attitude exhibits a distinct phased evolution over the simulation timeline . by the end of the observed period, the average total livestock count rises to approximately 47.55 units, reflecting a partial rebound from the lowest point . this pattern of initial precipitous decline followed by a slow recovery implies that the household population engaged in a period of significant risk management . non-adaptive behaviors are naturally displaced over a medium time horizon . this suggests that behaviors are unsustainable in this system environment . decision makers have modeled the heterogeneous social-ecological pressures .
