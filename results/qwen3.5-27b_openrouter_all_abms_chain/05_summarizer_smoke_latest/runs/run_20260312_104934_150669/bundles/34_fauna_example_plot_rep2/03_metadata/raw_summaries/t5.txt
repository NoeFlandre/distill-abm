the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . the model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . the cumulative number of female prey animals killed starts at zero and rises sharply, reaching approximately 110 to 120 individuals by step 600 . this rapid increase suggests that hunters actively target male prey, likely due to their higher food value compared to females and calves . by step 2000, the total number of males killed remains constant, indicating that hunting pressure on males has effectively ceased . the number of male prey animals starts at approximately 125 and declines rapidly over the first 400 time steps . this initial sharp decline suggests significant predation pressure or environmental factors leading to high mortality rates early in the simulation . between 400 and 800 time steps, the rate of decline slows somewhat . the overall trend remains downward, with total energy levels continuing to fall to below 1,000 units by 800 steps . a sharp and significant decline occurs around step 380, where the percentage of green patches drops precipitously to a minimum value near 23% .
