 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape .  The simulation ends with the total energy of foragers around -38,000, indicating a significant energy deficit over time . The cumulative number of female prey animals killed starts at zero and rises sharply within the first 400 steps, reaching approximately 100 kills . By step 1000, the cumulative count of males killed stabilizes, with values plateauing around 110 to 125 .  The number of male prey animals begins at approximately 125 in all simulation runs . By step 600, the majority of the simulation runs show the male population has fallen below 10 . The eventual extinction across all runs indicates the simulated conditions are unsustainable for the male megaherbivore population over the long term .  A rapid, monotonic decline is observed from the first step, with the energy levels dropping precipitously to approximately 8,000 units by step 300 . This initial phase of energetic loss correlates with the high predation efficiency of the 20 forager agents and the high caloric yield of adult males (food-value-males: 30) and females)
