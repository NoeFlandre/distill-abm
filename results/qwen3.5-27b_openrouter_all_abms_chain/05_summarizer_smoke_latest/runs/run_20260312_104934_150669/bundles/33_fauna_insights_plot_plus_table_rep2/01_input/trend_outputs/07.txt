The cumulative number of prey deaths exhibits a rapid upward trend from the start of the simulation, increasing from 0 to approximately 230 by time step 500. This initial phase, characterized by a steep slope averaging roughly 0.35 deaths per step, reflects a period of high mortality driven by the established internal mortality rate of 0.025 and external hunting pressure from the 20 initialized foragers. The rate of increase begins to decelerate around time step 1200, where the cumulative count stabilizes within a range of roughly 236 to 240. This plateau indicates that the population has reached a quasi-stable mortality equilibrium, where the number of new deaths per cycle is effectively zero or extremely low relative to the total accumulated count. Statistical analysis confirms a significant slowing of the trend after step 1160, with oscillating fluctuations suggesting minor stochastic variations rather than new systemic trends. For decision-makers, this trajectory implies that the current parameters regarding hunter density and environmental carrying capacity lead to a rapid decimation of the population followed by a prolonged period of low-turnover survival.
