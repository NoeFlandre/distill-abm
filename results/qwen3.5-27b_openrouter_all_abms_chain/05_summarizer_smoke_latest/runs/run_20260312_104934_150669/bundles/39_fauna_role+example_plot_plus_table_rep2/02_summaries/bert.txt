The megafaunal hunting pressure model (mhpm) is an interactive, agent - based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction. It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival. Context : the model simulates a socio - ecological system containing human foragers and sexually dimorphic megaherbivore herds. It incorporates principles of optimal foraging theory, where hunters make decisions based on energy costs and food value. Goals : the primary goal is to determine conditions under which human hunting pressure, combined with environmental constraints, leads to the extinction of megafauna.
