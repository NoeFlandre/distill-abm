The average available green biomass across all pasture patches follows a trajectory of initial depletion followed by sustained recovery over the 100 time steps. At the commencement of the simulation, the biomass level is recorded at 45000 units. During the early phase, the system experiences a decline in vegetation condition, reaching a minimum value of 42057 units at time step 27. Statistical trend analysis confirms a significant decreasing trend between time steps 11 and 21. Following this valley, an inflection occurs, leading to a change point identified at time step 50. From this juncture, the biomass exhibits a consistent and significant increasing trend across subsequent intervals, continuing through to the final time step. By time step 100, the average green biomass reaches its maximum value of 60535 units. This dynamic reflects the interaction within a population of 60 households comprising twenty agents of each behavioral type. The initial reduction suggests a period of adjustment or elevated grazing pressure under the specified risk parameters and lack of resting protocols. The subsequent long-term increase indicates that the learning capabilities inherent in the E-LBD and E-RO-SL1 types, alongside the baseline E-RO behavior, facilitated a shift in decision-making regarding livestock placement that allowed the pasture vegetation to regenerate and surpass initial levels despite environmental variability.
