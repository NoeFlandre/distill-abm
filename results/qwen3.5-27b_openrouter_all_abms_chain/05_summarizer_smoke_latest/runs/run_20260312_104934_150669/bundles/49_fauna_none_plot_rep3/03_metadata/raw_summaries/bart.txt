 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history .  Initially, there were approximately 120 female prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model . The number of deaths among the prey animal population exhibits a rapid increase from step 0 to approximately step 800 . Beyond step 800, the cumulative deaths stabilize, with most simulations converging between 230 and 250 deaths by step 2000 .  The rate of decline suggests that interactions between hunting behavior, environmental conditions, and animal life history dynamics play significant roles in determining the speed and extent of population reduction . After step 1000, the number of animals remains near zero across all simulations, indicating a complete or near-complete extinction of the prey population .
