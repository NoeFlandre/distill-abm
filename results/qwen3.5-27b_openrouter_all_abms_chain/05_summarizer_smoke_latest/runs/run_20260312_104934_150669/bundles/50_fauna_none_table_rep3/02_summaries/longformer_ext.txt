The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges on a 211x211 patch matrix. The goals are to manipulate socio-natural dynamics to observe how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to either accelerate or buffer extinction or create equilibrium conditions.
