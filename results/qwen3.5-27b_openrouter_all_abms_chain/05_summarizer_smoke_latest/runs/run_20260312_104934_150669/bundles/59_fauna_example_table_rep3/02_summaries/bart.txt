Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model suggests that cumulative energy expenditure from movement and processing costs consistently exceeds energy intake from hunting activities. The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed begins at zero and exhibits a consistent upward trajectory during the initial phase of the simulation. The system reaches a peak value of approximately 120.17 at tick 1876.17. The number of male prey animals initiates at 125.0 individuals, representing the maximum population observed throughout the simulation. A rapid decline occurs immediately following the start, with statistical change points identified at time steps 125, 250, 375, and 500, indicating a steep reduction in population density during the initial phase. The population reaches zero individuals at time step 2284 and remains at this level for the remainder of the observation period. The average population count of prey animals begins at approximately 167 individuals at the start of the simulation, reaching zero by the final recorded time step at index 2311. The final outcome suggests that the combined pressures of hunting, internal mortality, and environmental constraints exceeded the herd's capacity for regeneration and survival over the long term. The absence of female individuals terminates the breeding cycle. The total energy available in prey animals begins at approximately 16,667 units at the start of the simulation. The metric experiences a rapid and substantial decline during the initial phase, dropping significantly before reaching the first inflection point at tick 309. Following this initial crash, the energy levels stabilize temporarily, exhibiting a series of local peaks and valleys between ticks 384 and 440, where values hover around 3,700 to 4,100.
