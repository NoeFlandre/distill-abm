The RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant throughout the 100 time steps at a value of 0.0074. The variance, standard deviation, and interquartile range across all simulation runs are zero. This metric exhibits no discernible trend, no peaks or valleys, and no change points over the duration of the simulation. The metric exhibits a distinct shift in trend around time step 55. From this inflection point, the livestock numbers begin a gradual recovery. The recovery does not return to the initial herd size, indicating a new, lower equilibrium. The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households begins at zero and increases steadily over the 100 time steps, reaching a final value of approximately 96.78. Early growth is steep, with the cumulative total climbing to approximately 70 by step 20. After this initial phase, the slope of accumulation decreases suggesting that households are destocking at a slower rate as the simulation progresses.
