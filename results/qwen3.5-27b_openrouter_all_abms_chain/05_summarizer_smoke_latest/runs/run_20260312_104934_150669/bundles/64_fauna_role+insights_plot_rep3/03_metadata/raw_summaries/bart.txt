 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape .  By the end of the recorded data at step 2,300, the total energy for foragers drops to approximately -38,000 . This continuous reduction suggests that the foragers are expending more energy through movement and hunting activities than they are gaining from the food resources available .  The number of female prey animals exhibits a sharp decline from an initial average of approximately 125 individuals at step 0, dropping to fewer than 25 animals by step 500 . The trajectories stabilize near zero between steps 1,500 and 2,300, indicating a complete population collapse in most scenarios . The implication for decision makers is that even moderate levels of hunting pressure can result in significant population declines within a relatively short period .  After an initial period of intense consumption or environmental stress leading to reduced vegetation, the system adapts or recovers, achieving a stable equilibrium . This implies that managing factors influencing the initial decline in vegetation could be crucial for maintaining a stable ecosystem and ensuring the sustainability of megaherbivore populations .
