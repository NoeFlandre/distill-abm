The total energy of foragers exhibits a consistent downward trend across all simulation runs. Starting at an initial value of approximately 0 at step 0, the energy declines steadily, reaching around -32,000 to -38,000 by step 2300. Early fluctuations occur between steps 0 and 500, with minor variations in the rate of decline among different runs. After step 500, the decline becomes more linear and uniform across simulations. This trend suggests that, under the specified parameters, the costs of hunting and movement consistently exceed the energy gained from prey, leading to a net energy deficit for forager bands over time. Decision makers should consider these findings when evaluating scenarios where energy balance is critical for the sustainability of foraging populations.
