 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape .  The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands shows a consistent upward trend across all simulation runs . Under the specified parameters, the costs of hunting and movement consistently exceed the energy gained from prey . These trends imply that under the given conditions, the hunting pressure can significantly impact the male prey population .  The combined effects of human predation and environmental factors lead to a critical reduction in the megafauna population, potentially pushing it towards extinction . For decision makers, this trend highlights the significant risk of rapid population collapse under sustained hunting pressure, emphasizing the need for careful management and conservation strategies to prevent extinction .  At approximately step 300, a sharp decline occurs, with the percentage dropping to about 23% . Following this drop, the values recover quickly, returning to levels near 32%. A similar pattern is observed around step 800, where another decline happens, this time reaching approximately 29% . After step 1100, the percentage of green patches stabilizes around 32% .
