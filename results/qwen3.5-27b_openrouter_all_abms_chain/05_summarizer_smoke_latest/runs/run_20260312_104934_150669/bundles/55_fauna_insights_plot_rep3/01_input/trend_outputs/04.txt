The cumulative number of female prey animals killed by forager bands increases rapidly from the start, reaching approximately 100 individuals by step 500 across all simulation runs. By step 1000, the majority of simulations stabilize, with cumulative kills ranging between 110 and 125. From step 1000 onward, there is minimal to no further increase in the number of females killed, indicating that the system reaches a state of equilibrium or exhaustion of available targets. A few simulations show slightly higher cumulative kills, peaking around 125, while others plateau earlier at lower values, such as 110. This pattern suggests that hunting pressure is initially high but quickly diminishes as the prey population declines or as foragers shift their strategy due to changing environmental or prey density conditions. These trends imply that female prey, likely critical for herd reproduction, are heavily targeted early in the simulation, potentially accelerating population decline. Decision makers should consider the long-term implications of such selective hunting on prey population sustainability and ecosystem balance.
