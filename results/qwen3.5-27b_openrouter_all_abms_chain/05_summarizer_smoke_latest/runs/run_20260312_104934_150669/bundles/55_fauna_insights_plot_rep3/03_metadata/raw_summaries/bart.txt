 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs .  The number of female prey animals killed by forager bands increases rapidly from the start, reaching approximately 100 individuals by step 500 across all simulation runs . From step 1000 onward, there is minimal to no further increase in the number of females killed, indicating that the system reaches a state of equilibrium or exhaustion .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model exhibits a rapid decline over time . The decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, until most simulation runs reach near-zero population levels between steps 1000 and 1500 . These trends highlight the vulnerability of the prey population and the importance of managing hunting practices and environmental conditions to prevent such drastic declines .
