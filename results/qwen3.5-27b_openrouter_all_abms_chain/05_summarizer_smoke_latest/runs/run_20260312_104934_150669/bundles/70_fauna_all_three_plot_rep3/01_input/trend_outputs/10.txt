Between steps 500 and 1000, the rate of decline slows significantly, with most runs stabilizing between 0 and 20 animals. By step 1000, the population approaches extinction in most runs, with values remaining near zero for the remainder of the simulation. The simulation ends with the total number of animals near zero across all runs, indicating that under these parameter settings, the combination of human hunting pressure and environmental constraints is sufficient to drive the megaherbivore population to extinction. For decision makers, this outcome highlights the critical vulnerability of large herbivore populations to sustained predation in highly seasonal environments, even when initial population sizes are moderate and environmental resources are partially renewable.
