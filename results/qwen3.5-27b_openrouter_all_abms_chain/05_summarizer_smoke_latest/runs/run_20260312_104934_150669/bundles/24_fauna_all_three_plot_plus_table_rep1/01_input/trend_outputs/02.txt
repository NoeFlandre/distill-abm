The total energy available to forager bands begins at 2,000 units and follows a consistent, monotonic decline across the simulation timeline, reaching a terminal value of approximately -38,233 units. The trend exhibits no periods of recovery or significant oscillation. The most distinct change in dynamics occurs around step 55, where energy peaks briefly near 1,263 before the downward trajectory steepens and stabilizes into a linear decline. Inflection points noted around steps 358 and 1238 do not interrupt the negative trend but coincide with the transition from variable early-stage energy expenditure to a steady-state rate of energy loss. Statistical analysis confirms a persistent decreasing trend throughout the duration, with the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicating significant negative slopes (p=0.000) in all segments. The rate of energy depletion accelerates significantly after the initial 400 steps, averaging a decline of roughly 20 units per step in the latter half of the simulation. This continuous negative accumulation implies that foraging yields and stored food reserves were insufficient to offset movement costs and metabolic requirements over time, leading to an irreversible energetic deficit for the human population.
