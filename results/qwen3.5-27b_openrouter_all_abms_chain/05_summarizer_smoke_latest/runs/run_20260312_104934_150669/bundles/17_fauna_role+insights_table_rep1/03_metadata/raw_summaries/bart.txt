 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model is an interactive, agent-based simulation designed to test hypotheses regarding megaherbivore extinction . It allows users to simulate a prehistoric landscape to observe how environmental factors and human predation interact to influence population survival . The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape .  In the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model simulation, the cumulative number of female megaherbivores hunted by forager bands exhibits a distinct trajectory of initial rapid accumulation followed by stabilization . For management and conservation planning, the concentration of kill events within the first 1876 steps (representing the peak pressure point) signals a critical window of vulnerability for the herd before environmental constraints or resource depletion naturally dampens the hunting rate .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model reveals a complex trajectory in the prey population's reproductive metrics over the simulation duration . The birth metric begins at 0.0000 and exhibits a statistically significant increasing trend for the majority of the early timeline . During this expansion phase, the metric reaches its maximum recorded value of 5.6000 at time step 1673 . Following this peak, the dynamics shift drastically .  The population of male megaherbivore subpopulation begins at 125 individuals at index 0 and concludes at 0.0 by time step 2311 . The female prey population initiates at 125.0 individuals at time step 0 . The mean population size stands at 16.48 with a median of 1.0 . The long tail of low abundance suggests that once the population falls below a critical threshold, functional extinction precedes numerical extinction .  The aggregate energy reserves of the female megaherbivore population exhibit a definitive downward trajectory resulting in total extinction . At tick zero, the energy metric initiates at 12500.00, the value consistently declines until reaching 0.00 at the final time step . The total energy available within the prey population exhibits a persistent downward trajectory throughout the observation period .  The median energy level of 146 units is substantially lower than the mean of 2,369 units, highlighting that the population spent the majority of the simulation time in a low-biomass state . The percentage of vegetated landscape patches initiates at 33.0002 percent at index 0, consistent with the configured initial grass proportion .
