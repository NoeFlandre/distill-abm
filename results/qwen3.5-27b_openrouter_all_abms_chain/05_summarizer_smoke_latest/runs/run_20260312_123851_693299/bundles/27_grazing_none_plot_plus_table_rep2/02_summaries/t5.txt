The RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remained constant at 0.0074 across all 100 time steps. The metric continues to increase, albeit with a slightly reduced rate of change, until it reaches a global maximum of 0.3814 at time step 33. The metric for healthy livestock starts at 0.0 at time step 0 and rises to 0.0726 by time step 100. The average value across all time steps is 0.0302. Key inflection points in the rate of increase occur at time steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57.
