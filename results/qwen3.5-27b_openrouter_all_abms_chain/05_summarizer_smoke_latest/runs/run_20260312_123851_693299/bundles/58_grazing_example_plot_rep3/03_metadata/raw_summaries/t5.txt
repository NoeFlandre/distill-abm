the RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . it implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences . the average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant throughout the 100 time steps . multiple distinct values are observed, ranging from approximately -0.11 to 0.17 . the lack of variation across time steps suggests that the initial disposition to deviate from the recommended number of livestock is an inherent property of the agents . the average number of livestock destocked by households begins near zero . this initial surge suggests an immediate pressure on resources . following the peak, there is a rapid decline over the next 15 to 20 time steps, with the average destocking falling below 3 . a lower tier of simulations shows a consistent decline, settling between 8 and 15 by time step 50, eventually reaching a steady state between 2 and 5 . by the end of the simulation at time step 100, the counts for E-RO households have plateaued in specific ranges for each simulation run .
