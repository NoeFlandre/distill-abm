The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. Initially, skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption is near zero at time step 0. By time step 5, consumption rises sharply to approximately 1200. From time step 28 onwards, consumption fluctuates slightly, ranging between 1180 and 1250. This trend indicates a significant shift in consumer preferences towards healthier milk options over time. Between steps 10 and 25, the growth rate slows, with the quantity stabilizing around 1,200 to 1,250 units. A secondary increase is observed after step 25, with consumption rising again to surpass 1,400 units by step 32. The overall trajectory implies that adoption behaviors are influenced by both immediate decision factors and long-term systemic dynamics within the model population. From step 10 onwards, the metric stabilizes and remains constant near 13.5 through step 32. This trajectory indicates a rapid initial misalignment between agents' internal environmental values and the implications of their milk choices.
