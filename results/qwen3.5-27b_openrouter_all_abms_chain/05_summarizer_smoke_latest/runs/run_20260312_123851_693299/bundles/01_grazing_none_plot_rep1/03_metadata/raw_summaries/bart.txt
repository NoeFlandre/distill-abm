 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The mean household risk attitude values begin near 0.0 at the initial step and exhibit an upward trend . The values stabilize or show slight fluctuations, maintaining higher levels compared to the initial phase . The average reserve biomass across all patches initially stands at approximately 90,000 units at the start of the simulation . By timestep 60, the biomass levels have surpassed the initial values .  The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households increases over the 100 timesteps of the simulation . The number of households following the E-RO strategy exhibits considerable variability across simulations . The final stable values at time step 100 range from approximately 16 to 54 households .
