The RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant across the 100 timesteps, with no changes observed at any key time steps. The mean household risk attitude values begin near 0.0 at the initial step and exhibit an upward trend. Post-peak, the values stabilize or show slight fluctuations, maintaining higher levels compared to the initial phase. Between time steps 5 and 15, a rapid increase is observed across all runs. From time step 40 to 100, the values stabilize, with each simulation run maintaining a constant count. The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households increases over the 100 timesteps of the simulation.
