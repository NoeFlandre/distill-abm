 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average household risk attitude in the simulated population begins near zero, indicating an initial state of neutrality or balanced risk perception among agents . A rapid and sustained increase occurs during the first 30 to 35 time steps, reaching a peak mean value of approximately 0.38.38 . This sharp ascent reflects a collective shift towards higher risk tolerance, likely as agents respond to early simulation dynamics .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero across all simulation runs and increases significantly during the initial phase of the simulation . The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock, measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at 0 and increases steadily across the simulation period . The trend is monotonically increasing throughout the 100 time steps .  The data shows a distinct downward trajectory over the first 50 time steps . The average number of households drops from the initial 20 to approximately 16.5 . This decline is statistically significant and steep during the early period, indicating a rapid shift away from the baseline E-RO strategy among the household population .
