Simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time. The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. These trends suggest an initial period of adjustment or overgrazing followed by a recovery phase driven by changes in farmer behavior or environmental conditions. For decision-makers, this implies that interventions aimed at encouraging sustainable grazing practices could yield significant improvements in pasture health, leading to higher reserve biomass levels. The number of households following the E-RO strategy exhibits a range of behaviors across the simulation steps. Some lines remain relatively stable around 20, while others experience a decline. The majority of the lines show a general downward trend, with most stabilizing between 0 and 10 by the end of the 100 steps.
