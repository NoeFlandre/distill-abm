the RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . it implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences . the average initial risk attitude of social learning agents remains constant at 0.0074 throughout the simulation duration . this static trend indicates that the initial disposition to deviate from recommended livestock numbers does not evolve dynamically within the model . for decision makers, this implies that risk attitudes are stable parameters set at initialization rather than outcomes of the learning process . the average total livestock count begins at 90 units across the household population . this downward trajectory continues through the first 30 time steps . the lowest point is reached at time step 37, where the average livestock count bottoms out at 41.01 units . the number of households engaging in social learning begins at zero . in the initial phase, the count increases rapidly, indicating a strong early response to the extension model's influence . by the conclusion of the simulation at time step 100, the value stabilizes near this peak, showing no further significant accumulation .
