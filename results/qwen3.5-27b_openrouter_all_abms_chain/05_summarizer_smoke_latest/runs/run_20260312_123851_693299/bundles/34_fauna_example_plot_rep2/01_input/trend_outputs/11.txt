The total energy of male prey animals starts at approximately 12,500 at the initial time step. This value drops rapidly, reaching around 5,000 by time step 250, and further decreases to roughly 1,000 by time step 500. The decline is consistent across all simulation runs, with minimal divergence observed in the early stages. By time step 750, the energy levels in most runs have fallen to near zero, indicating that the male population has been significantly depleted or eliminated. This trend suggests that the interaction between hunting pressure and the life history of male prey animals leads to a rapid reduction in their total energy. The parameters such as high processing costs for males and their food value might contribute to their prioritization by hunters, leading to their quicker depletion compared to other groups within the population.
