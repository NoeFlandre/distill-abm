The number of female prey animals begins at approximately 125 in all simulation runs. There is a rapid decline in the number of female animals across all runs, occurring consistently within the first 600 time steps. The majority of simulation runs reach zero female animals by approximately 1,000 time steps. A few individual runs show slower declines, maintaining small populations into the 1,200 to 1,500 time step range, with the final extinctions in these delayed cases occurring between 1,500 and 2,200 time steps. The steep initial decline suggests high mortality pressure on the female population early in the simulation. This mortality likely results from the combined effects of the initial forager population, the specific hunting costs and values assigned to females compared to males, and environmental factors such as seasonality and fodder density. The eventual extinction across the vast majority of runs indicates that, under the current parameter settings, the female population cannot sustain itself or recover from initial losses, leading to the collapse of the herd's reproductive capacity.
