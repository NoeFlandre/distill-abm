Critical transition points are identified at timesteps 475, 600, 875, and 2310, marking structural shifts in population dynamics. The population does not remain stable at any intermediate level but continues declining punctuated by these change points. Multiple peak detections occur between timesteps 710-1038, with values ranging from 1.25 to 6.51, indicating failed recovery attempts or seasonal rebounds that ultimately prove insufficient for population persistence.

Two brief recovery windows appear around timesteps 1160-1391 and 1856-2087, yet these upward deviations are statistically significant but ultimately unsustainable.
