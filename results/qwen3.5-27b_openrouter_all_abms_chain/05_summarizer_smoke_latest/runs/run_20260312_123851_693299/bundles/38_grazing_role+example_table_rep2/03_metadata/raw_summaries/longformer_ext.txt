Context and Goals of the RAGE model are the following:This simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. In this version, each farmer stays on their own plot of land. They decide how many animals to keep based on how the grass is doing and what others are doing. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time. There are three main ways farmers behave: baseline (E-RO), learning from their own results (E-LBD), and learning from neighbors (E-RO-SL1). The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. This is the first time the model has been used to study the behavior of smallholder farmers.
