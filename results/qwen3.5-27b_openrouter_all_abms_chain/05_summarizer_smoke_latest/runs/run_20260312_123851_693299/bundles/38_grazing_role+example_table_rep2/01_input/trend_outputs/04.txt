The average available green biomass begins at 45,000 units at the start of the simulation. An initial decline is observed during the early phase, with the biomass dropping to a minimum value of 42,057.04 at time step 27. This early reduction likely reflects the immediate grazing pressure exerted by the initial livestock population before adaptive management strategies take effect. Statistical analysis confirms a significant decreasing trend between time steps 11 and 21. Following this trough, the system undergoes a sustained period of recovery. From time step 27 onwards, the biomass shows a consistent upward trajectory. Statistical trend analysis indicates a significant increasing trend beginning around time step 33 and persisting through the end of the simulation. The biomass peaks at the final time step, reaching 60,535.28, which is substantially higher than the starting level. This long-term increase suggests that the farming households, utilizing a mix of experiential learning and social observation, gradually adjusted their livestock holdings to levels that allowed the pasture to regenerate and exceed its initial carrying capacity. A structural change point near time step 50 coincides with a period of accelerated growth between steps 44 and 54, after which the accumulation continues at a slower but steady pace until stabilization near the maximum recorded value.
