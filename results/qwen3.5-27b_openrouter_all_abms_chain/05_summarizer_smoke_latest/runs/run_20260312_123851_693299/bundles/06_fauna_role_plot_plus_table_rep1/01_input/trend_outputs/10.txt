The total prey population exhibits a rapid, monotonic decline from an initial mean of approximately 166.67 to an absolute zero. The aggregate mean value of the population across the simulation duration is 25.01, with a median of 1.48, indicating that the population spends the majority of the time at critically low levels or is extinct. The maximum population is observed at the initial time step, while the minimum value of zero is reached at the final time step (index 2311). A statistically significant decreasing trend is identified across the entire dataset, confirmed by rolling Mann-Kendall analyses which consistently report high negative tau values (e.g., -1.000 in the initial phase) and p-values of 0.000. The decline is most precipitous in the initial phase, with change points detected at indices 125, 475, 625, and 875, suggesting a stepwise reduction in population density. While the population approaches extinction rapidly, minor oscillations with a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps are observed, along with local inflection points around indices 357 and 747. Despite a brief statistical window indicating a non-significant increase between indices 1392 and 1623, the overall trajectory remains downward, culminating in the complete extinction of the megafauna by the end of the simulation.
