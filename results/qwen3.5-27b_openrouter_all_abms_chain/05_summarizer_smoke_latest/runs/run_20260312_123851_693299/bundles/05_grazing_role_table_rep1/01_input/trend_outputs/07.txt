The simulation tracks the inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households using the Gini coefficient. Over the course of 100 timesteps, the metric demonstrates a consistent and statistically significant upward trend. At the onset of the simulation, the inequality is negligible, starting at a value of approximately 0.0000. By the conclusion of the run at timestep 100, the coefficient rises to 0.0726, indicating a gradual divergence in livestock wealth among the agents. Statistical analysis confirms this trajectory, with rolling Mann-Kendall tests across ten consecutive windows all reporting significant increasing trends with Tau values ranging from 0.978 to 1.000. The mean value across the series is 0.0302, with a standard deviation of 0.0239. Several inflection points are identified at timesteps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, suggesting shifts in the rate of inequality growth. Additionally, change points occur at timesteps 51, 71, and 100. In the later stages, specifically between timesteps 78 and 93, oscillatory patterns are detected with a dominant period of 49.00, implying minor fluctuations superimposed on the overall growth. In the context of the extended RAGE model, this progression implies that despite homogeneous initial conditions and a mix of behavioral strategies including baseline rationality, experience-based learning, and social learning, disparities in herd health accumulate over time. The final Gini value remains relatively low, suggesting that while stratification occurs, the overall distribution of healthy livestock does not reach extreme levels of inequality within the simulated timeframe.
