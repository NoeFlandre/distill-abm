The mean household initial risk attitude across all social learning agents remains constant throughout the 100 time steps. The values are consistently distributed in a range from approximately -0.1 to 0.18, with no upward or downward trend observed. This stability reflects the fixed nature of the risk-attitude parameter at the beginning of the simulation, as it serves as a baseline for agents' decision-making under uncertainty. The lack of variation implies that initial dispositions to deviate from recommended livestock numbers remain unchanged, which is expected given that the r-parameter is set as an agent property before the simulation begins. For decision-makers, this indicates that the heterogeneity in risk attitudes is pre-defined and static, potentially highlighting the importance of understanding initial agent behavior in shaping long-term outcomes.
