This simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time. The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. The average green biomass across all patches begins at approximately 45,000 units and declines over the first 27 time steps. The recovery phase begins around time step 49, where growth accelerates, likely due to learning effects or reduced stocking rates as farmers adapt their decisions based on experience or peer observations. The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households showed a consistently increasing trend over the 100 time steps. This pattern indicates that households progressively reduced their livestock numbers over time. The number of households adhering to the E-RO (non-learning) strategy begins at 20.0 at the start of the simulation. The rate of decline continues, albeit more gradually, through time step 76.
