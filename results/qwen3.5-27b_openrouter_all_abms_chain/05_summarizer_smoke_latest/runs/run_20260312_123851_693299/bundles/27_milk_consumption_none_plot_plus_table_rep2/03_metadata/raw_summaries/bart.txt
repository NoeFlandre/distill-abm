 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . The simulation uses both a theoretical grounding and empirical data to inform the ABM, with calibration performed against macro level data .  The overall pattern reflects a rapid early decline in whole milk consumption, followed by periods of relative stability interspersed with renewed downward shifts . The total number of milk choices made by all agents exhibits a consistent and monotonic increasing trend over the entire simulation period, starting from 0 at the initial time step and reaching a final value of 2194.57 at the final time step 32 .  Trend culminates at step 32, where the mean habit factor attains its maximum observed value of 1.0502 . Trend is characterized by a rapid initial rise followed by a period of statistical stability with no significant oscillations or decline . Overall, the metric demonstrates a robust increasing trend over the 33 time steps, shifting from initial stability to sustained linear growth .
