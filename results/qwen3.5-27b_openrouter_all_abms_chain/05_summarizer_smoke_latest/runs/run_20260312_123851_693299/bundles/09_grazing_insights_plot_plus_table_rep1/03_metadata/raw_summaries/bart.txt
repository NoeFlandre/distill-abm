 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average household risk attitude exhibits a clear three-phase dynamic over the simulation period . The data indicates a convergence from high risk tolerance to a moderate, stable risk-averse state in the long term . The average total number of livestock, including healthy livestock and destocked animals, begins at an initial value of 90 units .  The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0.0 at time step 0 and rises to 0.0726 by time step 100 . The number of households practicing social learning started at zero and showed a consistent upward trend . The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households increases consistently over the simulation period .
