The total number of prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model starts at 250 and exhibits a rapid decline over time. By approximately step 500, the population drops to around 50 animals. The decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, until most simulation runs reach near-zero population levels between steps 1000 and 1500. A few simulations show the population stabilizing slightly above zero, maintaining around 1 to 5 animals up to step 2000. This trend suggests a high susceptibility of the prey population to hunting pressure and environmental dynamics, indicating that under current model parameters, the system struggles to maintain a stable prey population. Decision makers should consider adjusting parameters related to forager behavior or prey reproduction rates to explore conditions that might support prey persistence.
