 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs .  The cumulative number of male prey killed by foragers increases sharply from 0 to approximately 100 over the first 500 time steps . This initial surge likely reflects the high density of available prey early in the simulation and the efficient foraging behavior of the agent bands under optimal conditions . The rate of accumulation slows significantly between steps 500 and 1000, with the average increase dropping to 0.03 per step, indicating that the most accessible male prey have been depleted .  The maximum cumulative cumulative death count recorded is approximately 240.3 at step 1876 . The total number of prey animals starts at approximately 250 and declines rapidly over the first 700 time steps, dropping to an average of fewer than 15 animals by time step 730 . By step 228, the total male population reaches zero and remains there, signifying the complete extinction of prey .  The total energy available in prey animals within the simulation begins at approximately 16,667 units at the start of the process . This trajectory is consistent with the parameters of high hunting pressure combined with fixed internal mortality (0.025) and starvation thresholds, leading to eventual collapse rather than equilibrium .
