This stabilization suggests that the prey population has adjusted to the hunting pressure and environmental constraints, achieving a dynamic equilibrium. The variance among simulation runs increases during the transition from the initial rapid phase to the plateau, indicating that stochastic elements in herd movement or hunting success become more influential as the system matures.
