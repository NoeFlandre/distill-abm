Following the minimum at time step 26, the trajectory shifts. Between time steps 22 and 32, the trend becomes statistically insignificant, marking a stabilization period before a recovery phase. The rate of increase is most pronounced between time steps 44 and 54, with a slope indicating rapid regeneration or reduced grazing pressure. By the final time step 100, the average reserve biomass reaches its maximum recorded value of 121,072.59, surpassing the initial conditions.

As the simulation progresses, the learning capabilities embedded in the behavioral types likely facilitate adaptive management decisions. The transition from depletion to significant accumulation implies that the agents' risk attitudes and learning mechanisms eventually align with ecological carrying capacities, allowing the pasture biomass to recover and exceed initial levels.
