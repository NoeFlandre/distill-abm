 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average household risk attitude within the extension agent population starts at 0.0074 at the beginning of the simulation . This metric exhibits no discernible trend, no peaks or valleys, and no change points . The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches starts at 45000 and experiences a slight initial increase before declining .  The average number of livestock destocked by households begins at zero and rises sharply within the first few time steps, reaching a maximum value of approximately 6.6 at step 3 . The data indicates that the highest frequency of destocking events occurs early in the simulation, suggesting households face the most severe resource pressure after the initial system dynamics begin .  The number of households adopting the E-RO (non-learning) strategy begins at 20 at the initial time step . During the first 10 steps, the count rises slightly, reaching a peak of approximately 21.1 around step 8 . The rate of decrease is most pronounced between steps 11 and 30, where the value drops from roughly 20 to below 17.5 .
