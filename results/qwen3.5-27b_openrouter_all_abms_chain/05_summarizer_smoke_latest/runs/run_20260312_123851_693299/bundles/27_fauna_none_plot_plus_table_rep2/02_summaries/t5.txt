The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. The cumulative number of male prey killed exhibits a rapid, monotonic increase from the start of the observation period until approximately step 1000. The aggregate mean of the simulation runs begins at 0.0000 and reaches a mean of 100.3948 by step 1828. By step 1200, the mean cumulative count stabilizes near the upper bounds of the distribution. The initial high value and subsequent sharp decrease suggest an event or process that leads to a substantial loss of male individuals early in the simulation.
