The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. Male prey animals killed exhibit a rapid, steep increase from the initial step. The rate of increase decelerates significantly, causing the curves to plateau between 110 and 125 kills by step 2000. Decision makers should consider the potential for rapid depletion of reproductive individuals in prey populations due to targeted hunting strategies. Population of female prey animals was recorded at approximately 125 individuals. Rapid initial decline followed by prolonged period of low numbers suggests that the population failed to recover from, leading to long-term extinction pressures on the prey population. By 500 steps, the population remained consistently low, hovering near zero. Notable transient dips are observed around steps 700 and 1100. By step 1500, the vegetation levels stabilize, remaining consistently near 33%. This pattern suggests that the fodder availability experienced a significant initial stress period.
