 Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . Model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history .  The cumulative number of female prey animals killed by forager bands increases rapidly from the start, reaching approximately 100 individuals by step 500 across all simulation runs . Beyond step 500, the rate of increase decelerates significantly, causing the curves to plateau between 110 and 125 kills by step 2000 . Decision makers should consider the implications of high initial hunting pressure on herd sustainability .  The total number of prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model starts at 250 and exhibits a rapid decline over time . The decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, with the population dropping to less than 10 by about 800 steps . The rapid initial decline followed by a prolonged period of low numbers suggests that the factors driving the decline, such as hunting or environmental conditions, were unsustainable for the population to recover .
