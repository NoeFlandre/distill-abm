Context and Goals: This agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005. The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties. We aim to explore how individual perception, established habits, and social influence drive decision-making processes. The model measures the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person to validate against observed macro-level data. The average weekly consumption of each milk type per agent is estimated to be approximately 5.29 units per person.Model Mechanics: The simulation consists of 1,000 agents representing consumers situated within a social network. Each agent evaluates milk choices based on perceived health and environmental characteristics. Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms. Agents may adjust their choices if their disposition to consider alternatives is triggered.Parameters:1- memory-lifetime: Range 1.0 to 10.0.
