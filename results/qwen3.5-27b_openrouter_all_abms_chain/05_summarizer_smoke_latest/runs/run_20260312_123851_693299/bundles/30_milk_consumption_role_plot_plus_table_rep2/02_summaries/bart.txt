The agent-based model simulates the purchasing behavior of British adults regarding milk consumption from 1974 to 2005. The primary goal is to replicate the historical shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed varieties. Decisions are influenced by habit strength, the likelihood of interacting with others, and susceptibility to social norms. The average value across the entire series was 1130.8818, with the median recorded at 1158.9667. The statistical analysis confirmed an increasing trend with high significance (p=0.027) across all evaluated rolling windows. No local peaks or valleys were identified, indicating a monotonic progression without reversals. The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents starts at 0.0000 at the initial time step and increases steadily over the course of the simulation. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistently increasing trend across all time windows, with each window showing a statistically significant increase.
