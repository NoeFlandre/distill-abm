The RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average initial risk attitude of social learning agents remains constant throughout the 100 time steps at a value of 0.0074. This stability is consistent across all simulation runs, with zero variance and no observable changes from the start to the end of the simulation. The data indicates that, in this specific configuration, the initial risk disposition of agents does not evolve. By the end of the simulation at time step 100, the average number of livestock has recovered to approximately 47.5. The system stabilizes at the end, settles at a level roughly half of the initial starting herd size, indicating a structural adjustment in the long-term carrying capacity of the system. The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households increases consistently throughout the simulation period, rising from 0 at the initial time step to approximately 97 by the final time step of 100. By the end of the simulation at time step 100, the cumulative destocking stabilizes around 97.
