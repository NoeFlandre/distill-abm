 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average household risk attitude within the extension model begins at a low value of approximately 0.007 . It undergoes a sharp, significant increase during the first 33 time steps, reaching a peak mean value of 0.3814 . This rapid ascent is statistically robust and indicates a swift collective shift toward greater risk-taking behavior among the heterogeneous agents early in the simulation .  The system exhibits an oscillatory pattern with a dominant period of approximately 49 steps . The number of households engaging in social learning begins at zero for all simulation runs and undergoes a rapid increase between time steps 7 and 30 . The average number of destocked livestock settles at 0.3111, suggesting that households have largely optimized their livestock levels to align with available resources, resulting in minimal further destocking . Following this initial increase, the count begins a significant and sustained decline, dropping to approximately 19.3 by time step 14.3 .
