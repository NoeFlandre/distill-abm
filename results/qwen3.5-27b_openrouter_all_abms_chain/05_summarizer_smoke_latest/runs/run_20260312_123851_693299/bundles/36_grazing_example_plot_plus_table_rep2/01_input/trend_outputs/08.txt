The number of households engaging in social learning begins at zero for all simulation runs and undergoes a rapid increase between time steps 7 and 30. During this phase, the mean count of households rises from 0 to approximately 32, indicating a swift adoption of social learning behaviors across the heterogeneous population. The trajectory shows distinct step-like increases, particularly pronounced around inflection points at time steps 7, 28, and 30. After time step 30, the rate of increase decelerates significantly. Several simulation runs stabilize around a mean of 20 households, while others continue to rise gradually, reaching a saturation point between 40 and 54 households. By the final time step, the mean number of households with social learning stabilizes around 32. Statistical analysis confirms a robust increasing trend throughout the initial and middle phases (tau=0.818, slope=1.430 for the first ten steps), though the slope flattens considerably in the later stages (slope=0.008 between steps 55 and 65), suggesting that the diffusion of social learning is most dynamic in the early years and reaches an equilibrium as the system evolves.
