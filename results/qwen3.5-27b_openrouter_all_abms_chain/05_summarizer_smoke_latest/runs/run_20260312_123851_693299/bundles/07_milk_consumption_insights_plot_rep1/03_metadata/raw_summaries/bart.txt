 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice . An agent's milk choice function is modified by habit, social influence, and evaluation of previous choices .  Initially, skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumption is near zero at time step 0 . By step step 5, consumption rises sharply, reaching approximately 900 . At step 10, consumption stabilizes slightly higher, around 1,200 . Trend suggests a growing inclination towards lower-fat milk options, likely driven by changes in perceived health benefits and social norms .  The data implies a steady and significant substitution of whole milk with skimmed and semi-skimmed alternatives, driven by individual decision-making factors and social dynamics . The mean habit factor for whole milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the simulation steps .
