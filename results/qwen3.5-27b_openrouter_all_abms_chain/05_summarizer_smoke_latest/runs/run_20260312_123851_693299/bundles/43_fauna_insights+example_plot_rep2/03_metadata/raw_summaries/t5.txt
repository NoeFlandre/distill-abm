the megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . the model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . cumulative number of male prey animals killed starts at zero and rises sharply . this rapid increase suggests an intense period of hunting pressure, likely driven by the initial abundance of prey and the energy demands of the forager bands . the cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population starts at zero and rises sharply, reaching between 150 and 240 deaths by approximately 600 time steps . this rapid increase in mortality is indicative of the combined effects of internal mortality, environmental factors, and foraging pressure by human hunters . after this period, the rate of deaths slows significantly, with the cumulative total stabilizing between 220 and 250 deaths by around 2000 time steps. the total energy of female prey animals starts at approximately 12,000 units . this initial sharp decrease could be attributed to high consumption rates by foragers combined with limited fodder regeneration . by step 600, the energy levels continue to decline, reaching near-zero values .
