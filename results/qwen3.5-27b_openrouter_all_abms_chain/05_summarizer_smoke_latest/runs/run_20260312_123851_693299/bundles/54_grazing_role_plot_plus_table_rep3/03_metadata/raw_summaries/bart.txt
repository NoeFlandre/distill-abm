 Simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors . The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time . The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock .  The data indicates that the grazing system successfully adjusted to its internal dynamics, moving from an initial depletion state to a state of high biomass accumulation and stability . By the final timestep 100, the biomass had rebounded to a peak level of 121,072.59, stabilizing at a level well above the initial starting value . The mean Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0.0 at the initial step and steadily increases .  The number of households following the E-RO strategy settles at 14.7.7 by the final time step of 100 . The metric enters a phase of stability with no significant trend, fluctuating around a mean value of approximately 15.2 .
