The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0 across all simulations and remains near zero for the first 10 time steps. After this initial period, there is a gradual and steady increase in inequality, which becomes more pronounced around time step 40. By time step 100, the Gini coefficient ranges from approximately 0.03 to 0.09 across different simulation runs. This trend suggests that initially, healthy livestock are distributed fairly equally among households. However, as time progresses, differences in livestock management strategies and learning behaviors lead to increasing disparities in livestock health distribution. For decision makers, this indicates that while initial conditions promote equality, long-term outcomes may favor some households over others, potentially necessitating interventions to support less successful farmers.
