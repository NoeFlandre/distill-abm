The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. Satisficing-threshold: Range from 0.0 to 100.0. We set it to 0. Social-susceptibility: range from 0 to 1.0. The average amount of reserve biomass starts at approximately 90,000 units. This initial decrease suggests an early period of high grazing pressure or insufficient grass regrowth relative to livestock consumption. The variance among simulation runs widens after time step 40. The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0 across all simulation runs. By time step 100, the gini coefficient ranges from approximately 0.03 to 0.09. This trend suggests that initially, healthy livestock are distributed fairly equally among households.
