The megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to conduct experiments to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses. The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history. Cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands shows a rapid initial increase, reaching a plateau around step 1000 to step 2000. The initial rise is steep, indicating high hunting activity and efficiency in the early stages of the simulation. By step 1500, the number of female prey animals remained at or near zero in all simulations. The population experiences a significant decline, with most trajectories falling below 100 animals.
