The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant at 20.0 throughout the entire simulation period. Across all 2312 time steps, the value shows no variation, with a mean, minimum, and maximum of 20.0 and a standard deviation of 0.0. This static trend reflects the model parameter setting where foragers are immortal and initialized at a fixed count. As a result, the human population does not grow or decline during the experiment. For decision makers, this indicates that hunting pressure is held constant in this scenario, allowing for the isolation of prey and environmental variables without the confounding factor of changing human demographics.
