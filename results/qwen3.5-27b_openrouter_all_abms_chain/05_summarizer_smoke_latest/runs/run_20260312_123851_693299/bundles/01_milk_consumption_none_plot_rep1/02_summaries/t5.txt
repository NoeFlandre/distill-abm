The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at 0. From step 1 there is a sharp increase in consumption, reaching approximately 1,200 by step 10. This rapid rise indicates a strong initial shift towards skimmmed milk. The model's aim is to replicate historical shifts in milk consumption preferences. At the final recorded time step of 32, the mean habit factor ranges between 1.045 and 1.056 across the various runs. The data indicates a persistent strengthening of the habit factor for whole milk consumption throughout the simulation period.
