The total number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk starts at zero and increases steadily over the simulation period. This increase is nearly linear, reaching approximately 25,000 choices by the 32nd time step. The low variance across simulation runs indicates that the trends are robust, suggesting that the mechanisms driving the adoption of skimmed or semi-skimmed milk are consistent across different iterations. This steady rise implies that factors such as perceived health benefits and social influence are effectively encouraging agents to switch from whole milk. For decision-makers, this suggests that public health campaigns emphasizing the benefits of lower-fat milk can effectively drive consumption changes over time.
