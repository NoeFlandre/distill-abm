The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches starts at 45000 and experiences a slight initial increase before declining. This decline continues through the first 27 time steps, reaching a minimum value of approximately 42057.0353. Following this trough, the biomass levels begin to recover, showing a significant upward trend that becomes increasingly pronounced after step 33. By the end of the simulation at 100 steps, the average green biomass reaches its maximum value of 60535.2808. The initial reduction in biomass suggests a period of intensive grazing by the household agents, likely due to the initial high livestock numbers combined with the learning dynamics of the E-RO, E-LBD, and E-RO-SL1 behavioral types adjusting their stocking rates. The subsequent recovery and sharp increase in biomass indicate a stabilization of the social-ecological system, where grazing pressure decreases relative to vegetation growth rates, allowing for resource regeneration. The variability among simulation runs, which widens significantly after the initial 10 steps, reflects the heterogeneous risk attitudes and the stochastic nature of rainfall (mean 200, standard deviation 100) influencing the decision-making and ecological outcomes.
