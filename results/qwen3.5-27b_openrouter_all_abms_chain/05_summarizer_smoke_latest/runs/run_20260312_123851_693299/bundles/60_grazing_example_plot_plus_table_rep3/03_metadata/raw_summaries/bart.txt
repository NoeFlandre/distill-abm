 RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system . It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences .  The average household risk attitude within the extension agent population starts at 0.0074 at the beginning of the simulation . This metric exhibits a rapid and significant upward trend during the initial phase, reaching an intermediate peak of approximately 0.2200 by time step 11 . This early increase suggests a collective shift in the agents' disposition to act . The average reserve biomass in the simulation begins at approximately 90,000 units .  This bifurcation likely reflects the heterogeneous risk dispositions and the varying success of learning strategies—specifically learning-by-doing and social learning—in adapting herd sizes to the stochastic rainfall conditions . By the end of the simulation, the average destocking rate remains relatively low and stable, hovering around 0.3 to 0.5 .  The number of households following the non-learning E-RO strategy starts at 20.0 at time step 0.0 . This value rises slightly to reach a peak of 21.1 around time step 8.1 . By the final time step 100, the mean number of homes adhering to the strategy stabilizes at approximately 14.7, representing a net loss of over 5 households from the initial population .
