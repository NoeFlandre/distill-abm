The number of households engaging in social learning begins at zero for all simulation runs at the initial time step. A rapid increase occurs between time steps 5 and 25, during which the majority of the runs show a steep upward trend, with the count reaching approximately 48 households by time step 25. Following this initial surge, the rate of adoption slows down significantly, and the trajectories of individual runs begin to diverge. By time step 50, the system stabilizes into distinct tiers of adoption levels. The upper tier of simulations stabilizes with a high count of households, ranging from 48 to 54, maintaining this level through the remainder of the simulation up to time step 100. Conversely, a lower tier of simulations stabilizes much earlier, around time step 30, with the count of households fluctuating between 16 and 21. This divergence suggests that while social learning spreads quickly across the population, the final prevalence of this behavior varies by scenario, resulting in two primary outcomes: one where social learning becomes widespread, and another where adoption is restricted to a small subset of the population.
