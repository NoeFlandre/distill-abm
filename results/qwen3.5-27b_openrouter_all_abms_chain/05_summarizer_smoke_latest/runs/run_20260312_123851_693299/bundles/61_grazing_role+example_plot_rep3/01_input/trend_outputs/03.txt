The mean reserve biomass begins at approximately 90,000 units across all simulation runs. During the initial phase, spanning from step 0 to roughly step 30, a general decline is observed, with most simulation trajectories converging towards a low point near 81,000 units around step 30. This reduction indicates an initial period of high grazing pressure or low regeneration rates relative to consumption. Following this trough, a sustained and sharp increase occurs from step 30 to step 60. The biomass levels rise rapidly from the 81,000 minimum to exceed 120,000 units. This surge suggests that the grazing strategies employed by the agents allowed for significant pasture recovery and accumulation of reserve biomass. From step 60 onwards, the rate of increase decelerates, and the biomass levels plateau, stabilizing between 110,000 and 128,000 units by step 100. The divergence of the trajectories during the recovery phase indicates variability in how different simulation runs approached equilibrium, likely due to stochastic interactions or agent-specific behaviors.
