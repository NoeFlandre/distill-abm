The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0 across all simulation runs at step 0, indicating an initially equal distribution of livestock among households. Over the first 40 steps, there is a slow and gradual increase in the Gini coefficient, with values reaching approximately 0.01 to 0.02. This early phase suggests minimal differentiation in livestock health outcomes among households. From step 40 to step 80, the rate of increase in the Gini coefficient accelerates significantly. At step 60, the coefficient ranges between 0.03 and 0.06, reflecting growing inequality as households begin to diverge in their livestock management outcomes. By step 100, the Gini coefficient spans a range from approximately 0.03 to 0.095, demonstrating substantial inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock by the end of the simulation. This divergence could be attributed to differences in grazing strategies, resource utilization, and potentially the impact of social learning behaviors among farmers, as indicated by the varying behavioral types included in the model.
