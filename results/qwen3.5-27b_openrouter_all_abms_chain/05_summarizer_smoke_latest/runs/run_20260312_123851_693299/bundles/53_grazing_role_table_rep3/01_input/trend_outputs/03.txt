The average reserve biomass across all patches demonstrates a biphasic trajectory over the 100-timestep simulation period, characterized by an initial depletion followed by a robust recovery. At the onset, the biomass stands at 90,000 units. A slight initial rise peaks at 90,522.74 at timestep 3, after which the system enters a decline. Statistical trend analysis indicates a significant decreasing trajectory between timesteps 0 and 21, culminating in the lowest observed value of 84,114.07 at timestep 26. This period reflects initial stress on the grazing resources. Subsequently, the trend stabilizes around timestep 22 before turning upward. A significant increasing trend is established from timestep 33 through the end of the simulation. The rate of biomass accumulation is most pronounced between timesteps 44 and 54, where the growth slope peaks. A structural change point occurs at timestep 50, separating the recovery phase from the final stabilization phase. The metric continues to rise monotonically through the remaining timesteps, concluding at a maximum of 121,072.59 at timestep 100. This final level significantly exceeds both the initial conditions and the mid-simulation minimum, suggesting that the learning behaviors adopted by the households ultimately facilitate a net positive restoration of the pasture's reserve biomass.
