 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an interactive, agent-based model designed to test megaherbivore extinction hypotheses . The model considers environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, herd movement, animal sexual dimorphism, and animal life history . Hunters follow dynamic optimal foraging theory, balancing energy status, prey value, density, and costs . Ungulates are sexually dimorphic, reproduce, age, and move as herds . Environment  Simulation tracks the cumulative tally of male prey animals removed by forager bands over 2312 time steps . Oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 63 ticks becomes apparent in the final stages, specifically between ticks 2170 and 2265 . For decision makers, this pattern indicates that under the current model parameters, the forager population experiences a cumulative energy deficit . The energy costs associated with movement and hunting exceed the energy gains from prey .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model suggests an initial phase of population expansion supported by environmental conditions and resource availability . The birth count values initiate at 0.0000 and demonstrate a prolonged period of growth punctuated by multiple inflection points . Subsequent to this peak, the metric enters a declining phase . The peak indicates a temporary saturation of reproductive potential .  The total prey population exhibits a unidirectional decline from an initial mean of 166.67 individuals at tick 0 to complete extinction at tick 2311 . The trajectory reveals three distinct phases of population dynamics driven by the interaction of hunting pressure and environmental factors .
