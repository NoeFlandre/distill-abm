The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards. The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. Initially, at step 0, the mean weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent is 0. A rapid increase begins immediately and continues through step 10, where consumption reaches approximately 1200 units. Between steps 10 and 11, a peak is observed, reaching values near 1270 units, following this peak. The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at approximately 1.045. Between steps 0 and 6, the habit factor continues to fluctuate slightly above 1.0. The increase accelerates thereafter, reaching approximately 1.1 by step 10.
