RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) These types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches in the grazing system begins at approximately 45,000 units at the start of the simulation. The average total number of livestock per household, including both healthy and destocked animals, starts at 90 for all agents at timestep 0.1 at step 0, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock among the households. The observed dynamics suggest an initial period of resource depletion, followed by recovery and stabilization. Around time step 20, a gradual increase in inequality begins, with the Gini Coefficient values slowly rising to approximately 0.030 by time step 40. Beyond step 60, the divergence in trajectories becomes more pronounced. This increasing divergence suggests that household-level strategies related to risk attitudes and learning capabilities contribute to significant long-term differences in livestock outcomes.
