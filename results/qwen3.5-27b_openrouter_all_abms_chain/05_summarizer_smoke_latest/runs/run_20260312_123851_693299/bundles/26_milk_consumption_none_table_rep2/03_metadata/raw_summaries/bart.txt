 The model looks to replicate the substitution of whole milk for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk from the 1970s onwards . The main outcome reported here is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . Each agent makes a choice of milk selection based on a function for each alternative, made up of the perceived health and environmental characterises of each choice .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent initiates at a value of 5.29 at time step 0, consistent with the historical dominance of whole milk at the start of the observation period . The aggregate trajectory illustrates a substantial shift in consumer behavior from low to high adoption of alternative milk types over simulation duration .  Simulation tracks the average consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk alternatives across 33 time steps, corresponding to the historical period of 1974 to 2005 . At the initial time step, consumption is minimal at 5.29 units, reflecting the dominance of whole milk at the start of the observation period . The rate of decline varies across time steps; the decrease is pronounced in the early phases, followed by periods where the reduction rate slows, such as between time steps 15 and 19 .  Simulation begins at time step 0 with a mean health value deviation of 0.0000, indicating initial alignment between agents' held health values and their milk choices . The metric demonstrates a persistent increasing trend over the 33 time steps . This rising deviation suggests that the interplay of social influence, habit strength, and individual perception of health and environmental factors causes agents to drift apart in their evaluation scores .
