The RAGE model is a multi-agent simulation model that captures feedbacks between pastures, livestock and household livelihood in a common property grazing system. It implements three stylized household behavioral types (traditional TRAD, maximizer MAX and satisficer SAT) these types can be compared regarding their long-term social-ecological consequences. The average initial risk attitude of the social learning agents remains constant. The metric holds a steady value of 0.0074 from time step 0 through time step 100, with no deviation observed in any of the 101 samples. For decision makers, the initial high divergence suggests that recommendations may face resistance or require adaptation during the early years of implementation. Adaptive management strategies can yield positive ecological outcomes even without rigid regulatory controls like mandatory resting. However, the initial period of decline underscores the importance of supporting stakeholders during the transition phase before the benefits of adaptive behavior materialize. Approximately half of the population adopts the social learning strategy. The rest persists in other behavioral modes. Policy should be deployed before timestep 40 to capitalize on the steeper growth curve.
