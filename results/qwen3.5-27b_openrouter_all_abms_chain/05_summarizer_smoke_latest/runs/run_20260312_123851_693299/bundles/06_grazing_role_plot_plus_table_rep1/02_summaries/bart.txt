Simulation extends the RAGE model to study how smallholder farmers manage grazing land when they can learn from their own experience or from neighbors. The goal is to see how different learning styles affect the health of the pasture and the farmers' success over time. The model runs for a set number of years to track changes in grass and livestock. The average number of livestock destocked by households begins at zero and rises sharply to a peak of 6.6144 at time step 3, reflecting initial adjustments to grazing conditions. By year 50, the growth rate remains high as biomass surpasses initial levels. The Gini coefficient for healthy livestock starts at 0.0 at the initial timestep and steadily increases. The number of households adhering to the E-RO (non-learning) strategy begins at 20.1. In the initial phase, specifically between time steps 0 and 10, there is a transient increase, with the value rising to a peak of 21.1 at time step 8. The trajectory reflects an initial period of rapid adjustment followed by sustained, steady destocking behavior over the long term.
