The model runs on a 211x211 patch matrix representing a toroidal landscape. It simulates a socio-ecological system containing human foragers and sexually dimorphic megaherbivore herds. By step 2,000, the total energy of foragers is near -30,000. This indicates that hunting success and energy intake are insufficient to offset the costs of movement and processing prey. The cumulative number of male prey animals killed starts at zero and rises sharply during the initial 500 time steps, reaching approximately 100 individuals in most simulation runs. By step 1000, the birth counts stabilize for most runs, with one simulation reaching a peak of 10 births by step 1500. The stepwise nature of the increases indicates discrete reproductive events, consistent with the set birth-spacing parameter of 730 steps. The total energy of male prey animals starts at approximately 12,500 at the initial time step. This metric experiences a rapid and steep decline within the first 400 steps, reaching levels between 2,000 and 3,000. A brief period of fluctuation and slightly slower decline occurs between 400 and 600 steps, where values hover between 1,000 and 5,000.
