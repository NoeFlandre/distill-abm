The cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of deaths is zero. As the simulation progresses, there is a rapid increase in the number of deaths, reaching approximately 200 deaths around the 500-tick mark. This period of rapid increase is followed by a gradual rise in deaths, which continues until around the 1250-tick mark, where the number of deaths stabilizes and plateaus at around 235 deaths. This plateau continues with minor fluctuations until the end of the simulation at 73000 ticks. The statistical analysis indicates several inflection points and change points, suggesting shifts in the rate of deaths at specific intervals. Notably, the rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals significant increasing trends in the early phases of the simulation, followed by periods of no significant trend and a slight decreasing trend towards the end. The oscillation analysis suggests a dominant periodicity of 63 ticks, indicating regular fluctuations in the death count during the plateau phase. The mean number of deaths is approximately 200.79, with a median of 233, reflecting the stabilization observed in the latter part of the simulation. The variance and standard deviation indicate some variability around the mean, but the overall trend remains consistent with the described pattern.
