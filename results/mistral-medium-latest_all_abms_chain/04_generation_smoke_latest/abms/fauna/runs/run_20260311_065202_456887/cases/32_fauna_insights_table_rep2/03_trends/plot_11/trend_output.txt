The combined total energy available in the population of male prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the energy level starts at 12,500 units. This high initial value rapidly declines, with significant drops observed early in the simulation. By the 65th tick, the energy level decreases to approximately 11,608 units. The decline continues steadily, with notable peaks and valleys indicating fluctuations but an overall downward trajectory. For instance, at tick 377, there is a peak with an energy level of about 4,002 units, followed by a slight drop and subsequent smaller peaks and valleys. The energy levels continue to decrease, with inflection points indicating changes in the rate of decline at various ticks such as 316, 453, and 516. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this decreasing trend across multiple windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values. The energy levels eventually approach zero, with the minimum value of zero units reached by the 2,284th tick. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations, but the dominant trend remains a steady decline. For decision makers, this trend indicates a continuous reduction in the energy available within the male prey population, which could have implications for the sustainability of the prey population and the overall ecosystem dynamics within the model.
