Following this rapid increase, the rate of deaths starts to plateau around the 625th tick, suggesting a stabilization in the number of deaths. The death count continues to rise but at a much slower rate, eventually approaching an equilibrium state around the 850th tick. This equilibrium is characterized by minor fluctuations in the death count, with the number of deaths oscillating slightly around a mean value. 

The statistical analysis reveals several inflection points and change points, indicating periods where the rate of change in the death count shifts. Notably, the rolling Mann-Kendall test identifies significant trends in different windows of the simulation. For instance, there are windows of increasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation, followed by periods with no significant trend and a slight decreasing trend towards the end.

In summary, the dynamics of the cumulative number of deaths in the MHPM show an initial phase of rapid increase, followed by a stabilization period where the death count reaches an equilibrium with minor fluctuations. This pattern reflects the interaction between environmental conditions, hunting behavior, and animal life history within the model.
