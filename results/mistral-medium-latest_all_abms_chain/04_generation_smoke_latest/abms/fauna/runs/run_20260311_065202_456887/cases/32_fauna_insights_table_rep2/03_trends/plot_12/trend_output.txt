The combined total energy available in the population of female prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the energy level starts at 12,500 units. This high initial value rapidly declines, indicating a significant reduction in the energy available within the female prey population early in the simulation. Throughout the simulation, the energy levels continue to decrease, with several inflection points and change points indicating shifts in the rate of decline. Notably, there are periods where the rate of decrease slows down or slightly stabilizes, as seen in the rolling Mann-Kendall windows, but the overall trend remains downward. The energy levels eventually reach zero by the end of the simulation period. The presence of multiple peaks and valleys suggests some oscillatory behavior, but these fluctuations do not reverse the overall declining trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, the female prey population's energy resources are consistently diminishing, which could lead to potential extinction events or significant reductions in population size over time.
