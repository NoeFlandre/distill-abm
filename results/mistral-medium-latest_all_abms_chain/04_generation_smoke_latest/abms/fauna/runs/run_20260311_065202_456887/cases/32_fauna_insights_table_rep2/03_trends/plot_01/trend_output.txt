The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration. Starting with an initial value of 20 bands, this number did not fluctuate at any point, maintaining a steady state from the beginning to the end of the simulation. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, with the mean, median, standard deviation, and variance all indicating no change over time. The absence of peaks and valleys further supports the lack of variation in the number of bands. The consistent number of hunter-gatherer bands suggests a stable human population within the simulation, which could imply a balance between the resources available and the needs of the hunter-gatherers. For decision makers, this trend indicates that under the given parameters, the human population in the model is neither growing nor declining, which could be useful for understanding the sustainability of human populations in similar ecological contexts.
