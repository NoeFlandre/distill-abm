The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a distinct trend over the simulation period. Initially, the total energy of all animals starts at a high value of approximately 25,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period, indicating a stable and abundant energy state among the prey population at the beginning of the simulation. 

As the simulation progresses, there is a noticeable and rapid decline in the total energy of all animals. By around 500 steps, the total energy drops significantly to approximately 5,000 units. This sharp decline suggests a substantial reduction in the energy reserves of the prey population, potentially due to increased predation pressure, environmental changes, or a combination of both. 

Following this steep decline, the total energy of all animals continues to decrease but at a slower rate. Between 500 and 1,000 steps, the energy levels gradually taper off, approaching near-zero values. This phase indicates a continued but less abrupt reduction in energy, possibly reflecting the diminishing number of prey animals and their struggle to maintain energy reserves under persistent hunting pressure and environmental constraints. 

Beyond 1,000 steps, the total energy of all animals stabilizes at very low levels, close to zero. This stabilization suggests that the prey population has reached a critical point where very few animals remain, and their energy reserves are minimal. The simulation effectively reaches a state where the prey population is nearly extinct, with little to no energy left in the system.
