The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a relatively stable trend over the simulation period, with minor fluctuations. Initially, the proportion of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33% and remains close to this value throughout the simulation, ending at around 32.998%. The mean percentage of vegetated patches is 32.6558%, with a median of 32.9696%, indicating a slight skew towards lower values. The standard deviation of 0.9503 suggests minor variability around the mean. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, while the maximum value is 33.0002% at the start of the simulation. The data shows several peaks and valleys, but these are minimal and do not represent significant deviations from the overall trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates periods of increasing and decreasing trends, but these changes are slight and not substantial. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the vegetation coverage. The inflection points and change points identified do not indicate dramatic shifts but rather subtle changes in the trend. For decision-makers, this stability in vegetation coverage implies a consistent availability of fodder for prey animals, which could influence the dynamics of the animal population and their interactions with human hunters. The minor fluctuations and cyclical patterns may reflect seasonal variations or other periodic factors affecting grass regrowth and consumption.
