The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the number of male prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 males and exhibits a sharp decrease in the early stages of the simulation. This downward trend continues steadily, with the population experiencing several inflection points indicating changes in the rate of decline. Notably, the most significant drops occur within the first 2000 ticks, after which the rate of decline slows down but remains consistently negative. The population eventually reaches zero, indicating the complete extinction of male prey animals by the end of the simulation period. Throughout the simulation, there are minor oscillations with a dominant period of approximately 63 ticks, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the population. However, these oscillations do not alter the overall decreasing trend. The statistical analysis confirms this trend with significant decreasing slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall tests across multiple windows, reinforcing the consistent decline in the male prey population.
