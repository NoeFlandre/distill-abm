The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the count starts at zero and begins to increase rapidly. This rapid increase is evident in the early time steps, with significant upward trends observed in the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis during the initial windows. The first inflection points occur around time steps 245 to 285, indicating early shifts in the rate of increase. By time step 475, a notable change point is detected, marking a transition in the trend dynamics. The increase continues but at a slower rate, as evidenced by the reduced slope values in subsequent rolling Mann-Kendall windows. Around time step 625, another change point is identified, followed by a more gradual increase in the cumulative count. The trend stabilizes and shows minor fluctuations, with several peaks and valleys identified between time steps 1000 and 2000. The peaks reach values close to 119, while the valleys hover around 117, indicating a period of relative stability with minor oscillations. The dominant oscillation period is approximately 63 time steps, suggesting a cyclic pattern in the latter part of the simulation. After time step 2310, another change point is noted, beyond which the trend shows a slight decrease, as indicated by the negative tau value in the final rolling Mann-Kendall window. Overall, the cumulative count of postpartum female prey hunted increases rapidly initially, stabilizes with minor oscillations, and exhibits a slight decrease towards the end of the simulation period.
