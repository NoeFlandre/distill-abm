The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend in the combined total energy available in all prey animals over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This value rapidly declines in the early stages of the simulation, with significant drops observed within the first few hundred ticks. By around tick 385, the energy level has decreased to about 4,102.18 units, marking the first notable peak in the declining trend. This downward trajectory continues, with several inflection points indicating changes in the rate of decline. For instance, around tick 730, there is a noticeable peak where the energy level is approximately 1,083.15 units. The energy levels continue to decrease, with smaller peaks and valleys observed at various points, such as around tick 709 with a valley at approximately 869.29 units. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend across multiple windows, with significant negative slopes indicating a steady decline in energy levels. The oscillation analysis suggests periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of 63 ticks, but these oscillations do not alter the overall decreasing trend. By the end of the simulation at tick 2311, the total energy available in prey animals reaches zero, indicating a complete depletion of energy resources within the prey population. The statistical summary supports this observation, with a mean energy level of approximately 2,368.77 units and a median of 146.38 units, highlighting the skewed distribution towards lower energy levels as the simulation progresses.
