The total energy of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent downward trend across all simulations. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This high initial energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps, indicating a sharp decrease in the energy reserves of the female animal population. By around 500 time steps, the total energy drops significantly to lower values, often below 2,500 units. 

Throughout the simulations, there are several inflection points and minor oscillations, suggesting periods where the rate of energy decline temporarily stabilizes or slightly reverses. For instance, there are notable inflection points around time steps 299, 472, 506, and others, indicating moments where the trend experiences slight changes in its rate of decline. Despite these minor fluctuations, the overall trend remains a steady decrease in total energy. 

The statistical analysis confirms this trend with multiple rolling Mann-Kendall tests showing significant decreasing trends across various windows of time steps. The presence of peaks and valleys in the data suggests periodic minor recoveries and subsequent declines in the energy levels, but these do not alter the overall downward trajectory. 

Key change points identified at time steps 375, 500, 625, 875, and 2310 mark significant shifts in the trend, often corresponding to periods where the decline rate changes. The dominant oscillation period of 63 time steps indicates a cyclical pattern within the overall decline, possibly reflecting periodic environmental or behavioral factors affecting the female animals' energy levels. 

For decision-makers, these trends highlight the critical impact of the modeled factors on the energy reserves of female animals. The rapid initial decline suggests that early interventions could be crucial to mitigate the energy loss. Understanding the inflection points and periodic oscillations may provide insights into the underlying dynamics driving these changes, potentially informing strategies to manage or alter these trends in real-world scenarios.
