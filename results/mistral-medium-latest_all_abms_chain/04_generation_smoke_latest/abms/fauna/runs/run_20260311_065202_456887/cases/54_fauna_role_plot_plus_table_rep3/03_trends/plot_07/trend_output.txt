The cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps. Initially, the number of deaths starts at zero and begins to increase rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the rate of increase starts to slow down. By approximately 1000 steps, the cumulative number of deaths reaches a plateau, stabilizing around 235 deaths. This plateau is maintained with minor fluctuations for the remainder of the simulation, extending up to 2000 steps. The statistical analysis indicates that the mean number of deaths is approximately 200.79, with a median of 233, suggesting that the data is slightly skewed towards lower values. The standard deviation of 65.03 indicates some variability in the number of deaths across different simulations. The initial phase of rapid increase in deaths is characterized by several change points, notably at 125, 250, 375, 500, and 625 steps, indicating significant shifts in the trend. The inflection points further highlight the transitions in the rate of increase, particularly around 362, 1241, and 1330 steps. The Mann-Kendall trend analysis reveals that the initial windows show a strong increasing trend, which gradually weakens and transitions to no significant trend and eventually a slight decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation. The oscillation analysis suggests a dominant period of 63 steps, indicating periodic fluctuations in the number of deaths once the plateau is reached. Overall, the dynamics of the cumulative number of deaths reflect an initial rapid increase followed by a stabilization phase with minor periodic fluctuations.
