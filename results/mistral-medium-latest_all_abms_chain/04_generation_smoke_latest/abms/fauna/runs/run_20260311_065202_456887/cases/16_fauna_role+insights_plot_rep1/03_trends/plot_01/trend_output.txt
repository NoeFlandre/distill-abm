The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the simulation. Initially, the number of foragers is set at 20, and this value does not change over the entire duration of 73000 ticks. This stability suggests that the parameters governing the hunter population, such as immortality and movement costs, are balanced in a way that neither increases nor decreases the number of active hunter-gatherer bands. For decision makers, this implies that under the given conditions and parameters, the human hunting pressure on megaherbivores remains consistent, which could be a critical factor in understanding the dynamics of megaherbivore extinction.
