The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct phases over the simulation period. Initially, there are no births, which is consistent with the starting conditions of the model. The first notable increase in births begins around time step 725, marking the first change point. This period shows a gradual rise in the number of births, indicating the initial establishment and growth of the prey population. The trend continues to increase significantly, reaching a peak of approximately 5.6 births around time step 1673. This period of growth is characterized by several inflection points, suggesting fluctuations in the birth rate. Following this peak, there is a noticeable decline in the number of births starting around time step 1850, which marks another change point. The decline continues, indicating a reduction in the prey population's reproductive activity. The number of births stabilizes at a lower level towards the end of the simulation, with minor oscillations observed around time steps 625 to 810, reflecting periodic variations in the birth rate. The overall trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these observations, with significant increasing trends detected in the early and middle phases of the simulation, followed by significant decreasing trends in the later phases. The dominant oscillation period of 50 time steps suggests regular intervals of fluctuation in the birth rate throughout the simulation.
