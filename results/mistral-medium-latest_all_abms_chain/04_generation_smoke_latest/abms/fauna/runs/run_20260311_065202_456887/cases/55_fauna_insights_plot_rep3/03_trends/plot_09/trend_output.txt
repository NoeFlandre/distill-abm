The number of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at approximately 120 and exhibits a rapid decline within the first 500 steps. By around 500 steps, the number of female animals drops to about 20. This steep decline continues more gradually until approximately 1500 steps, where the number of female animals stabilizes and approaches zero. Beyond 1500 steps, the number of female animals remains consistently low, nearing extinction. This trend suggests that under the given parameters, the population of female animals is not sustainable, leading to a near-complete decline within the first 1500 steps. For decision-makers, this indicates that the current settings of the model lead to a scenario where the female animal population cannot sustain itself, highlighting the potential impact of hunting pressure and environmental conditions on the survival of the species.
