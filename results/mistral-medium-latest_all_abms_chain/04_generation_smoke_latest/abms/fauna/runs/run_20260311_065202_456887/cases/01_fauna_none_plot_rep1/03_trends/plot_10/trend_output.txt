The total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at an initial value of 250. In the early stages, up to approximately 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of animals. This decline is steep and consistent across simulations, indicating a significant reduction in the animal population. By around 500 steps, the number of animals has decreased to roughly 50 or fewer. Following this sharp decline, the rate of decrease slows down, and the population continues to diminish more gradually. Between 500 and 1500 steps, the number of animals further reduces, eventually approaching near-zero values. Beyond 1500 steps, the population stabilizes at very low numbers, often reaching zero and remaining there for the rest of the simulation. This trend suggests that under the given parameters, the animal population experiences a rapid initial decline followed by a slower reduction, ultimately leading to near-complete or complete extinction.
