The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the number of births remains at zero, indicating no reproductive activity. Around the 725th tick, there is a noticeable change point where the number of births begins to increase. This increasing trend continues and becomes more pronounced, reaching a peak value of approximately 5.6 births around the 1673rd tick. Following this peak, there is a significant change point at the 1850th tick, where the trend shifts to a decreasing pattern. The number of births gradually declines, stabilizing around a value of 1 birth per tick towards the end of the simulation period. The data also shows several inflection points and oscillations, indicating fluctuations in the birth rate throughout the simulation. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these trends, with significant increasing trends identified in the early and middle phases of the simulation, followed by significant decreasing trends in the later phases. The dominant oscillation period of 50 ticks suggests regular intervals of fluctuation in the birth rate.
