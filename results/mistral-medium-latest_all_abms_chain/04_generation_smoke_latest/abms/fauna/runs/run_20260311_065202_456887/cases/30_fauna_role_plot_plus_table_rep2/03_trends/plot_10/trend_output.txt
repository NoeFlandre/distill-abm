The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear trend of decreasing total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the number of animals starts at approximately 166.67. This number rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps, indicating a sharp decrease in the prey population. By around 500 time steps, the number of animals has significantly reduced, and the rate of decline begins to slow down. Throughout the simulation, the population continues to decrease, albeit at a slower rate, with occasional minor fluctuations. By the end of the simulation at 73000 time steps, the total number of animals reaches zero, indicating complete extinction. The statistical analysis reveals several inflection points and change points, suggesting periods where the rate of decline changes. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent decreasing trend across various windows, reinforcing the overall decline in the prey population. The presence of minor peaks and valleys suggests some oscillations in the population dynamics, but these are not sufficient to reverse the overall downward trend. The dominant period of oscillation is relatively short, indicating frequent but small fluctuations in the population size. The simulation results highlight the progressive decline and eventual extinction of the prey population under the given model parameters and conditions.
