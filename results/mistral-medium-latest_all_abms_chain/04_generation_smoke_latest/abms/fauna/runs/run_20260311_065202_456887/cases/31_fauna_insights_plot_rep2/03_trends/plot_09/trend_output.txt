The number of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent declining trend across all simulations. Initially, the number of female animals starts at around 120. Within the first 500 steps, there is a sharp decline in the number of female animals, dropping to approximately 20. This rapid decrease continues more gradually, reaching near zero around the 1500-step mark. Beyond this point, the number of female animals remains very low, approaching zero and staying there for the remainder of the simulation period up to 2000 steps. This trend indicates a significant reduction in the female animal population over time, suggesting high hunting pressure or other environmental factors leading to their decline. For decision-makers, this implies that under the current model parameters, the female animal population is not sustainable, which could lead to potential extinction events. This insight is crucial for understanding the dynamics of prey populations under given hunting pressures and environmental conditions.
