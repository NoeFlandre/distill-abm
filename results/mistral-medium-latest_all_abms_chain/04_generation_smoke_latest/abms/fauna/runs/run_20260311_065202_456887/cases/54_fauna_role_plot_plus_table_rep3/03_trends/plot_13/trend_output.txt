The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations exhibits a clear and consistent declining trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high energy level rapidly decreases within the first few hundred time steps, indicating a sharp decline in the overall energy of the prey population. By around 500 time steps, the energy levels have significantly dropped, marking a critical change point in the dynamics of the system. 

Throughout the simulation, the energy levels continue to decrease, albeit at varying rates. Notable inflection points occur at several intervals, such as around 309, 460, and 513 time steps, indicating periods where the rate of decline changes. These inflection points suggest shifts in the underlying dynamics, potentially due to interactions between hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and prey demographics. 

The statistical analysis reveals several peaks and valleys in the energy levels, although these fluctuations are relatively minor compared to the overall downward trend. For instance, small peaks are observed around time steps 385, 388, and 402, but these do not significantly alter the general declining pattern. The valleys, similarly, show minor dips in energy levels but follow the overall trend of decline. 

The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms the decreasing trend in energy levels across multiple time windows, with significant negative slopes indicating consistent declines. The oscillation analysis suggests some periodic fluctuations in the energy levels, with a dominant period of 63 time steps, but these oscillations are superimposed on the broader trend of decline. 

By the end of the simulation, the total energy of all animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy within the prey population. This trend suggests a scenario where the prey population's energy reserves are continuously diminished over time, potentially leading to extinction or severe depletion under the given model parameters and conditions.
