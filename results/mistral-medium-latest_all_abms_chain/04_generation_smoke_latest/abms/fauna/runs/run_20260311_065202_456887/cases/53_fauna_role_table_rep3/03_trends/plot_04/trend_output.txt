The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the count starts at zero and begins to increase rapidly. This rapid increase is evident in the early time steps, with significant inflection points occurring around time steps 125, 245, 262, and 285, indicating notable changes in the rate of increase. The trend continues to rise steadily, reaching a series of peaks and valleys that suggest periodic fluctuations in the hunting rate. Key peaks are observed at time steps 1851, 1732, 1516, 1395, and 1335, with values around 123.6667, 122.2, 120.375, 120.2, and 119.6667 respectively. These peaks are interspersed with valleys at time steps such as 1757, 2175, and 1411, where the values slightly dip but remain close to the peak values. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent increasing trend in the early and middle phases of the simulation, with significant positive slopes and tau values close to 1.0, suggesting a strong upward trend. However, towards the later stages of the simulation, around time step 1856 onwards, the trend shifts to a decreasing pattern, as indicated by a negative tau value of -0.453. This shift suggests a reduction in the rate of female prey being hunted. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps, with multiple oscillation indices identified towards the end of the simulation period, indicating periodic fluctuations in the hunting rate. Overall, the data suggests an initial rapid increase in the hunting of female prey, followed by a period of stabilization with periodic fluctuations, and a final phase where the hunting rate begins to decline.
