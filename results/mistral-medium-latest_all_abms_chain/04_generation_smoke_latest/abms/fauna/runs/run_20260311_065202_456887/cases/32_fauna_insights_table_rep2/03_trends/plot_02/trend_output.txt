The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. However, this value steadily declines over time, reaching a final value of approximately -38229 units. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a continuous and significant decrease in energy levels across all examined windows, with slopes ranging from -8.823 to -20.000, indicating a worsening energy status for the foragers. Key change points in the trend are observed at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310, marking significant shifts in the rate of energy decline. The presence of multiple inflection points further underscores the dynamic yet consistently negative trajectory of forager energy. Additionally, the data shows minor oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks, suggesting periodic fluctuations in energy levels, although these do not alter the overall downward trend. For decision-makers, these trends imply that under the given parameters, forager bands face a continuous decline in energy, which could impact their hunting efficiency and survival. This insight is crucial for understanding the sustainability of human hunting practices and their long-term effects on both the forager population and the prey dynamics within the model.
