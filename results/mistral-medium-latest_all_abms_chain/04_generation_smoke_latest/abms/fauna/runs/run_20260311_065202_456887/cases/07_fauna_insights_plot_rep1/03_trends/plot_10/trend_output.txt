The total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at an initial value of 250. Over the first 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of animals, with the count dropping sharply to around 50. This initial steep decline indicates a high rate of mortality or predation pressure early in the simulation. Between 500 and 1000 steps, the rate of decline slows, but the number of animals continues to decrease steadily, reaching approximately 10 animals. Beyond 1000 steps, the decline becomes more gradual, and the number of animals stabilizes close to zero around 2000 steps. This trend suggests that the population of prey animals is being significantly reduced over time, potentially due to a combination of hunting pressure, environmental factors, and internal mortality. For decision makers, this implies that under the given parameters, the prey population is not sustainable and tends towards extinction. This insight can be crucial for understanding the dynamics of predator-prey interactions and the impact of hunting pressure on animal populations, guiding conservation efforts and policy-making.
