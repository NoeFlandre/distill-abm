The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear trend over the simulation period. Initially, the count starts at zero and begins to increase steadily. Early inflection points around ticks 245, 262, and 285 indicate the initial phases of acceleration in the hunting rate. The trend continues to rise with varying rates of increase, as evidenced by subsequent inflection points at ticks 1204, 1336, 1487, 1631, 1725, and 1800. The most significant increases occur in the early phases of the simulation, with the rate of increase slowing down as the simulation progresses. The maximum value of approximately 123.67 female prey animals hunted is reached around tick 1826. Following this peak, the trend shows a slight decrease, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis, which identifies a decreasing trend in the window from tick 1856 to 2087. The data also exhibits periodic oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks, particularly noticeable towards the end of the simulation period. Change points at ticks 125, 375, 475, 625, 850, 1600, and 2310 mark significant shifts in the trend dynamics. Overall, the cumulative count of hunted female prey animals stabilizes around a mean value of approximately 104.56, with a median of 119.06, indicating that while there are fluctuations, the general trend is towards an equilibrium in the latter part of the simulation.
