The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration of the simulation. Initially, the number of foragers is set at 20, and this value does not change over the 73000 time steps. This stability indicates that the parameters governing the hunter population, such as immortality and potentially balanced energy costs and gains, are configured in a way that prevents any fluctuation in the number of active hunter-gatherer bands. For decision makers, this implies that under the given parameter settings, the hunter population is stable and does not face conditions that would lead to an increase or decrease in their numbers. This could be useful for understanding scenarios where hunter populations remain constant despite other dynamic environmental and ecological factors.
