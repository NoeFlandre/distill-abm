The combined total energy available in the population of female prey animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a general decreasing trend over time, starting from an initial value of 12500 and eventually reaching zero. The decline is not uniform, with several distinct phases and fluctuations. Notable peaks and valleys occur around time steps 376 to 410, suggesting periodic fluctuations in energy levels. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms these trends, with significant decreasing trends in most windows and brief periods of increasing trends, particularly around time steps 1160 to 1391 and 1856 to 2087. The dominant oscillation period of 63 time steps indicates a cyclical pattern in the energy levels, likely reflecting underlying periodic dynamics in the model. The final change point at time step 2310 marks the approach to the minimum energy level of zero, indicating the eventual depletion of energy in the female prey population.
