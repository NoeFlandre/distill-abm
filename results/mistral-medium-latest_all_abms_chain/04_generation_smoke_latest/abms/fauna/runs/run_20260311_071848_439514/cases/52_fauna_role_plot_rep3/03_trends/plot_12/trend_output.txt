The total energy of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent trend across the simulations. Initially, the total energy of female animals is high, starting at approximately 12,000 units. This high energy level is maintained briefly before a rapid decline begins. By around 500 steps, the total energy of female animals drops sharply to about 2,000 units. This steep decline continues, and by approximately 1,000 steps, the total energy levels off and approaches near zero. Beyond 1,000 steps, the total energy of female animals remains very low, close to zero, for the remainder of the simulation period up to 2,000 steps. This trend suggests a rapid depletion of energy resources among female animals early in the simulation, followed by a stabilization at minimal energy levels.
