The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of males killed starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the rate of increase begins to slow down. By approximately 1000 steps, the cumulative number of males killed reaches a plateau, stabilizing around 120 males killed. This plateau is maintained with minor fluctuations for the remainder of the simulation period up to 2000 steps. The statistical analysis indicates that the mean number of males killed is approximately 100.4, with a median of 116.5, suggesting that the data tends to cluster around the higher end of the range. The standard deviation of 32.5 and variance of 1057.3 indicate some variability in the number of males killed across different simulations. The maximum value recorded is 120.2 males killed, which occurs around the 1876th step. The analysis also identifies several inflection points and change points, indicating shifts in the trend at specific steps. Notably, the rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals periods of increasing trends followed by periods of no significant trend and a final period of decreasing trend, reflecting the dynamic nature of the hunting pressure over time.
