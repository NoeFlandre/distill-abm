The cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of deaths starts at zero and remains relatively low for the first few hundred time steps. Around time step 125, there is a noticeable change point where the rate of deaths begins to increase more rapidly. This trend continues with several inflection points indicating periods where the rate of increase in deaths changes. Significant inflection points occur at time steps 362, 1241, 1330, 1397, 1402, 1419, 1455, 1474, 1595, 1713, and 1828, suggesting variations in the dynamics of the system. The most rapid increases in deaths are observed in the early to mid-time steps, with the rate of increase slowing down as time progresses. By around time step 2040, the cumulative number of deaths stabilizes, reaching a plateau near 235 deaths. This stabilization indicates that the rate of new deaths has significantly decreased, and the population has either stabilized or gone extinct. The statistical analysis reveals that the initial phases show a strong increasing trend in deaths, which becomes less pronounced and eventually stabilizes, reflecting the dynamics of prey population under the given environmental and hunting pressures.
