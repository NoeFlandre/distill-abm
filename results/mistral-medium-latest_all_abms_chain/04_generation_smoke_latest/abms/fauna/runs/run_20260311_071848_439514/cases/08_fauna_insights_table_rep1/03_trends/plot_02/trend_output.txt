The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, but the overall trajectory is a steady decline. Key time steps show notable values, such as around tick 394 where the energy level is approximately -2006 units, and further significant drops at ticks 1594 and 1826 with values around -23807 and -28403 units respectively. The decline continues sharply, reaching a minimum value of -38233 units by the end of the simulation at tick 2310. The statistical analysis confirms this downward trend with all rolling Mann-Kendall windows indicating a significant decreasing trend with high negative slopes and tau values close to -1. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points suggests periods where the rate of energy decline shifts, but the overall direction remains consistently downward. The oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation indicate some periodic fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the overall trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, the energy status of forager bands deteriorates continuously, which could lead to potential extinction events or significant changes in hunting behavior and success over time.
