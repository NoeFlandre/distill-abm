The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 female prey animals. This number steadily decreases, exhibiting a strong downward trend throughout the simulation. The most significant drops occur early in the simulation, with the population reducing rapidly within the first few hundred ticks. By the end of the simulation, the population of female prey animals reaches zero. The statistical analysis reveals several inflection points and change points, indicating shifts in the rate of decline. Notably, there are periods where the decline slows or briefly stabilizes, particularly around the mid-point of the simulation, but the overall trend remains decisively downward. The presence of multiple peaks and valleys suggests some oscillations in the population, but these are minor compared to the overall declining trend. The dominant period of these oscillations is approximately 63 ticks, indicating some cyclical patterns in the population dynamics, possibly due to seasonal effects or other periodic factors in the model. Despite these oscillations, the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant decreasing trend across all windows, reinforcing the overall decline in the female prey population throughout the simulation.
