The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the count starts at zero and begins to increase rapidly. This rapid increase is evident in the early time steps, with significant upward trends observed in the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis during the initial windows. The first inflection points occur around time steps 245, 262, and 285, indicating notable changes in the rate of increase. 

As the simulation progresses, the rate of increase starts to slow down, as indicated by the decreasing tau values and slopes in the subsequent rolling Mann-Kendall windows. The change points at time steps 475, 625, and 850 mark significant shifts in the trend dynamics. The trend continues to show an increasing pattern but at a much slower rate, with occasional fluctuations. 

Around the middle of the simulation period, the trend stabilizes, and the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis shows periods of no significant trend, particularly around the window starting at time step 1392. This stabilization is further supported by the presence of multiple peaks and valleys with minimal prominence, indicating minor fluctuations around a relatively stable mean value. The peaks and valleys are closely clustered, suggesting a period of equilibrium in the cumulative count of hunted postpartum females. 

Towards the later stages of the simulation, there is a slight decrease in the trend, as indicated by the negative tau value in the rolling Mann-Kendall window starting at time step 1856. However, this decrease is not substantial, and the overall count remains high, approaching the maximum values observed. The final change point at time step 2310 marks another shift, leading to a period of oscillation with a dominant period of 63 time steps. 

The oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation suggest a regular pattern of minor fluctuations in the cumulative count. The mean value of approximately 102.44 and the median value of 116.86 indicate that, despite the fluctuations, the cumulative count of hunted postpartum females remains relatively high throughout the latter half of the simulation. The maximum value reached is 119, which is sustained over several time steps, indicating a saturation point in the cumulative count.
