The data from the repeated simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the number of female prey animals starts at around 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By approximately 500 steps, the number of female prey animals has decreased to around 80. The decline continues, and by around 1000 steps, the number of female prey animals drops to approximately 40. This downward trend persists, with the number of female prey animals reaching near zero by around 2000 steps. Beyond this point, the number of female prey animals remains very low, approaching extinction. The simulations indicate a rapid and consistent decline in the female prey population, suggesting significant pressures leading to their near-complete extinction within the modeled timeframe.
