The cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the number of deaths starts at zero and remains relatively low for the first few time steps. Around the 125th time step, a noticeable increase in the death count begins, marking the first significant change point. This upward trend continues steeply until approximately the 500th time step, where the rate of increase starts to slow down. By the 625th time step, the death count reaches a plateau, indicating a stabilization in the number of deaths. This plateau persists with minor fluctuations until around the 2310th time step, which marks another change point where the trend shifts slightly but remains relatively stable. The death count eventually stabilizes around 235 deaths, with minor oscillations observed towards the end of the simulation period. The statistical analysis indicates several inflection points and change points, highlighting periods of significant shifts in the trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these trends, showing initial increasing trends that transition to periods of no significant trend and eventually to a slight decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation. The oscillations observed towards the end have a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps, indicating regular fluctuations in the death count during the stabilization phase.
