The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent trend of decreasing prey population over time. Initially, the prey population starts at approximately 166.67 animals. This number steadily declines throughout the simulation, reaching zero by the final time step. The mean number of prey animals across the entire simulation is 25.01, with a median value of 1.48, indicating that for the majority of the simulation, the prey population is quite low. The standard deviation of 44.90 reflects significant variability in the prey population, particularly in the earlier stages of the simulation. The statistical analysis reveals several key change points and inflection indices, indicating periods where the rate of decline varies. Notably, the rolling Mann-Kendall test consistently shows significant decreasing trends in prey population across multiple time windows, with occasional brief periods of increase. The oscillation analysis suggests periodic fluctuations in the population, with a dominant period of 63 time steps. These fluctuations, however, do not alter the overall downward trend. For decision-makers, these trends highlight the critical impact of hunting pressure and environmental factors on prey populations, underscoring the need for careful management of hunting practices and habitat conservation to prevent extinction.
