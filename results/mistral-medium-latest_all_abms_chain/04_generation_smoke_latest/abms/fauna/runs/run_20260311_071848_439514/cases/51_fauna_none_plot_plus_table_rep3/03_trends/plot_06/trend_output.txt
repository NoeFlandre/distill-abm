The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps. Initially, the number of births remains at zero until around 725 ticks, indicating no births during this period. Following this, there is a gradual increase in the number of births, with notable inflection points at approximately 733, 903, and 1121 ticks, suggesting periods of accelerated growth in the birth rate. The number of births peaks at around 1673 ticks, reaching a maximum value of 5.6 births. This peak is followed by a period of fluctuation and eventual decline, with significant change points at 1325, 1850, 2050, and 2310 ticks. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates significant increasing trends in the number of births between 696 to 1623 ticks, followed by significant decreasing trends from 1624 to 2087 ticks. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 50 ticks, indicating regular intervals of fluctuation in the birth rate. Overall, the birth rate dynamics show an initial period of stability, followed by growth, peaking, and subsequent decline with periodic oscillations.
