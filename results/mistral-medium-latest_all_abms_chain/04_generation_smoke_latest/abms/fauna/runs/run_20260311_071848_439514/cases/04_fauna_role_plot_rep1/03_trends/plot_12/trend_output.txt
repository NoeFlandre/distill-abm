The total energy of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid decline in the initial time steps. Initially, the total energy of female animals is approximately 12,000 units. Within the first 500 steps, this value drops sharply to around 2,000 units. This steep decline continues more gradually until about 1,000 steps, where the total energy stabilizes at lower values, generally under 1,000 units. Beyond 1,000 steps, the total energy levels off and remains relatively constant with minor fluctuations until the end of the simulation at 73,000 steps. This trend suggests a significant initial impact on the energy reserves of female animals, likely due to a combination of hunting pressure and environmental factors, followed by a stabilization phase where the remaining population reaches a new equilibrium.
