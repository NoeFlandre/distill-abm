The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 female prey animals. This number steadily decreases throughout the simulation, with the population eventually reaching zero by the end of the observed period. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a significant decreasing trend across all windows, with occasional brief periods of increase that are not sustained. Key inflection points and change points suggest shifts in the rate of decline, but the overall trajectory remains downward. The presence of multiple peaks and valleys indicates some fluctuations in the population, but these are minor compared to the overall decline. The dominant oscillation period of 63 ticks suggests some cyclical patterns, but these do not alter the primary trend of decline. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, the female prey population is not sustainable and heads towards extinction. This insight can be crucial for understanding the dynamics of hunting pressure and its impact on prey populations, particularly in scenarios where environmental conditions and hunting behaviors are modeled similarly.
