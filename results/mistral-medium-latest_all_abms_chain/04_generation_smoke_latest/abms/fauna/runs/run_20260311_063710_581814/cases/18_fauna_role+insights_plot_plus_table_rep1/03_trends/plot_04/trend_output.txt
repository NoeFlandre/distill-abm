The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid initial increase followed by a gradual stabilization. Initially, the number of females killed rises sharply from 0 to approximately 120 within the first 500 steps. This rapid increase is followed by a more gradual rise, eventually stabilizing around 120 females killed. The trend remains relatively constant with minor fluctuations for the remainder of the simulation, extending up to 73000 steps. Key inflection points occur around steps 245, 262, and 285, indicating significant changes in the rate of increase. The data also shows several change points, notably at steps 125, 375, 475, 625, 850, 1600, and 2310, which mark shifts in the trend dynamics. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early phases, transitioning to a significant decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation. The dominant oscillation period is 63 steps, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the latter part of the simulation. For decision makers, these trends imply that initial hunting pressure is intense but stabilizes over time, indicating potential equilibrium in predator-prey dynamics under the given model parameters.
