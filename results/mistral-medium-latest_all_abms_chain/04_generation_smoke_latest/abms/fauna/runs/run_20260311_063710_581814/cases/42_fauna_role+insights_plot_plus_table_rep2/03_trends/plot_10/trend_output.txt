The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at approximately 166.67 animals. This number rapidly decreases within the first few hundred time steps, indicating a sharp decline in the prey population. By around 500 time steps, the population has significantly reduced, continuing to decline more gradually thereafter. The trend remains consistently downward, with the population approaching zero as the simulation progresses towards 2312 time steps. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend across multiple rolling windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values indicating strong downward trends. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points suggests periods where the rate of decline varies, but the overall trajectory remains a consistent decrease in the prey population. The oscillation indices and dominant period indicate some periodic fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the overall declining trend. For decision makers, this suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the prey population is highly susceptible to extinction pressures, with significant implications for conservation strategies and understanding the dynamics of megaherbivore populations under hunting pressure and environmental factors.
