The percentage of green patches, representing vegetated areas in the landscape, exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable for a short period. Around time step 150, there is a noticeable decline, reaching a minimum of about 25% at time step 365. This decline indicates a significant reduction in the availability of fodder for prey animals. Following this drop, the percentage of green patches begins to recover, gradually increasing and stabilizing around 32% by time step 725. This recovery suggests a period of regrowth and stabilization in the vegetation. Throughout the remainder of the simulation, the percentage of green patches fluctuates slightly but generally remains stable around the 32% mark, with minor oscillations. The stability in the latter part of the simulation indicates a balance between the consumption of grass by prey animals and the regrowth of vegetation. For decision makers, these trends highlight the resilience of the vegetation under the given conditions and the importance of monitoring the initial periods of decline to implement potential interventions that could mitigate the impact on prey animals.
