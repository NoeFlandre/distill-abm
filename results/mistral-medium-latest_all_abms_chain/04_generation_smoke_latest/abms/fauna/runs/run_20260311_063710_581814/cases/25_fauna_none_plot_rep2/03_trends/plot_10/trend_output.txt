The total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at an initial value of 250. In the early stages of the simulation, there is a rapid decline in the number of animals. By around 500 steps, the number of animals has decreased significantly, with many simulations showing a reduction to approximately 50 animals. This trend continues, and by around 1000 steps, the number of animals in most simulations drops below 20. The decline continues more gradually, and by around 2000 steps, the number of animals in nearly all simulations approaches zero. This indicates a trend towards extinction or near-extinction of the prey population over the course of the simulation. The variability among different simulation runs decreases as the number of steps increases, suggesting a convergence towards a similar outcome across different runs.
