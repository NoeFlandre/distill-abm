The percentage of green patches, representing vegetated areas in the landscape, exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable. Around time step 150, there is a noticeable decline, reaching a minimum of about 25% at time step 365. Following this decline, the percentage of green patches gradually recovers and stabilizes around 33% again. Throughout the remainder of the simulation, the percentage of green patches fluctuates slightly but remains close to this stabilized value. The data shows several inflection points and change points, indicating periods of significant change in the trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals both increasing and decreasing trends in different windows, suggesting periods of recovery and minor declines in vegetation coverage. The dominant oscillation period of 63 time steps indicates a cyclical pattern in the vegetation coverage. For decision-makers, these trends suggest that while the vegetation coverage can experience significant short-term declines, it tends to stabilize over the long term. This stability could imply a resilient ecosystem capable of recovering from disturbances, which is crucial for the survival of prey animals dependent on this vegetation.
