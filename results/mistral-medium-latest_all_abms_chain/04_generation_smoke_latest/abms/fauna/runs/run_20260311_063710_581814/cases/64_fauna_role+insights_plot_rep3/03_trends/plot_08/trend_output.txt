The number of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent declining trend across all simulations. Initially, the number of male animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of male animals drops to about 80. The decline continues, and by 1000 steps, the number of male animals further reduces to approximately 40. Beyond 1000 steps, the decline becomes more gradual, with the number of male animals approaching zero around 2000 steps. This trend suggests a rapid initial reduction in the male animal population followed by a more gradual decline as the population nears extinction. For decision makers, this trend indicates that under the given parameters, the male animal population is highly susceptible to extinction pressures, with significant reductions occurring within the first 2000 steps of the simulation. This insight could inform conservation strategies and highlight the need for interventions to mitigate factors contributing to the rapid decline of male animals.
