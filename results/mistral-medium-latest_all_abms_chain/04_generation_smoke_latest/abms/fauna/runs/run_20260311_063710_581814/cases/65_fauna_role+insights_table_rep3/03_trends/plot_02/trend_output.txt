The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. However, it rapidly declines, reaching a value of approximately -38233 units by the end of the simulation. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this consistent decrease across all windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values close to -1, indicating a strong and persistent downward trend. Key inflection points and change points further highlight the continuous decline in energy levels, with notable changes occurring around time steps 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310. The presence of oscillations towards the end of the simulation period suggests some periodic fluctuations, but these do not alter the overall decreasing trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that the energy status of forager bands is continuously deteriorating over time, which could have significant implications for the sustainability of hunting practices and the overall dynamics of the ecosystem within the model.
