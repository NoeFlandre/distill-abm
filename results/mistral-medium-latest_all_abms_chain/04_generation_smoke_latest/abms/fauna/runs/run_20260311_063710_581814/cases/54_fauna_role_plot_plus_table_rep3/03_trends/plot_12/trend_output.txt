The total energy of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent declining trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This high energy level rapidly decreases within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has dropped significantly to approximately 2,000 units. This rapid decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, reaching around 1,000 units by 1,000 time steps. Beyond this point, the decline becomes more gradual, with the total energy hovering around a few hundred units and eventually approaching zero by the end of the simulation at 2,312 time steps. Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks, but the overall trend remains a steady decrease in total energy. The statistical analysis confirms this trend, with multiple significant decreasing trends identified in the rolling Mann-Kendall test. The inflection points and change points further highlight the key stages in this decline, marking significant shifts in the rate of energy reduction. The dominant oscillation period of 63 time steps suggests some cyclical patterns within the overall decline, but these oscillations do not alter the primary downward trajectory.
