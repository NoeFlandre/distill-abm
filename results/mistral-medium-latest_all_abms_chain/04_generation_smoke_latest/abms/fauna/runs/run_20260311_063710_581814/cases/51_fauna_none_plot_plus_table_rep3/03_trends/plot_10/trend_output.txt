The total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at an initial value of approximately 166.67. This number experiences a rapid and consistent decline over time. The decline is steep initially, with the number of animals dropping significantly within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the number of animals has decreased to less than half of the initial population. This downward trend continues, albeit at a slower rate, with occasional minor fluctuations. By approximately 1000 time steps, the number of animals is reduced to a small fraction of the initial population. The decline persists, and by around 2000 time steps, the number of animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete extinction event. Throughout the simulation, there are minor peaks and valleys, but the overall trend remains a steady decline in the animal population. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend, with significant negative slopes in the rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows, indicating a consistent reduction in the number of animals over time.
