Around the 500-step mark, the rate of increase begins to slow, suggesting a potential reduction in the male prey population or a stabilization in the hunting success rate. The plateau continues with minor fluctuations, reflecting a steady-state where the number of males killed stabilizes, likely due to equilibrium between the hunting pressure and the prey population dynamics.
