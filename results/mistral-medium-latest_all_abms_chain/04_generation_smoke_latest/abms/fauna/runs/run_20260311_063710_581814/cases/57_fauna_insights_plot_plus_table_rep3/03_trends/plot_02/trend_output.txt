The total energy of foragers in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and steady decline over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a value of 2000 units. This value gradually decreases, showing a clear downward trend without any significant periods of recovery or increase. The energy levels drop steadily, reaching approximately -38229 units by the end of the simulation period. Key time points in the simulation show a continuous decrease in energy, with notable inflection points around ticks 358, 1238, and several others, indicating periods where the rate of energy decline changes slightly but remains negative. The statistical analysis confirms this trend, with all rolling Mann-Kendall windows indicating a significant decreasing trend in energy levels. The consistent decline in total energy suggests that the foragers are expending more energy than they are gaining from hunting and other activities over time. This trend implies that under the given parameters, the foragers' energy reserves are not sustainable in the long term, which could have significant implications for their survival and hunting efficiency. For decision-makers, this trend highlights the need to consider the balance between energy expenditure and gain in hunter-gatherer societies, particularly in environments where prey availability and energy costs are critical factors.
