The total energy of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of 12500 and exhibits a general decreasing trend over time. Initially, there is a rapid decline in energy levels, with the total energy dropping significantly within the first few hundred time steps. This steep decline continues until around 500 time steps, where the energy levels stabilize somewhat but continue to decrease at a slower rate. Throughout the simulation, there are several minor peaks and valleys, indicating small fluctuations in the total energy levels. Notable peaks occur around time steps 65, 377, and 380, with corresponding valleys closely following these peaks. The inflection points suggest changes in the rate of decline, with key points around time steps 316, 453, and 516. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates a consistent decreasing trend in energy levels across multiple time windows, with significant decreases noted in the early and middle phases of the simulation. There are brief periods of increasing trends, but these are not sustained. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations in the energy levels, with a dominant period of 63 time steps. The total energy eventually approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy in the male animal population by the end of the simulation.
