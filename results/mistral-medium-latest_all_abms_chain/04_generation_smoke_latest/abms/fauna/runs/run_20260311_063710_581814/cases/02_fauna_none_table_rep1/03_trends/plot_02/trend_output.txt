The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, with slight peaks and valleys around the initial value. For instance, at tick 2, the energy level is slightly below the starting value at 1989.925 units, and at tick 55, it briefly rises to 1263.3 units. However, these early variations are relatively small compared to the overall trend. The energy levels begin to decline more noticeably after the initial phase, with significant drops observed at various points. By tick 394, the energy level falls to -2005.95 units, marking a substantial decrease from the starting point. This downward trend continues steadily, with the energy levels reaching -23807 units by tick 1594 and further dropping to -28403 units by tick 1826. The decline accelerates as the simulation progresses, with energy levels plummeting to -37713 units by tick 2284 and ultimately reaching a low of -38233 units by the end of the simulation at tick 2310. The statistical analysis confirms this persistent decreasing trend, with all rolling Mann-Kendall windows indicating significant negative slopes and tau values close to -1, underscoring the consistent reduction in forager energy throughout the simulation period. The change points identified at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 further highlight the key stages in this downward trajectory. Additionally, the presence of oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks suggests some periodic variations, but these do not alter the overall decreasing trend.
