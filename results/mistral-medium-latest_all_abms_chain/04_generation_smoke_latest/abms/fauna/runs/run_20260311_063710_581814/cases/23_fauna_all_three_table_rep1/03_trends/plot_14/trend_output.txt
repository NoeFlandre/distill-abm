The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) begins at approximately 33% and exhibits minor fluctuations throughout the simulation. The initial value of 33.0002% slightly decreases to a minimum of 24.9108% at time step 365, indicating a temporary reduction in vegetation cover. Following this decline, the vegetation percentage gradually recovers and stabilizes around the initial value, ending at 32.9979%. The mean vegetation cover across the simulation is 32.6558%, with a standard deviation of 0.9503%, suggesting relatively low variability. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in vegetation cover during the initial time windows, followed by significant decreasing trends in later windows, reflecting periodic oscillations in vegetation dynamics. The dominant oscillation period is 63 time steps, indicating cyclic patterns in vegetation regrowth and consumption. The identified change points and inflection indices suggest shifts in the vegetation dynamics at specific time steps, potentially due to interactions between grazing pressure and regrowth processes. For decision-makers, these trends imply that while the vegetation cover remains relatively stable over the long term, there are periodic fluctuations that may impact the availability of fodder for prey animals. Understanding these cycles can help in managing hunting pressures and ensuring sustainable grazing practices.
