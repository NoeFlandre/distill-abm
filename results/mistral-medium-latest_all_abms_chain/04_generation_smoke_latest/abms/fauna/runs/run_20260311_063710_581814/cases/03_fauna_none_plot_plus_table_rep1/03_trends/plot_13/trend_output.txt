The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent downward trend over time. Initially, the total energy is at its maximum value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps, indicating a sharp decrease in the overall energy of the prey population. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has significantly reduced to approximately 4,000 units. This period marks a phase of rapid energy depletion among the animals. 

Following this initial sharp decline, the rate of energy decrease slows down, but the overall trend remains downward. Between 500 and 1,000 time steps, the total energy continues to drop, albeit at a slower pace, reaching values around 1,000 units. This phase shows a more gradual decline in energy levels. 

Beyond 1,000 time steps, the total energy of the animals continues to decrease, eventually approaching zero. By the final time steps, the total energy stabilizes at zero, indicating the complete depletion of energy among the prey population. The statistical analysis confirms this overall decreasing trend with multiple significant change points and inflection points that highlight the phases of rapid and gradual energy decline. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results consistently show significant decreasing trends across various time windows, reinforcing the observation of a continuous reduction in total energy over time.
