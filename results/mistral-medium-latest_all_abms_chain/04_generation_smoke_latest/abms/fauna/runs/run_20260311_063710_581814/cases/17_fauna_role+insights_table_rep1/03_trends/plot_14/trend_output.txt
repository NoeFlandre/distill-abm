The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a relatively stable trend over the simulation period, with minor fluctuations. Initially, the vegetated patches start at approximately 33.0002% and end very close to this value at 32.9979%. The mean percentage of vegetated patches is 32.6558%, with a median of 32.9696%, indicating a slight skew towards lower values. The standard deviation of 0.9503 suggests low variability around the mean. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, indicating a notable dip in vegetation coverage. The data shows several peaks and valleys, but these fluctuations are minimal, with peaks reaching around 32.9957% and valleys at approximately 32.9942%. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant trends in various windows, with initial increasing trends followed by decreasing trends towards the latter part of the simulation. The dominant oscillation period is 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in vegetation coverage. The inflection points and change points highlight specific ticks where notable changes in the trend occur. For decision makers, the stability in vegetation coverage suggests a resilient grassland environment within the model parameters. However, the slight dip around tick 365 and the observed oscillations may indicate periodic stresses or seasonal effects that could impact the availability of fodder for prey animals. Understanding these patterns can help in assessing the sustainability of the ecosystem under given hunting pressures and environmental conditions.
