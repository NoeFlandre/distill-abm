The combined total energy available in all prey animals within the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulation exhibits a clear overall decreasing trend from the initial value of 16,666.67 to zero by the end of the simulation. The decline is marked by several distinct phases and inflection points, indicating changes in the rate of energy depletion. Initially, there is a steep decline in energy levels, followed by periods of varying rates of decrease and brief intervals of stabilization or slight increase. Key inflection points occur at time steps such as 309, 460, and 513, where the rate of energy decline changes notably. The simulation also shows periodic oscillations with a dominant period of 63 time steps, suggesting cyclical patterns in the energy dynamics. Despite these oscillations, the overall trend remains a significant decrease in energy, culminating in the complete depletion of energy in prey animals by the final time step. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms these trends, with most windows showing a significant decreasing trend in energy levels, except for a few windows where slight increases are observed. The change points at 350, 500, 625, and 875 further highlight significant shifts in the energy dynamics throughout the simulation.
