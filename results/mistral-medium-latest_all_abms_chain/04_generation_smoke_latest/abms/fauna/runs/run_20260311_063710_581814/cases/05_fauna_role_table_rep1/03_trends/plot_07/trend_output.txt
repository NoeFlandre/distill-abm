The cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear trend over the simulated time steps. Initially, the death count starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation. This rapid increase is evident from the start up to around the 500th tick, with significant change points noted at the 125th, 250th, and 375th ticks. During this phase, the death count rises sharply, indicating a high rate of mortality among the prey population. 

Following this initial phase, the rate of increase in the death count begins to slow down. Between the 500th and 850th ticks, the trend continues to rise but at a more gradual pace. This period is marked by additional change points at the 500th, 625th, and 850th ticks, suggesting a shift in the dynamics of the system. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis supports this observation, indicating a significant increasing trend in the early windows, which gradually diminishes over time. 

After the 850th tick, the death count approaches a plateau, fluctuating around a mean value of approximately 200.79 deaths. The median death count is 233, with the values oscillating slightly around this median. The peaks and valleys identified in the data indicate minor fluctuations, but the overall trend remains relatively stable. The highest peak is observed at the 1876th tick with a value of 240.33, while the lowest valley is around the 1958th tick with a value of 239. 

The latter part of the simulation, particularly after the 2310th tick, shows no significant trend in the death count, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis. The oscillation indices suggest a dominant period of 63 ticks, indicating regular fluctuations in the death count towards the end of the simulation. Overall, the data suggests an initial phase of high mortality followed by a stabilization in the death count, reflecting a potential equilibrium in the predator-prey dynamics within the model.
