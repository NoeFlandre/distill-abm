The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands starts at zero and remains constant for a brief period. This initial period of inactivity could be attributed to the initial setup of the simulation where hunters are not immediately engaging with the prey. Around the 100th time step, there is a rapid increase in the number of females killed, reaching approximately 100 by the 500th time step. This sharp rise indicates a phase where hunters are actively targeting and successfully hunting female prey, possibly due to the initial abundance and accessibility of the prey. Following this rapid increase, the rate of hunting stabilizes, and the cumulative number of females killed continues to rise more gradually, reaching around 120 by the 2000th time step. This stabilization suggests that the hunting pressure has reached a steady state where the rate of successful hunts balances with the remaining prey population and their ability to evade hunters. The variance among simulation runs is relatively low initially but increases slightly as the simulation progresses, indicating some variability in hunting success and prey availability across different runs.
