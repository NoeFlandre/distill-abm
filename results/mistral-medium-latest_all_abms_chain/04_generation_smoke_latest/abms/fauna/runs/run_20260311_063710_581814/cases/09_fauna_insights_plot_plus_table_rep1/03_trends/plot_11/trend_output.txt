The total energy of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This initial energy level rapidly decreases within the first few hundred time steps, indicating a sharp decline in the energy reserves of the male animal population. By around 500 time steps, the energy level drops to approximately 2,000 units, marking a significant reduction. This trend continues with a more gradual decline, reaching near-zero levels by around 2,300 time steps. Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks, but the overall trend remains a consistent decrease in total energy. The statistical analysis reveals multiple inflection points and change points, suggesting periods where the rate of energy decline changes. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a predominantly decreasing trend across various windows, with a few instances of temporary increases. The oscillation indices indicate periodic fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the overall downward trend. For decision-makers, these trends suggest that under the given parameters, the male animal population experiences a rapid and sustained decline in energy reserves, which could imply increased vulnerability and potential extinction risks over time.
