The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 female prey animals. This number steadily decreases throughout the simulation, with the population eventually reaching zero by the end of the observed period. The trend is characterized by a series of small fluctuations and minor peaks, but the overall trajectory remains a continuous decline. Notable peaks occur at various time steps, such as at tick 730 with a value of approximately 6.51, and other smaller peaks at ticks 710, 803, 820, and so on, but these peaks do not significantly alter the downward trend. The inflection points and change points identified in the data further emphasize the consistent decline, with significant changes in the rate of decline observed at specific time steps. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results confirm this decreasing trend across multiple windows, with significant decreases noted in each window except for brief periods of increase or stability. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations, but these are minor compared to the overall declining trend. The dominant period of oscillation is around 63 ticks, indicating some regularity in the minor fluctuations. Despite these oscillations, the primary trend remains a steady decrease in the female prey population, ultimately leading to their extinction in the simulated environment.
