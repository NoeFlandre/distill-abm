The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration of the experiment. Initially, the number of foragers is set at 20 and this value does not change over the 73000 ticks of the simulation. This stability suggests that the parameters governing the hunter population, such as immortality and movement costs, are balanced in a way that neither promotes growth nor decline in their numbers. For decision-makers, this implies that under the given conditions and parameters, the population of hunter-gatherer bands is sustainable and does not fluctuate, which could be useful for understanding stable socio-natural dynamics in the modeled environment.
