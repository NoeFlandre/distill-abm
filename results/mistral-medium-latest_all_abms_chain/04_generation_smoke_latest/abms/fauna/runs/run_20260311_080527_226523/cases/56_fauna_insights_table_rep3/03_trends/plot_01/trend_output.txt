The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration. Starting with an initial value of 20, this number did not fluctuate at any point, maintaining a steady state from the beginning to the end of the simulation. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, with the mean, median, standard deviation, and variance all indicating no change over time. The absence of peaks and valleys further underscores the lack of variation in the number of active bands. For decision makers, this trend suggests a stable human hunting pressure within the simulated environment, which could imply consistent interactions between hunters and prey populations. The implications of this stability should be considered in the context of other dynamic factors within the model, such as prey population trends and environmental conditions.
