The number of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at 125 and exhibits a consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the decline is steep, with the number of male animals dropping rapidly. This sharp decline continues until around 2310 ticks, where the rate of decrease slows down significantly. Throughout the simulation, the number of male animals approaches zero, eventually reaching zero by tick 2284. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this overall decreasing pattern, with significant decreasing trends observed in the initial windows and a transition to no significant trend in the later windows. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points indicates various phases in the rate of decline. Additionally, there are minor oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks, suggesting some periodic fluctuations in the number of male animals, although these are relatively small compared to the overall downward trend.
