The number of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent declining trend across all simulations. Initially, the number of female animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of female animals has decreased significantly to about 40. The decline continues more gradually, reaching near zero by approximately 2000 steps. This trend indicates a rapid initial reduction in the female animal population followed by a slower decline as their numbers dwindle. For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters, the female animal population is not sustainable and heads towards extinction within the simulated timeframe. This insight could be crucial for understanding the dynamics of hunting pressure and its impact on animal populations, particularly in scenarios where conservation efforts are being considered.
