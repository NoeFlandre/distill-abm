The cumulative number of postpartum females killed in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation, followed by a gradual stabilization. Initially, the number of postpartum females killed rises sharply from 0 to around 117 within the first 500 steps. This rapid increase indicates a high hunting pressure on postpartum females during the early stages of the simulation. After this initial surge, the rate of increase slows down significantly, and the cumulative number of postpartum females killed stabilizes around 117 to 119. This stabilization suggests that the hunting pressure reaches a plateau, where the number of postpartum females killed does not change significantly over time. The inflection points and change points in the data further support this trend, with notable changes in the rate of increase occurring around steps 245, 262, and 285, followed by a more stable phase with minor oscillations. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates significant increasing trends in the early stages, transitioning to no significant trend and slight decreases in later stages, reflecting the stabilization of the cumulative number of postpartum females killed.
