The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. However, this value steadily declines over time, reaching a final value of approximately -38229 units. The mean energy level across the simulation is around -15984 units, with a median of -15096 units, indicating a skewed distribution towards lower energy values. The standard deviation of 12170 units suggests substantial variability in energy levels over time. The consistent downward trend is corroborated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, which shows significant decreasing trends across all windows with high statistical significance (p=0.000). Key change points are identified at time steps 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310, indicating notable shifts in the rate of energy decline. The presence of multiple inflection points and peaks further highlights the dynamic nature of energy fluctuations, although the overall trajectory remains downward. The dominant oscillation period of 63 time steps suggests periodic fluctuations within the broader declining trend. For decision makers, these trends imply that the energy status of forager bands is under continuous pressure, likely due to the combined effects of hunting costs, movement costs, and potentially diminishing returns from prey availability. This consistent decline in energy levels could have significant implications for the sustainability of hunting practices and the long-term viability of forager bands within the model's parameters.
