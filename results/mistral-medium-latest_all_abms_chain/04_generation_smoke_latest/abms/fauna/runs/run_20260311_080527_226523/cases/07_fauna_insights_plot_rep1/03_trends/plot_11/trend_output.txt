The total energy of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of approximately 12,000 units. This initial high energy level indicates a robust population of male prey animals at the beginning of the simulation. Over the first 500 steps, there is a steep decline in the total energy, dropping to around 2,000 units. This significant reduction suggests a rapid decrease in the male animal population, potentially due to high hunting pressure or other mortality factors. From steps 500 to 1,500, the decline continues but at a slower rate, eventually stabilizing near zero. This stabilization indicates that the male animal population has been nearly or completely depleted. For decision-makers, these trends highlight the vulnerability of male prey animals under the given conditions and suggest that current parameters may lead to rapid extinction. Adjustments to hunting pressure, mortality rates, or other factors may be necessary to achieve a more sustainable balance within the ecosystem.
