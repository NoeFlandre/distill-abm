The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has significantly decreased to roughly 4,000 units. This period marks a steep decline in the energy levels of the prey animals.

Following this rapid decline, the rate of decrease in total energy slows down, but the overall trend remains downward. Between 500 and 1,000 time steps, the total energy continues to drop, reaching values around 1,000 units. The decline becomes more gradual as time progresses, with occasional minor fluctuations and small peaks and valleys, but the general trend remains a decrease in total energy.

By around 2,000 time steps, the total energy levels have diminished to very low values, approaching zero. The final time steps show the total energy stabilizing at zero, indicating the complete depletion of energy among the prey animals. The statistical analysis confirms this trend, with multiple change points and inflection points indicating significant shifts in the rate of decline at various stages. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results consistently show a decreasing trend across different time windows, reinforcing the overall decline in total energy over the course of the simulation.
