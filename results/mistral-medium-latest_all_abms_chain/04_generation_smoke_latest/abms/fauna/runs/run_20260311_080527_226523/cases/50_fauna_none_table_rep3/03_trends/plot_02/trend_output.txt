The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, but the overall trajectory is a steady decline. By the end of the simulation, the energy level drops to approximately -38229 units. Key time steps highlight this downward trend, with notable values at specific points: around tick 394, the energy level is approximately -2006 units, and by tick 1594, it further declines to about -23807 units. The trend continues with energy levels reaching around -28403 units by tick 1826 and approximately -37713 units by tick 2284. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this decreasing trend across various windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values close to -1, indicating a strong and consistent decline in forager energy throughout the simulation. The model shows several inflection points and change points, suggesting periods where the rate of decline shifts, but the overall trend remains downward. Oscillations are detected towards the end of the simulation, but these do not alter the primary decreasing trend. The dominant period of these oscillations is relatively short, indicating minor fluctuations around the overall downward trajectory.
