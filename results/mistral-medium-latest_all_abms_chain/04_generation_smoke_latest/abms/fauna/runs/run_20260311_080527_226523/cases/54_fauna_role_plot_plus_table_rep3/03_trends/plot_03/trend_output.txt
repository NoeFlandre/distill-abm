The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of males killed starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the rate of increase begins to slow down. By approximately 1000 steps, the cumulative number of males killed reaches a plateau, stabilizing around 120 males killed. This plateau is maintained with minor fluctuations until the end of the simulation at 2312 steps, where the final value is approximately 117.5 males killed. The mean number of males killed across the simulation is around 100.4, with a median of 116.5, indicating that the data is slightly skewed towards lower values. The standard deviation of 32.5 suggests some variability in the number of males killed across different simulations. The analysis of trends using the rolling Mann-Kendall test shows significant increasing trends in the early stages of the simulation, followed by periods of no significant trend and a slight decreasing trend towards the end. The inflection points and change points indicate key transitions in the rate of males killed, particularly around steps 362, 1241, and 2310. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations in the latter part of the simulation, with a dominant period of 63 steps.
