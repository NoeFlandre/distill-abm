The total energy of foragers in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and steady decline throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy of foragers starts at a value of 2000 units. This value gradually decreases over time, showing a clear downward trend without any significant recovery or upward shifts. By the end of the simulation, the total energy of foragers reaches approximately -38229 units. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a continuous decreasing trend across all windows, with significant negative slopes ranging from -8.823 to -20.000, indicating a persistent decline in forager energy levels. Key change points identified at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 mark notable shifts in the rate of decline, though the overall direction remains consistently downward. The presence of oscillations with a dominant period of 63 ticks suggests periodic fluctuations, but these do not alter the overall decreasing trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, foragers consistently lose energy over time, which could lead to potential energy deficits and impact their hunting efficiency and survival in the long term.
