The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. This value rapidly declines, indicating that the foragers are expending more energy than they are gaining from hunting and other activities. Early inflection points around ticks 358 and 1238 suggest initial adjustments in the energy dynamics, possibly due to the initial hunting success and subsequent depletion of easily accessible prey. The energy levels continue to drop steadily, with notable change points at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310, each marking a shift towards a more rapid decline in energy. By the end of the simulation, the energy level reaches approximately -38229 units, indicating a severe energy deficit. The consistent negative trend across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, with significant p-values, underscores the continuous decline in forager energy. The presence of oscillations towards the end of the simulation suggests periodic fluctuations in energy levels, potentially due to cyclical patterns in hunting success or prey availability. For decision-makers, this trend highlights the unsustainable energy dynamics for foragers under the given model parameters, suggesting a need to reassess hunting strategies or environmental conditions to ensure long-term viability.
