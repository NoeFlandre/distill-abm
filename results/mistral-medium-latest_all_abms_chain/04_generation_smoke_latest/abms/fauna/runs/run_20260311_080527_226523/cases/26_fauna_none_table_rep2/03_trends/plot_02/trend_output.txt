The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations, with slight peaks and valleys around the initial value. For instance, at tick 2, the energy level is slightly below the starting value at 1989.925 units, and at tick 55, it is at 1263.3 units. However, these early variations are relatively small compared to the overall trend. The energy levels continue to decline steadily, with notable inflection points indicating changes in the rate of decline. Key inflection points occur at ticks 358, 1238, 1329, 1397, 1402, 1422, 1452, 1477, 1597, 1713, 1828, 2040, 2156, and 2167. These points mark significant changes in the rate of energy decrease. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend across all windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values close to -1, indicating a strong and steady decline in forager energy. Change points at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 further highlight significant shifts in the energy dynamics. Towards the end of the simulation, the energy levels reach their lowest points, with the final value at -38229 units at tick 2310. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 ticks, suggesting some periodic variations superimposed on the overall declining trend. However, these oscillations are minor compared to the overall decrease in energy. The consistent decline in forager energy reflects the challenging conditions faced by the hunters, influenced by the costs of movement, processing prey, and the availability of food resources in the simulated environment.
