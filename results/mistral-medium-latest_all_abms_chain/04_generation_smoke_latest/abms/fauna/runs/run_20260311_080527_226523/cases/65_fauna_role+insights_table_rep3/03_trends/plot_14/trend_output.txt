The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a relatively stable trend over the simulation period, with minor fluctuations. Initially, the percentage of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33.0002% and ends very close to this value at 32.9979%. The mean percentage of vegetated patches across the simulation is 32.6558%, with a median of 32.9696%, indicating a slight skew towards lower values. The standard deviation of 0.9503 suggests minimal variability around the mean. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, indicating a notable dip in vegetation coverage at this point. The maximum value remains at the initial value of 33.0002%. The data shows several peaks and valleys, but these are very close in value, indicating minor oscillations. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals periods of increasing and decreasing trends, with significant changes detected in various windows. The initial windows show increasing trends, while later windows, particularly from window 8 onwards, show decreasing trends. The dominant oscillation period is 63 ticks, suggesting a cyclical pattern in vegetation coverage. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points further indicates periods of shifting trends. For decision makers, the stability in vegetation coverage suggests a resilient grassland environment within the model parameters. However, the slight dip around tick 365 and the detected periods of decreasing trends warrant attention, as they may indicate potential stress points in the ecosystem that could impact megaherbivore populations.
