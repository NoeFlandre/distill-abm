The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero and rapidly increases within the first 500 steps, reaching approximately 100. This rapid increase is followed by a more gradual rise, eventually stabilizing around 120 females killed. The stabilization occurs around the 1000-step mark, with minor fluctuations observed thereafter. The data shows a series of inflection points and change points indicating shifts in the rate of increase, particularly notable around steps 245, 262, 285, and further points up to 2040. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals significant increasing trends in the early and middle phases, transitioning to a significant decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation period. The oscillation analysis indicates a dominant period of 63 steps, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the latter part of the simulation. The overall mean number of females killed is approximately 104.56, with a median of 119.06, indicating that the data is somewhat skewed towards lower values initially but stabilizes at higher values. The variance and standard deviation values of 967.08 and 31.10, respectively, highlight the variability in the number of females killed across different simulation runs.
