The total energy of foragers in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy of foragers starts at 2000 units. This value rapidly declines, reaching approximately -2000 units by around 400 steps. The decline continues steadily, with notable values around -23807 units at 1594 steps and -28403 units at 1826 steps. The energy levels further decrease to approximately -37713 units by 2284 steps and ultimately reach a minimum of -38233 units by the end of the simulation at 2310 steps. The statistical analysis confirms this downward trend, with all rolling Mann-Kendall windows indicating a significant decreasing trend with high negative tau values and slopes ranging from -8.823 to -20.000. The model identifies several change points at 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 steps, marking significant shifts in the rate of energy decline. The consistent decrease in total energy suggests that the foragers are experiencing a continuous net loss of energy over time, likely due to a combination of hunting costs, movement costs, and insufficient energy gain from prey.
