The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 female prey animals. This number steadily decreases throughout the simulation, with the population eventually reaching zero by the final tick. The trend is consistently downward, with no significant periods of recovery or growth in the female prey population. Early in the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks, such as at tick 730 with a value of approximately 6.51, but these are followed by continued declines. The most notable inflection points, where the rate of decline changes, occur at various ticks including 305, 740, and 833 among others, indicating periods where the decline rate either slows down or accelerates slightly. Despite these inflection points, the overall trend remains a decrease in the female prey population. The statistical analysis confirms this downward trend with significant decreasing trends in multiple rolling Mann-Kendall windows, particularly in the early and middle phases of the simulation. The oscillation analysis indicates some periodic fluctuations, but these do not alter the overall declining trajectory. The dominant period of oscillation is 63 ticks, suggesting some regularity in the minor fluctuations observed. Ultimately, the simulation results in the complete extinction of female prey animals by the end.
