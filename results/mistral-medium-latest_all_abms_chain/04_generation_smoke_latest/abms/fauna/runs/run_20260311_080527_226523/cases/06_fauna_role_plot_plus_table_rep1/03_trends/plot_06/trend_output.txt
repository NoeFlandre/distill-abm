The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct phases over the simulation period. Initially, there are no births, as indicated by a value of 0.0 at the start. The first notable increase in births begins around time step 725, marking the first change point. This increasing trend continues, with the number of births rising steadily. By time step 1325, another change point is observed, where the cumulative number of births reaches approximately 2.875. This period is characterized by a significant upward trend in births, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis showing increasing trends with high Kendall tau values and significant p-values. The number of births peaks around time step 1673, reaching a maximum value of 5.6. Following this peak, there is a noticeable decline in the number of births starting around time step 1850, which is the third change point. This decreasing trend continues, with the cumulative number of births dropping to around 3.8889 by time step 2050, the fourth change point. The final phase of the simulation shows a stabilization in the number of births, with values fluctuating slightly but generally maintaining a lower level compared to the peak. The oscillation analysis indicates a dominant period of 50 time steps, suggesting regular intervals of fluctuation in the number of births. Overall, the simulation demonstrates an initial increase in births, a peak period, followed by a decline and eventual stabilization.
