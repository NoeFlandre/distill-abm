The total energy of foragers in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and steady decline throughout the simulation period. Initially, the total energy starts at 2000 units. This value gradually decreases over time, with notable inflection points occurring at various intervals, indicating changes in the rate of decline. Key inflection points are observed around ticks 358, 1238, 1329, 1397, 1402, 1422, 1452, 1477, 1597, 1713, 1828, 2040, 2156, and 2167. These inflection points suggest periods where the rate of energy decline either accelerates or decelerates slightly, but the overall trend remains a decrease. The energy levels reach significant low points, or valleys, at ticks 2310, 2283, 1825, 1593, 393, 1, 367, and 54, with the lowest energy level recorded at -38233 units at tick 2310. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend across all analyzed windows, with significant negative slopes ranging from -11.070 to -20.000, indicating a strong and steady decline in forager energy over time. Change points identified at ticks 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 further highlight shifts in the rate of energy decline. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 63 ticks, suggesting periodic fluctuations within the overall declining trend. For decision makers, these trends imply that the energy dynamics of foragers are consistently worsening over time, which could impact their hunting efficiency and survival. Understanding these trends can help in assessing the sustainability of hunting practices and the potential need for interventions to balance energy expenditure and acquisition in the simulated environment.
