The simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent trend of decreasing prey animal population over time. Initially, the population starts at approximately 166.67 animals. This number steadily declines throughout the simulation, with the population reaching zero by the final tick. The decline is marked by several key phases and inflection points, indicating changes in the rate of decrease. Early in the simulation, there is a rapid decline in the prey population, followed by periods of slower decline interspersed with minor fluctuations. Notable peaks and valleys in the population occur around ticks 700 to 1000, where minor increases and decreases are observed, but the overall trend remains a steady decline. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms significant decreasing trends across multiple windows, with only brief periods of slight increase. The oscillation analysis indicates periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of 63 ticks, suggesting some cyclical behavior in the population dynamics, though the overarching trend is a consistent decline to extinction.
