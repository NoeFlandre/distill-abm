The total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent declining trend across all simulations. Initially, the total energy of male animals starts at approximately 12,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period before a sharp decline begins around the 100th step. By the 500th step, the total energy drops significantly to around 2,000 units. This rapid decrease continues, and by the 1,000th step, the total energy of male animals falls below 1,000 units. Beyond this point, the decline becomes more gradual, with the total energy approaching near-zero levels around the 2,000th step. This trend indicates a rapid initial reduction in the energy reserves of male animals, followed by a more gradual decline as the simulation progresses.
