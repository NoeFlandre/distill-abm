The cumulative number of postpartum females killed in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation, followed by a gradual plateau. Initially, the number of postpartum females killed rises sharply from 0 to approximately 100 within the first 500 steps. This rapid increase indicates a high hunting pressure on postpartum females at the beginning of the simulation. Following this initial surge, the rate of increase slows down significantly. Between 500 and 1500 steps, the cumulative number of postpartum females killed continues to rise but at a much slower pace, eventually stabilizing around 120 postpartum females killed. This plateau suggests that the hunting pressure on postpartum females reaches a steady state, where the number of postpartum females killed per unit time becomes relatively constant. The stabilization could be due to various factors such as the depletion of easily accessible prey, changes in hunter behavior, or environmental constraints.
