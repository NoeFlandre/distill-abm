The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of 12500 and exhibits a rapid and consistent decline over time. Initially, the energy levels drop sharply, indicating a significant reduction in the energy available within the female prey population. This steep decline continues until around 500 ticks, where the rate of decrease begins to slow down. The energy levels continue to decrease but at a more gradual pace, showing a series of small peaks and valleys, suggesting minor fluctuations in the energy levels. These fluctuations are relatively small and occur periodically, with a dominant oscillation period of approximately 63 ticks. Despite these minor oscillations, the overall trend remains a steady decline in total energy. By the end of the simulation at 2311 ticks, the total energy of female animals reaches zero, indicating the complete depletion of energy within this population. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend across various time windows, with significant decreases noted in the rolling Mann-Kendall tests. The inflection points and change points further highlight the key stages in the decline of energy levels, marking the transitions in the rate of energy depletion.
