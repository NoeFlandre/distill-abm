The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over time. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly. This rapid increase continues until around 500 steps, where the rate of increase begins to slow down. By approximately 1000 steps, the cumulative number of females killed reaches around 120, indicating a significant hunting impact during this period. After this point, the rate of increase further diminishes, and the cumulative number of females killed stabilizes, fluctuating slightly around a mean value of approximately 123. The data shows minor oscillations and inflection points, suggesting periods of slight increases and decreases, but overall, the trend remains relatively stable after the initial rapid increase. The statistical analysis indicates several change points and inflection indices, highlighting key transitions in the trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these observations, showing significant increasing trends initially, which then transition to a stable or slightly decreasing trend towards the end of the simulation period. The dominant oscillation period of 63 steps suggests regular minor fluctuations in the latter part of the simulation.
