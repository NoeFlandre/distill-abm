The total energy of male animals begins at approximately 12,000 units. This high initial value indicates a healthy and robust population of male ungulates at the start of the simulation. Over the first 500 time steps, there is a rapid decline in the total energy, dropping to around 2,000 units. This sharp decrease suggests a significant reduction in the male population, potentially due to high hunting pressure or environmental factors leading to increased mortality or reduced food availability. Between 500 and 1,000 time steps, the decline continues but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 1,000 units. This period indicates a phase where the remaining male population might be adapting to the pressures, but the overall energy levels are still decreasing. After 1,000 time steps, the total energy levels off and approaches zero, indicating that the male population is nearly extinct or severely diminished. The trend suggests that the socio-natural dynamics and hunting pressures modeled in the MHPM lead to a significant decline and eventual near-extinction of male ungulates over time. For decision makers, this trend highlights the potential impact of hunting pressure and environmental changes on male ungulate populations, emphasizing the need for sustainable hunting practices and conservation efforts to maintain ecological balance.
