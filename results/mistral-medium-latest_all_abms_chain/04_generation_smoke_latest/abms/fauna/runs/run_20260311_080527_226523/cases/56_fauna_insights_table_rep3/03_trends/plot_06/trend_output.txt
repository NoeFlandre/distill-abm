The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits several distinct trends and patterns over the simulation period. Initially, the number of births starts at zero and gradually increases. The standard deviation of 1.83 suggests moderate variability in the birth rates over time. 

Key time steps and values highlight significant changes in the birth rates. The maximum value of 5.6 is observed at time step 1673, marking a peak in the cumulative births. Other notable peaks occur at time steps 1392, 1214, and 1073, with values of 4.3, 3.45, and 2.875 respectively. These peaks suggest periods of increased birth rates, potentially due to favorable environmental conditions or reduced predation pressure.
