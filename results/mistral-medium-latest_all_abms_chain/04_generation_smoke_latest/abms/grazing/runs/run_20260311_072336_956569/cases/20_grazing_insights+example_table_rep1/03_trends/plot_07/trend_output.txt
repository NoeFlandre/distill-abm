The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at a value of approximately 0. This indicates an initially equal distribution of healthy livestock among households. Over the course of the simulation, the Gini coefficient exhibits a consistent increasing trend, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 at the final time step. This trend suggests a gradual increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock over time. The increase is steady and significant across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, indicating a persistent and consistent trend towards greater inequality. The presence of multiple inflection points suggests that while the overall trend is increasing, there are periods where the rate of increase varies. The absence of detected peaks and valleys further supports the steady nature of this trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that over time, disparities in the distribution of healthy livestock among households are likely to grow, which could have implications for the equity and sustainability of the pastoral system. Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions to mitigate increasing inequality and promote more balanced livestock distribution.
