The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of approximately 0.0, indicating a relatively equal distribution of healthy livestock among households. Over time, the inequality gradually increases, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 by the end of the simulation at time step 100. The mean Gini coefficient over the entire period is 0.0302, with a median value of 0.0229, suggesting that the distribution of inequality values is slightly skewed towards higher inequality. The standard deviation of 0.0239 indicates some variability in the inequality values over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend in inequality across all windows, with consistent positive tau values and significant p-values. Several inflection points are detected at time steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, suggesting changes in the rate of increase in inequality. Additionally, change points are identified at time steps 51, 71, and 100, indicating notable shifts in the trend. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 49 time steps, with multiple oscillation indices detected towards the latter part of the simulation period. Overall, the data suggests a steady and significant increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households over the course of the simulation.
