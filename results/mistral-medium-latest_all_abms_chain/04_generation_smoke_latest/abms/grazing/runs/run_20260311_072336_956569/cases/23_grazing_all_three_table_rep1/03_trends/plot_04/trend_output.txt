After this low point, the biomass begins a steady and consistent increase. This trend becomes statistically significant from time step 33 onwards, indicating a robust recovery and growth phase in the vegetation. The increasing trend continues throughout the remainder of the simulation, ending with a biomass value of 60,536 units at time step 100. The consistent upward trend in the latter part of the simulation suggests effective adaptive management practices by the households, leading to sustainable vegetation growth.
