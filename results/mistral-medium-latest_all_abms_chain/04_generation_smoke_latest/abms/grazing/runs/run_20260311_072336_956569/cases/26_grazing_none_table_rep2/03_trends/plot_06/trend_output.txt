The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average number of livestock destocked across all households over time. Initially, the destocking rate starts at zero and rapidly increases to a peak of approximately 6.61 livestock at time step 3. This sharp rise is followed by a series of fluctuations with decreasing magnitudes, indicating a period of instability and adjustment. Notable peaks occur at time steps 9, 12, 17, and 19, with values of approximately 3.37, 2.74, 1.47, and 1.38 livestock respectively. Corresponding valleys are observed at time steps 8, 11, 16, and 18, with values around 3.33, 2.47, 1.45, and 0.90 livestock. After time step 27, the destocking rate stabilizes and exhibits a general decreasing trend, eventually leveling off to a relatively steady state around 0.31 livestock by the end of the simulation at time step 100. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates significant decreasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation, with periods of no significant trend and a brief increasing trend towards the latter part of the simulation. The model exhibits oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 49 time steps, suggesting cyclic patterns in the destocking rates. The inflection points and change points further highlight the dynamic adjustments and shifts in the destocking trends over the simulation period.
