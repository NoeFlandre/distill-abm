The average cumulative number of destocked livestock across all households in the simulation exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the entire time period. Initially, the rate of increase is relatively steep, with a notable slope of approximately 4.956 in the first ten time steps. This rate of increase gradually diminishes but remains positive throughout the simulation. By the final time step, the cumulative number of destocked livestock reaches around 96.7783. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across all windows, indicating a steady rise in destocking activities. Inflection points are observed at various time steps, suggesting periods where the rate of increase slightly changes, but the overall trend remains upward. The presence of multiple oscillation indices hints at minor fluctuations within the general increasing pattern. For decision makers, this trend suggests a continuous and increasing pressure on households to destock livestock over time, which could imply growing challenges in maintaining livestock under the given conditions. Understanding these dynamics can help in planning interventions or support mechanisms to assist households in managing their livestock more sustainably.
