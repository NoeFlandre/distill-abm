The simulation of the agent-based model, which extends the original RAGE model to include learning capabilities, shows distinct trends in the mean household risk attitude over 100 time steps. Initially, the mean household risk attitude starts at a low value of approximately 0.0074. During the early stages, up to around time step 20, there is a rapid increase in the risk attitude, reaching a value of about 0.2200. This period is marked by a significant upward trend, indicating that households are becoming more inclined to take risks. Following this initial rise, the risk attitude continues to increase but at a slower rate, peaking around time step 33 with a value of approximately 0.3814. This peak represents the highest risk attitude observed during the simulation. After reaching this peak, there is a gradual decline in the risk attitude, with notable fluctuations. By time step 46, the risk attitude drops to around 0.2691. From this point onward, the risk attitude stabilizes and exhibits minor oscillations, eventually settling at a value of approximately 0.1911 by the end of the simulation at time step 100. The overall mean risk attitude across the simulation is 0.2333, with a median value of 0.2013, indicating that the risk attitude tends to be slightly above the median for a significant portion of the simulation. The standard deviation of 0.0936 suggests moderate variability in the risk attitude over time. The simulation identifies several inflection points and change points, indicating shifts in the trend of risk attitude. Notably, the rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals significant increasing trends in the early stages and significant decreasing trends following the peak, with no significant trends observed in the later stages of the simulation. This analysis provides insights into how household risk attitudes evolve over time in response to the adaptive management strategies and learning capabilities incorporated into the model.
