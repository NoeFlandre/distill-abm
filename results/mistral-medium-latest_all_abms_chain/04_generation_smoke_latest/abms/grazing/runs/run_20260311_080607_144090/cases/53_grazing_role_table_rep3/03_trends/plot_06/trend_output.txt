The simulation results indicate that the average number of livestock destocked across all households exhibits a general decreasing trend over the 100 timesteps, with notable fluctuations and periods of stability. Initially, the destocking rate starts at 0 and rapidly increases, reaching a peak of approximately 6.61 at timestep 3. This peak is followed by a sharp decline to around 3.33 by timestep 8. The trend continues to decrease, with smaller peaks and valleys observed at subsequent timesteps. For instance, notable peaks occur at timesteps 9, 12, and 17 with values of approximately 3.37, 2.74, and 1.47 respectively. These peaks are interspersed with valleys at timesteps 11, 16, and 18, with values around 2.47, 1.45, and 0.90 respectively. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms significant decreasing trends in the initial windows, with the first window (timesteps 0-10) showing a strong decreasing trend (tau=-0.491, p=0.043), and the second window (timesteps 11-21) exhibiting an even stronger decreasing trend (tau=-0.855, p=0.000). This decreasing trend continues, albeit less strongly, in the third window (timesteps 22-32) with a tau value of -0.527 and a p-value of 0.029. The trend stabilizes in the fourth window (timesteps 33-43), showing no significant trend (tau=-0.127, p=0.640). However, a decreasing trend resumes in the fifth window (timesteps 44-54) with a tau value of -0.618 and a p-value of 0.010. The sixth window (timesteps 55-65) again shows no significant trend (tau=-0.109, p=0.669), followed by an increasing trend in the seventh window (timesteps 66-76) with a tau value of 0.509 and a p-value of 0.026. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 49 timesteps, indicating a cyclical pattern in the destocking rates. The inflection points and change points further highlight the dynamic nature of the destocking process, with significant changes occurring at timesteps 30 and 100. Overall, the data suggests that while there are periods of stability and slight increases, the overarching trend is a decrease in the average number of livestock destocked over time.
