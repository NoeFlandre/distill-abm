The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households shows a clear decreasing trend initially, followed by a stabilization and slight recovery. At the beginning of the simulation, the average number of livestock starts at 90.0000. This value rapidly decreases, reaching a valley at time step 37 with a value of 41.0089. This initial decline indicates a significant reduction in livestock numbers early in the simulation. Following this low point, the trend stabilizes and begins to show signs of recovery. From time step 44 onwards, there is a consistent increasing trend in the number of livestock, although the rate of increase slows down over time. The inflection points at time steps 3 and 46 suggest changes in the rate of decline and recovery, respectively. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms these trends, showing significant decreasing trends in the initial windows and significant increasing trends in the later windows. The change points at time steps 20, 55, and 100 further highlight the shifts in the trend dynamics. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations in the data, particularly around the mid-point of the simulation. For decision makers, these trends imply that initial conditions or strategies may lead to a significant reduction in livestock, but adaptive management strategies can stabilize and potentially reverse this decline over time. The slow recovery suggests that while adaptive strategies are beneficial, their effects may take time to manifest fully.
