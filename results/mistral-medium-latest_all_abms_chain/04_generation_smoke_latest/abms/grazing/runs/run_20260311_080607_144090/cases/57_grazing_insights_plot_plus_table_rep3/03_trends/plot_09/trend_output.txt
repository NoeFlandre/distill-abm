The mean destock total of households in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the 100 steps of the simulation. Initially, the destock total starts at 0 and rises sharply in the early steps, indicating a rapid increase in the number of livestock being destocked. This sharp increase begins to taper off around step 25, which is identified as the first change point. Beyond this point, the rate of increase slows down, but the trend remains positive throughout the simulation. The second change point occurs at step 60, after which the rate of increase becomes even more gradual. By the end of the simulation at step 100, the mean destock total reaches approximately 96.78 livestock. The statistical analysis reveals that the trend is consistently increasing across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, with significant p-values indicating strong evidence of this trend. The inflection points and oscillation indices suggest periods where the rate of increase experiences minor fluctuations, but the overall trajectory remains upward. For decision makers, these trends imply that over time, households are increasingly destocking livestock, which could be influenced by the adaptive management strategies and learning behaviors incorporated into the model. The consistent upward trend suggests a long-term adjustment in livestock management practices among households, potentially reflecting responses to ecological and climatic conditions simulated in the model.
