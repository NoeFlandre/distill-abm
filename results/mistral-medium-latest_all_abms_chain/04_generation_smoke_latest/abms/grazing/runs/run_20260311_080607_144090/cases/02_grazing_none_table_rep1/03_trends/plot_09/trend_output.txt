The trend is characterized by a steady increase, with the rate of destocking being more pronounced in the early stages and gradually stabilizing as time progresses. Key inflection points are observed at various time steps, notably around time steps 45, 47, 53, 56, 57, 62, 67, 72, 74, and 75, indicating periods where the rate of change in destocking experiences slight variations. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across all windows, with the slope of the trend decreasing over time, suggesting a slowing rate of increase in later time steps. Change points are identified at time steps 25, 60, and 100, marking significant shifts in the trend. Additionally, the data exhibits oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 49 time steps, indicating cyclical patterns in the destocking process. Overall, the simulation demonstrates a clear and sustained increase in the cumulative number of livestock destocked, reflecting the adaptive management strategies and learning behaviors of the households within the model.
