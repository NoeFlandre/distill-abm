The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at a value of 0.0, indicating perfect equality. Over the course of the simulation, the Gini coefficient exhibits a consistent increasing trend, reaching a final value of approximately 0.0726. This trend suggests a gradual shift towards a more unequal distribution of healthy livestock among households. The mean value of the Gini coefficient across the simulation is 0.0302, with a median of 0.0229, indicating that the central tendency of the data remains relatively low but positive throughout the simulation. The standard deviation of 0.0239 and variance of 0.0006 suggest low variability in the inequality measures over time. Several inflection points are detected at time steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, indicating periods where the rate of change in inequality experiences notable shifts. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend in inequality across all windows, with significant p-values and high Kendall tau coefficients, reinforcing the steady rise in inequality. Change points are identified at time steps 51, 71, and 100, marking significant shifts in the trend. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 49 time steps, with oscillations becoming more frequent towards the end of the simulation, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the rate of change of inequality. Overall, the simulation indicates a steady and significant increase in the inequality of healthy livestock distribution among households, with periodic variations in the rate of this increase.
