The initial rapid decline followed by a gradual increase suggests an adaptive management strategy where households reduce their livestock numbers initially to match carrying capacity and then gradually increase as they optimize their grazing strategies. For decision makers, this trend indicates the importance of initial conservative stocking rates followed by adaptive increases as conditions and management practices improve. The variability in livestock numbers decreases over time, indicating a stabilization in household strategies and ecological conditions.
