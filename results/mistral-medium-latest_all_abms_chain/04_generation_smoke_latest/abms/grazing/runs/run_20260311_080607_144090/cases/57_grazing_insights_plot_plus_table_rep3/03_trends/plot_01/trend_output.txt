The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant at approximately 0.0074 throughout all time steps from 0 to 100. This indicates that there is no change in the initial disposition of households to deviate from the recommended number of livestock over the entire simulation period. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, showing no significant trends or variations in the data. The peaks and valleys identified in the data all share the same value, further emphasizing the lack of fluctuation. The rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows also indicates no significant trends, reinforcing the consistency of the household risk attitude. For decision makers, this implies a stable initial risk attitude among households, suggesting that any interventions or policies aimed at influencing risk attitudes would need to consider this inherent stability and potentially explore other factors that could induce changes in household behavior.
