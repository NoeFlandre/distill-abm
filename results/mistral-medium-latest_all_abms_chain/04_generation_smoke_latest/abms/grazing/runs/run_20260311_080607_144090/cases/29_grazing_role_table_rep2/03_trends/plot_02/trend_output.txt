The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several notable trends in the average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents over the course of 100 timesteps. Initially, the deviation starts at a low value of 0.0074. The early phase of the simulation, up to around timestep 32, is characterized by a significant increasing trend in the deviation, reaching a peak value of 0.3814 at timestep 33. This period of increase is marked by several inflection points and oscillations, indicating fluctuations in the deviation values. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms significant increasing trends during these initial windows, with high tau values and significant p-values. Following the peak at timestep 33, the deviation begins to decrease, exhibiting a significant decreasing trend as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis for the subsequent windows. This downward trend continues until around timestep 54, where the deviation values stabilize and the significant trends diminish. The latter part of the simulation, from timestep 66 onwards, shows no significant trends in the deviation values, indicating a period of relative stability. The deviation values during this stable period hover around the mean value of 0.2333, with the end value recorded at 0.1911. The simulation thus exhibits an initial phase of increasing deviation, followed by a period of decrease, and ultimately stabilizes with no significant trends in the latter timesteps.
