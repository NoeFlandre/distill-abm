This sharp decrease suggests a significant reduction in livestock, potentially due to initial adaptive management strategies and environmental pressures. Following this decline, the number of livestock gradually increases, showing a trend of recovery and stabilization. By the end of the simulation at 100 time steps, the average number of livestock stabilizes around 47.55. The overall mean across the entire period is approximately 48.79, with a median value of 47.31, indicating a slight positive skew in the distribution. The standard deviation of 9.57 reflects moderate variability in the data. The initial rapid decline is followed by a period of stabilization and gradual recovery, indicating adaptive management and possible learning effects among households. The significant decreasing trends in the early time windows, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis, transition to increasing trends in the later windows, suggesting a shift from initial stress responses to adaptive strategies and recovery.
