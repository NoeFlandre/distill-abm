Initially, the increase is gradual, with the coefficient reaching approximately 0.015 by step 20. This upward trend continues steadily, with notable inflection points around steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, indicating slight changes in the rate of increase. By step 50, the Gini coefficient reaches around 0.03, and the rate of increase becomes more pronounced after this point. Significant change points are detected at steps 51, 71, and 100, suggesting shifts in the dynamics of livestock distribution inequality. The coefficient continues to rise, reaching approximately 0.0726 by the final step. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across all windows, with significant p-values indicating strong statistical evidence of this trend. The oscillation indices suggest some periodic fluctuations in the later stages, particularly from step 78 onwards, but the overall trend remains upward. This indicates a growing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households as the simulation progresses.
