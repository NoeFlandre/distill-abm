The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at a value of approximately 0, indicating perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock. Over time, the inequality gradually increases, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 by the end of the simulation at time step 100. The mean value of the Gini coefficient over the entire period is 0.0302, with a median value of 0.0229, suggesting that the distribution is slightly skewed towards higher inequality values. The standard deviation of 0.0239 indicates some variability in the inequality levels over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend in inequality across all windows, with a consistent positive slope and high Kendall tau values, indicating a strong and steady increase in inequality. Several inflection points are detected, suggesting changes in the rate of increase at specific time steps. Additionally, multiple change points are identified, notably at time steps 51, 71, and 100, which may indicate shifts in the underlying dynamics of the system. The oscillation analysis reveals a dominant period of 49 time steps, with several oscillation indices detected towards the latter part of the simulation, suggesting some periodic fluctuations in the inequality levels. For decision makers, the consistent increase in inequality highlights the need for interventions aimed at promoting more equitable distribution of livestock resources among households. Understanding the inflection and change points can help in identifying critical periods where policy interventions might be most effective.
