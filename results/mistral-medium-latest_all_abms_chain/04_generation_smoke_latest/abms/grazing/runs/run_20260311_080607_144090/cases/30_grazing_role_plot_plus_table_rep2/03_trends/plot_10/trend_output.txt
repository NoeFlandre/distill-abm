The simulation of the agent-based model, which extends the RAGE model to include learning capabilities, shows distinct trends in the number of non-learning households (E-RO strategy) over 100 time steps. Initially, the number of non-learning households starts at 20. The count exhibits a slight increase, peaking at approximately 21.1 households around the 8th time step. Following this peak, there is a noticeable decline in the number of non-learning households, dropping to around 14.7 households by the 92nd time step. This downward trend is marked by several inflection points and periods of oscillation, indicating fluctuations in the number of non-learning households. Key change points occur around the 20th and 45th time steps, suggesting significant shifts in the dynamics of household behavior. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis reveals significant decreasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation, with periods of no significant trend towards the end. The oscillation analysis indicates a dominant period of 49 time steps, highlighting recurring patterns in the data. Overall, the simulation demonstrates a general decline in the number of non-learning households, with notable periods of fluctuation and stabilization.
