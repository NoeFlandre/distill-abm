The number of households engaging in social learning, as indicated by the variable household-SL being true, exhibits a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, no households are engaged in social learning. However, this number steadily rises, reaching a peak of approximately 32 households. The trend is characterized by several inflection points, notably at time steps 7, 28, 30, 33, 38, 57, and 60, indicating periods where the rate of increase changes. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across multiple windows, with high tau values and significant p-values, underscoring the robustness of this upward trajectory. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations around the general trend, particularly between time steps 22 and 59. Change points are detected at time steps 40 and 100, marking significant shifts in the trend. For decision makers, these trends imply a growing adoption of social learning among households over time, which could influence the overall adaptive management strategies and resilience of the pastoral systems. The consistent increase suggests that social learning becomes more prevalent, potentially enhancing the collective decision-making processes and adaptive capacities of the households.
