The average amount of actual available green biomass across all patches shows distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the green biomass starts at 45000.0000 units. In the early stages, there is a slight increase followed by a notable decrease, reaching a minimum value of 42057.0353 units at time step 27. This initial fluctuation indicates a period of adjustment where grazing impacts are more pronounced. Following this low point, the biomass begins a consistent upward trend. Significant increasing trends are observed starting from time step 33 onwards, with the biomass steadily rising and reaching a maximum value of 60535.2808 units by the end of the simulation at time step 100. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these trends, showing significant increasing patterns in multiple windows, particularly from time step 33 to the end of the simulation. The presence of change points at time steps 50 and 100 suggests shifts in the underlying dynamics of biomass accumulation. The oscillation analysis indicates periodic fluctuations, particularly around the mid-range of the simulation. For decision makers, these trends highlight the resilience and recovery capacity of the grazing system over time. The initial decrease in biomass underscores the immediate impact of grazing, while the subsequent recovery and growth suggest effective adaptive management and ecological resilience. This information can guide policies aimed at sustainable grazing practices and long-term ecological health.
