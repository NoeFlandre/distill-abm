As the simulation progresses, the Gini coefficient gradually increases, reflecting a growing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock.

The trend persists, and by the final time step of 100, the Gini coefficient reaches about 0.073, signifying a substantial increase in inequality over the course of the simulation.

The statistical analysis confirms this increasing trend, with all rolling Mann-Kendall windows showing a significant increasing trend in the Gini coefficient. The change points at time steps 51, 71, and 100 further emphasize the periods where the rate of increase in inequality becomes more pronounced. The oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation suggest some variability in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains consistently upward.
