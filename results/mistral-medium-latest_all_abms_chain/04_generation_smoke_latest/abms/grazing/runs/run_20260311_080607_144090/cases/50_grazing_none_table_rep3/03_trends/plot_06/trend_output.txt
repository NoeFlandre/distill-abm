The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average number of livestock destocked across households over time. Initially, there is a sharp increase in destocking, peaking significantly at time step 3 with an average of 6.6144 livestock. This peak is followed by a rapid decline, reaching a local minimum at time step 8 with a value of 3.3333. Subsequent fluctuations are observed, with notable peaks at time steps 9, 12, and 17, where the values are 3.3733, 2.7406, and 1.4661 respectively. These peaks are interspersed with valleys at time steps 11, 16, and 18, with values of 2.4717, 1.4450, and 0.9028 respectively. After time step 30, the trend stabilizes, and the destocking values exhibit smaller oscillations around a lower mean. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates significant decreasing trends in the initial phases, particularly from time steps 0 to 32, followed by periods of no significant trend and a slight increasing trend towards the end of the simulation. The dominant oscillation period is 49 time steps, suggesting a cyclical pattern in the destocking behavior. Overall, the initial high variability in destocking rates gradually gives way to more stable and lower destocking numbers as the simulation progresses.
