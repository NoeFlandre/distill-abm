The simulation results indicate a clear and consistent downward trend in the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents over the observed time period. Initially, the mean consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units. This value experiences a significant and steady decline, reaching a low of approximately 253.10 units by the end of the simulation. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this decreasing pattern, with significant decreases observed in multiple windows, particularly in the early and middle phases of the simulation. Inflection points are noted at several time steps, indicating periods where the rate of decline changes. Notable inflection points occur around time steps 5, 13, 16, 19, 24, 25, 27, and 30, suggesting shifts in the rate of consumption decline. Peaks and valleys identified in the data suggest minor fluctuations around the overall downward trend, but these do not significantly alter the primary decreasing pattern. The oscillation analysis indicates periodic fluctuations, but the dominant period of oscillation does not overshadow the primary trend of decreasing consumption. The change point detected at time step 32 marks a significant shift in the consumption pattern, likely indicating a stabilization at a lower consumption level towards the end of the simulation period. Overall, the data suggests a strong and consistent reduction in whole milk consumption among agents throughout the simulation.
