The simulation of the agent-based model shows the trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over a series of steps. Initially, the mean quantity of milk consumed is very low, starting at approximately 5.29 units. This initial phase is followed by a rapid increase in consumption, reaching around 1265.47 units by step 11. This period of rapid growth indicates a significant shift in agents' behavior towards adopting skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. 

Following this peak, the consumption quantity exhibits minor fluctuations but generally stabilizes around a mean value of approximately 1220.21 units. The fluctuations include several peaks and valleys, such as a peak at step 13 with a value of 1240.35 units and a valley at step 12 with a value of 1233.62 units. These minor oscillations suggest periodic variations in consumption behavior, potentially due to the interplay of habit, social influence, and other model parameters. 

Towards the end of the simulation, there is another notable increase in consumption, reaching a maximum value of 1456.60 units at step 32. This final increase suggests a renewed or intensified adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk by the agents. The overall trend indicates an initial rapid adoption phase, followed by a stabilization period with minor fluctuations, and ending with a slight increase in consumption. 

The statistical analysis reveals several key inflection points and oscillation indices, highlighting the dynamic nature of the consumption behavior over the simulation steps. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the initial and final phases of the simulation, while the middle phase shows no significant trend, further supporting the observed stabilization period.
