The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption starts at a baseline value of 1.0000. It then exhibits a rapid increase within the first few time steps, reaching approximately 1.02 by the 5th time step. This initial surge indicates a quick adoption or reinforcement of habits related to whole milk consumption among the agents. Following this initial increase, the mean habit factor continues to rise steadily, showing a consistent upward trend throughout the simulation period. By the 32nd time step, the mean habit factor reaches its peak value of 1.0502. This gradual increase suggests a continuous strengthening of the habit factor over time, likely influenced by the agents' interactions and the reinforcement of their consumption habits. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across various windows, with significant p-values indicating the robustness of this upward trajectory. The absence of detected peaks and valleys further supports the steady and continuous nature of this increase. The inflection points identified at the 7th and 12th time steps suggest slight changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. The oscillation analysis reveals periodic fluctuations, but these do not disrupt the general upward trend. For decision makers, this trend implies that interventions aimed at altering milk consumption habits would need to be robust and sustained to counteract the reinforcing nature of these habits. The steady increase in the habit factor highlights the importance of early and continuous engagement strategies to influence consumer behavior effectively.
