The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several key trends in the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent over the observed time steps. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29. This low initial value suggests that at the beginning of the simulation, agents predominantly consume whole milk, reflecting strong initial habits and low initial perceptions of the health and environmental benefits of alternative milk types.  \n\nThe consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a rapid increase in the early time steps. This trend is evident from the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis, which shows significant increasing trends in the initial windows. For instance, from time steps 0 to 4 and 5 to 9, the consumption increases sharply with slopes of approximately 140.433 and 139.015 respectively. This rapid adoption phase indicates that agents are quickly becoming disposed to consider alternative milk types, likely influenced by the initial parameters such as `alt-health-mean-initial` and `alt-env-mean-initial`, which set the perceived health and environmental benefits of alternative milk types. \n\nFollowing this initial surge, the consumption levels enter a phase of stabilization. From time steps 10 to 24, the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates no significant trend, suggesting that the consumption levels have reached a plateau. During this period, the consumption values oscillate around a mean of approximately 1220.21, with minor fluctuations. This stabilization phase implies that the agents have largely integrated the consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk into their habits, and the influence of social interactions and norms has balanced out. \n\nTowards the end of the simulation, there is another notable increase in consumption. From time steps 25 to 29, the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis shows a significant increasing trend with a slope of 48.544. This late-stage increase could be attributed to the cumulative effects of social influence and the reinforcement of habits over time. The final consumption value reaches a peak of 1456.60, indicating a substantial shift towards the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. \n\nThe oscillation analysis reveals periodic fluctuations in consumption, with a dominant period of 15 time steps. These oscillations suggest that there are recurring patterns of influence and adjustment among the agents, possibly due to the interplay of social interactions, habit reinforcement, and cognitive dissonance. \n\nFor decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial perceptions and social influence in driving the adoption of healthier milk alternatives. The rapid initial increase underscores the potential impact of targeted campaigns to raise awareness of the health and environmental benefits of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The subsequent stabilization and final increase suggest that sustained efforts in reinforcing these perceptions and leveraging social norms can lead to long-term changes in consumption behavior.
