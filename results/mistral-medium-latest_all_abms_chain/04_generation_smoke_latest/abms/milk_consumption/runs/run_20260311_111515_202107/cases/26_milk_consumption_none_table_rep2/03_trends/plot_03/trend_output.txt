The simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend in the total number of times agents chose whole milk from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the value starts at zero, indicating no consumption of whole milk. Over time, the consumption steadily increases, reaching a peak of approximately 2194.57 at the end of the simulation period. The mean value of whole milk consumption across the time steps is around 1130.88, with a median value of 1158.97, indicating a relatively symmetric distribution of values around the mean. The standard deviation of 654.69 suggests some variability in the consumption patterns over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across multiple windows, with consistent positive slopes and significant p-values. This indicates a steady rise in whole milk consumption throughout the simulation period. The model identifies several inflection points and oscillation indices, suggesting periods where the rate of increase changes slightly, but the overall trend remains consistently upward. The absence of detected peaks and valleys further supports the steady increase in whole milk consumption without significant fluctuations.
