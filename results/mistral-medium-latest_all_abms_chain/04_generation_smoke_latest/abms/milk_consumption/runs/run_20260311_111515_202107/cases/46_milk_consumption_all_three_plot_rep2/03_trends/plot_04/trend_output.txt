The total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero and remains relatively low for the first few steps. This initial phase indicates a slow adoption rate, likely due to strong initial habits favoring whole milk and low initial perceptions of the health and environmental benefits of alternative milk types. Around the fifth step, there is a noticeable increase in the number of choices for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk. This uptick suggests that agents are beginning to consider and adopt alternative milk types, possibly due to the influence of social interactions and the gradual weakening of initial habits. From the tenth step onwards, the number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices increases steadily and almost linearly. This trend indicates a consistent and growing adoption of alternative milk types over time. The steady rise could be attributed to the cumulative effects of social influence, habit adjustment, and the perceived benefits of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk becoming more widely recognized and accepted. By the thirtieth step, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 25,000. This high value suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior, with a substantial portion of the population regularly choosing alternative milk types. The consistent upward trend with low variance across simulation runs implies a predictable and stable adoption pattern, which can be valuable for decision-makers in forecasting future consumption trends and planning marketing or health campaigns.
