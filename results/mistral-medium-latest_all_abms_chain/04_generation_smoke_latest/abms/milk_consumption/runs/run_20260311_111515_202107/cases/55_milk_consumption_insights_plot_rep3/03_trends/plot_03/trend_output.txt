The data from the repeated simulations of the agent-based model show a consistent upward trend in the total number of whole milk choices made by agents over the time steps. Initially, at time step 0, the total number of whole milk choices starts close to zero. As the time steps progress, the total number of whole milk choices increases steadily and linearly. By the time step of 30, the total number of whole milk choices reaches approximately 2000. This trend indicates a continuous and steady adoption of whole milk choices by the agents throughout the simulation period. For decision makers, this trend suggests that despite the introduction and potential influence of alternative milk types, whole milk continues to be chosen consistently over time. This could imply that factors such as habit, social influence, and initial perceptions significantly contribute to the sustained preference for whole milk among the agents.
