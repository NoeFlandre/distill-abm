The simulation of the agent-based model reveals several key trends in the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents over time. Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of 5.29 units. This is followed by a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation, indicating a swift adoption of alternative milk types. By the 11th time step, the consumption reaches a peak of approximately 1265.47 units. This period of rapid growth is statistically significant, as evidenced by the increasing trends in the rolling Mann-Kendall test during the initial windows. Following this peak, the consumption levels exhibit minor fluctuations around a high mean value, with several smaller peaks and valleys. Notable peaks occur at time steps 13, 19, and 21, with values around 1240.35, 1221.57, and 1226.86 units respectively. The valleys are observed at time steps 12, 18, 20, and 23, with values around 1233.62, 1183.39, 1214.46, and 1209.70 units respectively. These fluctuations suggest periods of stabilization and minor adjustments in consumption patterns. The latter part of the simulation shows a slight increasing trend again, culminating in a maximum value of 1456.60 units at the final time step. The overall mean consumption across the simulation is approximately 1053.21 units, with a median of 1220.21 units, indicating a general trend towards higher consumption levels of alternative milk types. The standard deviation of 401.07 units highlights the variability in consumption patterns over time. The oscillation analysis indicates periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of 15 time steps, suggesting cyclical patterns in the adoption behavior of the agents. The inflection points and change points further emphasize the dynamic nature of the consumption trends, reflecting the complex interplay of habits, social influence, and individual decision-making processes in the model.
