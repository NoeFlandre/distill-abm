The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the mean factor starts at a value of 1.0000. Throughout the simulation, the mean factor gradually increases, reaching a final value of 1.0502. The trend is consistently upward, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results, which show significant increasing trends across multiple windows with a slope of 0.001. The inflection points occur at indices 7 and 12, suggesting slight changes in the rate of increase around these points. The oscillation analysis indicates some periodic fluctuations, with a dominant period of 15 time steps. Despite these oscillations, the overall trend remains steadily increasing, reflecting a gradual strengthening of the habit factor influencing the choice of whole milk over the simulated period.
