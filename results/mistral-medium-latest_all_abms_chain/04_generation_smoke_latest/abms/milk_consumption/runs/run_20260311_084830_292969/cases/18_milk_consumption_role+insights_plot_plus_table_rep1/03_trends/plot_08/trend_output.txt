The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, the mean habit factor starts at a baseline value of 1.0000. In the early steps, there is a rapid increase, indicating a quick adjustment phase where agents start forming habits related to whole milk consumption. By around the 7th step, the rate of increase begins to stabilize, marking the first inflection point. This suggests that agents are settling into their habit patterns. The second inflection point occurs around the 12th step, after which the mean habit factor continues to rise but at a more gradual and steady pace. Throughout the simulation, the mean habit factor reaches a maximum value of approximately 1.0502 by the final step. The statistical analysis confirms a consistent increasing trend across all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, with significant positive slopes indicating steady growth in the habit factor. The absence of detected peaks or valleys further supports the steady and consistent nature of this trend. For decision-makers, this trend implies that habits related to whole milk consumption strengthen over time, which could have implications for marketing strategies and public health interventions aimed at influencing milk consumption behaviors. The consistent increase suggests that once habits are formed, they tend to reinforce themselves, making it potentially challenging to shift consumer behavior away from whole milk without significant interventions.
