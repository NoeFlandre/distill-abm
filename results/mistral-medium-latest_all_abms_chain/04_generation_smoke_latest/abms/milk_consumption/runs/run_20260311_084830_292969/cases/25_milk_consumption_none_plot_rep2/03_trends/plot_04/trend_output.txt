The data from the repeated simulations of the agent-based model show a clear and consistent trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices over time. Initially, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices is very low, starting close to zero. This indicates that at the beginning of the simulation period, which corresponds to the year 1974, the majority of agents are not choosing skimmed or semi-skimmed milk. As the simulation progresses, there is a gradual increase in the number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices. By around the 5th step, the total number of these choices begins to rise more noticeably, reaching approximately 2,500. This upward trend continues steadily, showing a significant increase in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. By the 10th step, the total number of choices has risen to around 7,500. The growth trend remains consistent and accelerates as the steps progress. By the 15th step, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches roughly 12,500. This upward trajectory continues, and by the 20th step, the total number of choices is approximately 17,500. As the simulation approaches the 30th step, which corresponds to the year 2005, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches its peak at around 25,000. Throughout the simulation, the trend demonstrates a steady and continuous increase in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk, reflecting a significant shift in consumer behavior over the simulated period.
