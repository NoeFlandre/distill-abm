The percentage of green patches in the landscape, representing the availability of fodder for prey animals, exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable for a short period. Around time step 150, there is a noticeable decline in the percentage of green patches, reaching a minimum of about 25% at time step 365. This decline indicates a significant reduction in the availability of grass, potentially due to grazing pressure from the prey animals or other environmental factors. Following this decline, the percentage of green patches begins to recover, gradually increasing and stabilizing around 33% again by time step 725. This recovery suggests a period of regrowth and restoration of grass patches. Throughout the remainder of the simulation, the percentage of green patches fluctuates slightly but generally remains stable around the 33% mark, indicating a dynamic equilibrium in the landscape's vegetation. The statistical analysis reveals several inflection points and change points, highlighting periods of significant shifts in the trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates both increasing and decreasing trends at different intervals, reflecting the dynamic nature of grass patch availability over time. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations in the percentage of green patches, further emphasizing the dynamic equilibrium in the landscape's vegetation.
