agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model adds learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . the simulations of the agent-based model show a variety of trends in the mean household risk attitude over 100 steps . within the first 20 steps, there is a noticeable divergence in the trends . some households exhibit an increasing risk attitude, rising sharply to values as high as 0.8 . while others show an decreasing trend, dropping to value as low as -0.2 . the metric representing the Gini coefficient of total healthy livestock starts at a value of 0 for all simulations, indicating an initial state of perfect equality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households . by 20 steps, the gini coefficient values are spread between approximately 0.02 and 0.06 . this suggests that by the end of the simulation period, there is a significant variation in the inequality of livestock distribution across different runs .
