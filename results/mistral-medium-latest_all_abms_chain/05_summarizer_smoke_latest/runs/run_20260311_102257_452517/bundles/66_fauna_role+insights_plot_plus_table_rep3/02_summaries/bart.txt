 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model allows users to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration .  The cumulative number of postpartum females killed in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid initial increase followed by a gradual stabilization . For decision makers, this trend implies that initial hunting efforts are highly effective, but sustained hunting pressure may lead to a stabilization in the number of males killed . Understanding these trends can inform strategies for sustainable hunting practices or conservation efforts to maintain prey populations .
