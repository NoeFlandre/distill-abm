The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration. Initially set at 20, this value does not change over time, indicating a stable population of foragers. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, with the mean, median, minimum, and maximum values all consistently at 20. There is no variance or standard deviation, reinforcing the lack of fluctuation in the number of foragers. The absence of peaks and valleys further supports this observation. The implications for decision-makers suggest that under the given parameters and conditions of the model, the number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains unaffected by the dynamics of the simulation, indicating a potential balance in the socio-natural system being modeled.
