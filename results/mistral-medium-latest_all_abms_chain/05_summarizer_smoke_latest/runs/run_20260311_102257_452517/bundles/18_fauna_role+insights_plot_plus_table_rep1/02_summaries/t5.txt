the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . the number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant at 20 throughout the entire duration of the simulation . the plateau phase indicates a potential equilibrium point where the hunting pressure balances with other factors such as prey availability and environmental conditions . the cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid initial increase followed by a gradual stabilization . the number of deaths starts at 0 and rises sharply within the first 500 steps, reaching approximately 200 deaths . following this rapid increase, the rate of deaths begins to slow down . this stabilization occurs roughly after 1000 steps and continues with minor fluctuations until the end of the simulation at 2312 steps . the population effectively reached extinction by the end of the simulation at 73000 time steps . by 500 steps, the population has dropped significantly . the rapid initial decline suggests early intervention might be crucial to prevent population collapse . by 2000 steps, the energy levels approach near zero, indicating the near-complete depletion of the prey population's energy . notable change points around steps 350, 500, 625, and 875 indicate shifts in the trend of energy decline .
