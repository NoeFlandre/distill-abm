 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . Ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values and processing costs for males and females .  The cumulative number of postpartum females killed starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 500 time steps . The rapid initial increase in hunting success underscores the need for careful monitoring and regulation during the early stages of resource exploitation . The insights from the simulation can inform policies aimed at maintaining ecological balance and preventing the extinction of vulnerable species due to overhunting .
