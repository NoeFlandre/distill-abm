After this steep rise, the rate of increase begins to slow down, and the cumulative number of females killed starts to plateau around 120 by 1000 steps. This plateau suggests that the hunting pressure stabilizes as the prey population potentially becomes more scarce or as hunters reach a saturation point in their hunting efficiency. Beyond 1000 steps, the cumulative number of females killed remains relatively stable, with minor fluctuations around the plateau value. This stability indicates a balance between the remaining prey population and the sustained hunting efforts of the foragers.
