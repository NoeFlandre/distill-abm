 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  Model allows users to manipulate parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment .  The total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at an initial value of 250 . In the early stages of the simulation, there is a rapid decline in the number of  animals . By around 500 steps, the number of animals has decreased significantly, with many simulations showing a reduction to approximately 50 animals . The decline continues more gradually, and by around 2000 steps,  the number in nearly all simulations approaches zero .
