The data from the repeated simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of female animals over time. Initially, the number of female animals starts at around 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By approximately 1000 steps, the number of female animals has decreased significantly to around 20. This downward trend continues more gradually, with the number of female animals approaching zero by around 2000 steps. Beyond this point, the number of female animals remains very low, close to zero, for the remainder of the simulation steps. This trend indicates a rapid initial decline in the female animal population followed by a more gradual reduction until near extinction.
