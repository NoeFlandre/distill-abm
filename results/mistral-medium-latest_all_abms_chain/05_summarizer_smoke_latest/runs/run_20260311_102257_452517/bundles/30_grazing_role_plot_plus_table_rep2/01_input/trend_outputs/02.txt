The average deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents in the simulation exhibits distinct trends over the 100 time steps. Initially, the deviation starts at a low value of approximately 0.0074. During the early stages, from time step 0 to around time step 20, there is a rapid increase in the deviation, reaching a peak value of about 0.3814 at time step 33. This period is characterized by a significant upward trend, indicating that households are increasingly deviating from the recommended livestock numbers. 

Following this peak, there is a noticeable decline in the deviation from time step 33 to around time step 50, where the deviation stabilizes. This decline suggests a correction phase where households adjust their livestock numbers closer to the recommended values. The deviation values during this phase fluctuate but generally trend downward, reaching a more stable state around a value of 0.1911 by the end of the simulation. 

The statistical analysis reveals several key inflection points and change points, notably around time steps 7, 12, 13, 39, 46, 51, 67, 70, 74, 75, 76, 81, 83, 85, 86, and 90, indicating periods of significant change in the trend of deviations. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these observations, showing significant increasing trends in the early phases and significant decreasing trends during the middle phases of the simulation. Towards the end of the simulation, the trend stabilizes with no significant changes detected. 

Overall, the simulation demonstrates an initial increase in deviation from recommended livestock numbers, followed by a correction phase where deviations decrease and stabilize, reflecting the adaptive behaviors and learning processes of the households in response to ecological and climatic conditions.
