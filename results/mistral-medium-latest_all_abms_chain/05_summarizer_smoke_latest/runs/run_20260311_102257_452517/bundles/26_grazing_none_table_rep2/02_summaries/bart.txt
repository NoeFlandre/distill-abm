 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  Simulation of agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average total number of livestock across all households over the observed time steps . The average amount of available green biomass across all patches in the simulation exhibits distinct trends over the 100 timesteps . The system shows signs of oscillation with a dominant period of 49 time steps, suggesting cyclical patterns in biomass levels .  The number of households engaging in social learning, as indicated by the variable household-SL being true, exhibits a clear increasing trend over the simulation period . The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households in the simulation demonstrates a consistent increasing trend . The Gini coefficient starts at a value of approximately 0, indicating a relatively equal distribution of healthy livestock among households .  The number of E-RO households reaches a minimum value of approximately 14.6667 at time step 92 . The analysis of these trends suggests a shift away from the E.RO strategy over time, potentially indicating the adoption of alternative strategies by the households in response to the model's ecological and climatic conditions .
