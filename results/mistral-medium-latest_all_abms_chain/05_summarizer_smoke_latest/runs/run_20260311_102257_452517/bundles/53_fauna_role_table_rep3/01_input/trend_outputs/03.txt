The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the count starts at zero and shows a rapid increase in the early time steps. This upward trend continues steadily, reflecting a consistent rise in the number of males hunted. The mean value of the cumulative count is approximately 100.39, with a median of 116.5, indicating that the central tendency of the data is skewed towards higher values as the simulation progresses. The standard deviation of 32.52 suggests some variability around the mean, but the overall trend remains clear. The maximum value reached is 120.17, indicating the peak cumulative count of male prey hunted. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals several windows of increasing trends, particularly notable in the early and middle phases of the simulation, with significant positive slopes. However, there are periods where no significant trend is detected, and towards the later stages, a decreasing trend is observed. This suggests that while the initial and middle phases are characterized by a steady increase in hunting success, the later phases see a stabilization or slight decline. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points further supports the dynamic nature of the hunting pressure over time. The oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation period indicate periodic fluctuations, suggesting some cyclical patterns in the hunting dynamics. Overall, the data reflects a complex interplay of increasing hunting pressure followed by periods of stabilization and slight decline, with underlying periodic oscillations.
