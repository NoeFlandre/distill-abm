 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  At the beginning of the simulation, the average number of livestock starts at 90.0000 . This decline indicates a significant reduction in livestock numbers early in the simulation . However, the overall trend indicates a resilient system that, after an initial decline, adapts and recovers leading to a higher biomass level than the starting point .  Initially, the destocking rate starts at 0 and rapidly increases, reaching a peak of approximately 6.61 livestock at time step 3 . Following this peak, there is a sharp decline to around 3.33 livestock by time step 8 . The data shows several inflection points, indicating changes in the rate of destocking, particularly around time steps 6, 21, 23, and 27 .
