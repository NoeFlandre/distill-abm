 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model allows users to manipulate parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment . The rapid increase followed by stabilization indicates that most of the hunting impact occurs early in the simulation . This insight could be crucial for understanding the dynamics of hunting pressure and environmental factors on male animal populations .  The total energy of male animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of approximately 12,000 units . By around 500 steps, the number of animals has significantly decreased, often falling below 100 . The decline in animal numbers can be attributed to various factors modeled in the MHPM, such as hunting pressure, internal and external mortality rates, and environmental conditions .  These trends suggest cyclical patterns in grass availability, which could influence the survival and movement of prey animals and the strategies of human hunters . Understanding these cycles may be crucial for managing the ecosystem and predicting the long-term sustainability of both prey and hunter populations .
