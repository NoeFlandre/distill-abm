The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the simulation. Initially, the number of foragers is set at 20 and this value does not change over the entire duration of the simulation, which spans 73000 ticks. This stability indicates that the conditions within the model, such as the availability of prey and the energy balance of the foragers, are sufficient to sustain the initial number of forager bands without any increase or decrease. For decision makers, this suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the ecosystem can support a steady population of hunter-gatherer bands without leading to an increase or decrease in their numbers.
