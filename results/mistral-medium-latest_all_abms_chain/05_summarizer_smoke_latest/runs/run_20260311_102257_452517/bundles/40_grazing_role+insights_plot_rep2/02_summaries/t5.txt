the agent-based model in question is an extension of the RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making . we have set it to 20. - **timesteps**: This parameter determines the number of random households . this parameter sets the standard deviation of rainfall . the model allows for the evaluation of various scenarios, such as the influence of demographic settings and herd size . the mean green biomass across all patches exhibits a consistent trend across the 30 simulations . over the first 20 steps, there is a slight decline, reaching a low point of around 44,000 units . by step 50, the mean red biomass has recovered to its initial levels and continues to rise . this upward trend persists until around step 80 . the subsequent recovery and stabilization indicate that the system reaches a new equilibrium . the majority of the simulations indicate that the number of households engaging in social learning stabilizes at different levels . several simulations show a sharp rise from 0 to around 42 households within this period . this suggests that early exposure and interactions significantly influence the adoption of social learning behaviors .
