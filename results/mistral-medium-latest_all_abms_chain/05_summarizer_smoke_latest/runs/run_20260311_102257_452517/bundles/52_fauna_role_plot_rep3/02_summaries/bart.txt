 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . Ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values and processing costs for males and females . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback .  The data from the repeated simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the number of male prey animals over time . Initially, the total number of animals starts at around 250, reflecting the set value for the initial number of prey . The trends indicate that the initial conditions and parameters set in the model lead to a significant early mortality event, followed by a stabilization phase .  Total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent trend across the simulations . Initially, the total energy of female animals is high, starting at approximately 12,000 units . The trend suggests a rapid depletion of energy resources among female animals early in the simulation, followed by a stabilization at minimal energy levels .
