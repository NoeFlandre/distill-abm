The simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend in the total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the total number of animals starts at around 250, reflecting the set value for the initial number of prey. In the early stages, up to approximately 500 steps, there is a steep decline in the number of animals. This rapid decrease suggests a high rate of mortality or predation pressure early in the simulation. By around 1000 steps, the number of animals has generally dropped to below 50, indicating a significant reduction in the prey population. Beyond this point, the decline continues but at a slower rate, with the number of animals gradually approaching zero. By around 2000 steps, most simulations show very few animals remaining, often close to or at zero. This trend suggests that the combination of hunting pressure, environmental factors, and internal dynamics of the prey population leads to a rapid initial decline followed by a more gradual extinction process. The simulations indicate that under the given parameters, the prey population tends to move towards extinction within the observed time steps.
