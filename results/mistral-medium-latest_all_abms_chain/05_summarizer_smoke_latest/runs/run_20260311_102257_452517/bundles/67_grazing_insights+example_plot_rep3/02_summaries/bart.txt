 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes . The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at 0 across all simulations, indicating an equal distribution of livestock among households at the beginning . Over time, the distribution of healthy livestock becomes increasingly unequal among households . For decision makers, this trend highlights the importance of early intervention and facilitation of information exchange to maximize the adoption of beneficial practices .  Trendy trends indicate that non-learning behaviors are less prevalent over time, which could imply that learning strategies become more dominant as simulation progresses . For decision makers, this suggests that interventions or conditions favoring learning behaviors could be beneficial in the long-term stability and adaptability of household strategies .
