 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  Significant decreasing trends are observed in the rolling Mann-Kendall windows from time steps 33 to 65, reflecting a period of adjustment and stabilization in the agents' decision-making processes . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial adaptive strategies and the eventual stabilization of livestock management practices .  The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households starts at 0 and increases steadily over the entire simulation period of 100 time steps . The number of households following the ERO strategy begins at 20 and exhibits a slight increase in the initial E-RO strategy . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of early intervention and support for social learning initiatives to accelerate adoption .  Trend analysis indicates significant decreasing trends in multiple time windows, particularly between time steps 11 to 65 . Trend suggests a consistent shift away from the E-RO strategy over the mid to long term . This insight could be crucial for understanding the long-term sustainability and resilience of pastoral systems .
