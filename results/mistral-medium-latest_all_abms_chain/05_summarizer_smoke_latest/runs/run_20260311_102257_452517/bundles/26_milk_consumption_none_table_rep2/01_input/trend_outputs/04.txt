The simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent and increasing trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk by the British public from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk was zero. Over time, this number steadily increased, reaching a peak of approximately 25,643.83 at the end of the simulation period. The trend is characterized by a continuous rise without any detected peaks or valleys, indicating a steady adoption process. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across multiple windows, with significant positive slopes ranging from 201.463 to 935.933, indicating a consistent upward trajectory in the adoption behavior. The inflection point at index 31 suggests a notable change in the rate of adoption around that time step. The oscillation analysis indicates some periodic fluctuations, but the dominant trend remains one of steady increase. This suggests that the factors influencing milk choice, such as perception, habits, and social influence, collectively contributed to a gradual shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the simulated period.
