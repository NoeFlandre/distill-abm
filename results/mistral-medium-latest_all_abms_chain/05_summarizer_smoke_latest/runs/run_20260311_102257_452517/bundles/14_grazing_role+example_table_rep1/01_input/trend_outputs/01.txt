The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents remains constant throughout the simulation period. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, showing a mean, median, standard deviation, and variance all consistent with a flat, unchanging trend. The identified peaks and valleys in the data all share the same value, further confirming the lack of fluctuation. This consistent behavior suggests that, under the given model parameters and conditions, the initial risk attitude of social learning agents is stable and unaffected by the dynamics of the simulation.
