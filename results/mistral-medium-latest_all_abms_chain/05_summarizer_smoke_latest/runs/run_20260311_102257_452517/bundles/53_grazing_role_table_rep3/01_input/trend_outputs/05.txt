The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households over 100 timesteps. Initially, the average number of livestock starts at 90.0000. A consistent decreasing trend is observed in the early stages of the simulation, with significant declines noted in the first three rolling Mann-Kendall windows. The most substantial drop occurs in the first window, where the slope of the decrease is -3.470, indicating a rapid reduction in livestock numbers. This trend continues but at a slower rate in the subsequent windows, with slopes of -0.963 and -0.368 respectively. By timestep 37, the average livestock number reaches its minimum value of 41.0089. Following this low point, the trend stabilizes briefly around timestep 33 to 43, showing no significant trend. However, from timestep 44 onwards, there is a notable shift to an increasing trend in livestock numbers. This upward trend is consistent and significant across multiple rolling Mann-Kendall windows, although the rate of increase diminishes over time. The slopes of these increasing trends start at 0.313 and gradually reduce to 0.002 by the final window. Key change points are identified at timesteps 20, 55, and 100, marking significant shifts in the trend dynamics. The simulation ends with an average livestock number of 47.5472, reflecting a partial recovery from the earlier decline but not reaching the initial levels. The overall mean and median values of 48.7946 and 47.3122 respectively, along with a standard deviation of 9.5663, indicate variability in livestock numbers throughout the simulation. The oscillation analysis suggests periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of 49 timesteps, contributing to the observed variability.
