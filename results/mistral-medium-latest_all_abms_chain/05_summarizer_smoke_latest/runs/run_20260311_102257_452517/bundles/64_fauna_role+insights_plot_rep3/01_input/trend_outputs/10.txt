The simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) show a consistent trend across all runs. Initially, the total number of animals starts at around 250, reflecting the set value for the initial number of prey. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By approximately 500 steps, the total number of animals has decreased significantly, often falling below 100. This decline continues more gradually, with the number of animals dropping to near zero by around 2000 steps. In most simulations, the population of animals is effectively extinct by this point, with very few or no animals remaining. 

For decision makers, these trends suggest that under the given parameters and conditions, the population of megaherbivores is highly susceptible to extinction within a relatively short time frame. This insight could be crucial for understanding the potential impacts of human hunting pressure and environmental factors on megaherbivore populations and for developing strategies to mitigate these effects.
