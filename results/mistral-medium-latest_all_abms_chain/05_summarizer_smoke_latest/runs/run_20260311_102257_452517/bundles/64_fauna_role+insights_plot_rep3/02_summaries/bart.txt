 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration of the simulation .  The number of postpartum females killed in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages of the simulation, followed by a gradual plateau . For decision makers, these trends highlight the importance of understanding the factors influencing birth rates and the need for strategies to manage and sustain prey populations .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent declining trend across all simulations . Initially, the total number of animals starts at around 250, reflecting the set value for the initial number of prey . This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline . The total energy of male animals in the model starts at a high value of approximately 12,000 units . This rapid decrease suggests a significant reduction in the male population's energy reserves .  The percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains stable for the first 500 steps . This initial stability suggests a consistent availability of grass for the prey animals, providing a stable food source . The rapid decline in total energy suggests that without intervention or changes in hunting practices and environmental management, prey populations could face severe depletion or extinction .
