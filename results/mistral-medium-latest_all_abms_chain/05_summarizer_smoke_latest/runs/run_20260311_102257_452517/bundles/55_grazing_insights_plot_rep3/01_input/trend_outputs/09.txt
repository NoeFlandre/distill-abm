The simulations of the agent-based model show a general trend of increasing mean destock total of households over time. During this period, the mean destock total of households increases rapidly, suggesting a significant rise in the number of livestock being destocked. After the 40-step mark, the rate of increase in destocking begins to slow down, and the curves start to flatten out, indicating a stabilization in the destocking activities. By the 100-step mark, the mean destock total of households ranges between approximately 60 and 160, showing variability in the final destocking totals across different simulations. The trends suggest that the initial phase of the simulation is characterized by a rapid response to conditions necessitating destocking, followed by a period of stabilization where the rate of destocking slows down. This implies that decision-makers might need to focus on strategies that can mitigate the initial rapid destocking phase and promote stability in livestock management over the long term.
