 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The extension introduces learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, which are central to adaptive co-management . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making .  The model is grounded in social theory and reflects empirical observations, providing a framework for studying the complex interactions in grazing systems . The model allows for the evaluation of various scenarios, such as the influence of demographic settings and herd size, the impact of different learning strategies, and the diffusion of learning behaviors among households .  Initially, the mean green biomass starts at approximately 45,000 units . In the early stages, there is a slight increase, reaching around 45,261 units by step 4 . This is followed by a notable decline, with the biomass dropping to its minimum value of approximately 42,057 units at step 27 . The overall mean biomass across all steps is around 51,529 units, with a median value of about 51,197 units .  The number of livestock destocked across all households shows a consistent increasing trend over the 100 time steps . For decision-makers, this implies that over time, the distribution of healthy livestock becomes increasingly unequal among households . Understanding these patterns can help in planning interventions and support systems to assist households in managing their livestock more effectively under varying conditions .  The stabilization in the latter part of the simulation indicates a possible equilibrium where the number of non-learning households remains relatively constant . For decision-makers, this trend implies that . interventions or policies promoting learning behaviors among households could significantly reduce the number . over time, leading to a more adaptive and potentially resilient grazing system .
