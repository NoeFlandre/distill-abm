The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households shows a clear trend over the simulated time steps. Initially, the number of livestock starts at 90.0000. There is a significant decreasing trend in the early stages, with the livestock count dropping sharply. This decline continues until around time step 37, where the lowest value of 41.0089 is observed. Following this point, the trend shifts, and the livestock numbers begin to stabilize and eventually show a gradual increasing trend. The inflection points at time steps 3 and 46 indicate notable changes in the trend direction. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis highlights several windows where the trends are statistically significant, showing initial decreases followed by periods of increase. The change points at time steps 20, 55, and 100 further emphasize these shifts in trend. The oscillation analysis suggests periodic fluctuations in the livestock numbers, with a dominant period of 49 time steps. For decision makers, these trends imply that initial management strategies may lead to a significant reduction in livestock numbers, but adaptive strategies can eventually stabilize and even increase livestock counts over time. Understanding these dynamics can help in planning long-term sustainable practices for pastoral systems.
