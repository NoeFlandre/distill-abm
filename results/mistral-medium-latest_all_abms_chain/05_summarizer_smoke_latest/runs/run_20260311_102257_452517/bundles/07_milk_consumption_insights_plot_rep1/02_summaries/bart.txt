 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over time . For decision makers, this trend indicates a continuous and steady preference for whole milk among the agents . This insight could be valuable for stakeholders in the dairy industry to understand consumer behavior and tailor their marketing and production strategies .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the time steps . This trend suggests that over time, the habit of consuming milk becomes increasingly influential in the decision-making process of the agents . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing timely and effective strategies to guide consumer choices towards desired outcomes .
