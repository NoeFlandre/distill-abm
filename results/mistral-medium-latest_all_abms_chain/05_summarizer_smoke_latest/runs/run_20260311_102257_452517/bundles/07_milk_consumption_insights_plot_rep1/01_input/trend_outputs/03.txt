The simulations of the agent-based model show a consistent upward trend in the total number of whole milk choices made by agents over time. Initially, the total number of whole milk choices starts at zero and increases steadily. By around the 5th step, the total number of whole milk choices reaches approximately 500. This upward trend continues linearly, with the total number of whole milk choices reaching around 1000 by the 10th step. By the 15th step, the total number of whole milk choices is roughly 1500, and it continues to rise, reaching about 2000 by the 20th step. This trend persists, and by the 30th step, the total number of whole milk choices approaches 3000. The linear increase suggests a steady and consistent adoption of whole milk choices by the agents throughout the simulation period. For decision makers, this trend indicates a continuous and steady preference for whole milk among the agents, which could imply a stable market for whole milk despite the introduction and promotion of healthier alternatives like skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. This insight could be valuable for stakeholders in the dairy industry to understand consumer behavior and tailor their marketing and production strategies accordingly.
