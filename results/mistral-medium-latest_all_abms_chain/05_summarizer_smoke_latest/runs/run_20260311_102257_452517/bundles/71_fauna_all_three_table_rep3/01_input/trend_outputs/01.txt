The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration. Starting at an initial value of 20, the number of bands does not fluctuate and remains at 20 until the end of the simulation. This stability indicates that the parameters governing the hunter-gatherer dynamics, such as immortality of hunters and their movement costs, are balanced in a way that neither promotes growth nor decline in the number of bands. For decision makers, this suggests that under the given conditions and parameters, the human hunting pressure on the megaherbivore population is consistent and unchanging, which could be a critical factor in assessing the impact of human activities on the extinction dynamics of the megaherbivores.
