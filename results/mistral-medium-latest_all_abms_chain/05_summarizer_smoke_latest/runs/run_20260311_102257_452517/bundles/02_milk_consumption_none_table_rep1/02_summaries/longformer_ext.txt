The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005. The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices, specifically the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data, with calibration performed against observed macro-level data. The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person. The model compares two variants: a threshold-based approach and a probability-based approach for how agents become disposed to consider their choices.
