 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  The average amount of reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps . For decision makers, these trends suggest an initial period of biomass reduction followed by a robust recovery and growth phase . Understanding these patterns can help in designing interventions that account for initial fluctuations and aim for long-term stability .  The average number of livestock destocked across all households in the simulation exhibits a general decreasing trend over time, with notable fluctuations in the early time steps . For decision makers, these trends suggest that initial conditions and early interventions may have substantial impacts on livestock management outcomes .  The number of households following the E-RO strategy exhibits a clear decreasing trend over the simulation period . The dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps suggests some cyclical patterns in the rate of change . For decision makers, this trend implies a gradual shift away from non-learning strategies among households .
