The number of households following the E-RO strategy, which represents non-learning agents, exhibits a clear decreasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, the number of such households starts at 20 and experiences a slight increase, peaking at around 21.1 households by the eighth time step. Following this peak, there is a consistent decline in the number of households using the E-RO strategy. Significant decreasing trends are observed in multiple time windows, particularly between time steps 11 to 65, where the decline is statistically significant. After time step 66, the trend stabilizes, showing no significant change and leveling off around 14.7 households by the end of the simulation period. Key inflection points and change points indicate shifts in the rate of decline, notably around time steps 20 and 45, suggesting periods where the rate of households switching from the E-RO strategy accelerates or decelerates. The dominant oscillation period of 49 time steps suggests some cyclical patterns in the rate of change, although the overall trend remains a steady decline. For decision makers, this trend implies a gradual shift away from non-learning strategies among households, which could indicate an increasing adoption of learning behaviors over time within the simulated pastoral system.
