The average number of livestock destocked across all households in the simulation exhibits a general decreasing trend over time, with notable fluctuations in the early time steps. Initially, the destocking rate peaks sharply at time step 3, reaching a maximum value of approximately 6.61 livestock. This peak is followed by a rapid decline, with subsequent smaller peaks and valleys indicating significant variability in the early stages of the simulation. By time step 21, the destocking rate stabilizes somewhat, although it continues to exhibit a decreasing trend with occasional minor increases. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms significant decreasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation, with periods of no significant trend interspersed. The oscillation analysis suggests a dominant periodicity of around 49 time steps, indicating cyclical patterns in the destocking rates. The presence of multiple inflection points further highlights the dynamic nature of the destocking process. For decision makers, these trends suggest that initial conditions and early interventions may have substantial impacts on livestock management outcomes. The early peaks in destocking rates could imply initial adaptive challenges, while the subsequent stabilization may reflect learning and adaptation by households over time.
