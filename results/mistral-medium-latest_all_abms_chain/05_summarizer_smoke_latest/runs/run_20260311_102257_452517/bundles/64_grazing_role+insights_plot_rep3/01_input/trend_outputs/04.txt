The mean green biomass across all patches exhibits a consistent trend across the 30 simulation runs. Initially, the mean green biomass starts at approximately 45,000 units. Over the first 20 steps, there is a slight decline in biomass, reaching a low point of around 44,000 units. Following this initial decline, the biomass begins to increase steadily. By step 40, the mean green biomass has recovered to its initial levels and continues to rise. This upward trend accelerates, and by step 60, the biomass reaches approximately 55,000 units. The growth continues more gradually thereafter, stabilizing around 62,000 units by step 100. This trend suggests an initial period of adjustment followed by a significant and sustained increase in biomass, indicating a potential recovery and growth phase in the vegetation sub-model. For decision makers, this implies that initial grazing pressures may reduce biomass, but given time, the system can recover and even exceed initial biomass levels, suggesting resilience in the grazing system under the given parameters.
