 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment .
