The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits relatively stable dynamics throughout the simulation. Initially, the proportion of vegetated patches starts at approximately 33.0002% and ends very close to this value at 32.9979%, indicating minimal overall change. The mean percentage of vegetated patches is 32.6558%, with a median of 32.9696%, suggesting that the data distribution is fairly consistent and centered around this mean value. The standard deviation of 0.9503 indicates low variability in the data. The minimum value observed is 24.9108% at tick 365, which is a notable dip in vegetation coverage. The statistical analysis reveals several inflection points and change points, indicating periods where the trend of vegetation coverage shifts slightly. The rolling Mann-Kendall test shows significant trends in different windows, with initial increasing trends followed by decreasing trends in later windows. This suggests that while there are minor fluctuations, the overall vegetation coverage remains relatively stable with slight periodic oscillations. The dominant oscillation period is 63 ticks, indicating a regular pattern of minor increases and decreases in vegetation coverage. For decision makers, this stability in vegetation coverage implies that the grassland environment in the model is resilient to the simulated conditions, maintaining a consistent level of fodder availability for the prey animals despite minor fluctuations.
