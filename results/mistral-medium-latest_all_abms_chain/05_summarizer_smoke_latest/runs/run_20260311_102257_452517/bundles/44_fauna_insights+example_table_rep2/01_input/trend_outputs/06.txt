The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model begins at zero and exhibits a series of fluctuations throughout the simulation period. Initially, there is a gradual increase in the number of births, reaching a peak of approximately 5.6 births at around the 1,673rd tick. This peak is followed by a series of smaller peaks and troughs, indicating periodic fluctuations in the birth rate. Notable peaks occur at around the 1,392nd tick with 4.3 births, the 1,214th tick with 3.45 births, and the 1,073rd tick with 2.875 births. These peaks suggest intervals of increased reproductive activity among the prey population. The valleys, or lower points, in the birth rate are observed at around the 1,422nd tick with 3.8889 births, the 1,281st tick with 3.0588 births, and the 1,097th tick with 2.871 births. These fluctuations indicate periods of lower reproductive activity. The simulation shows significant increasing trends in birth rates during the early and middle phases, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis, with notable increasing trends between the 696th to 1,623rd ticks. However, there is a shift to a decreasing trend starting from the 1,624th tick onwards, indicating a decline in the birth rate towards the latter part of the simulation. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points suggests that the birth rate undergoes several shifts in its trend, reflecting the dynamic interactions between the prey population, environmental conditions, and hunting pressures. The dominant oscillation period of 50 ticks indicates a regular pattern of fluctuations in the birth rate. For decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of considering the dynamic and periodic nature of prey reproduction when evaluating the impact of hunting pressures and environmental conditions on the prey population. The initial increase followed by periodic fluctuations and eventual decline in birth rates can inform strategies aimed at managing prey populations and understanding the factors influencing their reproductive dynamics.
