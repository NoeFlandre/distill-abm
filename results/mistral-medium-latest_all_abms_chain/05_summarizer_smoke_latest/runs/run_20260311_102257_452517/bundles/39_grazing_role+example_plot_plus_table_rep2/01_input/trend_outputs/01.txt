The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant at approximately 0.0074 throughout the entire simulation period of 100 time steps. This indicates that there is no change in the initial disposition of households to deviate from the recommended number of livestock over time. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, showing no significant trends or variations in the data. The standard deviation and variance are both zero, reinforcing the lack of variability in the risk attitude. The peaks and valleys identified in the data all share the same value, further emphasizing the consistent nature of this metric across all time steps. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results indicate no significant trends in any of the analyzed windows, supporting the observation of a steady and unchanging risk attitude throughout the simulation.
