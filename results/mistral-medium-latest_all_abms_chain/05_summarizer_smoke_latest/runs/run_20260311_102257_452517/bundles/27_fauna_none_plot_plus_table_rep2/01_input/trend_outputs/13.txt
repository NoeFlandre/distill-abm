The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy is at its highest value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has significantly decreased to a much lower level, indicating a sharp drop in the energy available among the prey animals. 

Throughout the simulation, the energy continues to decline, albeit at a slower rate compared to the initial drop. There are minor fluctuations and small peaks observed around time steps 385, 388, 402, 408, 433, 440, 730, and 709, but these do not represent significant deviations from the overall downward trend. The peaks and valleys identified in the data are relatively close in value, suggesting minor oscillations rather than substantial changes in the trend. 

The inflection points and change points further highlight the phases of the energy decline. Notable change points occur around time steps 350, 500, 625, 875, and 2310, marking significant shifts in the rate of energy decrease. The rolling Mann-Kendall test results confirm the decreasing trend across various windows, with significant negative slopes indicating a consistent reduction in total energy over time. 

By the end of the simulation, the total energy of all animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy among the prey population. The statistical analysis supports the observation of a continuous decline, with the mean energy value being 2368.7661 and the median at 146.3750, reflecting the skewed distribution towards lower energy levels as the simulation progresses.
