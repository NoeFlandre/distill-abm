This sharp rise indicates a phase where forager bands become highly effective at hunting male prey, possibly due to increased encounters or improved hunting strategies. This plateau suggests that the system has reached a state of equilibrium, where the number of males killed stabilizes due to either a reduction in the male prey population or a balance between hunting success and prey availability. The variability among simulation runs increases slightly after the initial 1000 time steps but remains relatively consistent as the system approaches equilibrium.
