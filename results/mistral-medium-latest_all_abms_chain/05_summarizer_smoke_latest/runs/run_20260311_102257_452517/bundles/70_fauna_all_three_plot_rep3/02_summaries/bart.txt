 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges .  The cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 500 time steps, reaching approximately 200 deaths . This rapid increase suggests that postpartum females are highly vulnerable to hunting pressure immediately after giving birth . Understanding these dynamics can inform conservation efforts aimed at protecting vulnerable populations during their most susceptible life stages .  The total number of animals in the simulation begins at 250, reflecting the initial population set by the parameters . Over the first 500 time steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of female animals, dropping to around 40 individuals . The number of male animals continues to decrease at a slower rate, reaching near-zero levels around the 2000 time step mark . The simulation ends with a complete collapse of the prey population under the given conditions .  The total energy of all animals begins at approximately 25,000 units . Over the first 500 time steps, there is a rapid decline in total energy, dropping to around 5,000 . This sharp decrease suggests a significant reduction in the energy reserves of the prey population . After this decline, the percentage of green patches recovers to around 33% by the 400th time step . This recovery indicates a period of regrowth or reduced grazing pressure, allowing the grassland to replenish .
