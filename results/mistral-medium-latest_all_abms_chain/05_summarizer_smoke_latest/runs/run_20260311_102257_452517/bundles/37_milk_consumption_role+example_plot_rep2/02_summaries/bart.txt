 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at approximately 2,600 units . This high initial value indicates a strong preference or habit for whole milk consumption at the beginning of the simulation . Over the first 10 time steps, there is a rapid decline in consumption, dropping to around 1,000 units . The decline continues but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 800 units .  Initially, the number of choices made is low, reflecting the initial stages of the simulation where agents are beginning to make decisions regarding their milk consumption . By step 10, approximately 10,000 choices have been made, indicating a gradual increase in agent activity and interaction . The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,500 units .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a value close to zero, indicating that initially, there is minimal difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents . Initially, the deviation increases rapidly, reaching a value of approximately 0.55 . The rapid rise indicates a growing divergence in the choice function score among agents, suggesting that agents are beginning to differentiate their preferences more distinctly .
