The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of approximately 16,666.67 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy has significantly dropped, marking a notable change point in the trend. The decline continues steadily, with occasional minor fluctuations and small peaks, but the overall trajectory remains downward. By the time step 730, the total energy has reduced to about 1,083.15 units, indicating another significant change point. The trend persists with a gradual decline, and by around 2,310 time steps, the total energy approaches zero, marking the final change point where the energy levels stabilize at minimal values. Throughout the simulation, the energy levels exhibit minor oscillations with a dominant period of approximately 63 time steps, but these do not significantly alter the overall decreasing trend. The statistical analysis confirms this consistent decline, with significant decreasing trends observed in multiple rolling Mann-Kendall windows, reinforcing the overall pattern of energy depletion among the animals over the course of the simulation.
