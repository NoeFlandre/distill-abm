The cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households begins at zero and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the rate of increase is relatively rapid, with a steep slope observed in the early time steps. This initial phase suggests a prompt response to the conditions set by the model parameters, likely influenced by the high initial number of livestock and the absence of pasture resting. As the simulation progresses, the rate of destocking gradually slows down, indicating a deceleration in the rate at which livestock are being removed from the system. This deceleration is evident through multiple inflection points, particularly around time steps 45 to 75, where the slope of the increase becomes less steep. Despite this deceleration, the trend remains consistently upward, reflecting a continuous but slowing process of destocking over time. The simulation concludes with a cumulative destocking value nearing 97 livestock, indicating that almost all initial livestock have been destocked by the end of the simulation period. The consistent upward trend, supported by significant increasing trends in all rolling Mann-Kendall windows, underscores a steady and persistent destocking process influenced by the model's parameters and household behaviors.
