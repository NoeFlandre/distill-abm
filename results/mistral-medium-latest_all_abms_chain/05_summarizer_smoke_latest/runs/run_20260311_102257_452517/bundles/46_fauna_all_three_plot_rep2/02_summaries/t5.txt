the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . the number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the simulation, which spans 73000 time steps . the rapid initial decline indicates a high initial hunting pressure on postpartum females, likely due to their vulnerability and the high food value they represent to hunters . around the 500 to 1000 time steps mark, the rate of increase slows down, and the cumulative number stabilizes around 120 . the variance among different simulation runs is relatively low initially but increases slightly as time progresses, indicating some variability in hunting success and prey behavior . over the first 500 time steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of female animals, dropping to around 20 individuals . this sharp decrease suggests a significant impact on the female population, potentially due to a combination of hunting pressure, environmental changes, and internal mortality factors . the rapid initial decline highlights the vulnerability of the prey population to predation and environmental factors . the subsequent slower decline indicates that while the population may not recover fully, interventions could potentially slow down the rate of decline and prevent complete depletion .
