The analysis of the simulation results reveals a consistent upward trend in the Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock over the 100 steps of the simulation. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at approximately 0.0, indicating a relatively equal distribution of healthy livestock among households. As the simulation progresses, the Gini coefficient gradually increases, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 by the end of the simulation. This trend suggests a growing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households over time. 

Key inflection points are observed at steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, indicating notable changes in the rate of increase in inequality. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across all windows, with significant p-values, reinforcing the robustness of this upward trend. Change points detected at steps 51, 71, and 100 further highlight periods where the rate of change in inequality becomes more pronounced. 

The oscillation analysis indicates a dominant period of 49 steps, with notable oscillations occurring between steps 78 and 93. This suggests periodic fluctuations in the rate of increase in inequality, although the overall trend remains upward. 

For decision-makers, these trends imply that over time, the distribution of healthy livestock becomes increasingly unequal among households. This growing inequality could have significant implications for the sustainability and equity of the grazing system. Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions aimed at promoting more equitable distribution of resources and improving the overall resilience of the system.
