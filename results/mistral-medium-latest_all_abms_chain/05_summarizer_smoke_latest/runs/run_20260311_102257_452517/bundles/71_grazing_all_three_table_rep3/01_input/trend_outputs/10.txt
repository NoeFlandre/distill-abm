The number of households following the E-RO strategy starts at 20 and exhibits a slight increase in the early time steps, peaking at 21.1 at time step 8. This initial rise could be attributed to the initial conditions and the immediate reactions of households to the environmental and social settings. Following this peak, there is a general declining trend in the number of E-RO households. The decline is relatively steady with minor fluctuations, reaching a low of approximately 14.7 by the end of the simulation at time step 100. The most notable change points occur around time steps 20 and 45, indicating shifts in the trend dynamics. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant decreasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation, with the trend becoming non-significant in the later phases. This suggests that while there is a clear initial trend of households moving away from the E-RO strategy, the rate of this change slows down and stabilizes over time. The oscillation analysis reveals periodic fluctuations, particularly around the middle time steps, indicating some cyclical behavior in household strategy adoption. For decision makers, these trends suggest that the E-RO strategy may lose prevalence over time in favor of other strategies, with significant shifts occurring early in the simulation period. Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions or policies that consider the evolving preferences and behaviors of households in grazing systems.
