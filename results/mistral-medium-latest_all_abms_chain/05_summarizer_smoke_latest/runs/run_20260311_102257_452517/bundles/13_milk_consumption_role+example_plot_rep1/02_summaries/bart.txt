 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  Average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent begins at approximately 2,600 units . This high initial value indicates a strong initial habit and preference for whole milk among the agents . Over the first 10 steps, there is a rapid decline in consumption, dropping to around 1,000 units . Between steps 10 and 20, the rate of decline slows, with consumption stabilizing around 800 units . From steps 20 to 30, the consumption continues to decrease gradually, reaching approximately 500 units .  The total number of choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 30 steps of the simulation . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,600 units . This high initial value indicates a strong preference or habit for whole milk consumption .  The overall trend shows a continuous, albeit slow, increase in the habit factor, reflecting the strengthening of habitual behavior towards whole milk consumption over the entire simulation period . The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0, indicating that habits have not yet significantly influenced the agents' choices . The trend continues to rise, and by the 30th step, the average habit factor approaches 1.05 by the end of the simulation .
