 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent declining trend across all simulations . For decision-makers, understanding these trends can provide insights into the dynamics of prey population growth and the factors influencing reproductive success . This information can be crucial for managing wildlife populations and ensuring their sustainability .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time . Trend suggests that the population of prey animals is being significantly reduced over time . For decision makers, this implies that under the given parameters, the prey population is not sustainable and tends towards extinction .  The percentage of green patches quickly recovers to around 32% by step 700, indicating a period of grass regrowth or reduced grazing pressure . The fluctuations suggest cyclical patterns of grass consumption and regrowth, which could be influenced by seasonal variations or dynamic interactions between the prey animals and their environment .
