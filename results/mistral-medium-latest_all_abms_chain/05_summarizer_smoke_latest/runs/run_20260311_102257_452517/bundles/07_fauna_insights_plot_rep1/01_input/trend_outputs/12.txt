The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of approximately 12,000 units. This initial high energy level indicates that the female prey population begins with a substantial amount of energy, likely due to the initial conditions set in the model. 

In the early stages, up to around 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the total energy of female animals. This sharp decrease suggests a high rate of energy consumption or loss among the female prey, which could be attributed to factors such as high movement costs, hunting pressure, or limited food availability. The steep drop indicates that the female animals are expending energy faster than they can replenish it during this period. 

Following this initial rapid decline, the rate of energy loss slows down significantly. Between 500 and 1500 steps, the total energy of female animals continues to decrease but at a much slower rate. This phase suggests that the remaining female animals are either more efficient at conserving energy, have found more sustainable food sources, or are experiencing reduced hunting pressure. The slower decline indicates a potential stabilization in the energy dynamics of the female prey population. 

Beyond 1500 steps, the total energy of female animals approaches zero and remains there. This indicates that the female prey population has either been completely depleted or has reached a state where their energy levels are negligible. The stabilization at zero suggests that the population has collapsed, and there are no significant energy reserves left among the female animals. 

For decision-makers, these trends highlight the critical importance of initial conditions and early interventions in managing prey populations. The rapid initial decline underscores the need for strategies to mitigate early energy loss, such as reducing hunting pressure or enhancing food availability. The subsequent slower decline and eventual stabilization at zero energy levels suggest that long-term sustainability measures are crucial to prevent complete population collapse.
