The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit shows a consistent increasing trend over the simulation period from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the mean factor starts at 1.0000 and gradually increases to 1.0502 by the end of the simulation. The trend is characterized by a steady rise, with no significant peaks or valleys detected throughout the time series. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across multiple windows, indicating a consistent and significant upward movement in the habit factor. The inflection points and oscillation indices suggest minor fluctuations within the overall increasing trend. For decision makers, this implies that the influence of habit on the choice of whole milk has been gradually strengthening over the years. This trend suggests that interventions aiming to change milk consumption patterns may need to account for the increasing role of habit in consumer choices.
