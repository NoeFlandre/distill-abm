The mean factor applied to the skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choice function due to habit shows a general increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, the habit factor starts at a value of 1.0000 and remains relatively stable with minor fluctuations in the early stages. Around the fifth time step, the factor begins to increase more noticeably. This increasing trend becomes statistically significant from the fifth time step onwards, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results. The habit factor continues to rise steadily, reaching a peak value of 1.5879 at the final time step. Key inflection points are observed at the third and twelfth time steps, suggesting changes in the rate of increase. The data also exhibits some oscillatory behavior with dominant periods around 15 time steps, indicating periodic fluctuations superimposed on the overall increasing trend. For decision makers, this trend suggests that the influence of habit on the choice of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk becomes stronger over time, which could imply that interventions aimed at promoting healthier milk choices might be more effective if introduced early and reinforced over time to leverage the growing influence of habit.
