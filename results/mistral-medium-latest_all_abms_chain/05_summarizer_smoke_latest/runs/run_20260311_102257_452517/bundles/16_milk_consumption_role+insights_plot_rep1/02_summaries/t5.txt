agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at 2600 units . this rapid decline suggests a shift in consumer behavior away from whole milk early in the simulation period . around step 28, there is a slight uptick in consumption, followed by another decline . the total number of choices reached approximately 12,500 by step 20 . this indicates a significant shift in consumer behavior towards skimmed/semi-skimmed milk over the simulated period . the steady increase also implies that once the adoption process begins, it can sustain itself and grow . habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the simulation period . by the fifteenth step, the habit factor reaches approximately 1.2, and it continues to rise steadily . this trend suggests that interventions aimed at promoting healthier milk choices should consider the growing influence of habits over time . deviation stabilizes and plateaus around the value of 13, maintaining this level consistently through to the end of the simulation at 30 steps . this stabilization suggests that after the initial period of adjustment, the agents' choices align more closely with their environmental values .
