 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent in the simulation shows a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time . This trend reflects a significant shift in consumption patterns, likely influenced by the model parameters governing agent behavior and interactions . The simulation data on the total number of times agents chose whole milk shows a consistent increasing trend over the observed time steps .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent and steady increase in the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined over the observed time steps . The mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents in the simulation exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time . The data reflects a robust and sustained shift towards the adoption of alternative milk types .  The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit in the agent-based model shows a consistent and gradual increasing trend over the simulation period . This overall upward trend, punctuated by periods of stabilization and minor fluctuations, reflects the complex dynamics of milk consumption behavior influenced by various social and psychological factors .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a rapid increase in the early time steps . Overall, the mean deviation demonstrates a steady increase with periodic fluctuations, reflecting the evolving dynamics of agents' health perceptions and milk choices over time .
