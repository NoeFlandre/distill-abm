The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero. In the early stages, up to approximately 500 steps, there is a rapid increase in the cumulative number of females killed, rising steeply from zero to around 100. This initial phase indicates a high rate of hunting success or a high encounter rate between hunters and female prey. Following this rapid increase, the rate of accumulation begins to slow down significantly. Between 500 and 1000 steps, the cumulative number of females killed continues to rise but at a much slower pace, reaching approximately 120. Beyond 1000 steps, the trend stabilizes, and the cumulative number of females killed plateaus around 120, indicating that the rate of hunting success or encounter rate has significantly decreased or that the population of female prey has been substantially reduced. This stabilization continues for the remainder of the simulation, suggesting a potential equilibrium or a significant reduction in the available female prey population.
