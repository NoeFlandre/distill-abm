the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . the maximum value observed is approximately 120 males killed, which is maintained from around 1000 steps onwards . the subsequent plateau indicates that either the hunting pressure has decreased, or the male population has adapted or been reduced to a level where further hunting does not significantly increase the cumulative count . this could imply that the initial hunting pressure was unsustainable and that the system has reached a new equilibrium . the rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals significant increasing trends in birth rates during the initial and middle phases of the simulation, followed by decreasing trends towards the later phases . change points are identified at approximately 725, 1,325, 1,850, 2,050, and 2,310 steps, marking shifts in the birth rate dynamics . these trends imply that the prey population experiences phases of growth and decline . the total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a consistent and significant decreasing trend over time . Initially, the number starts at approximately 166.67 and steadily declines to zero by the end of the simulation period . this rapid decrease continues, albeit at a slower rate, as the simulation progresses . the dominant oscillation period of 63 steps suggests periodic fluctuations in the energy levels, but these are minor compared to the overall downward trend . the percentage of green patches, representing vegetated areas in the landscape, exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period .
