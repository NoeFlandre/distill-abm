The simulation of the grazing system model shows distinct trends in the average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households over 100 time steps. Initially, the mean livestock count starts at 90 and exhibits a sharp decline in the early stages, reaching a minimum value of approximately 41 at time step 37. This significant decrease indicates a rapid reduction in livestock numbers, possibly due to initial adaptive behaviors or environmental pressures. Following this decline, the livestock numbers stabilize and begin to gradually increase from around time step 44 onwards. The trend analysis reveals a consistent decreasing trend in the initial phases, followed by a stabilization period and subsequent gradual increase in livestock numbers. Key inflection points are observed at time steps 3 and 46, marking transitions in the trend dynamics. The oscillation analysis suggests periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of 49 time steps, indicating cyclical patterns in livestock numbers. For decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial adaptive strategies and the potential for recovery and stabilization of livestock numbers over time. The insights suggest that while initial declines may be steep, long-term strategies can lead to stabilization and gradual improvement in livestock numbers.
