The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant at approximately 0.0074 throughout the entire simulation period of 100 steps. This indicates that there is no variation or trend in the initial risk attitude of households over time. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, showing a mean, median, standard deviation, and variance all consistent with a constant value. The rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows also indicates no significant trends, reinforcing the lack of change in household initial risk attitude. The implications for decision-makers are that the initial risk attitude of households does not fluctuate, suggesting a stable baseline disposition towards risk among social learning agents in this grazing system model. This stability can be crucial for predicting household behavior and planning interventions, as it indicates that initial risk attitudes are not influenced by temporal factors within the scope of this simulation.
