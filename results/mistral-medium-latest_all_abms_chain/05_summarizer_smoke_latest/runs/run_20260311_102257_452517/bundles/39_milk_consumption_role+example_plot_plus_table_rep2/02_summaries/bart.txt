 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at approximately 2655 units . The consumption then declines rapidly over the first 10 steps, dropping to around 1000 units . This sharp decrease suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior, likely influenced by changing perceptions, social interactions, or other model parameters .  Total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps, reaching a final value of 32,000 . Means and median values of the total choices made are both 16,000, reflecting a symmetric distribution of choices over time . The rolling Mann-Kendall test results indicate a consistent increasing trend across various windows of the simulation .  The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation . By the 30th time step, the mean quantity consumed reaches approximately 1456.60 units . The trend reflects the ongoing influence of various parameters such as social influence, habits, and perceptions on the agents' choices .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at 0.0000 and increases rapidly in the initial steps, reaching approximately 13.4756 by step 13 . This rapid increase indicates that agents quickly start to perceive differences between their health expectations and the actual health outcomes of their milk choices . From step 6 to step 16, the rate of increase in the mean value health deviation slows down, stabilizing around 0.5 . This period of stabilization suggests that agents are adjusting their perceptions and habits .
