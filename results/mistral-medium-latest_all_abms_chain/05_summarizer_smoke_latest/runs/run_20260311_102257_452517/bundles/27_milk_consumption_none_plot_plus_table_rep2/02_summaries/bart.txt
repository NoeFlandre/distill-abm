 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . Model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The mean value of whole milk choices across all steps is around 1130.88, with a median of 1158.97, indicating a relatively symmetric distribution around the central tendency . The rolling Mann-Kendall test results indicate a consistent increasing trend across all analyzed windows, with significant positive slopes ranging from approximately 64.07 to 76.20 .  The mean habit factor for whole milk consumption starts at a baseline value of 1.0000 . Initially, there is a rapid increase in the habit factor within the first few steps, indicating a quick adjustment period . The consistent increase in habit factor implies a growing influence of habit on the choice of whole milk over time .  The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates an initial increasing trend in the early steps, followed by periods with no significant trend, suggesting that after the initial adjustment phase, the system reaches a steady state with minor fluctuations . The peaks and valleys identified in the data show minor fluctuations, with the highest peak at step 13 .
