agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model adds learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . in the initial 20 steps, there is a rapid divergence in risk attitudes . the higher risk attitudes remain around 0.7 to 0.8, while the lower risk attitudes stabilize around 0.2 to 0.3 . this stabilization suggests that households have adapted to their perceived environment . after 40 time steps, the mean destock of households fluctuates slightly but generally remains stable around 1 livestock per household for the remainder of the simulation . this stability suggests that households have reached a new equilibrium where the rate of destocking is minimal and consistent . the simulations that started with higher counts of non-learning households tend to maintain higher values, while those that started lower remain on the lower end . this stability suggests that the initial conditions and early dynamics significantly influence the long-term behavior of the system regarding non-Learning households .
