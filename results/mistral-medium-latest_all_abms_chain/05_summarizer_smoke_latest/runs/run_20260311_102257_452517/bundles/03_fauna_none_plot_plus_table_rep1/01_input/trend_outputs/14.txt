The percentage of green patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33.0002%. This value remains relatively stable for a short period before experiencing a sharp decline around the 365th tick, reaching a minimum of about 24.9108%. Following this decline, there is a rapid recovery, with the percentage of green patches increasing back to around 33%. After this recovery, the percentage of green patches stabilizes and remains relatively constant for the remainder of the simulation, fluctuating slightly around the mean value of 32.6558%. The data shows several inflection points and change points, indicating periods of significant shifts in the trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals various windows of increasing and decreasing trends, with significant changes noted particularly in the early and middle phases of the simulation. The oscillation analysis suggests a dominant period of 63 ticks, indicating a cyclical pattern in the percentage of green patches. Overall, the model demonstrates an initial stability, followed by a sharp decline and recovery, and finally a prolonged period of relative stability in the percentage of green patches.
