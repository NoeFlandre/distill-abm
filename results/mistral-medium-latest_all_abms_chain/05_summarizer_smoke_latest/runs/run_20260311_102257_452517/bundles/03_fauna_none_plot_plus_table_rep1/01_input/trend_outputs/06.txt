The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct phases over the simulation period. Initially, the number of births remains at zero, indicating no births in the early stages. Around the 725th tick, there is a noticeable change point where the number of births begins to increase gradually. This upward trend continues, with significant increases observed around the 1325th tick, where the number of births reaches higher values. The most substantial peak in births occurs around the 1673rd tick, with a maximum value of 5.6 births. Following this peak, there is a decreasing trend starting around the 1850th tick, indicating a reduction in the number of births. This downward trend continues until around the 2050th tick, after which the number of births stabilizes. Throughout the simulation, there are several inflection points and oscillations, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the birth rate. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis confirms these trends, with significant increasing trends identified in the early and middle phases and significant decreasing trends in the later phases of the simulation.
