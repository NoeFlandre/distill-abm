 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The mean weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent begins at a low value close to zero . This initial state reflects the low adoption of these milk types at the start of the simulation period in 1974 . Over the first 10 steps, there is a rapid increase in consumption, reaching approximately 1,200 units . Over time, the rate of increase in whole milk choices accelerates, indicating a growing preference or habit formation towards whole milk .
