agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . this parameter indicates the degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . over the first 10 steps, there is a rapid decline in consumption, dropping to around 1,000 units . this sharp decrease suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior . between time steps 5 and 15, there is a rapid increase in the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices . this period of rapid growth could be due to the cumulative effects of social influence, habit formation, and positive evaluations . by step 30, the mean quantity consumed reaches approximately 1,400 units . this second increase could be attributed to renewed marketing efforts, further social influence, or additional information dissemination about the benefits of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . the deviation continues to increase but at a much slower and more gradual pace, eventually approaching a value slightly above 0.7 . the subsequent stabilization and gradual increase in deviation suggest that while consumer behaviors continue to evolve, the rate of change slows down, providing a window of opportunity for targeted marketing and policy interventions to influence consumer choices effectively .
