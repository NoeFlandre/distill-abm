 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at approximately 2600 units at the initial time step . There is a sharp decline in consumption within the first five steps, dropping to around 1000 units . This trend indicates a significant initial reduction in whole milk consumption, followed by a period of stabilization and minor adjustments .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents exhibits a rapid increase in the early steps of the simulation, followed by a stabilization phase and a subsequent gradual rise . The mean habit factor for whole milk consistently increases, indicating a growing influence of habit on the consumption of whole milk over time .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero and rapidly increases within the first few steps . After this rapid increase, the deviation stabilizes and remains relatively constant for the remainder of the simulation steps . This trend indicates that initially, there is a significant change in the agents' environmental values relative to their milk choices .
