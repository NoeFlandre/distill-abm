 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, economic outcomes . The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration of the model run .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows distinct trends in the average total number of livestock across all households over the 100 time steps . The average reserve biomass in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time steps. The simulation demonstrates a clear recovery and growth phase in the biomass after an initial decline, reflecting the adaptive management strategies of the agents in the model .  The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the entire simulation period of 100 time steps . The Gini coefficient suggests a growing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock over time . The number of households engaging in social learning shows a steady and significant adoption of social learning behaviors .  The mean number of E-RO households is 16.3696, with a median of 15.2667, indicating a slight skew in the distribution . The standard deviation of 1.9174 suggests moderate variability around the mean . The analysis of rolling Mann-Kendall windows indicates significant decreasing trends in the initial and middle periods .
