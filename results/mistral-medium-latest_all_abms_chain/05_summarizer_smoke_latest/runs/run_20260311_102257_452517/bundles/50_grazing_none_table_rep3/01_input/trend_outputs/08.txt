Following this, the number of households participating in social learning begins to rise steadily. By the 28th time step, another inflection point is noted, and the growth rate starts to slow down slightly, although the overall trend remains upward. The number of households engaging in social learning continues to increase, reaching a peak of approximately 32 households around the 63rd time step. Throughout the simulation, several inflection points are observed at time steps 30, 33, 38, 57, and 60, indicating periods where the rate of increase changes. The oscillation indices suggest some periodic fluctuations, but the dominant trend remains one of steady increase. By the end of the simulation, the number of households engaged in social learning stabilizes around 32, indicating a substantial adoption of social learning behaviors within the population.
