the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . around 1000 steps, the cumulative number of postpartum females killed stabilizes, fluctuating slightly around 120 . this stabilization suggests that the population has reached a state where the rate of new deaths is balanced by the remaining population size, suggesting a potential equilibrium between births and deaths . by the end of the simulation, the number of animals consistently hovers around zero, indicating a complete or near-complete extinction event . following this initial stability, there is a sharp decline in total energy starting around step 100 and continuing until around step 500 . this significant decrease suggests a period of high energy expenditure or reduced energy intake among the male animals .
