 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the simulation .  The total number of animals in the simulation begins at 250, reflecting the initial population set for the prey animals . At this point, the number of births begins to increase gradually, reaching approximately 2 births by 900 time steps . The number of deaths among the prey animal population starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 500 time steps, . This initial rapid increase suggests a high initial mortality rate, which could be attributed to a combination of predation pressure from human hunters and natural causes .  The trend shows low variance across simulation runs initially, but deviations become more noticeable as time progresses . The percentage of green patches starts at approximately 32% and remains stable for the first 300 time steps . At this point, there is another sharp decline in green patches, dropping to approximately 30% . This decline may indicate a period of increased grazing activity or a reduction in grass regrowth rates .
