The average green biomass across all patches in the grazing system exhibits distinct trends over the 100 time steps of the simulation. Initially, the mean green biomass starts at approximately 45,000 units. During the first 27 time steps, there is a notable decline, reaching a minimum value of around 42,057 units. This period of decrease is followed by a significant and steady increase in green biomass. From time step 27 onwards, the green biomass consistently rises, ultimately reaching a maximum value of approximately 60,535 units by the end of the simulation at time step 100. The overall mean green biomass across the entire simulation period is about 51,529 units, with a median value of 51,197 units. The standard deviation of 8,006 units indicates variability around the mean. The data shows a clear inflection point around time step 50, marking the transition from a decreasing trend to an increasing trend. Statistical analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these observations, indicating a significant decreasing trend early in the simulation followed by a significant increasing trend for the remainder of the period. The oscillation analysis suggests periodic fluctuations, particularly around the mid-point of the simulation, but the dominant pattern is the overall increase in green biomass after the initial decline.
