The mean value environmental deviation in the agent-based model simulating milk consumption behaviors shows a rapid initial increase followed by stabilization. Initially, the deviation starts at 0 and rises sharply within the first few steps, reaching approximately 13.4 by step 13. This rapid increase indicates a significant initial adjustment period where agents' environmental values and their milk choices are not aligned. After this initial phase, the deviation stabilizes around the value of 13.4, with minimal fluctuations. The statistical analysis reveals that the mean deviation across the simulation is approximately 12.6, with a median of 13.43, indicating that the data is slightly skewed towards lower values. The standard deviation is 2.59, showing some variability but overall consistency in the later stages of the simulation. The Mann-Kendall trend analysis indicates a significant increasing trend in the early stages (steps 0 to 9), followed by periods of no significant trend, suggesting that the system reaches a steady state after the initial adjustment. The presence of multiple inflection points and oscillation indices further supports the observation of initial volatility followed by stabilization. This trend suggests that the agents' environmental values and their milk choices become more consistent over time, reflecting a stabilization in their decision-making process influenced by habits, social interactions, and perceptions.
