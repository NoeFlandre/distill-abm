The number of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at approximately 120 individuals. This initial population size remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of male animals has decreased to about 80 individuals. The decline continues, and by approximately 1000 steps, the population further reduces to around 20 individuals. Beyond this point, the number of male animals continues to decrease gradually, approaching near extinction by around 2000 steps. The trend indicates a consistent and significant reduction in the male animal population over time, suggesting that the socio-natural dynamics and parameters set in the model lead to a substantial decline in male prey animals. This insight implies that under the given conditions, male animals face high mortality rates, which could be due to a combination of hunting pressure, environmental factors, and internal dynamics of the animal population. For decision-makers, this trend highlights the potential vulnerability of male animals under the simulated conditions and the need to consider conservation strategies or adjustments to hunting practices to mitigate such declines.
