the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . around 500 steps, the rate of increase begins to slow, suggesting that the prey population is becoming depleted or that the foragers are reaching a saturation point in their hunting efficiency . the cumulative number of postpartum females killed starts at zero and remains constant until around 700 time steps . this initial surge could be attributed to the initial population of prey animals reaching maturity and starting to reproduce . between 500 and 1000 steps, the decline continues but at a slower rate, with the number of female animals dropping to near zero . this period likely represents the ongoing pressure from hunting and mortality factors, as well as the reduced birth rate due to the decreasing numbers of females . the total number of animals in the simulation begins at 250, reflecting the initial population set by the parameters . the percentage of green patches quickly recovers to around 32% by the 400th time step . this recovery indicates a period of regrowth or reduced grazing pressure, allowing the grass to recover . a second sharp decline occurs, dropping to about 26% again .
