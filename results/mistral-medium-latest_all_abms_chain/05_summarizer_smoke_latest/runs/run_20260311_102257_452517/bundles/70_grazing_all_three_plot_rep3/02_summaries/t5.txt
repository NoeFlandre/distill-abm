the agent-based model in question is an extension of the RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making . we have set it to 20. - **timesteps**: This parameter determines the number of random households . this parameter sets the standard deviation of rainfall . the model allows for the evaluation of various scenarios . over the first 20 time steps, there is a slight decline, indicating initial grazing activity by the livestock . the mean green biomass continues to decrease gradually until around the 40th time step, reaching a lower point of approximately 43,000 units . this period of gradual decline suggests a steady consumption rate as livestock graze across the patches . between time steps 20 and 60, the Gini coefficient continues to rise more steeply . this period marks a critical phase where disparities among households become more pronounced . decision-makers should note interventions aimed at promoting equitable access to resources or supporting struggling households might be most effective if implemented during this phase .
