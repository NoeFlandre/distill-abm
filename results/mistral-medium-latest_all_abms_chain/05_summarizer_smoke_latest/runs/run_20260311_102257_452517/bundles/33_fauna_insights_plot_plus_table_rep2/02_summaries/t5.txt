the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . the cumulative number of postpartum females killed in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a rapid increase initially, followed by a gradual stabilization . this rapid increase continues more gradually until around 1000 steps, where it starts to plateau . by around 1500 steps, the number of women killed stabilizes around 120, with minor fluctuations . by around 1000 ticks, the cumulative number of deaths stabilizes, fluctuating slightly around a mean value of approximately 235 deaths . key inflection points and change points in the data indicate periods where the rate of deaths changes significantly . 125, 250, 375, 500, 625, and 850 ticks suggest shifts in the dynamics of the model . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend across various windows, with significant negative slopes . the implications for decision-makers suggest that under the given parameters, the animal population is not sustainable and tends towards extinction . around time step 150, there is a noticeable decline, reaching a minimum of about 25% at time step 365 . after this decline, the percentage of green patches gradually recovers and stabilizes around 33% again .
