The number of households engaging in social learning continues to increase gradually, reaching around 32 households by time step 63. After time step 63, the number of households engaging in social learning stabilizes, maintaining a relatively constant value around 32 households for the remainder of the simulation. This stabilization indicates that the system has reached a state where the majority of households that are likely to adopt social learning behaviors have already done so. The median value of 32 households further supports the stabilization observed in the latter part of the simulation. The standard deviation of around 9 households indicates some variability in the number of households engaging in social learning at different time steps, but the overall trend remains consistent.
