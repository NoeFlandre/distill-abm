the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . by 1000 steps, the cumulative number of females killed stabilizes . this stabilization suggests that the system has reached a near-equilibrium state . the dominant oscillation period and various oscillation indices suggest minor periodic fluctuations around the stabilized value . the peaks and valleys in the death count data suggest minor oscillations around the equilibrium state, with the dominant oscillation period being approximately 63 ticks . this pattern reflects the interaction between environmental conditions, hunting behavior, and animal life history within the model . over time, there is a consistent and significant decreasing trend in the number of male animals . the decline is evident from the start and continues throughout the simulation . several minor peaks and valleys suggest small fluctuations in total energy levels . notable peaks occur around time steps 65, 377, and 380, with corresponding valleys closely following these peaks . the inflection points suggest changes in the rate of decline . the percentage of green patches stabilizes and remains relatively constant for the remainder of the simulation, fluctuating slightly around the 33% mark . the rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals both increasing and decreasing trends in different windows .
