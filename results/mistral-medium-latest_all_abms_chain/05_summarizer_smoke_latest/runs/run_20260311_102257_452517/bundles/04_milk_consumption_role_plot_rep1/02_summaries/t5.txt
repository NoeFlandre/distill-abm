agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . this parameter indicates the degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . higher conformity means agents are more likely to align their choices . average weekly consumption of whole milk starts at approximately 2600 units . by step 10, approximately 10,000 choices have been made . this trend continues uniformly, reaching around 20,000 choices by step 20 . the mean whole milk quantity consumed by agents starts at a high value of approximately 2600 units . in the initial steps, from 0 to around 5, there is a steep decline . the deviation continues to increase but at a slower rate, stabilizing around 0.6 . by step 30, the deviation reaches a value slightly above 0.7 and shows minimal further increase . this suggests that the system has reached a near-equilibrium state .
