The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a distinct trend over the simulation period. Initially, the total energy of all animals starts at a high value of approximately 25,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period, indicating that the prey animals begin with substantial energy reserves.  \n\nAround the 500th tick, there is a noticeable decline in the total energy of all animals. This decline continues steeply until approximately the 1000th tick, where the total energy drops to around 5,000 units. This sharp decrease suggests a significant reduction in the energy reserves of the prey animals, potentially due to increased hunting pressure, environmental factors, or a combination of both. \n\nFollowing this steep decline, the rate of energy loss slows down considerably. From the 1000th tick onwards, the total energy of all animals continues to decrease but at a much slower rate. By around the 2000th tick, the total energy levels off and approaches near zero. This indicates that the energy reserves of the prey animals have been almost entirely depleted over the course of the simulation. \n\nThe trend suggests that the initial conditions and parameters set in the model lead to a scenario where the prey animals' energy reserves are gradually exhausted, potentially leading to a significant reduction in their population or even extinction over time.
