 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The model tracks the total number of times agents choose whole milk over the simulation period . Initially, the number of whole milk choices starts at zero and increases steadily over time . The mean value of the total choices over the entire period is around 11,157.35, with a median of 10,679.43 . The minimum value remains zero at the start, while the maximum value is 32,000 .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents in the simulation shows distinct trends over the time steps . Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . This is followed by a rapid increase in consumption, reaching around 1265.47 units by the 11th time step . After this peak, consumption slightly fluctuates but generally stabilizes around the range of 1200 to 1265 units .  The mean value health deviation in the agent-based model starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . The deviation reaches its maximum value of 0.7508 at step 32, indicating that the agents' choices have diversified significantly over time .
