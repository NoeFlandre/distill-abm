agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . constant rise indicates a gradual adoption or preference for whole milk among the agents over time . the total number of whole milk choices reaches approximately 2000 by the final step . this trend suggests that the factors influencing the agents' choices, such as perception, habits, and social influence, are consistently applied . the mean habit factor for skimmed/semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 . it remains relatively constant for the first few steps, indicating a minor shift in habits . this rapid increase continues until around the 15th step, where it begins to increase more rapidly .
