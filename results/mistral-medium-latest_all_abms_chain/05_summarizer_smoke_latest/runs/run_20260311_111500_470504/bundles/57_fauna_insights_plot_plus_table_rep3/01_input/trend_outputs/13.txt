The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. The trend continues to decrease gradually, with minor fluctuations, until it approaches zero by the final steps of the simulation around 2,312 steps. The presence of several inflection points and change points suggests periods where the rate of energy decline shifts, potentially due to varying interactions between predators and prey or environmental factors. The implications for decision-makers include understanding that under the given parameters, the prey population's energy consistently diminishes over time, leading to potential extinction scenarios.
