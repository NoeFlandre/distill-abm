 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes . Initial decline in mean reserve biomass highlights the importance of managing grazing pressure and environmental conditions to prevent overgrazing and biomass depletion . The subsequent recovery and stabilization phases demonstrate the resilience of the grazing system and the potential for sustainable management practices to maintain biomass levels over the long term .  Initially, the mean destock of households starts at a high value of around 7. This high initial value indicates that a significant number of households are destocking livestock at the beginning of the simulation . For decision makers, the initial high destocking rate suggests a critical period where interventions might be necessary to support households . The rapid decline and subsequent stabilization indicate that households can adapt over time .  A significant number of households quickly adopt learning strategies early in the simulation . Between steps 10 and 20, the number of non-learning households fluctuates but generally stabilizes around 20 to 25 households . This stabilization indicates a period where the adoption of learning strategies slows down .
