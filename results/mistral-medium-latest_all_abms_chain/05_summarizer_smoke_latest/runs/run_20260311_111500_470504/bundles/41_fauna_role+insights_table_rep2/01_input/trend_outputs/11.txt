The combined total energy available in the population of male prey animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear decreasing trend over time. Initially, the energy level starts at 12500 units. This high energy level experiences a rapid decline in the early stages, dropping to around 11608 units by tick 65. Following this initial sharp decline, the energy levels continue to decrease but at varying rates, with notable peaks and valleys indicating periods of slight recovery and subsequent declines. Key peaks occur at ticks 377, 380, 388, 385, 390, and 408, with energy values ranging from approximately 3847 to 4002 units. These peaks are followed by corresponding valleys, suggesting periodic fluctuations in energy levels. The inflection points identified at various ticks indicate changes in the rate of decline, with significant changes noted around ticks 316, 453, 516, and others. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend across multiple windows, with significant decreases noted in the early stages and more moderate changes later on. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of 63 ticks. The energy levels eventually decline to zero by tick 2284, indicating the complete depletion of energy in the male prey population. For decision makers, these trends highlight the critical impact of hunting pressure and environmental factors on the energy dynamics of male prey animals, emphasizing the need for sustainable management practices to prevent population collapse.
