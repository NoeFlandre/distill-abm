The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation period. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. However, it rapidly declines, reaching approximately -2000 units around the 400th tick. This downward trend continues steadily, with notable energy levels of around -23807 units at the 1594th tick, -28403 units at the 1826th tick, and -37713 units at the 2284th tick. By the end of the simulation at the 2310th tick, the energy level plummets to -38229 units. The statistical analysis confirms this persistent decline, with all rolling Mann-Kendall windows indicating a significant decreasing trend and negative slopes ranging from -8.823 to -20.011. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points further underscores the continuous and significant drop in energy levels. For decision makers, this trend suggests that the current parameters and dynamics of the model lead to a rapid depletion of energy resources for forager bands, which could imply unsustainable hunting practices or insufficient energy intake relative to movement and processing costs. This insight is crucial for understanding the potential pressures on human hunters and the broader implications for the survival of both hunters and prey in the modeled ecosystem.
