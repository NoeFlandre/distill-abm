agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . boolean parameter indicates degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . higher conformity means agents are more likely to align their choices with social norm . by the 15th step, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 25,000 . at step 10, approximately 10,000 choices have been made, and at step 20, this number doubles to around 20,000 choices . this linear trend indicates a consistent and steady increase in the number of choices made over time . from steps 5 to 10, the mean habit factor gradually increases, reflecting a growing influence of habits on milk choice . between steps 10 and 15, there is a stronger entrenchment of habits, indicating that habits are becoming increasingly significant in the agents' decision-making processes .
