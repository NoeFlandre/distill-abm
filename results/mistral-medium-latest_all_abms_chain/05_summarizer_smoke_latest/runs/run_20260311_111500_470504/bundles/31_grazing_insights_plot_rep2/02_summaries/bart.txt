 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes . The data from repeated simulations of the agent-based model reveal several distinct trends in the number of households engaging in social learning over time . For decision-makers, these trends suggest that over time, certain households accumulate more livestock, leading to greater disparities .  The number of non-learning households starts at 60, as all households begin with the E-RO strategy . Within the first 10 steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of households, dropping to approximately 20 . Between steps 10 and 20, the number fluctuates but generally stabilizes around 20 to 25 households . This suggests a core group of households either resist or are slower to adopt learning strategies .
