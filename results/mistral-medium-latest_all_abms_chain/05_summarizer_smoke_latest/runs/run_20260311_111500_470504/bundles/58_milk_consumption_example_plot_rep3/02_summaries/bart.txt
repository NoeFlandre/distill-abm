 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  Total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 30 time steps . By the end of the simulation, the total number of whole milk choices reaches approximately 2200, indicating a cumulative adoption trend where agents increasingly choose whole milk over time . The overall trend demonstrates a clear and consistent shift in milk consumption preferences over time, driven by the interplay of social influence and habit formation .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0 . The variability among the simulation runs is relatively low, suggesting consistent behavior across different simulations . The mean choice function deviation increases rapidly within the first five steps, reaching approximately 0.55 . This rapid rise indicates a quick divergence in the choice function scores among agents .
