agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the consumption stabilizes and fluctuates slightly around the 1200 to 1300 unit range between the 10th and 25th time steps . the market has reached a temporary equilibrium where the rate of new adopters balances out with those who continue to consume whole milk . the mean quantity of whole milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 2,655 units . this high initial value indicates a strong preference for whole milk at the beginning of the simulation period . the quantity declines rapidly within the first few time steps . constant upward trend indicates a steady increase in the habit factor . this trend suggests that over time, the agents' habits towards whole milk consumption become increasingly entrenched . the mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a value of 1.0000 . later stages of the simulation imply that the system has settled into a stable pattern of behavior . this trend provides insights for decision-makers regarding the stabilization period of consumer behavior in relation to environmental values .
