Between time steps 20 and 60, the Gini coefficient continues to rise more steeply, indicating an accelerating inequality in livestock distribution. Decision-makers should note that interventions aimed at promoting equitable access to resources or supporting struggling households might be most effective if implemented during this phase to prevent further divergence. 

After time step 60, the rate of increase in the Gini coefficient starts to slow down, and by time step 100, the values begin to stabilize.
