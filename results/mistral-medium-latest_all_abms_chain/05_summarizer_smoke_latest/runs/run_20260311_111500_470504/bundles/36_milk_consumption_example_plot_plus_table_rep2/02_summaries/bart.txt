 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices, specifically the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk .  The total number of milk choices made by all agents combined starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . The mean value of whole milk choices is approximately 1130.88, with a median of 1158.97, indicating a consistent upward trend throughout the simulation . The number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 1,000 by the 5th time step .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . There are minor fluctuations observed around the 19th to 27th time steps, with slight peaks and valleys, indicating some variability in consumption patterns . Overall trend suggests a significant reduction in whole milk consumption over the simulated period .  The trend stabilizes near 0.75 by the final step, suggesting that the agents' choices have diversified significantly and reached a relatively stable state . The overall trend shows a consistent increase in the mean choice function deviation, reflecting the growing diversity in agents' milk consumption choices over time .
