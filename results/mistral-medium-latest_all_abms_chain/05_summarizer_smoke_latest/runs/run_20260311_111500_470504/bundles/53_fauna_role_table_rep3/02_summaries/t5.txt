the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . animals can reproduce, and users can control sex ratios and reproduction probability . the cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period . Initially, the count starts at zero and begins to increase rapidly . this rapid increase is evident in the early time steps, with significant inflection points occurring around time steps 125, 245, 262, and 285, indicating notable changes in the rate of increase . the birth count begins to decline and continues until the end of the simulation period . several valleys, along with a dominant oscillation period, suggest periodic fluctuations in the death count . the simulation shows an initial growth phase in the cumulative number of births, reaching a peak, followed by a decline phase . the dominant period of oscillation is 63 time steps, indicating a cyclical pattern within the overall declining trend . despite these oscillations, the overarching trend remains a steady decrease in the prey population, ultimately leading to extinction by the end of the simulation . the rolling Mann-Kendall test identifies several inflection points and change points, indicating significant shifts in the trend of energy levels . there are also periods where the trend temporarily shifts to increasing, such as around time steps 1392 to 1623 and 1856 to 2087 .
