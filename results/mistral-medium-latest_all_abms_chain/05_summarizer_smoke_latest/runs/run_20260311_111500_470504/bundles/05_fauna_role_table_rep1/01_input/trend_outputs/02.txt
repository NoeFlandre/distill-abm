The total energy available to all forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent and significant decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the energy level starts at 2000 units. However, from the very beginning, there is a sharp decline in energy levels. Early inflection points around time steps 358 and 1238 indicate notable changes in the rate of energy decrease. The energy levels continue to drop steadily, with several peaks and valleys observed, but these are relatively minor fluctuations within the overall downward trend. Key peaks are observed at time steps such as 2284, 1826, and 1594, with values of -37713, -28403, and -23807 respectively, while valleys are noted at time steps like 2310, 2283, and 1825, with values of -38233, -37717, and -28458.75 respectively. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend across all windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values close to -1, indicating a strong and steady decline. Change points at time steps 650, 1075, 1500, 1925, and 2310 further highlight shifts in the rate of energy decrease. Towards the end of the simulation, the energy levels reach a minimum value of -38233 at time step 2310. The dominant oscillation period of 63 time steps suggests some regularity in minor fluctuations, but these do not alter the overall decreasing trend. The consistent decline in energy levels reflects the challenging conditions faced by the forager bands, likely due to a combination of high movement costs, processing costs of prey, and limited food value gained from hunting, leading to a steady depletion of their energy reserves over time.
