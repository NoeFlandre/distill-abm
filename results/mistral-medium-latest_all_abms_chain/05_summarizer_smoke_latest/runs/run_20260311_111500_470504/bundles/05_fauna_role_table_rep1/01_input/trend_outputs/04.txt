The cumulative number of female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the number of females killed starts at zero and begins to increase rapidly. This rapid increase is evident in the early time steps, with significant inflection points occurring around time steps 125, 245, 262, and 285, indicating a steep rise in hunting activity. The trend continues to show a consistent increase, albeit at a slower rate, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis, which shows increasing trends with high significance in the initial windows. The slope of the increase is particularly steep in the early phases, with slopes of 0.213 and 0.226 in the first two windows, respectively. As the simulation progresses, the rate of increase in the number of females killed starts to taper off, with inflection points at time steps 1204, 1336, and 1487, suggesting a slowing down in the rate of hunting. The peaks and valleys identified in the data indicate minor fluctuations around a relatively stable high value, with the maximum value of 123.6667 reached at time step 1826. The latter part of the simulation shows a slight decrease in the cumulative number of females killed, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall window 9, which shows a decreasing trend. The oscillation indices towards the end of the simulation suggest minor periodic variations, but these are relatively small in magnitude. Overall, the trend demonstrates an initial rapid increase in hunting activity, followed by a stabilization period, and a slight decline towards the end of the simulation.
