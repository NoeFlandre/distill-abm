The simulation results indicate a consistent and significant increasing trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk by agents over time. Initially, the number of times agents chose alternative milk types starts at zero. However, this value steadily increases, reaching a maximum of approximately 25,643.83 by the end of the simulation. The mean number of choices for alternative milk types across all time steps is about 11,157.35, with a median of 10,679.43, suggesting a right-skewed distribution. The standard deviation of 8,241.32 highlights considerable variability in the data, indicating diverse adoption rates at different time steps. The rolling Mann-Kendall test across various windows consistently shows a significant increasing trend, with high tau values and significant p-values, reinforcing the robustness of the upward trend. The absence of detected peaks and valleys suggests a smooth and continuous increase without significant fluctuations. An inflection point is noted at time step 31, indicating a potential change in the rate of adoption around this period. The dominant oscillation period of 15 time steps suggests some periodic variability, but the overall trend remains upward. For decision-makers, these trends imply that interventions or policies aimed at promoting healthier milk choices could be effective over time, as the model shows a clear shift towards the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The consistent increase suggests that initial efforts to influence consumer behavior could lead to sustained long-term changes.
