The mean household initial risk attitude remains constant throughout the simulation period, with a value of approximately 0.0074. This indicates that there is no change in the initial disposition of households to deviate from the recommended number of livestock over the 100 steps of the simulation. The statistical analysis confirms this stability, showing no significant trends or variations in the data. The standard deviation and variance are both zero, reinforcing the lack of variability in the risk attitude. The rolling Mann-Kendall test across different windows also indicates no significant trends, further supporting the consistency of the household's initial risk attitude. For decision-makers, this implies that under the given model parameters and conditions, households maintain a steady initial risk attitude, which could suggest stability in decision-making processes related to livestock management. This stability might be useful for predicting household behavior and planning interventions or policies aimed at managing grazing systems.
