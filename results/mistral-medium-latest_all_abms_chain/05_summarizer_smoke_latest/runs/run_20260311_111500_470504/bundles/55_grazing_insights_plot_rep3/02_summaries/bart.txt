 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes . Initial decline in biomass suggests a period of adjustment or stress in the system, possibly due to initial grazing pressures . The subsequent recovery and stabilization phases indicate that with appropriate management, the grazing system can achieve sustainable biomass levels .  Simulations show a variety of trends in the adoption of social learning among households over time . Initially, the mean destock total starts at zero and remains relatively low for the first 20 steps . The number of households engaging in social learning varies significantly across simulations, with some showing as few as 15 households and others as many as 54 households .  The number of non-learning households varies widely, with counts ranging from approximately 5 to 40 . This high variability suggests a diverse initial distribution of household strategies . By step 100, most trajectories converge to a lower range, typically between 0 and 15 households . This indicates a shift away from the E-RO strategy as households potentially adopt learning strategies .
