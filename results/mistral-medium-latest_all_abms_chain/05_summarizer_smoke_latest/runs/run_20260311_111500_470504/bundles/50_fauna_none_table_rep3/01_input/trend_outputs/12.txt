The combined total energy available in the population of female prey animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear overall decreasing trend throughout the simulation. Initially, the energy level starts at 12,500 units. This high initial value is followed by a rapid decline in the early stages of the simulation. The most significant drop occurs within the first few hundred ticks, as indicated by the steep negative slopes in the early rolling Mann-Kendall windows. By the 375th tick, a notable change point is observed, marking a shift in the rate of decline. Following this, the energy levels continue to decrease but at varying rates, with several inflection points indicating changes in the trend's steepness. Between ticks 1160 and 1391, there is a brief period where the trend shifts to an increasing pattern, although this increase is minimal and short-lived. Post this period, the energy levels resume their downward trajectory. The latter part of the simulation, particularly after the 2087th tick, shows a more gradual decline in energy levels. The simulation concludes with the energy level reaching zero by the final tick, indicating the complete depletion of energy in the female prey population. Throughout the simulation, there are minor oscillations with a dominant period of approximately 63 ticks, but these do not significantly alter the overall decreasing trend.
