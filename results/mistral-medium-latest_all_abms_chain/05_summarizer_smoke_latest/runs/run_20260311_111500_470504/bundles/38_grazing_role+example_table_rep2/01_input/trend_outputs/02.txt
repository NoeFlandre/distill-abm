The average degree of deviation from the recommended number of livestock for all extension agents begins at a low value of 0.0074. This metric shows a rapid increase in the early time steps, reaching a peak value of approximately 0.3814 around time step 33. This initial rise suggests that households are initially adjusting their livestock numbers significantly as they respond to the ecological and climatic conditions. Following this peak, the deviation metric exhibits a general decreasing trend, indicating a gradual alignment of livestock numbers closer to the recommended values. Notable inflection points occur around time steps 7, 12, 13, 39, 46, 51, 67, 70, 74, 75, 76, 81, 83, 85, 86, and 90, suggesting periods of significant change in the rate of deviation. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates significant increasing trends in the early phases (time steps 0-32) and significant decreasing trends in the middle phases (time steps 33-65). The latter part of the simulation (time steps 66-98) shows no significant trend, indicating a stabilization in the deviation metric. The simulation ends with a deviation value of 0.1911, suggesting that while there is still some deviation from the recommended livestock numbers, it is considerably lower than the peak values observed earlier. The oscillation indices and change points further highlight the dynamic adjustments and periodic fluctuations in household livestock management strategies throughout the simulation.
