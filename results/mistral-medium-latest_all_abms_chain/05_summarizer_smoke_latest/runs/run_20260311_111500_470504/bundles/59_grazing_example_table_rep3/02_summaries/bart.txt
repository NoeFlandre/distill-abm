 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households begins at 90.0000 . Initial rise suggests that agents are initially adjusting their livestock numbers significantly, possibly due to initial learning and adaptation processes . After the initial peak, there is a general decreasing trend in the deviation metric, with significant decreases observed in the rolling Mann-Kendall windows from time steps 33 to 65 .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the initial time steps . By the 10th time step, there is a noticeable uptake, indicating that households begin to adopt social learning behaviors early in the simulation . The overall trend highlights an initial crisis period followed by a gradual stabilization, with periodic minor disruptions reflecting ongoing but less severe challenges .  Trend analysis shows significant decreasing trends in multiple rolling windows, particularly between the 11th and 65th time steps . The data suggests a consistent reduction in the number of households adhering to the E-RO strategy over time . This decline continues steadily, with minor fluctuations, until it reaches a low of 14.67 households around the 92nd time step .
