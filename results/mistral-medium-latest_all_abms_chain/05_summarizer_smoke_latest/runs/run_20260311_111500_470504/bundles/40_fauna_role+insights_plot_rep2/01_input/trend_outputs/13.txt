The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations exhibits a consistent pattern across multiple runs. Initially, the total energy of all animals starts at a high value of approximately 25,000 units. This high energy level is maintained briefly before a sharp decline begins around the 50th step. By the 250th step, the total energy drops significantly to around 5,000 units. This rapid decrease continues, and by the 500th step, the total energy of all animals falls to near zero. Beyond the 500th step, the total energy remains at or near zero for the remainder of the simulation, indicating a complete depletion of energy among the animals. 

For decision makers, this trend suggests that under the given parameters and initial conditions, the prey population experiences a rapid and severe decline in energy levels, leading to a potential collapse of the population within a relatively short time frame. This insight could be crucial for understanding the dynamics of megaherbivore extinction and the impact of various factors such as hunting pressure, environmental conditions, and animal life history on the sustainability of prey populations.
