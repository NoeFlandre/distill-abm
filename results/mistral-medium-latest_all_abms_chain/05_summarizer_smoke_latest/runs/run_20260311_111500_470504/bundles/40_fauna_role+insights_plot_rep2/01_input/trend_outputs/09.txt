The number of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations exhibits a consistent declining trend across all runs. Initially, the number of female animals starts at approximately 120. This number remains relatively stable for a brief period before beginning a steady decline. By around 500 steps, the number of female animals drops to about 60. The decline continues, and by approximately 1000 steps, the number of female animals further reduces to around 20. Beyond 1000 steps, the number of female animals continues to decrease gradually, approaching near-zero values as the steps progress towards 2000. This trend indicates a significant reduction in the female prey population over time, suggesting high hunting pressure or other environmental and demographic factors leading to a sharp decline. For decision-makers, this trend underscores the potential vulnerability of female prey populations under the given model parameters and highlights the need for careful consideration of hunting pressures and environmental conditions to ensure the sustainability of prey populations.
