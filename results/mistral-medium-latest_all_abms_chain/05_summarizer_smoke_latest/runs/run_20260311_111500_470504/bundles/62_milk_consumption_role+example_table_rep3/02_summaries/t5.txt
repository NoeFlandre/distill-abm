agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . this parameter indicates the degree to which agents conform to social norms . the range is from 0.0 to 1.0, and it is set to 0.28 . a higher conformity means agents are more likely to align their choices . by the early stages of the simulation, the consumption drops substantially . slopes reach around 935.933 between time steps 25 to 29 . this suggests a growing preference and acceptance of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the agents . the simulation ends with a total of 32,000 milk choices . the mean value across all time steps is 1.0359, with a median of 1.0376, suggesting a slight upward trend in the habit factor over time . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms this increasing trend across multiple windows . the dominant oscillation period of 15.00 and multiple oscillation indices indicate periodic fluctuations in the deviation, suggesting cyclical patterns in how agents' choices deviate from their health perceptions . the change point at time step 32 marks a notable shift in the trend, indicating a significant change in the dynamics .
