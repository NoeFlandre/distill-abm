 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  Initially, at step 0, the total number of choices is close to zero . As the steps progress, the number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices rises gradually at first and then more rapidly . The trend indicates a steady increase in the cumulative number of whole milk choices made by the agents over the simulated period .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at a low value close to zero and rapidly increases within the first few steps . By step 10, the deviation is around 0.6, and it continues to rise steadily, reaching about 0.65 by step 15 . After step 25, there is a gradual increase in the mean value health deviation, reaching a peak of 0.7 by step 30 .
