agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the average weekly consumption of whole milk per person starts at 2654.81 units . this high initial value indicates a strong preference and habit for whole milk consumption . the consumption then declines rapidly over the first few steps, reaching around 773.96 units by step 19 . the absence of detected peaks or valleys further supports a smooth and continuous increase in whole milk choices . for decision-makers, this trend implies that the initial strong habit for whole milk plays a significant role in its continued consumption . the total number of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices starts at zero and increases linearly over the 33 time steps . the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 5.29 units . this initial value indicates a very low adoption rate of alternative milk types at the beginning of the simulation . the consumption then increases rapidly, reaching around 1220.21 units by the 11th time step . the overall trend demonstrates a clear shift in consumer behavior . it suggests interventions aimed at influencing consumer habits should be sustained over time . the mean choice function deviation starts at 0.0, indicating that initially, all agents have identical choice function scores . this deviation increases rapidly within the first 7 steps, reaching approximately 0.55 .
