 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The data shows a consistent upward trend in the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices over the simulation period . For decision makers, this trend suggests a growing preference or habit formation towards whole milk among the agents . Understanding these trends can help in designing interventions or strategies to influence milk consumption patterns effectively .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk shows a gradual increase over the time steps . For decision makers, this implies that habits related to whole milk consumption become more entrenched as time progresses . This trend could influence strategies aimed at shifting consumption patterns towards healthier or more environmentally friendly alternatives .
