agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at approximately 2600 units at the initial time step . this high initial value indicates a strong initial habit and preference for whole milk among the agents . over the first five steps, there is a sharp decline in consumption, dropping to around 1000 units . the rapid initial adoption phase highlights the importance of early interventions and marketing strategies to encourage the switch to healthier milk options . starting from 0 choices at step 0, the total number of choices rises steadily, reaching approximately 32,000 choices by step 30 . this linear trend indicates a consistent and uniform increase in the number of decisions made by the agents over time . by step 10, the mean habit factor is approximately 1.03, and it continues to rise incrementally . this trend indicates that the influence of habit on the consumption of whole milk strengthens over time . for decision-makers, interventions aimed at reducing whole milk consumption may need to account for the increasing role of habit . the deviation remains relatively constant, showing minimal fluctuations and maintaining a steady state through to step 30 . this trend indicates that initially, there is a significant change in the agents' environmental values relative to their milk choices, which quickly stabilizes as the simulation progresses .
