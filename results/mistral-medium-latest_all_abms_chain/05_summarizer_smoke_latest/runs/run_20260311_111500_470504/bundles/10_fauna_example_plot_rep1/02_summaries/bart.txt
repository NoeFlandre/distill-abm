 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy .  The number of postpartum females killed varies slightly across different simulation runs, reflecting the inherent variability in the interactions between foragers and prey . The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains constant until around 700 time steps . At this point, there is a sharp increase in the number of deaths, reaching approximately 150 deaths by the 500-step mark .
