agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the mean consumption over the entire period is around 1051.6 units . a median of 1220.2 units indicates that the latter half of the simulation period generally exhibits higher consumption levels . notable peaks occur at time steps 11, 13, 21, and 19, with values around 1265.5, 1240.3, 1226.9, and 1221.6 units respectively . by the midpoint of the simulation, the consumption has decreased to around 773.96 units, indicating a substantial shift in consumption patterns . this trend continues with some fluctuations, as evidenced by minor peaks and valleys . the mean factor applied to the skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choice function starts at a value of 1.0000 . from around the fifth time step, the factor begins to increase steadily . this upward trend becomes statistically significant and continues consistently throughout the remaining time steps, reaching a final value of 1.5879 . the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates a significant increasing trend in the early time steps, followed by periods of no significant trend . this suggests that the initial phase of the simulation is characterized by rapid changes, which then stabilize into a more consistent pattern .
