This divergence indicates that households are quickly adapting their strategies based on initial conditions and early experiences in the simulation. Between time steps 20 and 40, the risk attitudes stabilize into distinct clusters. Households with higher risk attitudes maintain values around 0.7 to 0.8, while those with lower risk attitudes stabilize around 0.2 to 0.4. A few households exhibit negative risk attitudes, stabilizing around -0.2. From time step 40 to the end of the simulation at time step 100, the risk attitudes remain relatively stable within these clusters. There is minimal fluctuation, suggesting that households have settled into their respective risk strategies for the long term. The distinct clusters of risk attitudes indicate different adaptive strategies among households, likely influenced by their learning capabilities and interactions with ecological and climatic parameters.
