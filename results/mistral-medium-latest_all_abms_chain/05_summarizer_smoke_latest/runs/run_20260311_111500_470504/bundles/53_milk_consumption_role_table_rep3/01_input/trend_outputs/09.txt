The mean factor applied to the skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choice function due to habit exhibits a clear increasing trend over the simulation period. Initially, the habit factor starts at a value of 1.0000, indicating no initial influence of habit on the choice of alternative milk types. This value remains relatively stable with minimal fluctuation in the early stages of the simulation. Around the fifth time step, the habit factor begins to increase gradually, marking the start of a consistent upward trend. This trend becomes statistically significant from the fifth time step onwards, as evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test results, which indicate a significant increasing trend in multiple windows. Key inflection points occur around the third and twelfth time steps, suggesting notable changes in the rate of increase. The habit factor continues to rise steadily, reaching its maximum value of 1.5879 at the final time step. This indicates a growing influence of habit on the choice of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time. The presence of oscillation indices towards the latter part of the simulation suggests some variability in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. The change points identified at the fifteenth and thirty-second time steps further highlight significant shifts in the habit factor's trajectory. In summary, the data shows a clear and consistent increase in the habit factor influencing the choice of alternative milk types, reflecting the growing impact of habit on consumer behavior over the simulated period.
