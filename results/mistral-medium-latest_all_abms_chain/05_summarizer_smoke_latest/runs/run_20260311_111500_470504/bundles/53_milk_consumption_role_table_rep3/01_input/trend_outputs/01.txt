The simulation of the agent-based model shows a clear and consistent downward trend in the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent from 1974 to 2005. Initially, the average consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units. This value experiences a steep decline in the early stages of the simulation, dropping significantly within the first few time steps. Throughout the simulation, the consumption continues to decrease, albeit at varying rates, with notable inflection points indicating changes in the rate of decline. Key time steps where significant changes in the trend occur include steps 5, 13, 16, 19, 24, 25, 27, and 30. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms these decreasing trends across multiple windows, with significant decreases noted in windows 1 through 3, 5, and 6. The final value of the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent reaches approximately 253.10 units by the end of the simulation period. The data also exhibits some oscillatory behavior, with dominant periods of oscillation identified around specific time steps, suggesting periodic fluctuations in the rate of decline. Overall, the simulation effectively captures a significant reduction in whole milk consumption over the studied period, reflecting the shifting preferences and behaviors of the agents in the model.
