agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . agent-based model shows a downward trend in the average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent from 1974 to 2005 . Initially, the average consumption starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units . this value experiences a steep decline in the early stages of the simulation . the data suggests a consistent and consistent trend towards increased whole milk consumption among the agents over the simulation period . Initially, the adoption of alternative milk types starts at zero, reflecting the baseline condition where no agents have yet chosen these options . the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early and later stages of the simulation . the dominant oscillation period of 15 time steps further supports the presence of periodic fluctuations around the stabilized mean . the rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals a steady increase in the mean deviation . the slopes of these trends vary, with the highest slope of 0.063 in the initial window (0 to 4) and lower slopes in subsequent windows . inflection points are observed at several time steps (4, 6, 16, 19, 22, 28, and 31), suggesting changes in the rate of increase .
