the agent-based model in question is an extension of the RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . it simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making . we have set it to 20. - **timesteps**: This parameter determines the number of random households . this parameter sets the standard deviation of rainfall . the model allows for the evaluation of various scenarios . over the first 20 time steps, there is a slight decline, indicating initial grazing activity by the livestock . after the 40th time step, the mean green biomass begins to increase steadily . this upward trend continues until around the 80th time . the increase in green biomass during this period indicates a recovery phase where vegetation growth outpaces . over the first 20 time steps, the Gini coefficient reaches approximately 0.015 . this indicates a slow divergence in livestock health distribution among households . by around 40 time steps the rate of increase in the gini coefficient accelerates . over time, certain households may be experiencing better management practices .
