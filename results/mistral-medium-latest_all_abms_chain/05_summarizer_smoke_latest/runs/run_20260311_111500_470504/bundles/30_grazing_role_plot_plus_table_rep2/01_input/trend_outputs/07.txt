The analysis of the simulation results reveals a consistent upward trend in the Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock over the 100 time steps. Initially, the Gini coefficient starts at approximately 0.0, indicating a relatively equal distribution of healthy livestock among households. As the simulation progresses, the Gini coefficient gradually increases, reaching a value of approximately 0.0726 by the end of the simulation. This trend suggests a growing inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across households over time.

Key inflection points are observed at time steps 8, 13, 15, 20, 25, 37, and 57, indicating notable changes in the rate of increase in inequality. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across all windows, with significant positive trends in each window, reinforcing the observation of growing inequality throughout the simulation period.

Change points are detected at time steps 51, 71, and 100, suggesting shifts in the dynamics of inequality at these points. Additionally, oscillations in the Gini coefficient are observed starting from time step 78 onwards, indicating periodic fluctuations in the rate of change of inequality. Despite these oscillations, the overall trend remains consistently upward, highlighting a persistent increase in inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock among households throughout the simulation.
