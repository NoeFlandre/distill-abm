the model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . the goal is to understand how environmental conditions, human hunting behavior, and animal life history interact to influence extinction events or create equilibrium conditions . the model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . the temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . the cumulative number of postpartum females killed rises sharply from 0 to around 100 steps . this rapid increase indicates a high initial hunting success rate . however, the rate of increase slows significantly as time progresses . it could inform strategies related to hunting quotas and conservation efforts to ensure the sustainability of prey populations. by around 500 steps, the number of female animals has decreased significantly to about 40 . this trend indicates a rapid initial reduction in the female animal population followed by a slower decline as their numbers dwindle . the total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at an initial value of 250 . by approximately 500 steps, the total energy of the animals drops significantly to around 5,000 units . this sharp decline suggests a rapid consumption or depletion of energy reserves among the prey animals . for decision-makers, this trend highlights critical periods where interventions could be necessary to prevent the drastic decline in animal energy levels .
