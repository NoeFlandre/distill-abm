The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent decline in the number of female prey animals over time. Initially, the population starts at 125 female prey animals. This number steadily decreases, with significant drops observed at various time steps. Early in the simulation, around time step 730, there is a notable peak where the population briefly increases to approximately 6.51, followed by a series of smaller peaks and valleys indicating fluctuations in the population. However, the overall trend remains a steady decline. By the end of the simulation, the population of female prey animals reaches zero. The statistical analysis indicates several inflection points and change points, suggesting periods where the rate of decline changes. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent decreasing trend across multiple windows, with occasional brief periods of increase. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations in the population, but the dominant trend is a steady decline to extinction.
