The mean destock total of households in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the 100 steps of the simulation. Starting from an initial value of 0, the destock total rises sharply in the early steps, reaching approximately 80 by step 25. This rapid increase is followed by a more gradual rise, with the destock total reaching around 96.78 by the final step. The trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a significant increasing trend across all windows, indicating a steady accumulation of destocked livestock over time. Inflection points are observed at various steps, notably around steps 45, 47, 53, 56, 57, 62, 67, 72, 74, and 75, suggesting periods where the rate of increase changes. Change points are detected at steps 25, 60, and 100, marking significant shifts in the trend. The oscillation analysis reveals periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of 49 steps, indicating cyclical patterns in the destocking process. For decision-makers, these trends highlight the progressive nature of destocking over time, with notable periods of acceleration and stabilization. Understanding these patterns can aid in planning and resource allocation to manage livestock and household livelihoods more effectively under varying ecological and climatic conditions.
