The data from the repeated simulations of the agent-based model show a clear and consistent trend in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choices over time. Initially, at step 0, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices is very low, close to zero. This indicates that at the beginning of the simulation period, which corresponds to the year 1974, the majority of agents are not choosing skimmed or semi-skimmed milk. As the steps progress, there is a gradual increase in the number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices. By step 5, the total number of these choices starts to rise more noticeably. Around step 10, the increase becomes more pronounced, indicating a growing trend towards the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. This upward trend continues steadily, showing a significant rise in the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices. By step 20, the total number of these choices has increased substantially, reflecting a broader adoption among the agents. As the steps approach 30, the total number of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk choices reaches its peak, indicating that by the end of the simulation period, which corresponds to the year 2005, a substantial majority of agents have shifted their preference towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The trend demonstrates a consistent and steady increase in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the entire simulation period.
