agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . trends suggest initial adoption is rapid due to health awareness and social influence followed by a period of stabilization . subsequent increases are likely driven by sustained social norms and continuous reinforcement of health benefits . the total number of whole milk choices starts at zero . the mean quantity stabilizes and fluctuates slightly around the 1200 to 1300 units range from step 10 to step 25 . this period of stability indicates that the adoption rate has plateaued, with most agents having incorporated skimmed and semi-skimmed milk into their consumption habits . aimed at influencing milk consumption choices should consider rapid initial diversification and subsequent stabilization period . the deviation starts at a value close to zero, indicating that initially, the agents' health values closely align with their milk choices . this sharp rise suggests that as agents begin to make milk choices, their health perceptions start to diverge significantly from their chosen milk types .
