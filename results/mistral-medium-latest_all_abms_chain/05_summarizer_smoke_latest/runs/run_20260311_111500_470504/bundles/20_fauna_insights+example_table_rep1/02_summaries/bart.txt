 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick .  The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands starts at zero and exhibits a rapid increase in the early time steps of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model . The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates a consistent increasing trend in early and middle phases of the simulation, which shifts to a decreasing trend towards the end .
