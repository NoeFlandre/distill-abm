The percentage of vegetated landscape patches in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) begins at approximately 33% and exhibits minor fluctuations throughout the simulation. The initial value of 33.0002% slightly decreases to a minimum of 24.9108% around the 365th tick, indicating a temporary reduction in grass availability. Following this dip, the percentage of vegetated patches gradually recovers and stabilizes around the initial value, ending at 32.9979%. The data shows several inflection points and minor peaks and valleys, suggesting periodic oscillations in grass regrowth and consumption. The rolling Mann-Kendall analysis indicates significant trends in different windows, with initial increasing trends followed by decreasing trends in later windows, reflecting dynamic changes in vegetation cover over time. The dominant oscillation period of 63 ticks suggests a cyclical pattern in grass regrowth and grazing. For decision-makers, these trends imply that while the overall vegetation cover remains relatively stable, there are periodic fluctuations that could impact the availability of fodder for prey animals, potentially influencing their population dynamics and interactions with human hunters.
