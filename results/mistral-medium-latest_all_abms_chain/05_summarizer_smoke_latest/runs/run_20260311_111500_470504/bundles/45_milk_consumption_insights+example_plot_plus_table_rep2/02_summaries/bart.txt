 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The total number of milk choices starts at 0 and increases steadily over the 33 time steps . The mean number of whole milk choices is approximately 1131, with a median of 1159, suggesting a consistent upward trend across the simulations . For decision makers, this trend suggests that interventions aimed at promoting healthier milk choices are likely to see gradual but consistent uptake . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing effective strategies for promoting healthier dietary habits .  The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents starts at approximately 5.29 units . Initially, there is a rapid increase in the habit factor within the first few steps, followed by a more gradual and steady rise . The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . The overall trend continues to show a decrease in whole milk consumption .  Trend suggests that over time, the consumption of skimmed/semi-skimmed milk becomes more habitual among the agents . Trend highlights the growing misalignment between public perception and actual health implications of milk choices . For decision makers, this trend highlights the need for targeted strategies to address varying consumer preferences .
