 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at approximately 2600 units at the initial time step . There is a sharp decline in consumption within the first five steps, dropping to around 1000 units . This rapid decrease suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior away from whole milk early in the simulation period .  The total number of choices made by all agents combined increases linearly over the 30 steps of the simulation . This linear trend indicates a consistent and uniform increase in the number of decisions made by agents at each step . For decision-makers, this trend suggests a steady engagement of agents in the decision-making process .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk exhibits a consistent upward trend over the simulation period . Initially, the habit factor starts at a value of approximately 1.0 at step 0, indicating that initially, there is no deviation among the agents' choice functions . As the simulation progresses, the deviation increases rapidly, reaching approximately 0.5 by step 5 .
