The percentage of green patches in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulation period. Initially, the percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33%. This value remains relatively stable for a brief period before experiencing a sharp decline around the 365th step, reaching a minimum value of about 24.91%. Following this decline, there is a rapid recovery, with the percentage of green patches increasing back to around 33% by approximately the 500th step. This recovery is followed by another period of stability. Around the 1000th step, there is another notable decline, although less severe than the first, followed by another recovery phase. After this second recovery, the percentage of green patches stabilizes again, maintaining a value close to 33% with minor fluctuations for the remainder of the simulation period. The statistical analysis indicates several inflection points and change points, highlighting periods of significant change in the trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test reveals both increasing and decreasing trends at different intervals, suggesting dynamic shifts in the landscape's vegetation coverage over time. The dominant oscillation period of 63 steps indicates a cyclical pattern in the percentage of green patches, reflecting periodic variations in the availability of grass patches.
