The total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent declining trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12,500 units. This high energy level is maintained briefly before beginning a steep decline. The most significant drop occurs within the first few hundred time steps, where the energy level rapidly decreases. By around 500 time steps, the energy level has already dropped to approximately 4,000 units, marking a substantial reduction from the initial value. 

Following this steep decline, the rate of energy loss slows down, but the overall trend remains downward. The energy levels continue to decrease gradually, with occasional minor fluctuations. These fluctuations are relatively small and do not significantly alter the overall declining trend. By around 1,000 time steps, the energy level has further reduced to approximately 2,000 units. 

As time progresses beyond 1,000 time steps, the decline in energy becomes more gradual, with the energy levels approaching zero. The energy levels eventually reach zero by around 2,300 time steps, indicating the complete depletion of energy in the male animal population. 

Throughout the simulation, several inflection points and change points are observed, indicating shifts in the rate of energy decline. However, these points do not reverse the overall trend but rather mark periods where the rate of decline either accelerates or decelerates slightly. The statistical analysis confirms the consistent decreasing trend in energy levels, with significant Mann-Kendall trends indicating a continuous decline across various time windows.
