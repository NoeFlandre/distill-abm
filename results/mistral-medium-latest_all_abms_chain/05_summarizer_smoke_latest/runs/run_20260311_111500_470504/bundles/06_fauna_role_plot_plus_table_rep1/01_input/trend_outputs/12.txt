The total energy of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent declining trend over time. Initially, the total energy is at its maximum value of 12,500 units. This high energy level rapidly decreases within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the total energy of female animals drops significantly to approximately 2,000 units. This steep decline continues, albeit at a slower rate, until around 1,000 time steps, where the total energy stabilizes to lower values, generally fluctuating around a few hundred units. Throughout the remaining time steps, the energy levels continue to decrease gradually, eventually approaching zero by the final time step of 2,312. The statistical analysis indicates multiple inflection points and change points, suggesting periods where the rate of decline shifts. Notably, there are several peaks and valleys identified around the 375 to 410 time step range, indicating minor fluctuations in the declining trend. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms significant decreasing trends across various windows, reinforcing the overall decline in total energy. The oscillation indices suggest periodic minor fluctuations, but the dominant trend remains a consistent decrease in the total energy of female animals over the simulation period.
