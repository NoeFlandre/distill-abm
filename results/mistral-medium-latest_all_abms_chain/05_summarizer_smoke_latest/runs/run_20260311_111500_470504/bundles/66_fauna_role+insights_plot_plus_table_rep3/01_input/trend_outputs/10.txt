The simulation of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent trend of decreasing total number of prey animals over time. Initially, the number of animals starts at approximately 166.67 and exhibits a sharp decline within the first few hundred time steps. By around 500 time steps, the number of animals has significantly reduced, continuing to decrease steadily thereafter. The rate of decline slows down as the number of animals approaches zero, with the population effectively reaching extinction by the end of the simulation at 73,000 time steps. Throughout the simulation, there are minor fluctuations and small peaks, but the overall trend remains a consistent decrease in the animal population. Key inflection points and change points indicate periods where the rate of decline changes, but the general downward trend persists. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend across multiple time windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values. For decision makers, this suggests that under the given parameters and conditions, the prey population is highly susceptible to extinction due to the combined pressures of hunting and environmental factors. The insights imply that without intervention or changes to the model parameters, the prey population is unlikely to sustain itself over the long term.
