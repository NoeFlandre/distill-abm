 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The overall trend demonstrates a significant shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the simulated period . The mean number of whole milk choices is approximately 1130.88, with a median of 1158.97, indicating a relatively symmetric distribution around the central tendency . The minimum value remains at 0 at the start, while the maximum value reaches approximately 2194.57 at the final time step .  The simulation ends with the mean whole milk quantity at approximately 253.10 units, reflecting a substantial overall reduction in whole milk consumption over the entire period . The mean habit factor for whole milk starts at 1.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . By the fifteenth step, the trend becomes consistently upward, indicating a significant shift in consumer habits towards skimmed and semi-skimmed milk .  The mean choice function deviation starts at 0.0000 and increases rapidly in the initial steps, reaching approximately 0.55 by step 7 . This rapid increase indicates a significant divergence in the choice function scores among agents as they evaluate their milk consumption options . By step 28, the deviation is around 0.70, showing that the majority of agents have settled into their preferred milk consumption patterns .
