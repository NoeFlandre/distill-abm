the model is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . the landscape is a simplified grassland environment with randomly generated patches of grass, male and female ungulates, and human hunters . the model allows users to conduct experiments to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . the number of human hunter-gatherer bands in the simulation remains constant throughout the entire duration of the simulation . cumulative number of postpartum females killed rises steeply from 0 to approximately 100 within the first 500 steps . this rapid increase indicates a high initial hunting pressure . however, over time, the efficiency of hunting diminishes, leading to a stabilization . the decline continues, and by 1000 steps, the number of male animals further reduces to approximately 40 . this trend suggests a rapid initial reduction in the male animal population followed by a more gradual decline as the population nears extinction . in most simulations, the population of animals is effectively extinct by this point, with very few or no animals remaining . the total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent declining trend across all simulations . around the 200th step, a noticeable decline in total energy begins, marking the onset of a significant reduction in the energy levels of the prey population . this suggests that without intervention or changes in hunting practices, prey populations could face severe depletion or extinction .
