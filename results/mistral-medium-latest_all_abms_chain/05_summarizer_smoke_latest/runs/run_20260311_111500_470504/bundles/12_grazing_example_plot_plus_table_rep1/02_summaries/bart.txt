 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  Trend analysis using the rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the initial windows, followed by significant decreasing trends, and eventually, no significant trends towards the end of the simulation . The presence of multiple inflection points indicates periodic adjustments and corrections in the households' livestock management practices .  The number of households engaging in social learning starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 20 time steps . The Gini coefficient for the total healthy livestock starts at 0, indicating an equal distribution among households . The trend of decreasing destocking rates continues, with minor peaks and valleys, until it stabilizes at a lower level . The presence of multiple inflection points and minor oscillations indicates periodic adjustments and variability in household responses to resource availability .  Learning households starts at 20 and exhibits a slight increase in the early stages . This initial rise is followed by a general downward trend, with notable fluctuations . By step 20, the count decreases to around 19, and this declining trend continues with some oscillations until around 45, where the number stabilizes .
