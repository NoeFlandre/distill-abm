The total energy of female animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent decreasing trend over time. Initially, the total energy starts at a high value of 12500 units. This high energy level rapidly declines within the first few hundred time steps, indicating a sharp drop in the energy available within the female animal population. By around 500 time steps, the energy levels have significantly reduced, marking a critical change point in the data. The decline continues steadily, with several inflection points indicating periods where the rate of decrease changes slightly but remains consistently downward. Notable inflection points occur around time steps 506, 744, and 836, suggesting phases where the rate of energy depletion varies but still trends downward. By approximately 2310 time steps, the total energy of female animals approaches zero, indicating a near-complete depletion of energy within this population. Throughout the simulation, the energy levels exhibit minor oscillations with a dominant period of 63 time steps, but these oscillations do not alter the overall decreasing trend. The statistical analysis confirms this decreasing trend across multiple time windows, with significant negative slopes and tau values, reinforcing the consistent decline in total energy over the course of the simulation.
