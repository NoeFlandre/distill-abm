 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits distinct trends over the simulated time period . Overall, the trend demonstrates a rapid initial increase, followed by periods of stabilization and minor oscillations . The data also exhibits some oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 63 ticks, particularly noticeable towards the end of the simulation period .  Simulation of the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a clear and consistent trend of decreasing prey animal population over time . Initially, the population starts at 125 male prey animals, with the population reaching zero by the end of the simulation . The decline is marked by several key phases and inflection points, indicating changes in the rate of decrease . By the 2311th tick, the energy level drops to zero, indicating the complete depletion of energy in the male prey population . This trend reflects the interactions between environmental conditions, hunting pressure, and the life history of the animals .
