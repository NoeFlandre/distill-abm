 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The average weekly consumption of whole milk per agent starts at approximately 2654.81 units and exhibits a general decreasing trend over the simulation period . Notable inflection points occur at steps 5, 13, 16, 19, 24, 25, 27, and 30, suggesting periods where the rate of change in consumption patterns shifts .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows the dynamics of mean skimmed and semi-skimmed milk quantity consumed by agents over a series of steps . Initially, the mean whole milk quantity starts at a high value of approximately 2654.81 units . By the end of the simulation, the total number of skimmed milk choices reaches approximately 25,643.83 .  The overall trend indicates an initial rapid adoption phase, followed by a stabilization period with minor fluctuations, and concluding with a renewed increase in consumption . The statistical analysis supports these observations, with significant increasing trends identified in the initial and final phases of the simulation, while the middle phase shows no significant trend, indicating stability .  The mean value environmental deviation in the agent-based model simulating milk consumption behaviors shows distinct trends over the time steps analyzed .
