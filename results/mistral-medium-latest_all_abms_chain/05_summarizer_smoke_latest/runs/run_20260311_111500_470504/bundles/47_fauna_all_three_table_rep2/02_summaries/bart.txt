 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . Animals can reproduce, and users can control sex ratios and reproduction probability . For decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of initial conditions and early interventions in managing hunting pressures . The cyclical patterns suggest that hunting pressure may vary with environmental or behavioral factors, which could be critical for understanding the dynamics of prey population decline and for designing conservation strategies .
