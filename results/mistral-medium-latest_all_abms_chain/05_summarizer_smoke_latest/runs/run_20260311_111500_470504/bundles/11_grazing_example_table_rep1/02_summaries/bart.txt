 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  The average total number of livestock, including both healthy livestock and those that have been destocked, across all households begins at 90.0000 . The average amount of available green biomass across all patches begins at 45,000 units . Initially, there is a slight increase, reaching a peak of approximately 90,523 units by the third time step . Following this peak, the reserve biomass experiences a gradual decline, hitting a low point of around 84,114 units at the 26th time step.  The inequality in the distribution of healthy livestock across all households, as measured by the Gini coefficient, starts at a value of approximately 0.0726 by the end of the simulation period . The number of households following the E-RO strategy begins at 20.0 and exhibits a slight increase in the initial time steps, reaching a peak of 21.1 at time step 8 .
