 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix with a relative spatial scale . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . Ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values and processing costs for males and females .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains constant until around 800 time steps . The number of male animals declines rapidly within the first 1000 time steps, dropping to around 20 individuals . For decision makers, understanding these trends can provide insights into the reproductive dynamics of the prey population under different environmental and hunting pressure scenarios .  The total energy of male ungulates begins at approximately 12,000 units . Over the first 500 time steps, there is a rapid decline in the number of animals, dropping to around 50 . The decline becomes more gradual, with the number approaching zero by around 2000 time steps . For decision makers, these trends highlight the potential for rapid population decline and eventual extinction of megaherbivores .  The percentage of green patches in the landscape starts at approximately 33%, reflecting the initial proportion of vegetated patches set in the model . The initial stability, followed by sharp declines and recoveries, underscores the need for monitoring and managing grazing activities to prevent overgrazing and ensure sustainable fodder availability for ungulates .
