 The ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . The main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk per agent starts at approximately 2600 units at the initial time step . There is a sharp decline in consumption within the first 5 steps, dropping to around 1000 units . The overall trend indicates a significant reduction in whole milk consumption over time, with key phases of rapid decline followed by periods of stabilization .  Initially, the consumption starts at a very low value, close to zero . During the first five steps, there is a rapid increase in the mean quantity consumed, reaching approximately 1000 units . This sharp rise indicates a significant shift in agents' behavior towards adopting skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . The trend suggests an initial strong preference for whole milk, followed by a significant reduction in consumption over time .  The trend shows an initial rapid increase in deviation, followed by a gradual increase, and eventually stabilizing with minimal changes . This pattern suggests that agents initially explore different choices widely but eventually settle into more consistent behavior patterns . The trend suggests that initially, there is a significant discrepancy between the agents' environmental values and their milk choices .
