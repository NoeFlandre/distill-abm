 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The model allows users to manipulate these parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The temporal units used in the model are 'ticks,' which can be scaled to any temporal granularity . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick .  Trend shows a rapid initial increase followed by a steady rise and eventual stabilization, reflecting the dynamic interactions between hunting pressure, prey population dynamics, and environmental factors . The cumulative number of postpartum female prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands starts at zero and increases rapidly within the first 500 time steps .  The total energy of male animals starts at 12,500 units and declines rapidly over the first 500 time steps . The energy levels drop sharply to around 1,300 units by the 500th time step, indicating a significant reduction in the population or energy reserves . The number of female prey animals approaches zero, indicating that the population could not sustain itself under the conditions .  Percentage of green patches starts at approximately 33% and remains relatively stable throughout the simulation . Change points at 350, 500, 625, 875, and 2310 mark significant shifts in the energy dynamics . These points suggest dynamic interactions between grazing and regrowth processes .
