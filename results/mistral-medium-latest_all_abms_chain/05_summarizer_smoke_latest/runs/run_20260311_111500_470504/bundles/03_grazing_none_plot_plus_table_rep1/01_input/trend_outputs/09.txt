The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households in the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend over the 100 steps of the simulation. Initially, the destocking starts at zero and rapidly increases in the early steps. The rate of increase is steepest at the beginning, with a slope of approximately 4.956 in the first 10 steps, indicating a rapid initial response in destocking. As the simulation progresses, the rate of increase in destocking gradually slows down but remains consistently positive throughout the entire period. By the end of the simulation at step 100, the mean destock total reaches around 96.78 livestock. The trend analysis using rolling Mann-Kendall tests confirms that the increasing trend is statistically significant across all windows, with the slope of the trend decreasing over time, indicating a slowing rate of destocking as the simulation progresses. Inflection points are noted at various steps, suggesting changes in the rate of increase, but the overall trend remains upward. The data does not exhibit any peaks or valleys, reinforcing the steady increase in destocking over time. The oscillation indices and change points indicate some variability in the rate of increase, but the dominant pattern is a consistent rise in the cumulative number of livestock destocked.
