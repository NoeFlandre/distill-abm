 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model . The goal is to evaluate how different behavioral types and learning strategies impact the resilience and sustainability of pastoral systems . The model allows for the adjustment of a range of parameters to observe their effects on social, ecological, and economic outcomes .  Initially, the mean household risk attitude starts at a low value of approximately 0.0074 . During the early stages, up to around time step 20, there is a rapid increase in the risk attitude, reaching a value of about 0.2200 . This period is marked by a significant upward trend, indicating that households are becoming more inclined to take risks . The overall mean risk attitude across the simulation is 0.2333, with a median value of 0.2013 .  The average number of livestock destocked across households in the agent-based model shows a distinct trend over the simulated time steps . Initially, the destocking rate is zero and rapidly increases in the early steps . The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at a baseline value of 0.0000, indicating an initially equal distribution of livestock health across households . Inflection points are noted at various time steps, suggesting periods where the rate of increase in inequality changes .  The dominant pattern is a consistent rise in the cumulative number of livestock destocked . The number of non-learning households in the simulation starts at 20 and exhibits an initial increase, peaking at around 21.1 at step 8 . The statistical analysis indicates significant decreasing trends in the early phases of the simulation .
