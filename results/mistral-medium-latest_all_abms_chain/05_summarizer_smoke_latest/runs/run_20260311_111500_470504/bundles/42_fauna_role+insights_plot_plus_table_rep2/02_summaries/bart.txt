 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model allows users to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges .  The cumulative number of postpartum females killed in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) simulations exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages, followed by a gradual stabilization . For decision-makers, these trends imply that initial hunting pressure is intense but stabilizes over time . Understanding these dynamics can help in designing interventions to manage hunting pressure and protect vulnerable populations .
