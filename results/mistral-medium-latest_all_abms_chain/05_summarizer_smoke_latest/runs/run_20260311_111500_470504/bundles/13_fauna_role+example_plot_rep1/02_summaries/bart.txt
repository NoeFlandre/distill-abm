 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, existing on a 211x211 patch matrix . Ungulate herds are sexually dimorphic, with distinct food values and processing costs for males and females . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback .  The cumulative number of births among the prey animal population starts at zero and remains constant until around 700 time steps . The initial period of no births could be attributed to the initial conditions of the simulation, where the population is either stabilizing or has not yet reached a state conducive to reproduction . The variability among different simulation runs is relatively low, indicating consistent trends across multiple simulations . This consistency suggests that the model's parameters and initial conditions lead to predictable outcomes in terms of prey mortality .  The total energy of male animals starts at approximately 12,000 units . It declines rapidly over the first 500 time steps, reaching around 2,000  units . The variability among different simulation runs increases slightly after the initial rapid decline, suggesting stochastic factors and individual differences in animal behavior and hunter success rates start to play a more significant role as the population decreases .
