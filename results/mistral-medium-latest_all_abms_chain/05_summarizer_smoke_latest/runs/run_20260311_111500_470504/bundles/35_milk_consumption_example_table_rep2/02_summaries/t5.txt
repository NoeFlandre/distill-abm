agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk . the model uses both theoretical grounding and empirical data . main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the average weekly consumption reaches its highest value of 1456.59 . by 2005, the majority of agents have adopted skimmed and semi-skimmed milk as their primary choice . the overall trend demonstrates a significant shift from whole milk to lower-fat alternatives . by the end of the simulation period in 2005, the mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed reaches 1456.5991 units . the mean consumption across the entire period is 1053.2062 units, with a median of 1220.2054 units, showing a substantial rise over the 32-year period . the rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend in deviation across multiple time windows . inflection points at indices 7, 12, and 28 suggest notable changes in the rate of increase in deviation . these oscillations reflect periodic variations in the agents' choice functions, possibly due to underlying cyclic influences .
