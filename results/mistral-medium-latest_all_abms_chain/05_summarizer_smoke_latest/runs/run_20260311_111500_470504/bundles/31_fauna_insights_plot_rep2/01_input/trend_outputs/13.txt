The total energy of all animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a sharp decline over time. Initially, the total energy of all animals starts at approximately 25,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period, indicating a stable initial condition with abundant energy resources among the prey population. However, around 500 steps, a significant and rapid decline in total energy begins. This decline continues steeply until approximately 1,000 steps, where the total energy drops to near zero. This trend suggests a rapid depletion of energy resources among the prey animals, potentially due to high predation pressure, limited food availability, or a combination of both. After 1,000 steps, the total energy of all animals stabilizes at a very low level, close to zero, indicating that the prey population has been significantly reduced or has very low energy reserves. For decision makers, this trend implies that under the given parameters, the prey population is not sustainable over the long term, leading to a rapid collapse in energy levels. This insight could inform strategies to adjust hunting pressures, improve food availability, or modify other environmental factors to promote a more stable ecosystem.
