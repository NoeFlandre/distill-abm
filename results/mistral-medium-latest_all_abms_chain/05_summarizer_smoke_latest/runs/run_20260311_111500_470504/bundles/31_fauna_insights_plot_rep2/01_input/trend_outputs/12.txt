The total energy of female animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at a high value of approximately 12,000 units. This initial high energy level indicates that the female prey population begins with a substantial energy reserve. In the early stages, up to around 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the total energy, dropping steeply to about 2,000 units. This sharp decrease suggests a significant initial impact on the female prey population, likely due to a combination of hunting pressure and environmental factors. Following this steep decline, the rate of energy loss slows down considerably. From around 500 to 2,000 steps, the total energy continues to decrease but at a much slower rate, eventually stabilizing near zero. This stabilization indicates that the female prey population has reached a point where the remaining individuals have minimal energy reserves. The trend implies that the initial conditions and interactions within the model lead to a rapid depletion of energy reserves in the female prey population, followed by a prolonged period of low energy levels. For decision-makers, this trend highlights the vulnerability of the female prey population under the given parameters and the potential for rapid energy depletion under sustained hunting pressure and environmental conditions.
