 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . The model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The total number of choices made by all agents combined shows a consistent linear increase over the time steps . The mean quantity of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents exhibits a rapid increase in the early stages, followed by a stabilization period and a subsequent gradual rise . For decision makers, this trend indicates a significant shift in consumer preferences towards healthier milk options .  The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk shows a gradual increase over the time steps in the simulation . For decision makers, this suggests that habits related to whole milk consumption become more entrenched as time progresses . Understanding this gradual increase in habit factor can help in designing strategies that encourage sustained behavioral change among consumers .
