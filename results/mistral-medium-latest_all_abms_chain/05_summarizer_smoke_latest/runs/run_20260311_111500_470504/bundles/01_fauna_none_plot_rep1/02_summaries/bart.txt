 The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbivores . The model simulates a simplified grassland environment with human hunters and male and female ungulates . The landscape is an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges, consisting of a 211x211 patch matrix .  The Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model allows users to manipulate parameters to test various hypotheses related to extinction events . The model includes summary statistics that are shown as continuous line graphs, reflecting data from each tick . These statistics include the number of prey items taken per 10 ticks, the proportion of grazed to ungrazed grass, forager energy, and number of animals in the environment . The number of human hunter-gatherer bands remains constant throughout the simulation .  The total number of animals in the Megafaunal Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) starts at an initial value of 250 . In the early stages, up to approximately 500 steps, there is a rapid decline in the animal population . Beyond 1500 steps, the population stabilizes at very low numbers, often reaching zero and remaining there for rest of the simulation .  The total energy of all animals in the simulation starts at a high value of approximately 25,000 units . This high energy level rapidly declines within the first 500 steps . By the 1,000th step, the energy levels stabilizes near zero, indicating that most animals have either died or their energy levels have significantly diminished .
