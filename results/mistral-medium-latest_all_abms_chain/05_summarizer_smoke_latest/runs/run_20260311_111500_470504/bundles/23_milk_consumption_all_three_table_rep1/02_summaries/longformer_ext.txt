An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents, with the goal of assessing their effects on the system as a whole. In this case, the ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice. The main outcome of the ABM is the average weekly consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk (whole milk) and skimmed and semi-skimmed milk (skimmed and semi-skimmed milk). The initial value indicates a strong preference or habit for whole milk consumption among the agents at the start of the simulation. The consumption then exhibits a rapid and significant decline over the initial time steps, dropping sharply. This steep decline suggests a substantial shift in consumer behavior away from whole milk, potentially due to changing perceptions, social influences, or other factors modeled in the simulation. For decision-makers, these trends highlight the importance of understanding the dynamic nature of consumer behavior and the factors driving shifts in milk consumption.
