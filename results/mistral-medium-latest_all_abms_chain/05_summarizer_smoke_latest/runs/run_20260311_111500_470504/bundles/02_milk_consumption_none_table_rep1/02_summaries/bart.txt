 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . Model includes 1,000 agents, each part of a social network where influence is a function of interaction probability and agent susceptibility .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent and steady increase in the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined over the observed time period . Initially, the number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk started at zero . Over time, this number steadily increased, reaching a peak of approximately 25,643.83 at the final time step . The mean number of choices for skimmed milk across all time steps is around 11,157.35  The mean consumption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk consumed by agents from 1974 to 2005 reveals several notable trends and patterns . Initially, the mean consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . This period of rapid increase is followed by a phase of stabilization where consumption values exhibit minor fluctuations around a high mean value . The mean factor applied to the whole milk choice function due to habit demonstrates a gradual and steady increase over the entire simulation period .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a rapid increase initially . By the 14th time step, it reaches a peak value of approximately 13.4756 . Following this peak, the mean deviation stabilizes around the range of 13.4153 to 13.4543 for the remainder of the observed period .
