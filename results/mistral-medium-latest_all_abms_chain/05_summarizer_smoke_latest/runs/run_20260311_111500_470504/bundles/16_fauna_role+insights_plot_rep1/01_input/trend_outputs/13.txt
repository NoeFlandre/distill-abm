The total energy of all animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a clear and consistent trend across simulations. Initially, the total energy of all animals is at its peak, approximately 25,000 units. This high energy level is maintained briefly before a sharp decline begins around the 100th step. The decline is steep and rapid, indicating a significant reduction in the total energy of the animals. By the 500th step, the total energy has dropped to around 5,000 units, marking an 80% reduction from the initial value. This steep decline continues more gradually until approximately the 1,000th step, where the total energy stabilizes around 1,000 units. Beyond this point, the total energy levels off and remains relatively constant, approaching zero but not quite reaching it even by the 2,000th step. 

For decision makers, this trend suggests that the initial conditions of the simulation lead to a rapid depletion of energy reserves among the animal population, likely due to a combination of hunting pressure and environmental factors. The stabilization of energy levels at a low value indicates a potential equilibrium state where the remaining animals are able to sustain themselves despite the pressures. This insight could inform strategies aimed at conservation and management of megaherbivore populations, highlighting the importance of initial conditions and the need for interventions to prevent rapid energy depletion.
