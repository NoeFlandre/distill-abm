The simulation results indicate a general trend of decreasing number of households following the E-RO strategy over time. Initially, the number of E-RO households starts at 20.0. There is a slight increase in the early stages, reaching a peak of 21.1 at time step 8. Following this peak, there is a significant decreasing trend, with the number of E-RO households dropping to around 14.7 by the end of the simulation at time step 100. The most notable changes occur within the first 20 time steps, where the number of E-RO households decreases sharply. This is evidenced by the rolling Mann-Kendall test, which shows significant decreasing trends in the early windows, particularly from time steps 11 to 65. After time step 66, the trend stabilizes, and no significant trends are observed, indicating a plateau in the number of E-RO households. The data also exhibits some oscillatory behavior with a dominant period of 49 time steps, suggesting periodic fluctuations around the general decreasing trend. Key inflection points are noted at various time steps, indicating changes in the rate of decrease. The change points at time steps 20 and 45 mark significant shifts in the trend dynamics. Overall, the simulation suggests a clear movement away from the E-RO strategy among households over the modeled period.
