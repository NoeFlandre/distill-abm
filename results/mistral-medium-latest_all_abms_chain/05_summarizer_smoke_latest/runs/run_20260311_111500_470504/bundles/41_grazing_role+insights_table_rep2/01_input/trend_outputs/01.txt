The average initial risk attitude of all social learning agents in the simulation remained constant throughout the entire duration of the model run. The value was consistently 0.0074 from the beginning to the end of the 100 timesteps. There was no variation or fluctuation in this metric, as indicated by the standard deviation and variance both being 0.0. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirmed the absence of any significant trends across all windows, reinforcing the stability of this metric over time. The implications for decision-makers are that, under the given parameter settings, the initial risk attitude of social learning agents does not change, suggesting a high level of stability and predictability in this aspect of household behavior. This stability could be useful for planning and managing grazing systems, as it indicates that initial risk attitudes are not influenced by the dynamics of the model over the simulated time period.
