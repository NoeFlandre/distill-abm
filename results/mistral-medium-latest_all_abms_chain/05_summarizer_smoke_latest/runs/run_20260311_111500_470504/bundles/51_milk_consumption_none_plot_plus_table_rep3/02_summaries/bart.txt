 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices, specifically the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk .  The agent-based model simulates the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . It focuses on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk . Initially, the number of skimmed milk choices starts at zero . The mean total number of whole milk choices across all time steps is around 1130.88, with a median value of 1158.97 .  The mean quantity of whole milk consumed consistently decreases with some minor fluctuations, indicating a significant reduction in whole milk consumption over time . The mean habit factor for skimmed and semi-skimmed milk starts at a baseline value of 1.0000 . Initially, there is a slight increase in the habit factor, reaching approximately 1.0205 by the first step . The trend shows a consistent upward trajectory .  The mean value environmental deviation starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period . The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates an increasing trend in the early steps, which becomes non-significant in later windows . After this rapid increase, the deviation stabilizes and fluctuates slightly around a mean value of approximately 13.4302 .
