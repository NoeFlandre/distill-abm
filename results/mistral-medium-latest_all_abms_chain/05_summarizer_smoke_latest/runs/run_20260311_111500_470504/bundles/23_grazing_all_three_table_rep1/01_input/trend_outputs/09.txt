The average cumulative number of livestock destocked across all households begins at zero and exhibits a consistent increasing trend throughout the simulation period, reaching approximately 96.78 by the final time step. The trend is characterized by a rapid initial increase, with the first significant rise occurring within the first 10 time steps, where the slope of the increase is quite steep. This initial phase is followed by a more gradual but steady increase in destocking rates. The inflection points, which indicate changes in the rate of increase, are scattered throughout the timeline, suggesting periodic adjustments in the destocking behavior of households. Notably, there are several change points identified at time steps 25, 60, and 100, indicating shifts in the underlying dynamics of the system. The rolling Mann-Kendall test confirms a consistent increasing trend across all windows, with significant p-values, reinforcing the steady rise in destocking over time. The oscillation indices suggest periodic fluctuations in the destocking rates, particularly around the mid-point of the simulation. For decision makers, the consistent increase in destocking highlights the progressive adaptation of households to the grazing conditions, possibly due to the learning capabilities introduced in the model. The periodic adjustments and oscillations suggest that households are continuously evaluating and responding to the ecological and climatic conditions, which could inform strategies for sustainable grazing management.
