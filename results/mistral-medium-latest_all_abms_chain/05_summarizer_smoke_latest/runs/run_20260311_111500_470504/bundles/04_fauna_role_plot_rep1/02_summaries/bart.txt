 The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) is an agent-based model designed to test hypotheses related to the extinction of megaherbiivores . Model considers various factors such as environmental change, human hunting behavior, herd demography, movement, sexual dimorphism, and life history of animals . The model allows users to manipulate various parameters to establish the socio-natural dynamics of the experiment matrix .  The Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model allows users to test how environmental conditions, human hunting, and animal life history interact to influence extinction dynamics . Hunters are embedded with logic approximating dynamic optimal foraging theory, making foraging decisions based on environmental feedback . The model's landscape consists of an abstract square universe that wraps at the edges .  Simulations of the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibit distinct trends in the cumulative number of deaths among the prey animal population over time . Initially, there are no births until around 800 steps, where a sharp increase begins . By around 2000 steps, the number of male prey animals in most simulations reaches zero, indicating a complete extinction event . Initially, the energy of female animals is approximately 12,000 units, followed by a more gradual decline as the simulation progresses .
