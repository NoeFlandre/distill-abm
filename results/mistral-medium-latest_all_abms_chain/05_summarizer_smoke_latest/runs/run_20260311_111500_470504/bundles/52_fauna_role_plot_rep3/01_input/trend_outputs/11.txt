The total energy of male animals in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) exhibits a consistent declining trend across all simulations. Initially, the total energy of male animals starts at approximately 12,000 units. This high energy level is maintained for a brief period, indicating a stable and healthy population of male ungulates at the beginning of the simulations. Around the 500-step mark, a sharp decline in total energy begins, dropping steeply to about 2,000 units by the 1,000-step mark. This significant reduction suggests a critical phase where male animals are experiencing substantial energy depletion, potentially due to increased hunting pressure, environmental changes, or a combination of both factors. Following this steep decline, the total energy continues to decrease but at a slower rate, eventually approaching zero around the 2,000-step mark. This trend indicates that the population of male animals is being severely impacted, leading to a near-complete depletion of their total energy reserves. The consistent pattern across multiple simulations suggests that the model parameters and interactions are driving the male animal population towards a critical state, reflecting the challenging survival conditions imposed by the model's dynamics.
