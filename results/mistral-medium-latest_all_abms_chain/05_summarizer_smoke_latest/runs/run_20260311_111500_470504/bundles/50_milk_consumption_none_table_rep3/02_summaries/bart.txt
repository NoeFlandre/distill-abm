 The agent-based model aims to simulate the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005 . The primary goal is to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choices . The main outcome is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person .  The simulation of the agent-based model shows a consistent increasing trend in the total number of milk choices made by all agents combined over the time period from 1974 to 2005 . The overall trend demonstrates a substantial shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over the three decades .  Initially, the mean consumption starts at a very low value of approximately 5.29 units . This value rapidly increases in the early stages of the simulation, indicating a swift adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk among the agents . The mean percentage difference between the maximum and minimum choice function scores among agents in the simulation starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a general increasing trend over time .  The mean deviation between the agents' held environmental values and the implied values by their milk choices starts at 0.0000 and exhibits a rapid increase in the early time steps . By the 13th time step, the deviation reaches its maximum value of 13.4756 . The data shows several peaks and valleys, indicating small oscillations .
