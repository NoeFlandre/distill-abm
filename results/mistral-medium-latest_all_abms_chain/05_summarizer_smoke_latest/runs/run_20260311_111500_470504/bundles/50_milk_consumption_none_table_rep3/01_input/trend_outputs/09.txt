The mean factor applied to the skimmed and semi-skimmed milk choice function due to habit starts at a value of 1.0000. Initially, there is no significant trend observed in the early time steps, as indicated by the rolling Mann-Kendall analysis. However, from the fifth time step onwards, there is a consistent increasing trend in the mean habit factor. This trend is statistically significant and continues through subsequent time windows. The mean habit factor reaches its maximum value of 1.5879 at the final time step. Throughout the simulation, the mean habit factor exhibits a general upward trajectory, indicating an increasing influence of habit on the choice of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk over time. The presence of oscillation indices suggests some periodic fluctuations, but the overall trend remains upward.
