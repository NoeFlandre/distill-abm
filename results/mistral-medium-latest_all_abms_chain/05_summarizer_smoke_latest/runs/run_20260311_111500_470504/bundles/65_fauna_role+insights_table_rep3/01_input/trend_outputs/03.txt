The cumulative number of male prey animals hunted and killed by forager bands in the Megaherbivore Hunting Pressure Model (MHPM) shows a general increasing trend over time, with notable dynamics and fluctuations. Initially, the number of males killed starts at zero and gradually increases. The mean number of males killed across the simulation is approximately 100, with a median of 116.5, indicating that in most simulations, the number of males killed tends to be higher than the mean. The standard deviation of 32.52 suggests variability in the number of males killed across different simulations. The maximum value of 120.17 males killed is reached at tick 1876, indicating a peak in hunting activity at this time step. The data exhibits several peaks and valleys, suggesting periods of increased and decreased hunting activity. The rolling Mann-Kendall test indicates significant increasing trends in the early and middle phases of the simulation, with periods of no significant trend and a decreasing trend towards the end. The presence of multiple inflection points and change points suggests shifts in the dynamics of hunting activity. The dominant oscillation period of 63 ticks indicates cyclical patterns in the hunting activity. For decision-makers, these trends highlight the variability and cyclical nature of hunting pressure on male prey, which could inform strategies for managing hunting activities and understanding their impact on megaherbivore populations.
