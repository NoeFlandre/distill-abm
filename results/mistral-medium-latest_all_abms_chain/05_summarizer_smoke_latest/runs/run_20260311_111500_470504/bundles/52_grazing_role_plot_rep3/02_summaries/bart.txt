 The agent-based model in question is an extension of the RAGE (Rangeland Grazing Ecology) model . It simulates the interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods in a common property grazing system . The extension introduces learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning, which are central to adaptive co-management . The model consists of two main sub-models: Vegetation and Household decision-making .  The model is grounded in social theory and reflects empirical observations, providing a framework for studying the complex interactions in grazing systems . The model allows for the evaluation of various scenarios, such as the influence of demographic settings and herd size, the impact of different learning strategies, and the diffusion of learning behaviors among households .  The Gini coefficient for total healthy livestock starts at 0 for all simulations, indicating an initially equal distribution of livestock among households . Initially, the metric starts at a high value of approximately 90. This high initial value rapidly declines within the first 10 steps, dropping to around 45 . After step 50, there is a gradual increase in the metric, with some trajectories showing a more pronounced upward trend .  Many of the simulations show a sharp decline in the number of non-learning households, with several dropping to below 10 . This trend continues, and by approximately 40 time steps, the majority of simulations stabilize . Beyond this point, the number . remains relatively stable, with minor fluctuations but no significant changes .
