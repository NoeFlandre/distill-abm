The simulation results indicate a clear and consistent increasing trend in the total number of times agents chose skimmed or semi-skimmed milk over the observed time steps. Initially, the value starts at zero, reflecting no adoption of alternative milk types. As the simulation progresses, there is a steady increase in the adoption rate. Key time steps show significant growth, particularly around the later stages of the simulation, where the maximum value reaches approximately 25,643.83. The mean value across all time steps is around 11,157.35, with a median of 10,679.43, indicating that the majority of the data points are concentrated around these values. The standard deviation of 8,241.32 suggests a considerable spread in the data, reflecting varying rates of adoption over time. The rolling Mann-Kendall test across multiple windows consistently shows a significant increasing trend, with high tau values and significant p-values, reinforcing the steady upward trajectory. The absence of detected peaks and valleys further supports a smooth and continuous increase in the adoption of skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The inflection point at time step 31 and the change point at time step 32 mark critical stages where the rate of increase becomes more pronounced. The oscillation analysis suggests periodic fluctuations with a dominant period of 15 time steps, indicating some cyclical patterns in the adoption behavior. Overall, the data reflects a robust and sustained shift towards the adoption of alternative milk types, driven by the underlying parameters and interactions within the agent-based model.
