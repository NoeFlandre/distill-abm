Between time steps 10 and 20, the consumption stabilizes around 1,200 units, suggesting a period of equilibrium where the adoption rate plateaus. This stabilization could imply that the market has reached a saturation point where most agents who are inclined to switch have already done so. This gradual rise suggests a steady, long-term adoption trend, possibly influenced by continuous marketing efforts, social influence, and habit formation. The trends indicate that while initial adoption can be rapid due to novel information and social influence, long-term sustained growth requires ongoing reinforcement and positive feedback loops. For decision-makers, these insights highlight the importance of initial awareness campaigns to spur rapid adoption, followed by sustained efforts to maintain and gradually increase consumption through social norms and habit reinforcement.
