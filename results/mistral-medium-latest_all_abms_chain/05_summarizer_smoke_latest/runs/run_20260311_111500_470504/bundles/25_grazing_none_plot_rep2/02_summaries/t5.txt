agent-based model is an extension of the original RAGE (Rangeland Use and Adaptation) model, which simulates interactions between pastures, livestock, and household livelihoods . the model adds learning capabilities to the agents, specifically learning-by-doing and social learning . the mean household risk attitude starts at a baseline value of approximately 0.5 . in the early stages, up to around step 20, there is a rapid divergence in the risk attitudes among households . some households exhibit a sharp increase in risk attitude, while others show a decrease . between steps 20 and 60, the Gini coefficient continues to increase at a slower rate . by step 100, the values range approximately between 0.03 and 0.09 . this trend suggests that over time, the disparity in the distribution of healthy livestock becomes more pronounced .
