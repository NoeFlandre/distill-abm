An agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents, with the goal of assessing their effects on the system as a whole. In this case, the ABM is designed to replicate and understand the adoption behaviors of milk consumption by the British public from 1974 to 2005, focusing on the shift from whole milk to skimmed and semi-skimmed milk. The model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice. The main outcome of the ABM is a gradual decline in consumption, dropping to around 1,000 units. This sharp decrease suggests a significant shift in consumer behavior, likely influenced by changing perceptions of health and environmental benefits associated with alternative milk types. Between steps 10 and 20, the rate of decline slows, with consumption stabilizing around 800 units.
