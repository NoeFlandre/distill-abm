agent-based model (ABM) is a computational framework used to simulate the actions and interactions of autonomous agents . the model aims to explore how perception, habits, and social influence affect an individual's decision-making process regarding milk choice . main outcome of interest is the average weekly consumption of each milk type per person . the average weekly consumption of whole milk begins at 2,600 units . this high initial value indicates a strong initial habit and preference for whole milk among the agents . over the first 10 steps, there is a rapid decline in consumption, dropping to around 1,000 units, suggesting a significant shift in consumer behavior . this initial slow growth could be attributed to the initial low perception of health and environmental benefits of alternative milk types . by 10 time steps, the total number of choices made reaches approximately 2,500, indicating a significant shift in consumer behavior . this rapid increase could be due to the cumulative effects of social influence, habit formation, and the evaluation of past choices . by the 10th step, the mean habit factor reaches around 1.03 . this gradual increase suggests a period of stabilization where habits are being reinforced over time . Towards the end of the simulation, a steady increase indicates a consistent reinforcement of whole milk consumption habits among the agents . the value of 13.5 for the remainder of the simulation indicates that the system has reached a steady state where the discrepancy between held and implied environmental values remains constant . this plateau suggests that after the initial period of adjustment and interaction, the agents' choices and their environmental values reach a form of equilibrium .
